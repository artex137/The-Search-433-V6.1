UFOs and Paranormal Sightings Highlight Coast to Coast AM Programs

On August 29, 2025, Coast to Coast AM featured discussions on UFOs, remote viewing, and the Hollow Earth Theory, captivating audiences with various intriguing topics. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these discussions or sightings.

This week’s programs have drawn attention to the ongoing fascination with UFOs and unexplained phenomena, prompting discussions on government transparency and scientific inquiry. Authorities and researchers continue to investigate sightings and claims, adding to the public's interest in these topics.

Key facts

  • Programs aired on August 24, 25, 27, and 28, 2025.
  • Richard Beckwith, Wyoming State Director of MUFON, spoke about secrecy laws on August 24.
  • Dr. Gregory Rogers, former NASA flight surgeon, discussed reverse-engineered technology on August 28.
  • Remote viewing was explored by Lyn Buchanan, a former member of a psychic spy program, on August 25.
  • Scientists from the University of Magellan's Environmental Studies Group captured mysterious lights in Chile.

Timeline

On August 24, Richard Beckwith discussed the challenges of government disclosure regarding UFOs and the role of private contractors in keeping information secret. On August 25, Lyn Buchanan shared insights from his experiences with remote viewing and the disbandment of Project Stargate. On August 27, Brooks Agnew revisited the Hollow Earth Theory, citing scientific studies that suggest gaps between the Earth's core and crust. Finally, on August 28, Dr. Gregory Rogers recounted observations of ET technology repurposed by the Air Force.

Official statements

“I am skeptical that the truth about UFOs will be revealed to the public,” said Richard Beckwith, highlighting the complexities of government secrecy.
“The group was eventually disbanded after Congress grew weary of experiments involving the mind,” Lyn Buchanan noted regarding his experiences in remote viewing.

Context

The topics covered this week reflect a growing public interest in UFO sightings and paranormal phenomena. Previous discussions on Coast to Coast AM have also included similar themes, contributing to ongoing debates about government transparency and the scientific exploration of unexplained events.

What’s next

Investigations into the mysterious lights in Chile and other sightings are ongoing, with researchers seeking to better understand these phenomena. Further discussions on UFOs and related topics are expected in future Coast to Coast AM programs.

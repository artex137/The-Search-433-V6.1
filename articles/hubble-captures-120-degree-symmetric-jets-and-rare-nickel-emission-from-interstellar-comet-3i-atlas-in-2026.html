Hubble captures 120-degree symmetric jets and rare nickel emission from interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS in 2026

The Hubble Space Telescope has observed 120-degree symmetric jets and rare nickel emission from the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on February 1, 2026. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this astronomical event.

This discovery is significant as it provides insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar comets. Astronomers are analyzing the data to understand the implications for future studies of such celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: February 1, 2026
  • Location: Observations conducted by the Hubble Space Telescope
  • Comet designation: 3I/ATLAS
  • Notable findings: 120-degree symmetric jets and nickel emission
  • No injuries or fatalities reported

Timeline

On February 1, 2026, the Hubble Space Telescope captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing its unique jet structure and emissions. This observation marks an important milestone in the study of interstellar comets.

Official statements

“The findings from Hubble provide a clearer picture of how interstellar comets behave and their composition,” said a representative from NASA.

Context

This observation of 3I/ATLAS adds to existing knowledge about interstellar comets, which are rare and provide valuable information about the early solar system. Previous studies have focused on the physical characteristics of such comets, but this new data highlights their chemical makeup.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to analyze the data collected from Hubble to gain further insights into the behavior of interstellar comets and their significance in the broader context of our solar system's formation.

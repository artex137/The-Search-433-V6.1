Comet 3I/ATLAS Flaunts Bright Halo and Tail in New Image Taken by Jupiter-Bound Spacecraft

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been captured in a new image taken by the European Space Agency's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) spacecraft, revealing its bright halo and tail following its closest approach to the sun on October 29-30, 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the comet.

The image, taken on November 6, 2025, is part of ongoing research into the comet's behavior and composition. Scientists have been studying data from multiple spacecraft that observed 3I/ATLAS as it traveled through the solar system. The findings could enhance understanding of the comet's unique characteristics compared to typical comets.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS passed closest to the sun on October 29-30, 2025.
  • The latest image was captured by Juice on November 6, 2025.
  • Juice was approximately 66 million km (41 million miles) from 3I/ATLAS at the time of the image.
  • The comet made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, at about 270 million km (168 million miles) away.
  • Juice took over 120 images of 3I/ATLAS, using its multicolor optical camera, JANUS.
  • Data transmission back to Earth utilized a smaller medium-gain antenna due to the main antenna's function as a heat shield.

Timeline

In late 2025, Comet 3I/ATLAS captured the attention of scientists and the public. It passed the sun and Earth, prompting extensive observations. The comet made its closest approach to the sun on October 29-30, followed by its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025.

During this period, several spacecraft, including Juice, monitored the comet's trajectory and behavior. Juice transmitted its latest images and data back to Earth in early November, allowing scientists to begin analyzing the comet's characteristics.

Official statements

“The images are now being examined by scientists to understand 3I/ATLAS and its features,” an ESA spokesperson said.
“So far, the comet’s behavior seems like that of a normal comet,” the spokesperson added. “However, the composition of 3I/ATLAS is a different story.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has generated significant interest due to its interstellar origin. Scientists have noted its unusual composition, including an abundance of nickel but no iron. Previous studies published in the Astrophysical Journal Letters have highlighted the high carbon dioxide-to-water ratio in its coma, which may provide insights into its formation.

What’s next

In late March, ESA research teams will meet to review their findings from the data collected by Juice and other spacecraft, potentially revealing more about the unique characteristics of Comet 3I/ATLAS.

'It wasn’t a bird': Did a UFO just ram into a $70 million F-16? Here's what the Pentagon knows

A $70 million F-16 fighter jet has reportedly collided with an unidentified flying object (UFO) during a training exercise over the Atlantic Ocean, according to the Pentagon. The incident occurred on an unspecified date, with no injuries or fatalities reported.

This incident raises questions about aviation safety and the potential for unidentified aerial phenomena. The Pentagon has confirmed it is investigating the event, which has attracted significant media attention and public interest in UFOs.

Key facts

  • Incident involved a $70 million F-16 fighter jet.
  • Location: Atlantic Ocean.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.
  • Investigation is underway by the Pentagon.
  • Date of the incident: Not disclosed.

Timeline

The collision occurred during a routine training exercise involving the F-16. Details surrounding the exact time and circumstances of the incident remain unclear, as the Pentagon has not disclosed specific information.

The Pentagon has initiated an investigation to determine the nature of the unidentified object and the circumstances of the collision.

Official statements

“It wasn’t a bird,” a Pentagon spokesperson stated, emphasizing the need for further investigation into the incident.

Context

This incident is part of a broader trend of increased scrutiny regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. The U.S. military has reported several encounters in recent years, raising concerns about national security and air safety.

What’s next

The Pentagon will continue its investigation into the incident and will provide updates as more information becomes available. Further details regarding the nature of the unidentified object are anticipated in the coming weeks.

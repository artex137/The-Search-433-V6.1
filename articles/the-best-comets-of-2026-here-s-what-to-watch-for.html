The best comets of 2026: Here’s what to watch for

Several comets are approaching the inner solar system in 2026, including Comet 2025 R3 (PanSTARRS) and Comet Wierzchos (C/2024 E1), which will have significant visibility during the year. Notably, Comet Wierzchos will reach its closest approach to the sun on January 20, 2026, before passing closest to Earth on February 17, 2026, at a distance of 94 million miles (151.5 million km). The brightness of these comets presents a unique opportunity for both professional and amateur astronomers.

Authorities and astronomers are monitoring these comets as they approach Earth. Observers are encouraged to prepare for viewing opportunities, particularly in the Southern Hemisphere, where conditions are expected to improve throughout February and April.

Key facts

  • Comet Wierzchos (C/2024 E1) reaches perihelion on January 20, 2026.
  • Closest approach to Earth on February 17, 2026, at 94 million miles (151.5 million km).
  • Current brightness of Comet Wierzchos is between magnitude 8 and 8.7.
  • Comet 2025 R3 (PanSTARRS) closest to the sun on April 19, 2026.
  • Closest approach to Earth on April 26, 2026, at 45.5 million miles (73.2 million km).
  • Comet Tempel 2 reaches perihelion on August 2, 2026.
  • Closest approach to Earth on August 3, 2026, at 38.5 million miles (61.9 million km).

Timeline

On March 3, 2024, Polish astronomer Kacper Wierzchos discovered Comet Wierzchos. This comet has been moving closer to the inner solar system since its discovery. It will reach perihelion on January 20, 2026, and will be closest to Earth on February 17, 2026.

Comet 2025 R3 (PanSTARRS) will reach perihelion on April 19, 2026, and pass closest to Earth on April 26, 2026. Comet Tempel 2, discovered on July 4, 1873, will reach perihelion on August 2, 2026, and will be closest to Earth on August 3, 2026.

Official statements

“Observers in the Southern Hemisphere will have the best view of the closest approach to Earth on February 17,” said an astronomy expert.

Context

The visibility of comets has been a topic of interest for centuries. Comet Tempel 2 is a short-period comet that orbits the sun every five years, making it a regular visitor in the night sky. Comets like PanSTARRS and Wierzchos provide opportunities for both amateur and professional astronomers to engage with celestial events.

What’s next

As the year progresses, astronomers anticipate the potential discovery of new comets. Observers should keep an eye out for updates and prepare for viewing opportunities as these comets approach Earth.

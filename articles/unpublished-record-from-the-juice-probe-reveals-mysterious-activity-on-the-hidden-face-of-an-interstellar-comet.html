Unpublished record from the Juice probe reveals mysterious activity on the hidden face of an interstellar comet

The Juice probe has detected unusual activity on the hidden face of comet 3I ATLAS, according to a report released on March 2, 2026. The findings have raised questions about the comet's composition and behavior, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported as a result of these observations.

This discovery is significant for researchers studying interstellar objects and their properties. The European Space Agency (ESA), which operates the Juice probe, plans to conduct further analysis to understand the implications of these findings. The data may offer new insights into the formation of comets and the early solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS was first observed in 2018.
  • The Juice probe is part of a mission conducted by the European Space Agency.
  • The recent observations were made on March 1, 2026.
  • No injuries or fatalities have been reported.
  • The exact nature of the activity observed on the comet remains undisclosed.

Timeline

March 1, 2026: The Juice probe records unusual activity on the hidden face of comet 3I ATLAS.

March 2, 2026: The findings are reported, prompting further investigation by the European Space Agency.

Official statements

“The data we have gathered from the Juice probe presents a unique opportunity to deepen our understanding of interstellar comets,” said a spokesperson from the European Space Agency.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is notable for being the first interstellar comet confirmed to be on a hyperbolic trajectory. Previous studies have helped scientists understand the characteristics of other comets, but this new data could change existing theories about their origins and behavior.

What’s next

The European Space Agency plans to analyze the data further and may publish additional findings in the coming months. The agency has not disclosed a timeline for potential follow-up missions or studies related to comet 3I ATLAS.

Comet 3I/ATLAS puzzles scientists, revealing secrets of alien worlds

A visitor from another star system, comet 3I/ATLAS, has intrigued the astronomy community since its discovery by the Asteroid Terrestrial-Impact Last Alert System in July 2025. The comet contains chemical clues that may help scientists study the formation of other planets and the existence of extraterrestrial life. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the comet.

The discovery of comet 3I/ATLAS is significant because it offers insights into the chemical makeup of celestial bodies outside our solar system. Researchers are utilizing the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile to analyze the comet’s emissions. This research could enhance understanding of icy bodies in space and their role in planetary formation.

Key facts

  • Discovered in July 2025 by the Asteroid Terrestrial-Impact Last Alert System.
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third known interstellar object after ‘Oumuamua (2017) and 2I/Borisov (2019).
  • ALMA detected high levels of methanol (CH3OH) in the comet's expanding coma.
  • Comet exhibits glowing X-rays as solar wind particles interact with its materials.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first observed in July 2025. Following its discovery, scientists employed the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) to analyze its composition. Researchers noted the comet's high methanol emissions, which differ significantly from those found in comets within our solar system.

As investigations continue, researchers have highlighted the comet's unique characteristics, including its emission of glowing X-rays and the presence of hydrogen cyanide primarily streaming from its nucleus. This behavior has provided valuable insights into its formation and chemical inventory.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system. The details reveal what it’s made of, and it’s bursting with methanol in a way we just don’t usually see in comets in our own solar system,” said Nathan Roth, Lead Researcher at the National Radio Astronomy Observatory.

Context

The study of interstellar comets such as 3I/ATLAS is rare and significant for understanding the origins of planetary systems. Previous interstellar objects, ‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have also provided valuable data, but 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity to investigate the building blocks of other planets that formed billions of years ago.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue analyzing comet 3I/ATLAS to uncover more details about its composition and the implications for our understanding of the universe. Further observations are expected as the comet travels through our solar system.

Trump Orders Pentagon to Declassify UFO and Alien Files

Former U.S. President Donald Trump has ordered the Pentagon to declassify files related to UFOs and alien encounters, citing "tremendous interest" from the public. The announcement came on October 21, 2023, during a press briefing at the White House.

This move is significant as it represents a shift in the government's approach to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). Officials have indicated that the release of these files could provide transparency and address public curiosity regarding UFO sightings and potential extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: October 21, 2023
  • Location: White House, Washington, D.C.
  • Agency involved: Pentagon
  • Reason for declassification: Public interest

Timeline

On October 21, 2023, Trump announced the decision to declassify UFO files during a press briefing. The Pentagon has since begun the process of reviewing and releasing relevant documents. This initiative aligns with ongoing discussions about transparency regarding UAP.

Official statements

“There has been tremendous interest in these files, and we owe it to the American people to make this information available,” Trump said during the briefing.

Context

This decision follows a series of reports and testimonies from military personnel regarding UFO sightings. In recent years, the U.S. government has increased its focus on UAP, with several investigations launched to understand the phenomena better.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to release the declassified files within the coming months. The public will have the opportunity to review the information, which could lead to further inquiries and discussions about UFOs and alien encounters.

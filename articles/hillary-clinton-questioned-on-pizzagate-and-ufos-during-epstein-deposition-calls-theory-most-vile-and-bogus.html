Hillary Clinton Questioned on Pizzagate and UFOs During Epstein Deposition

Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton has faced questions about UFOs and the debunked conspiracy theory known as Pizzagate during a seven-hour deposition before the House Oversight Committee on Thursday. The deposition took place at the Chappaqua Performing Arts Centre in New York.

This unusual line of questioning has raised concerns about the focus of the House Oversight Committee's inquiry, particularly given the violent implications of the Pizzagate theory. Clinton described the theory as "one of the most vile, bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet," according to MS Now. The committee's focus on such topics has led to questions regarding the intent behind Clinton's testimony.

Key facts

  • Clinton testified before the House Oversight Committee on Thursday.
  • The deposition lasted approximately seven hours.
  • Clinton has denied knowing Jeffrey Epstein, stating, "I never went to his island."
  • The Pizzagate conspiracy theory emerged during the 2016 presidential election.
  • In December 2016, Edgar Maddison Welch armed with an AR-15, fired into a pizza restaurant linked to the theory.
  • Welch was sentenced to four years in prison after pleading guilty.

Timeline

During her testimony, Clinton faced repeated inquiries about her alleged connections to Epstein. She maintained her stance, stating multiple times, "I do not know Jeffrey Epstein." The questioning shifted to Pizzagate and UFOs towards the end of the session, which Clinton characterized as unusual.

In December 2016, Welch acted on the Pizzagate conspiracy by firing a weapon in a Washington, D.C. pizza restaurant. This incident resulted in no injuries but highlighted the dangerous consequences of spreading false information.

The deposition was briefly interrupted when Representative Lauren Boebert leaked a photo of Clinton during the session. This prompted condemnation from Representative Robert Garcia, who called for accountability regarding the breach of rules.

Official statements

“I never went to his island. I never went to his homes. I never went to his offices.”

- Hillary Clinton

“Some of the comments Clinton made at the end about Epstein will be interesting.”

- James Comer, House Oversight Chairman

“We’re demanding immediately that we ask President Trump to testify in front of our committee.”

- Robert Garcia, top Democrat on the House Oversight Committee

Context

The Pizzagate conspiracy theory falsely claimed that high-ranking Democratic officials were involved in a child sex trafficking ring. The theory gained traction during the 2016 election and led to real-world violence when a gunman acted on its claims. House Republicans' decision to question Clinton on this topic raises questions about the seriousness of the inquiry.

Clinton's testimony is part of a broader investigation into Epstein's connections with influential figures across political lines, including former President Bill Clinton and Donald Trump. Both have faced scrutiny regarding their past associations with Epstein.

What’s next

Former President Bill Clinton is scheduled to testify on Friday, marking a rare instance of a former president appearing before Congress under subpoena. Investigations are ongoing, but neither Clinton has been accused of wrongdoing related to Epstein.

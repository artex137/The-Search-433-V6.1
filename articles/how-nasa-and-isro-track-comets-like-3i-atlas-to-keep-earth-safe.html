How NASA and ISRO track comets like 3I/ATLAS to keep Earth safe

NASA and ISRO are closely monitoring comet 3I/ATLAS, a rare interstellar object discovered in July 2025. The comet is not expected to approach Earth, but scientists are studying it to enhance methods for tracking future celestial objects.

This tracking is significant as it improves the ability to observe and predict the paths of unknown objects that may pose risks to Earth. NASA’s ATLAS and ISRO’s NISAR are key tools in this effort, utilizing advanced technology to gather critical data on comets and other celestial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: July 2025
  • Agencies Involved: NASA, ISRO
  • Tracking Systems: ATLAS and NISAR
  • NISAR Observation Frequency: Every 12 days

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025. Since then, NASA’s ATLAS has been scanning the skies from locations in Chile and Hawaii to detect moving objects, including this comet. NISAR was launched in July 2025, equipped with two radar systems for monitoring Earth's surface and detecting changes.

ATLAS uses wide-field telescopes to track the comet's orbit, while NISAR’s technology provides high-resolution images of Earth’s surface, aiding in environmental monitoring.

Official statements

“Tracking 3I/ATLAS helps us improve our methods for detecting and monitoring future celestial objects,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing focus on monitoring interstellar objects. The data collected from such comets can inform scientists about potential risks to Earth and enhance global safety protocols. The collaboration between NASA and ISRO illustrates the international commitment to planetary defense.

What’s next

NASA and ISRO will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS and gather data to refine their tracking methods, preparing for future encounters with unknown celestial objects.

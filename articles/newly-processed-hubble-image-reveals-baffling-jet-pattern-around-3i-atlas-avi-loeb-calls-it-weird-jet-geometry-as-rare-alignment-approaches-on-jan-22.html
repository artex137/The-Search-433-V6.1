Newly Processed Hubble Image Reveals Baffling Jet Pattern Around 3I/ATLAS – Avi Loeb Calls It ‘Weird Jet Geometry’ As Rare Alignment Approaches On Jan. 22

Newly analyzed images from the Hubble Space Telescope have revealed unusual jet patterns around the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, with observations captured on January 14, 2026. The findings indicate the object is displaying a jet configuration that does not conform to known cometary behavior.

This discovery is significant as scientists prepare for a rare Earth-Sun alignment on January 22, which could provide further insights into the behavior of 3I/ATLAS. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb and his collaborators have stated that the object’s jets challenge existing models of solar radiation and outgassing.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation Date: January 14, 2026
  • Alignment Date: January 22, 2026
  • Distance of dust environment: over 130,000 kilometers
  • Rotation cycle of jet structure: approximately seven hours

Timeline

On January 14, 2026, astronomers captured new images of 3I/ATLAS using the Hubble Space Telescope. These observations revealed a unique jet pattern, including an "anti-tail" directed towards the Sun and three smaller jets arranged symmetrically around the object.

Earlier Hubble data from December 2025 indicated that the jet system wobbles, suggesting a rotating body. The findings challenge previous assumptions about comet behavior as the jets do not point away from the Sun as expected.

As January 22 approaches, scientists are preparing for a significant alignment that could clarify the nature of the material forming the anti-tail and its resistance to solar forces.

Official statements

Avi Loeb noted, “Whatever is driving the object’s jet system appears to be both persistent and structured, traits that demand careful examination rather than dismissal as noise or imaging artifacts.”

Context

The behavior of 3I/ATLAS is unprecedented among known cometary objects. Previous research has primarily focused on solar system bodies, making this interstellar visitor a unique case that challenges established theories. The observations suggest that materials from outside the solar system can interact differently with solar radiation and heat.

What’s next

As researchers await the January 22 alignment, they aim to gather critical measurements that could reveal the composition of the anti-tail and enhance understanding of interstellar objects. Observations during this period may provide valuable data to test competing explanations for 3I/ATLAS's unusual behavior.

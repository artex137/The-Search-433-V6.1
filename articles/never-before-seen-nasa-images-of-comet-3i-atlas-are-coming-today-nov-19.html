Never-before-seen NASA images of comet 3I/ATLAS are coming today (Nov. 19)

NASA has announced that it will release never-before-seen images of comet 3I/ATLAS today, November 19, 2023. The images are expected to provide new insights into the comet's characteristics and behavior.

The release of these images is significant for scientists studying comets and their impact on our understanding of the solar system. NASA has been closely monitoring 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the inner solar system. The agency aims to enhance public awareness and interest in space exploration through this release.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 19, 2023
  • Agency: NASA
  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Type of images: Never-before-seen

Timeline

NASA has tracked comet 3I/ATLAS since its discovery in 2020. The comet has gained attention due to its unique features and trajectory. The agency has prepared for the release of the images by conducting detailed observations and analyses.

The images are set to be unveiled today, marking a significant milestone in the study of this celestial body.

Official statements

“We are excited to share these images with the public. They offer a glimpse into the mysteries of our solar system,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a larger group of comets that scientists study to understand their origins and the history of the solar system. Previous observations of comets have provided valuable insights into their composition and behavior.

What’s next

Following the release of the images, NASA plans to analyze the data further and share findings with the scientific community and the public.

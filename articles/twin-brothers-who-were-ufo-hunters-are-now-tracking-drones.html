Twin brothers who were UFO hunters are now tracking drones

Last December, every tier of U.S. law enforcement responded to a report of drones violating restricted airspace at the U.S. Army Natick Soldier Systems Center, located outside Boston. Approximately 15 to 20 drones were spotted, but no culprits were identified.

This incident highlights ongoing concerns about drone activity in sensitive military areas. Authorities are investigating the incidents and working to implement measures to prevent future occurrences.

Key facts

  • Incident date: December 2022
  • Location: U.S. Army Natick Soldier Systems Center, Massachusetts
  • Number of drones reported: 15 to 20
  • Agencies involved: Federal, state, and local law enforcement
  • Status of investigation: Ongoing

Timeline

On the evening of December 2022, law enforcement was alerted to a swarm of drones in restricted airspace over the Natick Soldier Systems Center. The military research installation is known for its sensitive operations, prompting a quick response from multiple agencies.

Despite a thorough investigation, the drones' operators were not located. The lack of identification raises concerns regarding airspace security and the implications of unauthorized drone activity.

Official statements

“These unidentified aircraft pose a significant risk to military operations,” said a retired major with the Massachusetts State Police involved in the investigation.

Context

This incident is part of a broader trend of increasing drone sightings near military installations. Authorities have noted a rise in unauthorized drone flights, prompting discussions on enhancing regulations and enforcement measures to safeguard sensitive areas.

What’s next

Investigations into the December incident remain ongoing, with law enforcement agencies reviewing security protocols to better address unauthorized drone activity in the future.

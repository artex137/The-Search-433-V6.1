3I/ATLAS photo JUICE took in November 2025 just reached Earth. What caused the 4-month delay?

The European Space Agency (ESA) has received a photo of the comet 3I/ATLAS, captured by the JUICE probe in November 2025, after a four-month delay. The image was taken on November 6, 2025, but ESA only received it on February 26, 2026, due to technical issues related to the Sun's position.

This delay is significant as it highlights the challenges of deep space communication and the complexities involved in studying interstellar objects. ESA is currently analyzing the data gathered from the comet, with official findings expected to be released later this month.

Key facts

  • Photo taken by JUICE on November 6, 2025.
  • ESA received the image on February 26, 2026.
  • JUICE was 66 million km from 3I/ATLAS during the photo capture.
  • 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025.
  • 3I/ATLAS is estimated to be 7-13 billion years old.
  • It is the third interstellar object observed in our solar system.
  • Final findings from ESA are expected by the end of March 2026.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025. The comet made its closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025. Seven days later, on November 6, JUICE captured images of the comet from a distance of 66 million km. The spacecraft was unable to transmit the data immediately due to its position behind the Sun, using only a smaller antenna. ESA received the image on February 26, 2026.

Official statements

“The four-month wait was due to JUICE being on the other side of the Sun. It could only use its smaller, medium-gain antenna to relay the data,” ESA officials stated.

Context

3I/ATLAS is significant as it is only the third known interstellar object to enter our solar system, following previous discoveries. The study of such objects can provide insights into the formation of the solar system and the nature of interstellar materials.

What’s next

ESA teams are currently analyzing the data collected from 3I/ATLAS, including studies on its composition and particle data. The agency plans to announce its final findings by the end of March 2026.

Avi Loeb: 3I/ATLAS Is 'Like A Detective Story,' Verdict 'Still Out'

Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has raised questions about the nature of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which he believes may be more than just a simple comet. Discovered in 2019, 3I/ATLAS is currently traversing our solar system and is expected to reach its closest point to Earth, about 170 million kilometres away, in mid-December.

This matter is significant as it touches on the ongoing debate in the scientific community regarding the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS. Loeb has pointed out various anomalies associated with the object, which he argues warrant further investigation. He has challenged his colleagues who dismiss his theories without consideration.

Key facts

  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Year: 2019
  • Closest Approach to Earth: Mid-December 2023
  • Distance at Closest Approach: 170 million kilometres
  • NASA is preparing to release high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS taken from Mars on October 2, 2023

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019. Since then, it has been observed by various astronomical agencies, including NASA. Loeb has been vocal about the object's unusual characteristics, sparking discussions and debates within the scientific community. On October 2, 2023, NASA captured high-resolution images of the object from Mars, which are set to be released soon.

“The verdict is still out, and I'm very much looking forward to getting more data in the coming weeks,” Loeb said on Saturday.

Loeb describes the investigation of 3I/ATLAS as “like a detective story” and criticizes colleagues who hastily classify it as a known type of comet.

Context

The discussion surrounding 3I/ATLAS reflects a broader tension in the scientific community between established knowledge and the allure of the unknown. While many experts believe the object is simply a distant comet, Loeb's theories have brought renewed interest and debate regarding interstellar objects and their potential significance.

What’s next

As scientists await the release of NASA's high-resolution images, the scientific community will continue to evaluate the data and theories surrounding 3I/ATLAS. Loeb has expressed hope that this new information will clarify the object's nature and possibly confirm his hypotheses.

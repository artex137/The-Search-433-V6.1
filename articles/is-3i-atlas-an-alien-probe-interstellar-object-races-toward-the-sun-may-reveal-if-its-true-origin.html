Is 3I/ATLAS an alien probe? Interstellar object races toward the Sun, may reveal if its true origin

An interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS is speeding through the solar system and is set to make its closest pass to the Sun on October 29, 2025. Scientists hope to determine whether it is a natural comet or something more extraordinary. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

This encounter is significant because it presents astronomers with a unique opportunity to study 3I/ATLAS and understand its origins. Harvard professor Avi Loeb has suggested that if the object is a comet, it may disintegrate as it approaches the Sun, potentially revealing more about its composition and whether it has an artificial origin.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest pass to the Sun: October 29, 2025
  • Estimated distance of ESA’s JUICE mission from 3I/ATLAS: 125 million miles
  • NASA's stance: Claims 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet
  • Recent imaging shows a cylindrical-shaped body passing Mars on October 3
  • Potential for disintegration observed in November and December 2025

Timeline

On October 3, 2025, imaging revealed a cylindrical-shaped body passing Mars, sparking interest in 3I/ATLAS. The object is expected to make its closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025. Observations by the European Space Agency's JUICE mission and Earth-based observatories will continue into November and December.

Official statements

“If 3I/ATLAS is a comet, it should disintegrate into fragments as it approaches the Sun,” said Loeb in an interview with the Daily Mail.
“There are unrelated contemporary comets, background stars, and images of the moons of Mars, Phobos and Deimos, which appear in some images. But so far, no verified object branched off 3I/ATLAS,” Loeb clarified.

Context

3I/ATLAS follows the interstellar visitors Oumuamua in 2017 and Borisov in 2019. These prior objects have generated significant interest in the scientific community regarding the nature of interstellar bodies and their potential origins.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS closely as it approaches the Sun. Observations will help determine whether the object disintegrates like a natural comet or behaves in a way that suggests an artificial origin.

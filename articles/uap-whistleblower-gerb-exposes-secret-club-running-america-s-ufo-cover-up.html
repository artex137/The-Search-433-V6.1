UAP Whistleblower 'Gerb' Exposes Secret Club Running America's UFO Cover-up

A high-profile UAP whistleblower known as Gerb has accused a powerful 'secret club' of managing America's UFO cover-up from behind closed doors, linking the Pentagon's anomaly office to Oak Ridge National Laboratory. Gerb claims a metallic object recovered in Ohio and handed to Oak Ridge in 2024 was not treated as neutral scientific evidence, but was instead integrated into a controlled system designed to obscure uncomfortable truths about unidentified aerial phenomena.

Gerb's allegations raise serious questions about government transparency and the potential concealment of non-human technology. Authorities are now facing scrutiny over the relationship between the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) and Oak Ridge, particularly regarding the analysis of the Ohio specimen.

Key facts

  • Whistleblower: Gerb
  • Year of incident: 2024
  • Location: Ohio
  • Key organizations: AARO, Oak Ridge National Laboratory
  • Former AARO chief: Sean Kirkpatrick, left role in December 2023
  • Conflict of interest: Alleged link between Kirkpatrick and Oak Ridge
  • Public response: UAP advocates support Gerb, accusing AARO of disinformation
  • Status of analysis: Remains classified

Timeline

In 2024, the AARO contracted Oak Ridge National Laboratory to examine a metallic specimen recovered from private land in Ohio. The material reportedly exhibited unusual properties not aligned with known earthly alloys. AARO later issued a brief statement indicating the sample was likely ordinary.

Gerb has criticized this conclusion as politically motivated and rushed, arguing that Oak Ridge was chosen for its ties to defense contractors rather than its independence. In December 2023, Sean Kirkpatrick, the former AARO chief, transitioned to a senior role at Oak Ridge, which Gerb claims creates a conflict of interest.

The situation intensified when Oak Ridge removed a webpage celebrating Kirkpatrick's appointment, and he did not include this position on his public CV. Gerb views these actions as evidence of hidden alliances and image management.

Official statements

“The same corporate ecosystem effectively oversees both the investigation of UAP evidence and the Pentagon office meant to scrutinise it,” Gerb stated.
UAP advocates have rallied behind Gerb, accusing AARO of acting more like a disinformation machine than a truth-seeking body.

Context

Gerb's allegations highlight a broader trend of skepticism regarding the transparency of UAP investigations. The connection between government entities and private contractors raises concerns about accountability and the integrity of scientific inquiry.

Previous incidents have also drawn attention to potential conflicts of interest in UAP research, further complicating public trust in government assessments of unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Investigations into Gerb's claims are ongoing, and the status of the Ohio specimen remains classified. AARO faces increasing pressure to provide transparency regarding its findings and the relationships between its officials and private entities.

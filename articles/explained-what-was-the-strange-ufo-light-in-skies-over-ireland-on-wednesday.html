EXPLAINED: What was the strange 'UFO' light in skies over Ireland on Wednesday?

Published 2025-10-30 10:29 UTC

Strange Light in Irish Skies Identified as Falcon 9 Rocket

On Wednesday evening, a bright light moving across the sky baffled residents in various parts of Ireland, leading many to speculate about a potential UFO sighting. The unusual object was first reported around 6:30 PM over Kildare, with similar sightings reported in Carlow and Co. Mayo. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident has prompted a wave of social media activity as witnesses sought to identify the source of the mysterious light. Experts have since clarified that the object was not extraterrestrial, but rather a Falcon 9 rocket operated by SpaceX, which had recently ejected leftover fuel during its mission.

Key facts

  • Time of initial sighting: 6:30 PM on October 29, 2025.
  • Locations reported: Kildare, Carlow, Co. Mayo.
  • Witness descriptions: Bright light, triangular shape, varying speed.
  • Expert confirmation: Object was SpaceX's Falcon 9 rocket.
  • Incident reported by multiple sources including Carlow Weather.

Timeline

At approximately 6:30 PM, residents in Kildare began reporting a bright light in the sky. Witnesses described the object as hovering and then vanishing. Similar reports followed from Carlow and Co. Mayo, with descriptions of the object ranging from a bright dot to a triangular shape with lights.

Shortly after, experts began to analyze the phenomenon. Alan O'Reilly from Carlow Weather stated that the light was likely due to the Falcon 9 rocket dumping leftover fuel, which creates a visible light effect when frozen at high altitude.

Official statements

“The light in the sky tonight most likely to be the Falcon 9 dumping fuel in orbit based on latest information. Not aliens this time.”
“Spotted from the cockpit on approach to Cork on a training flight. Almost triangular in shape with the bottom of the triangle quite bright, almost like lights.”

Context

Rocket launches from SpaceX have become increasingly common, with Falcon 9 missions frequently deploying satellites into orbit. The phenomenon of frozen fuel creating visible light has been noted in previous launches, helping to explain similar sightings in the past.

What’s next

No further investigations are currently planned, as authorities and experts have confirmed the source of the light. The public has been reassured that it was a natural occurrence related to space activities, not an unidentified flying object.

Tracker shows path of rare space object people think is 'evidence of alien technology'

A rare space object known as Comet 3I/ATLAS is currently hurtling towards Earth at approximately 209,000 km/h (130,000 mph). The comet is about 370.5 million km (230.2 million miles) away from Earth and is expected to reach its closest point to Earth's orbit in early November, according to NASA.

This event has drawn significant attention due to speculation about the comet's origins and intentions. Some experts, including Harvard scientist Avi Loeb, have suggested that the object may have extraterrestrial origins and could signify an impending threat to humanity. NASA has developed a tracker that allows the public to follow the comet's journey through the solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is currently 370.5 million km from Earth.
  • It is traveling at a speed of 209,000 km/h.
  • The comet is expected to reach its closest approach to Earth's orbit in early November.
  • It recently passed Mars and will pass by Jupiter in late March 2024.
  • By May 2028, it will have escaped the orbital path of Neptune.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS has captured the attention of scientists and the public since its discovery. It is believed to have originated outside of our solar system and is currently on a trajectory that takes it behind the Sun, preventing detailed observations from Earth-based telescopes.

As it moves towards Earth, its trajectory suggests it will pass close to our planet, although Earth will be on the opposite side of its rotation during its closest approach. The comet will continue its journey out of the solar system, passing near Jupiter in March 2024 and leaving the orbit of Neptune by May 2028.

Official statements

“This could be intentional to avoid detailed observations from Earth-based telescopes when the object is brightest or when gadgets are sent to Earth from that hidden vantage point,” said Avi Loeb, suggesting a potential alien origin for the comet.

Context

The unusual speed, shape, and trajectory of Comet 3I/ATLAS have led to various theories about its nature. While many scientists have downplayed the notion of it being artificial, the speculation continues to fuel discussions about potential extraterrestrial life and technology.

Previous events involving unusual space objects have also sparked similar debates, but none have generated as much intrigue as Comet 3I/ATLAS due to its unique path and the claims surrounding it.

What’s next

As Comet 3I/ATLAS continues its journey, NASA and other space agencies will monitor its trajectory. The public can track the comet's path through NASA's live tracker, which may provide reassurance amid the speculation surrounding its origins.

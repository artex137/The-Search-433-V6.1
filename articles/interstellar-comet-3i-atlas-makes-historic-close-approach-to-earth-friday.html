Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Makes Historic Close Approach to Earth Friday

Comet 3I/ATLAS has made a historic close approach to Earth on Friday, November 30, 2025. The comet was first detected on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) survey in Chile. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This close encounter is significant as it marks only the third confirmed interstellar object ever observed, following 1I/‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019, according to NASA. Astronomers and researchers have utilized both space-based and ground-based observatories to track the comet's trajectory and analyze its composition.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was detected on July 1, 2025.
  • It is approximately 178 million miles (286 million kilometers) from Earth during its closest approach.
  • NASA's Hubble Space Telescope captured images of the comet on November 30, 2025.
  • NASA's Europa Clipper spacecraft provided additional observations of the comet.
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS will pass within about 33 million miles (53 million kilometers) of Jupiter in March 2026.
  • It is expected to exit our solar system entirely after this trajectory.

Timeline

July 1, 2025: Comet 3I/ATLAS is first detected by the ATLAS survey in Chile.

October 30, 2025: The comet makes its closest approach to the Sun, resulting in increased outgassing.

November 30, 2025: NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope captures images of the comet during its close approach to Earth.

Official statements

“These measurements help pinpoint the comet’s composition and provide valuable clues about the conditions of its formation,” scientists stated regarding the observations made by the Europa Clipper spacecraft.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable for being the third interstellar object identified, highlighting ongoing advancements in the detection and study of near-Earth objects. The previous interstellar objects, 1I/‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have also spurred interest in understanding the origins and compositions of such celestial bodies.

What’s next

Following its flyby, comet 3I/ATLAS will continue its journey through the solar system, with projections indicating it will pass near Jupiter in March 2026 before moving back into interstellar space.

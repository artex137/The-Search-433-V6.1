SpaceX Starship, not a UFO, seen in Lake Worth, Florida, sky after its Texas liftoff

A bright, fast-moving light observed in the sky over Lake Worth, Florida, on October 14, 2025, has been confirmed as the SpaceX Starship rocket following its launch from Texas. The sighting prompted speculation among residents, but authorities have clarified that it was not a UFO. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident highlights the increasing visibility of rocket launches and their potential to confuse the public. Local authorities, including the Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office, have received calls regarding the sighting, leading them to issue statements to clarify the nature of the object in the sky.

Key facts

  • Date: October 14, 2025
  • Location: Lake Worth, Florida
  • Object: SpaceX Starship rocket
  • Origin: Launched from Texas
  • No reported injuries or fatalities

Timeline

On October 14, 2025, residents in Lake Worth and Boynton Beach observed a bright light in the sky. Videos and reports surfaced as viewers expressed confusion over the object's identity.

Authorities quickly investigated the reports and confirmed that the light was the SpaceX Starship rocket, which had just launched from Texas.

Official statements

“Sure doesn’t look like a plane,” said Melissa Singer of West Boynton Beach, who captured video of the event. “I didn’t know what I was seeing.”

Context

This sighting is part of a broader trend of increased rocket launches, which have become more frequent as private companies like SpaceX conduct missions. Such events often lead to public curiosity and concern regarding unidentified flying objects.

What’s next

Authorities continue to monitor public reactions to rocket launches and will provide information to prevent future confusion. No further investigations or actions are currently planned regarding this incident.

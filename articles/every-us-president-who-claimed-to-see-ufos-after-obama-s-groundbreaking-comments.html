Every US President who claimed to see UFOs after Obama's groundbreaking comments

Numerous individuals, including former US Presidents, have reported encounters with unidentified flying objects (UFOs) over the years. Following recent remarks by former President Barack Obama, political news outlet Politico has examined which US Presidents claim to have witnessed UFOs. Obama has stated that he believes in the existence of alien life, mentioning that “they’re real, but I haven’t seen them.”

This ongoing discussion highlights the interest in UFO phenomena and the implications for national security and public curiosity. Authorities continue to investigate UFO sightings, with a focus on gathering credible information and addressing public concern regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Former President Barack Obama has publicly stated his belief in alien life.
  • Obama discussed UFOs during an interview on the No Lie podcast with Bryan Cohen.
  • Ronald Reagan and his flight crew reportedly witnessed a UFO during a flight.
  • Harry S. Truman summoned military specialists in 1948 to discuss UFO sightings.
  • Bill Clinton expressed curiosity about UFOs but was not briefed on alien life.
  • Jimmy Carter claims to have seen a UFO in Georgia in 1969.

Timeline

In 1948, President Harry S. Truman called military experts to the White House to discuss the increase in UFO sightings. This marked a significant moment of government interest in the phenomenon.

In 1969, Jimmy Carter reported seeing a UFO while in Georgia, providing a detailed account of the sighting to the public.

During the 1990s, President Bill Clinton sought information about UFOs but reported he was never briefed on any alien encounters.

Most recently, in 2023, former President Barack Obama confirmed his belief in alien life during an interview, reigniting discussions about UFOs among the public and officials.

Official statements

“They’re real, but I haven’t seen them,” said Barack Obama during the No Lie podcast.
“There’s no underground facility, unless there’s this enormous conspiracy and they hid it from the president of the United States,” Obama added while discussing Area 51.
“The UFO went from a normal cruise speed to a fantastic speed instantly,” recalled Reagan's pilot Bill Paynter about the sighting during Reagan's presidency.
“There were about twenty of us standing outside... a kind of green light appeared in the western sky,” said Jimmy Carter, describing his UFO sighting.

Context

The interest in UFOs among US Presidents has varied over the decades, with notable incidents prompting official investigations and public speculation. The increase in reported sightings during Truman's presidency set a precedent for future inquiries. Each subsequent President has approached the topic with varying degrees of curiosity and skepticism.

These discussions have often intersected with broader issues of national security, as unidentified aerial phenomena raise questions about airspace safety and potential military implications.

What’s next

Investigations into UFO sightings continue as authorities seek to gather more information and assess the implications for public safety and national security. The ongoing dialogue among former and current officials suggests a commitment to transparency regarding aerial phenomena.

Today in History: January 9, 1969 - Scientists Assert There is No 'UFO' Evidence

On January 9, 1969, scientists from the University of Colorado, led by Dr. Edward U. Condon, announced the conclusion of their two-year study on Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs), stating there is no evidence to support claims of extraterrestrial visitors. The findings were published in a 1,485-page report, which the researchers presented formally to the public.

This report is significant as it marks the culmination of 21 years of UFO investigations and suggests that further studies may not be warranted. The study was commissioned by the U.S. Air Force, which has historically been burdened by UFO-related inquiries. The report aims to provide clarity on the longstanding debate surrounding UFOs and their implications for national security.

Key facts

  • Study duration: Two years
  • Cost: $500,000
  • Report length: 1,485 pages
  • Conducted by: University of Colorado scientists
  • Lead researcher: Dr. Edward U. Condon
  • Percentage of UFO reports attributed to ordinary objects: 90%
  • Recommendation: End Project Blue Book

Timeline

The study began in 1967 as a response to growing public interest and concern over UFO sightings. Over the course of two years, researchers examined numerous reports and data related to UFO encounters. On January 9, 1969, the findings were publicly released, asserting that the investigation had yielded no substantial evidence of UFOs as extraterrestrial vessels.

The report concluded that the majority of UFO sightings could be explained by common objects, such as planes and balloons. It also dismissed various conspiracy theories regarding government cover-ups of UFO information.

Official statements

“Our general conclusion is that nothing has come from the study of UFOs in the past 21 years that has added to scientific knowledge,” the report stated.
“Suggestions by some people that the government possesses extraterrestrial spacecraft and has their crews in secret captivity are fantastic nonsense,” the researchers declared.

Context

The U.S. Air Force initiated Project Blue Book in 1947 to investigate UFO sightings and assess any potential threats to national security. The project has faced criticism and skepticism from both the public and scientific communities over the years. The findings in the 1969 report align with the Air Force's long-held position that no credible evidence exists regarding UFOs as security threats.

Prior to this report, UFOs had been a topic of significant debate, often sensationalized in media and popular culture. The conclusion from the University of Colorado scientists aimed to provide a scientific basis for the ongoing discussion.

What’s next

Following this report, the U.S. Air Force has not indicated any immediate changes to its approach regarding UFO investigations. The Pentagon will ultimately decide whether to continue Project Blue Book or implement alternative measures for addressing UFO-related inquiries.

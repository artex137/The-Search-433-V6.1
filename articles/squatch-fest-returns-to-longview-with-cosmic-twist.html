sQuatch Fest returns to Longview with cosmic twist

The annual sQuatch Fest has returned to Longview, Washington, featuring a unique focus on UFO exploration alongside its traditional Bigfoot themes. The event, which began on January 6 and runs through January 8, has attracted a diverse crowd of enthusiasts and visitors.

This year’s festival is significant as it expands its scope to include discussions and exhibits related to unidentified flying objects, reflecting a growing interest in the topic. Organizers have partnered with various UFO researchers and enthusiasts to provide a platform for sharing information and experiences.

Key facts

  • Event Name: sQuatch Fest
  • Location: Longview, Washington
  • Date: January 6-8, 2026
  • Focus: Bigfoot and UFO exploration
  • Attendance: Not disclosed
  • Organizers: Local festival committee

Timeline

The sQuatch Fest began on January 6, 2026, in Longview, Washington. Attendees have engaged in various activities, including lectures, panels, and exhibitions focused on Bigfoot lore and UFO phenomena. The event continues through January 8, with a schedule packed with discussions and presentations.

“This year, we wanted to broaden the conversation beyond just Bigfoot,” said one of the festival organizers. “UFOs have always been a part of the mystery surrounding the Pacific Northwest, and we thought it was time to explore that.”

Context

The sQuatch Fest has been a staple in Longview for several years, celebrating local folklore and the region's connection to Bigfoot. The addition of UFO themes aligns with national trends, where interest in unidentified aerial phenomena has surged in recent years, partly due to increased media coverage and official government reports.

New “Nearly Interstellar” Object C/2025 V1 Discovered

On November 2, 2025, astronomer Gennady Borisov identified a new “nearly interstellar” object, C/2025 V1 (Borisov), in the inner solar system. The discovery follows the perihelion passage of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. The object is catalogued by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the Minor Planet Center.

This discovery is significant as it raises questions about the origins and characteristics of C/2025 V1, particularly its relationship with 3I/ATLAS. Authorities are currently studying the orbits and properties of both objects to understand their trajectories and potential implications for future research.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: November 2, 2025
  • Discoverer: Gennady Borisov
  • Object Designation: C/2025 V1 (Borisov)
  • Orbital Inclination: 113 degrees relative to the ecliptic plane
  • Closest separation from 3I/ATLAS: 75 million kilometers (0.5 au)
  • Orbital eccentricity of C/2025 V1: 1.0095
  • Catalogued by: NASA’s JPL and Minor Planet Center

Timeline

On November 2, 2025, Gennady Borisov discovered C/2025 V1 after its perihelion passage. The object was catalogued shortly thereafter by NASA and the Minor Planet Center. Researchers have since begun analyzing its orbit and characteristics, particularly in relation to 3I/ATLAS.

Official statements

“C/2025 V1 is likely to have originated from the Oort cloud at the outskirts of the Solar System,” said Borisov. “Its orbital characteristics suggest it has been influenced by gravitational forces, indicating a possible connection to other celestial objects.”

Context

The discovery of C/2025 V1 continues the trend of identifying and analyzing interstellar objects. The previous interstellar comet, 2I/Borisov, was discovered by Borisov in 2019. The nature of these objects raises questions about their origins and potential technological implications, especially in the case of 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor C/2025 V1 and 3I/ATLAS as they approach their respective perihelion passages. Upcoming observations will focus on determining the composition and behavior of these objects, particularly as 3I/ATLAS nears Earth on December 19, 2025.

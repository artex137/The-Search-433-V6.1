Europe launches historic planetary-defence drill as 3I/ATLAS sparks global alert

Europe has initiated its largest-ever planetary-defence simulation as the comet 3I/ATLAS approaches the solar system, currently located nearly 350 million kilometres from Earth. The drill began on December 10, 2025, and involves defence ministries, space agencies, and emergency units across multiple European countries.

This exercise is significant as it underscores the need for preparedness against potential threats from space. Although scientists have confirmed that 3I/ATLAS poses no actual danger, its arrival has prompted authorities to simulate responses to an impactor of this scale. The drill aims to enhance coordination and readiness among various agencies in the event of a real threat.

Key facts

  • Event: Planetary-defence simulation drill
  • Location: Various countries across Europe
  • Comet distance: Approximately 350 million kilometres from Earth
  • Start date: December 10, 2025
  • Participating entities: Defence ministries, space agencies, emergency units

Timeline

On December 10, 2025, Europe commenced its planetary-defence simulation drill in response to the nearing comet 3I/ATLAS. The drill aims to prepare agencies for potential responses to space threats, despite the comet posing no actual risk.

Official statements

“The arrival of 3I/ATLAS proves humanity must plan now, not later,” a scientist stated regarding the importance of the drill.

Context

This simulation marks a significant step in planetary defence efforts, as previous incidents have highlighted the need for better preparedness against celestial threats. The drill reflects ongoing advancements in international collaboration to address potential risks from space.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches, with further drills planned to enhance readiness and response strategies for potential future threats.

NASA MAVEN captures interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA’s MAVEN spacecraft has observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS for ten days, starting on September 27, 2025. The comet was monitored as it moved behind the Sun, making it impossible to track from Earth-based telescopes. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This observation is significant because it allows scientists to gather crucial data about the comet's chemistry and composition, which enhances understanding of interstellar objects. Various spacecraft across the Solar System, including ESA’s Trace Gas Orbiter and several NASA solar missions, have contributed to tracking 3I/ATLAS during its passage.

Key facts

  • MAVEN began observing 3I/ATLAS on September 27, 2025.
  • The comet moved behind the Sun in October 2025.
  • MAVEN used its Imaging Ultraviolet Spectrograph (IUVS) for observations.
  • Initial results indicate the presence of hydrogen and hydroxyl gas.
  • MAVEN mapped atoms and molecules around the comet.
  • Principal investigator Shannon Curry described the initial results as “incredible.”

Timeline

On September 27, 2025, MAVEN commenced its observations of 3I/ATLAS. During the following ten days, the spacecraft collected data on the comet's coma, mapping various molecules. In October 2025, 3I/ATLAS moved behind the Sun, limiting visibility from Earth-based telescopes.

Throughout this period, other spacecraft, including ESA’s Trace Gas Orbiter and NASA’s STEREO-A and SOHO, captured images that helped refine the comet's trajectory.

Official statements

“The initial results are incredible,” said MAVEN principal investigator Shannon Curry. “We have detected hydroxyl gas, which provides clues of water.”

Context

The observation of 3I/ATLAS is part of ongoing efforts by NASA to study interstellar objects. Past missions have focused on understanding the composition and behavior of comets and asteroids, contributing to the broader knowledge of our Solar System and beyond.

What’s next

Scientists plan to conduct further analysis of the data collected by MAVEN to enhance understanding of interstellar comets and their characteristics.

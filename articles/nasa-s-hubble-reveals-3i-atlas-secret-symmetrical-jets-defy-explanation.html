NASA's Hubble Reveals 3I/ATLAS Secret: Symmetrical Jets Defy Explanation

A rare comet known as 3I/ATLAS has been travelling through our solar system and is now revealing unexpected characteristics, according to NASA's Hubble Space Telescope. The comet, which is only the third extra-solar object detected by humanity, is now making its way back into the depths of space after a close approach to the sun.

This discovery is significant as it challenges existing theories about the behaviour of comets. Researchers are now re-evaluating initial observations, which were previously dismissed as unclear, to understand the comet's unusual properties and behaviour.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS is the third extra-solar object detected, following 'Oumuamua and Borisov.
  • The comet has an estimated nucleus diameter between 440 metres and 5.6 kilometres.
  • 3I/ATLAS approached the sun at a speed of 246,000 kilometres per hour (153,000 miles per hour).
  • Perihelion, or closest approach to the sun, occurred on October 29, 2025.
  • The object exhibits three symmetrical jets, a feature that defies traditional comet models.
  • 3I/ATLAS is rich in carbon dioxide and contains very little water ice.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been observed over the past few months, with initial images captured two to three months ago. These images were initially regarded as uninformative but have since been re-evaluated following further analysis.

On October 29, 2025, the comet reached its closest point to the sun, marking a significant moment in its journey.

NASA's Hubble Space Telescope has provided high-resolution images that have clarified the nature of the comet, showing unexpected features such as its unusual jets and chemical profile.

Official statements

“The object's behaviour does not align with what we expect from a typical comet,” said Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb, highlighting the mystery surrounding 3I/ATLAS.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a small group of extra-solar objects that have been detected, with the previous two, 'Oumuamua and Borisov, also prompting scientific inquiry into the nature of interstellar objects. The unusual properties of 3I/ATLAS may lead to a reevaluation of our understanding of cometary behaviour.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS as it moves away from the sun, focusing on its unique characteristics and behaviour. Future observations may provide further insights into this enigmatic object.

Mural marks 60 years since town's UFO frenzy

A new mural has been unveiled in the town of Shag Harbour, Nova Scotia, to commemorate the 60th anniversary of the 1967 UFO incident that captured the attention of residents and UFO enthusiasts alike. The mural was revealed on October 4, 2023, during a community event that drew a large crowd.

This mural serves as a reminder of the events that unfolded in Shag Harbour, where a series of unexplained lights in the sky led to a significant investigation by local authorities and the Canadian military. The town has become known as a hotspot for UFO sightings, and the mural aims to celebrate this unique aspect of its history.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 4, 2023
  • Location: Shag Harbour, Nova Scotia
  • Incident date: 1967
  • Type of event: Mural unveiling
  • Attendees: Large community turnout

Timeline

In 1967, residents of Shag Harbour reported seeing strange lights in the sky. The incident prompted an investigation by the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) and the Canadian military, who searched the area for potential evidence.

The community has since held events and discussions to keep the memory of the incident alive. The mural is the latest initiative to celebrate Shag Harbour’s unique place in UFO lore.

Official statements

“This mural symbolizes our community’s connection to a significant event in history,” said the mayor of Shag Harbour during the unveiling ceremony.
“We hope it inspires curiosity and brings visitors to our town,” the mayor added.

Context

The Shag Harbour UFO incident has been the subject of various investigations and documentaries over the years. It has cemented the town's reputation as a notable location in UFO history. Local authorities have embraced this notoriety to promote tourism and community engagement.

What’s next

Local officials plan to host additional events and discussions related to the UFO phenomenon in the coming months to further engage the community and attract visitors.

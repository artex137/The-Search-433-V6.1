UFO documentary Age of Disclosure is a major success on Prime Video

The Age of Disclosure, a documentary directed by Dan Farah, has achieved record-breaking success on Prime Video since its release, outperforming numerous blockbuster films. The documentary features testimony from 34 members of the U.S. government regarding Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) and has quickly become the highest-grossing documentary on the streaming platform within 48 hours of its debut.

This documentary matters as it arrives during historic bipartisan Congressional hearings on UAP and the proposed UAP Disclosure Act, which seeks to promote transparency regarding government knowledge of UAP and non-human intelligent life. The film's revelations about an alleged 80-year cover-up of non-human intelligent life have sparked significant interest and conversation about the implications for humanity's future.

Key facts

  • Directed by Dan Farah, known for producing Steven Spielberg’s Ready Player One.
  • Released on Prime Video, breaking the record for highest-grossing documentary within 48 hours.
  • Outperformed major films including One Battle After Another, Jurassic World: Rebirth, and Mission: Impossible – The Final Reckoning.
  • Debuted at #1 and #2 in Prime Video’s Best Selling Movies chart for purchase and rental, respectively.
  • Remains in the top ten of Prime Video's New Releases Best Sellers chart two weeks after release.
  • Features testimony from 34 members of the U.S. Government, military, and intelligence communities.

Timeline

The Age of Disclosure premiered on Prime Video, breaking records for viewership within just 48 hours. Following its release, the documentary quickly secured the top spots in Prime Video’s sales charts. Over the next two weeks, it maintained a strong presence, remaining in the top ten of the New Releases Best Sellers chart.

In conjunction with the film's release, bipartisan Congressional hearings have been held regarding UAP, reflecting a growing interest in government transparency on the issue. The proposed UAP Disclosure Act, led by Senators Schumer, Rounds, and Gillibrand, has further amplified discussions about the government’s knowledge of UAP and non-human life.

Official statements

“Everyone interviewed in the film has direct knowledge of the UAP issue as a result of their work for the U.S. Government, and is breaking their silence in the film with what they can lawfully disclose,” said Dan Farah.
Farah also stated, “The Age of Disclosure is unprecedented in terms of its access, credibility, and the information it reveals. It’s the biggest issue of our time.”

Context

The Age of Disclosure highlights ongoing concerns regarding government transparency about UAP. Previous incidents of alleged cover-ups have fueled public interest and speculation about the existence of non-human life. The documentary’s release coincides with increased scrutiny of the government’s handling of UAP information, particularly in light of recent Congressional hearings.

What’s next

As discussions surrounding UAP and government transparency continue, further investigations and hearings are expected. The proposed UAP Disclosure Act aims to clarify what the government knows and may lead to additional legislative actions in the future.

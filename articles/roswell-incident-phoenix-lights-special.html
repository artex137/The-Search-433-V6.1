Roswell Incident & Phoenix Lights Special

A special event discussing the Roswell UFO incident and the Phoenix Lights took place recently, marking the 50th anniversary of the Roswell crash. Investigative reporter Linda Moulton Howe led the discussion, featuring interviews and insights from various experts in the field of UFO research.

This event is significant as it revisits critical moments in UFO history and raises questions about government transparency regarding unidentified flying objects. Authorities and researchers have continued to explore the implications of alleged cover-ups related to these incidents.

Key facts

  • Date of event: not disclosed
  • Location: not disclosed
  • Key speakers: Linda Moulton Howe, Francis Barwood, Richard C. Hoagland, Steve Bassett
  • Topics discussed: Roswell crash, Phoenix Lights, government cover-up
  • Notable claims: recovered technology from Roswell, mass UFO sighting in 1997

Timeline

In July 1947, a UFO crash reportedly occurred in Roswell, New Mexico. This incident has been a subject of speculation and investigation for decades. In March 1997, the Phoenix Lights mass sighting was witnessed by thousands, leading to public inquiries and discussions about potential government cover-ups.

During the special, Howe presented an audio interview with computer company chief Jack Shulman, who discussed a source confirming that technology allegedly recovered from the Roswell incident was "seeded" to American private industry. The second hour featured discussions on the implications of the Phoenix Lights and a recall election linked to City Council member Francis Barwood's inquiries.

Official statements

“There seem to be other bipeds around that may be smarter than us, and they definitely drive a much later model automobile than us, and they seem to have some interest in us,” said Steve Bassett during the discussion.

Context

The Roswell incident has long fueled speculation about extraterrestrial life and government secrecy. Similarly, the Phoenix Lights incident remains one of the most documented mass UFO sightings, with ongoing debates about the nature of the objects seen that night.

What’s next

Investigations into both the Roswell incident and the Phoenix Lights continue, with researchers and authorities expected to delve deeper into the claims made during the special. Public interest in these topics remains high, prompting ongoing discussions about transparency and accountability from government agencies.

Live Odds on the US Confirming Aliens Exist Before 2027

The odds of the U.S. government confirming the existence of extraterrestrial life before December 31, 2026, remain low, according to prediction markets Polymarket and Kalshi. As of today, Kalshi reports a 5.8% probability for a confirmation, while Polymarket lists it at 5.5%. A No outcome is trading at 94.2%. The low odds follow recent comments from former President Donald Trump, who stated at a Turning Point USA event that "we found many very interesting documents," although this does not constitute an official confirmation.

This situation is significant as it reflects ongoing public interest and congressional pressure regarding UFO disclosures. The Pentagon missed a key deadline on April 14, 2023, to release 46 classified UAP videos requested by Congress, sparking accusations of a cover-up. The public and congressional frustration stems from a perceived lack of transparency surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena.

The Pentagon's failure to comply with specific congressional requests has heightened speculation about government transparency regarding UFOs. The distinction between ambiguous statements and definitive confirmations is crucial for the prediction market, as many past comments from officials have not met the strict criteria for a "Yes" outcome.

For a payout on the Yes side, a current government official must definitively state that extraterrestrial life exists. This has created a challenging environment for those betting on confirmation, as the No side remains heavily favored.

What’s next

As the December 31, 2026, deadline approaches, the market will continue to react to any statements or actions from government officials regarding UFOs. Ongoing investigations and congressional pressure may influence odds as traders look for opportunities in this evolving situation.

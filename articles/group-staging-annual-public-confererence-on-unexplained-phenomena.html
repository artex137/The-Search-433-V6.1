Group staging annual public conference on unexplained phenomena

LAPIS (Lancashire’s Anomalous Phenomena Investigation Society) is staging its annual conference on UFOs and other unexplained phenomena at St Annes Ex-Servicemen’s Club in Blackpool on the weekend of November 22 and 23. The event will run from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. each day. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to the conference.

This conference is significant as it brings together experts and enthusiasts to discuss a variety of topics, including UFOs, folklore, and cryptozoology. LAPIS holds monthly meetings to foster interest in these subjects. The group invites anyone curious about unexplained phenomena to attend.

Key facts

  • Event dates: November 22 and 23, 2023
  • Location: St Annes Ex-Servicemen’s Club, 22 Alexandra Road, Blackpool
  • Start time: 10 a.m. each day
  • End time: 5 p.m. each day
  • Monthly meetings: First Thursday of each month at The Guards Club, 37E Whitegate Drive, Blackpool, starting at 8:30 p.m.

Timeline

LAPIS has organized its annual conference to take place over the weekend of November 22 and 23. The group has held informal meetings on the first Thursday of each month at The Guards Club, promoting discussions on various unexplained phenomena.

This year’s event features several guest speakers who will present on diverse topics related to unexplained phenomena.

Official statements

“These guest speakers are fabulous - they have dedicated their time to their chosen subjects and have a wealth of fascinating knowledge and experience,” said a LAPIS spokesman.

Context

LAPIS has been active in the investigation of anomalous phenomena, providing a platform for discussion and exploration of topics that intrigue the public. The society's previous conferences have garnered attention for their engaging presentations and expert speakers.

What’s next

LAPIS encourages public attendance at the conference and continues to promote interest in unexplained phenomena through its monthly meetings and events.

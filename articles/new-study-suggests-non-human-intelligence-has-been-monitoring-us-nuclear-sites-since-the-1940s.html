New Study Suggests Non-Human Intelligence Has Been Monitoring US Nuclear Sites Since The 1940s

A new study has suggested that non-human intelligence has been monitoring U.S. nuclear sites since the 1940s. The research, conducted by scientists Dr. Beatriz Villarroel and Dr. Stephen Bruehl, analyzed photos from the Palomar Observatory Sky Survey during the Cold War, focusing on 124 above-ground nuclear bomb tests conducted by the United States, Soviet Union, and United Kingdom.

This study matters because it provides potential evidence of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) appearing near nuclear tests. The findings could prompt further investigation into the implications of UAP sightings at sensitive locations, including nuclear power plants and missile bases.

Key facts

  • Study published in the journal Scientific Reports.
  • Research analyzed photos from 1949 to 1957.
  • UAPs were 45% more likely to appear within one day before or after a nuclear test.
  • For every additional UAP reported, there was an 8.5% increase in the number of transients identified.
  • Significant associations noted between nuclear testing and UAP reports (p = 0.015).

Timeline

From 1949 to 1957, the United States, Soviet Union, and United Kingdom conducted 124 above-ground nuclear tests. During this period, researchers from the Palomar Observatory analyzed satellite images and noted the presence of transient star-like objects following these tests. Their findings suggest a correlation between the timing of nuclear tests and UAP sightings.

Dr. Villarroel and Dr. Bruehl's study indicates that multiple transients appeared in photos taken during this time, exhibiting characteristics that do not align with natural phenomena, such as gravitational lensing or gamma ray bursts.

Official statements

“Nature can always surprise us with something we could never have imagined. So, I cannot exclude that there might be some other explanation that is just outside my imagination. But from what I see, I cannot find any other consistent explanation than that we are looking at something artificial.” - Dr. Beatriz Villarroel

Context

This study builds on previous reports of UAPs causing issues around nuclear sites. Historical records, including newspaper accounts and investigations from the Air Force’s Project Blue Book, have documented sightings of unusual, metallic objects near nuclear tests. These incidents have raised questions about the nature and origin of UAPs in sensitive areas.

What’s next

The findings of this study may lead to further investigations into UAPs at nuclear sites. Researchers may explore additional data or conduct new studies to better understand the implications of these sightings.

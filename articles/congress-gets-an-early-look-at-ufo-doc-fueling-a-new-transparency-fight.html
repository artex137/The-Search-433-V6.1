Congress Gets an Early Look at UFO Doc Fueling a New Transparency Fight

Members of Congress have received preliminary access to a document concerning unidentified flying objects (UFOs), also known as unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), during a briefing on November 21, 2025, at the U.S. Capitol. The details of the document have not been disclosed.

This development has sparked renewed discussions about transparency and accountability regarding the government's handling of UFO sightings. Lawmakers are now pushing for more information on UAPs, which have garnered significant public interest and concern in recent years.

Key facts

  • Date of briefing: November 21, 2025
  • Location: U.S. Capitol
  • Document type: Preliminary UFO/UAP report
  • Disclosure status: Details not disclosed

Timeline

On November 21, 2025, Congress received early access to a document related to UFOs during a closed-door briefing. This meeting is part of ongoing efforts by lawmakers to obtain more information about UAPs and the government’s response to them.

In recent years, there has been a growing demand for transparency from various stakeholders, including the public and government officials. This briefing marks a significant step in addressing these demands.

Official statements

“This document is an important step toward understanding what we know about UAPs and how we can ensure transparency moving forward,” a congressional source said.

Context

The discussion around UFOs and UAPs has gained traction since the release of several military videos showing unexplained aerial phenomena. Previous government reports have acknowledged encounters with these objects, leading to calls for more comprehensive investigations and disclosures.

Legislative efforts have been initiated to establish clearer protocols for reporting and investigating UAP sightings, reflecting a shift in how the government addresses these incidents.

What’s next

Members of Congress plan to continue their investigations into UAPs, with expectations for further briefings and reports in the coming months. Lawmakers are focused on ensuring that relevant information is made available to the public.

Hillary Clinton annoyed as 'unusual' Epstein deposition derailed by UFOs and Pizzagate

Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton has expressed frustration after her closed-door deposition before the House Oversight Committee in New York on Thursday became derailed by questions about conspiracy theories, including UFOs and Pizzagate.

This incident highlights ongoing political tensions and the impact of conspiracy theories on serious inquiries. The House Oversight Committee is investigating matters related to Jeffrey Epstein, but the focus shifted during Clinton's testimony.

Key facts

  • Event: Closed-door deposition of Hillary Clinton
  • Date: Thursday, not disclosed
  • Location: New York, House Oversight Committee
  • Focus of deposition: Jeffrey Epstein
  • Topics raised: UFOs, Pizzagate
  • Status: Ongoing investigation by the House Oversight Committee

Timeline

On Thursday, Hillary Clinton attended a closed-door deposition before the House Oversight Committee in New York. The deposition was initially focused on Jeffrey Epstein but quickly shifted as Republicans began to ask her about various conspiracy theories.

After the deposition, Clinton addressed reporters, expressing her annoyance at the direction of the questioning. She described the situation as "quite unusual," indicating the unexpected nature of the topics raised.

Official statements

“It got quite unusual,” Clinton said, referring to the questions about conspiracy theories during her testimony.

Context

This incident is part of a broader trend where conspiracy theories have increasingly infiltrated political discourse. The House Oversight Committee's investigation into Jeffrey Epstein has drawn significant public and media attention, making it a target for various conspiracy narratives.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee is expected to continue its investigation into Jeffrey Epstein, with further hearings and testimonies planned in the coming weeks.

Perseverance Mars rover may have captured historic images of interstellar comet

NASA’s Perseverance rover has photographed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS while exploring Mars on October 4, 2025. The comet, first spotted by an ATLAS telescope in Río Hurtado, Chile, is only the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through our solar system.

This event is significant as it marks the first time a rover on another planet has captured images of an interstellar comet. The images provide a unique perspective on the comet's trajectory and complement existing observations from Earth and various spacecraft. NASA has identified Perseverance, along with other telescopes and spacecraft, as key assets for studying the comet.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was first observed in July 2025.
  • Perseverance captured images on October 4, 2025.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object, following 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • The comet will come as close as 1.8 astronomical units (about 170 million miles) from Earth.
  • Its closest approach to the sun will occur around October 30, at 1.4 astronomical units (130 million miles).

Timeline

In July 2025, the ATLAS telescope in Chile first spotted comet 3I/ATLAS. NASA identified Perseverance as a capable asset for observing the comet. On October 4, 2025, the Perseverance rover captured images of the comet using its Right Navigation Camera. The comet is expected to reach its closest point to the sun around October 30, 2025.

Official statements

Nasa said, “3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth and will remain far from our planet.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects, following the earlier discoveries of 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. These objects offer scientists a rare opportunity to study the chemistry and building blocks of distant star systems.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze the images taken by Perseverance and monitor the comet's trajectory as it approaches the sun and moves further through the solar system.

New UFO documents confirm reports of secret 1949 ‘green fireballs’ meeting

The Pentagon has released a document confirming that scientists at Los Alamos held a secret conference in 1949 to discuss the phenomenon of "green fireballs." The U.S. Air Force's Project Twinkle and Project Grudge investigated these sightings and concluded that they were mostly attributed to man-made and natural phenomena. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the sightings.

This release is significant as it sheds light on the historical context of UFO investigations in the United States. Authorities are now reviewing the findings as part of a broader reassessment of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and their implications for national security.

Key facts

  • Pentagon document released on the investigation of green fireballs.
  • Conference held in 1949 at Los Alamos.
  • Investigated by U.S. Air Force's Project Twinkle and Project Grudge.
  • Conclusions indicated mostly man-made and natural phenomena.

Timeline

In 1949, scientists at Los Alamos convened to discuss reports of green fireballs. The U.S. Air Force subsequently initiated investigations through Project Twinkle and Project Grudge. The investigations concluded that the sightings were primarily due to identifiable causes.

Official statements

“The findings from the 1949 conference provide important context for understanding early UAP investigations,” said a Pentagon spokesperson.

Context

This release adds to the historical narrative surrounding UFOs in the U.S., marking a period when the government began to take reports of unidentified phenomena seriously. The investigations during this era laid the groundwork for contemporary UAP studies.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to review historical documents and findings as part of ongoing investigations into UAP, with an emphasis on transparency and public engagement.

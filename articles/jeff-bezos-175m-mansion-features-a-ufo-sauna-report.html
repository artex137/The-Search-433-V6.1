Jeff Bezos’ $175M Mansion Features a ‘UFO Sauna’ — Report

Jeff Bezos and Lauren Sanchez's mansion in Beverly Hills has drawn attention due to its unique features, including a sauna designed to resemble a UFO. The couple's property, valued at $175 million, has undergone significant renovations and is now a focal point of discussions about luxury real estate.

This renovation matters as it showcases Bezos’ personal taste and investment in high-end properties. The mansion, which spans 10 acres and includes a historic main house from the 1930s, reflects a blend of classic charm and modern design elements, making it one of the most expensive private estates in the United States.

Key facts

  • Property value: $175 million
  • Main house purchased in 2020 for $165 million
  • Neighboring property added for $10 million
  • Construction took several years, with delays reported in 2023
  • Features include a UFO-shaped sauna, aquatic-themed pool, and multiple sports courts

Timeline

Jeff Bezos purchased the main mansion in Beverly Hills in 2020. He then acquired an adjacent property in 2021 for $10 million. Renovations began shortly after and have continued over the years, encountering delays due to planning issues in 2023. The compound has now been completed and is noted for its distinctive design.

Official statements

“The finished compound stands as one of the most expensive and unique private estates in the country,” a source stated.

Context

This mansion is part of Bezos's extensive real estate portfolio, which includes properties in Florida, Washington, D.C., Maui, Texas, and Manhattan. The Beverly Hills estate is particularly significant due to its unique design features, which reflect current trends in luxury home design.

What’s next

There are no reported plans for further renovations at this time, as the property has recently been completed and is currently one of the standout homes in Beverly Hills.

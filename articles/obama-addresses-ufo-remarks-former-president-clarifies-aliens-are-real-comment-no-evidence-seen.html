Obama Addresses UFO Remarks: Former President Clarifies “Aliens Are Real” Comment, No Evidence Seen

Former U.S. President Barack Obama has clarified his comments regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), stating that while he finds the subject intriguing, he has seen no concrete evidence of extraterrestrial life. Obama made these remarks on February 16, 2026, amid heightened public interest in UFOs and recent government disclosures.

This clarification is significant as it reflects ongoing discussions about UAPs and the government's transparency regarding such phenomena. Authorities and experts continue to investigate sightings and gather data, but Obama emphasized the lack of verified proof of alien life visiting Earth.

On February 16, 2026, Obama addressed his previous comments suggesting that “aliens are real.” He stated that while the topic is intriguing, he has not encountered definitive evidence supporting the existence of extraterrestrial life.

“While I find the topic of UAPs fascinating, I have seen no concrete evidence of alien life,” Obama said. “It is essential to continue our scientific exploration and inquiry.”

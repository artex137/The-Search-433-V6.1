Trump says he doesn’t know if aliens are real but directs government to release files on UFOs, more

Published 2026-02-23 23:18 UTC

Trump directs government to release UFO files amid public interest

President Donald Trump has announced that he is directing the Pentagon and other government agencies to identify and release files related to extraterrestrials and UFOs, citing “tremendous interest” in the subject. The announcement was made on Thursday, hours after Trump accused former President Barack Obama of disclosing “classified information” regarding aliens during a recent podcast interview.

This directive comes in the wake of renewed public interest in unidentified flying objects and the possibility of government secrecy surrounding extraterrestrial life. Trump’s remarks reflect ongoing discussions about UFOs, which have gained traction over the past few years, prompting governmental agencies to increase transparency.

Key facts

  • Announcement made on Thursday, not disclosed time.
  • Trump directed the Pentagon and other agencies to release files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life.
  • Obama discussed aliens in a podcast interview over the weekend, later clarifying he had no evidence of contact.
  • Public interest in UFOs surged following the release of Navy videos in 2017.
  • Congress held its first hearings on UFOs in 50 years in May 2022.
  • Pentagon established the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) in July 2022.
  • In June 2024, Congress received an 18-page unclassified report detailing 485 military UFO reports from the past year.

Timeline

In 2017, former Pentagon officials leaked Navy videos of unidentified flying objects to major news outlets, sparking public interest. This led to Congress holding its first hearings on UFOs in 50 years in May 2022. In July 2022, the Pentagon created the AARO to centralize reports of military UFO encounters.

In June 2024, an unclassified report submitted to Congress revealed that out of 485 reports from military personnel, 118 were identified as prosaic objects, such as balloons and birds. The report emphasized that no evidence of extraterrestrial beings or technology has been discovered to date.

Official statements

“I don’t know if they’re real or not,” Trump said regarding extraterrestrial visitors. He added, “A lot of people do. A lot of people believe it.”
“It is important to underscore that, to date, AARO has discovered no evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity, or technology,” the June 2024 report stated.

Context

Interest in UFOs has fluctuated over the decades, but recent events, including the declassification of military footage and public hearings, have reignited discussions. The establishment of the AARO represents a commitment from the Pentagon to address these phenomena more transparently.

Despite numerous reports, most military UFO sightings remain unexplained, although identified cases tend to be benign, according to the AARO.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to begin the process of releasing the requested files. Additionally, investigations into recent UFO sightings will continue, as the AARO works to provide further clarity on these phenomena.

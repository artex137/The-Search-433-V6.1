Comet 3I/ATLAS Imaged by JUICE Spacecraft

The European Space Agency's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (JUICE) spacecraft has captured its first detailed image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing a glowing coma and sweeping tail. The image was taken on November 6, 2025, just days after the comet made its closest approach to the sun. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery is significant as comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third known interstellar object detected passing through our solar system. Unlike typical comets, which originate from the Kuiper Belt or the Oort Cloud, 3I/ATLAS formed around another star before entering our solar system. Scientists are currently analyzing the data collected from the JUICE spacecraft to better understand the comet's composition and activity.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 6, 2025
  • Distance of JUICE from comet: 41 million miles (66 million kilometers)
  • Comet's closest approach to the sun: November 2025
  • Instruments used: JANUS, MAJIS, SWI, PEP, UVS
  • Data transmission delay: Due to JUICE's position on the opposite side of the sun from Earth
  • Next meeting for scientists: Late March 2026 to discuss findings

Timeline

On November 6, 2025, JUICE captured images of 3I/ATLAS as it traveled through the solar system. This occurred shortly after the comet's closest approach to the sun. Throughout November, JUICE's five instruments collected data on the comet's composition and activity. Due to JUICE's positioning, there was a delay in data transmission to Earth, which only arrived recently.

Official statements

“The bright, egg-shape glow at the center of the image is the comet's coma, a vast cloud of gas and dust released as sunlight heats the comet's icy nucleus,” a spokesperson for the European Space Agency stated.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of only three interstellar objects ever detected in our solar system. This discovery highlights the potential for more interstellar visitors and the importance of ongoing space exploration to understand these cosmic wanderers.

What’s next

Scientists will analyze the data collected from the JUICE spacecraft and meet in late March 2026 to discuss their findings regarding comet 3I/ATLAS.

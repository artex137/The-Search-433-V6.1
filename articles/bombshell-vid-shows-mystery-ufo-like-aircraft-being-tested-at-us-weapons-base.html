Bombshell vid shows mystery ‘UFO-like’ aircraft being tested at US weapons base

A video has surfaced showing a mysterious “UFO-like” aircraft being tested at Lockheed Martin’s Helendale radar-testing facility in California. The footage was recorded by investigative YouTuber Anders Otteson, who filmed the object from public land overlooking the site, which is known for its history of developing classified technology.

This development has raised questions about the nature of the aircraft and its potential implications for military technology. Authorities and experts are examining the footage to understand what it might reveal about advanced aircraft being developed by Lockheed Martin’s Skunk Works division.

Key facts

  • Location: Lockheed Martin’s Helendale facility, California, USA
  • Filmed by: Anders Otteson, an investigative YouTuber
  • Object description: Dark, wing-shaped object mounted on a white pillar
  • Facility history: Known for developing classified military technology
  • Skunk Works: Official pseudonym for Lockheed Martin’s Advanced Development Programs
  • Previous projects: U-2 spy plane, SR-71 Blackbird, F-117 Nighthawk
  • Speculated project: Potentially the SR-72 “Darkstar” hypersonic aircraft

Timeline

In recent years, Lockheed Martin’s Helendale facility has been a site of speculation regarding secretive military projects. The footage filmed by Otteson shows an object that resembles an aircraft fuselage being radar-tested. The facility features several test pylons and radar arrays, designed to keep advanced technology hidden from potential adversaries.

Otteson, who has documented various secret sites across the US, speculates that the object could represent advanced aircraft technology in development. He notes that it may be a prototype for a new fighter jet or drone.

Official statements

“It could be something that is in an early stage of development, it could be a new manned fighter or a new drone,” Otteson said. “It could also be a model that is developed specifically for a test.”
“If the US government possesses advanced or ‘alien’ technology, Skunk Works would almost certainly be involved,” he added.

Context

The Helendale facility has a long-standing reputation for housing secret military projects, particularly during the Cold War. Its design aimed to protect advanced aircraft from Soviet surveillance. Recent discussions among US officials and politicians regarding unidentified aerial phenomena have fueled public interest and speculation surrounding the facility's activities.

Speculations have also arisen around the potential of the SR-72 “Darkstar,” a hypersonic reconnaissance aircraft project that could significantly enhance the capabilities of the US military. With expectations of reaching speeds of Mach 6, the project has garnered attention from military analysts and aviation enthusiasts alike.

What’s next

Investigations into the footage and the object’s significance are ongoing. The US Air Force and the Pentagon have not confirmed or denied the existence of the SR-72 project, and further developments may clarify the nature of the aircraft being tested at the Helendale facility.

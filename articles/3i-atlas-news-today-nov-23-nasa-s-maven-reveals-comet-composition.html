Published 2025-11-23 20:29 UTC

3I/ATLAS Comet Revealed by NASA’s MAVEN Spacecraft

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has been studied by NASA’s MAVEN spacecraft, which has provided significant insights into the comet’s hydrogen composition. This discovery came to light on November 23, 2023, and is important for understanding the chemical properties of interstellar objects.

This research matters because it enhances astronomers' understanding of cosmic materials outside the solar system. According to NASA, the findings from 3I/ATLAS could lead to breakthroughs in the study of planetary systems and the evolution of cosmic chemistry.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object.
  • NASA’s MAVEN spacecraft has captured detailed data on the comet's hydrogen composition.
  • The discovery was announced on November 23, 2023.
  • Hydrogen is the most abundant element in the universe.
  • The findings could impact future astronomical research and understanding of planetary formation.

Timeline

The comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected due to its unusual trajectory and speed, indicating it originated from outside the solar system. NASA's MAVEN spacecraft, originally designed to study the Martian atmosphere, has adapted its instruments to collect data on this interstellar visitor. On November 23, 2023, NASA announced the significant findings regarding the comet's hydrogen composition.

Official statements

"The revelation of 3I/ATLAS comet’s hydrogen composition marks a milestone in understanding interstellar objects," NASA stated. "This data enriches our knowledge and sparks curiosity about the vast unknown."

Context

Prior to this discovery, comets originating from outside the solar system have been rare subjects of study. The analysis of 3I/ATLAS provides a unique opportunity to understand the chemical makeup and environmental history of such interstellar bodies, contributing to the broader field of cosmic chemistry.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data collected by MAVEN to further understand the implications of the hydrogen composition on the origins of 3I/ATLAS and its potential impact on future studies of interstellar objects.

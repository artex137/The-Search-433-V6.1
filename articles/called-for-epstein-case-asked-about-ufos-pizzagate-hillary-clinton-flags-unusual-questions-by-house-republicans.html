Called for Epstein case, asked about UFOs, Pizzagate: Hillary Clinton flags ‘unusual’ questions by House Republicans

Former US Secretary of State Hillary Clinton has criticized House Republicans for asking her about UFOs and the 'Pizzagate' conspiracy during her deposition on Thursday as part of the House Oversight Committee's investigation into convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein.

Clinton described the questions as “unusual” and stated that they detracted from the main focus of the inquiry. She indicated that the hearing, which lasted nearly seven hours, included a series of questions that she found repetitive and unproductive. The implications of these questions have drawn media attention, particularly in light of recent discussions surrounding UFOs by high-profile figures.

Key facts

  • Hearing lasted nearly seven hours.
  • Clinton did not disclose who asked her about UFOs and Pizzagate.
  • Representative Suhas Subramanyam stated that transcripts will be available soon.
  • Bill Clinton is scheduled for his deposition on 27 February.
  • Clinton asserted she answered all questions posed to her.

Timeline

On Thursday, during her deposition, Hillary Clinton faced questions from House Republicans regarding Jeffrey Epstein. As the hearing progressed, she reported being asked about UFOs and the Pizzagate conspiracy theory. Clinton later spoke to reporters, expressing her frustration with the nature of the questions.

Following her deposition, Representative Suhas Subramanyam indicated that the transcripts would provide more clarity on the topics discussed. He anticipated the release of these documents within 24 hours.

Bill Clinton is set to appear for his deposition on 27 February.

Official statements

“It then got, at the end, quite unusual because I started being asked about UFOs and a series of questions about Pizzagate — one of the most vile bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet.”
“I thought it was very repetitive. I thought that they literally asked the same questions over and over again, which didn’t seem to me to be very productive.”

Context

The Pizzagate conspiracy theory emerged during the 2016 presidential election and falsely accused top Democratic politicians of operating a child sex trafficking ring out of a Washington, DC pizzeria. Recently, discussions about UFOs have gained traction, particularly following comments from US President Obama affirming the existence of unidentified aerial phenomena and the Trump administration's move to declassify related files.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee is expected to release transcripts of Clinton's deposition shortly. Bill Clinton's deposition is scheduled for 27 February, and further inquiries may follow depending on the information revealed in the transcripts.

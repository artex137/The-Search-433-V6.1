6 U.S. Presidents Who Were Totally Obsessed with UFOs

Published 2025-11-06 20:44 UTC

U.S. Presidents' Fascination with UFOs

Several U.S. presidents have shown a notable interest in unidentified flying objects (UFOs) over the decades, revealing an unexpected fixation on what might be in the skies. This interest spans across party lines and has led to significant public discussions about UFOs and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs).

This fascination with UFOs matters as it reflects a broader cultural curiosity and has influenced governmental transparency on the subject. Recent developments have seen increased military attention to UAPs, prompting discussions about potential investigations and disclosures regarding such phenomena.

Key facts

  • In 1966, Congressman Gerald Ford called for a congressional hearing on UFOs after lights were reported in Michigan skies.
  • Jimmy Carter reported a UFO sighting in Georgia in 1969 and promised UFO transparency during his presidential campaign.
  • Ronald Reagan expressed interest in UFOs, establishing a space advisory council during his presidency.
  • Bill Clinton investigated the Roswell incident and expressed curiosity about extraterrestrial life.
  • Barack Obama acknowledged unexplained military footage of flying objects, lending credibility to the topic.
  • Donald Trump oversaw the public release of UAP videos and established the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force.
  • John F. Kennedy is linked to UFO discussions through a 1963 memo, though claims about his interest remain speculative.
  • George H.W. Bush, former CIA director, did not publicly discuss UFOs, despite speculation surrounding his knowledge.

Timeline

In 1813, Thomas Jefferson documented an unusual aerial event in Kentucky, marking one of the earliest mentions of strange occurrences in the sky by a president. In 1966, Gerald Ford prompted the first official congressional hearing on UFOs after sightings in Michigan.

Jimmy Carter reported a UFO sighting in 1969, promising transparency during his campaign but facing challenges once in office. Ronald Reagan expressed his fascination with UFOs in the 1970s and included sci-fi authors in his advisory councils.

In the 1990s, Bill Clinton investigated the Roswell incident and communicated his curiosity about UFOs to his cabinet. Barack Obama later acknowledged military footage of unexplained flying objects, while Donald Trump facilitated the public release of UAP videos and established a task force to study the phenomena.

Official statements

“I’m disappointed that no aliens have shown up,” Bill Clinton reportedly said, reflecting his playful curiosity about extraterrestrial life.
Barack Obama stated, “Some military footage shows things flying in ways we can’t explain,” acknowledging the credibility of reported UAPs.

Context

Historically, the fascination with UFOs among U.S. presidents has contributed to a cultural narrative surrounding extraterrestrial life. This interest has evolved alongside advancements in technology and increased military scrutiny of aerial phenomena.

The establishment of the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force under Trump marks a notable shift in governmental approach, indicating a growing seriousness about investigating these sightings.

What’s next

Future investigations into UAPs are anticipated as military and governmental agencies continue to address public interest and transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

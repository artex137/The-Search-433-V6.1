Recent Colorado UFO sightings: ‘Sparkling’ craft, ‘possible abduction’

Six UFO sightings have been reported in Colorado throughout September, including a “sparkling” craft and an incident involving a possible abduction, according to the National UFO Reporting Center. The sightings occurred at various locations, with the most notable incident taking place at the Bureau of Land Management in Rabbit Valley on September 25.

This surge in sightings has drawn attention from both the public and authorities. The National UFO Reporting Center, a non-profit agency based in Washington state, has opened investigations into the claims. The center is known for its independence and anonymity policies, which encourage reports from the public.

Key facts

  • Six reported UFO sightings in Colorado during September 2023.
  • Notable incident on September 25 at 8:40 p.m. in Rabbit Valley.
  • Size of the black disc estimated at 40 to 50 feet long.
  • Other sightings occurred in Longmont, Denver, Colorado Springs, and Brighton.
  • No photographs or videos have been documented for the sightings.
  • The National UFO Reporting Center is a non-profit organization.

Timeline

On September 1, two sightings occurred in Longmont. One observer reported lights moving from west to east, with the lights appearing to sparkle. The second sighting featured a blue-grey oval object, about 100 feet long, flying quietly across the sky.

On September 9, a dark orb was spotted in Denver. The center attributed this sighting to a possible balloon. On September 18, observers in Colorado Springs reported lights in a triangular formation. The most prominent incident took place on September 25 when a visitor at the Knowles Campground in Rabbit Valley reported a black disc that landed nearby.

Official statements

“I was camping in site No. 3 of the Knowles overlook campground... then half an hour later, I hear a swooshing, humming, whirring sound and see a black object come overhead.” - Witness from Rabbit Valley
“The lights did not blink. Sometimes they would become dimmer but never blinking.” - Observer from Longmont

Context

The National UFO Reporting Center has a long-standing history of documenting sightings and operates a hotline for the public to report unusual aerial phenomena. The center is recognized by various agencies, including law enforcement and military organizations, which often refer sightings to them.

Prior incidents of UFO sightings in Colorado have generated interest and speculation, but many cases remain unresolved. The recent uptick in reports has reignited discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

The National UFO Reporting Center continues to investigate the recent sightings. The agency has not disclosed any specific timelines for concluding these investigations or any further actions that might be taken based on the reports received.

Comet 3I/ATLAS from another star system approaches perihelion on 29th with dominant CO2 makeup

Published 2025-10-28 21:42 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS Approaches Perihelion on October 29

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, the third confirmed object from outside the Solar System, approaches perihelion on October 29, 2025, at a distance of 210 million kilometers from the Sun. Detected by the ATLAS telescope in Chile on July 1, this comet travels at a speed of 209,000 km/h and poses no collision risk to Earth, according to astronomers.

This event is significant as it challenges traditional models of comet formation due to its CO2-rich composition. Observations continue as astronomers track the comet's activity and its potential implications for our understanding of interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Perihelion Date: October 29, 2025
  • Distance from Sun: 210 million kilometers
  • Speed: 209,000 km/h
  • Estimated Age: Over 7 billion years
  • Comet Diameter: 300 meters to 11 kilometers
  • Mass Estimates: Billions of tons
  • Dust Production Rate: 30 kg per second

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was detected by the ATLAS telescope in Chile on July 1, 2025. Its entry into the Solar System occurred in May 2025, at a distance of 6.4 astronomical units from the Sun. Initial imaging captured a diffuse coma, with cometary activity confirmed on July 2. The comet passed Mars on October 3, 2025, at a distance of 28 million kilometers.

As it approaches perihelion, the comet displays a 10,000-kilometer tail formed by dust and gas jets. Observations by the James Webb Space Telescope have detected organic materials on the comet's surface.

Official statements

“The intense activity of 3I/ATLAS and its unique CO2-rich composition offer new insights into the nature of interstellar comets,” said an astronomer involved in the observations.

Context

3I/ATLAS is designated as the third interstellar comet, following 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Its hyperbolic orbit confirms its origin beyond the Solar System, and its composition has raised questions regarding existing models of comet formation.

What’s next

NASA is coordinating with the International Asteroid Warning Network for ongoing monitoring of 3I/ATLAS from November 2025 to January 2026. The comet will cross Jupiter's orbit post-October and exit the Solar System, becoming unobservable from ground telescopes in December.

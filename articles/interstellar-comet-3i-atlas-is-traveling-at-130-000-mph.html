Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Is Traveling At 130,000 mph

Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS has reached speeds of 130,000 miles per hour (209,215 kilometers per hour) as it travels through space, according to NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center. This comet is noted as the fastest ever recorded and is currently moving through our solar system.

The significance of Comet 3I/ATLAS lies in its unique interstellar origin, which provides scientists with valuable insights into the composition and behavior of objects from outside our solar system. NASA continues to study the comet's trajectory and characteristics to enhance understanding of interstellar bodies.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is traveling at 130,000 mph (209,215 km/h).
  • It is the fastest comet ever recorded.
  • The comet is of interstellar origin.
  • NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center has been monitoring the comet.

Timeline

NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center has provided updates on Comet 3I/ATLAS since its discovery. The comet's speed has been documented as it approaches the inner solar system, where it will be closely observed for further scientific analysis.

“3I/ATLAS is the fastest comet ever recorded and its interstellar,” said a spokesperson from NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center.

Context

Previously, comets entering the solar system have been studied to understand their origins and trajectories. Comet 3I/ATLAS is particularly significant due to its interstellar nature, which distinguishes it from typical solar system comets.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system, providing updates on its trajectory and any findings related to its composition and behavior.

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is traveling at 130,000 mph

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is currently traveling at a speed of approximately 209,000 kilometres per hour (130,000 miles per hour) as it approaches the inner solar system. The comet, first detected in 2019, is expected to make its closest approach to Earth on December 2, 2023.

This event is significant for astronomers, as it provides a rare opportunity to study an interstellar object passing through the solar system. Scientists aim to gather data on the comet's composition and trajectory, enhancing our understanding of such celestial bodies. Observatories worldwide are preparing to monitor the comet closely during its approach.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Speed: 209,000 km/h (130,000 mph)
  • Closest Approach: December 2, 2023
  • First Detected: 2019
  • Observation Agencies: Various global observatories

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected in 2019, marking it as an interstellar object. Since its discovery, astronomers have tracked its trajectory, noting its approach to the inner solar system.

As the comet nears Earth, scientists have begun preparations to observe it closely, aiming to gather important data during its closest approach.

Official statements

“The approach of 3I/ATLAS offers a unique chance to learn more about interstellar objects,” said a spokesperson from the observatory. “We are excited to gather data that could provide insights into the origins of such comets.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few confirmed interstellar comets to pass through the solar system. The study of these objects can reveal information about the materials present in other star systems, contributing to our understanding of the universe.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches, astronomers will continue to observe and analyze the comet's behaviour and characteristics. Data collected during its closest approach will contribute to ongoing research into interstellar objects.

The Swedish film UFO Sweden, directed by Victor Danell and originally released in 2022, has gained international attention in 2025 following its distribution in other markets. The film blends science fiction with family drama, centering on teenager Denise as she investigates her father's mysterious disappearance years earlier, linked to a group of UFO enthusiasts. The production culminates in a poignant exploration of family loyalty and the unknown.

This film matters as it addresses themes of family, obsession, and the quest for understanding the universe. Authorities in Sweden have not disclosed any investigations related to the film's content, but the narrative raises questions about UFO phenomena and their implications on personal relationships.

Timeline

Denise, a teenager, investigates her father’s disappearance, which happened years earlier when he was involved with a UFO enthusiast group. The film reveals that Denise grows up in foster homes during the 1990s, grappling with her father’s absence.

As she digs deeper, she discovers gravitational anomalies linked to a wormhole. The climax features Denise and Lennart, a veteran member of the group, launching her father's red Saab car from a multi-story parking lot to test their theories. After various confrontations and discoveries, they activate the wormhole, which leads to a transformative experience for both characters.

In the end, Denise chooses loyalty over following her father into the unknown, returning to help Lennart instead of crossing into the wormhole.

Official statements

“Denise’s journey illustrates the conflict between personal relationships and the allure of the unknown,” said a spokesperson for the film. “It underscores the importance of human connections.”

NASA’s New SPHEREx Mission Observes Interstellar Comet

NASA’s SPHEREx (Spectro-Photometer for the History of the Universe, Epoch of Reionization and Ices Explorer) has observed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from August 7 to August 15, 2025. The observations provide new insights into the comet's properties, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This mission is significant as it enhances understanding of interstellar objects. NASA’s space telescopes, including Webb and Hubble, have also contributed to the research, offering a comprehensive view of the comet’s size, physical properties, and chemical makeup. The data collected may inform future studies and missions regarding solar system objects.

Key facts

  • Comet observed from August 7 to August 15, 2025.
  • NASA’s SPHEREx is one of the telescopes involved in the observation.
  • Other telescopes include NASA’s Webb and Hubble.
  • No threat to Earth has been reported from the comet.
  • Research note available online from the SPHEREx team.

Timeline

NASA’s SPHEREx began its observations of comet 3I/ATLAS on August 7, 2025. The data collection continued until August 15, 2025. Following the observation period, the SPHEREx team has started analyzing the insights gathered, with a research note made available online.

Official statements

“While the comet poses no threat to Earth, NASA’s space telescopes help support the agency’s ongoing mission to find, track, and better understand solar system objects,” said Alise Fisher from NASA Headquarters.

Context

Interstellar comets, like 3I/ATLAS, are rare and provide valuable information about the formation of solar systems. Previous missions and observations have highlighted the importance of understanding these objects to expand knowledge of cosmic phenomena.

What’s next

The SPHEREx team will continue analyzing the data from the observations. Further research and publications are expected to follow, contributing to the broader understanding of interstellar comets.

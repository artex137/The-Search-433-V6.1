Trump Orders UFO, Alien Records Released by Pentagon

President Donald Trump has directed the Pentagon and other government agencies to release files related to extraterrestrials and unidentified flying objects (UFOs) on Thursday. The announcement comes amid ongoing public interest and speculation about UFOs, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these matters.

This directive aims to satisfy what Trump described as “tremendous interest” in the subject. The President has called for the inclusion of "any and all other information connected to these highly complex, but extremely interesting and important, matters." Trump made the announcement on social media shortly after former President Barack Obama discussed the existence of aliens during a podcast interview.

Key facts

  • Trump made the announcement on social media on Thursday.
  • The directive includes files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial matters.
  • Obama stated he believes in the existence of aliens but has not seen evidence of them.
  • The Pentagon previously released a report in March 2024 stating no evidence of alien technology was found.

Timeline

On Thursday, President Trump announced his directive to the Pentagon regarding the release of UFO and alien-related files. This announcement followed a podcast in which former President Obama discussed the possibility of alien life. Obama clarified his remarks on Instagram, stating he has not seen evidence of extraterrestrial contact during his presidency.

The interest in UFOs has surged recently, particularly after the Pentagon's investigations into unexplained aerial phenomena. The discussion around these topics has been fueled by public fascination and numerous conspiracy theories over decades.

Official statements

“I don’t know if they’re real or not,” Trump told reporters aboard Air Force One. “I may get him out of trouble by declassifying,” he added, referring to Obama’s comments.
Obama said, “Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there’s life out there. But the distances between solar systems are so great that the chances we’ve been visited by aliens is low, and I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrials have made contact with us. Really!”

Context

The public fascination with UFOs and extraterrestrials has led to various conspiracy theories and significant online discussions. Notably, the “Storm Area 51” event drew attention when approximately 100 people gathered at the entrance of the classified military base in September 2019, purportedly to “see them aliens.” Recent government investigations into unexplained aircraft have further intensified interest in the topic.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to begin the process of declassifying and releasing the requested files. Further investigations into UFO sightings and phenomena may also continue as public interest remains high.

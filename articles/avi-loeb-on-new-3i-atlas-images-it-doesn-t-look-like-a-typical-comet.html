Avi Loeb on new 3I/ATLAS images: ‘It doesn’t look like a typical comet’

Harvard professor Avi Loeb has discussed the first images of comet 3I/ATLAS after its recent pass by Mars on October 8, 2025. The early photos, released by international space agencies, indicate features that differ from those of a typical comet. However, NASA's higher-resolution images remain delayed due to a government shutdown.

This development is significant as it may provide new insights into the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS. Loeb has expressed optimism about forthcoming data, stating, “the best is yet to come.”

Key facts

  • Event date: October 8, 2025
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Location of pass: Near Mars
  • Images released by: International space agencies
  • NASA's images: Delayed due to government shutdown

Timeline

On October 8, 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS passed Mars. Following this event, early images were shared by various international space agencies, showcasing details that do not align with typical comet characteristics. However, NASA's higher-resolution images, which could provide more comprehensive data, have yet to be released due to ongoing government shutdown issues.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, “the best is yet to come.”

Context

This is not the first time that unusual characteristics of comets have drawn attention from the scientific community. Comet 3I/ATLAS's unique features could contribute to ongoing research about the formation and behavior of comets in the solar system.

What’s next

NASA's upcoming release of higher-resolution images is anticipated, pending resolution of the government shutdown. Researchers will analyze the new data to further understand the properties of 3I/ATLAS.

What’s Up: December 2025 Skywatching Tips from NASA

Published 2025-12-01 20:44 UTC

3I/ATLAS Comet Approaches Earth in December 2025

The 3I/ATLAS comet is making its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, with no reported injuries or fatalities. This event coincides with the peak of the Geminid meteor shower on December 13 and 14 and a conjunction of the Moon and Jupiter on December 7.

This celestial event is significant as Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third object from outside our solar system discovered within it. NASA has been actively observing the comet with various spacecraft and telescopes to gather data while it is in proximity to Earth. Officials have confirmed that the comet poses no threat to our planet, remaining approximately 170 million miles away during its closest approach.

Key facts

  • December 19: Comet 3I/ATLAS closest approach to Earth
  • December 13-14: Peak of the Geminid meteor shower
  • December 7: Moon and Jupiter conjunction
  • 3I/ATLAS will be about 170 million miles from Earth
  • Up to 120 Geminid meteors may be visible per hour
  • Look for the comet under the star Regulus in the constellation Leo

Timeline

On December 7, the Moon and Jupiter will appear close together in the eastern sky, creating a visually striking conjunction. The Geminid meteor shower will peak on the evenings of December 13 and 14, offering a chance to view bright and colorful meteors. Finally, on December 19, Comet 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Earth.

Official statements

“Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third object in history from outside of our solar system to be discovered within our solar system,” said a representative from NASA.
“This could be your best bet to see this interstellar interloper,” the representative added regarding the comet's visibility.

Context

Prior to the discovery of 3I/ATLAS, only two other interstellar objects have been documented entering our solar system. The study of such objects provides insights into the formation and evolution of solar systems and the nature of the universe.

What’s next

Skywatchers are encouraged to look for local observatories and skywatching events to enhance their viewing experience of these celestial occurrences throughout December.

Five bright comets approaching Earth: Here’s when they will be visible in the skies

Sky watchers have an exciting few months ahead, as five comets are approaching Earth. The first, C/2025 A6 (Lemmon), will be closest on 21 October 2025. The interstellar visitor 3I/Atlas will arrive on 18–19 December 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to these astronomical events.

This rare occurrence matters to astronomers and stargazers alike, as it presents unique opportunities for observation. Authorities and astronomers are preparing to provide guidance on how to track the comets as they approach, with special attention on Comet Lemmon, which is predicted to be the brightest.

Key facts

  • C/2025 A6 (Lemmon) closest approach: 21 October 2025
  • C/2025 K1 (Atlas) passing: 24–25 November 2025
  • 3I/Atlas arrival: 18–19 December 2025
  • 24P/Schanmasse visibility: 4 January 2026
  • C/2024 E1 (Wierzchos) passing: 17 February 2026

Timeline

Comet C/2025 A6 (Lemmon) was discovered on 3 January 2025 in Arizona. It orbits the sun approximately every 1,396 years, making its last visit around the year 629. After its upcoming approach, it will not return until 3421.

As of now, the comet is currently in the Gemini constellation during dawn skies. Reports indicate that its brightness has increased from magnitude 16.5 to 11 recently. Japanese comet expert Seiichi Yoshida predicts further brightening could occur.

Official statements

“If the trend continues, it may reach magnitude 5 or 4, which would make it visible to the unaided eye,” said Seiichi Yoshida, a Japanese comet expert.

Context

Comet Lemmon's expected brightness has garnered significant interest within the astronomical community. Comets often display erratic and unpredictable behaviour, making regular observation crucial in the weeks leading up to their closest approaches.

What’s next

As the comets approach, astronomers will provide finder charts and detailed tracking information. Observers are encouraged to choose locations with unobstructed western horizons for optimal viewing opportunities.

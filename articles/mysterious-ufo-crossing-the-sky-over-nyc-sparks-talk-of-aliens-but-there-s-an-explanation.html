Mysterious ‘UFO’ crossing the sky over NYC sparks talk of aliens — but there’s an explanation

A mysterious orb of light with a glowing tail was spotted soaring across New York City’s skies early Tuesday morning, leading some to speculate about extraterrestrial life. No injuries or fatalities have been reported. The object was later identified as part of the Polaris Dawn mission, which launched from Kennedy Space Station in Florida at 5:23 a.m. and is carrying NASA scientists and a civilian crew.

This sighting matters as it highlights the public's fascination with space exploration and the potential for human activities in orbit to be misinterpreted. Authorities have clarified that the orb is not an alien spacecraft but part of a commercial space mission.

Key facts

  • Object spotted over New York City on Tuesday morning.
  • Polaris Dawn launched at 5:23 a.m. from Kennedy Space Station.
  • Mission includes NASA scientists and civilian billionaire Jared Isaacman.
  • Planned altitude of 870 miles, surpassing records set in 1966.
  • Spacewalk scheduled for Thursday.

Timeline

At 5:23 a.m. on Tuesday, the Polaris Dawn mission launched from the Kennedy Space Station. Shortly after, the spacecraft became visible across the East Coast, including New York City. As the object moved horizontally across the sky, some residents began to speculate about its origins, with social media buzzing with theories of alien encounters.

The Polaris Dawn mission aims to set a record altitude of 870 miles, surpassing previous human spaceflight limits. The crew will spend five days in space, with a spacewalk planned for Thursday.

Official statements

“That’s aliens dawg!” one person wrote on social media, reflecting the excitement and speculation surrounding the sighting.
“There are enough extraterrestrials in NYC, if you ask me,” wrote another observer.

Context

This incident follows a trend of increased interest in space exploration and commercial missions. The Polaris Dawn mission is notable for including non-professional astronauts in a spacewalk, a first for the industry. Previous records in human spaceflight have been set by NASA’s Apollo and Gemini programs.

What’s next

The Polaris Dawn crew will continue their mission, with a spacewalk scheduled for Thursday. Further updates will be provided as the mission progresses.

3.8bn files on secret alien and UFO programs vanish hours after Trump's order

A collection of 3.8 billion files related to aliens, UFOs, and conspiracy theories has disappeared from a public archive just hours after former U.S. President Donald Trump ordered full disclosure on the matter. The circumstances surrounding the disappearance remain unclear, with authorities stating it is not known whether the incident was a deliberate act or an error.

This incident raises concerns regarding transparency and access to government information on UFOs, a topic of increasing interest among the public. Authorities are currently investigating the situation to determine the cause of the disappearance and to assess the implications of such a significant loss of data.

Key facts

  • Files involved: 3.8 billion
  • Event date: Hours after Trump's disclosure order
  • Location: Public archive (specific location not disclosed)
  • Status: Investigation ongoing

Timeline

On the day of the incident, Donald Trump issued an order for full disclosure regarding UFOs and related matters. Shortly thereafter, a significant number of files—amounting to 3.8 billion—vanished from a public archive.

The exact time of Trump's order and the subsequent disappearance of the files has not been disclosed. Authorities have begun their investigation to determine the nature of the disappearance.

Official statements

“It is not known whether it was deliberate or a mistake,” authorities said regarding the incident.

Context

This incident follows a growing trend of interest in government transparency concerning UFOs. Past disclosures have led to increased public curiosity and speculation about unidentified aerial phenomena and potential government cover-ups.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue their investigation into the disappearance of the files, with updates likely to follow as new information becomes available.

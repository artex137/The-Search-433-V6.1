Trump Directs Government to Release Files on UFOs and Aliens

Former President Donald Trump has directed the Pentagon and other government agencies to identify and release files related to extraterrestrials, UFOs, and unidentified aerial phenomena, according to a social media post made on March 1, 2026. Trump stated he is unsure if aliens are real but desires greater transparency on the topic due to significant public interest.

This announcement follows former President Barack Obama’s recent remarks in a podcast interview, where he suggested the possibility of alien life, although he clarified he has not seen evidence of contact. The renewed focus on UFOs has prompted increased government scrutiny and transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Announcement Date: March 1, 2026
  • Agency Involved: Pentagon
  • Previous Investigations: No evidence of extraterrestrial activity found
  • Obama's Podcast Remarks: Suggested aliens may exist but lacks evidence of contact
  • Trump's Quote: "I don't know if they're real or not."
  • Sean Kirkpatrick (AARO Head): "To date, AARO has discovered no evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity, or technology."

Timeline

On March 1, 2026, Donald Trump announced his directive through a social media post. This came shortly after Barack Obama suggested in a podcast that the universe may harbor alien life. Trump's directive aims to enhance transparency regarding UFOs and has sparked discussions about the government's past investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena.

The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), established in 2022, has previously collected reports of military UFO encounters but has not reported any evidence of extraterrestrial technology or activity.

Official statements

“I don't know if they're real or not.” — Donald Trump
“Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there's life out there.” — Barack Obama
“It is important to underscore that, to date, AARO has discovered no evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity, or technology.” — Sean Kirkpatrick, Head of AARO

Context

The topic of UFOs and potential extraterrestrial life has gained traction in recent years, particularly following the establishment of the AARO. Previous government investigations have largely dismissed the possibility of alien visitation, yet public interest remains high. Trump's directive may prompt a reevaluation of the information currently held by the government.

What’s next

The Pentagon's AARO is expected to continue its investigations and collection of reports on unidentified aerial phenomena. It remains unclear what new information may emerge from Trump's directive to release government files.

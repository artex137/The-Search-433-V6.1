John Greenewald: “The government lied about its interest in UFOs”

President Donald Trump has directed the Pentagon and other federal agencies to release records related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), unidentified flying objects (UFOs), and alien life as of last month. This announcement marks a significant shift from decades of government claims that they were no longer studying these phenomena.

The directive has raised questions about the government's previous transparency regarding UFOs and UAPs. Authorities are now tasked with identifying and disclosing all relevant information, which some experts believe could shed light on past investigations and public interest in extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Directive issued by President Donald Trump last month.
  • Involves the Pentagon and other federal agencies.
  • Focus on records related to UAPs, UFOs, and alien life.
  • Contradicts decades of government statements on the study of these phenomena.

Timeline

In June 2020, President Trump issued a directive to federal agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing information on UFOs and UAPs. This followed years of government silence on the subject, during which agencies claimed they had ceased investigations.

Since the directive, federal agencies have been working to compile relevant records. The full scope and details of the information to be released remain undisclosed.

Official statements

“The government lied about its interest in UFOs,” said John Greenewald, a prominent UFO researcher. “This new directive could change everything.”

Context

For decades, various government agencies, including the Department of Defense, have publicly stated they were not actively studying UFOs or UAPs. This has led to criticism and skepticism from researchers and the public regarding the government's transparency on the matter.

The recent directive appears to signal a change in policy, potentially opening the door for more openness about the government's historical investigations into these phenomena.

What’s next

Federal agencies are now expected to begin identifying and releasing relevant records. The timeline for this release has not been disclosed, but it marks a significant step in addressing public interest and scrutiny regarding UAPs and UFOs.

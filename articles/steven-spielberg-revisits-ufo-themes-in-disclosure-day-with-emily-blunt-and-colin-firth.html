Steven Spielberg revisits UFO themes in ‘Disclosure Day’ with Emily Blunt and Colin Firth

Steven Spielberg has returned to the UFO genre with his upcoming film, Disclosure Day, scheduled for release in summer 2026. The film features a cast led by Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, and Colin Firth, and it explores themes of secrecy and public disclosure surrounding alien encounters.

This film is significant as it taps into the growing public interest in real-world UFO hearings and debates about transparency. Spielberg's approach emphasizes emotional responses and human doubt rather than visual spectacle, aligning with a cultural moment marked by curiosity about extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Film Title: Disclosure Day
  • Director: Steven Spielberg
  • Release Date: Summer 2026
  • Cast: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Colman Domingo, Eve Hewson, Wyatt Russell, Henry Lloyd-Hughes, Elizabeth Marvel
  • Trailer Tone: Emotionally restrained, focusing on human reactions
  • Marketing Rollout: Billboards in Times Square, teaser attached to Avatar theatrical release

Timeline

Steven Spielberg has revisited the UFO genre with Disclosure Day, his first feature since The Fabelmans in 2022. The film's trailer, which has been released recently, sets a tone of uncertainty and emotional engagement. The screenplay has been written by David Koepp and is based on a story by Spielberg.

The trailer introduces characters grappling with the implications of alien discovery, emphasizing the question of who controls the information regarding such events. Public interest in UFOs has surged, coinciding with the release of related documentaries and increasing discussions around transparency.

Official statements

“The whole world deserves full disclosure,” said Josh O’Connor in the trailer, highlighting the film's focus on access to truth.
Colman Domingo expressed his emotional reaction to the script, stating, “I cried after reading it,” indicating a focus on internal conflict rather than military response.

Context

Spielberg's earlier works, such as E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial and Close Encounters of the Third Kind, have shaped modern perceptions of alien encounters. These films examined human behavior in the context of the unknown, and Disclosure Day appears to continue this tradition by exploring emotional consequences rather than confrontations.

Recent cultural trends have shown a heightened curiosity about UFOs, especially following the success of The Age of Disclosure, a documentary that performed well on Amazon rentals.

What’s next

As the release of Disclosure Day approaches, marketing efforts are set to intensify, aiming to maintain public interest. A longer trailer is expected to be released, which may provide further insights into the film's narrative and thematic focus.

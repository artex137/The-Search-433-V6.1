Vanished Retired Air Force General Linked to Bizarre UFO-Themed X Account That Fell Silent the Day He Disappeared

A retired United States Air Force general, Maj Gen William Neil McCasland, has vanished from his Albuquerque home on 27 February. McCasland, known for his extensive experience in advanced defence research, left without his mobile phone or watch, prompting his family to report him missing shortly after he failed to return.

This case has drawn significant attention due to McCasland's military background and the coincidental silence of a UFO-themed X account, '@TMBSPACESHIPS,' which stopped posting on the same day he disappeared. Local authorities, including the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office and the FBI, are actively investigating the situation, searching for clues about McCasland's whereabouts.

Key facts

  • McCasland was last seen on 27 February at approximately 11:00 AM.
  • He left his home on Quail Run Court in Albuquerque, New Mexico.
  • A Silver Alert was issued shortly after he was reported missing.
  • Search efforts have canvassed over 600 homes in the area.
  • The FBI is assisting local law enforcement with the investigation.
  • The X account '@TMBSPACESHIPS' ceased posting on 27 February.

Timeline

On 27 February, at around 11:00 AM, McCasland left his home without any personal communication devices. Family members reported him missing shortly thereafter. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office issued a Silver Alert, prompting extensive search efforts that included canvassing over 600 homes and reviewing security footage.

On the same day, the X account '@TMBSPACESHIPS' published its last post, which occurred roughly thirty minutes before McCasland was last seen. Investigators began examining this account due to its unusual timing and its focus on topics relevant to McCasland's professional expertise.

Official statements

“Despite the extensive search effort, there have been no confirmed sightings of the retired general,” said a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office.

Context

McCasland has had a distinguished career in the Air Force, working on sensitive technological programmes and serving as director of special programmes within the Office of the Under Secretary of Defence. His expertise in advanced aerospace technologies raises questions about the abrupt silence of the X account, which appears to have shared content relevant to his work.

Previously, the X account had posted over 1,600 messages discussing technical topics like plasma physics and experimental spacecraft technologies, which are closely aligned with McCasland's background.

What’s next

Investigators continue to search for McCasland while exploring potential links between his disappearance and the X account. Authorities have not confirmed any direct connection, but the unusual circumstances have prompted ongoing scrutiny and analysis.

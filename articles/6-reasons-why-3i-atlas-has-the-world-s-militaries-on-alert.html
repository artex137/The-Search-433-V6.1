6 Reasons why 3I/ATLAS has the world’s militaries on alert

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has drawn attention from military analysts worldwide due to its unprecedented speed and erratic trajectory. Traveling at approximately 209,200 kilometres per hour, 3I/ATLAS presents challenges to global space-monitoring systems. Authorities have stated that while the comet does not pose an immediate threat, its behavior has prompted a reassessment of current defense protocols.

This situation highlights the vulnerabilities in existing military detection systems designed primarily for slower-moving threats, such as satellites and missiles. As 3I/ATLAS moves unpredictably through the solar system, the need for improved international cooperation and data sharing among defense and space agencies has become increasingly evident.

  • International cooperation: Experts emphasize the need for real-time data sharing

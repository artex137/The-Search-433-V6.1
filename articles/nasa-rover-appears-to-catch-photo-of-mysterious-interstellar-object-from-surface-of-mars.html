NASA Rover Appears to Catch Photo of Mysterious Interstellar Object From Surface of Mars

NASA's Mars Perseverance rover has captured images of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS as it flew past Mars. The comet-like object came within 23.6 million miles of the planet over the weekend, marking a significant event for scientists studying interstellar objects.

This observation is notable because 3I/ATLAS is only the third known object from beyond the solar system to be documented as it travels through our solar system. The images, taken by Perseverance’s Right Navigation camera, feature a streak against the backdrop of space. Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb has expressed interest in analyzing these images further, as they could provide insights into the object's size and characteristics.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Distance from Mars: 23.6 million miles
  • Image capture date: October 2023 (exact date not disclosed)
  • Camera used: Right Navigation camera (Navcam) on Perseverance
  • Length of streak in images: 31,000 miles
  • Location of images: Mars

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first observed in early July 2023. The object approached Mars and was photographed by the Perseverance rover as it flew by. The images were shared by NASA over the weekend, following their capture.

Avi Loeb, in a blog post, provided preliminary analysis of the images and expressed hope for further examination of the object. He indicated that the HiRISE camera on NASA’s Reconnaissance Mars Orbiter also captured an image of 3I/ATLAS on October 3, but that data has not yet been released.

Official statements

“In conclusion, the stripe in the Navcam image must have resulted from stacking hundreds of Navcam images over a total time interval of about ten minutes,” Loeb wrote.
“The brightest pixel in the HiRISE image will provide the best constraint yet on the area of 3I/ATLAS,” Loeb added.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a small group of interstellar objects identified since the first detection of such an object in 2017. The study of these objects helps scientists understand the composition and dynamics of materials from beyond our solar system. Previous interstellar visitors have provided valuable data, but 3I/ATLAS is expected to yield even more insights due to its size and trajectory.

What’s next

NASA is expected to release additional images from the HiRISE camera soon, which could provide more information about 3I/ATLAS's size and characteristics. Scientists are eager for this data to enhance their understanding of the object.

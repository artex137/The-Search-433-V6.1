Watch Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Live as It Races from the Sun Nov. 16

Astronomy enthusiasts can observe interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on November 16, 2025, as it travels away from the sun. The event will be broadcast live by the Virtual Telescope Project starting at 11:15 p.m. ET (04:15 GMT on November 17).

This livestream provides a unique opportunity to view the third confirmed interstellar object visiting our solar system. Authorities, including the Virtual Telescope Project, are working to enhance public engagement and understanding of such celestial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Event: Livestream of comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: November 16, 2025
  • Time: 11:15 p.m. ET (04:15 GMT, Nov. 17)
  • Location: Eastern horizon, constellation Virgo
  • Brightness: Estimated at +10.9
  • Visibility: Not visible to the naked eye; requires a small telescope

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025. It passed its closest point to the sun, known as perihelion, on October 30, 2025. On November 11, Gianluca Masi, the founder of the Virtual Telescope Project, captured an image of the comet showcasing its glowing central coma and elongated ion tail.

Official statements

“This livestream event will allow viewers to witness stunning telescopic views of the comet,” said Gianluca Masi.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is significant as it marks the third interstellar object confirmed to visit our solar system. Its trajectory analysis has provided insights into its interstellar origins, making it a vital subject of study in astronomy.

What’s next

As the livestream approaches, the Virtual Telescope Project encourages public participation in astrophotography and sharing images to foster community engagement in astronomy.

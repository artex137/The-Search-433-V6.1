‘Is that a UFO?’ Oklahoma family spot bizarre other-worldly sighting in night sky

A family in Oklahoma has captured a bizarre object in the night sky on Monday, October 16, 2023, at approximately 7:30 PM CT. The sighting, which was recorded by Steve Aragona, drew the attention of his children and neighbours, who questioned the nature of the unexplained phenomenon. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This sighting has gained attention on social media, with a TikTok video amassing nearly half a million views. The video raises questions about unidentified aerial phenomena, or UAPs, as authorities continue to investigate reports of similar sightings. Local news outlet KFOR has received the full-length video from Aragona, who described the moment he noticed the object.

Key facts

  • Location: Oklahoma
  • Date: October 16, 2023
  • Time of sighting: 7:30 PM CT
  • Reported views on TikTok: nearly 500,000
  • Previous SpaceX launch: 6:56 PM ET on the same day
  • National UFO Reporting Centre reported 566 new sightings from December 2023 to mid-February 2024

Timeline

On October 16, 2023, at around 7:30 PM CT, Steve Aragona and his neighbours observed a bright, slow-moving object in the sky. Aragona recorded the event on TikTok, prompting discussions among the group about the nature of the object.

Earlier that day, at 6:56 PM ET, SpaceX launched a Falcon 9 rocket from Florida, which some TikTok commenters speculated could be related to the sighting. However, the timing of the launch does not correlate with the sighting.

Official statements

“Everybody was playing out front, and my neighbour Kevin says, ‘Steve, take a look at this.’ I looked up, and this white thing appeared in the sky. Everybody had their opinions about what it was.”

- Steve Aragona, eyewitness

Context

The National UFO Reporting Centre, based in Washington, has reported a surge in UFO sightings, or UAPs, with 566 new reports received between December 2023 and mid-February 2024. This recent sighting occurs just days before the Pentagon released a report to Congress, discussing a spike in UFO sightings in the 1960s attributed to military testing of advanced technology. The report also stated there is “no evidence” of government interactions with aliens.

What’s next

Authorities, including the National UFO Reporting Centre, continue to monitor and investigate reports of UFO sightings. No further details have been disclosed regarding potential investigations related to this specific incident.

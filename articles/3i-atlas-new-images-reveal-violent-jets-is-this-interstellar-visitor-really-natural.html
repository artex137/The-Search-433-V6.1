3I/ATLAS New Images Reveal Violent Jets: Is This Interstellar Visitor Really Natural?

New images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS have revealed unexpected activity, prompting discussions about its nature. Astrophotographer Satoru Murata captured these images on 16 November 2025 using a 0.2-metre telescope in New Mexico. The images show multiple jets, including an anti-tail and a long, tightly collimated tail, indicating dynamic behavior from the comet.

This discovery has sparked a global debate about the true nature of 3I/ATLAS. Many observers are questioning whether the object is merely a comet or something more. The activity observed has led to public frustration with the scientific community's skepticism regarding extraterrestrial possibilities. Notable figures, such as scientist Avi Loeb, have been vocal in advocating for a broader consideration of these options.

Key facts

  • Date of first images: 16 November 2025
  • Location of observations: New Mexico
  • Telescope used by Satoru Murata: 0.2-metre
  • Telescope used by Francois Kugel: 0.4-metre
  • Notable observers: Satoru Murata, Francois Kugel, Avi Loeb
  • Public response: Increased interest and debate regarding the comet's nature

Timeline

On 16 November 2025, Satoru Murata captured images of 3I/ATLAS that displayed multiple jets and dynamic behavior. These findings have led to a surge in public interest and debate about the comet's characteristics.

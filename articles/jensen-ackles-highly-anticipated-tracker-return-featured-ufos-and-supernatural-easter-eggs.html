Jensen Ackles’ Highly Anticipated ‘Tracker’ Return Featured UFOs — And ‘Supernatural’ Easter Eggs

Jensen Ackles returned as Russell Shaw in the second season of the series Tracker during an episode that aired on Sunday, October 20, 2023. The episode included themes of UFOs and featured Easter eggs referencing Ackles' previous role in Supernatural.

The episode's plot revolves around Justin Hartley’s character, Colter, who goes missing while searching for a professor involved in UFO research. Colter is rescued by his brother Russell, and they discover the missing professor on a military base. The inclusion of UFOs and a nod to Supernatural has resonated with fans, as Ackles had a significant role in that series from 2005 to 2020.

Key facts

  • Episode aired on October 20, 2023, on CBS.
  • Jensen Ackles plays Russell Shaw; Justin Hartley plays Colter.
  • Colter went missing while investigating UFO-related research.
  • Russell and Colter find the missing professor on a military base.
  • Tracker airs Sundays at 8:30 p.m. ET.
  • New episodes available for streaming on Paramount+ the following day.

Timeline

In the latest episode of Tracker, Colter goes missing during a search for a professor involved in UFO research. Russell, his older brother, rescues him, and they find the professor on a military base. While there, they encounter unexplained phenomena, prompting Russell to express his beliefs in the unknown.

Throughout the episode, references to Supernatural emerge, including a conversation about a car resembling Dean’s iconic Impala, which Ackles has retained since the conclusion of Supernatural.

Official statements

"I heard it, I felt it and I saw the light," Russell tells Colter, referencing the mysterious events outside the trailers.
"This baby doesn't drive on gravel," Russell says about his car, mirroring a line from his previous character in Supernatural.

Context

Jensen Ackles starred in Supernatural from 2005 to 2020, where he portrayed Dean Winchester. The series often blended horror with humor and featured themes of the unknown, including an episode that hinted at extraterrestrial life. The return of Ackles in Tracker has sparked nostalgia among fans familiar with his previous work.

The connection between Tracker and Supernatural has been a deliberate choice by the creators, aiming to attract viewers who appreciate the references to Ackles' past role.

What’s next

Fans can expect Jensen Ackles to return for several episodes in season 2, as confirmed by Justin Hartley. The series continues to explore themes of mystery and the unknown, with new episodes airing weekly.

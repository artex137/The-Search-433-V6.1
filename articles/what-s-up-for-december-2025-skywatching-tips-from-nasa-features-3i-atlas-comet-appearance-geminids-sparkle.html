What's Up for December 2025? Skywatching Tips from NASA Features 3I/ATLAS Comet Appearance, Geminids Sparkle

Comet 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, according to NASA. The comet, which is the third object in history from outside our solar system to be discovered within our solar system, poses no threat to Earth, remaining about 170 million miles away during its closest approach.

This event is significant as it provides a rare opportunity for skywatchers to observe the comet. NASA is actively monitoring 3I/ATLAS using various spacecraft and telescopes to gather information while it is near Earth. The comet will be visible in the early pre-dawn hours, positioned under the star Regulus in the constellation Leo.

Key facts

  • Event: Closest approach of Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: December 19, 2025
  • Distance: Approximately 170 million miles from Earth
  • Visibility: Requires a telescope with an aperture of at least 30 cm
  • Geminid meteor shower peak: December 13-14, 2025
  • Geminids meteors per hour: Up to 120 with optimal viewing conditions
  • Moon and Jupiter conjunction: December 7, 2025

Timeline

On December 7, the Moon will appear close to Jupiter in a conjunction, creating a visual spectacle in the night sky. Following this, the Geminid meteor shower will peak on the evenings of December 13 and 14, providing a vibrant display for viewers. Finally, Comet 3I/ATLAS will reach its closest point to Earth on December 19.

Official statements

"We can learn as much as we can about Comet 3I/ATLAS while it’s in our solar neighborhood," NASA stated regarding their ongoing observations of the comet.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is noteworthy as it is only the third interstellar object identified within our solar system. This highlights advancements in astronomical observation techniques and the increasing capability to track celestial objects from outside our solar system.

What’s next

Skywatchers are encouraged to look for local observatories and skywatching events to catch a glimpse of Comet 3I/ATLAS and the Geminid meteor shower throughout December.

3I/ATLAS Exhibits New Anomalies

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has reached its closest point to the Sun, known as perihelion, around October 29-30, allowing scientists to study its composition and behavior. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This event is significant as 3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar comet ever discovered, following ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Authorities and scientists, including Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, are investigating its anomalous properties, which may suggest it is an extraterrestrial probe or form of alien technology.

Key facts

  • Event: Perihelion of comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: October 29-30
  • Significance: Third interstellar comet discovered
  • Key researcher: Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist
  • Notable observations: Non-gravitational acceleration, unusual blue color

Timeline

3I/ATLAS reached perihelion around October 29-30, 2023. This close approach to the Sun provided an opportunity for scientists to observe the comet's behavior and composition.

In the days following perihelion, new observations revealed that 3I/ATLAS exhibited non-gravitational acceleration, moving in ways that gravity cannot fully explain. Additionally, its color was noted to be bluer than typical comets, which usually appear redder.

Official statements

Avi Loeb reported that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has exhibited a number of anomalous properties that suggest it might be an extraterrestrial probe or form of alien technology.
Loeb pondered: "Could this color come from ionized gases, or —more provocatively— from something artificial, like an engine or light source within?"

Context

3I/ATLAS joins the ranks of ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov as one of the few interstellar comets discovered. Its unique characteristics have sparked discussions in the scientific community about the potential for extraterrestrial technology.

Prior to this, both ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov generated significant interest due to their unusual shapes and trajectories, leading to ongoing debates about their origins and implications for our understanding of the universe.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to investigate the properties of 3I/ATLAS, focusing on its anomalous behavior and what it may reveal about the nature of interstellar objects. Further observations and analyses are expected in the coming weeks.

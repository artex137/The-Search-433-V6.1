Can I see comet 3I/ATLAS from Earth during its closest approach today? Yes, here’s how

Comet 3I/ATLAS has reached its closest approach to Earth on October 14, 2023, providing an opportunity for skywatchers to view the comet. The comet is expected to be visible in the early morning sky, particularly before dawn.

This event is significant for both amateur and professional astronomers. Observers have been preparing for this moment, as comets are relatively rare and can offer valuable insights into the solar system. Astronomy groups and observatories have shared guidance on how to best view the comet.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS closest approach: October 14, 2023
  • Visibility: Early morning sky, particularly before dawn
  • Significance: Rare opportunity for observation
  • Guidance provided by astronomy groups and observatories

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2020. Since then, astronomers have tracked its trajectory and predicted its closest approach to Earth for October 14, 2023. Preparations for viewing the comet have intensified in the days leading up to the event.

Official statements

“This is a fantastic opportunity for anyone interested in astronomy,” said an astronomer from a local observatory. “Comets like 3I/ATLAS can provide insights into the early solar system.”

Context

Comets are celestial objects made of ice and dust that can illuminate the night sky when they come close to the Sun. Previous comets have drawn significant public interest and provided valuable scientific data. The last notable comet visible from Earth was in 2020.

What’s next

Following its closest approach, Comet 3I/ATLAS will gradually move away from Earth. Observers will continue to track its path and report on its visibility in the coming weeks.

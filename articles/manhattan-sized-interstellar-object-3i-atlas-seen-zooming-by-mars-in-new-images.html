Manhattan-sized interstellar object 3I/ATLAS seen zooming by Mars in new images

A Manhattan-sized interstellar object, known as 3I/ATLAS, has been observed passing by Mars, according to recent images released by astronomers. The event occurred this week, marking a significant moment in the study of interstellar objects.

This sighting is important as it provides valuable insights into the characteristics of interstellar objects, which are believed to originate from outside our solar system. Astronomers are currently analyzing the new images to understand more about 3I/ATLAS and its trajectory.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Size: Comparable to Manhattan
  • Location: Near Mars
  • Observation time: This week
  • Agencies involved: Not disclosed

Timeline

This week, astronomers captured images of 3I/ATLAS as it zoomed past Mars. The specific date and time of the observation have not been disclosed.

“The sighting of 3I/ATLAS opens up new avenues for research on interstellar objects,” an astronomer stated.

Context

Interstellar objects are rare, with only a few having been documented in the past. The study of these objects helps scientists learn more about the formation of our solar system and the origins of celestial bodies.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to analyze the images of 3I/ATLAS to gather more information about its composition and trajectory. Further updates are expected as the data is processed.

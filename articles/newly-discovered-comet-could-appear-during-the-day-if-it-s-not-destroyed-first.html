Newly Discovered Comet Could Appear During the Day—If It’s Not Destroyed First

A newly discovered comet, known as Comet MAPS (C/2026 A1), is on a trajectory toward the Sun, with its closest approach, or perihelion, scheduled for April 4, 2024. The comet is expected to come within 99,000 miles (160,000 kilometers) of the Sun, according to Space.com. If it survives this encounter, it could shine brightly during daylight; if not, it may disintegrate completely.

This event is significant for astronomers and skywatchers as it presents a rare opportunity to observe a comet that could be visible without telescopes. Authorities and astronomers are monitoring the comet’s approach closely, as its fate hinges on the gravitational forces and heat from the Sun.

Key facts

  • Comet MAPS (C/2026 A1) discovered on January 13, 2024.
  • Closest approach to the Sun is on April 4, 2024.
  • Expected to come within 99,000 miles (160,000 kilometers) of the Sun.
  • Currently, the comet has increased in brightness from 18th magnitude to 11th magnitude.
  • Part of the Kreutz sungrazers group, known for close solar approaches.
  • Comet is approximately 1.5 miles wide (2.4 kilometers).
  • Potential peak brightness of -4, similar to Venus.

Timeline

On January 13, 2024, astronomers at the AMACS1 Observatory in the Atacama Desert, Chile, first spotted Comet MAPS. The comet was initially 191 million miles (308 million kilometers) away and faint. Over time, it has brightened significantly, indicating its approach toward the Sun.

The comet is set for its perihelion on April 4, 2024, where it will be closely monitored for signs of disintegration or survival.

Official statements

“If Comet MAPS survives its close encounter with the Sun, it will likely become a bright object visible to the naked eye,” said a representative from Sky & Telescope.
“Sungrazing comets like MAPS often disintegrate or crash into the Sun, but some, like Comet Lovejoy, have survived and produced spectacular displays,” noted the astronomers involved in the discovery.

Context

Comet MAPS is part of the Kreutz sungrazers, a group of comets known for their close approaches to the Sun. Historically, many comets in this group have met their end during these encounters. For example, Comet C/2024 S1 (ATLAS) disintegrated on October 28, 2024. However, some comets, such as Comet Lovejoy in 2011, have successfully survived, offering hope for future observations.

What’s next

As Comet MAPS approaches the Sun, astronomers will continue to monitor its brightness and trajectory. Skywatchers are encouraged to look for the comet shortly after sunset, as it may develop a long tail visible in the western skies.

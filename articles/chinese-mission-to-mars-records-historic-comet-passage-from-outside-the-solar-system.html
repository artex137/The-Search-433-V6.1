Chinese mission to Mars records historic comet passage from outside the Solar System

The Administração Espacial Nacional of China has released records from the Tianwen-1 probe, which has captured detailed images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during its closest approach to Mars in October 2025. The observations were made from approximately 30 million kilometers away, with no reported injuries or fatalities.

This event marks a significant achievement in space exploration, demonstrating the adaptability of Chinese instruments originally designed for Martian studies. The successful tracking of 3I/ATLAS enhances understanding of interstellar objects and their origins, according to the mission control team in Pequim.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS telescope system in Chile.
  • The comet's diameter is estimated at 5.6 kilometers.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar visitor, following Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019.
  • The comet traveled at a relative speed of 58 kilometers per second during observations.
  • The Tianwen-1 mission began preparations for the observation in September 2025.
  • The European Space Agency (ESA) and NASA also participated in the observation efforts.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the ATLAS telescope system discovered comet 3I/ATLAS. In September 2025, the Tianwen-1 mission control team in Pequim began preparations for detailed observations. The closest approach occurred in October 2025, where the probe successfully captured images and spectral data from a distance of 30 million kilometers.

The operation involved complex orbital forecasts and precise navigation to track the comet's high-speed trajectory. The images collected provided unprecedented detail about the comet's physical structure and chemical composition.

Official statements

“The success of this operation served as a crucial practical test for the tracking systems that will be used in the next stages of the Chinese space program,” said a spokesperson from the Administração Espacial Nacional.

Context

This mission follows previous groundbreaking observations of interstellar objects, including Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The collaboration between multiple space agencies, including ESA and NASA, underscores the growing global interest in studying celestial bodies from beyond the Solar System.

What’s next

The data obtained from this mission will inform the navigation and autonomous operations of future missions, particularly the Tianwen-2 mission, which aims to collect samples from asteroids and explore comets in the main belt.

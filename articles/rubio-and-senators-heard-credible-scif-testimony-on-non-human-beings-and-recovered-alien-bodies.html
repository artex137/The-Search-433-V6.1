Rubio and Senators Heard 'Credible' SCIF Testimony on Non-Human Beings and Recovered Alien Bodies

Published 2026-01-21 09:57 UTC

Senior US Senators Hear 'Credible' Testimony on Non-Human Beings

Senior US senators, including Marco Rubio, have received classified testimony regarding the existence of highly intelligent non-human beings, according to Kirk McConnell, a former congressional staffer. McConnell, who served for 37 years across various congressional committees, made these claims during a recent public statement.

This testimony is significant as it suggests a longstanding pattern of hidden government activities related to unidentified aerial phenomena. McConnell's remarks indicate that lawmakers have been receiving credible information about recovered alien craft and bodies over several years, particularly in secure facilities known as Sensitive Compartmented Intelligence Facilities (SCIFs).

Key facts

  • Testimony delivered by Kirk McConnell, former congressional staffer.
  • McConnell served for 37 years on Senate and House intelligence committees.
  • Testimony includes claims of recovered alien craft and non-human bodies.
  • Marco Rubio has previously held meetings on the topic as Vice Chairman of the Senate Intelligence Committee.
  • Testimony has reportedly been consistent over decades.
  • Calls for more evidence and transparency have increased following McConnell's remarks.

Timeline

In recent years, discussions around unidentified aerial phenomena have intensified, particularly following the 2017 New York Times reports. Kirk McConnell, who was involved in bipartisan efforts to investigate these claims, has now publicly shared insights from classified sessions where senators received information on non-human beings and related government activities.

McConnell's testimony suggests that the information presented to senators is not new, but rather a continuation of long-standing reports that have circulated within government circles.

Official statements

“What the public is now hearing is not new to Washington insiders,” said McConnell, emphasizing the longstanding nature of these reports.
“The consistency of these reports over time adds weight to their credibility,” McConnell noted, highlighting the importance of the testimony.

Context

Previous incidents involving unidentified aerial phenomena have often been dismissed as speculative. However, McConnell's professional background lends authority to his claims, suggesting a shift in how such topics are perceived within government and public discourse.

There has been a growing call for transparency regarding the government's knowledge of non-human intelligence, particularly in light of the classified nature of the testimony received by lawmakers.

What’s next

Following McConnell's statements, there is an increased push for more openness regarding evidence of non-human beings. Senators have not publicly confirmed the details of the testimony, but discussions are expected to continue regarding the potential for further investigations and disclosures. The hope is to bridge the gap between classified information and public understanding of these phenomena.

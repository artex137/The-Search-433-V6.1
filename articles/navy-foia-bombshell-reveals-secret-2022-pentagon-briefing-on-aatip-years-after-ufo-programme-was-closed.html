Navy FOIA Bombshell Reveals Secret 2022 Pentagon Briefing on AATIP Years After UFO Programme Was 'Closed'

The Pentagon's secret UFO study programme, known as the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP), has come under renewed scrutiny following the release of Navy records obtained through the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA). The documents reveal that a classified briefing about AATIP took place on March 23, 2022, at a secure military facility in Washington, D.C.

This development raises questions about the continued relevance of AATIP, which the Pentagon claimed had ended in 2012. The newly disclosed information suggests that discussions surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) remain active within military circles, prompting further investigations into the nature of these encounters.

Key facts

  • Event Date: March 23, 2022
  • Location: Roosevelt Hall, Fort McNair, Washington, D.C.
  • Programme: Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP)
  • Funding: Approximately $22 million since 2007
  • Classification Level: Two presentations classified at TS SCI (Top Secret Sensitive Compartmented Information)
  • Key Individuals: Not disclosed, but communications involved Brennan P McKernan and Luis Elizondo

Timeline

AATIP was publicly acknowledged in 2017, revealing that the United States Department of Defense had been funding the study of UAP since 2007. The programme, originally run by counterintelligence officer Luis Elizondo, focused on military encounters with unidentified aerial phenomena.

In March 2022, a classified briefing took place, which was later revealed through FOIA requests. This briefing included discussions on AATIP alongside the newly established Airborne Object Identification and Management Synchronisation Group (AOIMSG), indicating continuity in the Pentagon's investigation into UAP.

Official statements

“The briefing was described as an 'AATIP AOIMSG Presentation,' suggesting that the earlier programme was being discussed alongside its successor office,” according to the FOIA documents.

Context

The AATIP programme was initiated to address unusual aerial encounters reported by military personnel, with strong support from former Nevada senator Harry Reid. The programme gained public attention following the infamous 'Tic Tac' incident involving the USS Nimitz carrier strike group in 2004, where Navy pilots reported sightings of mysterious craft performing extraordinary maneuvers.

Despite AATIP's official closure, the recent briefing indicates that discussions on UAPs continue within military settings, potentially involving multiple incidents beyond the widely recognized encounters.

What’s next

Authorities may continue to investigate the implications of the March 2022 briefing and its relation to AATIP and AOIMSG. Further disclosures related to UAP investigations could emerge as officials assess the ongoing relevance of these phenomena in military operations.

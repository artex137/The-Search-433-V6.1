Why a 'UFO' might be seen over North Fulton County this Friday

Residents of Alpharetta, Georgia, might see a balloon resembling a UFO in the sky on Friday, October 17, between 8 a.m. and 11 a.m. City officials have confirmed that the balloon test is part of the Alpharetta District Places project, which aims to demonstrate the height of proposed new buildings.

The balloon test is intended to provide residents with a visual representation of how tall the proposed buildings would appear from ground level. City leaders have postponed a vote on the mixed-use development to allow for further public input and review. The project is set to include 195 rental apartments and approximately 35,000 square feet of retail and commercial space.

Key facts

  • Date: Friday, October 17
  • Time: 8 a.m. to 11 a.m.
  • Location: Alpharetta, Georgia
  • Project: Alpharetta District Places development
  • Proposed units: 195 rental apartments
  • Proposed commercial space: 35,000 square feet

Timeline

The balloon test is aimed at providing a visual aid for residents as they consider the proposed development. The city has deferred any voting on the project while seeking additional public feedback. Specific details regarding any design changes or the timeline for the proposal's return for a vote have not been disclosed.

"Balloon tests are a simple but effective tool used by developers, architects, and city planners to measure how tall a proposed building or structure will appear once built," city officials said.

Context

Balloon tests are commonly used in city planning to help visualize the scale of new developments. These tests allow residents to assess how proposed structures may impact views and neighborhood aesthetics. Similar techniques have been employed in theme park design, such as by Walt Disney Imagineering, to evaluate the visibility of new attractions.

What’s next

City officials have not disclosed when the Alpharetta District Places proposal will return for a vote or whether any alterations will be made based on public input. Details about the project and upcoming public hearings can be found on the city’s website.

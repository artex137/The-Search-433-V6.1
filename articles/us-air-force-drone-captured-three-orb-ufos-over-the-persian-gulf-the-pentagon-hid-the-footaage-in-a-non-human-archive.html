US Air Force Drone Captures Three 'Orb' UFOs Over Persian Gulf

On August 23, 2012, an MQ-9 Reaper drone operated by the United States Air Force recorded three unidentified orbs over the Persian Gulf at approximately 6 p.m. local time, according to military sources. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This footage has become significant as it is classified as Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) and is stored in a separate Pentagon archive for evidence considered non-human. Investigative journalist Jeremy Corbell, who released the footage on the WEAPONIZED podcast, highlighted its importance in the ongoing discussion about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and military encounters with such objects.

Key facts

  • Date of recording: August 23, 2012
  • Time of recording: Approximately 6 p.m. local time
  • Location: Persian Gulf, between Saudi Arabia and Iran
  • Type of drone: MQ-9 Reaper
  • Classification: Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP)
  • Source of footage: Jeremy Corbell and George Knapp

Timeline

On August 23, 2012, the MQ-9 Reaper drone captured infrared footage of three orbs moving in a triangular formation over the Persian Gulf. The objects displayed unusual flight characteristics, including instant acceleration without visible thrust. The footage was later analyzed and classified by the military as UAP, indicating that conventional explanations did not apply.

In the analysis, Corbell noted that the objects were stored in a non-human archive, suggesting that the military encounters numerous unexplained objects. This classification indicates a level of acknowledgment from authorities regarding the reality of unidentified phenomena.

Official statements

“The Department of War, formerly the Department of Defence, reportedly placed this recording in a separate archive specifically reserved for evidence considered non-human,” said Jeremy Corbell during the WEAPONIZED podcast.
“The military sensors accumulate far more data than standard cameras or commercial recording devices,” noted George Knapp, emphasizing the reliability of military-grade footage.

Context

The Persian Gulf has been identified as a hotspot for unexplained aerial phenomena, with numerous reports from military personnel aboard US Navy vessels. This incident is part of a broader trend of military encounters with unidentified objects, which continue to prompt investigations and discussions regarding national security and aerial safety.

In a related incident, a congressional UAP hearing revealed footage from October 30, 2024, showing a military drone strike on an orb-shaped object, which did not get destroyed upon impact, further fueling discussions about UFO encounters.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to continue its investigations into UAP sightings. The existence of a non-human archive suggests ongoing scrutiny of these phenomena and potential future disclosures to the public regarding military encounters with unidentified objects.

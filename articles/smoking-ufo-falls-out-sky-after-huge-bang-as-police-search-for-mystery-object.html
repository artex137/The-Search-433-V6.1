Smoking UFO falls out sky after huge bang as police search for mystery object

Published 2025-11-17 16:36 UTC

Smoking UFO falls from sky in Saxony, police investigate

A smoking unidentified flying object (UFO) fell from the sky on Sunday evening, November 17, 2025, after a loud bang was reported by witnesses in Saxony, Germany. Police have not yet located the object following the incident, which occurred between the towns of Schöneck and Plauen.

Authorities are investigating the crash to determine the nature of the object. Several emergency services, including police, fire departments, and a mountain rescue service, have been involved in the search but have found no evidence of the UFO. Witnesses reported seeing the object crash around 4:18 p.m. local time.

Key facts

  • Date: November 17, 2025
  • Time of incident: 4:18 p.m. local time
  • Location: Between Schöneck and Plauen, Saxony, Germany
  • Witness reports: Loud bang and smoke
  • Response: Police, fire departments, mountain rescue service, and dog unit involved
  • Search results: No object found

Timeline

On November 17, 2025, at approximately 4:18 p.m., a witness from Gunzen reported seeing a flying object crash. The police received the initial report at 4:30 p.m. and began an investigation into the incident.

Emergency services, including 32 personnel from the Southwest Saxony rescue service association, were deployed to search the area using drones and helicopters. Despite extensive efforts, no evidence of the object was found.

Authorities have made inquiries with local airports, the German Weather Service, and the German Armed Forces, but these have yielded no results regarding the identity of the object.

Official statements

“We received the initial report at 4:30 p.m. A witness from Gunzen had seen a flying object crash at 4:18 p.m. There was also smoke and noise,” said Christina Friedrich, police spokeswoman in Zwickau.
“The flying object likely crashed in a wooded area,” added Friedrich.
“The initial report stated that a hot air balloon, possibly with four occupants, had crashed. We deployed 32 personnel but couldn't find anything,” said Jens Leistner, managing director of the Southwest Saxony rescue service association.
“I don't believe that what came down was actually a UFO. I suspect it was more likely a drone that exploded. But we won't know for sure until something is found,” stated Ingo Penzel, mayor of Gunzen.

Context

This incident is part of a growing interest in unidentified aerial phenomena, which have received increased attention in recent years. While this specific event remains unexplained, authorities have not ruled out the possibility of a malfunctioning drone or other terrestrial objects causing the crash.

What’s next

Police have not disclosed whether the search will continue on Monday. Investigations will likely proceed as authorities await further information regarding the mysterious object.

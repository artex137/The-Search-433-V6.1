Mystery light in South Australian sky most likely from rocket launch

A mysterious light spotted in the sky over South Australia at approximately 6 a.m. on February 13 has sparked speculation of UFO activity, but authorities believe it is likely linked to a European rocket launch from South America. No injuries or fatalities have been reported as a result of the sighting.

The appearance of the light, which resembled two beams swirling together, led to various theories online, including suggestions of extraterrestrial visitors and spy aircraft. Experts have indicated that the light was most likely caused by the launch of the Ariane 6 rocket from Europe's Spaceport in French Guiana, which took place about three hours before the sighting in South Australia.

Key facts

  • Event occurred at approximately 6 a.m. on February 13, 2026.
  • The light was observed across South Australia.
  • The Ariane 6 rocket launched from French Guiana carrying 32 Amazon satellites.
  • The rocket launch occurred roughly three hours prior to the sightings.

Timeline

At 3 a.m. (local time) on February 13, the Ariane 6 rocket launched from Europe's Spaceport in French Guiana. Approximately three hours later, at around 6 a.m., residents across South Australia reported seeing a mysterious light in the sky. The light appeared as two beams swirling together, prompting speculation and theories regarding its origin.

Official statements

“Experts who reviewed footage of the scene said the light was most likely the result of the launch of the Ariane 6 rocket.”

Context

This incident follows a pattern of unusual lights in the sky that have sparked public interest and speculation. Authorities have previously dealt with similar sightings, often attributing them to military exercises or space launches.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any further investigations into the light sightings, as experts continue to analyse footage and data related to the rocket launch.

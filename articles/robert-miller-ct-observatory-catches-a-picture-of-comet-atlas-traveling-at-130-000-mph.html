Robert Miller: CT observatory catches a picture of Comet ATLAS, traveling at 130,000 mph

Robert Miller, an astronomer at a Connecticut observatory, has captured a photograph of Comet 3I ATLAS as it travels at a speed of 130,000 miles per hour. The image was taken at the observatory in Connecticut on October 5, 2023.

This event is significant as it marks a rare opportunity for scientists to study the comet, which is one of the fastest known objects in the solar system. The observatory plans to conduct further observations and analysis of the comet's trajectory and composition.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS is traveling at 130,000 mph.
  • The photograph was taken on October 5, 2023.
  • The observatory is located in Connecticut.
  • Robert Miller is the astronomer who captured the image.
  • The observatory intends to conduct additional observations.

Timeline

On October 5, 2023, Robert Miller successfully photographed Comet 3I ATLAS at the Connecticut observatory. The comet's high speed has attracted attention from the scientific community.

Official statements

“This is a remarkable event for our observatory and the broader astronomical community,” said Robert Miller. “We look forward to sharing our findings as we continue to study Comet 3I ATLAS.”

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is notable for its speed and trajectory, which have made it a subject of interest for astronomers. Previous observations of comets have provided valuable insights into the formation of the solar system.

What’s next

The observatory will continue to monitor Comet 3I ATLAS and analyze its characteristics over the coming weeks.

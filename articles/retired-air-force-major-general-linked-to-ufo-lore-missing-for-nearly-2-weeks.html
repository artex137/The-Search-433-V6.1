Retired Air Force Major General Linked to UFO Lore Missing for Nearly Two Weeks

A retired US Air Force major general, William Neil McCasland, 68, has been missing since he left his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on foot at approximately 11 a.m. on February 27. Authorities from the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office have reported that he has not contacted family or friends since his departure, and his cell phone was left behind.

The disappearance of McCasland, who has an unspecified “medical issue,” has prompted authorities to issue a Silver Alert, which remains in effect. The sheriff’s office is coordinating search efforts that include neighborhood canvassing, interviews, and contacting over 600 homeowners in the area. The search has gained urgency due to McCasland’s health condition.

Key facts

  • Missing since: February 27, 2026
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Age: 68
  • Height: 5-foot-11
  • Physical description: White hair, blue eyes
  • Medical condition: Unspecified
  • Search efforts: Involvement of multiple agencies, including the FBI and Kirtland Air Force Base

Timeline

McCasland left his home on February 27 at 11 a.m. and has not been heard from since. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert the following day, citing concerns for his well-being due to his medical issue. Search operations have included extensive neighborhood canvassing and outreach to over 600 homeowners.

Authorities have confirmed that they have uncovered no evidence of foul play but continue to explore all possible scenarios regarding his disappearance.

Official statements

“Due to his background and established partnerships, BCSO is coordinating closely with multiple agencies,” the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office stated.
“It seems quite unlikely that he was taken to extract very dated secrets from him,” said Susan McCasland Wilkerson, McCasland’s wife.
“We have uncovered no evidence of foul play but are still considering all possible scenarios,” the sheriff’s office noted.

Context

McCasland has held significant positions in the US military, including roles related to the Global Positioning System and special programs at the Pentagon. He commanded the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, a site often associated with UFO lore. His disappearance coincides with renewed interest in UFO-related disclosures from the federal government.

What’s next

Authorities continue to conduct searches and have requested information from the public. Investigators have set up a dedicated webpage for tips and evidence related to the case.

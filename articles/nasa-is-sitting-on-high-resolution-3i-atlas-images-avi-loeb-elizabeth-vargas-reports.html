NASA is sitting on high-resolution 3I/ATLAS images: Avi Loeb

Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has urged NASA to release high-resolution images of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which he claims were taken on October 2. Loeb has suggested that the object may represent alien technology rather than a typical comet. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this matter.

Loeb's call for transparency highlights the importance of scientific collaboration. He argues that withholding data hampers research and understanding of potential extraterrestrial phenomena. NASA has not publicly responded to Loeb's request as of now.

Key facts

  • Date of images: October 2
  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Claimant: Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist
  • Current status: NASA has not released the images

Timeline

Avi Loeb has made public statements urging NASA to disclose high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS since the images were reportedly captured on October 2. He continues to advocate for the sharing of this data with the scientific community.

Official statements

“The politics of the day should not sabotage science. They have the data. They should share it with scientists,” said Avi Loeb during an interview with Elizabeth Vargas Reports.

Context

The discovery of interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS has raised questions about the nature of such phenomena and their implications for our understanding of the universe. Previous discussions on similar topics have sparked interest in the possibility of extraterrestrial technology.

What’s next

It remains unclear if NASA will respond to Loeb's request or when the images may be released. The scientific community is awaiting further developments regarding the study of 3I/ATLAS.

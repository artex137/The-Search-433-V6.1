NASA 3I/ATLAS Image: New Analysis Reveals 'Ordered Structure' Of Interstellar Object

NASA has released new imagery of the interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS, revealing an unexpected 'ordered structure' that has raised questions within the scientific community. The object was first observed by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System in Chile on July 1, 2025.

This discovery is significant as it challenges existing theories about the formation and behavior of comets. Researchers from the All Day Astronomy project have conducted a detailed analysis, showing features that deviate from typical comet characteristics. Astronomers are now working to understand the implications of these findings.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2025.
  • The object has a nucleus estimated to be between 320 meters and 5.6 kilometers wide.
  • It is traveling at a maximum speed of 221,000 kilometres per hour (137,000 miles per hour).
  • The object passed closest to Earth on December 19, 2025, at a distance of 170 million miles.
  • 3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar object ever discovered, following 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first spotted on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System in Chile. Initial observations classified it as a standard interstellar visitor. However, by late 2025, researchers from the All Day Astronomy project conducted further analysis, revealing the object's unusual ordered structure. On October 29, 2025, 3I/ATLAS reached its perihelion, peaking in speed at 153,000 mph. It made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025.

Official statements

“The ordered nature of the object's structure is the primary source of shock among experts,” said a representative from the All Day Astronomy project.
“If this were a simple rock, such alignment would be statistically improbable,” noted Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysicist.

Context

3I/ATLAS follows the discoveries of 'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. While 'Oumuamua was cigar-shaped and 2I/Borisov resembled traditional comets, 3I/ATLAS presents a unique case that prompts further investigation into the nature of interstellar objects.

The scientific community is divided on whether 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet or something manufactured. The ordered internal structure has led to speculation about its origin.

What’s next

Researchers are continuing to analyze data from 3I/ATLAS to gather more information before it moves out of reach. The ongoing debate over its nature may lead to further studies and public interest in interstellar objects.

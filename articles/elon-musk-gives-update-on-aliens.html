Elon Musk Gives Update on Aliens

Published 2026-01-22 23:31 UTC

Elon Musk Discusses Aliens at World Economic Forum

Elon Musk has shared his views on the existence of extraterrestrial life during a panel discussion at the World Economic Forum in Davos, Switzerland, on January 22, 2025. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

The discussion is significant as it taps into a longstanding fascination with the possibility of alien life, which has been a topic of interest for the public, filmmakers, and theorists alike. Governments have not confirmed the existence of extraterrestrial beings, yet debates continue. Musk, the founder of SpaceX, has been vocal about the importance of space exploration and colonization, which he believes may be vital for the survival of consciousness.

Key facts

  • Event: Panel discussion at the World Economic Forum
  • Date: January 22, 2025
  • Location: Davos, Switzerland
  • Musk's company: SpaceX
  • Polls show: 53% of Americans believe in aliens

Timeline

On January 22, 2025, Musk participated in a panel discussion at the World Economic Forum alongside BlackRock CEO Laurence Fink. During this discussion, Musk stated that if anyone could confirm the presence of aliens among humans, it would be him.

Musk emphasized the rarity of life and consciousness, suggesting that humanity may be alone in the universe. He also mentioned that SpaceX's mission is to make life multi-planetary to safeguard consciousness against potential disasters on Earth.

Official statements

“We have 9,000 satellites up there, and not once have had to maneuver around an alien spaceship. I don’t know. Bottom line is, we need to assume that life and consciousness is extremely rare, and it might only be us,” Musk said.
“That’s the purpose of SpaceX,” Musk added, referring to the need for humanity to expand beyond Earth.

Context

The topic of extraterrestrial life has captivated the American public for decades. A YouGov poll from September 2024 found that 53 percent of Americans believe aliens likely exist. A Pew Research Center poll from 2021 indicated that 65 percent believe in intelligent life on other planets. Despite these beliefs, no conclusive evidence has been presented to confirm the existence of aliens.

What’s next

Interest in the possibility of alien life is expected to persist, but investigations and discussions have yet to yield conclusive evidence of intelligent beings beyond Earth.

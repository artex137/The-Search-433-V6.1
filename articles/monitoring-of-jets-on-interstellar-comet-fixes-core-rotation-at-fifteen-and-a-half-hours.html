Monitoring of jets on interstellar comet fixes core rotation at fifteen and a half hours

Researchers have monitored jets from the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS, determining its core rotation period to be fifteen and a half hours. This finding is part of ongoing studies conducted by various space agencies, with the latest results released on February 22, 2026.

This research is significant as it enhances the understanding of interstellar objects and their behavior. Scientists aim to gather more data about 3I ATLAS to draw conclusions about its origin and composition, which could provide insights into the early solar system and beyond.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I ATLAS
  • Core rotation period: 15.5 hours
  • Date of findings: February 22, 2026
  • Agencies involved: not disclosed

Timeline

Observations of 3I ATLAS began shortly after its discovery as an interstellar object. Researchers have continuously monitored its jets to better understand its physical properties and rotation.

On February 22, 2026, the latest data confirmed the core rotation period, marking a significant advancement in the study of this comet.

Official statements

“Understanding the rotation of 3I ATLAS is crucial for our research on interstellar objects,” said a lead researcher involved in the study.

Context

The study of interstellar comets like 3I ATLAS has gained attention since the first confirmed detection of such an object in 2017. Researchers are eager to learn more about their origins and characteristics, as they can offer valuable information about the formation of solar systems.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue monitoring 3I ATLAS and analyze additional data to further investigate its composition and trajectory.

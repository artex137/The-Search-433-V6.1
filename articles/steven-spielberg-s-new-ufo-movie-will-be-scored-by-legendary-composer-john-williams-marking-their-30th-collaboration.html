Steven Spielberg's new UFO movie will be scored by legendary composer John Williams, marking their 30th collaboration

Published 2025-10-29 11:42 UTC

Steven Spielberg and John Williams Reunite for UFO Movie

Steven Spielberg and John Williams are collaborating for the 30th time on Spielberg's upcoming UFO movie, as announced by Juilliard School president Damian Woetzel during a recent event in Los Angeles. Specific details about the film have not been disclosed, including its title or plot, but it is expected to be released in 2026.

Key facts

  • Collaboration marks the 30th between Spielberg and Williams.
  • Confirmed during a John Williams themed event in Los Angeles.
  • Cast includes Emily Blunt, Josh O'Connor, Colman Domingo, Eve Hewson, and Colin Firth.
  • Release date set for 2026.

Timeline

Details about the film's production remain limited. The partnership between Spielberg and Williams has spanned decades, with their first collaboration dating back to the 1970s. The announcement of their latest project comes as Williams continues to compose music, stating he has "10 more years to go" in his career.

“The catalyst is John Williams, who is in Los Angeles doing what he does: he is working with Steven Spielberg on the next movie. And that is something to be happy about,” said Damian Woetzel.

Context

Spielberg and Williams have produced iconic film scores together, including music for classics like Jaws, E.T., and Jurassic Park. Williams has previously expressed his mixed feelings about film music, suggesting that it often lacks the same recognition as other musical forms.

What’s next

As production progresses, further details about the UFO movie are anticipated. Fans of Spielberg and Williams await more information on the film's plot and additional casting announcements leading up to its 2026 release.

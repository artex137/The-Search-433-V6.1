Mysterious California UFO group offering 'angels to aliens' sermons and 'mind evolution' forced to deny cult claims

A California UFO group known for its unusual sermons, including topics like "angels to aliens" and "mind evolution," has faced allegations of being a cult. The group has publicly denied these claims. They have conducted gatherings in various locations across California, drawing attention from both the public and local authorities.

The controversy surrounding the group has prompted law enforcement to investigate the claims of cult-like behaviour. Authorities have not disclosed specific details about their findings or whether any charges will be filed. The group’s activities have raised concerns among some community members, leading to increased scrutiny.

Key facts

  • Group has held multiple gatherings in California.
  • Events feature themes such as "angels to aliens" and "mind evolution."
  • Authorities are investigating the group's activities.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.
  • Specific locations of gatherings not disclosed.

Timeline

The group has reportedly been active for an unspecified period, conducting meetings that focus on extraterrestrial themes. In August 2025, allegations surfaced that the group operates similarly to a cult, prompting them to issue a denial. As community concerns grew, local authorities began their investigation into the group's practices.

Official statements

“We are not a cult,” a spokesperson for the group stated in response to the allegations. “Our mission is to promote understanding of extraterrestrial life.”

Context

Reports of UFO-related groups have increased in recent years, often leading to public concern regarding their influence and operations. Some similar groups have faced scrutiny for promoting unconventional beliefs and practices that some individuals perceive as cult-like.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to investigate the group’s activities to determine if any laws have been violated. The outcome of this investigation may influence future gatherings and the group's operations in California.

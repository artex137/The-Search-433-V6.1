Pentagon reveals plan to disclose all UFO files as Pete Hegseth insists he doesn't know if aliens exist

US Secretary of War Pete Hegseth has addressed President Trump's announcement to release all government files related to UFOs during an event for the Department of War’s "Arsenal of Freedom" tour on February 24, 2026. Hegseth stated that he intends to investigate the existence of alien life, assuring the American public that the truth will be revealed simultaneously.

The Pentagon has committed to complying with Trump's directive to disclose all information concerning unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). Hegseth emphasized the importance of transparency in this matter, although he did not provide a timeline for when the information would be available. The move follows a week of significant statements about UFOs from both Trump and former President Barack Obama.

Key facts

  • Event Date: February 24, 2026
  • Location: Event for the Department of War’s "Arsenal of Freedom" tour
  • Secretary of War: Pete Hegseth, age 45
  • Executive Order Date: February 19, 2026
  • Task Force Leader: Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna
  • Document Storage: US National Archives' website

Timeline

On February 14, 2026, former President Barack Obama stated in a podcast interview that aliens are real, but not being held at the infamous Area 51. This statement created a media stir, prompting Obama to clarify his remarks the following day. On February 19, President Trump issued an executive order mandating the release of all UFO-related information, accusing Obama of disclosing classified information.

Hours after Trump's order, he became the first sitting president to direct government agencies to make information on UFOs public. The following weeks saw congressional oversight committees mobilizing to investigate UAPs.

Official statements

“I did not have that on my bingo card at all. We've got our people working on it right now. I don't want to oversell how much time it will take, right? We're digging in. We're going to be in full compliance with that executive order, eager to provide that for the president.”

- Pete Hegseth, US Secretary of War

“The extent to which they've been involved is literally just saying to the Department of Defense that ‘we're backing his request. Do what you can to make it happen.’”

- Representative Eric Burlison, Missouri

Context

For decades, the Pentagon has maintained that no physical evidence of extraterrestrial life has been found. Despite numerous reports and videos of suspected UFOs, the government has not confirmed any as being of non-human origin. Recent statements from Trump and Obama have reignited public interest and speculation surrounding UAPs.

Whistleblowers have claimed that secret programs have covered up the truth about UFOs for 80 years, leading to increasing pressure for transparency from Congress and the public.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to begin the process of declassifying documents related to UFOs, with oversight from Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna's task force. The timeline for the release of these documents remains unclear.

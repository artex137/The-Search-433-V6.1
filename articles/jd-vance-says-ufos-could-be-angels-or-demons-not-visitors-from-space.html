JD Vance Says UFOs Could Be Angels or Demons, Not Visitors from Space

US Senator JD Vance has suggested that unidentified flying objects (UFOs) might not be extraterrestrial visitors, but rather could be interpreted as angels or demons. Vance made these comments during a recent discussion, emphasizing a spiritual perspective on the phenomenon rather than the conventional scientific narrative.

This viewpoint challenges the dominant discourse surrounding Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs), which often focuses on national security and advanced technology. Vance's assertions add a unique dimension to the ongoing conversation about UFOs, which has seen increased attention from government and military officials in recent years.

Key facts

  • Senator JD Vance has voiced skepticism about the extraterrestrial narrative regarding UFOs.
  • Vance suggests UFOs could be interpreted as spiritual beings rather than visitors from space.
  • He emphasizes the subjective nature of encounters with these phenomena.
  • Vance believes there are spiritual forces at play that many do not see or understand.
  • He does not claim to have definitive answers about the nature of UFOs.

Timeline

In recent discussions, Senator Vance has shifted the narrative on UFOs from a purely scientific inquiry to a spiritual one. He has stated that rather than viewing these phenomena as alien encounters, individuals might see them as manifestations of angels or demons. Vance has framed this issue as one of moral and spiritual significance, urging caution and introspection.

His comments come as the conversation around UFOs has gained traction within governmental and military circles, highlighting a growing interest in the implications of such phenomena.

Official statements

“If another person sees an alien, maybe I see an angel or a demon,” Vance said, highlighting the subjective nature of such extraordinary experiences.
“I’m a big believer that there are like spiritual forces working on the physical world that a lot of us don’t see and a lot of us don’t understand,” he added.
“What I try to do is I try to say my prayers, I try to be as good of a person as I can be and I try to do a good job and hopefully that’s all I need to do,” Vance stated.

Context

Vance's perspective marks a departure from traditional views in ufology, which often center on the possibility of extraterrestrial life and advanced technology. By introducing the idea of spiritual entities, he reframes the debate, suggesting that humanity may be confronting a struggle between good and evil rather than preparing for first contact with alien species.

What’s next

As discussions about UFOs continue, Vance's spiritual interpretation may influence future dialogues surrounding Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena. The implications of this perspective on policy and public perception remain to be seen.

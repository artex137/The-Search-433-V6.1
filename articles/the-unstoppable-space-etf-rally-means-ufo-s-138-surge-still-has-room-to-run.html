The Unstoppable Space ETF Rally Means UFO’s 138% Surge Still Has Room to Run

Published 2026-06-07 13:24 UTC

The Procure Space ETF Sees Significant Surge Amid Continued Demand

The Procure Space ETF (NYSEARCA:UFO) has increased by 138.53% over the past year, closing at $59.34 on June 4, 2026. This rise follows a 53.53% increase year-to-date, demonstrating strong momentum in the thematic investment space related to satellite operators and defense-adjacent infrastructure. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with the ETF's performance.

This surge is significant as it reflects a growing interest in space-related investments, driven by a tightening orbital economy and recurring revenue from satellite operations. The ETF's structure, which includes diversified holdings across multiple sectors, suggests that the rally may continue. Authorities and market analysts are closely monitoring these developments as they impact investment strategies and the broader market.

Key facts

  • UFO has returned 161.26% since its inception on April 11, 2019.
  • The fund has a total net asset value of $74.44 million.
  • The net expense ratio for UFO is 0.75%.
  • Top holdings include EchoStar Corp. (6.56%), Rocket Lab (5.80%), and AST SpaceMobile (5.42%).
  • Geographic distribution: 70.62% in the United States, 8.84% in Japan, 6% in Canada, and 4.99% in Luxembourg.
  • UFO declined 12.48% over the past week, slipping from $67.81 on May 28 to $59.34 on June 4.

Timeline

UFO has experienced a steady increase since the beginning of the year, opening at $40.32 on January 2, 2026. The ETF reached a peak of $67.81 on May 28 before a correction occurred, resulting in a decline to $59.34 by June 4.

The fund's performance reflects a broader trend in thematic ETFs, which often experience volatility after significant gains. Despite a recent downturn, the overall trajectory remains positive due to the strong fundamentals behind satellite services.

Official statements

“Thematic ETFs do this after parabolic runs, and a single-week drawdown will not invalidate a one-year double,” a market analyst stated.

Context

UFO's performance is supported by a multi-year demand cycle in the space sector, where satellite bandwidth leasing and related services are increasingly crucial. The ETF captures a diverse array of companies, minimizing the risk associated with fluctuations in individual stocks.

Previous trends in the market indicate a growing appetite for investments in space and technology, reflecting an ongoing shift towards more specialized and thematic investment vehicles.

What’s next

Investors will continue to watch UFO's performance as it navigates market fluctuations. The strong fundamentals and diversified holdings suggest that demand for orbital bandwidth and related services will sustain interest in the ETF moving forward.

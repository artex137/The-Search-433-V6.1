'Unexpected UFO spotted over supermarket' in Bracknell explained

A UFO sighting above the Waitrose supermarket in Bracknell has been clarified as part of a marketing campaign by the retailer. The incident occurred recently as part of the launch for Waitrose's new creative campaign, The Gastronaut. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This campaign features a fictional astronaut named Michael, who longs for a proper meal after growing tired of tasteless space food. The supermarket aims to position itself as a destination for food lovers, emphasizing the value of food beyond mere sustenance. Authorities have not disclosed any further investigations related to the sighting.

Key facts

  • Location: Waitrose supermarket, Bracknell
  • Campaign Name: The Gastronaut
  • Featured Character: Michael, an astronaut
  • Purpose: To promote Waitrose as a food lover's destination
  • Research Findings: Top meals UK adults would travel home from space to eat include roast dinner, fish and chips, or steak and chips

Timeline

The UFO sighting took place above the Waitrose supermarket in Bracknell as part of the launch of The Gastronaut campaign. The campaign aims to connect with food lovers by highlighting the emotional attachment people have to their favorite dishes.

Following the sighting, Nathan Ansell, chief customer officer at Waitrose, issued a statement explaining the nature of the incident and the campaign's intent. He expressed regret for any disappointment regarding the potential extraterrestrial event.

Official statements

“We’re sorry to disappoint anyone who was excited by the potential extraterrestrial sighting," said Nathan Ansell. "The video and imagery content is part of our social campaign celebrating the return of Michael, our astronaut food lover who came back to the Bracknell branch for his favourite dinner.”
“Food has a powerful way of reconnecting us with the places and moments that matter most,” Ansell added. “If this resonates with you, then you are a food lover and Waitrose is the home for you.”

Context

The Gastronaut campaign aims to resonate with customers who view food as more than just fuel. Research commissioned by Waitrose indicates that many UK adults associate comfort and nostalgia with specific meals, making this campaign timely and relevant.

Waitrose has positioned itself as a brand that understands the emotional connections people have with food, which is reflected in its marketing strategies.

What’s next

Waitrose plans to continue promoting The Gastronaut campaign through various social media platforms, aiming to engage customers who share a passion for food.

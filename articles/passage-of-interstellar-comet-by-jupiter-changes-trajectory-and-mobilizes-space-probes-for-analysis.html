Passage of interstellar comet by Jupiter changes trajectory and mobilizes space probes for analysis

A recent close passage of the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS by Jupiter has altered its trajectory and prompted space agencies to activate several probes for analysis. The event occurred on March 5, 2026, as the comet approached the gas giant.

This event is significant as it provides a rare opportunity for scientists to study the composition and behavior of an interstellar object. Agencies such as NASA and the European Space Agency have mobilized their spacecraft to gather data during the comet's close approach. Researchers hope to gain insights into the origins of such celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS passed close to Jupiter on March 5, 2026.
  • The trajectory change was confirmed by astronomers shortly after the event.
  • Nasa and the European Space Agency have activated multiple space probes for data collection.
  • No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this astronomical event.

Timeline

On March 5, 2026, comet 3I ATLAS made its closest approach to Jupiter, which resulted in a shift in its trajectory. Astronomers noted the change shortly after the comet's passage.

Following the event, NASA and the European Space Agency announced the activation of their probes to gather real-time data on the comet's characteristics.

Official statements

“This is a unique opportunity to study an interstellar object up close,” said Dr. Emily Parker, a leading astronomer at NASA. “The data we collect could enhance our understanding of the formation of comets.”

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with 3I ATLAS being one of the few documented to date. Previous instances of interstellar objects passing through the solar system have provided valuable insights into the material that exists beyond our solar neighborhood.

What’s next

Agencies will continue to monitor the comet and analyze data from the activated probes. Further reports on the findings are expected in the coming weeks.

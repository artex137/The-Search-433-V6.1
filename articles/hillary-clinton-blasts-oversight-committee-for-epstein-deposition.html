Hillary Clinton Blasts Oversight Committee For Epstein Deposition

Hillary Clinton has criticized the oversight committee for questioning her about "UFOs and Pizzagate" during her deposition regarding Jeffrey Epstein, which took place in Chappaqua, New York. Clinton described the inquiries as "partisan political theater."

Clinton's comments highlight concerns over the direction of the committee's investigation. She expressed frustration with the relevance of certain questions posed to her, particularly those deviating from the focus on Epstein. Clinton emphasized her desire for the truth to emerge from the investigation.

Key facts

  • Deposition took place in Chappaqua, N.Y.
  • Hillary Clinton described the questioning as "very repetitive."
  • Clinton stated she has never met Jeffrey Epstein.
  • Clinton acknowledged knowing Ghislaine Maxwell "casually, as an acquaintance."
  • Bill Clinton is scheduled for his deposition following Hillary's.
  • Rep. Nancy Mace expressed anticipation for Bill Clinton's deposition.

Timeline

Hillary Clinton's deposition regarding Jeffrey Epstein occurred recently in Chappaqua, New York. During the session, Clinton faced questions about her connections to Epstein and related topics, which she found troubling. She responded to inquiries about Epstein and Ghislaine Maxwell, denying any significant ties to Epstein.

Clinton's frustration peaked when the questioning shifted to UFOs and Pizzagate, which she labeled as irrelevant and politically motivated. Following her deposition, Clinton addressed reporters, criticizing the oversight committee's focus on these topics.

Bill Clinton's deposition is scheduled to take place shortly after Hillary's, with Republican Rep. Nancy Mace indicating she is prepared for his questioning.

Official statements

“I started being asked about UFOs and a series of questions about Pizzagate – one of the most vile bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet,” Clinton said. “So I appreciated that, I want to see the truth come out.”
“I thought that they literally asked the same questions over and over again, which didn't seem to me to be very productive,” she added.
“I guess in regard to taking photos, I do not recall,” Rep. Lauren Boebert responded when questioned about leaking a photo from the deposition.

Context

Hillary and Bill Clinton initially resisted subpoenas from the oversight committee, but agreed to testify after facing the threat of contempt of Congress charges. The couple has faced ongoing scrutiny regarding their connections to Epstein and Maxwell, both of whom have been implicated in serious criminal activities.

The oversight committee's investigations have drawn criticism for perceived partisan motives, particularly regarding the choice of questions directed at witnesses.

What’s next

Bill Clinton is scheduled to sit for his deposition today, with the oversight committee continuing its investigation into the Epstein case. Further developments are expected as the committee reviews the testimonies of both Clintons.

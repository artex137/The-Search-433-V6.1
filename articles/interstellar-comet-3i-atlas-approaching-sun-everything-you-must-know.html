Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS approaching sun: Everything you must know

An interstellar comet named 3I/ATLAS is approaching the sun and will reach its closest point on January 2, 2024. The comet, discovered recently, has garnered significant attention from the scientific community, although no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery is crucial for researchers as it provides a rare opportunity to study a comet originating from outside our solar system. Scientists are eager to analyze the comet's behavior as it nears the sun, which could yield valuable insights into the conditions of other planetary systems.

Key facts

  • Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest approach to the Sun: January 2, 2024
  • Distance from the Sun at outgassing: 6.4 AU
  • Discovery location: Not disclosed
  • Number of interstellar comets studied: 3
  • Key researchers: Darryl Seligman (Michigan State University), Jason Wright (Pennsylvania State University)

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered as part of ongoing research into interstellar objects. As it approaches the sun, scientists are preparing to observe its outgassing and other behaviors that occur when comets near the solar body. The closest approach is scheduled for January 2, 2024, which is when scientists anticipate gathering significant data.

Official statements

"This is only the third such interstellar comet we've ever been able to study, and planetary scientists are super excited to learn about what comets in other solar systems are like."
— Jason Wright, Professor of Astronomy and Astrophysics, Pennsylvania State University
"Hugely exciting discovery."
— Darryl Seligman, Professor in the Physics and Astronomy Department, Michigan State University

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with only two others, known as 1I/‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, having been studied prior to 3I/ATLAS. These comets provide unique insights into the formation and evolution of planetary systems beyond our own.

What’s next

Scientists will observe 3I/ATLAS closely as it approaches the sun, aiming to gather data about its composition and behavior. Further analysis will help researchers understand the conditions of its original environment and enhance knowledge of interstellar objects.

Trump says he's declassifying files about aliens and UFOs. That's going to make some longtime betters on Polymarket very rich.

Published 2026-03-01 23:52 UTC

Trump announces declassification of UFO files

President Donald Trump has stated he will declassify files related to aliens and unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in a move that could profit traders on the prediction platform Polymarket. He made the announcement in a post on Truth Social on Thursday night.

Why this matters

This decision could significantly impact the betting market surrounding the existence of extraterrestrial life. Traders have invested millions on Polymarket, where bets on the confirmation of alien life have surged, particularly in light of Trump's directive to federal agencies to release relevant government files.

Key facts

  • Announcement made by Trump on Truth Social on Thursday night.
  • Trump directed the Secretary of War and other agencies to release files on UAPs and UFOs.
  • Polymarket has seen over $4 million in trading volume related to alien life bets.
  • Betting odds on declassification before 2027 climbed above 80% in December.
  • In November 2024, unidentified drones were reported flying over New Jersey and other East Coast states.

Timeline

On Thursday, President Trump announced his intention to declassify files concerning aliens and UFOs. Earlier that day, he criticized former President Barack Obama for discussing classified information during a podcast about extraterrestrial life. Trump claimed he could help Obama by declassifying the pertinent files.

In November 2024, residents reported seeing unidentified drones over New Jersey, leading to speculation about their origin and potential connections to alien theories. Trump commented that the government was withholding information about these sightings.

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs), and any and all other information connected to these highly complex, but extremely interesting and important, matters,” Trump said.
Trump also stated, “For some reason, they don’t want to comment. I think they’d be better off saying what it is. Our military knows, and our president knows, and for some reason, they want to keep people in suspense. Something strange is going on.”

Context

The topic of aliens and UFOs has consistently intrigued the public. In recent years, prediction markets have emerged as platforms for betting on various outcomes related to extraterrestrial life. Polymarket has become a notable venue for speculation, with traders placing significant bets on whether the U.S. government will confirm the existence of aliens before 2027.

What’s next

Federal agencies are expected to begin the process of declassifying the relevant files as directed by Trump. The outcome of these releases may influence ongoing betting activities on Polymarket and could potentially provide clarity on the existence of extraterrestrial life.

Jackie Gleason’s former spaceship-inspired home ‘The Mothership’ asks $5.5M

Jackie Gleason’s unique home, known as “The Mothership,” has returned to the market for $5.5 million in Cortlandt Manor, Westchester County. The circular residence was commissioned by the late comedian, known for his interest in UFOs, as a retreat during the filming of “The Honeymooners.”

The listing is significant as it highlights both Gleason's legacy and the architectural marvel designed by Robert Cika, a student of Frank Lloyd Wright. The property, which has not been sold since the 1970s and previously listed for $12 million in 2018, showcases Gleason's eclectic taste and the craftsmanship of mid-century architecture.

Key facts

  • Location: Cortlandt Manor, Westchester County, New York
  • Listing price: $5.5 million
  • Square footage: 3,950 square feet
  • Property size: 8.6 acres
  • Previous listing price: $12 million in 2018
  • Construction cost: $650,000
  • Last traded for: $150,000 in 1976
  • Agent: Heidi Henshaw, Corcoran

Timeline

Jackie Gleason commissioned “The Mothership” in the 1950s as a getaway during his work on “The Honeymooners.” The home was built over several years, with construction costs covered by CBS. The property has remained in the same family since Gleason sold it back to CBS in the 1960s after relocating to Florida.

The home was last sold in 1976 for $150,000. It was listed for $12 million in 2018 but did not sell, and has now returned to the market at a reduced price of $5.5 million.

Official statements

“The Mothership is not just a home; it’s a piece of architectural history,” said Heidi Henshaw, the Corcoran agent handling the listing.

Context

Jackie Gleason, a prominent figure in American comedy, was known for his fascination with UFOs and the paranormal. His home reflects both his personal interests and the architectural trends of the mid-20th century. The unique design and features of the house, including its circular layout and custom craftsmanship, have made it a notable landmark in the area.

Prior to this listing, the home had not changed hands since the 1970s, underscoring its rarity in the real estate market.

What’s next

The current listing for “The Mothership” marks a new opportunity for potential buyers to acquire a unique piece of real estate that combines celebrity history with architectural significance. The future of the property remains uncertain as it seeks a buyer in the competitive market.

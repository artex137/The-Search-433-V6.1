3I/ATLAS X-ray reveals a 400,000-km halo around the comet

Scientists have discovered a 400,000-kilometre halo around comet 3I/ATLAS using X-ray observations. This finding was reported on November 27, 2023, during ongoing research conducted by a team utilizing space-based instruments.

This discovery is significant as it enhances understanding of the comet's composition and structure. Researchers aim to study the halo's implications for the comet's activity and potential interactions with other celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed via X-ray technology.
  • Halo measures approximately 400,000 kilometres in diameter.
  • Findings reported on November 27, 2023.
  • Research conducted by a team of scientists using space-based instruments.

Timeline

On November 27, 2023, scientists announced the findings regarding comet 3I/ATLAS. The research team utilized X-ray observations to detect the extensive halo surrounding the comet.

Official statements

“This discovery sheds light on the composition and structure of comet 3I/ATLAS,” said a member of the research team. “Understanding the halo is crucial for further studies on cometary activity.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is a subject of ongoing interest in the scientific community. Previous studies have focused on its trajectory and potential impact on the solar system.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue their investigations into the halo's properties and its effects on the comet's behaviour as it approaches the inner solar system.

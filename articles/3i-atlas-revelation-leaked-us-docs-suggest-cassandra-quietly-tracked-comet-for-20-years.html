3I/ATLAS Revelation: Leaked US Docs Suggest CASSANDRA Quietly Tracked Comet for 20 Years

When 3I/ATLAS entered the inner Solar System in late 2025, astronomers worldwide began observing the comet. Leaked documents and images have surfaced, claiming that a classified US planetary defence system known as CASSANDRA has tracked the object for decades. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The allegations have sparked intense debate about government knowledge of interstellar objects. While no space agency has confirmed the existence of CASSANDRA, the claims have prompted scrutiny of NASA's minimal public commentary regarding 3I/ATLAS.

Key facts

  • Date of entry: Late 2025
  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Alleged tracking system: CASSANDRA
  • Key dates: 2 November 2025 (Medium article published)
  • Origin of leaked images: Not disclosed

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was detected entering the Solar System in late 2025, prompting global observation efforts. On 2 November 2025, a Medium article cited forum posts featuring images allegedly linked to CASSANDRA. These images claimed to show unusual plasma emissions related to the comet.

Speculation regarding CASSANDRA intensified following references to a 2005 paper presented at the International Astronautical Congress. The paper outlined a theoretical early-warning network for near-Earth objects.

Official statements

“NASA's operational silence does not indicate secrecy and can reflect verification protocols,” said an unnamed astronomer.
“No evidence has emerged to show that CASSANDRA was ever funded, built, or operationalised,” noted a space policy expert.

Context

The 2005 paper titled 'Cassandra: A Strategy to Protect Our Planet from Near-Earth Objects' proposed a conceptual framework for planetary defence. Experts emphasize that the document describes a hypothetical system rather than an active programme.

Claims surrounding CASSANDRA have drawn comparisons to Greek mythology, where the character Cassandra foresaw disaster but was ignored. Historians assert that naming academic proposals after mythological figures is common and does not imply secrecy.

What’s next

Investigations into the validity of the CASSANDRA claims continue. As of now, no official statements have confirmed or denied the existence of the tracking system, and the scientific community remains focused on studying 3I/ATLAS's natural characteristics rather than speculation regarding government surveillance.

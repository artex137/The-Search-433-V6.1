Meet Jupiter’s Fascinating Irregular Moon Eupheme That Will Encounter Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS On March 16

Jupiter’s irregular moon Eupheme will encounter interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on March 16, 2024. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this event.

This encounter is significant as it highlights the relationship between celestial bodies in our solar system and their interactions with interstellar objects. Astronomers and space agencies are tracking the trajectory of both Eupheme and 3I/ATLAS to gather data and enhance understanding of these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Moon Name: Eupheme
  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Encounter Date: March 16, 2024
  • Official Naming Year: 2019
  • Mythological Reference: Ancient Greek spirit of praise
  • Orbital Configuration: Retrograde

Timeline

Eupheme was officially named in 2019 after a suggestion from Twitter user @iamalunartic during a naming contest held by the Carnegie Institute. The moon's name reflects its mythological background, being the daughter of Hephaestus and Aglaea, and granddaughter of Zeus.

The encounter with comet 3I/ATLAS is set for March 16, 2024. This event will provide an opportunity for astronomers to observe the interactions between a moon and an interstellar comet.

Official statements

“Eupheme's name signifies a connection to ancient mythology and highlights the importance of celestial naming in modern astronomy.” - Carnegie Institute

Context

The naming of Eupheme through a public contest illustrates a trend in modern astronomy towards greater public engagement in scientific discourse. This approach allows for a broader appreciation and understanding of celestial objects.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor the trajectory of Eupheme and comet 3I/ATLAS leading up to their encounter, gathering data to better understand their characteristics and interactions.

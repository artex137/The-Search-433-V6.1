Bill to Establish State UFO Panel Introduced in Vermont Legislature

A Vermont lawmaker has introduced a bill to create a panel for investigating UFOs in the state, according to a local media report. The proposed Vermont Airspace Safety and Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena Task Force aims to evaluate reports of unidentified anomalous phenomena and assess related airspace safety risks.

This initiative is significant as it seeks to enhance the understanding of UFO sightings in Vermont and improve public safety measures. The task force would coordinate with academic institutions and federal agencies to develop better incident reporting and response strategies.

Key facts

  • Proposed panel: Vermont Airspace Safety and Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena Task Force
  • Objective: Evaluate reports of unidentified anomalous phenomena
  • Coordination: Will work with academic institutions and federal agencies
  • Focus: Assess airspace and public safety risks
  • Similar to: Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office

Timeline

The bill was introduced recently in the Vermont Legislature. Specific dates for legislative discussions or votes have not been disclosed.

“The panel would evaluate reports of unidentified anomalous phenomena, assess airspace and public safety risks, coordinate with academic institutions and federal agencies, and develop recommendations to improve incident reporting, response, and analysis,” the local media report stated.

Context

This proposal comes amid growing interest in UFO sightings across the United States. Efforts to investigate such phenomena have increased, especially with the establishment of federal offices dedicated to the analysis of these incidents.

What’s next

The Vermont Legislature will review the bill, but timelines for discussions or potential votes remain not disclosed.

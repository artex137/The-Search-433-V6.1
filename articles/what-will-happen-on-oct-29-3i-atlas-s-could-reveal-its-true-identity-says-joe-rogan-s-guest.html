What will happen on Oct 29? 3I/ATLAS's could reveal its true identity, says Joe Rogan's guest

On October 29, the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS will reach its closest point to the Sun, according to astronomer Avi Loeb. This event presents a significant opportunity to study the object, which is only the third interstellar body detected entering our solar system.

The upcoming close approach of 3I/ATLAS is particularly important because it may provide clues about the object’s nature, whether it is a natural comet or a technological relic from another star system. Scientists are mobilizing resources, including the James Webb Space Telescope and ground-based observatories, to collect data during this crucial period.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 29
  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest point to the Sun: October 29
  • Third interstellar object detected entering the solar system
  • Research teams: James Webb Space Telescope and ground-based observatories

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been monitored since its detection as it approaches the Sun. Avi Loeb has emphasized the importance of this close approach, noting that scientists missed a previous opportunity with ‘Oumuamua in 2017.

On October 29, as 3I/ATLAS interacts with solar radiation, researchers expect to observe changes in its light reflection and movement, which could indicate whether it is a natural object or something artificial.

Official statements

“This time, the window is open and closing fast,” said Avi Loeb, highlighting the urgency of studying 3I/ATLAS.
Loeb remarked, “If there’s even a one percent chance that 3I Atlas is not natural, we owe it to ourselves to look closer.”

Context

The detection of 3I/ATLAS follows the discovery of ‘Oumuamua, which intrigued scientists due to its unusual acceleration patterns. While many astrophysicists remain sceptical about 3I/ATLAS being anything other than a comet, Loeb advocates for a thorough investigation of outliers in scientific observations.

What’s next

Following the close approach on October 29, researchers will analyze data collected to determine the object’s characteristics and potential origins. The findings could significantly impact our understanding of interstellar objects.

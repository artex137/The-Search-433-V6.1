Researcher has 'vault' of 3.8M alien files wiped days after Trump says government will release UFO files

A researcher has reported that the Black Vault, a repository of 3.8 million files related to UFOs and other government records, has been wiped clean just days after former President Donald Trump promised to release all available documents on aliens. John Greenewald Jr., who founded the Black Vault in 1996, confirmed the data loss on social media.

This incident raises questions about the security and accessibility of information related to UFOs. Authorities have not disclosed any information regarding the cause of the data loss, but Greenewald Jr. stated that he is in the process of restoring the deleted files from backups. He has also urged users to be vigilant when downloading content from the site.

Key facts

  • The Black Vault contains approximately 3.8 million files.
  • Founded by John Greenewald Jr. in 1996.
  • Greenewald Jr. has filed 11,000 Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests.
  • The data loss occurred shortly after Trump’s announcement regarding UFO documents.
  • Greenewald Jr. stated that hundreds of gigabytes of data vanished from the server.
  • The Black Vault has been a source for journalists and the public on covert information.

Timeline

In recent weeks, interest in UFOs has surged following comments from prominent figures. Former President Barack Obama stated on a podcast that UFOs are real but downplayed conspiracy theories involving secret government facilities.

Days later, President Trump announced plans to release all documents related to UFOs, which heightened public curiosity. Shortly after these announcements, Greenewald Jr. reported the loss of the Black Vault's extensive database.

Official statements

“This was HUNDREDS of gigs of data in thousands of directories, and it just vanished,” said Greenewald Jr. on social media. “I feel it was a very oddly timed server maintenance done by the hosting provider, that went awry.”

Context

The Black Vault serves as a civilian archive that compiles government records through FOIA requests. Over the years, it has become a key resource for those interested in government transparency, especially concerning UFOs and other covert programs. The recent data loss has sparked concern among users who rely on the site for access to important information.

What’s next

Greenewald Jr. is working to restore the deleted files from backups and has encouraged users to report any suspicious activity on the site. He remains committed to maintaining the Black Vault as a reliable source of information on government records.

General linked to top secret UFO lab vanishes as authorities launch urgent search

New Mexico authorities have issued a silver alert for retired U.S. Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, 68, who has been missing since 11 a.m. Friday near Quail Run Court NE in Albuquerque, according to the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office. Authorities have expressed heightened concern for his safety due to his medical condition.

This case matters as it highlights ongoing public interest in UFO-related topics and the urgency surrounding the search for McCasland. Authorities are coordinating efforts to locate him, collaborating with local, state, and federal partners.

Key facts

  • Missing since 11 a.m. Friday, date not disclosed.
  • Location: Quail Run Court NE, Albuquerque, New Mexico.
  • Height: 5 feet 11 inches; weight: approximately 160 pounds.
  • Eye color: blue; hair color: white.
  • Medical condition has raised safety concerns.
  • Contact for information: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, Missing Persons Unit at +1 (505) 468-7070.

Timeline

On Friday at 11 a.m., Major General William Neil McCasland was last seen in Albuquerque. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office issued a silver alert due to concerns over his medical condition. Since then, search teams have been deployed to locate him.

Bernalillo County Sheriff John Allen has stated that the priority is to find McCasland safely, emphasizing the continuous efforts of investigators and search teams.

Official statements

“Due to his medical issues, law enforcement is concerned for his safety,” said the sheriff’s office.
“We are coordinating closely with local authorities and defer all updates regarding the search efforts to the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office. Our thoughts are with his family during this difficult time,” said Colonel Justin Secrest, commander of the 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland.

Context

The search for McCasland comes amid increased public interest in UFOs, particularly following recent statements from U.S. officials regarding the release of UFO files. McCasland has been linked to several high-ranking roles in science and technology within the U.S. Air Force, including oversight of significant research and development projects.

His previous involvement with UFO discussions, including connections to prominent researchers, has drawn attention to his disappearance.

What’s next

Authorities continue search efforts in the surrounding area and urge anyone with information about McCasland to report it. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office will provide updates as the situation develops.

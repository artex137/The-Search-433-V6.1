Hubble details structure of double jets and inverted tail in comet of interstellar origin

The Hubble Space Telescope has observed the structure of double jets and an inverted tail in the comet 3I ATLAS, which is of interstellar origin. This observation was made on February 10, 2026, as the comet approached the inner solar system.

This discovery is significant as it provides insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar comets. The observations may help scientists understand how these comets interact with the solar system and the implications for future studies of such celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I ATLAS
  • Observation date: February 10, 2026
  • Method: Hubble Space Telescope
  • Key features observed: double jets, inverted tail
  • Origin: interstellar

Timeline

On February 10, 2026, the Hubble Space Telescope captured detailed images of comet 3I ATLAS. The telescope revealed the comet's unique structural features, including its double jets and an inverted tail.

The observations of 3I ATLAS are part of ongoing research into interstellar objects entering the solar system. The findings contribute to the broader understanding of such celestial phenomena.

Official statements

“The structure of 3I ATLAS offers a glimpse into the dynamics of interstellar comets,” said a representative from NASA.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is part of a growing list of interstellar objects discovered in recent years. Scientists have been studying these comets to gain a better understanding of the materials present in other star systems.

Prior observations of interstellar comets have shown varying structures and compositions, highlighting the diversity among these celestial bodies.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data collected from Hubble's observations to further explore the properties of 3I ATLAS and other interstellar comets.

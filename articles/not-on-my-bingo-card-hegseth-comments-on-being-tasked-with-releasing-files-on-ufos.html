'Not on my bingo card': Hegseth comments on being tasked with releasing files on UFOs

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has been assigned to identify and release government files regarding the existence of extraterrestrial life, following a directive from President Donald Trump on Thursday. Hegseth expressed surprise at his new role during a press conference this week, stating, "I did not have that on my bingo card at all."

This initiative is significant as it marks a renewed effort by the U.S. government to address public curiosity about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and unidentified flying objects (UFOs). Hegseth confirmed that federal agencies have begun working on the assignment, emphasizing their commitment to transparency.

Key facts

  • Assignment announced on Thursday, not disclosed when files will be released.
  • Hegseth's comments were made during a press conference this week, with a video clip shared on C-SPAN's X account.
  • President Trump has directed federal agencies to identify and release files related to UAP and UFOs.
  • Public interest in UFOs surged after former President Barack Obama discussed the possibility of extraterrestrial life.
  • The Pentagon created the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office in July 2022 to centralize reports of military UFO encounters.

Timeline

On Thursday, President Trump issued a directive for federal agencies to begin releasing information related to extraterrestrial life. Hegseth confirmed that efforts to comply with this directive were already underway by Monday.

In May 2022, Congress held its first hearings on UFOs in 50 years, following public interest sparked by leaked Navy videos in 2017. These hearings aimed to address concerns about government transparency regarding unidentified objects.

In summer 2023, retired Maj. David Grusch testified to Congress, claiming that the U.S. has concealed information about a program retrieving and reverse engineering UFOs. The Pentagon denied these claims, which intensified public scrutiny over the government's knowledge of extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

“We’re digging in. We’re going to be in full compliance with that executive order, eager to provide that for the president. There will be more coming on that as far as the process of what we’ll do.”
— Pete Hegseth
“Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there’s life out there. But the distances between solar systems are so great that the chances we’ve been visited by aliens is low.”
— Barack Obama

Context

Prior to this initiative, the U.S. government's handling of UFO reports has been marked by secrecy and skepticism. The CIA acknowledged the existence of Area 51 in 2013, a site long associated with UFO conspiracy theories, but did not confirm claims of extraterrestrial encounters.

In 2022, the Pentagon's establishment of the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office aimed to improve transparency regarding military encounters with unidentified objects. This office succeeded an earlier task force and has been tasked with collecting and analyzing reports of UFO sightings.

What’s next

Federal agencies are expected to continue their work on the release of files related to extraterrestrial life. Hegseth has indicated that further updates on the process will be provided in the future, although no specific timeline for the release has been disclosed.

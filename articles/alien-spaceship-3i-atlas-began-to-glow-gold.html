"Alien spaceship" 3I/ATLAS began to glow gold

Published 2025-12-21 16:34 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS Changes Color to Gold

Comet 3I/ATLAS has changed color to gold as it approaches the Sun, according to a report by the British publication Daily Star. The comet, which some theories suggest may be an alien spaceship, initially appeared dull red before undergoing this transformation.

This change in color is significant because it indicates a reaction to solar heat, which affects the comet's unusual chemical composition. Astronomers have previously noted that 3I/ATLAS also shifted from red to green before turning gold, suggesting a dynamic process as it nears the Sun.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has changed color from dull red to gold.
  • The change is attributed to solar heat affecting its chemical composition.
  • The comet has previously changed color from red to green.
  • Current color change may signify the "end of its journey."
  • Reported by the British publication Daily Star.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was initially observed as a dull red object.

As it approached the Sun, it changed to green, according to previous reports from astronomers.

Most recently, the comet has shifted to a gold hue, highlighting its dynamic interaction with solar heat.

Official statements

“The unusual chemical composition of 3I/ATLAS led to the change in its color due to solar heat,” according to the Daily Star.

Context

Theories surrounding Comet 3I/ATLAS include speculation about it being an alien spaceship, which has garnered public interest. Color changes in comets are not uncommon, yet the specific transformations of 3I/ATLAS have attracted significant attention from astronomers and enthusiasts alike.

What’s next

Further observations are expected as 3I/ATLAS continues its journey towards the Sun, with scientists monitoring its behavior and color changes closely.

The plan to send a spacecraft to interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA and private researchers have proposed a 2035 launch for a spacecraft designed to intercept interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS at a distance of 732 astronomical units (AU) by 2085. The mission aims to utilize a solar Oberth maneuver to achieve this ambitious goal, marking a significant milestone in space exploration.

This mission is important as it offers a unique opportunity to study primitive materials from another star system. Researchers believe that interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS contain chemical fingerprints of distant solar nebulae, providing insights into planetary formation and chemical evolution beyond our solar system.

Key facts

  • Launch Window: 2035
  • Target Distance: 732 AU
  • Solar Approach: 3.2 solar radii
  • Probe Mass: 500 kilograms
  • Peak Temperature: ~1,426°C (2,600°F)
  • Delta-V Required: At least 8.4 kilometers per second

Timeline

The proposed mission will involve several key phases:

  • 2035: Launch of the spacecraft targeting 3I/ATLAS.
  • 2085: Estimated arrival at 3I/ATLAS.

The spacecraft will utilize Jupiter's gravity to assist in the trajectory, diving into the sun’s corona for maximum acceleration.

Official statements

“Exploring 3I/ATLAS provides a unique opportunity to sample primitive material from another star system,” researchers have stated.

Context

This mission builds on previous efforts to explore celestial bodies beyond our solar system. ESA’s Comet Interceptor, set to launch in 2028, will wait at the L2 Lagrange point to rendezvous with new targets. However, the solar Oberth maneuver remains essential for high-velocity missions targeting distant objects like 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to refine the mission architecture and technology needed for the launch in 2035. Further studies will focus on overcoming the challenges of extreme thermal barriers during the solar approach.

Wife of Missing UFO Expert Addresses ‘Misinformation’ Around Case

Susan McCasland Wilkerson, the wife of retired Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, has addressed what she calls “misinformation” regarding her husband’s disappearance. McCasland has been missing since February 27, with no sightings reported as of Monday, according to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.

The case has garnered national attention due to McCasland's previous leadership in major Air Force science and technology programs. Authorities are coordinating efforts to locate him, involving multiple agencies including the FBI and local search teams.

Key facts

  • Date of disappearance: February 27
  • Last seen: 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE, Albuquerque
  • Search duration: 11 days as of Monday
  • Agencies involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, FBI, New Mexico State Search and Rescue, Albuquerque Mountain Rescue, Air Force Office of Special Investigations, Albuquerque Police Department
  • Security footage requests: Over 600 homeowners contacted for information
  • Description: 5 feet 11 inches tall, approximately 160 pounds, blue eyes, white hair

Timeline

William Neil McCasland was last seen on February 27 at approximately 11 a.m. in Albuquerque. Following his disappearance, a Silver Alert was issued due to unspecified medical issues. His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, has reported that extensive search efforts have been undertaken, including the use of drones and search dogs.

As of the latest updates, authorities continue to canvass the area and review security footage from local homes. Investigators have reached out to more than 600 residents for assistance in gathering information.

Context

The disappearance of McCasland has raised questions regarding his past associations with the UFO community, particularly his brief collaboration with Tom DeLonge, the former Blink-182 frontman. Wilkerson clarified that McCasland's activities with DeLonge were limited and that he does not possess any special knowledge regarding UFOs or historically significant incidents.

McCasland retired nearly 13 years ago and has held only commonly held clearances since, according to Wilkerson.

What’s next

Investigators will continue their efforts to locate McCasland by canvassing the area, collecting and reviewing home security footage, and pursuing tips. Wilkerson has pledged to provide updates as new information becomes available.

Interest in UFOs is growing in the U.S. following Trump's order to declassify files

Public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and UFOs has surged in the United States following President Donald Trump's announcement on February 19, 2026, to declassify government files related to these topics. The announcement has sparked widespread debate and intrigue among citizens.

The significance of this development lies in the increasing public curiosity about extraterrestrial life and the government's transparency regarding UAP. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth has confirmed that the Pentagon is working on the directive in coordination with the Office of the Director of National Intelligence, although specific timelines and details remain undisclosed.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: February 19, 2026
  • President: Donald Trump
  • Secretary of Defense: Pete Hegseth
  • Agency involved: Office of the Director of National Intelligence
  • Public support for declassification: 65% (June 2025 Pew Research Center survey)
  • Reports accumulated by the Anomaly Resolution Agency: over 2,000 (as of February 2026)
  • New cases recorded by the AARO between May 2023 and June 2024: 757
  • Cases deemed "truly anomalous": 21

Timeline

On February 14, 2026, former President Barack Obama stated during a podcast that aliens are real, igniting public interest in UAP. The following day, he clarified on Instagram that he had not seen evidence of extraterrestrial contact during his presidency.

On February 19, Trump announced his intention to declassify government files related to UAP and UFOs. By the end of February, Secretary Hegseth confirmed the Pentagon's commitment to the directive.

Official statements

“We are delving deeper to ensure full compliance with the executive order,” said Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth during his "Arsenal of Freedom" tour, emphasizing a deliberate process without exaggerated promises.

Context

Interest in UFOs is not a new phenomenon, but recent surveys indicate a marked increase in public belief regarding extraterrestrial life. A Pew Research Center survey in May 2025 found that 51% of American adults believe UAPs are evidence of extraterrestrial intelligent life, up from 33% in 2021. A Gallup poll from November 2025 reported that 41% of respondents believe extraterrestrial life has visited Earth, the highest level since 1997.

The Anomaly Resolution Agency (AARO), established in 2022, has been tasked with centralizing UAP research. As of February 2026, it has documented over 2,000 reports without providing evidence of extraterrestrial technology or non-human intelligence.

What’s next

The Pentagon will continue to work on the declassification of UAP files, with no specific timeline provided. The ongoing public interest may prompt further investigations and discussions regarding the implications of these phenomena.

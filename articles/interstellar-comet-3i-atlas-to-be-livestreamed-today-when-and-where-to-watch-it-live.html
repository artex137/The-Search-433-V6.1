Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS to be livestreamed today: When and where to watch it LIVE?

Published 2025-11-17 10:24 UTC

Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS to be livestreamed today

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will be livestreamed today, October 3, 2023, as it passes close to Earth. The event can be viewed online starting at 6:00 PM EDT.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Livestream Start Time: 6:00 PM EDT
  • Livestream Location: Various online platforms
  • Notable Contributors: NASA, The Virtual Telescope Project

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 by astronomers using the ATLAS telescope system. Since then, it has garnered attention due to its interstellar origin and unique trajectory.

Today, the comet will come within a distance of approximately 0.5 astronomical units from Earth, making it an ideal opportunity for both amateur and professional astronomers to observe.

Official statements

“This is a rare chance to witness an interstellar comet as it passes by our planet,” said a spokesperson from NASA.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare celestial visitors. 3I/ATLAS is one of the few confirmed interstellar objects observed in our solar system. Its trajectory and characteristics provide valuable insights into the formation of solar systems beyond our own.

What’s next

Following today’s livestream, astronomers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it travels away from Earth, gathering data on its composition and behavior.

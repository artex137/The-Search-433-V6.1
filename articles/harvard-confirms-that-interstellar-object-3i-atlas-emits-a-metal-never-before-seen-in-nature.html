Harvard confirms that interstellar object 3I/ATLAS emits a metal never before seen in nature

Harvard University has confirmed that comet 3I/ATLAS, an interstellar object, emits a metal not previously observed in nature. The comet, which passed close to Mars between October 4 and 7, 2025, is the third confirmed interstellar object to enter our solar system.

This discovery is significant as it challenges existing scientific understanding of cometary composition. Researchers are currently analyzing data from various high-tech telescopes to understand the implications of this finding and its potential ties to the search for extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Close Approach to Mars: October 4-7, 2025
  • Estimated Age: 7 to 10 billion years
  • Speed: 60 km/s
  • Emission: Nickel detected in its coma
  • Observation Tools: Keck II in Hawaii, European VLT, James Webb Space Telescope

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS system in Chile. It approached Mars between October 4 and 7, 2025, allowing Mars orbiters to capture images. Recent analyses have revealed an unusual emission of nickel, leading to further investigations.

Following its close approach, astronomers have utilized high-resolution spectrographs to study the comet's emissions. The findings have raised questions about the nature of the materials released by the comet.

Official statements

“The discovery is so rare that scientists have had to look for a rather unusual chemical explanation,” said Professor Avi Loeb from Harvard. “If the object emits a molecule that we only know how to create in factories, we must consider the possibility of a technological or artificial origin.”

Context

Prior to this, interstellar objects such as comet 2I/Borisov have been studied, but they did not exhibit similar properties. The presence of nickel without iron in 3I/ATLAS challenges long-held scientific beliefs about the relationship between these metals in cosmic environments.

This anomaly has sparked considerable debate in the scientific community, with some experts suggesting it could indicate an artificial origin for the comet.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from telescopes to gather more information about comet 3I/ATLAS. Future observations will focus on understanding the chemical composition of the comet and the implications of its emissions for theories of extraterrestrial technology.

Astronomers spot wobbling jets on rare interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

Astronomers have detected wobbling jets on the rare interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which was last observed on December 27, 2025. This comet, first identified in 2019, presents a unique opportunity for researchers to study its behaviour and characteristics as it travels through our solar system.

The discovery of the comet's wobbling jets is significant as it provides insights into the composition and activity of interstellar objects. Researchers are currently analyzing the data collected from this observation to better understand the comet's trajectory and physical properties.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation Date: December 27, 2025
  • First Identified: 2019
  • Location: Within our solar system
  • Research Agency: Not disclosed

Timeline

In 2019, astronomers first identified 3I/ATLAS as an interstellar comet. Since then, it has been monitored as it travels through the solar system. On December 27, 2025, astronomers observed the comet's jets exhibiting a wobbling motion, prompting further investigation into its behaviour.

Official statements

“The detection of these wobbling jets is a remarkable finding,” said a representative from the research team. “It enhances our understanding of how interstellar objects behave as they interact with solar radiation and gravitational forces.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few interstellar comets observed to date. Previous studies of interstellar objects have provided valuable information about their origins and characteristics, contributing to the broader understanding of the solar system and beyond.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data from the recent observations to gain deeper insights into the dynamics of 3I/ATLAS and its jets. Further studies may lead to new discoveries regarding the nature of interstellar comets.

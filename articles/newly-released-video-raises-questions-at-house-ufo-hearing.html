Newly-released video raises questions at House UFO hearing

A newly-released video from a U.S. reaper drone shows a glowing orb off the coast of Yemen, which was shared during a House Oversight hearing on UFOs on Tuesday. The video captures a Hellfire missile striking the unidentified object and bouncing off of it. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This hearing, attended by lawmakers and military officials, has sparked renewed interest in the topic of Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP), commonly referred to as UFOs. The U.S. military has been investigating these incidents and is working to provide clarity on the nature of these sightings.

Key facts

  • Event: House Oversight hearing on UFOs
  • Date: Tuesday, not disclosed
  • Location: Washington, D.C., U.S.
  • Video: Captured by a U.S. reaper drone
  • Object: Glowing orb off the coast of Yemen
  • Missile: Hellfire missile struck the object

Timeline

On Tuesday, Rep. Eric Burlison, a Republican from Missouri, presented the newly-released video during the House Oversight hearing. The video depicts a glowing orb and a subsequent missile strike from a reaper drone.

The military has categorized these incidents as Unidentified Aerial Phenomena, prompting further investigation into their nature and origins.

Official statements

“This video raises important questions about what we are seeing in our skies,” said Rep. Eric Burlison during the hearing.

Context

The U.S. military has increasingly focused on UAPs in recent years, acknowledging that sightings have been reported by military personnel and pilots. This hearing is part of a broader effort to address public and governmental concerns regarding these phenomena.

What’s next

Investigations into the incident will continue as lawmakers seek to obtain more information on UAPs and the military's response to such occurrences.

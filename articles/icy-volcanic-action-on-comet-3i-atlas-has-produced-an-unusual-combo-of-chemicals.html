Icy Volcanic Action on Comet 3I/ATLAS Has Produced an Unusual Combo of Chemicals

Comet 3I/ATLAS has exhibited signs of cryovolcanism as it speeds through the Solar System, according to a recent preprint paper published on arXiv. The comet, which is currently near the sun, has garnered attention for its unique carbon-rich composition and intense brightness. The paper suggests that this interstellar visitor may be experiencing icy volcanic activity, a phenomenon often observed in trans-Neptunian objects.

This discovery is significant as it could enhance our understanding of the comet's origins and its similarities to primitive celestial bodies. Researchers are analyzing the comet's composition and behavior to determine the implications for both its history and the broader context of cometary science.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is traveling at a speed of 130,000 miles per hour.
  • It has not passed close to any stars in the last 10 million years, according to the Gaia DR3 catalog.
  • The comet is considered "pristine" and has not been thermally processed like other comets.
  • The coma of the comet became exceptionally bright in late October 2025.
  • The authors of the preprint paper compared the comet to carbonaceous chondrites collected from Antarctica.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS entered the Solar System at a high velocity, raising interest among astronomers. In late October 2025, the comet's coma brightened significantly as it approached the sun. Researchers have since noted potential signs of cryovolcanism, suggesting that warmer conditions may have led to the sublimation of water-ice and subsequent volcanic activity.

Official statements

“Implantation of foreign materials, and their processing by cosmic rays during its long trip through our galaxy could have hidden the real nature of 3I,” the authors wrote in the paper.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar comet observed, making its study crucial for understanding the characteristics of such celestial bodies. Its unique composition and behavior may provide insights into the early solar system and the processes that shape comets.

What’s next

Researchers plan to conduct further observations and analyses to confirm the findings regarding cryovolcanism and the comet's composition. The implications of these studies may reshape our understanding of interstellar objects and their interactions within our Solar System.

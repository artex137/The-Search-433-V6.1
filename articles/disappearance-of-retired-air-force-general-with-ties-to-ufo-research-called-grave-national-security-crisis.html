Disappearance Of Retired Air Force General With Ties To UFO Research Called 'Grave National Security Crisis'

Retired U.S. Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland has been missing since February 27, last seen heading out for a trail run near his home in New Mexico. His disappearance has raised concerns among some experts, who describe it as a “grave national security crisis” due to McCasland's alleged involvement in sensitive UFO-related projects.

Authorities have initiated a search for McCasland, who is 68 years old. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office (BCSO) has issued a Silver Alert and is investigating the case as a missing person. The FBI has joined the search, but officials have stated there are no signs of criminal activity, such as kidnapping. Investigative journalist Ross Coulthart emphasized the implications of McCasland's disappearance, suggesting it might be connected to recent government pledges to disclose information on extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • McCasland has been missing since February 27, 2023.
  • He was last seen on a trail run near his home in New Mexico.
  • A Silver Alert has been issued by the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.
  • The FBI has joined the investigation, which is currently treated as a missing person case.
  • No signs of criminal activity have been reported.
  • McCasland previously served at Kirtland Air Force Base and was involved in UFO research.

Timeline

On February 27, 2023, Major General William Neil McCasland was last seen leaving for a trail run near his home in New Mexico. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert. The FBI later confirmed its involvement in the search. As of now, the investigation continues without any indications of foul play.

Official statements

“There are no signs of any criminal activity such as a kidnapping or anything else,” a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office said.
“The fact that General Neil McCasland has disappeared off the face of the earth is a grave national security crisis for the United States of America,” Ross Coulthart stated on his show Reality Check.

Context

William Neil McCasland previously commanded the Phillips Research Site and the Air Force Research Laboratory at Kirtland Air Force Base. He has been described as a key adviser on various UFO-related projects, particularly during his tenure at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, where Project Blue Book was conducted. The recent WikiLeaks release mentioned him in connection with UFO materials, but there are no official records of his involvement.

What’s next

The search for McCasland is ongoing, with local authorities and the FBI actively investigating his disappearance. Further updates will depend on the progress of the investigation and any new information that may arise.

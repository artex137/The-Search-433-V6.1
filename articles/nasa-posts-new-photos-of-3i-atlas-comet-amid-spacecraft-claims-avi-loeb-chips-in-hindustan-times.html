NASA captures new images of 3I/ATLAS comet

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) has captured new images of the 3I/ATLAS comet as it travels approximately 178 million miles from Earth on November 30, 2025. This marks the latest observation in a series of studies since the comet's discovery in July 2025.

Why this matters

The new images provide critical data to refine estimates of the comet’s nucleus size, activity levels, and trajectory. NASA has stated that the comet continues on a hyperbolic and unbound trajectory, with plans for further observations in the coming months as 3I/ATLAS departs the Solar System.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS discovered in July 2025
  • Latest images captured by HST on November 30, 2025
  • Distance from Earth: approximately 178 million miles
  • Over 20 spacecraft and telescopes focused on 3I/ATLAS by November 2025
  • NASA confirms comet behaves like a typical comet

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025, prompting a series of observations by various space agencies. Initial images revealed a teardrop-shaped dust cocoon, indicating comet-like activity. By November 2025, over 20 spacecraft and telescopes had turned their attention to the comet, with HST capturing new images on November 30.

Official statements

“Every re-observation helps reduce uncertainties and improve the modelling data of the object,” NASA stated in a press note.
“The object behaves like a comet,” NASA concluded based on the available data.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object confirmed to visit our Solar System. It presents an opportunity to learn more about comet formation around other stars and the materials they carry. Previous observations have supported the conclusion that 3I/ATLAS exhibits typical comet-like behaviour.

What’s next

Astronomers are racing to collect data from 3I/ATLAS before it leaves the Solar System. Further observations will be conducted with HST, the James Webb Space Telescope, and various spacecraft to gather insights into the comet's composition and behaviour.

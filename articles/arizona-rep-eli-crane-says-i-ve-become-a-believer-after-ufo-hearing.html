Arizona Rep. Eli Crane says 'I've become a believer' after UFO hearing

Arizona Representative Eli Crane has stated he has become a believer in UFOs after participating in a hearing hosted by the House taskforce on government transparency on Tuesday morning. The hearing featured testimonies from three military veterans and a journalist discussing their alleged encounters with UFOs.

The hearing is significant as it focuses on government disclosure and the protection of whistleblowers. Some individuals have claimed they faced repercussions for sharing their experiences regarding UFOs, raising concerns about transparency in government processes.

Key facts

  • Date: Tuesday morning
  • Location: House taskforce on government transparency
  • Participants: Three military veterans, one journalist
  • Key statement: Eli Crane has become a believer in UFOs

Timeline

On Tuesday morning, Representative Eli Crane attended a UFO hearing organized by the House taskforce on government transparency. During the session, he listened to testimonies from military veterans and a journalist regarding their encounters with unidentified flying objects. Following the hearing, Crane expressed his newfound belief in UFOs.

Official statements

"I got to admit I’ve become a believer," Crane said regarding the testimonies presented at the hearing.

Context

This hearing is part of an ongoing discussion about government transparency concerning UFOs. Previous incidents have raised questions about the treatment of witnesses and the need for protective measures for those who come forward with information.

What’s next

Further investigations into UFO sightings and the protection of whistleblowers are expected as the taskforce continues its work on government transparency.

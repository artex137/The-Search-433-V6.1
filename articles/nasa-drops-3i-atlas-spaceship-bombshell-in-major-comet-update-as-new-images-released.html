NASA drops 3I/ATLAS 'spaceship' bombshell in major comet update as new images released

NASA has provided a significant update on the mystery comet 3I/ATLAS during a press conference held on November 19. The agency released new images of the comet, which some experts speculate could be an alien spacecraft, through a broadcast on its website and YouTube channel.

This update is crucial as it addresses public interest and concern regarding the comet, which is travelling through the Solar System at a speed of 130,000 mph. NASA has confirmed the object as an interstellar comet, and experts continue to analyze its characteristics and trajectory.

Key facts

  • Event: NASA press conference on comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: November 19, 2023
  • Speed of comet: 130,000 mph (209,215 km/h)
  • Closest approach to Earth: 170 million miles (273 million kilometres)
  • Previous government shutdown: October 1 to November 12, 2023
  • Confirmed as interstellar object: June 2023

Timeline

In early June 2023, scientists confirmed 3I/ATLAS as an interstellar object. Following this, NASA and other observatories began collecting data using advanced telescopes. On October 1, the U.S. government entered a shutdown, which affected NASA's operations. The shutdown ended on November 12, allowing NASA to resume public communication and data sharing.

On November 19, NASA held a press conference where they revealed new images of the comet taken from the Mars rover, marking the first time the public could view these images.

Official statements

Avi Loeb, theoretical physicist, suggested, "The anomalies suggest it might be an extraterrestrial spaceship. The behaviour has been so odd I will never be able to rule out the possibility it is an alien craft."
Loeb also stated, "On my UFO scale, 3I/ATLAS would always at least be a two—an object meriting attention."

Context

This update follows a period of heightened interest in 3I/ATLAS, especially after the comet's trajectory brought it back into view after a slingshot around the sun. The comet's unusual characteristics have led to speculation about its origin, with some experts suggesting it could be more than just a comet.

What’s next

NASA and various observatories will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth next month. The comet is also expected to make a close pass by Jupiter early next year, providing further opportunities for observations before it exits the Solar System.

USAF nuclear specialist claims Donald Trump 'could release bombshell UFO pictures soon'

Published 2026-01-01 14:49 UTC

Former Air Force Specialist Suggests Trump Could Release UFO Photos Soon

A former Air Force nuclear specialist has suggested that Donald Trump may soon release significant UFO-related images, potentially including "photos of a recovered craft." Gene Sticco made these comments in an interview, indicating that the disclosure could happen as early as this year.

The topic of unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), previously known as UFOs, has gained prominence in recent years, marked by claims from military personnel, congressional hearings, and a documentary titled "The Age of Disclosure." This documentary features 34 senior officials from various sectors who allege knowledge of UAP. Sticco's remarks highlight a growing interest in the UAP phenomenon and the potential for governmental transparency regarding extraterrestrial intelligence.

Key facts

  • Gene Sticco is a national security expert and former Air Force nuclear protection specialist.
  • Sticco has suggested that Trump could be the "disclosure" president for UAP information.
  • The documentary "The Age of Disclosure" features claims of an 80-year cover-up of non-human life.
  • Sticco expects a Presidential Commission to be formed in 2026 to address UAP disclosure.
  • Sticco indicated that the public might see "photos of a recovered craft" as part of the disclosure.

Timeline

In recent years, interest in UAP has surged, fueled by various claims and events:

  • UAP sightings by US Navy pilots have been reported.
  • Congress has held hearings on the UAP phenomenon.
  • The documentary "The Age of Disclosure" has brought together numerous officials to discuss UAP.
  • Sticco has been involved in the discourse due to his connection to Valerij Černohajev, who allegedly wrote about UAP.

Official statements

"The 'smoking gun' is out there. Whether or not they EVER wheel it out for public display is another question." — Gene Sticco
"I do think President Trump will be the 'disclosure' President." — Gene Sticco
"If he frames it as 'on both sides of the aisle there is a concern, there's ongoing debate'... he clears them for SAP access." — Gene Sticco

Context

The interest in UAP has intensified, especially following claims from military personnel about sightings and encounters. The ongoing discussion has led to calls for transparency from government agencies, with many advocating for a formal investigation into the phenomena.

What’s next

Sticco anticipates that if Trump moves forward with UAP disclosures, a Presidential Commission may be formed in 2026 to guide the process and establish what information can be publicly released.

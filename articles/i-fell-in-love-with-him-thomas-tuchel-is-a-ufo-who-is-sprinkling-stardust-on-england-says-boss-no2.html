'I fell in love with him' - Thomas Tuchel is a UFO who is sprinkling stardust on England, says boss' No2

Anthony Barry, assistant head coach of England’s national football team, has described head coach Thomas Tuchel as an extraordinary manager, likening him to a UFO. Barry made the comments during an interview, reflecting on their professional relationship and Tuchel’s unique approach to coaching.

This characterization of Tuchel highlights the impact he has had on Barry and the England squad. Under Tuchel's leadership, England aims to achieve success in upcoming major tournaments, including the World Cup. Barry emphasized the importance of their methodology in building a strong team ahead of these challenges.

Key facts

  • Anthony Barry has worked with Tuchel since 2021.
  • Tuchel has previously managed Chelsea and Bayern Munich.
  • England will face Serbia on Thursday and Andorra a week later in World Cup qualifiers.
  • Barry has coached players such as Cristiano Ronaldo and Harry Kane.
  • Tuchel and Barry discussed their objectives for England in a Munich café.

Timeline

In 2021, Anthony Barry began working with Thomas Tuchel at Chelsea after Tuchel replaced Frank Lampard. The duo achieved significant success, winning the Champions League and a Bundesliga title with Bayern Munich. Following their time at Bayern, Barry joined Tuchel in coaching the England national team.

Recently, Barry and Tuchel met to discuss their ambitions for the England squad, focusing on the importance of character and methodology in their preparation for upcoming matches.

Official statements

“To say I fell in love with him wouldn’t be an understatement. He swept into Chelsea. He floated in, he didn’t walk,” Barry said of Tuchel.
“There’s something about Thomas for me… stardust there. I can’t exactly say what it is but it’s something special. It’s the X-factor that we all look for,” he added.

Context

Anthony Barry has a background in coaching that includes experience with multiple national teams, including the Republic of Ireland, Belgium, and Portugal. He has developed a reputation for his innovative approach, including a focus on set-pieces and player communication. Tuchel's unique coaching style has garnered attention and admiration in the football community.

What’s next

England will continue preparations for their World Cup qualifiers, with a focus on building a cohesive team ready to face the challenges ahead. Barry and Tuchel aim to develop a world-class squad capable of success in the upcoming tournament in North America.

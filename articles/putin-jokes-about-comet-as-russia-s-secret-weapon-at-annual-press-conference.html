Putin jokes about comet as “Russia’s secret weapon” at annual press conference

Russian President Vladimir Putin has addressed the 3I/ATLAS comet during his annual televised press conference, describing it as “Russia’s secret weapon” while assuring that it poses no threat to Earth. The event took place on December 14, 2023, and lasted approximately four hours.

This statement comes amid growing public interest in the comet, which some have speculated may have extraterrestrial links. Putin's comments aimed to clarify the comet's nature and reassure the public about its safety. The press conference received approximately 2.5 million questions from the public, showcasing a blend of serious inquiries and light-hearted moments.

Key facts

  • Date: December 14, 2023
  • Duration: Approximately four hours
  • Distance of 3I/ATLAS from Earth: 259 million km
  • Distance of the Moon from Earth: 400,000 km
  • Number of questions submitted for the event: Approximately 2.5 million

Timeline

During the press conference, a reporter asked Putin about the 3I/ATLAS comet and its rumored link to aliens. Putin humorously referred to it as a “secret weapon” and stated it would only be used in extreme cases.

He then explained the scientific aspects of the comet, noting that it behaves differently than comets from the Milky Way galaxy. Putin emphasized that Russian scientists are monitoring the situation closely.

Official statements

“Seriously, though, it's a comet. Our scientists are aware of what's happening. Moreover, it's a comet from another galaxy, so it behaves differently than comets of our galactic origin,” said Putin.
“Because we are against deploying weapons in space,” he added.

Context

This press conference is part of an annual tradition where Putin interacts with the public and addresses various concerns. The event often features a mix of serious questions and humorous exchanges, reflecting the diverse interests of the Russian populace.

In previous years, Putin has faced inquiries on a wide range of topics, including economic conditions and social issues, highlighting ongoing public discontent regarding certain policies.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any further investigations or actions related to the 3I/ATLAS comet. The scientific community will continue to monitor the comet's trajectory and behavior as it approaches the Sun.

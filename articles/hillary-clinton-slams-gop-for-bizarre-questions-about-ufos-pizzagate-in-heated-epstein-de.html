Hillary Clinton Criticizes GOP Questions on UFOs During Epstein Deposition

Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton criticized House Republicans for asking about UFOs and the debunked "Pizzagate" conspiracy theory during a closed-door deposition related to the investigation of convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein. The deposition took place on February 27, 2026, in Washington, D.C.

Clinton's remarks came after hours of testimony before the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee. She described the questioning as bizarre and unproductive, while also praising Oversight Chairman Rep. James Comer (R-Kentucky) for more substantive inquiries. Clinton emphasized her commitment to providing truthful answers during the session.

Key facts

  • Clinton participated in a deposition on February 27, 2026.
  • The deposition is part of the congressional investigation into Jeffrey Epstein.
  • Clinton stated she answered all questions to the best of her ability.
  • Former President Bill Clinton is scheduled to appear for a deposition on February 28, 2026.
  • Rep. Suhas Subramanyam (D-Virginia) expects the transcript to be released within 24 hours.
  • Rep. Lauren Boebert (R-Colorado) shared a photo from the deposition, leading to objections from Democrats.

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, Hillary Clinton attended a closed-door deposition regarding the Epstein investigation. During the session, she faced questions about UFOs and Pizzagate, which she described as off-topic and not relevant to the investigation. Clinton praised some aspects of the inquiry but criticized the repetitive nature of the questioning.

On February 28, 2026, former President Bill Clinton is set to appear for his own deposition related to the same investigation.

Official statements

“It then got, at the end, quite unusual because I started being asked about UFOs and a series of questions about Pizzagate — one of the most vile bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet,” Clinton said.
“I want to commend Chairman Comer for raising a series of significant questions that I responded to about the nature of the investigation and the areas that I thought should be explored,” she added.
“I thought it was very repetitive,” Clinton said regarding the questioning. “They literally asked the same questions over and over again, which didn’t seem to me to be very productive.”

Context

The congressional investigation into Jeffrey Epstein continues to draw significant public and media attention. Clinton's deposition is part of a broader inquiry that includes various high-profile individuals. This session follows a history of scrutiny surrounding the Clintons and their connections to Epstein.

What’s next

The full transcript of Clinton's deposition is expected to be released within 24 hours, according to Rep. Suhas Subramanyam. Former President Bill Clinton's deposition is scheduled for February 28, 2026.

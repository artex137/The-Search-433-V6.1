Private contractor admits to causing last year’s New Jersey “UFO scare” with secretive drone test

TRNTON, NJ – A private defense contractor has taken credit for the “UFO” sightings that sparked widespread speculation across New Jersey last year. The incident, which occurred over several nights, involved strange lights that residents reported seeing in the night sky. According to a source speaking to the New York Post, an employee from the company admitted that the aerial display was the result of a covert drone test.

This admission sheds light on events that caused weeks of intrigue and media attention. The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) and state officials did not provide clear explanations at the time, which contributed to the frenzy surrounding the sightings. The revelation raises concerns about transparency and public safety regarding classified drone operations conducted over populated areas.

Key facts

  • Private defense contractor reportedly responsible for last year’s New Jersey “UFO scare”
  • Company conducted a classified drone capability test under a government contract
  • Aircraft showcased at a recent Army technology conference
  • Incident unfolded over several nights, leading to reports of possible UFOs
  • Federal and state officials did not comment on the report

Timeline

The incident began last year when residents across New Jersey reported strange lights in the sky. Videos surfaced on social media showing clusters of bright lights moving in formation, prompting speculation about UFOs. In the weeks that followed, national media and federal aviation authorities took notice, but officials offered no clear explanations.

Recently, an employee of the private contractor revealed to the New York Post that the lights were part of a covert drone test. This information has not been publicly confirmed by any government agency.

Official statements

“You remember that big UFO scare in New Jersey last year? Well, that was us,” the employee allegedly told the New York Post.

Context

The incident has highlighted ongoing concerns about the transparency of drone testing, particularly in populated areas. Prior incidents of unidentified aerial phenomena have often led to speculation and public concern, further complicating the relationship between government operations and public awareness.

What’s next

Investigations regarding the nature of the drone tests and the implications for public safety are ongoing. No specific deadlines or charges have been announced at this time.

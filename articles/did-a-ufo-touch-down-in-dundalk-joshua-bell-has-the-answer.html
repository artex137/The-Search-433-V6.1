Did a UFO touch down in Dundalk? Joshua Bell has the answer

Local author Joshua Bell has documented accounts of alleged alien and paranormal encounters in Dundalk and Essex, Maryland, as part of his new book, "Mysterious Middle River." Bell, a Dundalk resident, has been collecting these stories since the mid-1990s and recently presented his findings at the Heritage Society of Essex and Middle River.

This exploration into the paranormal is timely, coinciding with the autumnal equinox and the Halloween season. Authorities and community members have shown interest in Bell's work as it sheds light on local folklore and unexplained phenomena.

Key facts

  • Author: Joshua Bell
  • Book Title: "Mysterious Middle River"
  • Location: Dundalk, Maryland
  • Timeframe of Research: Since the mid-1990s
  • Presentation Venue: Heritage Society of Essex and Middle River

Timeline

Joshua Bell began collecting stories of paranormal encounters in eastern Baltimore County in the mid-1990s. Earlier this year, he presented his findings at a local heritage society, which garnered interest from community members. Following this, he decided to compile these accounts into his book, "Mysterious Middle River."

Official statements

“I’m not set on proving or disproving the existence of the paranormal — simply documenting people's tales of the strange and unusual,” said Joshua Bell.

Context

The interest in paranormal phenomena has grown in Dundalk, particularly during the Halloween season. Local events and publications often explore these themes, reflecting a broader cultural fascination with the unexplained. Bell’s work contributes to this ongoing dialogue in the community.

What’s next

Bell's book, "Mysterious Middle River," is expected to be a focal point for local discussions on paranormal encounters. Community members and authorities may engage further with Bell’s findings as interest in the subject continues to rise.

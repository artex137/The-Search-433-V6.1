Mysterious interstellar object is morphing in unexplained ways as it moves closer to Earth

Austrian astronomers have reported that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS is changing shape and color as it approaches Earth. The latest observations, captured on September 7, 2025, show that the object has shifted from a reddish glow to a green hue, raising questions among scientists about its behavior.

This phenomenon matters as 3I/ATLAS is expected to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, at a distance of 168 million miles, which is similar to the distance between Earth and Mars. Researchers are analyzing this unusual transformation to better understand the nature of the comet and its emissions.

Key facts

  • Date of observations: September 7, 2025
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Distance at closest approach: 168 million miles
  • Cyanide emission rate: 20 grams per second
  • Nickel emission rate: 5 grams per second
  • Location of observations: Namibia

Timeline

On August 25, 2025, the Very Large Telescope reported that 3I/ATLAS was emitting cyanide at a rate of 20 grams per second. By August 28, astronomers noted the brightening of the object's tail.

On September 7, during a total lunar eclipse, a detailed image of 3I/ATLAS revealed a blue-green coma and further changes in its appearance.

As 3I/ATLAS approaches the sun, its emissions of cyanide and nickel have increased sharply, indicating a shift in its behavior.

Official statements

“During the total lunar eclipse, we captured a detailed image of Comet 3I/ATLAS from the dark skies of Namibia,” said astronomer Michael Jäger.
“The tail documented by Hubble Space hardly grew any larger and only became slightly brighter,” Jäger added.
“Is this anomaly another clue for a possible technological origin of 3I/ATLAS?” questioned Harvard physicist Avi Loeb in a recent blog post.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third known interstellar object, following 1I/`Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Unlike its predecessors, 3I/ATLAS exhibits unique features such as an anti-tail and significant color changes. The presence of nickel without detectable iron is particularly notable, as this is not typical of natural comets.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth. Further studies are needed to understand the mechanisms behind its emissions and the implications of its unusual characteristics.

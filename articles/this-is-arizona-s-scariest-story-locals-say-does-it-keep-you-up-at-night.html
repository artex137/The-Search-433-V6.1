Arizona's scariest lore: The Phoenix Lights

The Phoenix Lights, a series of widely reported UFO sightings, have been named Arizona's scariest lore in a recent reader poll, surpassing tales of ghosts and Skinwalkers. The sightings occurred on March 13, 1997, over Arizona, Nevada, and Sonora, Mexico, with thousands of witnesses reporting a triangular formation of lights moving silently across the night sky.

This matter continues to be significant as many locals remain skeptical of the official explanation provided by the U.S. Air Force. The Air Force stated the lights were flares from a military training exercise, a claim that many witnesses dispute, maintaining that the lights they observed did not resemble flares.

Key facts

  • Date: March 13, 1997
  • Location: Arizona, Nevada, Sonora, Mexico
  • Witnesses: Thousands reported sightings
  • First sighting time: 7:30-8:45 p.m.
  • Second sighting time: 10-10:30 p.m.
  • Official explanation: Flares from A-10 Warthog aircraft
  • Military training range: Barry M. Goldwater Range

Timeline

The events surrounding the Phoenix Lights unfolded in two distinct sightings on March 13, 1997. The first sighting occurred between 7:30 p.m. and 8:45 p.m., when witnesses reported seeing a formation of lights traveling southward from northern Arizona toward Tucson. Descriptions varied, with some stating the lights appeared as orbs while others described them as a solid object with a row of lights.

The second sighting took place later that night, from 10 p.m. to 10:30 p.m., when a larger V-shaped formation of lights passed over the Phoenix metropolitan area. This sighting garnered significant attention as it was captured on video by several witnesses.

Official statements

The U.S. Air Force later identified the lights as flares dropped during a military exercise, but many witnesses have disputed this claim, insisting that what they saw did not resemble flares.

Context

The Phoenix Lights have become a pivotal part of Arizona's folklore, overshadowing other local legends like skinwalkers and ghost stories. The incident has inspired numerous theories and speculations, contributing to ongoing discussions about unidentified flying objects and extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

There are currently no ongoing investigations or charges related to the Phoenix Lights, but interest in the phenomenon continues as locals and enthusiasts share their stories and theories surrounding the event.

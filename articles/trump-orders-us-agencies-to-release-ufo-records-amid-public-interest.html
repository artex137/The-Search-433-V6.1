Trump orders US agencies to release UFO records amid public interest

President Donald Trump has directed federal agencies to begin releasing government files related to aliens and unidentified flying objects (UFOs) on February 23, 2026, citing strong public interest in the issue. Trump made the announcement in a social media post, emphasizing the importance of the matter and ordering Pentagon chief Pete Hegseth to facilitate the release.

This directive comes amid heightened discussions about UFOs and government transparency. Trump accused former President Barack Obama of improperly disclosing classified information concerning aliens, although he did not provide evidence for his claim. Trump's comments have reignited public curiosity about the existence of extraterrestrial life and the government's knowledge of it.

Key facts

  • Date: February 23, 2026
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • President: Donald Trump
  • Pentagon chief: Pete Hegseth
  • Former President: Barack Obama
  • UFO investigations: Conducted by the Pentagon since World War Two
  • Public interest: Cited as a reason for the release of records

Timeline

On February 23, 2026, President Trump announced his order for federal agencies to release UFO-related records. Earlier in the day, he accused Obama of disclosing classified information about aliens, a claim that has not been substantiated. Trump also stated he has not seen evidence of aliens' existence.

In prior investigations, the Pentagon reported in 2022 that there was no evidence aliens had visited Earth. A 2024 Pentagon report concluded that most UFO sightings were misidentified ordinary objects.

Official statements

“He made a big mistake,” Trump said regarding Obama's comments on aliens. “He took it out of classified information … He’s not supposed to be doing that.”
Obama has stated, “I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrials have made contact with us. Really!”
“The statistical odds of life beyond Earth are high because the universe is so vast,” Obama added, while also noting the low chances of extraterrestrial life visiting Earth.

Context

The discussion of UFOs and extraterrestrial life has been a topic of interest for decades. Area 51, a classified Air Force facility in Nevada, has long been associated with conspiracy theories involving aliens. In 2013, CIA archives indicated that the site was primarily a test location for top-secret spy planes.

In recent years, the U.S. government has faced increasing pressure for transparency regarding UFO investigations, with many citizens demanding access to classified information.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to begin releasing the requested records in response to Trump’s directive. Further investigations into UFO sightings and government knowledge of extraterrestrial life may also continue as public interest remains high.

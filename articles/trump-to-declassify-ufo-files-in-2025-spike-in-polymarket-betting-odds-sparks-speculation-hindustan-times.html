Trump to declassify UFO files in 2025? Spike in Polymarket betting odds sparks speculation | Hindustan Times

Published 2025-12-07 20:45 UTC

Trump administration may release UFO files in 2025

The demand for the release of files related to Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs) has surged following a spike in betting odds on Polymarket, a platform that allows users to wager on future events. Although the Trump administration has not made any formal announcements regarding UFO files, speculation has intensified that a decision could be forthcoming.

The potential release of these files matters as it aligns with the Trump administration's previous support for increased transparency concerning UFO sightings. The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office has stated it has the administration’s backing to release more unclassified reports while safeguarding sensitive information.

Key facts

  • Polymarket betting odds for Trump declassifying UFO files have risen from nearly 0% to 87% within a day.
  • A trader, identified as @ster, has been purchasing "Yes" bets on the platform.
  • No formal White House order has been issued regarding the declassification of UFO files as of late 2025.
  • The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office supports the release of more evidence and reports.

Timeline

In recent months, the demand for UFO file releases has not been prominent in the news. However, on a recent Sunday, a notable change occurred when betting activity on Polymarket indicated a strong belief in the potential declassification of these files by the Trump administration.

Prior to this, the Trump administration had shown support for transparency regarding UFO sightings, but no specific directives had been made regarding the release of UFO-related documents.

Official statements

“The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office has the full support of the administration in releasing more raw evidence and unclassified reports while protecting sensitive equities,” a Pentagon spokesperson said.

Context

Previous discussions around UFO files have been sporadic, with interest often peaking during significant announcements or events. The current speculation appears to be driven by a combination of increased public interest and recent betting activity, which has raised questions about the administration's intentions.

What’s next

Looking ahead, the Trump administration has not set a timeline for any formal announcement regarding the declassification of UFO files. The situation remains fluid, with ongoing interest from both the public and betting platforms.

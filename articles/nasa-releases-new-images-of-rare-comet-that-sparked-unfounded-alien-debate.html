NASA releases new images of rare comet that sparked unfounded ‘alien’ debate

NASA has released new images and data of Comet 3I/ATLAS, the rare interstellar object that has generated speculation about its nature, during a live briefing at NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center outside Washington, D.C., on Wednesday. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the comet.

The release of the new imagery comes after weeks of debate over whether the comet could be an alien spacecraft. NASA officials have clarified that 3I/ATLAS is a natural object, emphasizing its interstellar origin. “This object is a comet,” said NASA Associate Administrator Amit Kshatriya. “But, it came from outside the solar system, which makes it fascinating.”

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS discovered on July 1, 2025.
  • NASA's SPHEREx mission observed the comet from August 7 to August 15, 2025.
  • Closest approach to Earth will be about 170 million miles in November 2025.
  • Passed within 19 million miles of Mars in early October 2025.
  • Images released capture observations from September 20 to October 3, 2025.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object detected.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2025, by the NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Chile. Initial discussions regarding its nature began shortly after its discovery, with some scientists suggesting it might be alien technology due to its unusual trajectory.

Between August 7 and August 15, NASA's SPHEREx mission conducted observations of the comet. During early October, it passed within 19 million miles of Mars, allowing NASA’s Mars missions to capture unique views.

NASA released new images and data about the comet during a live briefing on Wednesday, addressing the controversy surrounding its classification.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS is not a danger to us,” said Nicky Fox, head of NASA’s Science Mission Directorate.
“It expanded people’s brains about how magical the universe could be,” said Amit Kshatriya, emphasizing the public's eagerness to engage with scientific discoveries.

Context

The debate over 3I/ATLAS intensified after Dr. Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysicist, suggested it could be an alien technology based on its characteristics. Loeb has been vocal about his concerns regarding government transparency on UFO-related issues.

NASA has reinforced that 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet, despite the ongoing speculation. The agency has stated that the comet poses no risk to Earth.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth and will release further data from ongoing observations. The comet is expected to leave the solar system permanently after its current pass.

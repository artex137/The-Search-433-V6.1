NASA Responds to Donald Trump’s UFO Declassification Push With a Reality Check on Aliens

President Donald Trump has ordered Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to urgently declassify UFO files, according to a statement made on Air Force One. Trump's request, made during a flight, follows former President Barack Obama’s comments about the existence of aliens in a recent podcast.

This development has sparked significant public interest and debate. However, NASA has responded by clarifying that there are no files confirming the existence of extraterrestrial beings. NASA Press Secretary Bethany Stevens stated that the agency continues to uphold transparency and open science practices.

Key facts

  • Date: February 20, 2026
  • Location: Air Force One
  • Agency: NASA
  • Official Quote: “There exists nothing today that says any alien or any alien machine has ever landed on the planet Earth.” - Dr. Gentry Lee
  • Public Reaction: Mixed responses on social media

Timeline

On February 20, 2026, President Trump announced his intention to declassify UFO files while aboard Air Force One. This announcement came shortly after Barack Obama discussed the possibility of alien life on a podcast.

In response, NASA issued a statement clarifying that there are no files supporting the existence of extraterrestrial beings. Bethany Stevens emphasized the agency's commitment to transparency and open science.

Dr. Gentry Lee, a prominent NASA scientist, reiterated that claims of alien visitation are based on misinformation.

Official statements

“We continue to embrace President Trump’s open science commitment as an agency. As [NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman] has said, there are certainly things he’s come across in the job that he can’t explain… but they relate more to unnecessarily costly programs than they do to extraterrestrial life!” - Bethany Stevens
“There exists nothing today that says any alien or any alien machine has ever landed on the planet Earth. If you believe otherwise, you are being misled.” - Dr. Gentry Lee

Context

NASA has a long-standing practice of maintaining transparency about its research and data. The agency has consistently made efforts to encourage public participation in the scientific process. The recent statements come after years of speculation regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial life, which have often been fueled by popular culture and public interest.

What’s next

NASA will continue to make its data publicly available and engage the public in scientific discussions. There are currently no scheduled investigations or releases related to UFO files, as agency officials have stated that no credible evidence exists to support claims of alien life.

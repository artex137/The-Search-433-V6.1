The 1975 Lumberton UFO Wave: Police Across North Carolina Report a Silent V-Shaped Craft

In the early morning hours of April 3, 1975, police officers in Lumberton, North Carolina, reported sightings of a silent V-shaped craft following a violent thunderstorm. Over the next week, more than thirty law enforcement officers across a seven-county area observed the same unusual object, described as having rows of red and red-green lights, maneuvering silently through the night sky.

This incident matters as it represents one of the most unusual law enforcement UFO cases in North Carolina. Authorities from the Robeson County Sheriff’s Department and investigators from the Center for UFO Studies (CUFOS) have compiled reports from witnesses, highlighting the consistency of their accounts despite geographical distances.

Key facts

  • Date of first sightings: April 3, 1975
  • Location: Lumberton, North Carolina, extending across seven counties
  • Number of witnesses: More than thirty law enforcement officers
  • Characteristics of the craft: V-shaped, lined with red and red-green lights, silent operation
  • Duration of sightings: April 3 to April 9, 1975

Timeline

On April 3, at approximately 1:45 a.m., three Lumberton police officers—Phil Stanton, P.H. Atkinson, and Neil McCormick—observed a strange light near a water tower behind Lumberton High School. They described the light moving across the sky before disappearing.

Following this initial sighting, reports of the same craft began to come in from various officers across the area. Over the next several nights, sightings were reported throughout southeastern North Carolina. Officers responded to civilian reports and often witnessed the object themselves, noting its silent flight and unusual movements.

By early April, a United Press International (UPI) story about the sightings gained national attention, prompting journalist and UFO investigator Lee Speigel to visit the region to interview witnesses and investigate the case.

Official statements

“It just went out,” Officer P.H. Atkinson recalled, describing the light that vanished from view.
“It passed overhead without a sound,” one officer noted about the silent craft.

Context

The Lumberton UFO wave is notable for involving numerous trained law enforcement witnesses who reported consistent observations. Prior to this incident, UFO sightings had been reported across the United States, but few involved such a large number of police officers in a concentrated area. The case has drawn interest from both investigators and the public, illustrating ongoing curiosity about unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Investigations by CUFOS and other researchers continue, although no definitive explanation for the sightings has been established. The case remains unresolved, with eyewitness testimony serving as the primary evidence for the unusual events reported in 1975.

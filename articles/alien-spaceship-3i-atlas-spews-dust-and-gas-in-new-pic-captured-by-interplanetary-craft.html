‘Alien spaceship’ 3I/ATLAS spews dust and gas in new pic captured by interplanetary craft

Space scientists have released a new image of Comet 3I/ATLAS, taken by the science camera aboard the European Space Agency's (ESA) Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) spacecraft on November 6, 2025. The photo shows the comet ejecting dust and gas as it travels through our solar system. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This image is significant as it continues to fuel debates about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has suggested that the comet may be an alien spacecraft, citing its unusual characteristics. The ESA announced the findings on February 26, 2026, indicating that the tiny nucleus of the comet is surrounded by a bright halo of gas known as the coma.

Key facts

  • Image captured on November 6, 2025.
  • Juice spacecraft was approximately 66 million km from 3I/ATLAS during capture.
  • More than 120 images of the comet have been captured across various wavelengths.
  • Comet discovered in July 2025.
  • Loeb's hypothesis suggests an unusual trajectory with a 0.2% probability of occurring by chance.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025. It completed its nearest approach to the Sun shortly before the image was taken. The Juice spacecraft captured the image of the comet on November 6, 2025, just seven days after the closest approach.

A separate study published earlier in February 2026 reported a "dramatic increase in brightness" of the comet two months after its closest approach. The ESA announced these findings on February 26, 2026.

Official statements

“The tiny nucleus of the comet (not visible) is surrounded by a bright halo of gas known as the coma. A long tail stretches away from the comet, and we see hints of rays, jets, streams and filaments,”
said the European Space Agency on February 26, 2026.

Context

Avi Loeb has argued that the unusual characteristics of 3I/ATLAS warrant further investigation into its nature. Although he acknowledges it is "most likely" a natural comet, he has raised concerns over its trajectory and composition, which differ from typical cometary behaviour. The broader scientific community remains skeptical, with NASA and ESA classifying 3I/ATLAS as a natural comet.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the images taken by the Juice spacecraft to gather more data about Comet 3I/ATLAS. The ongoing debate regarding its classification will likely persist within the scientific community as new findings emerge.

What We’re Listening To: Roswell, Everest, and Art Crime

The Last Podcast on the Left has released an episode that explores the infamous 1947 UFO incident in Roswell, New Mexico, where a rancher reportedly discovered debris from a mysterious craft. The United States military initially claimed to have found a "flying disc," but later retracted that statement, leading to ongoing speculation about the event.

This podcast episode matters because it delves into one of the most talked-about UFO incidents in history. It examines the implications of the military's contradictory statements and the public's fascination with unidentified flying objects. The episode aims to provide listeners with a comprehensive understanding of the Roswell incident and its impact on UFO lore.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred in 1947 in Roswell, New Mexico.
  • Initial military claim of a "flying disc" was made and then retracted.
  • The Last Podcast on the Left has tackled this incident in a recent episode.
  • Other podcasts featured include Lost on Mount Everest and Art Crime Podcast.

Timeline

In 1947, a rancher in Roswell discovered debris on his property, which he reported to the military. The military publicly announced they had recovered a "flying disc." Shortly after, they retracted this statement, leading to widespread speculation about what actually crashed in Roswell.

In 1924, climbers George Mallory and Andrew Irvine attempted to summit Mount Everest, a story recounted in the podcast Lost on Mount Everest. The podcast discusses their ascent into the ‘death zone’ and features insights from climber Jake Norton.

The Art Crime Podcast explores various art theft cases, detailing the lives of artists whose works were stolen, including Vincent van Gogh and Henri Matisse.

Official statements

“The military's quick retraction of the flying disc claim has fueled decades of speculation,” said the hosts of The Last Podcast on the Left.

Context

The Roswell incident has remained a focal point in discussions about UFOs and government transparency. It has influenced public perception and interest in extraterrestrial life. Similarly, the climbing history surrounding Everest has captivated audiences, highlighting the dangers faced by early explorers.

The Art Crime Podcast series reflects ongoing interest in art theft and the stories behind famous artworks, showcasing how these incidents resonate with audiences today.

What’s next

Listeners can expect further exploration of the Roswell incident and its implications in future episodes. The podcasts will continue to delve into historical mysteries and art crimes, providing new insights and perspectives.

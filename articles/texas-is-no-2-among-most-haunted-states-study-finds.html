Texas is No. 2 among most haunted states, study finds

A study by Hard Rock Bet has ranked Texas as the second most haunted state in the U.S., following California. The findings, released just before Halloween, highlight Texas's many haunted locations, ghost towns, UFO sightings, and high ratings for haunted attractions. The study did not report any injuries or fatalities related to these phenomena.

This ranking is significant as it draws attention to Texas's unique haunted heritage and encourages tourism in the state. Local authorities and businesses may leverage this information to promote Halloween events and attract thrill-seekers to the area.

Key facts

  • Texas ranks second with 550 ghost towns.
  • The state has 120 haunted locations.
  • Texas reports 6,592 UFO sightings.
  • Haunted attractions in Texas have an average rating of 9.63/10.
  • California ranks first with 136 haunted locations and 16,969 UFO sightings.
  • Florida is third with 120 haunted locations and 8,757 UFO sightings.

Timeline

Hard Rock Bet conducted the study, analyzing data from various sources, including ghost towns and UFO sightings. The results were released in October 2025, coinciding with the Halloween season.

The study gathered information on abandoned ghost towns in Texas from batchgeo.com, revealing that Smeltertown in El Paso is among the notable ghost towns. The community was abandoned in the 1970s due to lead poisoning concerns.

Official statements

“Texas has a rich history of ghost towns and haunted locations, making it a prime destination for those seeking thrills,” said a representative from Hard Rock Bet.

Context

Prior studies have highlighted the popularity of haunted attractions in Texas, with many locations boasting high visitor ratings. The presence of numerous ghost towns adds to the state's spooky lore, making it a sought-after destination for paranormal enthusiasts.

What’s next

Local tourism boards and businesses may utilize the study’s findings to promote Halloween events and haunted tours in Texas, capitalizing on the state's reputation as a haunted hotspot.

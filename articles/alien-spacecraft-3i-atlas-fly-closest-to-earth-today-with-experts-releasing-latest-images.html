'Alien spacecraft' 3I/ATLAS fly closest to Earth today with experts releasing latest images

Comet 3I/ATLAS has made its closest approach to Earth today, October 20, 2023. The comet, often referred to as an "alien spacecraft" due to its unusual appearance, has drawn significant attention from astronomers and space enthusiasts alike.

This event is significant as it provides a unique opportunity for scientists to study the comet in detail. Experts have released the latest images captured during its flyby, allowing for further analysis of its composition and trajectory. The close approach enhances public interest in space exploration and the ongoing study of celestial bodies.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed since its discovery, and its unique characteristics have sparked discussions about the nature of comets and their potential to hold clues about the early solar system. The interest in this comet aligns with broader trends in space exploration and public engagement with science.

What’s next

Experts will continue to analyze the data collected from the comet, with further findings expected to be published in the coming weeks.

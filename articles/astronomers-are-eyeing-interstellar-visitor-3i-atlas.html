Astronomers Are Eyeing Interstellar Visitor 3I/ATLAS

A comet from another star system, known as 3I/ATLAS, has captured the attention of astronomers as it reemerges from the Sun’s glare. Discovered in 2024 by the ATLAS Survey, this interstellar comet offers a unique opportunity to study an object that has traveled for billions of years beyond our solar system.

The significance of 3I/ATLAS lies in its status as one of only three confirmed interstellar objects ever detected from Earth. As it approaches the Sun, astronomers have noted its increasing brightness and unusual outgassing composition, which may provide insights into its origins and the conditions of its home planetary system. Observations from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) have already indicated that 3I/ATLAS is producing a higher ratio of carbon dioxide to water vapor than typical solar system comets.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2024.
  • It is one of three confirmed interstellar objects detected from Earth.
  • Early observations have shown an unusual outgassing composition.
  • JWST observations are scheduled for December 2025.
  • 3I/ATLAS is expected to be visible with small telescopes.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2024 by the ATLAS Survey, which focuses on detecting potential threats to Earth. Since July 2025, the comet has been brightening as it approaches the Sun. Initial observations indicated a unique mixture of gases, prompting further study.

As the comet passed its closest point to the Sun, astronomers began to closely monitor its outgassing behavior. JWST observations are planned for December 2025 to gather more data about the comet's composition.

Official statements

“This comet in particular is interesting since it's actually from outside of our solar system,” said Qicheng Zhang, a postdoctoral fellow at Lowell Observatory.
“It could mean that this object just for some reason has a lot of carbon dioxide relative to water, which could be an indication of maybe where it formed or perhaps just the composition of the star or the planetary system that it came from,” Zhang added.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a rare class of interstellar objects that provide astronomers with insights into the formation of planetary systems. Studying such comets helps researchers understand the conditions that lead to the development of life and planets similar to Earth.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it moves away from the Sun, aiming to understand how its outgassing changes and what it reveals about its origins. The upcoming JWST observations in December 2025 are anticipated to provide a clearer understanding of the comet's composition.

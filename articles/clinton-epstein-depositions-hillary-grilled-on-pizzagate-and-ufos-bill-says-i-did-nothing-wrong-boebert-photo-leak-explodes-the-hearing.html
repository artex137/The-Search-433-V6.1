Clinton Epstein Depositions: Hillary Grilled on Pizzagate and UFOs, Bill Says "I Did Nothing Wrong" — Boebert Photo Leak Explodes the Hearing

Hillary and Bill Clinton have appeared before the House Oversight Committee for depositions regarding the investigation into Jeffrey Epstein, marking the first time a former first couple has been subpoenaed to testify. The hearings took place on February 26 and 27, 2024, in Chappaqua, New York, and featured intense questioning and controversies surrounding their testimonies.

This unprecedented congressional event has drawn significant attention due to the high-profile nature of the witnesses and the sensitive topics discussed, including conspiracy theories like Pizzagate. The committee aims to gather information related to Epstein's activities and connections, while both Clintons have denied any wrongdoing or knowledge of Epstein's criminal actions.

Key facts

  • Depositions occurred on February 26 and 27, 2024.
  • Location: Arts center in Chappaqua, New York.
  • Hillary Clinton testified for approximately six hours.
  • Bill Clinton's opening statement emphasized his wife's lack of involvement.
  • Rep. Lauren Boebert leaked photos during the deposition, violating House rules.
  • Pizzagate, a debunked conspiracy theory, was brought up during the hearings.
  • Committee plans to release video and transcripts of both depositions publicly.

Timeline

On February 26, 2024, Hillary Clinton testified for about six hours, asserting that she had no knowledge of Jeffrey Epstein's actions. She criticized the committee's investigation as partisan and raised questions about transparency. During her testimony, she faced inquiries about UFOs and the Pizzagate conspiracy theory.

The following day, February 27, 2024, Bill Clinton began his deposition with a personal statement defending his wife, stating she had no involvement with Epstein. He acknowledged knowing Epstein but claimed he had no memory of meeting him. The hearings were briefly interrupted due to a photo leak by Rep. Lauren Boebert.

Official statements

“I had no idea about their criminal activities. I do not recall ever encountering Mr. Epstein.”
“What is being held back? Who is being protected? And why the cover-up?”
“You made Hillary come in. She had nothing to do with Jeffrey Epstein. Nothing.”

Context

Pizzagate is a false conspiracy theory that emerged in 2016, alleging that senior Democrats operated a child sex-trafficking ring from a Washington, D.C., pizza restaurant. This theory has been widely discredited, yet it has resurfaced in political discourse, particularly among far-right groups. The current hearings have reignited discussions around the theory, despite its lack of credibility.

Bill Clinton, 79, and Hillary Clinton, 78, are the first former president and first lady to testify before Congress in over 40 years. Their testimonies have raised questions about their past associations with Epstein and the broader implications of the investigation.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee plans to release video and transcripts of the depositions. Democrats have called for President Trump to testify regarding his connections to Epstein, although Committee Chair James Comer has not indicated any immediate action on that request.

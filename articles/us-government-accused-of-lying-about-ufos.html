US government accused of lying about UFOs

Rep. Jared Moskowitz (D-Fla.) has accused the U.S. government of lying about its knowledge of unexplained aerial phenomenon (UAP) during a House Oversight subcommittee hearing on Tuesday, October 17, 2023. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this accusation.

This allegation raises significant questions about transparency regarding UAPs and the government's handling of related information. The House Oversight subcommittee is currently investigating the government's disclosures about UAPs and seeking to clarify its position on the matter.

Key facts

  • Date of hearing: October 17, 2023
  • Location: House Oversight subcommittee, Washington, D.C.
  • Accuser: Rep. Jared Moskowitz (D-Fla.)
  • Subject: Unexplained aerial phenomenon (UAP)

Timeline

On October 17, 2023, Rep. Jared Moskowitz raised concerns during a House Oversight subcommittee hearing, alleging that the U.S. government has misled the public regarding its knowledge of UAPs. The hearing was part of ongoing investigations into government transparency and accountability about aerial phenomena.

Official statements

“The U.S. government has not been forthright about what it knows regarding UAPs,” said Rep. Moskowitz during the hearing.

Context

This hearing is part of a broader trend of increasing scrutiny on the government’s handling of UAP reports. In recent years, there has been growing public interest and concern regarding aerial phenomena, prompting calls for more transparency from federal agencies.

What’s next

The House Oversight subcommittee will continue its investigation into the U.S. government's disclosures about UAPs, with further hearings expected in the coming months.

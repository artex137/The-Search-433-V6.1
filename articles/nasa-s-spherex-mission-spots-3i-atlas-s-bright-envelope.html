NASA's SPHEREx Mission Spots 3I/ATLAS's Bright Envelope

NASA's Spectro-Photometer for the History of the Universe, Epoch of Reionization and Ices Explorer (SPHEREx) has captured images of the bright envelope surrounding comet 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the outer Solar System. The observations were made in December 2022 after the comet re-emerged from behind the Sun.

This discovery matters as it provides scientists with crucial data on the composition of 3I/ATLAS and the behavior of its ices as they react to solar heating. The mission's findings will contribute to a deeper understanding of the origins and evolution of the Universe itself.

Key facts

  • Mission: NASA's SPHEREx
  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation Date: December 2022
  • Number of Galaxies Surveyed: Over 450 million
  • Number of Stars Surveyed: 100 million
  • Mission Duration: Two years

Timeline

In late October 2022, 3I/ATLAS disappeared behind the Sun. Scientists monitored its trajectory and awaited its re-emergence. By December 2022, SPHEREx successfully captured images of the comet's coma, revealing significant information about its composition.

Official statements

“The IR images highlight key elements observed by SPHEREx, including water vapor, carbon dioxide, dust, and organic molecules,” said a spokesperson from NASA.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object detected in our Solar System. Previous observations of interstellar objects have provided insights into their origins and compositions, enhancing our understanding of celestial bodies from outside our Solar System.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS remains visible, scientists will continue to analyze data from SPHEREx and other missions to learn more about its system of origin and the complex organic molecules it may contain.

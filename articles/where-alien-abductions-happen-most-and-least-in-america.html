New Jersey Ranks Low for Alien Abduction Risks

New Jersey has been identified as one of the states where residents are least likely to experience alien abductions, according to a report by the New York Post. The odds of being abducted by an alien in New Jersey are 1 in 3,086, making it one of the top five safest states in the U.S. for such incidents.

This information matters as it provides some reassurance to residents concerned about extraterrestrial encounters. Authorities have not disclosed any specific measures related to alien abductions, but the statistics may help to alleviate public fears regarding UFOs and their potential implications.

Key facts

  • New Jersey's alien abduction odds: 1 in 3,086
  • Safest state for alien abductions: Louisiana
  • Other safe states: Mississippi, Alabama, Virginia
  • State with the highest risk: California, odds of 1 in 1,075
  • Source of information: New York Post

Timeline

Recent discussions about alien abductions have highlighted varying risks across states. New Jersey's ranking emerged from a broader analysis of abduction statistics published by the New York Post.

The report revealed a ranking of states based on their respective odds of alien abduction, with New Jersey placed among the safest. California was noted as the state with the highest risk.

Steven Spielberg talks about his fascination with things that can’t be explained

Hollywood filmmaker Steven Spielberg has expressed his belief in the existence of extraterrestrial life while promoting his new UFO movie, Disclosure Day. The 79-year-old director shared his thoughts in a behind-the-scenes video released by Universal Pictures.

This statement is significant as it reflects a growing public interest in UFOs and the concept of alien life. Spielberg noted that the fascination has reached a "critical mass" among people, prompting discussions about our place in the universe and the potential knowledge that may be withheld from the public.

Key facts

  • Spielberg is 79 years old.
  • The film Disclosure Day focuses on UFOs.
  • Spielberg has won a Grammy Award for the documentary Music by John Williams.
  • He has achieved EGOT status, having won four Emmys, three Oscars, and one Tony Award.

Timeline

In a recent video, Spielberg discussed his lifelong curiosity about the night sky and the existence of life beyond Earth. He has directed numerous films that explore themes of the unexplained, including sharks and UFOs.

Earlier this month, Spielberg received a Grammy Award for Best Music Film, contributing to his prestigious EGOT status.

Official statements

“I’ve always been fascinated with things that cannot be explained, and I’ve made a lot of movies about things that could not be explained, from sharks to saucers,” Spielberg said.
“People’s questions about what is not only going on in our skies but what is going on in our worlds, in our realities, has reached a critical mass of people’s complete fascination with, are we alone, or are we not alone?” he added.

Context

Spielberg's comments come at a time when public interest in UFOs has surged, with many questioning the government's transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. His films have often reflected societal anxieties and curiosities about the unknown.

What’s next

As Spielberg continues to promote Disclosure Day, discussions around UFOs and alien life are likely to remain prominent in public discourse, reflecting a broader cultural fascination with the unexplained.

NASA says 3I/ATLAS is 'not a danger to Earth' after revealing unseen images

NASA has confirmed that comet 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth, following the release of new images on October 17, 2023. The agency has stated that the comet will pass safely by our planet.

This announcement is significant as it alleviates concerns over potential impacts from celestial bodies. NASA continues to monitor the comet's trajectory and has encouraged the public to observe its passage through the night sky.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of NASA announcement: October 17, 2023
  • Status: Not a danger to Earth
  • Agency: NASA

Timeline

On October 17, 2023, NASA released new images of comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing details that led to the conclusion that the comet is not a danger to Earth. The agency's monitoring efforts have confirmed the comet’s safe trajectory.

Official statements

“Comet 3I/ATLAS is not a danger to Earth,” NASA stated in its announcement.

Context

NASA routinely monitors near-Earth objects to assess any potential threats. The agency has developed protocols for tracking comets and asteroids to ensure the safety of the planet.

What’s next

NASA will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it passes by Earth and will provide updates on its trajectory and visibility for the public.

An interstellar comet packed with alcohol? What ALMA found in 3I/ATLAS

Comet 3I/ATLAS has drawn significant attention after astronomers using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) discovered it contains an unusually high amount of methanol, a type of alcohol. The findings were released on March 9, 2026, revealing the comet's unique chemical composition as it approaches the sun.

This discovery is important because it provides insight into the formation of comets and the conditions in different solar systems. Researchers believe that studying 3I/ATLAS can help understand how organic molecules are distributed in space and may inform theories about the origins of life.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed by ALMA in late 2025.
  • Lead author Nathan Roth is a professor at American University.
  • Observations revealed methanol-to-hydrogen cyanide ratios of approximately 70 and 120.
  • This places 3I/ATLAS among the most methanol-rich comets studied.
  • Previous observations indicated a coma dominated by carbon dioxide.

Timeline

In late 2025, astronomers used ALMA's Atacama Compact Array in Chile to observe comet 3I/ATLAS as it approached the sun. The comet's icy surface warmed, causing it to release gas and dust and form a coma. The team analyzed this coma to identify the chemical composition of the comet.

The findings were published on March 9, 2026, highlighting significant levels of methanol compared to hydrogen cyanide, which is uncommon in comets from our solar system.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” said Nathan Roth. “The details reveal what it's made of, and it's bursting with methanol in a way we just don't usually see in comets in our own solar system.”

Context

This discovery builds on previous research conducted with the James Webb Space Telescope, which showed that 3I/ATLAS had a coma largely composed of carbon dioxide when it was farther from the sun. The high methanol content adds to the understanding of the comet's unique chemical inventory.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue studying 3I/ATLAS to gain further insights into its composition and the conditions that led to its formation. Future observations will likely focus on how different molecules behave as the comet approaches the sun.

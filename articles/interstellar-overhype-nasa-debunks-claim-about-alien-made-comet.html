Interstellar overhype: Nasa debunks claim about alien-made comet

Nasa has dismissed a theory proposed by Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb, which suggested that Comet 3I/ATLAS, set for a close pass with Mars next month, could be an artificial object created by an extraterrestrial civilization. The comet was discovered on 1 July by the Asteroid Terrestrial-Impact Last Alert System (Atlas) survey telescope in Río Hurtado, Chile. Nasa stated that the comet poses no threat to Earth.

This matter is significant as it highlights the ongoing search for extraterrestrial intelligence and the scientific scrutiny such claims receive. Nasa has been observing Comet 3I/ATLAS closely using ground and space telescopes since its discovery, and experts emphasize that the object behaves like a natural comet.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025 in Río Hurtado, Chile.
  • The comet is approximately 5.6 km wide and travels at 209,000 km/h.
  • Nasa has stated it poses no threat to Earth.
  • The comet will make close passes with Mars, Jupiter, and Venus.
  • Avi Loeb is the head of Harvard University’s Galileo Project.

Timeline

On 1 July 2025, Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered by the Atlas survey telescope in Río Hurtado, Chile. Following its discovery, Nasa began monitoring the comet with various telescopes. In a July scientific paper, Avi Loeb presented his hypothesis regarding the comet potentially being an artificial object. In September, Nasa publicly refuted Loeb's claims, asserting that the comet behaves like a natural celestial body.

Official statements

Nasa’s lead scientist for solar system small bodies, Tom Statler, stated, “It looks like a comet. It does comet things. It very, very strongly resembles, in just about every way, the comets that we know.”
Statler added, “The evidence is overwhelmingly pointing to this object being a natural body. It’s a comet.”

Context

This incident comes amid increasing interest in the search for extraterrestrial life, especially following previous claims about unidentified aerial phenomena and other celestial observations. Loeb’s hypothesis, while provocative, reflects a broader trend in the scientific community to explore unconventional ideas while maintaining rigorous standards of evidence.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Mars in the coming weeks, gathering more data to enhance understanding of interstellar comets and their characteristics.

Mystery space comet is 'full of booze' as scientists baffled by discovery

Scientists have discovered that the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is heavily enriched with alcohol, specifically methanol. This finding was made using the ALMA telescope in Chile, where researchers found that the comet contains up to four times more methanol than typical comets from our solar system. The study highlights that 3I/ATLAS is the second most methanol-rich comet ever measured.

This discovery is significant as it suggests that 3I/ATLAS formed in a much colder or chemically unique environment than those found in our solar system. Researchers are now investigating the comet further, particularly its unusual chemical composition, which includes high levels of carbon dioxide, iron, and nitrogen.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is heavily enriched in methanol.
  • It contains up to four times more methanol than typical solar system comets.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the second most methanol-rich comet ever measured.
  • It is currently moving away from our solar system at 60 kilometres per second.
  • Researchers used the ALMA telescope in Chile for this study.
  • High levels of carbon dioxide, iron, and nitrogen have also been detected.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first observed in 2019 as it made its approach to the solar system. The recent study revealing its unique chemical composition was published following extensive observations made by researchers using the ALMA telescope.

As the comet continues its journey, it is classified as the third confirmed interstellar object ever spotted.

Official statements

“A significant fraction of methanol came from detached ices that sublimated during the comet's approach to the sun,” researchers noted regarding the comet's chemical composition.
Despite the excitement, scientists emphasized that this “natural, extremely cold, and chemically complex object” is definitely not the work of aliens.

Context

Prior to this discovery, interstellar comets have generated significant interest in the scientific community due to their potential to provide insights into the formation of the solar system. 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing body of knowledge regarding such objects, particularly in relation to their chemical properties.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue studying 3I/ATLAS to better understand its formation and the implications of its unique chemical makeup. Further observations may provide more insights into the comet’s behavior as it moves away from the solar system.

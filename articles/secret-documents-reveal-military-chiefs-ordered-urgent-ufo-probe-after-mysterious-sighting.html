Secret documents reveal military chiefs ordered urgent UFO probe after mysterious sighting

Military intelligence officials have ordered staff to investigate unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) following concerns that alien craft could outmaneuver military aircraft. Secret documents released from the National Archives detail these urgent probes into UFO technology, particularly during the 1990s.

The significance of these findings emphasizes the military's recognition of potential threats posed by UAPs to national defense. The Ministry of Defence has highlighted the need to understand these phenomena, which may represent advanced technology not currently possessed by the military.

Key facts

  • Documents released from the National Archives include memos from the 1990s.
  • One memo from 1997 refers to UFOs as unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs).
  • Reported sightings of large, low-flying black triangles occurred in Belgium in 1989 and 1990.
  • A notable incident involved the Rendlesham Forest sighting in Suffolk in 1980.
  • The Ministry of Defence has stated that UAPs may pose potential threats to national defense.

Timeline

In 1980, U.S. Airforce personnel reported a “craft of unknown origin” in Rendlesham Forest, Suffolk, an event that has since been marked by a sculpture at the site.

From 1989 to 1990, numerous sightings of large, low-flying black triangles were documented in Belgium, leading to increased military scrutiny.

In 1997, internal memos from the Ministry of Defence began to address UAPs directly, acknowledging the potential risks associated with these sightings.

Official statements

“If significant numbers are reporting seeing strange objects in the sky then there may be a basis in fact. It could be argued that UAPs pose a potential threat to the defence of the realm since we have no idea what they are!”
“The reported technology, if true, represents technology we do not have. No matter the origin, the determination of technology, and possible acquisition, is a matter for Defence Intelligence Staff.”

Context

The Ministry of Defence has released thousands of secret files to public archives after over 20 years, revealing a history of military interest in UFOs and UAPs. Previous incidents, such as the Rendlesham Forest sighting, have fueled ongoing public interest and speculation about the existence of extraterrestrial technology.

What’s next

Investigations into UAPs will continue as the Ministry of Defence seeks to understand the implications of these sightings for national security and military capabilities.

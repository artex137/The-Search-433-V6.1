3I/ATLAS: NASA and ISRO capture interstellar comet images; discover what it reveals about distant worlds

Published 2025-11-20 06:41 UTC

NASA and ISRO Capture Images of Comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) have captured new images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing significant details about its composition and behavior. The images were taken on November 19, 2025, as the comet approached the inner solar system.

This discovery is important as it enhances the understanding of comets and their role in the solar system. NASA and ISRO are now analyzing the data to learn more about the comet's structure and the implications for other celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Event Date: November 19, 2025
  • Agencies Involved: NASA, ISRO
  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Key Findings: New insights into comet composition
  • Location of Observations: Not disclosed

Timeline

On November 19, 2025, NASA and ISRO successfully captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS. The collaboration aimed to study the comet's characteristics as it neared the inner solar system.

Following the imaging, both agencies have begun a detailed analysis of the data collected to understand the comet's structure and its implications for planetary science.

Official statements

“The images provide a unique window into the composition of this interstellar visitor,” said a NASA spokesperson. “We are excited to share these findings with the scientific community.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 and is notable for being the first interstellar comet detected by astronomers. Its study contributes to ongoing research into the origins and evolution of celestial bodies in the solar system.

What’s next

NASA and ISRO will continue to analyze the images and data collected, aiming to release further findings in the coming months. The research may lead to new insights into the nature of comets and their formation.

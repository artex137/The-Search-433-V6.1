The Mysterious Object Cruising Toward Mars Just Did Something Very Peculiar

A mysterious object from interstellar space, dubbed 3I/ATLAS, is currently travelling through our solar system and is expected to pass by Mars and Jupiter in the coming months. The object, which was discovered in July, is believed to be a comet made of ice and dust particles. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this astronomical event.

Astronomers have expressed significant interest in 3I/ATLAS due to its unusual characteristics. As it approaches its closest point to the Sun, the object has begun to show increased activity, shedding gases and dust that are forming a growing tail. Notably, according to images captured by NASA's Hubble Space Telescope, 3I/ATLAS has developed a unique "anti-tail" that points towards the Sun, a phenomenon not previously reported for solar system comets.

Key facts

  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: July 2023
  • Expected Closest Approach: Not disclosed
  • Location: Near Mars and Jupiter
  • Primary Composition: Carbon dioxide and water ice
  • Distinct Features: Anti-tail pointing towards the Sun
  • Previous Interstellar Objects: 1I/'Oumuamua (2017), 2I/Borisov (2019)

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2023. As it nears the Sun, the object has shown increased activity, shedding gas and dust. Recent observations have revealed the formation of an anti-tail, which has intrigued astronomers.

Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb has suggested that this anti-tail could be caused by ice fragments released from 3I/ATLAS's surface. He noted that the primary mass of the object is carbon dioxide, while the longer-lasting grains are primarily made of water ice.

Official statements

“The anti-tail is an extension of the glow of scattered sunlight around 3I/ATLAS towards the Sun and not away from it — as typically the case for comets,” said Avi Loeb.
“Natural comets generically show iron and nickel simultaneously, as both elements are produced together in the ejecta of supernova explosions,” Loeb explained regarding the object's composition.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object detected, following 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Unlike its predecessors, 3I/ATLAS exhibits signs of gas and dust activity, providing a unique opportunity for scientists to study its characteristics as it approaches the Sun.

The phenomenon of an anti-tail, as observed in this object, has never been reported before for solar system comets, prompting further investigation into its implications and causes.

What’s next

Astronomers plan to utilize NASA and European Space Agency space probes to observe 3I/ATLAS more closely as it approaches the Sun. As the object continues its journey, time is limited for researchers to gather data and conduct further analysis.

The 1966 Michigan UFO Case That Reached Congress

A UFO sighting in 1966 near Dexter, Michigan, has drawn renewed attention following recent interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. The incident began when farmer Frank Mannor, along with his wife and son, reported seeing a "strange light" in a swamp on March 9, 1966. Witnesses, including police officers and Hillsdale College students, numbered around 40, but no injuries or fatalities were reported.

This case is significant as it prompted an investigation by the Air Force and led to congressional scrutiny. Congressman Gerald R. Ford requested a hearing after the Air Force's explanation, which he termed "flippant," failed to satisfy local residents who experienced the sightings. The renewed interest in UFOs, especially since the Pentagon's release of UFO videos in 2020, has highlighted the ongoing fascination with such phenomena.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: March 9, 1966
  • Location: Near Dexter, Michigan
  • Number of witnesses: ~40
  • Investigating Air Force physicist: Dr. J. Allen Hynek
  • Congressional request for hearing: Gerald R. Ford

Timeline

On March 9, 1966, Frank Mannor and his family observed a strange light that changed colors and landed in a nearby swamp. After the sighting, Mannor reported the event to local police.

Multiple witnesses, including police officers, corroborated the sighting. The Air Force dispatched Dr. J. Allen Hynek to investigate the matter under Project Blue Book.

Dr. Hynek concluded that the lights were a natural phenomenon known as "swamp gas," which caused public outrage and led to Congressman Ford's request for a congressional hearing.

Official statements

“People did, in fact, see something at night, but what they saw was a rare phenomenon called 'swamp gas' that occurs during a spring thaw,” said Dr. J. Allen Hynek during a press conference.
“I regret ever mentioning the sightings,” Frank Mannor stated in an interview with Walter Cronkite, reflecting on the backlash he faced from the public.

Context

The Dexter sighting is part of a broader history of UFO reports in the United States, with the Air Force actively investigating such claims through Project Blue Book from 1952 to 1969. The focus on UFOs has surged in recent years, particularly following the Pentagon's release of previously classified videos showing unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

There are currently no ongoing investigations specific to the 1966 case, but the renewed interest in UFOs continues to prompt discussions about transparency from government agencies regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Published 2025-09-19 11:16 UTC

Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Defies Scientific Norms

In the summer of 2025, astronomers using the ATLAS telescope in Chile discovered the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. This comet has drawn significant attention due to its unusual chemical composition, trajectory, and rapid speed of 130,000 mph, challenging established astronomical norms. Currently, it is nearing the Sun and is expected to pass close to Mars in October 2025 before making its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025.

The significance of 3I/ATLAS lies in its potential to enhance our understanding of interstellar objects and the early solar system. Authorities in the scientific community are closely monitoring its trajectory and characteristics, as they may provide insights into the formation and behavior of celestial bodies beyond our solar system.

Key facts

  • Discovered in summer 2025 using the ATLAS telescope in Chile.
  • Speed: Approximately 130,000 mph.
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025.
  • Expected to pass close to Mars in October 2025.
  • Only the third confirmed interstellar object after 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • Unusual composition: Dominated by carbon dioxide (CO₂) rather than water.
  • Size estimates: Nucleus may range from 3 miles to as much as 12 miles in diameter.

Timeline

In summer 2025, astronomers discovered 3I/ATLAS while observing the night sky from Chile. As it approached the Sun, scientists noted its high speed and retrograde trajectory, which is unusual for interstellar objects. In October 2025, the comet will pass close to Mars, allowing for detailed observations. By late October, it will be near the Sun, obscuring it from view due to glare. It will reappear in early December, with the closest approach to Earth occurring on December 19, 2025.

Official statements

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object confirmed to enter our solar system, following the discoveries of 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Its peculiar behavior and chemical makeup challenge existing models of celestial formation and behavior, prompting a reevaluation of current astronomical theories.

What’s next

Scientists plan to conduct further observations of 3I/ATLAS during its close approach to Mars and Earth. These observations may provide valuable data to enhance our understanding of interstellar objects and their origins.

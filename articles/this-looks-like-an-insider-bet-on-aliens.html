This Looks Like an Insider Bet on Aliens

On Monday night, a trader placed a significant bet on the prediction market Kalshi, wagering nearly $100,000 that the Trump administration will confirm the existence of alien life or technology by the end of December. This bet was made at 7:45 p.m. ET and was followed by another wager of almost double the amount about 35 minutes later, according to data reviewed by The Atlantic.

This unusual activity has raised questions about potential insider knowledge among traders. Authorities and market analysts are now examining the implications of such large bets, especially in the context of recent comments by former President Barack Obama regarding extraterrestrial life, which have sparked renewed interest in the alien-prediction market.

Key facts

  • Time of initial bet: 7:45 p.m. ET, Monday night
  • Initial wager: Nearly $100,000
  • Follow-up wager: Almost double the initial amount
  • Market activity: About $1 million traded in early February
  • Previous notable comment: Barack Obama stated, “They’re real, but I haven’t seen ’em.”
  • Kalshi’s response: Reported an employee for improper trading

Timeline

In December 2022, the alien-prediction market opened on Kalshi but initially drew little interest. By early February 2023, around $1 million had been traded. Interest surged after Obama’s comments on February 20, leading to increased betting activity. The significant wagers occurred on the night of February 23, following a period of no major news related to aliens.

Following the large bets, speculation arose about the traders' motivations and whether they had insider knowledge. In response to the suspicious trading, Kalshi announced actions against an employee related to improper trading activities.

Official statements

“The other possibility is that it’s an insider,” said Ben Shindel, an expert on prediction markets, when discussing the unusual trades.

Context

Prediction markets have become controversial due to their potential for insider trading. Recent incidents include charges against an Israeli military reservist for using classified information to place bets. Additionally, a civilian was charged for similar actions. These occurrences have led to calls among Congress members for a ban on public officials participating in such markets.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to investigate the recent high-stakes bets further and assess the need for regulatory actions regarding prediction markets, especially concerning insider trading and the involvement of public officials.

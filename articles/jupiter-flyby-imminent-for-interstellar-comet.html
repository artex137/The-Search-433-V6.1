Jupiter flyby imminent for interstellar comet

The interstellar comet 3I ATLAS is set to make its closest approach to Jupiter on December 2, 2023, according to astronomers. This flyby will occur approximately 1,000 kilometres from the planet's cloud tops, marking a significant event in the study of interstellar objects.

This flyby is important as it provides scientists with a unique opportunity to observe the comet's characteristics and trajectory. Researchers from various space agencies are preparing to gather data during the event, which could enhance understanding of the solar system's formation and the origins of comets.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS will fly by Jupiter on December 2, 2023.
  • The closest approach will be approximately 1,000 kilometres from Jupiter's cloud tops.
  • This event is significant for interstellar object research.
  • Data collection will involve multiple space agencies.
  • 3I ATLAS is one of the first known interstellar comets.

Timeline

Comet 3I ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 as it entered the solar system. Since then, it has been tracked by astronomers to study its trajectory and composition. As it approaches Jupiter, researchers have intensified their observations in preparation for the flyby on December 2.

The comet's path has provided critical insights into how interstellar bodies interact with the solar system. This upcoming flyby presents a rare chance to observe an object from outside the solar system in close proximity to a major planet.

Official statements

“The flyby of comet 3I ATLAS is a remarkable opportunity to enhance our knowledge about interstellar objects,” said Dr. Emily Carter, an astrophysicist involved in the research.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with 3I ATLAS being one of the first identified. Prior to this, only a handful of interstellar objects have been observed as they passed through the solar system. The study of such comets is vital for understanding the dynamics of our galaxy.

What’s next

Following the flyby, scientists will analyze the data collected to gain insights into the comet's structure and composition. This research may lead to further discoveries regarding the origins of comets and the solar system.

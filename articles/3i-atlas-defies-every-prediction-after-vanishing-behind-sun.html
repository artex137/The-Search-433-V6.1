3I/ATLAS defies every prediction after vanishing behind sun

Comet 3I/ATLAS has unexpectedly reappeared after disappearing behind the sun, according to astronomers on January 5, 2026. The comet was initially predicted to vanish completely, but its re-emergence has surprised scientists.

This event is significant as it challenges current models of comet behavior and might provide new insights into their composition and trajectories. Astronomers are currently observing the comet to gather data on its characteristics and potential for future visibility.

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Location: Near the sun
  • Observing agency: NASA
  • Predicted behavior: Total disappearance
  • Current status: Re-emerged

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first observed in 2020 and has been closely monitored by astronomers. It was predicted to vanish behind the sun in early January 2026, leading to concerns about its visibility. However, on January 5, 2026, it reappeared, defying predictions.

The unexpected return of the comet has prompted astronomers to adjust their observation strategies to study its properties and trajectory more closely.

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in 2020 and has been a subject of interest due to its unusual trajectory and brightness. Past comets have also shown unexpected behavior, leading to ongoing research in the field of astronomy.

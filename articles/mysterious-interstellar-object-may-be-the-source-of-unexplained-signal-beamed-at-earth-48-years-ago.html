Mysterious interstellar object may be the source of unexplained signal beamed at Earth 48 years ago

A mysterious space signal, known as the WOW! signal, which has puzzled scientists since it was received in 1977, may have originated from the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has suggested this connection as 3I/ATLAS approaches Mars, having been detected by astronomers this summer.

This development could reshape our understanding of interstellar communication and the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Authorities, including NASA, are preparing to observe the object as it passes close to Mars on October 3, 2025. Scientists are eager to investigate whether 3I/ATLAS may emit radio signals.

Key facts

  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Detection: First detected summer 2025
  • Close Pass by Mars: October 3, 2025
  • WOW! Signal Date: August 12, 1977
  • Connection Probability: 0.6 percent
  • Signal Duration: 72 seconds
  • Signal Source: Ohio State University's Big Ear radio telescope

Timeline

In August 1977, astronomer Jerry Ehman recorded the WOW! signal, which lasted for 72 seconds and displayed unique characteristics. On August 12 of the same year, 3I/ATLAS was in proximity to the region of the sky from which the signal was detected. Fast forward to summer 2025, 3I/ATLAS was identified as an interstellar object making its way through our solar system.

As it approaches Mars, scientists are preparing to observe 3I/ATLAS closely, with hopes of gathering data that could clarify its nature and any potential signals it may emit.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS could be a spacecraft powered by nuclear energy, and the dust emitted from its frontal surface might be from dirt that accumulated on its surface during its interstellar travel,” said Avi Loeb in a statement.
“In case we detect an artificial signal from an interstellar object, how should we engage with it?” Loeb posed in his latest paper. He warned that an alien intelligence could pose a threat to the human race.

Context

The WOW! signal remains one of the most intriguing unexplained phenomena in radio astronomy. Its characteristics have led to speculation about extraterrestrial intelligence. Meanwhile, 3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object discovered passing through our solar system, with previous objects generating significant interest among scientists.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches Mars, NASA has unveiled a new tracking tool for the public to observe the object. Scientists are preparing to investigate whether 3I/ATLAS emits any radio signals, potentially providing more insight into its origins and nature.

Sobriety Checkpoints in South Dakota Set for September

Law enforcement officials in South Dakota have announced sobriety checkpoints across the state for September. The Department of Public Safety (DPS) has scheduled checkpoints in 15 locations, following 13 counties that were targeted in August. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to these checkpoints.

This initiative is part of ongoing efforts to reduce impaired driving incidents. The monthly checkpoints are funded by the South Dakota Office of Highway Safety and are conducted by the South Dakota Highway Patrol in collaboration with local law enforcement agencies.

Key facts

  • Checkpoints Scheduled: 15 locations
  • Counties Involved: not disclosed
  • Previous Checkpoints: 13 counties in August
  • Funding Source: South Dakota Office of Highway Safety
  • Conducting Agency: South Dakota Highway Patrol

Timeline

In August, the South Dakota Department of Public Safety set up sobriety checkpoints in 13 counties. Following this, they announced that 15 locations will be targeted throughout September. The exact dates and times for these checkpoints have not been disclosed.

Official statements

"The checkpoints are aimed at reducing impaired driving and enhancing road safety," a DPS spokesperson said.

Context

Sobriety checkpoints have been a common practice in South Dakota, aimed at preventing impaired driving. The monthly checkpoints are part of a broader strategy to improve traffic safety and reduce alcohol-related incidents on the road.

What’s next

The South Dakota Highway Patrol will continue to monitor and conduct these sobriety checkpoints throughout September, with further details on specific locations and times remaining undisclosed.

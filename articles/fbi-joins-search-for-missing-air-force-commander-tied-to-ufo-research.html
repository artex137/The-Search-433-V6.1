FBI Joins Search For Missing Air Force Commander Tied To UFO Research

Maj. Gen. William Neal McCasland, a retired Air Force commander, has been missing since February 24, 2023, in New Mexico. The FBI has joined local authorities in the search for the 68-year-old McCasland, who has connections to UFO research at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, according to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.

The disappearance of McCasland has raised concerns due to his prominent role in military research related to unidentified aerial phenomena. The FBI Albuquerque Field Office is actively participating in the search as it has now entered its fourth day. Authorities are urging anyone with information to come forward.

Key facts

  • Name: Maj. Gen. William Neal McCasland
  • Age: 68
  • Date missing: February 24, 2023
  • Location: New Mexico
  • Agencies involved: FBI Albuquerque Field Office, Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office
  • Contact: Text BCSO at 847411 or call 505-468-7070

Timeline

Maj. Gen. William Neal McCasland was last seen on February 24, 2023. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office reported the incident and initiated a search operation.

On February 28, the FBI Albuquerque Field Office confirmed its involvement in the ongoing search efforts, marking the fourth day since McCasland went missing.

Official statements

“The FBI is assisting in the search for Maj. Gen. McCasland and is committed to finding him,” said a spokesperson from the FBI Albuquerque Field Office.

Context

McCasland has a significant history with the Air Force, having served as the commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory and overseeing research at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base. This base is historically linked to Project Blue Book, a military initiative focused on UFO investigations that concluded in 1969 but remains a topic of public interest.

What’s next

The search for McCasland continues as authorities encourage the public to provide any information that could assist in locating him. Further updates will be provided as the investigation unfolds.

Alien spaceship or comet? NASA shares update on mysterious object racing through the Solar System

Published 2025-08-26 08:28 UTC

NASA shares update on interstellar object 3I/ATLAS

NASA has reported detailed observations of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, a comet discovered on 1 July 2025 by the ATLAS survey in Chile. This comet is the third confirmed interstellar object to enter our Solar System, following 1I/Ê»Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. It is moving on a hyperbolic trajectory and will safely pass Earth, never coming closer than 1.8 astronomical units (AU).

Why this matters

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS has sparked excitement and speculation regarding its origins. While some commentators have suggested it could be an alien spacecraft, NASA and other scientific teams have confirmed that current data indicates it is a natural comet, rich in carbon dioxide and water ice.

Key facts

  • Discovered: 1 July 2025
  • Location of discovery: ATLAS survey, Chile
  • Closest approach to Earth: 1.8 AU
  • Size of nucleus: Likely smaller than 1 km
  • Composition: Water ice, carbon monoxide, and high levels of carbon dioxide
  • Observatories involved: NASA, ESA, James Webb Space Telescope, SPHEREx, Hubble Space Telescope

Timeline

On 1 July 2025, the ATLAS survey in Chile discovered the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. Observations from NASA's James Webb Space Telescope revealed its molecular composition, including water ice and carbon dioxide. The comet's hyperbolic trajectory and chemical makeup have been confirmed to align with known processes of icy comets.

Official statements

“All current data indicate a natural comet, rich in CO₂ and water ice,” NASA stated.
“No evidence supports extraterrestrial or artificial origins,” emphasized representatives from NASA and ESA.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing list of interstellar objects, following 1I/Ê»Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, which have captured scientific interest. The analysis of these objects provides insights into planetary formation beyond our Solar System.

What’s next

Ongoing observations will focus on understanding the structure and activity of 3I/ATLAS, as well as its implications for future studies of interstellar comets.

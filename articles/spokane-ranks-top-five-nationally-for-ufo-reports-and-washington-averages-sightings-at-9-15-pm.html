Spokane Ranks Top Five Nationally For UFO Reports, And Washington Averages Sightings At 9:15 PM

Spokane has ranked among the top five cities in the United States for UFO sightings, according to a recent analysis of reports from the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC). The findings coincide with a significant increase in public interest regarding unidentified flying objects, with Google searches related to UFOs rising by more than 50% in the past year.

This surge in UFO reports highlights a growing curiosity among Americans and has prompted further investigation into the phenomenon. The Pentagon has released additional UFO files, and the White House has appointed a Harvard astronomer to assess the national security implications of UFO sightings.

Key facts

  • Average time for UFO sightings in Washington: 9:15 PM
  • Total UFO reports in Washington: nearly 7,700 since the late 1890s
  • Recent sighting near West Richland: May 31, reporting a shiny metal object
  • Spokane's ranking: Top five cities nationally for UFO sightings
  • Lowest reported sightings per square mile: Alaska, Montana, North Dakota, South Dakota, and Wyoming

Timeline

In recent months, interest in UFO sightings has intensified, particularly following the Pentagon's release of a third batch of UFO files. As of July 2, World UFO Day has served as a focal point for discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena.

On May 31, a sighting near West Richland was reported, describing a darting, shiny metal object in the sky. This incident adds to the nearly 7,700 total reports collected in Washington state.

Official statements

“The study of UFO sightings used data from the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC), which has documented these phenomena for over a century.”

Context

The increased number of UFO sightings reported in Washington reflects a broader trend across the United States. While populous states like California and Florida experience a higher number of sightings, the analysis also considers the area of the states to determine sightings per square mile.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to monitor and investigate UFO sightings as public interest grows. Further reports and analyses are expected as more data becomes available through NUFORC and government investigations.

NASA and ISRO track interstellar comet 3I/Atlas racing at 130,000 mph past Mars

NASA and India’s space agency, ISRO, have observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS using a telescope in Mount Abu, India, on November 19. The comet is travelling at approximately 130,000 miles per hour (209,215 km/h) and is one of only three known interstellar objects.

This collaboration between NASA and ISRO represents a significant advancement in the global effort to study interstellar comets. The data collected provides valuable insights into the comet's composition and trajectory, enhancing understanding of materials from other star systems.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is travelling at 130,000 mph (209,215 km/h).
  • ISRO captured high-resolution images from Mount Abu Observatory between November 12 and 15.
  • The comet was 19 million miles (30.6 million km) away when observed by NASA’s Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter.
  • Nasa's Perseverance rover recorded a faint streak of the comet from 29.9 million km away.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object, following Comet Borisov and ‘Oumuamua.

Timeline

NASA and ISRO have been tracking 3I/ATLAS as it approached Mars in recent months. The Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter took one of the closest images when the comet was 19 million miles away. Observations continued through November, with ISRO’s Mount Abu telescope capturing high-resolution images just days before the announcement on November 19.

In September, NASA’s Psyche spacecraft made observations of the comet from 33 million miles away, while Lucy captured images from 240 million miles on September 16.

Official statements

“This collaboration marks a significant step forward in the global effort to study one of only three known interstellar objects,” NASA officials said.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object, following Comet Borisov and ‘Oumuamua. Unlike typical comets that originate from the Oort Cloud, interstellar comets come from outside our Solar System and are influenced by different stellar environments.

What’s next

NASA and ISRO will continue to analyze data from their observations, aiming to refine understanding of the comet's characteristics and trajectory as it travels through the solar system.

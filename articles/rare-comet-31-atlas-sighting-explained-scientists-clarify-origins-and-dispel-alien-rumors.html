Rare Comet 3I/Atlas Sighting Explained: Scientists Clarify Origins and Dispel Alien Rumors

The sighting of comet 3I/Atlas has sparked significant discussion among the public, particularly on social media, where some users have speculated about its extraterrestrial origins. The Indonesian National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN) has refuted claims that the comet is an alien spacecraft, emphasizing that there is no scientific basis for such theories.

This clarification is important as it addresses widespread speculation surrounding the comet's origins. Authorities, including BRIN, are actively monitoring the comet's trajectory and physical characteristics to provide accurate information to the public and the scientific community.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/Atlas is approximately seven billion years old.
  • It travels at a speed of about 215,000 kilometers per hour.
  • The nucleus of the comet has an estimated diameter of 25,000 kilometers.
  • The coma surrounding the comet extends up to 700,000 kilometers.
  • 3I/Atlas follows a hyperbolic trajectory, passing through the solar system only once.

Timeline

Comet 3I/Atlas was first identified by the Atlas telescope system, which scans the skies for moving objects. Since its discovery, discussions regarding its characteristics and origins have emerged, particularly in light of its unusual size and speed.

BRIN has since issued statements to clarify the comet's nature, reinforcing that all interpretations are based on scientific data and observations.

Official statements

“Astronomers never speculate beyond the results of careful scientific observation. There’s no reason to suspect it as an alien spacecraft,” said Thomas Djamaluddin, a professor of Astronomy and Astrophysics at BRIN.
“All interpretations remain strictly bound to data and observation,” Djamaluddin emphasized, reiterating BRIN's commitment to scientific integrity amid rumors.

Context

Comet 3I/Atlas is classified as an interstellar object, meaning it has originated from outside our solar system. This classification is significant because it provides scientists with a rare opportunity to study celestial material that predates the formation of our solar system.

Previous interstellar objects have also sparked curiosity and speculation, but BRIN and international astronomical bodies continue to emphasize the importance of scientific observation in understanding such phenomena.

What’s next

As comet 3I/Atlas continues its journey through the solar system, scientists will analyze its path, composition, and behavior to gain insights into the history of our galaxy. Further observations are expected to enhance understanding of this ancient cosmic entity.

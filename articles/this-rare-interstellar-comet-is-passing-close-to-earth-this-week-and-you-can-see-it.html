This Rare Interstellar Comet Is Passing Close to Earth This Week—and You Can See It!

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is set to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, offering a unique opportunity for astronomers to observe an object from beyond our solar system. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this event.

This close approach is significant as it allows scientists to study the comet's behavior and composition, potentially revealing insights into planetary formation in distant star systems. Darryl Z. Seligman, an Assistant Professor of Physics and Astronomy at Michigan State University, emphasizes the importance of this event for astronomical research.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest Approach Date: December 19, 2025
  • Origin: Beyond the Milky Way
  • Observation Tools: Small telescopes or powerful binoculars
  • Significance: Opportunity to study interstellar objects and planetary formation

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered earlier this year and has since drawn interest from astronomers worldwide. As it travels along a hyperbolic orbit, it will not return after this close approach.

On December 19, the comet will be at its closest point to Earth, providing an unparalleled chance for observation and analysis.

Official statements

“On Dec. 19, just six days before Christmas, this erratic wanderer will be the closest to Earth that it will ever be over the lifetime of the entire universe. You’ll be able to get a glimpse of it up close with a small telescope or very powerful binoculars,” said Darryl Z. Seligman.
“This close approach also offers astronomers our best opportunity to look up close and learn about how planet formation in exoplanetary systems is similar or different to how it unfolded in our solar system,” Seligman added.

Context

The comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a unique group of interstellar objects that have garnered significant attention from the scientific community. Its trajectory and origin from outside the solar system make it a valuable subject for research on the formation of celestial bodies.

Previous interstellar objects, such as ‘Oumuamua, have also provided insights into the characteristics of materials found in other star systems, highlighting the importance of studying such phenomena.

What’s next

As the December 19 close approach nears, astronomers will prepare to observe and analyze the comet's characteristics and behavior. This event could lead to new discoveries regarding the formation of planets in the galaxy.

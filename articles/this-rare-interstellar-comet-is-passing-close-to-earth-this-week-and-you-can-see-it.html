This rare interstellar comet is passing close to Earth this week—and you can see it!

The interstellar comet 3I ATLAS is making a close approach to Earth this week, reaching its closest point on October 14, 2023. The comet, which is visible to the naked eye, presents a unique opportunity for skywatchers around the world to observe a celestial object from outside our solar system.

This event is significant because it is a rare occurrence for an interstellar comet to pass this close to Earth, allowing scientists and amateur astronomers alike to study it. Authorities and astronomers are encouraging the public to take advantage of this rare sighting and to share their observations.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I ATLAS
  • Closest approach date: October 14, 2023
  • Visibility: Naked eye
  • Origin: Interstellar space
  • Encouragement for public observation from astronomers

Timeline

The comet 3I ATLAS was first discovered on December 19, 2020. Since then, it has traveled through our solar system, with its closest approach to Earth scheduled for October 14, 2023. This marks a significant moment for both scientific research and public interest in astronomy.

Official statements

“This is a rare opportunity for people to see a comet that has traveled from outside our solar system,” said an astronomer from the International Astronomical Union.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with only a few identified in history. The first confirmed interstellar object, 'Oumuamua, was detected in 2017, sparking interest in the study of such celestial bodies. 3I ATLAS offers a chance to learn more about the composition and behavior of comets originating from beyond our solar system.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, astronomers will continue to monitor its trajectory and behavior. Observations will be documented, providing valuable data for future studies of interstellar objects.

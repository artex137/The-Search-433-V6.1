'Not natural' space object approaching Mars as NASA breakthrough could reveal all

A space object named 3I/ATLAS is approaching Mars, having been first spotted by astronomers on 1 July. It was initially described as a "potentially hostile alien threat" but has since been confirmed as a massive interstellar comet. The object is currently within 30 million kilometres of the Red Planet.

This discovery is significant as it provides an opportunity for scientists to gather new insights about interstellar objects. The European Space Agency (ESA) has begun tracking 3I/ATLAS with its Mars orbiters, while NASA's Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter is expected to capture detailed images soon.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • First spotted: 1 July 2023
  • Distance from Mars: 30 million kilometres
  • ESA orbiters involved: ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter and Mars Express
  • Expected resolution of NASA images: 30 kilometres per pixel
  • Confirmed as third comet from outside our Solar System

Timeline

On 1 July 2023, astronomers first observed 3I/ATLAS racing through space at nearly twice the speed of typical interstellar objects. Following further analysis, it was confirmed to be a large interstellar comet.

Recently, the ESA's Mars orbiters captured images of 3I/ATLAS, revealing it as a fuzzy white dot with an icy-rock nucleus and surrounding coma. The comet is now being closely monitored as it approaches Mars.

Official statements

"Though our Mars orbiters continue to make impressive contributions to Mars science, it’s always extra exciting to see them responding to unexpected situations like this one," said Colin Wilson, a Mars Express and ExoMars project scientist at ESA.
"We eagerly await NASA's data," wrote Avi Loeb, head of the Galileo Project and founding director of Harvard University’s Black Hole Initiative.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third comet recorded to originate from outside our Solar System, following 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. These interstellar comets are believed to hold vital information about the formation of distant worlds.

What’s next

NASA's Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter is set to capture images of 3I/ATLAS, which will provide crucial data on the comet's characteristics and composition. Further analysis of this data is anticipated to yield new insights into interstellar objects.

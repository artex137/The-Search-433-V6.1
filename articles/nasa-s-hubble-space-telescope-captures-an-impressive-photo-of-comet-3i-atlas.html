NASA's Hubble Space Telescope captures an impressive photo of comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA's Hubble Space Telescope has photographed comet 3I/ATLAS on November 30, 2025, while it was 228 million kilometers from Earth. The image, taken with Hubble's Wide Field Camera 3 (WFCC3), shows the comet as a bright spot surrounded by streaks of stars.

This observation is significant as it highlights Hubble's continued relevance in astronomical research, even with the advent of more advanced telescopes like the James Webb Space Telescope. NASA plans to conduct further observations of comet 3I/ATLAS as astronomers seek to uncover its many mysteries.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS photographed on November 30, 2025.
  • Distance from Earth at the time of the photograph: 228 million kilometers.
  • Image captured using Hubble's Wide Field Camera 3 (WFCC3).
  • Hubble has been in service for 35 years since its launch on April 25, 1990.

Timeline

On April 25, 1990, the Hubble Space Telescope was launched, marking the beginning of its role in astronomical observation.

Between July 16 and 22, 1994, Hubble observed the impact of comet Shoemaker-Levy 9 on Jupiter.

On November 30, 2025, Hubble captured a striking image of comet 3I/ATLAS as it passed through the solar system.

Official statements

“Many mysteries are still present regarding this comet, and astronomers are trying to elucidate them,” a NASA spokesperson said.

Context

The Hubble Space Telescope has played a crucial role in expanding our understanding of the universe, including estimating its size, age, and speed of expansion. Despite the introduction of the James Webb Space Telescope, Hubble continues to be utilized for significant observations.

What’s next

NASA plans to conduct additional observations of comet 3I/ATLAS, but as it moves further away from the solar system, opportunities for observation will soon become limited.

Pentagon reveals plan to disclose all UFO files as officials leak where records will be seen

US Secretary of War Pete Hegseth has announced plans to release all government files related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) following President Trump's directive on February 19, 2026. Hegseth spoke during the Department of War’s 'Arsenal of Freedom' tour in Washington, D.C., confirming that the Pentagon will comply with the president's order.

This development is significant as it could provide the public with unprecedented access to information about UFOs and potential extraterrestrial life. Hegseth emphasized that the Department of War is committed to transparency and will work diligently to fulfill the directive, although he did not provide a timeline for the release of the information.

Key facts

  • Announcement made on February 24, 2026, during the 'Arsenal of Freedom' tour.
  • President Trump issued an executive order on February 19, 2026.
  • All documents will be housed on the US National Archives' website, according to Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna.
  • Hegseth stated the Pentagon has been tasked with full compliance with the order.
  • Multiple whistleblowers have claimed that the US government has covered up information on UAPs for 80 years.

Timeline

On February 14, 2026, former President Barack Obama claimed in a podcast that aliens are real but are not kept at Area 51. This statement spurred significant public interest and debate about government transparency regarding UAPs. On February 15, Obama attempted to clarify his comments, suggesting he had provided a brief answer during a rapid-fire question session.

President Trump responded to Obama's remarks on February 19, asserting that the former president had disclosed classified information. Hours later, Trump issued an executive order requiring the release of all information related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

In the following days, Hegseth confirmed that the Pentagon is actively working to comply with this order, while Congress has begun investigating the claims made by whistleblowers regarding UAPs.

Official statements

“I did not have that on my bingo card at all,” Hegseth said. “We’ve got our people working on it right now. I don’t want to oversell how much time it will take, right? We’re digging in.”
“The extent to which they’ve been involved is literally just saying to the Department of Defense that ‘we’re backing his request. Do what you can to make it happen,’” Representative Eric Burlison stated regarding the government's response to UFO inquiries.

Context

The US government has historically maintained that there is no physical evidence of extraterrestrial life. However, the recent surge in public interest regarding UFOs has prompted renewed scrutiny of government policies on transparency. This includes the acknowledgment of past whistleblower claims that suggest a long-standing cover-up of information related to UAPs.

Trump's recent comments and actions mark a significant shift in the government's approach toward disclosing information about UFOs, aligning with a growing public demand for accountability and transparency.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to continue its review of UAP-related documents as mandated by the president’s executive order. Further announcements regarding the timeline for the release of these files are anticipated in the coming months.

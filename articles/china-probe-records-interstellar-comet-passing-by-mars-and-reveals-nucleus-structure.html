China probe records interstellar comet passing by Mars and reveals nucleus structure

A Chinese space probe has successfully recorded the passing of the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS near Mars, according to the China National Space Administration (CNSA) on March 5, 2026. The probe captured significant data about the comet's nucleus structure during this historic event.

This discovery is important for understanding the origins of interstellar objects and the composition of comets. The CNSA has indicated that the data collected will enhance scientific knowledge about the early solar system and inform future space exploration missions.

Key facts

  • Event date: March 5, 2026
  • Comet name: 3I ATLAS
  • Agency involved: China National Space Administration (CNSA)
  • Location: Near Mars
  • Data collected: Nucleus structure of the comet

Timeline

On March 5, 2026, the Chinese probe recorded the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS as it flew past Mars. The CNSA announced the successful data collection shortly after the event.

The probe has been operational since its launch, conducting various observations of celestial bodies within our solar system.

Official statements

“The data we have obtained is crucial for understanding the formation of our solar system,” said a CNSA representative.

Context

The passing of comet 3I ATLAS marks a significant milestone in the study of interstellar objects. Previous missions have focused on asteroids and other comets, but this is one of the first instances where an interstellar comet has been closely observed.

What’s next

The CNSA plans to analyze the data collected from the probe in the coming months, with findings expected to be published in scientific journals. Further missions may be planned to explore additional interstellar objects.

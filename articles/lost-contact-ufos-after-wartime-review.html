Lost Contact: UFOs After Wartime – Review

A documentary titled Lost Contact: UFOs After Wartime revisits the mysterious Thomas Mantell crash, providing a detailed exploration of UFO sightings during the mid-20th century. The film features a variety of experts and historical accounts, presenting a compelling narrative about unidentified aerial phenomena that have intrigued the public since World War II. The documentary opens at the crash site, grounding viewers in a real historical event that has shaped UFO discourse in America.

This documentary is significant as it comes at a time when the possibility of UFO files being released looms large, and several U.S. presidents have publicly acknowledged unidentified aerial phenomena. The film raises questions about the connection between these sightings and the global upheaval of the 1940s and 1950s, prompting renewed interest in historical UFO encounters.

Key facts

  • Documentary title: Lost Contact: UFOs After Wartime
  • Focus: Thomas Mantell crash and mid-20th century UFO sightings
  • Key experts featured: Dewey Edwards, Elanor Hasken Wagner, Zach Poehlein, Kyle Kadel, Blake Stillwell
  • Key event date: July 27, 1948, involving pilot George Gorman
  • Geographical hotspots mentioned: West Virginia, Asheville (North Carolina), Great Smoky Mountains

Timeline

The documentary begins by recounting the Thomas Mantell incident, where Air Force pilot Thomas Mantell pursued a mysterious object over Kentucky. Reports indicate he opened fire before being shot down, leading to his death. The official explanation attributes Mantell’s pursuit to the planet Venus, a claim that has drawn skepticism.

The film also discusses various UFO sightings reported during the 1940s and 1950s, including incidents where military forces engaged with unidentified objects. Notable encounters involve RAF squadron leaders experiencing radar failures and B-17 bomber crews witnessing strange aerial crafts. These accounts contribute to the film’s narrative of a world captivated by unexplained aerial phenomena.

Official statements

“The documentary does not claim definitive answers, but it forces viewers to wrestle with a deeper question. Are these extraterrestrial events, advanced undisclosed technology, or something stranger still?”

Context

The Mantell incident serves as a pivotal moment in the history of UFO sightings in the United States. The film suggests that the cultural and historical context of the 1940s and 1950s, marked by global conflict and technological advancement, contributed to a surge in UFO reports. This period has become a focal point for understanding the evolution of public perception regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

The documentary encourages viewers to consider the implications of the Mantell incident and similar encounters, highlighting the ongoing interest in UFO research and potential future investigations into classified materials related to aerial phenomena.

More than 1,000 UFOs reported over Canada in 2025, including lights and triangles

Canadians have reported more than 1,000 UFO sightings in 2025, including various shapes and lights, according to the annual Canadian UFO Survey released on Monday. The survey indicates that there were 1,052 reports from every province and territory, with no injuries or fatalities reported.

This increase in sightings, up from 1,008 in 2024, highlights ongoing public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are considering the implications of these reports as they seek to standardize and study such incidents more effectively. Lead researcher Chris Rutkoski has called for the creation of a dedicated office to investigate UFO reports in Canada.

Key facts

  • Total UFO reports in 2025: 1,052
  • Percentage of unexplained sightings: 3%
  • Most common sighting type: Lights in the sky (52%)
  • Top reporting province: Ontario (307 reports)
  • Reports from Transport Canada: 18
  • Notable sighting: Silver disc-shaped object near Hafford, Sask.

Timeline

In 2025, the Canadian UFO Survey collected reports from various sources, including public submissions and official databases. The survey documented a range of sightings, from lights to geometrical shapes.

Last December, a witness near Hafford, Sask., reported seeing a domed, silver disc hovering 20 feet above the ground. In April, another report from Sudbury, Ont., described a glowing orange diamond that transitioned into an orb.

Transport Canada included 18 reports from its aviation incident database, noting that these contain preliminary and unconfirmed data.

Official statements

“The vast majority of reported UFOs are easily explainable as stars, planes, and satellites, but a small number didn’t have easy explanations,” said Chris Rutkoski, lead researcher of the survey.
“Given that thousands of Canadians each year believe they have seen UFOs, and since there is a need to better understand incursions into Canadian airspace regardless of UAP origin, Canada should create an office dedicated to UAP research,” Rutkoski added.

Context

The acronym “UFO” has increasingly been replaced by “UAP” (unidentified aerial phenomena) in official discussions. In the U.S., the Pentagon has established a dedicated UAP research office. A 2025 report from the government’s top scientific advisor recommended creating a federal body to standardize and study UAP reports in Canada.

Rutkoski has been involved in investigations since 1989, cataloguing over 26,000 sightings. He emphasizes the importance of collecting more data from witnesses to better understand the phenomenon.

What’s next

Authorities are considering the establishment of a new office to collect and analyze UAP reports. Rutkoski encourages Canadians to submit their sightings to improve data collection and understanding of UFO phenomena.

NASA tracks two large asteroids as comet 3I/ATLAS mystery unfolds

NASA has confirmed it is tracking two large asteroids, each the size of a bus, that are approaching Earth this week. The agency has not reported any injuries or fatalities related to these asteroids, which are expected to fly by the planet in the coming days.

This development comes amid ongoing speculation regarding the comet 3I/ATLAS, which has drawn interest from astronomers and the public alike. NASA is monitoring the asteroids closely to assess any potential threat they may pose to Earth.

Key facts

  • NASA is tracking two bus-sized asteroids.
  • The asteroids are expected to approach Earth this week.
  • No injuries or fatalities have been reported.
  • Details on the exact sizes and trajectories of the asteroids have not been disclosed.
  • The status of the comet 3I/ATLAS remains unclear.

Timeline

NASA has begun tracking the two asteroids as they approach Earth. The agency has issued statements regarding the status of the asteroids and their proximity to the planet.

The comet 3I/ATLAS has also been a subject of interest, with astronomers investigating its characteristics and trajectory.

Official statements

“NASA is monitoring the situation closely,” said a spokesperson from the agency. “We are taking all necessary precautions to ensure safety.”

Context

This is not the first time NASA has tracked near-Earth objects. The agency regularly monitors asteroids and comets to assess any potential risks to the planet. The interest in comet 3I/ATLAS reflects ongoing efforts to study celestial bodies that may impact Earth.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor the asteroids as they approach Earth. Further updates will be provided as more information becomes available regarding their trajectories and the status of comet 3I/ATLAS.

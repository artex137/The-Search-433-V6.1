Not on my bingo card: Pentagon moves on UFO file release after Trump push

The Pentagon has initiated the release of files related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs), following pressure from former U.S. President Donald Trump. The announcement comes as part of ongoing efforts to address public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) in Washington, D.C., on October 24, 2023.

This decision is significant as it reflects a shift in how military and government agencies handle information about UAPs. Authorities have increasingly acknowledged the need for transparency, particularly in light of public demand for answers regarding unexplained sightings and encounters.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 24, 2023
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Agencies involved: Pentagon, U.S. military
  • Pressure from: Former President Donald Trump
  • Public interest: Increasing demand for transparency on UAPs

Timeline

In recent months, there has been a growing focus on UAPs within government circles. Following a series of reports and congressional hearings, the Pentagon announced that it would begin releasing relevant files.

On October 24, 2023, the Pentagon confirmed its commitment to transparency regarding UFO information. This action aligns with previous statements made by former President Trump, who has advocated for the release of UAP-related data.

Official statements

“The Pentagon recognizes the importance of providing the public with information on UAPs,” said a spokesperson for the Department of Defense. “We are committed to transparency and will continue to release data as it becomes available.”

Context

This move follows a series of high-profile discussions about UAPs, including congressional hearings that have examined military encounters with unidentified objects. The trend indicates an increasing willingness among authorities to confront the topic of UFOs openly.

Previously, the government had been more secretive about its findings related to UFOs, but recent public interest has prompted a reevaluation of policies surrounding UAP disclosures.

What’s next

The Pentagon plans to continue releasing additional UAP files in the coming months, as part of its ongoing commitment to transparency and public engagement on the issue.

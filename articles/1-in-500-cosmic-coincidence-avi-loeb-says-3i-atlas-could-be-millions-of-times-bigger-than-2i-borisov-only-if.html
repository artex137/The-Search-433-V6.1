1-in-500 cosmic coincidence? Avi Loeb says 3I/ATLAS could be millions of times bigger than 2I/Borisov, only if...

NASA has reported the observation of a carbon dioxide cloud around interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which is currently moving towards the inner solar system. The observations were made by NASA's SPHEREx space observatory between August 8 and August 12, 2023. Harvard scientist Avi Loeb has claimed that the absence of a water cloud indicates that 3I/ATLAS is unlikely to be a natural space rock.

This discovery is significant as it raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Loeb has suggested that the object may not be a comet but rather an artificial construct sent by extraterrestrial beings. Authorities continue to analyze data from SPHEREx and other observatories to determine the object's characteristics and origins.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation dates: August 8–12, 2023
  • Observatory: NASA's SPHEREx
  • Claimed mass loss rate: 70 kilograms per second
  • Diameter of 3I/ATLAS: 46 kilometres
  • Comparison: 1 million times larger than interstellar comet 2I/Borisov

Timeline

NASA's SPHEREx space observatory conducted observations of 3I/ATLAS from August 8 to August 12, 2023. During this time, a cloud of carbon dioxide was detected around the object, while no water cloud was observed. Loeb has stated that the absence of a water gas coma is puzzling given the object's proximity to the solar system's water ice line.

In a subsequent blog post, Loeb detailed his theories regarding the object's nature and trajectory, suggesting a purposeful design by an intelligent civilization.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, "The reflected spectrum from the surface of 3I/ATLAS shows a mix of water and carbon dioxide ices combined with organics." He added, "The chances of such a random orientation happening are only one in 500."

Context

3I/ATLAS is the second known interstellar object to enter the solar system, following 2I/Borisov, which was detected in 2019. Loeb's theories about extraterrestrial life and artificial objects in space have drawn both interest and skepticism from the scientific community. His assertions about 3I/ATLAS challenge traditional views on the formation and behavior of comets.

What’s next

NASA and other astronomical agencies will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS and analyze data from various observatories. Further studies may provide clarity on the nature of the object and its implications for our understanding of interstellar phenomena.

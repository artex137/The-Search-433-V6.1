Hubble Takes Best Picture Yet Of Comet From Another Solar System

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured its best image of the comet 3I ATLAS, which is currently passing through our solar system. The image was taken in October 2023, showcasing the comet's tail and nucleus in unprecedented detail.

This event is significant as it provides valuable insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar comets. NASA scientists are analyzing the data collected from Hubble to enhance our understanding of these celestial objects and their origins.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS was observed by the Hubble Space Telescope in October 2023.
  • Hubble's image reveals detailed features of the comet's tail and nucleus.
  • The comet is an interstellar object, originating from outside our solar system.
  • Nasa's Goddard Space Flight Center is leading the analysis of the data.

Timeline

In October 2023, Hubble captured images of comet 3I ATLAS as it traverses through our solar system. The telescope has been instrumental in providing high-resolution images, helping scientists study the comet's characteristics.

Since its discovery, 3I ATLAS has been a subject of interest for astronomers, as interstellar comets are rare and can offer insights into the conditions of other solar systems.

Official statements

“This image represents a significant leap in our ability to observe and understand interstellar comets,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is one of the few known interstellar comets, following the discovery of 1I/'Oumuamua in 2017. The study of such comets has gained prominence as they can provide clues about the formation of solar systems beyond our own.

What’s next

NASA scientists will continue to analyze the data from Hubble to learn more about 3I ATLAS and its characteristics, with further studies planned as the comet continues its journey through the solar system.

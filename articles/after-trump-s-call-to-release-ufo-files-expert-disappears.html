After Trump's Call to Release UFO Files, Expert Disappears

Published 2026-03-11 14:34 UTC

Former Air Force General Missing in New Mexico

Police in New Mexico are searching for retired Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland, 68, who has been missing since February 28. Authorities from the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office are investigating his disappearance near his Albuquerque residence, where he vanished on foot, leaving his phone behind.

The case has garnered attention due to McCasland's background in UFO research and his previous leadership at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, where he oversaw classified space weapons programs. The sheriff's office has reached out to over 600 homeowners for security footage and information while expressing concern for his safety due to reported medical issues.

Key facts

  • Name: Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland
  • Age: 68
  • Date of disappearance: February 28
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Investigation agency: Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office, with assistance from the FBI
  • Number of homeowners contacted: Over 600
  • Alert status: Silver Alert in effect

Timeline

On February 28, 2023, Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland was reported missing after he left his home in Albuquerque without his phone. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office began a search, reaching out to over 600 local residents for assistance.

Authorities expressed concern for McCasland's safety due to his medical issues. Despite numerous tips, there have been no confirmed sightings of him since he went missing.

Journalist Ross Coulthart has described the situation as "a grave national security crisis," linking the timing of McCasland's disappearance to President Trump's recent call for the Pentagon to release UFO-related files.

Official statements

“We are pursuing every lead and urge anyone with information to contact our Missing Persons Unit or submit tips online,” said a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office.
“We are concerned for his safety due to his medical issues,” a representative from the sheriff's office stated regarding McCasland's condition.

Context

McCasland's disappearance has drawn attention not only because of his military background but also due to his expertise in UFO-related matters. His previous role at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base involved overseeing sensitive programs that have been linked to government knowledge of extraterrestrial phenomena.

The case has raised public interest in UFO disclosures, especially following President Trump's recent directive for the Pentagon to prepare related files for release.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office continues to investigate the case, working closely with the FBI. Authorities are actively pursuing leads and encouraging anyone with information to come forward.

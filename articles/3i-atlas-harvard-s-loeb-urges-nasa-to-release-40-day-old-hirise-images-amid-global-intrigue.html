3I/ATLAS: Harvard's Loeb Urges NASA To Release 40-Day-Old HiRISE Images Amid Global Intrigue

Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has called on NASA to release images captured by the HiRISE camera of the comet 3I/ATLAS, taken 40 days ago. The request comes amid increasing public interest in the object's origins and characteristics.

Loeb's plea follows the end of the recent U.S. government shutdown. He argues that sharing the photographs is critical for scientific understanding and public engagement regarding the mysterious visitor. The call for transparency reflects broader concerns about the accessibility of scientific data.

Key facts

  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Image Capture Date: 40 days ago
  • Camera: HiRISE on Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter
  • Closest Approach to Earth: 19 December 2025
  • Distance from Mars during capture: 29 million kilometres
  • Resolution of Images: 30 km/pixel
  • Public Interest: High, with significant social media engagement

Timeline

On 11 November 2025, Avi Loeb published a Medium post urging NASA to release the HiRISE images of 3I/ATLAS. He emphasized the importance of humility in scientific exploration and criticized the reluctance to consider alternative explanations for the comet's unusual features. Following the post, Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna publicly supported Loeb's demand, highlighting the need for scientific knowledge over bureaucratic delays.

As of 11 November 2025, Earth-based observatories have resumed tracking 3I/ATLAS since it is now more than 30 degrees away from the Sun.

Official statements

“As the government shutdown is about to end, can NASA please release the data that was held hostage by politics for 40 days? Scientific knowledge should not be second in priority to bureaucracy,” said Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna.
“Seeking scientific data is key to learning the truth,” Loeb stated, emphasizing the importance of collaboration in research.

Context

The ongoing debate about transparency in scientific research has intensified with the recent government shutdown, during which NASA withheld the HiRISE images. Meanwhile, the Chinese space agency released lower-quality images of 3I/ATLAS from its Tianwen-1 probe, further fueling public curiosity. Loeb has previously compared 3I/ATLAS to 'Oumuamua, suggesting it may exhibit characteristics beyond conventional comet definitions.

What’s next

NASA has not yet indicated when it will release the requested images. The scientific community and the public continue to await further developments regarding the nature of 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth.

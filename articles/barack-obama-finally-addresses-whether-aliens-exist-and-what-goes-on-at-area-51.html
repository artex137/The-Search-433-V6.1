Barack Obama finally addresses whether aliens exist and what goes on at Area 51

Barack Obama has spoken about the existence of aliens and the operations at Area 51 during an interview on February 15, 2023, with podcaster Brian Tyler Cohen. The highly classified United States Air Force facility is located at Groom Lake in the Nevada Desert, approximately 133 kilometres from Las Vegas. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to the statements made during the interview.

This discussion matters as it touches on public curiosity and longstanding conspiracy theories surrounding Area 51. The facility has been associated with secret military projects, particularly regarding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and extraterrestrial life. Authorities continue to monitor public interest in these topics, especially following viral events like the "Storm Area 51" plan in 2019.

Key facts

  • Area 51 is located at Groom Lake, Nevada, about 133 kilometres from Las Vegas.
  • Obama stated, "They’re real, but I haven’t seen them," regarding aliens.
  • He mentioned no underground facility exists at Area 51 unless hidden from the president.
  • Area 51 is used for testing military aircraft and reconnaissance technology.
  • Workers are believed to be transported daily via private jets from McCarran International Airport.

Timeline

In 2019, the "Storm Area 51" event gained viral attention, prompting widespread public interest in the facility.

On February 15, 2023, Obama addressed the topic of aliens and Area 51 in an interview with Brian Tyler Cohen.

Obama's comments include a humorous take on his curiosity about aliens when he entered office.

Official statements

"They’re real, but I haven’t seen them, and they’re not being kept in, what is it... 'Area 51. There’s no underground facility, unless there’s this enormous conspiracy, and they hid it from the president of the United States." - Barack Obama
"First of all, I think it's important to recognise that the majority of the American people find this behaviour deeply troubling." - Barack Obama, on a separate topic regarding a video shared by Donald Trump.

Context

Area 51 has long been the subject of conspiracy theories, often linked to UFO sightings and alleged government cover-ups. The public's fascination with the site has been fueled by various cultural references and events, including the viral "Storm Area 51" plan.

Obama's comments come amid ongoing discussions about transparency and government accountability regarding classified information.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue monitoring public interest in UFOs and Area 51, as well as addressing conspiracy theories in an effort to promote transparency and factual information.

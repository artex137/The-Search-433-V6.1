Spoiler-Free Review: LOST CONTACT: UFOs AFTER WARTIME

Documentary filmmaker Seth Breedlove has released a new film, "Lost Contact: UFOs After Wartime," which premiered on streaming platforms on October 7, 2025. The film examines the 1948 death of World War II pilot Thomas Mantell Jr., who died while pursuing a UFO in Kentucky. The circumstances surrounding his death raise questions of accident or possible government cover-up.

This documentary matters as it delves into one of the most chilling cases in UFO history. It offers insights into the life of Mantell and explores the controversies surrounding his death, including the UFO "shootdown" theory. The film aims to honor Mantell’s legacy while providing a balanced view of historical events and ongoing debates about UFOs.

Key facts

  • Documentary title: Lost Contact: UFOs After Wartime
  • Director: Seth Breedlove
  • Premiere date: October 7, 2025
  • Focus: Death of pilot Thomas Mantell Jr. in 1948
  • Location: Kentucky
  • Production team: Small Town Monsters
  • Notable interviewees: Mantell family members, historians, military journalists

Timeline

In 1948, Thomas Mantell Jr., a decorated World War II pilot, died while pursuing an unidentified flying object. His crash in Kentucky sparked speculation about whether he was shot down or if it was an accident.

In the documentary, Breedlove and his team explore the historical context of the incident and the various theories surrounding it. The film also addresses the possibility of a government cover-up related to Mantell's death.

The documentary was made available to viewers on October 7, 2025, on platforms like Prime Video and Vimeo on Demand.

Official statements

“Breedlove has done a great service to those people and to keeping the memory of Mantell alive,” said a reviewer, highlighting the film's focus on the personal stories of those affected by Mantell's death.

Context

The film builds on a long history of UFO sightings and military encounters, particularly during the post-World War II era. It reflects ongoing interest and debate about unidentified aerial phenomena and government transparency regarding such incidents.

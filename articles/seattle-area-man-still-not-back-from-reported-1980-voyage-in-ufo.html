Seattle Area Man Still Not Back From Reported 1980 Voyage In UFO

A man who disappeared in 1980 near Seattle has become a legend in one of the strangest cold cases in U.S. history. Granger Taylor left a note for his parents on November 29, 1980, stating he would embark on a nearly four-year journey in an alien craft. He has not been seen since.

As the 45th anniversary of his disappearance approaches, authorities and community members remain intrigued by Taylor's case. His story has sparked discussions about UFOs and unexplained phenomena, leading to ongoing interest from the public and various media outlets.

Key facts

  • Date of disappearance: November 29, 1980
  • Location: Northwest of Seattle, Washington
  • Origin: Duncan, British Columbia
  • Duration of reported voyage: 42 months
  • Community impact: Taylor was known as one of the smartest and most honest members of his community

Timeline

Granger Taylor, a promising engineering student, left a note for his family on November 29, 1980, informing them of his planned interstellar voyage. He claimed to have been visited by extraterrestrial beings in the nights leading up to his disappearance. Taylor vanished on the same evening he announced his departure.

Since then, his story has been referenced in various media, including podcasts and reality shows, sparking fascination and speculation about his fate.

Official statements

“He left a note for his family telling them he was taking a 'Forty-two month, interstellar voyage to explore the universe,'” according to details provided by CBC.

Context

Granger Taylor's case is notable not only for its mystery but also for the cultural impact it has had over the decades. It has been featured in multiple podcasts and shows, illustrating a continued public interest in UFO sightings and extraterrestrial encounters.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any new leads in the investigation. Community discussions and media coverage are expected to continue, especially as the anniversary of Taylor's disappearance approaches.

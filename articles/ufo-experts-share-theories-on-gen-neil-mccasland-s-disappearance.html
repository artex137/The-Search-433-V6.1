UFO experts share theories on Gen. Neil McCasland’s disappearance

Retired Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, 68, has been reported missing after leaving his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on March 10, 2026. Authorities have not disclosed any injuries or fatalities related to his disappearance.

The case has raised concerns among UFO experts, who speculate on the implications of his disappearance. Local police are currently investigating the circumstances surrounding McCasland's last known whereabouts, with a focus on gathering information from those who may have seen him last.

Key facts

  • Last seen in Albuquerque, New Mexico.

Timeline

On March 10, 2026, General Neil McCasland left his home in Albuquerque. He has not been seen since, prompting a search by local authorities.

Following his disappearance, UFO experts have started to weigh in on the situation, discussing potential theories and the broader implications of his knowledge of unidentified aerial phenomena.

McCasland's disappearance comes amid ongoing discussions about UFOs and government transparency. The retired general has previously spoken publicly about unidentified aerial phenomena, raising interest in the military's handling of such encounters.

Historically, the military has been reticent about UFO sightings, leading to speculation and various theories among experts and the public alike.

SoVerA presents “Journey to a Comet”

The Southern Vermont Astronomy Group (SoVerA) has announced a meeting titled “Journey to a Comet” on March 10, 2026, at 7 p.m. The virtual event will feature Gregory T. Shanos, a NASA Solar System Ambassador, who will discuss the recent passage of interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS.

This meeting is significant as it aims to raise public awareness about comets and their composition, particularly following the heightened interest in Comet 3I/ATLAS. SoVerA encourages participation from the community to engage with the topic of astronomy and learn from an expert.

Key facts

  • Date: March 10, 2026
  • Time: 7 p.m.
  • Location: Online via Zoom
  • Speaker: Gregory T. Shanos, NASA Solar System Ambassador
  • Focus: Composition of comets and spacecraft visits

Timeline

In 1985, Gregory T. Shanos developed an interest in astronomy after witnessing the apparition of Halley’s Comet. Since then, he has pursued a career in science and education, culminating in his role as a NASA Solar System Ambassador.

Recently, the internet has seen a surge of interest in Comet 3I/ATLAS, which has contributed to the decision to host this meeting.

Official statements

“Join SoVerA on March 10 for Journey to a Comet,” the organization stated in its announcement.

Context

The interest in celestial events like comets has grown significantly in recent years, particularly with advancements in technology that allow for better observation and understanding of these phenomena. The passage of Comet 3I/ATLAS has sparked discussions about the importance of studying comets and their implications for our understanding of the solar system.

What’s next

Participants can join the meeting via the SoVerA website or directly through Zoom. Further details about the event are available on the SoVerA platform.

Retired Air Force Major General with Links to UFO Researcher Goes Missing - Search Underway

A retired Air Force major general, William Neil McCasland, 68, has been missing for two weeks as authorities search for him in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has launched an appeal to the public for assistance in locating him, while the FBI is also involved in the ongoing search.

The disappearance of McCasland, who has connections to UFO research, has raised concerns among authorities and his family. The sheriff’s office has stated that it has found “no evidence of foul play” but continues to explore all possible scenarios. McCasland left his home on foot around 11 a.m. on February 27 and has not been heard from since.

Key facts

  • Missing person: Retired Air Force Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland, 68
  • Date last seen: February 27, 2026
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Time last seen: Approximately 11 a.m.
  • Alert issued: Silver alert for individuals over 50
  • Involvement: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, FBI
  • Medical issues: Not disclosed

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, William Neil McCasland left his Albuquerque home around 11 a.m. and has not been heard from since. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office began its search shortly after his disappearance was reported. The sheriff’s office has indicated that there is no evidence of foul play, but they are considering all scenarios as they continue their investigation.

A silver alert was issued for McCasland due to unspecified medical issues, which has prompted the involvement of local and federal authorities in the search efforts.

Official statements

“Our priority is finding Mr. McCasland safely,” said Bernalillo County Sheriff John Allen. “Our investigators and search teams are working continuously, and we're coordinating closely with our local, state, and federal partners.”

Context

McCasland is known for his previous role in command of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, which has been linked to UFO folklore. He managed a significant science and technology program during his tenure. His connection to UFO research includes working with Tom DeLonge, a musician known for his interest in unidentified aerial phenomena.

His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, emphasized that despite the silver alert, her husband was not confused or disoriented at the time of his disappearance. She has publicly stated that his previous military connections do not suggest any reason for abduction.

What’s next

The search for McCasland is ongoing, with local, state, and federal agencies continuing their efforts. Further updates are expected as authorities gather more information and monitor developments in the case.

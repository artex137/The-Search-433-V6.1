European Mars orbiter spies interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS zooming past Red Planet

The European Mars orbiter has captured images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it passes by Mars on October 5, 2023. This event marks a significant moment in astronomical observation, as the comet travels through the solar system after being discovered in 2019.

This observation is important as it provides scientists with valuable data about interstellar objects and their behavior in the solar system. The European Space Agency (ESA) has confirmed the successful imaging of the comet, which is expected to contribute to ongoing research in planetary science and astrophysics.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 5, 2023
  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Location: Near Mars
  • Agency: European Space Agency (ESA)
  • Discovery year: 2019

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2019, marking it as the first interstellar comet observed within the solar system. Since then, it has been tracked as it approaches the inner solar system. On October 5, 2023, the European Mars orbiter successfully captured images of the comet as it zoomed past Mars.

Official statements

“The imaging of 3I/ATLAS is a remarkable achievement for our Mars orbiter,” said an ESA spokesperson. “This data will help us understand more about the origins and characteristics of interstellar objects.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable as it is one of the few interstellar objects detected in our solar system. Its discovery has led to heightened interest in the study of such celestial bodies, which offer insights into the formation of solar systems and the materials present in the universe.

What’s next

ESA plans to analyze the data collected from the orbiter to further investigate the composition and trajectory of 3I/ATLAS, contributing to the broader understanding of interstellar comets.

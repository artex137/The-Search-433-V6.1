Keck Observatory Discovers Mysterious Anti-Tail and Unusual 594-Kilometer Feature on Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS

A study at the W. M. Keck Observatory has revealed new features of interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, including a rare “anti-solar tail” and a unique distribution of elements. Discovered on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS), 3I/ATLAS is recognized for its remarkable speed and eccentricity.

This discovery is significant as it enhances understanding of interstellar comets and their formation. Researchers utilized advanced imaging technology to analyze the comet's composition and behavior, leading to insights that could inform future studies of similar celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Discovered: July 1, 2025
  • Eccentricity: Ranges from 6.1 to 6.2
  • Distance of nickel concentration: Approximately 594 kilometers
  • Distance of cyanide extension: Up to 841 kilometers
  • Research date: August 24, 2025

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS was discovered by ATLAS. Following this, the research team at the W. M. Keck Observatory conducted observations to analyze the comet's spectra on August 24, 2025. This analysis led to the identification of various elements, including nickel and cyanide, and the discovery of its unusual anti-tail.

Official statements

“The unusual ratio of iron/nickel suggests a complex process may be involved in the formation of the comet’s coma,” researchers stated.

Context

The study of 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing body of knowledge regarding interstellar objects. This is the third such object identified, following previous discoveries which have helped scientists better understand the origins and compositions of these celestial bodies.

What’s next

Astronomers are looking forward to advancements in observational technology, particularly with the upcoming Vera C. Rubin Telescope, which is expected to enhance the discovery of similar interstellar objects.

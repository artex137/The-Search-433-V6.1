UFO writer, paranormal YouTuber dies in suicide at home after mental health call: police

David Wilcock, a 53-year-old author known for his work on UFOs and paranormal phenomena, has died in an apparent suicide at his home outside of Nederland, Colorado, on April 20. The Boulder County Coroner’s Office confirmed the death following a 911 call regarding a mental health crisis at approximately 10:45 a.m., according to the Boulder County Sheriff’s Office.

This incident has drawn attention to the challenges of mental health in public figures, particularly those in niche communities such as UFO disclosure. Authorities have stated there is no ongoing threat to the public following Wilcock's death.

Key facts

  • Wilcock died on April 20, 2023, at his residence outside Nederland, Colorado.
  • 911 call reporting a mental health crisis was received at approximately 10:45 a.m.
  • Upon arrival, deputies found Wilcock outside holding a weapon.
  • Wilcock reportedly used the weapon on himself shortly after deputies arrived.
  • No other individuals were found in the residence.
  • Wilcock had a significant following on social media platforms, including hundreds of thousands on YouTube and Facebook.

Timeline

On April 18, 2023, Wilcock posted on X, formerly known as Twitter, indicating he might not release a show the following day due to "very intense stuff going on." He expressed gratitude for his followers' support and encouraged them to remember that "the Creator is within." Two days later, at approximately 10:45 a.m., authorities received a 911 call about a mental health crisis. When deputies arrived, they encountered Wilcock outside holding a weapon, and he subsequently took his own life.

Official statements

“Within minutes of deputies’ arrival, Wilcock used the weapon on himself,” the Boulder County Sheriff’s Office stated.
In a post on December 11, 2022, Wilcock wrote: “I plan on LIVING. Not suicidal at all. Just concerned about what happens when you prove God is real.”

Context

Wilcock was a notable figure in the UFO disclosure movement, which contends that the U.S. government is withholding information about extraterrestrial life. He authored several books, including New York Times bestsellers, and made regular appearances on the History Channel’s “Ancient Aliens.” His death highlights ongoing concerns about mental health issues among individuals in high-pressure public roles, particularly those discussing controversial subjects.

What’s next

Authorities are not pursuing any criminal investigation related to Wilcock's death, as they have stated there is no threat to the public. Mental health resources and support services may be revisited in light of this incident.

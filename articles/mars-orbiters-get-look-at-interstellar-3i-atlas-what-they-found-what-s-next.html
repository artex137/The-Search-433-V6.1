Mars orbiters get look at interstellar 3I/ATLAS. What they found, what's next

Mars orbiters have observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during its recent passage through the solar system. The observations, made by NASA's Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter and the European Space Agency's Mars Express, occurred as the comet approached its closest point to the Sun on September 12, 2023.

This observation is significant as it provides insights into the composition and behaviour of interstellar objects. Scientists aim to learn more about the origins of 3I/ATLAS and the implications for our understanding of the solar system. The agencies involved continue to analyze the data gathered during the flyby.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS passed closest to the Sun on September 12, 2023.
  • The observations were made by NASA's Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter and ESA's Mars Express.
  • This is the first time Mars orbiters have observed an interstellar comet.
  • Data analysis is ongoing to determine the comet's composition.
  • 3I/ATLAS was discovered in December 2019.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected on December 28, 2019. Since then, it has made its way through the solar system, with several observations conducted by different space agencies.

The close approach to the Sun on September 12, 2023, marked a key moment for scientists, as it provided a unique opportunity to gather data from Mars orbiters.

Official statements

“The close approach of 3I/ATLAS allows us to gather data that could provide insights into the origins of such interstellar objects,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few interstellar objects detected in the solar system. Previous interstellar visitors, such as ‘Oumuamua, have sparked interest and debate among scientists regarding their origins and characteristics. The study of these objects can help improve our understanding of the formation of solar systems.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data collected from the observations of 3I/ATLAS, with findings expected to contribute to upcoming studies on interstellar objects.

Alien-hunting NASA clipper catches comet 3I/ATLAS by accident as it nears Earth

A NASA spacecraft has inadvertently spotted comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth. The detection occurred on October 12, 2023, during the spacecraft’s routine observations. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery is significant as it highlights the capability of NASA's spacecraft to detect celestial objects. Authorities are now monitoring the comet's trajectory as it nears its closest approach to Earth.

Key facts

  • Date of detection: October 12, 2023
  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Spacecraft: NASA's alien-hunting clipper
  • Closest approach date: Not disclosed

Timeline

On October 12, 2023, NASA's spacecraft detected comet 3I/ATLAS during its regular observation schedule. The comet is currently moving closer to Earth, prompting further monitoring by NASA.

Official statements

“The accidental detection of comet 3I/ATLAS showcases the advanced capabilities of our spacecraft,” a NASA spokesperson said.

Context

This incident follows a series of observations by NASA aimed at tracking near-Earth objects. The agency has increased its monitoring efforts in recent years to ensure public safety from potential impacts.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to track comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth, providing updates on its trajectory and any potential implications.

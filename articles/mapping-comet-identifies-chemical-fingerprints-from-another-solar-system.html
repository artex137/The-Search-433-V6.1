Mapping comet identifies chemical fingerprints from another solar system

Astronomers using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) have discovered that comet 3I/ATLAS contains a significant amount of the organic molecule methanol. This finding, revealed on March 10, 2026, marks a notable observation in the field of astronomy as researchers analyze the comet's unique chemical composition.

This discovery is important as it provides insights into the chemical makeup of celestial bodies from other solar systems. Researchers aim to understand the implications of these findings on the formation of comets and the potential for organic molecules in other environments. Nathan Roth, a professor at American University and lead author of the research, emphasized the significance of these observations in a scientific context.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: March 10, 2026
  • Location of Observation: Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA)
  • Key Chemical: Methanol (CH3OH)
  • Additional Chemical: Hydrogen cyanide (HCN)
  • Lead Author: Nathan Roth, American University

Timeline

On March 10, 2026, astronomers from ALMA conducted observations of comet 3I/ATLAS. They identified an unusually high concentration of methanol within the comet's structure, which is not commonly found in comets within the solar system. This data allows researchers to draw comparisons between 3I/ATLAS and other celestial bodies.

According to Roth, the observations serve as a "fingerprint from another solar system," providing a unique opportunity to study the chemical composition of extraterrestrial bodies.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” Nathan Roth said. “The details reveal what it’s made of, and it’s bursting with methanol in a way we just don’t usually see in comets in our own solar system.”

Context

This discovery contributes to the growing body of research on the chemical makeup of comets. Previous studies have suggested that comets may play a significant role in delivering organic compounds to planets, potentially influencing the origins of life. The analysis of 3I/ATLAS adds to the understanding of how comets from other solar systems differ from those in our own.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue their studies of comet 3I/ATLAS to further analyze its chemical composition and understand the implications for the study of comets in general. Future observations may reveal more about the origins and characteristics of celestial bodies from beyond our solar system.

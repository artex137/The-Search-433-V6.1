What's the latest on 3I/ATLAS? Interstellar comet to exit solar system

Published 2026-02-26 18:02 UTC

3I/ATLAS: Interstellar Comet Exiting Solar System

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which made headlines in 2025, is currently making its exit from our solar system after a close approach to the sun in late October. The comet, discovered in July 2025, is not expected to return and poses no threat to Earth, according to NASA.

3I/ATLAS is significant as it is the third comet confirmed to originate from outside Earth's solar system. Its journey has attracted global attention, and various space agencies are monitoring its path and characteristics as it travels away from the sun and Earth.

Key facts

  • Discovery: July 2025 by a telescope in Chile, part of NASA's ATLAS program.
  • Speed: 137,000 mph (approximately 220,000 km/h).
  • Closest Approach to Earth: 170 million miles (about 274 million kilometers) on December 19, 2025.
  • Size: Estimated nucleus diameter between 1,400 feet (approximately 427 meters) and 3.5 miles (approximately 5.6 kilometers).
  • Current Trajectory: Traveling on a hyperbolic orbit, indicating it did not originate in our solar system.
  • Observation Period: Expected visibility until spring 2026 in the pre-dawn sky.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2025, marking it as a significant interstellar object. It made its closest approach to the sun at the end of October 2025, followed by a close pass to Earth on December 19, 2025. NASA has utilized various telescopes, including the Hubble Space Telescope and the Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS), to gather data on the comet's characteristics and trajectory.

In December 2025, NASA's SPHEREx telescope observed the comet for the second time in infrared light. As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey, it will remain observable until spring 2026.

Official statements

“No aliens. No threat to life here on Earth,” said Sean Duffy, then-Acting Administrator of NASA, in response to conspiracy theories regarding the comet.
Nicola Fox, associate administrator of NASA's Science Mission Directorate, stated, “We certainly haven't seen any technosignatures or anything from it that would lead us to believe it was anything other than a comet.”

Context

3I/ATLAS has been at the center of various conspiracy theories, including claims that it could be an alien spaceship. Scientists have actively worked to dispel these notions, emphasizing that 3I/ATLAS is a comet and not of extraterrestrial origin.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its departure from the solar system, NASA will monitor its trajectory and provide updates on its movements as it becomes less visible. Observations will continue until spring 2026, after which the comet will exit into interstellar space.

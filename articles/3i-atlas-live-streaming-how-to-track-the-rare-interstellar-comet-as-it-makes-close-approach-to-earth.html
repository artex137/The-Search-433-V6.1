3I/ATLAS Live Streaming: How To Track The Rare Interstellar Comet As It Makes Close Approach To Earth?

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has been making headlines as it approaches Earth, with its closest approach occurring on December 19, 2025. Discovered on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS), this comet is the third interstellar object to enter the solar system, following 1I/Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. It has sparked interest among astronomers and stargazers alike.

The significance of 3I/ATLAS lies in its unusual characteristics and the potential for scientific discovery. According to Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, the comet displays 15 anomalies that may indicate a technological origin. As it approaches Earth at a distance of 1.79 AU (approximately 270 million miles), observatories worldwide are preparing to gather valuable data. Stargazers are encouraged to observe the comet, although its luminosity of 15.2 magnitudes may require telescopes with a minimum aperture of 30 mm for a better view.

  • Closest approach to Earth is on December 19, 2025, at 1.79 AU (270 million miles).

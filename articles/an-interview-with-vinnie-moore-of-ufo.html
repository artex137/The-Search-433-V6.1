An Interview With Vinnie Moore Of UFO

Vinnie Moore, the lead guitarist of the rock band UFO, has reflected on his musical journey in a recent interview with ClassicRockHistory.com. Moore has been with UFO since 1999 and has contributed to seven records with the band, making him their longest-tenured guitarist. He has expressed gratitude for his continued ability to create music, stating, “Thank God I could keep going… it wasn’t always easy, but I kept pushing.”

This interview highlights Moore's dedication to his craft and the influence of his musical background. Authorities in the music industry have recognized his contributions, and his work has inspired many upcoming musicians. Moore’s experience offers insight into the evolution of rock music and its enduring appeal.

Key facts

  • Years Active: Moore has been with UFO from 1999 to 2024.
  • Solo Albums: Notable records include 1986’s Mind’s Eye, 1988’s Time Odyssey, and 1991’s Meltdown.
  • Collaborations: Featured on Alice Cooper's 1991 album Hey Stoopid.
  • Influences: Cited bands such as The Beatles, Led Zeppelin, Deep Purple, and Queen as early inspirations.
  • Recording Experience: Recorded the song “Invader” for Vicious Rumors in six hours, which he later critiqued.

Timeline

Moore has shared his musical journey, beginning with early memories of music in his home. His interest in guitar was sparked by listening to iconic bands. After connecting with Mike Varney at Shrapnel Records, he recorded with Vicious Rumors before deciding to focus on his solo career.

In the early 1990s, Moore released his first solo album, Mind’s Eye, which received unexpected acclaim, leading to further instrumental projects. His tenure with UFO began in 1999, where he remained until 2024, contributing significantly to the band’s sound.

Official statements

“Music was always there,” Moore said. “What you listen to is always going to influence and inspire, you gravitate toward things you like.”
“I love what I do,” Moore stated. “It’s all I ever wanted to do.”
“I can’t put my finger on what makes me whatever people perceive me as being,” he admitted. “No clue. I don’t even want to think about that.”

Context

Moore's career reflects broader trends in the rock genre, where guitar-driven music continues to resonate with audiences. His long-standing role in UFO showcases the importance of collaboration and the evolution of sound within the band. Moore’s influence extends beyond his recordings, as he has inspired many guitarists through his unique style.

What’s next

Moore continues to perform and create music, focusing on his passion for instrumental rock. Future plans include potential new projects, although specific details have not been disclosed.

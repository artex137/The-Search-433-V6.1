Hubble observations reveal complex jet geometry in interstellar object 3I/ATLAS

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has shown a complex configuration of evolving jets, based on recent observations from the Hubble Space Telescope conducted on November 30, December 12, and December 27, 2025. These findings provide critical insights into the nature of this celestial body as it travels through space.

This research is significant as it raises important questions about the formation and characteristics of interstellar comets. The data gathered from Hubble’s observations may help scientists understand the processes behind jet dynamics and the object’s interaction with solar radiation.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation dates: November 30, December 12, and December 27, 2025
  • Distance of inner jets: Up to 24,000 kilometers from 3I/ATLAS
  • Length of smaller jets: Varies by up to 5,000 kilometers over approximately 8 hours
  • Jet speed: Approximately 0.2 kilometers per second
  • Estimated rotation period: About 16 hours

Timeline

On November 30, 2025, Hubble captured images of the jets from 3I/ATLAS, revealing intricate structures. This was followed by additional observations on December 12 and December 27, which provided more data for analysis.

Scientists have analyzed the gaseous flows and noted significant variations in the jets’ configuration. The largest jet, an antitail, is oriented towards the Sun, while the smaller jets display a more complex pattern.

The observations indicate that the orientation of the jets changes over time, likely due to the rotation of the object’s core.

Official statements

“The symmetry and alignment of the jets present intriguing questions about the physical mechanisms at play,” said a representative from NASA. “This complexity suggests that there may be more to these jets than previously understood.”

Context

Previous observations of interstellar objects have not revealed such intricate jet structures. The unique characteristics of 3I/ATLAS prompt scientists to compare its behavior with other known comets to better understand the dynamics involved.

The statistical improbability of the jets’ alignment with the Sun raises further questions about the formation processes of such celestial bodies.

What’s next

Future research will involve continuous analysis of Hubble images to refine models of gas sublimation and jet dynamics. Scientists aim to determine whether the observed features are natural phenomena or indicative of technological signatures, requiring further investigation and evidence.

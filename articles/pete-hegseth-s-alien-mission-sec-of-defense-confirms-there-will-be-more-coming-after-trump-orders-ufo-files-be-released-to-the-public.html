Pete Hegseth's Alien Mission: Sec. of Defense Confirms 'There Will Be More Coming' After Trump Orders UFO Files Be Released to the Public

Pete Hegseth has shifted his focus to extraterrestrial life following President Donald Trump's directive for the Defense Secretary to release UFO files to the public. During a C-SPAN interview on February 23, Hegseth, a former Fox News personality, stated that he is prepared to lead the effort in reviewing potentially declassified information regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and unidentified flying objects (UFOs).

This initiative is significant as it marks a renewed governmental effort to address public interest in UFOs and the possibility of alien life. Authorities are now tasked with sifting through extensive files and determining what information can be made public, according to Hegseth.

Key facts

  • President Trump ordered the release of UFO-related files on February 19, 2024.
  • Hegseth confirmed he is leading compliance with this executive order.
  • Hegseth stated, "There will be more coming" regarding the process.
  • Trump has expressed skepticism about aliens but acknowledges the public's fascination.
  • Former President Barack Obama confirmed on a podcast that extraterrestrials are "real," but he has not seen them.

Timeline

On February 19, 2024, President Trump announced via Truth Social that he had directed federal agencies to begin releasing information related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This announcement set the stage for Hegseth's recent comments regarding his role in the process. On February 23, Hegseth addressed the media, indicating that his team is actively working on the review of these files.

Official statements

“We're digging in. We're going to be in full compliance with that executive order, eager to provide that for the president,” Hegseth said.
“Did you ever think that you would be the Secretary of War in charge of potentially declassifying extraterrestrial life?” Hegseth responded, “I did not have that on my bingo card at all.”
“Well, I don’t know if they're real or not,” Trump stated when asked about alien existence, adding, “I can tell you [Obama] gave classified information.”

Context

This development continues a long-standing public fascination with UFOs and the possibility of alien life. Area 51, a classified United States Air Force facility, has been a focal point for conspiracy theories regarding government secrecy about extraterrestrials. Previous comments from both Trump and Obama have reflected differing views on the existence of aliens, contributing to ongoing public debate.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to review and release relevant information regarding UFOs as mandated by the executive order. Further updates on the process are expected in the coming weeks as Hegseth and his team work to comply with the directive.

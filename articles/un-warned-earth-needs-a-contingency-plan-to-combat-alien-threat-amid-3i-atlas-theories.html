UN warned Earth needs a “contingency plan” to combat alien threat amid 3I/ATLAS theories

The United Nations is being urged to prepare for an extraterrestrial threat to Earth linked to the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, detected earlier this year. Scientists and politicians have raised concerns about humanity's lack of preparedness for a potential alien encounter.

This matter has gained urgency as experts warn of the implications of 3I/ATLAS, the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through the solar system. Authorities are now discussing the need for a contingency plan to address possible threats from such objects, which may not be entirely natural.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was detected in 2023.
  • It is the third confirmed interstellar object in the solar system.
  • Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb estimates a 70% chance that 3I/ATLAS is alien technology.
  • Loeb has submitted a white paper to the UN advocating for an international committee on interstellar threats.
  • NASA has previously been tasked to identify 90% of potentially hazardous space rocks larger than 140 meters.

Timeline

In 2023, the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS was detected, prompting discussions among scientists and politicians about its implications for Earth. Avi Loeb raised the possibility that it may be alien technology and has called for a coordinated response from governments. The urgency of the situation has led to proposals for establishing an international committee to address potential threats from such objects.

Official statements

“As far as I know, there are no protocols for responding to the discovery of functioning alien devices near Earth. A visitor in our backyard requires immediate attention because it could enter through the front door and pose an imminent threat,” said Avi Loeb.
“I believe the recent delay in data dissemination from NASA due to the government shutdown does not flag extraterrestrial intelligence but rather terrestrial stupidity,” Loeb added.

Context

This warning follows past efforts by the U.S. Congress, which tasked NASA in 2005 with identifying 90% of space rocks that could collide with Earth. However, the unpredictability of alien technology has raised concerns about the adequacy of existing plans. The mystery surrounding 3I/ATLAS has intensified the debate over how humanity would respond to genuine alien contact.

What’s next

Experts are advocating for immediate action, including the establishment of a full-sky monitoring system and the creation of advanced observatories in both hemispheres. Loeb argues that funding for these initiatives must significantly increase to ensure preparedness for potential alien threats.

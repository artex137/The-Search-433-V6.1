Live: Watch interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as seen from telescopes in Italy

The Virtual Telescope Project has begun broadcasting live views of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from instruments located in Italy as of November 16, 2025. This event allows observers worldwide to view the comet's activity in real-time.

This live broadcast is significant as it offers a rare opportunity to witness an interstellar object passing through our solar system. The Virtual Telescope Project aims to educate the public and increase interest in astronomical phenomena through this event.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 16, 2025
  • Location: Italy
  • Hosted by: The Virtual Telescope Project
  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS

Timeline

The Virtual Telescope Project initiated the live broadcast of comet 3I/ATLAS on November 16, 2025. This event marks a significant moment in astronomical observation, as the comet is one of the few interstellar comets detected to date.

Observers have been able to engage with the live feed, providing a unique opportunity for real-time viewing of the comet's trajectory.

Official statements

“The Virtual Telescope Project is excited to share this live viewing experience with the public,” said Gialuca Masi, founder of the project.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable as one of the few interstellar comets identified in our solar system. Its appearance has sparked interest among both amateur and professional astronomers.

What’s next

The Virtual Telescope Project will continue to provide updates and live views of comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through our solar system. Observers are encouraged to participate in the live broadcasts for ongoing insights into this interstellar event.

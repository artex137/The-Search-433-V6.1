Trump administration plans to release UFO files

US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has updated the public on the Trump administration's plans to release government files concerning extraterrestrial life and unidentified flying objects (UFOs) during a visit to Colorado on Monday. Last week, former President Donald Trump announced he would instruct Hegseth to begin identifying and declassifying these documents.

This announcement matters as it reflects growing public interest in the existence of extraterrestrial life. According to a 2021 study by the Pew Research Center, 65% of Americans believe intelligent life could exist on other planets. Hegseth's comments indicate that the Defense Department is actively working on this issue, although specific timelines remain unclear.

Key facts

  • Event: Announcement of UFO file release
  • Location: Colorado, USA
  • Date: Monday, not disclosed
  • Secretary: Pete Hegseth
  • Public support: 65% of Americans believe in extraterrestrial life (Pew Research Center, 2021)
  • Deadline: No final date disclosed for the release process

Timeline

Last week, Trump stated he would direct Hegseth to declassify UFO-related documents. On Monday, Hegseth visited Colorado as part of the Defense Department's "Arsenal of Freedom" tour, where he spoke about the ongoing efforts to disclose this information.

During an interview aired by C-SPAN, Hegseth acknowledged the significance of the task, stating that structures are already in place to handle the issue. He emphasized the commitment to comply with the executive order but did not provide a specific timeline for the release.

Official statements

“Of course, we have our people working on this issue. I don't want to speculate on the time it will take. We are working and we will be fully compliant with the executive order, committed to presenting this to the president. There will be more details regarding the process and the steps that we will take,”

— Pete Hegseth

“I don’t have a timeline for you yet, but we will,”

— Pete Hegseth

“We'll see,”

— Pete Hegseth, on his personal belief in the existence of aliens

Context

Interest in extraterrestrial life has surged in recent years, particularly following comments from former President Barack Obama. During an interview, Obama jokingly stated "they're real" in response to questions about aliens but later clarified that he had not seen evidence and dismissed claims of extraterrestrial creatures being held at Area 51.

What’s next

The Defense Department will continue its work on the declassification process. More details regarding the timeline and steps for the release of UFO-related files are expected to be announced in the coming weeks.

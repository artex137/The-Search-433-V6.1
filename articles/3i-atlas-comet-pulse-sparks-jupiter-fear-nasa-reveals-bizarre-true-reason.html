3I/ATLAS Comet Pulse Sparks Jupiter Fear: NASA Reveals Bizarre True Reason

A mysterious object, comet 3I/ATLAS, has sparked intrigue among astronomers after a rhythmic pulse, detected in its light, has been linked to Jupiter's moon Callisto. The comet, which is only the third confirmed interstellar visitor to our solar system, has shown a periodic brightness variation every 16.16 hours, prompting speculation and concern regarding its implications for Jupiter.

This discovery is significant for scientific understanding of both interstellar objects and the dynamics of our solar system. Authorities, including NASA, are investigating the nature of the comet's pulse, which has led to discussions about its potential links to external forces, including the magnetic field of Jupiter.

Key facts

  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Pulsation period: 16.16 hours
  • Confirmed interstellar visitors: 3 (including 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov)
  • Jet speeds: Up to 440 metres per second
  • Location of investigation: Not disclosed
  • Agencies involved: NASA, Hubble Space Telescope, James Webb Space Telescope

Timeline

The initial discovery of the comet's pulsing light has led to various interpretations:

  • Initial observations confirmed a rhythmic brightness variation.
  • Speculation arose regarding a potential alien message after the pulse's discovery.
  • NASA and SETI researchers debunked the alien technology claims.
  • Further analysis revealed that the light variation is likely due to jets of gas erupting from the comet's nucleus.
  • Researchers have begun investigating the potential influence of Callisto's magnetic interaction on the comet's activity.

Official statements

“The rhythm of the 3I/ATLAS jets may not be purely an internal cometary rotation but could, incredibly, be influenced by an external, possibly electromagnetic, force.”

Context

Previous interstellar objects, such as 1I/'Oumuamua, have raised questions about the nature of cosmic visitors and their origins. The investigation into 3I/ATLAS is part of an ongoing effort to understand how celestial bodies interact within our solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to observe the jets of 3I/ATLAS to determine whether their orientation and repetition align with natural processes or if they are influenced by external forces from Jupiter's magnetic field.

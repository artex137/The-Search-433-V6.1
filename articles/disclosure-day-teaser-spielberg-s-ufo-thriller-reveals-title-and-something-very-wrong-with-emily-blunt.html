‘Disclosure Day’ Teaser: Spielberg’s UFO Thriller Reveals Title and Something Very Wrong with Emily Blunt

Steven Spielberg has announced the title of his upcoming film “Disclosure Day,” set for release on June 12, 2026, according to a teaser shared online by Universal Pictures on Tuesday. The film features notable actors Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, and Colman Domingo. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the film’s production.

This announcement is significant as it marks Spielberg’s return to the theme of extraterrestrial life, a subject he has explored in previous classics like “Close Encounters of the Third Kind” and “War of the Worlds.” With a trailer that hints at apocalyptic imagery and disturbing extraterrestrial noises, audiences are eager to see how the film connects to the broader cultural discourse around UFOs.

Key facts

  • Film title: “Disclosure Day”
  • Release date: June 12, 2026
  • Teaser shared: Tuesday, date not disclosed
  • Production company: Universal Pictures
  • Cast includes: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo
  • Script by: David Koepp
  • Producer: Kristie Macosko Krieger

Timeline

In early October 2023, Universal Pictures shared a teaser for “Disclosure Day,” revealing the film’s title and key elements of its plot. The teaser hints at unsettling themes, including a weather forecast interrupted by extraterrestrial sounds.

A billboard promoting the film appeared in Times Square earlier this month, featuring a bird and the phrase “all will be disclosed.” This promotional strategy is consistent with Spielberg’s history of creating buzz around his films.

Official statements

“If you found out we weren’t alone, if someone showed you, proved it to you, would that frighten you? This summer, the truth belongs to seven billion people. We are coming close to … Disclosure Day,” Universal Pictures stated in their teaser.

Context

Spielberg has a longstanding interest in the theme of extraterrestrial life. His previous films, including “Jurassic Park” and “War of the Worlds,” have collectively grossed over $3 billion worldwide. “Disclosure Day” continues this exploration within the framework of contemporary concerns about UFOs and alien life.

What’s next

Production for “Disclosure Day” is ongoing, with further promotional materials expected as the release date approaches. Audiences are anticipated to see the teaser play ahead of “Avatar: Fire and Ash” this December.

NASA to Share Comet 3I/ATLAS Images From Spacecraft, Telescopes

NASA will host a live event at 3 p.m. EST on Wednesday, November 19, to share imagery of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The event will take place at NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to the comet.

This event is significant as it marks the opportunity to observe one of only three objects identified as entering the solar system from outside the galaxy. 3I/ATLAS was discovered by the NASA-funded ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System) observatory on July 1. The comet will not pose a threat to Earth, remaining at least 170 million miles away, although it flew within 19 million miles of Mars in early October.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 19, 2023
  • Event time: 3 p.m. EST
  • Location: NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt, Maryland
  • Discovery date of comet 3I/ATLAS: July 1, 2023
  • Closest approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Closest approach to Mars: 19 million miles in early October
  • Broadcast platforms: NASA+, NASA app, agency website, YouTube, Amazon Prime

Timeline

On July 1, 2023, NASA’s ATLAS observatory discovered comet 3I/ATLAS. In early October, the comet passed within 19 million miles of Mars. NASA has monitored the comet using various spacecraft and ground-based observatories.

The live event on November 19 will provide imagery and insights from multiple NASA missions. Participants will include notable NASA officials, such as Amit Kshatriya and Nicky Fox.

Official statements

“Assets within NASA’s science missions give the United States the unique capability to observe 3I/ATLAS almost the entire time it passes through our celestial neighborhood,” said a NASA representative.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable as only the third identified interstellar object to enter the solar system. Such comets provide valuable data on the formation and evolution of celestial bodies beyond our solar system.

What’s next

NASA will continue to study comet 3I/ATLAS using its range of scientific instruments and will share findings from the event with the public. Members of the media can participate in the event by submitting their information to NASA.

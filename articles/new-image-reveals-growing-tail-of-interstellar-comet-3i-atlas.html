New Image Reveals Growing Tail of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

A new image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1 ATLAS) has shown a significant expansion of its ion tail, captured by the Virtual Telescope Project on November 10, 2023, at 11:31 p.m. EDT (0431 GMT on November 11) in Manciano, Italy. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery is important as it indicates increasing activity from the comet as it travels through the inner solar system. The Virtual Telescope Project plans to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS to gather further insights into its behavior and evolution.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 10, 2023
  • Time of image capture: 11:31 p.m. EDT (0431 GMT November 11)
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Number of exposures: 18 separate 120-second exposures
  • Visibility: 14 degrees above the eastern horizon
  • Moon phase: 61% waxing moon

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was initially detected in July 2023. On November 10, 2023, the Virtual Telescope Project successfully captured an image showing a notable increase in the comet's ion tail activity.

“Looking at the picture, we see how the ion tail of 3I/ATLAS is clearly showing better and better,” said Gianluca Masi, founder of the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object detected, following 1I/‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Unlike its predecessors, 3I/ATLAS is bright enough to be observed in detail with ground-based telescopes, offering astronomers a unique opportunity to study an interstellar comet influenced by solar conditions.

What’s next

The Virtual Telescope Project will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the inner solar system, providing updates on its activity and further developments.

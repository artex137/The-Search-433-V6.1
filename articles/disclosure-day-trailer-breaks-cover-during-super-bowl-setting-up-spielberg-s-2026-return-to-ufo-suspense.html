Disclosure Day trailer breaks cover during Super Bowl, setting up Spielberg’s 2026 return to UFO suspense

A trailer for “Disclosure Day,” directed by Steven Spielberg, premiered during the Super Bowl on February 8, 2026. The film is set to open in theaters on June 12, 2026. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to the film’s content.

The trailer introduces the concept of mass disclosure of extraterrestrial life, highlighting the societal panic, awe, and misinformation that may follow. Authorities in the film are depicted as struggling to control the narrative as the truth emerges. “Disclosure Day” marks Spielberg’s return to alien-themed storytelling, which has been absent from his recent works.

Key facts

  • Trailer premiered during the Super Bowl on February 8, 2026
  • Film release date: June 12, 2026
  • Starring: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Colman Domingo, Eve Hewson
  • Focus on mass public reaction to extraterrestrial revelation
  • Trailer emphasizes media shockwaves and competing interpretations

Timeline

On February 8, 2026, the trailer for “Disclosure Day” was released during the Super Bowl, capturing immediate attention and shifting conversations about upcoming films. The storyline centers on a Kansas City meteorologist, portrayed by Emily Blunt, as she navigates a sudden and overwhelming event that reveals extraterrestrial existence.

The trailer showcases rapid imagery of societal responses, including anxious crowds and breaking transmissions, suggesting a collective struggle to interpret the unfolding reality. Spielberg’s approach appears to focus on the human experience in the face of such a revelation.

Official statements

“The trailer frames disclosure as a human stress test: whether certainty brings relief—or an even more terrifying kind of chaos,” said a film analyst.

Context

The title “Disclosure Day” resonates culturally, tapping into decades of UFO lore and the notion of withheld truths. Spielberg’s previous works have often explored themes of wonder and dread related to extraterrestrial life, but this film appears to address the implications of instant communication and public discourse in the digital age.

What’s next

The film is set for a broad release on June 12, 2026, with significant marketing efforts expected to position it as a major summer event. The studio aims to engage audiences with a fresh narrative while capitalizing on the cultural relevance of its themes.

‘The Age of Disclosure’ Review: Release the Extraterrestrial Files

The documentary The Age of Disclosure has premiered on November 20, 2025, presenting claims by former U.S. government officials regarding unidentified anomalous phenomena (U.A.P.), previously known as UFOs. The film features insights from Luis Elizondo, who led the Pentagon’s Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program until 2017.

This documentary is significant as it calls for the U.S. government to reveal what it knows about U.A.P. Elizondo and other contributors argue that excessive secrecy has hindered public understanding of the phenomena. The film aims to shed light on the alleged cover-ups and the need for transparency in government dealings with U.A.P.

Key facts

  • Film Title: The Age of Disclosure
  • Director: Dan Farah
  • Runtime: 1 hour 49 minutes
  • Premiere Date: November 20, 2025
  • Key Contributor: Luis Elizondo, former head of the Pentagon's U.A.P. program

Timeline

In 2017, Luis Elizondo resigns from the Pentagon in protest of secrecy surrounding U.A.P. investigations. He later authors a memoir and appears before Congress to advocate for transparency. The documentary, featuring Elizondo and others, is released on November 20, 2025, aiming to inform the public about government knowledge of U.A.P.

Official statements

“These individuals are breaking their silence, as much as they can, anyway, without divulging conveniently classified information,” said a spokesperson for the film.

Context

The interest in U.A.P. has surged in recent years, particularly following government reports acknowledging the existence of such phenomena. This documentary is part of a broader movement urging governments to disclose information about U.A.P. and address public concerns regarding national security and transparency.

What’s next

Following the film’s release, discussions regarding government accountability and the potential for further investigations into U.A.P. are expected to continue among lawmakers and the public.

High Point Confidential: Alien invasion - Did a flying saucer really visit High Point in 1947?

HIGH POINT — Two local men reported seeing a UFO just outside of High Point on July 13, 1947, shortly after an alleged alien spacecraft crashed in Roswell, New Mexico. The sighting has sparked renewed interest in the events of that summer, which has been dubbed “The Year of the Flying Saucer.” No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this incident.

The significance of this sighting lies in its place within a broader wave of UFO reports during 1947, a year that has become iconic in UFO lore. Local authorities and historians are examining these accounts to better understand the phenomenon and its impact on public perception of unidentified flying objects.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: July 13, 1947
  • Location: Near High Point, North Carolina
  • Initial report made by two local men
  • Notable timing: Less than a week after the Roswell incident
  • Historical context: 1947 widely recognized as “The Year of the Flying Saucer”

Timeline

In July 1947, the United States experienced a surge in reported sightings of UFOs across the country. On July 8, reports surfaced of a supposed alien spacecraft crash in Roswell, New Mexico, igniting widespread speculation.

On July 13, two men near High Point reported their own sighting of a UFO. This account added to the growing number of UFO reports that summer, capturing the attention of both the local community and national media.

Official statements

“The sighting has opened discussions about what people experienced during that period,” a local historian said. “It reflects a time when the public was particularly fascinated with the unknown.”

Context

The summer of 1947 marked a significant moment in American history regarding UFO sightings. The term “flying saucer” emerged as a common descriptor for the unidentified aerial phenomena reported during this time. These sightings contributed to a lasting interest in extraterrestrial life and government secrecy surrounding such incidents.

What’s next

Local historians and enthusiasts continue to investigate the 1947 sightings, seeking to gather more eyewitness accounts and documents related to the events. Future discussions and presentations are planned to explore the cultural impact of these UFO reports on local communities.

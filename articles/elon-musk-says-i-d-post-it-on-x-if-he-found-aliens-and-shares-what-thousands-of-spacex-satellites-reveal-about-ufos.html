Elon Musk says “I’d post it on X” if he found aliens and shares what thousands of SpaceX satellites reveal about UFOs

Published 2026-01-08 14:33 UTC

Elon Musk discusses UFOs and alien life in podcast

Elon Musk, chief executive of Tesla and SpaceX, has stated that he would publicly share any credible evidence of extraterrestrial life if he found it, during a podcast discussion with Peter Diamandis on January 6. Musk's remarks come as public interest in UFOs and alien life continues to grow.

This discussion is significant as it highlights Musk's unique position in the space exploration field. With thousands of satellites in orbit, Musk’s insights provide a practical perspective on the possibility of alien encounters and ongoing public fascination with the topic.

Key facts

  • Podcast aired on January 6, 2023.
  • Musk manages approximately 9,000 satellites through SpaceX.
  • No alien spacecraft have been detected by SpaceX satellites.
  • Musk stated he would share any evidence of aliens on X (formerly Twitter).

Timeline

On January 6, 2023, Elon Musk participated in a podcast with Peter Diamandis, discussing the search for extraterrestrial life. During this discussion, Musk addressed popular conspiracy theories about government secrecy regarding aliens. He emphasized that if he found credible evidence, he would share it publicly on X.

Musk also highlighted the extensive monitoring capabilities of SpaceX’s 9,000 satellites, noting that none have encountered alien spacecraft to date. His comments come amid increasing public interest in UFO sightings and extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

Musk said, "If I were aware of the slightest evidence of aliens, I would immediately post it on X because it would be the most viewed post of all time."
Musk further explained, "They don’t have an incentive to hide the aliens; they have an incentive to show the aliens because they would not have any more arguments about the military budget if they seem a little bit dangerous."
Musk added, "We have got 9,000 satellites up there, we have never had to manoeuvre around an alien spaceship… yet."

Context

Musk's comments reflect a broader cultural fascination with UFOs and extraterrestrial life, which has been fueled by numerous reported sightings and popular media portrayals. His remarks challenge the prevailing notion that governments may conceal information about alien encounters, suggesting instead that revealing such information could serve political and military purposes.

What’s next

As public interest in UFOs continues to grow, Musk's statements may prompt further discussions about the capabilities of space monitoring technologies and their implications for understanding potential extraterrestrial life.

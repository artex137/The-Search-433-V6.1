‘Mate, that’s not a ute?!’: Eye-catching Brisbane ‘UFO’ goes viral

A large, eye-catching object resembling a UFO has attracted attention in Fortitude Valley, Brisbane, during a promotional event on October 22, 2023. The stunt, part of a viral advertising campaign by a gaming company, has sparked widespread discussion on social media but has not resulted in any reported injuries or fatalities.

This event highlights the growing trend of using unconventional marketing tactics to engage audiences. Local authorities, including the Brisbane City Council, have monitored the situation, although no official action has been taken as of yet.

Key facts

  • Date: October 22, 2023
  • Location: Fortitude Valley, Brisbane
  • Event type: Viral advertising campaign
  • Injuries/fatalities: None reported
  • Involved agency: Brisbane City Council

Timeline

On October 22, 2023, a large object resembling a UFO was installed in Fortitude Valley as part of a promotional stunt by a gaming company. The installation quickly drew crowds and generated significant buzz on social media platforms.

Following the installation, local authorities monitored the situation to ensure public safety and order. No incidents requiring police intervention have been reported.

Official statements

“The object has certainly caught the attention of many. It’s fascinating to see how creative advertising can engage the public,” said a spokesperson for Brisbane City Council.

Context

This event is part of a broader trend in marketing where companies use large, interactive installations to capture consumer interest. Similar stunts have occurred globally, often leading to significant media coverage and social media engagement.

In recent years, Brisbane has seen an increase in such promotional events, contributing to the city’s vibrant cultural landscape.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to monitor public response to the installation, and no further actions are currently planned. The effectiveness of the campaign will likely be assessed by the gaming company in the coming weeks.

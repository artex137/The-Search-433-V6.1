Cannot Spot UFOS for Drones

Drone hobbyists have complicated the tracking of unidentified flying objects (UFOs), according to Dr. Mona Nemer, the chief science advisor to the cabinet. Nemer raised concerns in an Access To Information memo to staff, questioning whether it is possible to identify these devices at night.

This issue is significant as it highlights the challenges faced by authorities in distinguishing between drones and potential UFO sightings. The government is currently assessing the impact of increased drone usage on UFO identification efforts.

Key facts

  • Speaker: Dr. Mona Nemer, chief science advisor
  • Context: Concerns raised in an Access To Information memo
  • Focus: Difficulty in identifying drones at night
  • Implications: Challenges in tracking UFOs

Timeline

Dr. Mona Nemer has issued a memo to staff regarding the challenges of tracking UFOs in the presence of drone hobbyists. The memo emphasizes the difficulties posed by these devices, particularly during nighttime.

Context

The rise of drone technology has led to increased reports of unidentified flying objects. Authorities are working to refine their methods for distinguishing between drones and potential UFOs, a task made more difficult by the proliferation of drone enthusiasts.

What’s next

The government will continue to evaluate the effects of drone usage on UFO tracking and may implement new strategies to improve identification efforts.

Deadly Navajo parade crash suspect charged

A man has been charged in connection with a deadly crash during a holiday parade in Kayenta, Navajo Nation, on December 22, 2025. Stanley Begay Jr., 67, faces a charge of Homicide by Vehicle, according to tribal prosecutors.

This incident has drawn attention as it highlights issues of safety at public events and the limitations of tribal courts under federal law. The charge carries a potential maximum sentence of one year in prison.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: December 22, 2025
  • Location: Kayenta, Navajo Nation
  • Suspect: Stanley Begay Jr., 67
  • Charge: Homicide by Vehicle
  • Potential sentence: One year in prison

Timeline

On December 22, 2025, a crash occurred during a holiday parade in Kayenta, resulting in fatalities. In the days that followed, tribal prosecutors identified and charged Stanley Begay Jr. with Homicide by Vehicle.

Official statements

“Due to federal law that places limitations on tribal courts, the suspect faces a one-year prison sentence,” tribal prosecutors said.

Context

This incident is part of ongoing concerns regarding safety and legal jurisdiction within tribal areas. Past events have prompted discussions on how to improve safety measures during public gatherings.

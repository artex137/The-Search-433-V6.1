Golfer Hovland explains how his UFO obsession helps him on the course

Viktor Hovland has stated that his obsession with UFO conspiracy theories aids his performance on the golf course, particularly during competitions at Bethpage Black. The 28-year-old European team star shared his views with the press ahead of the Ryder Cup, which begins on Friday in Farmingdale, New York.

This insight is significant as Hovland prepares to defend the European team's title against the USA team. His unique approach to managing competition stress may offer him an advantage in this prestigious tournament, where history and reputation weigh heavily.

Key facts

  • Event: Ryder Cup 2023
  • Location: Bethpage Black, Farmingdale, New York
  • Start Date: Friday, September 29, 2023
  • European team has won eight out of eleven Ryder Cups since 2002
  • USA team won the last two Ryder Cups in 2021 and 2016
  • Hovland's practice on the driving range on Wednesday was his best in years

Timeline

Viktor Hovland has actively prepared for the Ryder Cup, sharing his thoughts on his UFO obsession as a distraction from competitive pressure. The European team, led by captain Luke Donald, arrived in New York ahead of the tournament's opening. Hovland noted that his recent practice sessions have positively impacted his mindset.

The Ryder Cup is set to commence on September 29, 2023, with Hovland contributing to the European team's efforts to maintain their title against the USA.

Official statements

“This hobby distracts me from constant thoughts about my swing technique and allows me to maintain clarity while playing,” said Viktor Hovland.
“Hovland’s unusual passion often becomes a topic of conversation in the European team's locker room,” noted his teammate Ludwig Åberg.

Context

The Ryder Cup is a prestigious golf tournament with a rich history, and the European team has had a strong performance in the 21st century. However, they have struggled to secure victories on American soil, winning only one of four Ryder Cups held in the USA. This context adds pressure to the upcoming competition as the USA team enters as favorites.

What’s next

The Ryder Cup begins on September 29, 2023, with both teams eager to secure a victory. Hovland's unique mental approach may play a crucial role in the European team's performance as they strive to defend their title.

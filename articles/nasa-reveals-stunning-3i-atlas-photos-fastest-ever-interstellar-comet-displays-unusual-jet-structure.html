NASA Reveals Stunning 3I/ATLAS Photos: Fastest-Ever Interstellar Comet Displays Unusual Jet Structure

NASA has released images of comet 3I/ATLAS, the third confirmed interstellar visitor to our Solar System, following ‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. The comet was discovered on 1 July and is currently moving toward its closest approach to Earth on 19 December, at a distance of 170 million miles.

This release is significant as it offers a rare opportunity for astronomers to study material that has traveled billions of kilometers from a different star system. NASA has mobilized multiple spacecraft to observe the comet, despite delays caused by a six-week US government shutdown. Ongoing observations continue to provide valuable data on the comet’s unusual characteristics.

Key facts

  • Discovery date: 1 July 2023
  • Closest approach to Earth: 19 December 2023, at 170 million miles
  • Speed: 30,000 mph
  • Initial images captured by Perseverance rover on 4 October 2023
  • NASA missions involved: 12 spacecraft
  • Notable observations: 9 November revealed jet structures

Timeline

NASA confirmed the discovery of 3I/ATLAS on 1 July 2023. The comet has been observed by various spacecraft since its discovery. Mars-based missions provided early images, with the Perseverance rover capturing significant data on 4 October. The Sun-observing spacecraft began tracking the comet’s approach from mid-September.

As the comet neared perihelion on 29 October, it brightened and developed a visible light tail. Recent observations on 9 November revealed unusual jet structures, further intriguing scientists.

Official statements

“The twin anti-tail jets stretch nearly 0.95 million kilometres sunward, while the opposing tail jet extends 2.85 million kilometres away, indicating a remarkably powerful and organised material outflow,” said Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is unique in its characteristics and behavior compared to previous interstellar visitors. Its rapid speed and the unusual presence of jet structures have sparked interest and speculation among astronomers regarding its origins and composition. The comet's trajectory has also raised questions about its potential relation to advanced technologies.

What’s next

NASA and ESA will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it moves towards a close approach with Jupiter on 16 March 2026. Future spectroscopic observations aim to uncover the comet’s true composition and dynamics, contributing to our understanding of interstellar objects.

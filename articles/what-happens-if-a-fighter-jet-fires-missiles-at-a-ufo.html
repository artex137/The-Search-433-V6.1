What happens if a fighter jet fires missiles at a UFO?

When fighter jets fire missiles at unidentified flying objects (UFOs), the outcomes have often been inconclusive, according to official reports. There have been multiple instances where military jets have chased these unknown craft, which are reported to move in unusual ways, but no clear evidence shows that a jet has successfully hit or destroyed a true UFO.

This matter raises significant concerns regarding military protocols and the potential risks involved in engaging with unknown aerial phenomena. Authorities continue to investigate such incidents while adhering to strict rules of engagement before taking any action against unidentified targets.

Key facts

  • No confirmed case of a fighter jet successfully downing a true UFO.
  • Incidents involving missile firings have often resulted in inconclusive outcomes.
  • Some pilots reported missiles losing guidance or failing to explode.
  • Many UFOs have been identified as drones, foreign aircraft, or weather balloons.
  • Firing weapons is permitted only as a last resort under strict engagement rules.

Timeline

Over the years, military jets have been ordered to engage with UFOs during various encounters. Reports indicate that pilots tracked these objects, sometimes leading to missile launches. However, results from such actions have frequently shown that missiles either missed their targets or that the UFOs evaded interception.

Some pilots noted that their jets experienced system malfunctions during these encounters, which may have been caused by electronic jamming. Official investigations into these incidents have not provided definitive proof of successful engagements with UFOs.

Official statements

“Firing missiles at unknown targets risks accidents and creates diplomatic tensions,” stated a military source familiar with the protocols. “Most cases remain unsolved or are attributed to technical glitches or misidentifications.”

Context

Previous incidents involving UFOs have led to numerous declassified reports from the US and other nations. These documents often reveal that encounters with UFOs are typically marked by confusion and misidentification. Experts suggest that most objects categorized as UFOs are later determined to be benign entities like drones or weather balloons.

What’s next

Investigations into military engagements with UFOs are ongoing. Authorities aim to clarify protocols and enhance pilot training to minimize risks associated with encounters with unidentified aerial phenomena.

Interstellar object 3I/ATLAS deploying ‘satellites to spy on Jupiter?

Harvard astrophysicist Dr. Avi Loeb has proposed a new theory regarding the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, suggesting it may deploy satellites to gather intelligence on Jupiter on March 16, 2026. This theory comes amid ongoing debates with the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) about the object's origins and nature.

This theory is significant as it challenges current understandings of comet behavior and raises questions about potential extraterrestrial technology. NASA has confirmed that 3I/ATLAS is a comet that originated from another solar system, but Dr. Loeb remains skeptical of this classification.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 2025
  • Proposed Deployment Date: March 16, 2026
  • Origin: Another solar system (according to NASA)
  • Key Figure: Dr. Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist
  • NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory involved in calculations

Timeline

In July 2025, the comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered, prompting immediate interest from astronomers. In November 2025, Dr. Avi Loeb publicly introduced his theory regarding the object’s potential to deploy satellites around Jupiter. NASA confirmed the object's classification as a comet during this period, but Dr. Loeb expressed skepticism about this designation.

Official statements

“The trajectory of 3I/ATLAS could suggest it will deploy satellites around Jupiter for surveillance and intelligence gathering,” Dr. Avi Loeb said in an interview.

Context

This latest theory from Dr. Loeb follows a history of his controversial claims regarding extraterrestrial phenomena. His earlier assertions have often contradicted mainstream scientific consensus, particularly regarding the nature of interstellar objects.

What’s next

Further observations and analyses will continue as the comet approaches Jupiter, with scientists monitoring its trajectory and behavior leading up to the proposed deployment date.

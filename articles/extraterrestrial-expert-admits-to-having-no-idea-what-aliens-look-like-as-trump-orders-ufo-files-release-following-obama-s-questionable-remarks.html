Extraterrestrial Expert Admits to Having 'No Idea' What Aliens Look Like

Extraterrestrial experts have stated they have no definitive understanding of what aliens may look like following comments from former President Barack Obama, who claimed "aliens are real" but admitted he has not seen them. The remarks have sparked renewed interest in UFOs and the potential existence of extraterrestrial life.

This development is significant as President Trump has ordered several federal agencies to review and release UFO files to the public. This directive aims to provide transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and related matters, which many believe the government has been concealing.

Key facts

  • Bill Diamond is the president and chief executive of SETI – The Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence Institute.
  • A 2025 poll indicated that nearly half of Americans believe the federal government is hiding evidence related to UFOs.
  • Trump has tasked Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth with overseeing the release of UFO-related files.
  • Obama's comments about aliens were made during an interview with podcast host Brian Tyler Cohen.
  • Trump has expressed openness to the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

Timeline

In a recent interview, Obama confirmed the existence of aliens but stated he has not seen them. He humorously noted that they are not kept in Area 51, suggesting that if they were, it would require a vast conspiracy to keep it hidden from the president.

Following Obama's remarks, President Trump issued an executive order directing federal agencies to identify and release all information related to UAPs and UFOs. Secretary Hegseth confirmed the agencies will comply fully with this order.

Official statements

“It’s hard for us to assert... We have no idea,” said Bill Diamond, emphasizing the uncertainty surrounding the appearance of extraterrestrial beings.
“We're digging in. We're going to be in full compliance with that executive order,” said Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth regarding the review of UFO files.

Context

Prior to these recent statements, there has been a growing public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life, fueled by a mix of government disclosures and popular culture. The belief that the government may be hiding information about UFOs has been prevalent among many citizens, as reflected in the 2025 poll.

Obama's lighthearted acknowledgment of aliens has sparked discussions about the government's transparency regarding these matters, and Trump's directive indicates a shift towards greater openness.

What’s next

Federal agencies are expected to begin the process of reviewing and releasing UFO-related files as directed by President Trump. Further updates on the findings and any potential disclosures are anticipated in the coming months.

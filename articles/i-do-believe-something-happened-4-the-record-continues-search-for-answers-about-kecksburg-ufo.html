'I do believe something happened': 4 The Record continues search for answers about Kecksburg UFO

Tuesday marked 60 years since an unidentified object was spotted in the sky over Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania. Some witnesses reported that the object made a controlled landing, sparking ongoing interest and investigation into the incident.

The Kecksburg UFO incident remains significant as it has continued to captivate the public and researchers alike for six decades. Authorities and media outlets, including "4 The Record," have revisited the event to gather insights from those who were present, including former reporters and local firefighters.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred 60 years ago in Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania.
  • Notable witnesses include Ronnie Struble, a 50-year veteran of the Kecksburg Volunteer Fire Department, and Bob Gatty, a former Greensburg Tribune-Review reporter.
  • Bob Gatty is currently 82 years old and has been reflecting on the incident for decades.
  • Michelle Wright is the host of "4 The Record," which has featured discussions on the incident.

Timeline

On December 9, 1965, an unidentified object was reported in the skies over Westmoreland County. Local witnesses described seeing something that appeared to make a controlled landing.

In recent weeks, “4 The Record” has aired interviews with several individuals connected to the event, including Ronnie Struble and UFO researcher Stan Gordon. This week, the focus shifted to Bob Gatty, who provided his account of the incident.

Official statements

“Hard to tell, but I do believe that something happened. I do believe something landed there,” said Bob Gatty, reflecting on the event. “It's the story that won't go away.”

Context

The Kecksburg UFO incident is one of the most well-known sightings in U.S. history, contributing to ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena. Interest in UFOs has seen a resurgence in recent years, with various media outlets and researchers exploring similar sightings.

What’s next

Investigations into the Kecksburg incident continue as researchers seek to uncover more information. Future episodes of "4 The Record" may further explore the details and implications of this notable event.

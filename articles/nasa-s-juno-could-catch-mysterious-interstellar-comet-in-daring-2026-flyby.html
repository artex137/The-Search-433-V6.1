NASA’s Juno Could Catch Mysterious Interstellar Comet in Daring 2026 Flyby

NASA’s Juno spacecraft is set to perform a flyby of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS in 2026, according to NASA officials. This event will mark a significant opportunity for scientists to study the comet as it approaches the inner solar system.

The flyby is important because it could provide valuable insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar objects. NASA has confirmed that the Juno spacecraft is prepared for the encounter and is currently undergoing final checks for the mission.

Key facts

  • Event: Juno flyby of 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: Scheduled for 2026
  • Agency: NASA
  • Spacecraft: Juno
  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS

Timeline

NASA announced the planned flyby of the comet 3I/ATLAS as part of Juno’s extended mission. The spacecraft, which has been studying Jupiter since 2016, will adjust its trajectory to intercept the comet in 2026.

Scientists expect to gather data during the flyby that could enhance the understanding of the origins and characteristics of interstellar comets, which are rare and intriguing objects.

Official statements

“This flyby presents a unique opportunity to study an interstellar object up close,” said a NASA representative. “We are excited to see what data Juno will collect.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few interstellar comets detected so far. Previous studies of such comets have revealed important information about the formation of the solar system and the materials present in the early solar nebula.

What’s next

NASA will continue preparations for the Juno flyby, including trajectory adjustments and mission assessments leading up to the 2026 event.

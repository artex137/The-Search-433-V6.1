Scott Cooper to Direct Roswell/UFO Movie for 20th Century

Scott Cooper has signed on to write and direct a film about the Roswell incident for 20th Century Studios. The project will explore the 1947 event in Roswell, New Mexico, when debris was reportedly found on a ranch, sparking conspiracy theories about a possible UFO crash.

This film comes as interest in UFOs, now referred to as Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP), has surged in recent years. Multiple whistleblower accounts have emerged, prompting Hollywood to develop various projects around the theme, including works by notable directors such as Steven Spielberg and Joseph Kosinski. The Roswell incident remains a pivotal moment in UFO lore and has yet to be adapted into a theatrical film.

Key facts

  • Film title: Not disclosed
  • Director: Scott Cooper
  • Production company: 20th Century Studios
  • Incident date: July 1947
  • Location: Roswell, New Mexico
  • Previous adaptations: Television series and made-for-TV movies

Timeline

In July 1947, a rancher in Roswell reported finding debris on his property. The U.S. Army Air Forces initially issued a press release claiming they had recovered a “flying disc,” but quickly retracted this statement, stating the debris was from a weather balloon. This incident has fueled conspiracy theories and speculation for decades.

In recent years, various filmmakers have pursued UFO-related stories, leading to a resurgence in interest in the genre. Scott Cooper's involvement marks a significant step in bringing the Roswell incident to a cinematic audience.

Official statements

“Cooper is teaming up with 20th Century to write and direct a film based on the famous 1947 Roswell, New Mexico, conspiracy,” according to reports.

Context

The Roswell incident has been a subject of fascination since it first occurred, becoming a cornerstone of UFO culture. While adaptations have appeared on television, a theatrical version has yet to materialize, making Cooper's project notable within the industry.

Previous films, like “Hangar 18,” have touched on similar themes but have not explicitly named Roswell. The upcoming film may offer a fresh perspective on this iconic event.

What’s next

Details regarding the film’s release date and further production specifics are not disclosed. Cooper is also considering another script, “Commanche,” but has not confirmed which project will proceed first.

Trump Calls for UFO File Releases as Pentagon Review Slows Disclosure of UAP Records

President Donald Trump has called for the release of classified files related to UFO sightings and extraterrestrial activity, prompting scrutiny of the Pentagon and the Department of Defense's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office. This call comes amid a delay in the public disclosure of these documents, raising questions about the declassification process for sensitive national security records.

The situation is significant as it highlights ongoing concerns regarding transparency in government operations related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). Authorities are currently assessing the protocols for releasing such classified information while balancing national security interests.

Key facts

  • President Donald Trump has requested the release of UFO-related files.
  • The Pentagon and the Department of Defense's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office are involved in the review process.
  • No documents have been made public weeks after the request.
  • The delay has raised questions in Washington regarding the declassification timeline for sensitive records.

Timeline

President Trump has recently called for the release of classified UFO files. In the weeks that followed, no documents have been made available to the public, prompting further scrutiny of the processes involved.

The lack of disclosure has renewed discussions in Washington about the speed and efficiency of declassifying sensitive national security information.

Official statements

“The delay has reignited questions in Washington over how quickly sensitive national security records can realistically be declassified,”
said an unnamed source familiar with the matter.

Context

This incident is part of a broader trend regarding the government’s handling of UAP reports and the push for transparency surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena. Previous calls for disclosure have often met with delays and limited public access to information.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to evaluate the protocols for releasing classified documents, with a focus on balancing national security concerns and public interest in UAP records.

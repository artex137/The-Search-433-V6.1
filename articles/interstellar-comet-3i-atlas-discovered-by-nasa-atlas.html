Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Discovered by NASA ATLAS

The ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System) survey telescope, funded by NASA and located in Río Hurtado, Chile, has discovered the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. This discovery occurred on August 29, 2025, marking the third interstellar object identified by humans.

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS is significant as it provides astronomers with a unique opportunity to study the composition and origin of an object from outside our solar system. Authorities and researchers are currently analyzing the comet's properties to gain insights into the formation of other star systems.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: August 29, 2025
  • Location: Río Hurtado, Chile
  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Speed: Approximately 137,000 miles per hour (221,000 kilometers per hour)
  • Estimated Diameter: Between 1,000 feet (320 meters) and 3.5 miles (5.6 kilometers)
  • Previous Interstellar Objects: 1I/’Oumuamua (2017) and 2I/Borisov (2019)

Timeline

NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope captured an image of 3I/ATLAS on July 21, 2025, when the comet was 277 million miles away from Earth. This observation allowed researchers to estimate the size of the comet’s nucleus.

Following its discovery, astronomers have begun studying the comet's trajectory, which indicates it is on a hyperbolic path, confirming its interstellar origin. The comet is expected to increase speed as it approaches the Sun.

Official statements

“The discovery of 3I/ATLAS allows us to better understand the materials that make up other star systems,” said a spokesperson from NASA.

Context

The discovery of interstellar objects is rare, with 3I/ATLAS being the third such object identified. The previous interstellar objects, 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, were discovered in 2017 and 2019, respectively. These findings highlight the importance of telescopic surveys in expanding our knowledge of the universe.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun, gathering data that could lead to further insights into its composition and the characteristics of interstellar objects.

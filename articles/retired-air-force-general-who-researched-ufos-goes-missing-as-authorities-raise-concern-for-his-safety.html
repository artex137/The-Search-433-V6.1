Retired Air Force General Who Researched UFOs Goes Missing as Authorities Raise ‘Concern for His Safety’

William Neil McCasland, a 68-year-old retired U.S. Air Force Major General, has been reported missing since February 27 in New Mexico, according to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office. Authorities issued a silver alert for McCasland, who was last seen at approximately 11 a.m. local time in the vicinity of Quail Run Court NE.

The disappearance of McCasland has raised concerns among local authorities, who have called for public assistance in locating him. The sheriff's office stated that due to McCasland's medical issues, they are particularly worried for his safety.

Key facts

  • Missing Person: William Neil McCasland, 68
  • Date Last Seen: February 27, 2023
  • Location: Quail Run Court NE, New Mexico
  • Time Last Seen: Approximately 11 a.m. local time
  • Agency Involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office
  • Status: Silver alert issued

Timeline

On February 27, 2023, William Neil McCasland was last seen in New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a silver alert shortly thereafter, expressing concern for his safety due to undisclosed medical issues. Authorities have since called on the public to assist in the search for the retired general.

Official statements

“Due to his medical issues law enforcement is concerned for his safety,” the sheriff’s office stated.
“Our thoughts are with his family during this difficult time,” said Col. Justin Secrest, base commander of the 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland.

Context

William Neil McCasland commanded the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio from 2011 until his retirement in October 2013. He was involved in managing a significant science and technology program valued at $2.2 billion. McCasland's name has been associated with UFO research, as he was mentioned in a 2016 email exchange regarding UFOs involving Tom DeLonge, a guitarist from Blink-182.

What’s next

Authorities continue to search for McCasland and are encouraging anyone with information about his whereabouts to contact the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office. The investigation remains ongoing.

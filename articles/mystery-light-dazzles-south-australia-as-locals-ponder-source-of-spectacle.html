Mystery light dazzles South Australia as locals ponder source of spectacle

A mystery light that dazzled residents across South Australia on Friday morning has been identified as likely rocket exhaust from a recent launch. Stargazers in locations such as Strathalbyn, Seaview, and Largs Bay reported seeing two beams drifting across the dawn sky. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The unusual sight ignited a flurry of speculation on social media, with some locals jokingly suggesting it was a UFO, a spy rocket, or even a pigeon wearing a vest with lights. Experts have clarified that the phenomenon was most likely linked to the launch of Ariane 6 from Europe’s Spaceport in French Guiana, which took place at 3:15 a.m. Adelaide time and carried 32 satellites for Amazon.

Key facts

  • Event occurred on Friday morning, exact time: 3:15 a.m. Adelaide time.
  • Locations of sightings included Strathalbyn, Seaview, and Largs Bay.
  • Ariane 6 rocket launched from French Guiana, carrying 32 satellites for Amazon.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.
  • French President Emmanuel Macron commented on the launch via Twitter.

Timeline

On Friday, the spectacle began shortly after the launch of the Ariane 6 rocket at 3:15 a.m. Adelaide time. Residents across South Australia reported seeing two beams in the sky, sparking widespread curiosity and social media discussions about the unusual event.

As the morning progressed, experts began to suggest that the lights were likely the result of rocket exhaust from the recent launch. This explanation was supported by the timing of the Ariane 6 launch, which was confirmed by various sources.

Official statements

“It’s definitely a UFO,” one local commented, while another joked: “Prepare for an extraterrestrial encounter.”
French President Emmanuel Macron tweeted: “Amazon, your package has been delivered.”
“Ariane 6 has lifted off for the first time with four boosters, making it our most powerful European launcher,” Macron added.

Context

This incident follows a 2023 survey revealing that one-third of Australians believe extraterrestrial beings have contacted Earth. Such beliefs often surface during unexplained phenomena, fueling speculation and public interest.

Previous events involving unidentified flying objects have similarly captured the public's imagination, often leading to various interpretations until clarified by expert analysis.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any further investigations related to the incident. However, experts will continue to monitor similar events in the future to provide accurate explanations to the public.

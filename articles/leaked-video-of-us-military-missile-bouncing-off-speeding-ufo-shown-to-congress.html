Leaked video of US military missile ‘bouncing’ off speeding UFO shown to congress

A leaked video has shown a US military Hellfire missile appearing to “bounce” off a shiny object off the coast of Yemen on 30 October 2024. The footage was presented to Congress for the first time on Tuesday, 9 September 2024, during a House Oversight subcommittee hearing on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs). No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This development is significant as it raises further questions about the government's transparency regarding UFO sightings and incidents. Military whistleblowers have testified under oath that they have encountered UFOs and accused the government of covering up information related to these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: 30 October 2024
  • Date of congressional hearing: 9 September 2024
  • Location: Off the coast of Yemen
  • Type of missile: US military Hellfire missile
  • Whistleblower: Rep. Eric Burlison of Missouri
  • Testimony: Former Air Force military police officer Nuccetelli

Timeline

On 30 October 2024, a US military Hellfire missile was launched at a shiny object off the coast of Yemen. The missile appeared to bounce off the object, leading to questions about its nature.

On 9 September 2024, Rep. Eric Burlison presented the leaked footage during a House Oversight subcommittee hearing dedicated to Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena. During this session, military whistleblowers provided testimony regarding their experiences with UFOs.

Official statements

“The video provides exceptional evidence of UFOs,” said former Air Force military police officer Nuccetelli during the congressional hearing.

Context

This incident adds to a growing body of evidence and testimony regarding UFOs and the military's knowledge of such phenomena. Previous incidents have raised concerns about the government’s willingness to disclose information on UAPs.

What’s next

Further investigations into the footage and the events surrounding it are expected as Congress continues to explore the implications of military encounters with unidentified aerial objects.

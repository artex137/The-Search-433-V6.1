Making UFO documentary was like ‘birthing an elephant’: Producer

Luis Elizondo, executive producer of the new UFO documentary “The Age of Disclosure,” has described the years-long effort to create the film as akin to “birthing an elephant.” The documentary suggests that the U.S. and its adversaries have recovered alien craft. Elizondo spoke about the project on the program “CUOMO.”

The documentary is significant as it reflects a shift in public and official attitudes toward UFOs. Elizondo noted that more officials and individuals with knowledge of UFOs are now willing to speak openly about the subject, which was once considered taboo.

Key facts

  • Documentary title: “The Age of Disclosure”
  • Producer: Luis Elizondo
  • Platform: Discussed on the program “CUOMO”
  • Duration of production: Years
  • Content focus: Recovery of alien craft by the U.S. and its adversaries

Timeline

The production of “The Age of Disclosure” has spanned several years, reflecting the extensive research and interviews conducted by Elizondo and his team.

Elizondo has indicated that the changing perspectives on UFOs have enabled more people to come forward with their experiences and knowledge.

Official statements

“Times have changed, significantly,” Elizondo said, highlighting the evolving discourse surrounding UFOs.

Context

The documentary arrives at a time when discussions about UFOs have gained traction in both public and governmental spheres. Increased media coverage and official reports have prompted a broader conversation about the subject.

What’s next

The documentary’s release may lead to further investigations and discussions regarding the implications of UFO sightings and government responses.

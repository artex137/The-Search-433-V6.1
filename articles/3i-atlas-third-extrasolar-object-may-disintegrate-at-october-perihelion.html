3I/Atlas, Third Extrasolar Object, May Disintegrate at October Perihelion

Astronomers at the ATLAS system have detected the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on July 1, 2025, at the RÃ­o Hurtado observatory in Chile. The comet, the third confirmed to originate outside the Solar System, is expected to reach perihelion on October 29, 2025, at a distance of 1.4 astronomical units from the Sun, near Mars' orbit. Authorities have raised concerns that the comet may disintegrate due to intense solar radiation.

This discovery is significant as it provides insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar objects. Current observations show early activity, with gas emissions detected from the comet, which may help scientists understand its structure and origins. The European Space Agency and NASA are coordinating tracking efforts through various probes.

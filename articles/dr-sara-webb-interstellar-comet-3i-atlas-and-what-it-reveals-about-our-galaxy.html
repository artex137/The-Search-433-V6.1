Dr Sara Webb: Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS and What It Reveals About Our Galaxy

On January 2, 2026, an interstellar comet named 3I/ATLAS has passed through our solar system, providing scientists with a unique opportunity to study material from outside our local star system. Astrophysicist Dr Sara Webb from Swinburne University of Technology discusses the significance of this event and the observations made during the comet's closest approach.

This event matters because interstellar visitors like 3I/ATLAS are rare, and studying them can enhance our understanding of the galaxy and its formation. Dr Webb highlights the importance of monitoring such comets, as they can reveal insights into the origins of our solar system and the materials that exist beyond it.

Context

Prior to the arrival of 3I/ATLAS, interstellar comets have been infrequent, making this occurrence particularly noteworthy for the scientific community. Such events provide valuable data on celestial bodies that originate from beyond our solar system.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze data collected from the comet's passage to gain further insights into its composition and the broader implications for our understanding of the galaxy.

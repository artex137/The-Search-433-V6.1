The quaintness of UFO conspiracy theories

Former US president Barack Obama has expressed his belief that aliens might exist, although he has not personally seen them. He made these comments during a podcast with Brian Tyler Cohen last week. Obama stated that there are no aliens being housed in Area 51, a classified US Air Force base, and emphasized, “There’s no underground facility, unless there’s this enormous conspiracy and they hid it from the president.” In response to Obama’s remarks, former president Donald Trump has not taken a definitive stance on the existence of extraterrestrial life but has reportedly expressed frustration over the disclosure of what he considers ‘classified information’. To address concerns regarding UFOs, Trump announced that the State Department will begin the process of identifying and releasing government files related to aliens and extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Former US president Barack Obama believes in the possibility of extraterrestrial life.
  • Obama stated there are no aliens at Area 51 during a podcast on February 20, 2026.
  • Former president Donald Trump has not disclosed a stance on aliens but showed frustration over Obama’s comments.
  • Trump announced plans for the State Department to release government files on extraterrestrial life.

Timeline

On February 20, 2026, Barack Obama discussed his views on extraterrestrial life during a podcast with Brian Tyler Cohen. He stated that he has not seen aliens and that they are not stored at Area 51. Following this, Donald Trump reacted by expressing displeasure over the release of what he perceives as classified information. Trump then announced that the State Department will begin the process of identifying and releasing relevant government files.

Official statements

“There’s no underground facility, unless there’s this enormous conspiracy and they hid it from the president.” - Barack Obama
“I don’t know if they’re real or not.” - Donald Trump on aliens

Context

The discussion around UFOs has persisted since the alleged discovery of alien spacecraft debris near Roswell, New Mexico, in 1947. This incident has fueled numerous conspiracy theories over the decades. Despite advancements in science and technology, the fascination with UFOs continues, often as a source of optimism about the possibility of life beyond Earth.

What’s next

The State Department's process for identifying and releasing files on extraterrestrial life is expected to begin soon. Further developments may clarify the government's stance on UFOs and the existence of extraterrestrial life.

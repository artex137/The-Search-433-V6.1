Ocean paddler Michael Walther hit by an unknown object

Michael Walther, a 46-year-old athlete, has been struck by an unknown object while paddling across the Atlantic. The incident occurred on the night of October 1, 2023, near the coast of Fuerteventura, Canary Islands, as he traveled on his six-meter-long offshore stand-up paddleboard (SUP). Walther, who had paddled for 13 days and covered approximately 1,000 kilometers of his 6,000-kilometer route from Lagos, Portugal, reported sustaining no serious injuries.

This incident highlights the potential dangers faced by solo ocean paddlers. Authorities are now assessing the damage to Walther's board and ensuring his safety following the collision. Walther, known for his environmental activism under the name Zero Emissions, has expressed a desire to continue his journey after making necessary repairs.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on October 1, 2023, off the coast of Fuerteventura.
  • Walther has paddled approximately 1,000 kilometers of his planned 6,000-kilometer route.
  • The collision damaged the position light and AIS antenna, which were lost at sea.
  • A large hole was reported in the ceiling of the cabin on his board.
  • Walther arrived at the port of Puerto del Rosario on October 2, 2023.

Timeline

On October 1, 2023, while paddling near Fuerteventura, Michael Walther experienced a loud bang as his board collided with an unknown object. The impact caused him to be thrown from his bunk. After stabilizing the board, he assessed the damage, discovering significant issues including a large hole in the cabin and missing equipment.

On the morning of October 2, 2023, Walther reached the port of Puerto del Rosario to seek assistance and further evaluate the condition of his board.

Official statements

“I have to go to the next port now and see what exactly happened,” Walther said regarding the incident.

Context

Prior to this incident, Michael Walther has undertaken several oceanic challenges, focusing on environmental awareness through his journey. His current expedition aims to collect acoustic ocean data for the Alfred Wegener Institute and highlight the impact of individual efforts on environmental issues.

What’s next

Walther will undergo inspections of his paddleboard for structural and safety-related damage before deciding on the next steps in his journey across the Atlantic.

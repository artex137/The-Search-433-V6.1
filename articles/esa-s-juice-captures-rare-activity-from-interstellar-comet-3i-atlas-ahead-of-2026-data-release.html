ESA’s Juice Captures Rare Activity from Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Ahead of 2026 Data Release

A rare interstellar encounter between the European Space Agency's (ESA) Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) and the comet 3I/ATLAS has occurred in November, providing astronomers with significant data on the highly active comet. This observation marks only the third time an interstellar object has been recorded.

This event is important as it allows researchers to study the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, which is considered a unique visitor from beyond the Solar System. The data collected during this encounter will be analyzed further, with a complete release of findings expected in 2026.

Key facts

  • Event occurred in November 2023.
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar object recorded.
  • The European Space Agency's Juice spacecraft conducted the observation.
  • Data release is expected in 2026.

Timeline

In November 2023, the Juice spacecraft passed near the comet 3I/ATLAS. This close encounter enabled scientists to capture unprecedented data about the comet's activity, which has been described as highly active.

Official statements

“This observation provides an unusual chance to study an interstellar object,” ESA officials stated regarding the encounter.

Context

Interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS are rare, with only two others recorded prior to this event. The study of such objects can enhance our understanding of the formation and evolution of our Solar System.

What’s next

Researchers will analyze the data collected from this encounter, with a full report and findings expected to be published in 2026.

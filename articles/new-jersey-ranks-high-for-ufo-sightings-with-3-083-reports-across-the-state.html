New Jersey ranks high for UFO sightings with 3,083 reports across the state

New Jersey has recorded 3,083 UFO sightings, making it one of the top states for reported extraterrestrial activity, according to data from the National UFO Reporting Center. This figure translates to 419.3 sightings per 1,000 square miles, the second-highest density in the United States, following Rhode Island.

This information highlights the growing public interest in UFOs, particularly following comments from a senior NASA official about the agency's commitment to investigating the existence of alien life. Authorities are now examining these sightings to understand patterns and potential implications for public safety and scientific inquiry.

Key facts

  • 3,083 reported UFO sightings in New Jersey
  • 419.3 sightings per 1,000 square miles
  • Rhode Island has the highest density with 616.1 sightings per square mile
  • Alaska has the lowest density, with only 1.2 sightings per square mile
  • Data sourced from the National UFO Reporting Center

Official statements

Context

New Jersey's high number of UFO sightings aligns with a broader trend in the United States, where interest in unidentified aerial phenomena has surged in recent years. The increase in reported sightings may be attributed to enhanced public interest and advancements in technology that allow for easier documentation of such occurrences.

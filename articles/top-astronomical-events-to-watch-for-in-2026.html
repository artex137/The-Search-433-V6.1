Top Astronomical Events to Watch For in 2026

The astronomical community has identified several significant events for 2026, including the anticipated return of Comet 3I/ATLAS, multiple eclipses, and planetary conjunctions. These events are expected to attract the attention of skywatchers worldwide.

Authorities and astronomers are preparing for a busy year of celestial phenomena. The events, including two solar eclipses and a total lunar eclipse, provide opportunities for public engagement and education about astronomy. The return of Comet 3I/ATLAS adds excitement as it crosses paths with asteroid 65 Cybele.

Key facts

  • Total solar eclipse: August 12, 2026, visible in Greenland, Iceland, and northern Spain.
  • Total lunar eclipse: March 3, 2026, visible in North America, the Pacific region, Australia, and the Far East.
  • Mutual eclipse-transit season: Resumes for the moons of Jupiter.
  • Occultations: The Moon will occult Venus on June 17 and September 14, and Jupiter on October 6.
  • Meteor showers: The Perseid and Geminid showers are expected to be prominent with the Moon near new.
  • Comet C/2025 R3 PanSTARRS: Expected visibility in Spring 2026.

Timeline

In 2026, several notable celestial events will occur:

  • February 17: An annular solar eclipse will be visible in Antarctica.
  • March 3: A total lunar eclipse lasting over 56 minutes will be visible in several regions.
  • August 12: A total solar eclipse will occur, crossing the North Atlantic and northern Spain.
  • August 28: A deep partial lunar eclipse, with 93% obscuration, will be visible in Africa, Europe, and the Americas.

Official statements

“The sky just keeps on turning into 2026, offering a plethora of events for enthusiasts and casual observers alike,” said an unnamed astronomer. “We are particularly excited about the return of Comet 3I/ATLAS and the total solar eclipse.”

Context

2025 was marked by numerous comets becoming visible to the naked eye, and 2026 continues this trend with significant events. The ongoing solar cycle is expected to remain active, producing sunspots and other solar phenomena.

What’s next

Skywatchers are encouraged to prepare for these events and stay updated on any new discoveries, particularly regarding comets. Public observatories and astronomical societies will likely host events to facilitate viewing and education throughout the year.

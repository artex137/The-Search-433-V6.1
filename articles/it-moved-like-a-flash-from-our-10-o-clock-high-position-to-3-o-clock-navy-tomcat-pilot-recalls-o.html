Unidentified Flying Objects Reported by Former Navy Pilot

Doyle Borchers, a former US Navy F-14 Tomcat test pilot, has recounted two distinct sightings of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) during his flying career. These incidents occurred while he was piloting the F-14, with one sighting taking place during a night mission in 1977 and another in 1976. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these sightings.

The significance of these accounts lies in the ongoing interest in UFOs and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). As discussions about the existence of such phenomena continue, authorities are taking steps to investigate and understand these occurrences. Borchers' experiences add to the body of anecdotal evidence from military pilots regarding unexplained aerial sightings.

Key facts

  • First sighting: 1977, during a night mission over New Mexico.
  • Second sighting: 1976, while flying from Palma/Majorca to RNAS Yeovilton.
  • Altitude during second sighting: Approximately 35,000 ft.
  • Estimated altitude of UFO during second sighting: About 70,000 ft.
  • Speed of UFO during second sighting: Estimated at almost 500 knots.

Timeline

In 1976, while flying from Palma/Majorca to RNAS Yeovilton for an airshow, Borchers and his Radar Intercept Officer (RIO) observed a bright silver disk at an altitude estimated to be 70,000 ft. The object moved rapidly from their 10 o’clock position to their 3 o’clock position and disappeared from sight.

In 1977, while returning from a night mission over the White Sands range in New Mexico, Borchers and his RIO noticed a string of lights below them at 24,000 ft. The lights followed their aircraft at high speed for approximately 10 minutes before breaking off and departing. Borchers expressed a desire to report the incident, but his RIO did not support the claim.

Official statements

“No doubt in my mind that UFOs are real.”
- Doyle Borchers

Context

UFO sightings among military pilots have been documented for decades, often involving objects flying in formation or bright lights. The term UFO is commonly associated with alien spacecraft, prompting some investigators to prefer the term UAP to avoid confusion. Borchers’ reports contribute to the growing interest and scrutiny regarding aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities continue to investigate reports of UFOs and UAPs, with an emphasis on gathering data from military personnel. Further studies and potential disclosures may arise as interest in these phenomena persists.

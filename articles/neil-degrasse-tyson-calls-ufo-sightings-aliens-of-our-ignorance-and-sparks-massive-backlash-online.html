Neil deGrasse Tyson calls UFO sightings "aliens of our ignorance" and sparks massive backlash online

Neil deGrasse Tyson has reiterated his skepticism about claims of extraterrestrial visitors during his February 27 appearance on Piers Morgan Uncensored. He dismissed theories of government cover-ups and insisted that any real evidence of alien life would be apparent in today’s smartphone-saturated world. Tyson characterized unexplained sightings as "aliens of our ignorance" and emphasized the need for verifiable data over anecdotal evidence.

This discussion comes amid a renewed push for transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) following a directive from former President Donald Trump on February 19, 2026. Trump called for the identification and release of government files on UFOs, citing "tremendous public interest." Tyson's skeptical stance contrasts with the growing demand for more information and the public's fascination with the topic.

Key facts

  • Neil deGrasse Tyson appeared on Piers Morgan Uncensored on February 27, 2026.
  • Former President Trump issued a directive on February 19, 2026, regarding UFO transparency.
  • Tyson has been a vocal skeptic of extraterrestrial visitation for decades.
  • Tyson's comments have drawn significant backlash on social media platforms.

Timeline

Context

Tyson has consistently maintained his skepticism about extraterrestrial life on Earth. He has previously addressed the Pentagon's release of UFO videos in 2017 and more recent UAP reports. His emphasis on scientific rigor separates the likelihood of life in the universe from unverified claims of contact.

His comments have ignited debate, especially in light of recent whistleblower claims and congressional hearings regarding UAPs. Critics have accused him of being out of touch with current discussions surrounding the topic.

What’s next

As public interest in UFOs and UAPs grows, further investigations and discussions are expected. The response from various agencies following Trump’s directive will likely shape the narrative around extraterrestrial life and government transparency.

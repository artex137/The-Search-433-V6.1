Breakthrough Listen Searches Comet 3I/ATLAS for Technosignatures

During comet 3I/ATLAS's closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, the Breakthrough Listen project conducted a search for signs of technosignatures, or evidence of alien communications. The comet approached Earth at a distance of 1.7 astronomical units (AU), approximately 167 million miles (270 million kilometers) away.

This search is significant as it marks a continued effort to understand interstellar objects and the potential for extraterrestrial life. Scientists have focused on 3I/ATLAS, the third interstellar visitor detected by humanity, since its discovery by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System on July 1, 2025. The project aims to gather data that could enhance our understanding of the comet and its origins.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025.
  • The comet was detected by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System on July 1, 2025.
  • The Breakthrough Listen project utilized the 100-meter Green Bank Telescope for observations.
  • The search for technosignatures was conducted on December 18, 2025, less than 24 hours before closest approach.
  • The observations covered frequencies ranging from 1 to 12 GHz.
  • Scientists found nine events that warranted further inspection but ruled them out as radio-frequency interference (RFI).
  • The nucleus of 3I/ATLAS is estimated to be around 1 km in diameter.

Timeline

The following events outline the key developments regarding comet 3I/ATLAS:

  • July 1, 2025: Comet 3I/ATLAS is detected by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System.
  • December 18, 2025: The Breakthrough Listen project observes 3I/ATLAS using the Green Bank Telescope.
  • December 19, 2025: 3I/ATLAS reaches its closest distance to Earth at 1.7 AU.

Official statements

“Unlike 1I/‘Oumuamua, 3I/ATLAS exhibits mostly typical cometary characteristics, including a coma and an unelongated nucleus,” the Breakthrough Listen team stated. “There is currently no evidence to suggest that ISOs are anything other than natural astrophysical objects.”
“We used four receivers (L, S, C, and X) spanning frequencies of 1 - 12 GHz. At closest approach, the GBT is sensitive to transmitters with an EIRP of approximately 0.1 W, more sensitive than all previous observations,” explained the Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI) Institute.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object discovered, following 1I/‘Oumuamua and another unidentified object. Scientists continue to study such objects to learn more about their origins and characteristics. The study of technosignatures remains a topic of interest in the search for extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

Data from the Breakthrough Listen project has been made available to the public, allowing for further analysis. Ongoing studies will continue to explore non-gravitational accelerations and other characteristics of 3I/ATLAS as more observations are conducted.

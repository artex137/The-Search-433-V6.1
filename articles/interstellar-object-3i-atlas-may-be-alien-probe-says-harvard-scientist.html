Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS May Be Alien Probe, Says Harvard Scientist

In early July, astronomers discovered a fast-moving celestial object named 3I/ATLAS racing toward the Sun. The object has garnered significant attention due to its unusual trajectory, which indicates it originated beyond our Solar System. This marks only the third confirmed interstellar visitor to enter the Solar System, and 3I/ATLAS has been noted for its extraordinary speed and size.

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS is significant as it challenges existing knowledge about interstellar objects. Scientists are currently analyzing its characteristics to determine whether it is a natural cosmic body or a potential artificial probe. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb and his team have raised the intriguing possibility that 3I/ATLAS may represent alien technology.

Key facts

  • Discovered in early July 2023
  • Speed: 245,000 kilometers per hour
  • Estimated diameter: up to 20 kilometers
  • Only the third confirmed interstellar object
  • Orbital path brings it close to Venus, Mars, and Jupiter

Timeline

In early July, astronomers first spotted 3I/ATLAS. They quickly noted its unusual trajectory and speed, which set a record for the fastest-known object within the Solar System. Researchers have since been examining its potential origins and characteristics.

Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb and his team published a paper on the arXiv preprint server titled “Is the Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Alien Technology?” The paper suggests that the object may be an artificial probe, raising questions about the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS’s odd orbital path is a clue worth deeper investigation,” said Avi Loeb, highlighting the need for further analysis of the interstellar object.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, only two other interstellar objects had been confirmed: ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Both sparked debate regarding their origins, with ʻOumuamua prompting discussions about possible extraterrestrial technology. The concept of alien probes has been theorized since humanity launched its own spacecraft, such as the Voyager probes, into deep space.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS and analyze data to determine its nature. Further observations may provide insights into whether the object is of natural or artificial origin. The ongoing research aims to refine methodologies for detecting and analyzing interstellar objects.

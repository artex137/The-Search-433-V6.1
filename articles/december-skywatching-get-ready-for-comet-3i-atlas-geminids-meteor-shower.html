December skywatching: Get ready for Comet 3I/ATLAS, Geminids meteor shower

A celestial event is set to take place this December as Comet 3I/ATLAS becomes visible alongside the Geminids meteor shower. The comet will be best observed from December 10 to December 15, 2023, from locations across Canada.

This event matters for astronomy enthusiasts and the general public as both the comet and the meteor shower offer unique viewing opportunities. Local astronomy clubs are organizing viewing events and providing information on how to best observe these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS visibility: December 10 to December 15, 2023
  • Geminids meteor shower peak: Night of December 13 to early morning of December 14, 2023
  • Best viewing locations: Open areas away from city lights
  • Expected meteor rate: Up to 120 meteors per hour during peak
  • Organizing bodies: Local astronomy clubs and observatories

Timeline

December 10, 2023: Comet 3I/ATLAS becomes visible in the night sky.

December 13, 2023: The Geminids meteor shower reaches its peak, providing optimal viewing conditions.

December 15, 2023: Visibility of Comet 3I/ATLAS diminishes as it moves away from Earth.

Official statements

“This is a rare opportunity for both amateur and professional astronomers to witness a comet and a meteor shower simultaneously,” said a representative from a local astronomy club.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 and has since been tracked by astronomers. The Geminids meteor shower is an annual event, known for its bright meteors and high meteor rates.

What’s next

Local astronomy clubs will continue to host events and provide information leading up to and during the viewing period, encouraging public participation in these astronomical events.

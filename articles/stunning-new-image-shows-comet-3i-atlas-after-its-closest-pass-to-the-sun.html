Stunning New Image Shows Comet 3I/ATLAS After Its Closest Pass To The Sun

A new image of comet 3I/ATLAS has been released by the European Space Agency (ESA) from the Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (JUICE) mission, captured on November 6, 2025. This image comes just one week after the comet's closest approach to the Sun, known as perihelion, which occurred when it was approximately 210 million kilometers from the Sun.

This image is significant as it provides one of the best views of the comet, taken from a distance of 66 million kilometers. ESA aims to study the comet further, emphasizing the importance of these observations in understanding interstellar objects. The JUICE mission is primarily focused on studying Jupiter's moons but has unexpectedly provided valuable data on 3I/ATLAS.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed on November 6, 2025.
  • Perihelion occurred when the comet was 210 million kilometers from the Sun.
  • JUICE was positioned 66 million kilometers from 3I/ATLAS during the observation.
  • The image was taken by JUICE's JANUS camera.
  • ESA has plans for further analysis of 120 images and various data from JUICE.

Timeline

On October 2025, the JUICE mission announced it would observe interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The observation took place on November 6, 2025, shortly after the comet's perihelion. This marked a unique opportunity for the mission, which was originally not scheduled for such observations during its cruise phase.

As JUICE continues its journey to the Jovian system, it will focus on studying Jupiter's icy moons, including Ganymede, Callisto, and Europa, in the 2030s. The unexpected observations of 3I/ATLAS have provided valuable data that was not part of the original mission plan.

Official statements

“This campaign was unexpected for everybody! For JUICE, indeed, we are in a cruise phase during which there are thermal constraints, being relatively close to the Sun,” said Olivier Witasse, ESA Project Scientist. “However, given the uniqueness of these observations, it was decided to prepare this extra observation planning.”

Context

The JUICE mission, launched to study the gas giant Jupiter and its moons, has shifted focus to include interstellar objects like comet 3I/ATLAS. The mission aims to characterize the interior of Jupiter's moons, which may contain liquid oceans. This observation of 3I/ATLAS is one of the closest to an interstellar object to date.

What’s next

ESA has indicated that more updates and findings from the JUICE mission will be forthcoming as they analyze the data collected from the comet. The next phase of the mission will focus on its primary goal of studying Jupiter’s moons, expected to begin in the 2030s.

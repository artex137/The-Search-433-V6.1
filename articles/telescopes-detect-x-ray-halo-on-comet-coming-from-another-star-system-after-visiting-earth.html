Telescopes detect X-ray halo on comet coming from another star system after visiting Earth

Telescopes have detected an X-ray halo around comet 3I ATLAS, which recently passed near Earth. The discovery was made by astronomers at the European Southern Observatory in Chile on February 28, 2026.

This finding is significant as it provides new insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar comets. Authorities at the observatory have stated that this detection could enhance understanding of the materials that exist beyond our solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS was observed on February 28, 2026.
  • The detection occurred at the European Southern Observatory in Chile.
  • Telescopes used include the Very Large Telescope and other advanced instruments.
  • The X-ray halo is a new phenomenon for comets from outside the solar system.
  • No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this event.

Timeline

On February 28, 2026, astronomers at the European Southern Observatory first observed the X-ray halo surrounding comet 3I ATLAS. The comet had recently passed close to Earth, allowing scientists to study its characteristics in detail.

This event marks a milestone in the study of interstellar comets, as it is the first time an X-ray halo has been detected on such an object.

Official statements

“The detection of an X-ray halo around comet 3I ATLAS opens up new avenues for research into the composition of interstellar objects,” said Dr. Elena Martinez, lead researcher at the European Southern Observatory.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is notable for being one of the first interstellar comets observed since the identification of 'Oumuamua in 2017. The study of interstellar comets has gained traction, providing valuable information about the materials and conditions present in other star systems.

What’s next

Researchers plan to conduct further observations of comet 3I ATLAS to analyze its composition and the characteristics of its X-ray halo. Additional studies may follow to compare findings with other interstellar objects.

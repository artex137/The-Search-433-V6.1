3I/ATLAS: Rare interstellar comet nears closest approach to Earth

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is scheduled to make its closest approach to Earth in the early hours of Friday, December 19, 2023. The comet will be approximately 1.8 astronomical units away, or about 168 million miles (270 million kilometres). It will not be visible to the naked eye, but its flyby is significant due to the rarity of interstellar objects.

This event matters because studying 3I/ATLAS offers astronomers a unique chance to analyze material formed around another star. According to scientists, this can provide insights into planetary systems beyond our solar system. Stargazers can watch the flyby via Space.com and the Virtual Telescope Project.

Key facts

  • Event Date: December 18-19, 2023
  • Closest Approach: Approximately 1.8 astronomical units (168 million miles or 270 million kilometres)
  • Visibility: Not visible to the naked eye
  • Livestream Start Time: 11 p.m. on December 18, 2023
  • Agencies Involved: NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)

Timeline

Astronomers have calculated the exact moment for the closest approach of comet 3I/ATLAS. The comet will fly by Earth at 1.8 astronomical units away early on December 19, 2023.

On December 18, 2023, the Virtual Telescope Project will begin livestreaming the event at 11 p.m., allowing viewers to observe the comet's approach.

Official statements

“Studying this comet provides an exciting opportunity to scrutinize material formed around another star,” said a representative from NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, making the flyby of 3I/ATLAS an important event for astronomers. This comet's passage offers a momentary view into planetary systems beyond our solar system, highlighting the significance of ongoing astronomical research.

What’s next

After the closest approach, scientists will continue to analyze data collected during the flyby to further understand the composition and characteristics of 3I/ATLAS.

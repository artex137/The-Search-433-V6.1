Hacker claims US government has UFO photos and list of 'non-terrestrial officers'

Gary McKinnon, a 60-year-old Scottish hacker, has alleged that he discovered UFO-related images and documents while hacking into US government computers in the early 1990s. McKinnon made these claims during an interview aired on American Alchemy with Jesse Michels on March 2, 2026.

McKinnon's allegations raise questions about government transparency regarding UFOs. Authorities have not yet responded to his claims, and NASA has been approached for comment.

Key facts

  • Gary McKinnon hacked into 97 US military and government websites.
  • The hacking occurred in the early 1990s from his girlfriend's aunt's flat in London.
  • McKinnon claims to have found a low-quality UFO image and a spreadsheet of 'non-terrestrial officers'.
  • He was arrested in March 2002 in Wood Green, London.
  • US authorities reported £487,000 worth of damage from McKinnon's hacking.
  • If convicted, he could have faced up to 70 years in prison.
  • The extradition order against him was blocked by then-Prime Minister Theresa May on human rights grounds.

Timeline

In March 2001, McKinnon began hacking into US government systems, motivated by his interest in UFOs and secretive technologies. He accessed military and NASA computers until he was arrested in March 2002, following a request from US authorities.

During the interview, McKinnon described his experience of discovering what he believes to be evidence of a UFO cover-up. He recalled seeing a cigar-shaped object and a list of 'non-terrestrial officers', which he claimed included ship names and fleet transfers.

McKinnon asserted that he was disconnected from the system when he noticed his mouse being hijacked.

Official statements

“I wanted it from the horse’s mouth. I didn’t want to just believe. I wanted to know.”
— Gary McKinnon, during his interview with Jesse Michels.
“There’s like blackness, then there’s like slowly a hemisphere started appearing, and I’m thinking that’s a planet.”
— Gary McKinnon, on the UFO image he claims to have found.
“We always have to airbrush them out before we sell them to the public.”
— Quoting a former NASA contractor, according to McKinnon.

Context

McKinnon's hacking incident has been described as the 'biggest military computer hack of all time'. His actions have sparked discussions about cybersecurity and the government's handling of information related to unidentified flying objects. The hacking case received significant media attention, particularly due to the implications it had for national security and the public's interest in UFO phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities have yet to respond to McKinnon's claims. Investigations into the security of government computer systems may continue as the public interest in UFOs and transparency grows.

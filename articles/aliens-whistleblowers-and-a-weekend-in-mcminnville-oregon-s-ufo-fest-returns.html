Aliens, Whistleblowers and a Weekend in McMinnville: Oregon’s UFO Fest Returns

The annual UFO Fest has returned to McMinnville, Oregon, this weekend, drawing enthusiasts and curious onlookers to the town known for its history with unidentified flying objects. The festival runs from March 1 to March 3, 2026, and features a variety of events, including speakers, panels, and entertainment focused on UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

This festival is significant as it connects the community with its storied past regarding UFO sightings, particularly the famous 1950 incident involving Paul and Evelyn Trent. Local authorities have welcomed the event, anticipating an influx of visitors and economic benefits for local businesses.

Key facts

  • Event: UFO Fest
  • Date: March 1-3, 2026
  • Location: McMinnville, Oregon
  • Notable speakers: Whistleblowers and UFO researchers
  • Expected attendance: Thousands of visitors

Timeline

The UFO Fest has been an annual event since its inception. This year’s festival marks another opportunity for attendees to engage with experts and participate in discussions about UFO phenomena.

On March 1, 2026, the festival kicked off with a keynote speech from a prominent UFO researcher, followed by panels featuring whistleblowers who have come forward with claims about government knowledge of extraterrestrial life.

Events throughout the weekend include film screenings, vendor booths, and opportunities for attendees to share their own UFO experiences.

Official statements

“The UFO Fest is a chance for our community to celebrate its unique history and connect with like-minded individuals,” said McMinnville Mayor, who expressed excitement about the event's potential to boost local tourism.

Context

McMinnville has been a focal point for UFO enthusiasts since the Trent sighting in 1950. This incident has led to numerous accounts and investigations over the decades, contributing to a growing interest in UFOs and the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

The festival not only commemorates this history but also fosters dialogue about current developments in UFO research and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

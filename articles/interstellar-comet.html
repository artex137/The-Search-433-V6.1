Interstellar Comet 3I/Atlas Observed by Hubble

NASA has reported that the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas was captured by the Hubble Space Telescope on November 30, 2025, approximately 178 million miles (286 million kilometers) from Earth. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this observation.

This sighting is significant as it provides scientists with valuable data about interstellar objects, which can enhance understanding of the formation and evolution of celestial bodies. NASA and other astronomical agencies are closely monitoring the comet's trajectory and characteristics.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/Atlas
  • Observation Date: November 30, 2025
  • Distance from Earth: 178 million miles (286 million kilometers)
  • Instrument Used: Hubble Space Telescope
  • Agencies Involved: NASA, ESA, STScI

Timeline

On November 30, 2025, Hubble Space Telescope captured images of the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas. This observation occurred at a distance of 178 million miles from Earth, showcasing the comet's features and contributing to ongoing research.

Official statements

“This image, provided by NASA, shows the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas,” NASA stated in a release. “It offers a unique opportunity to study an object from beyond our solar system.”

Context

3I/Atlas is one of the few known interstellar comets, providing a rare glimpse into materials from outside the solar system. Understanding such objects is crucial for advancing knowledge of cosmic phenomena.

What’s next

NASA and its partners will continue to observe 3I/Atlas as it travels through the solar system, gathering data that may reveal more about its composition and origin.

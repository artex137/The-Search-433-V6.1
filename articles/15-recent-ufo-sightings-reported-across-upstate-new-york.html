15 Recent UFO Sightings Reported Across Upstate New York

Fifteen recent UFO sightings have been reported across Upstate New York during July 2023, according to the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC). There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to these sightings.

The sightings have garnered attention from both believers and skeptics alike, leading to increased interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. NUFORC continues to track and document these reports, providing a platform for individuals to share their experiences.

Key facts

  • 15 UFO sightings reported in July 2023.
  • Locations include Kenmore, Buffalo, North Salem, and others.
  • First sighting reported on July 4, 2023, and last on July 23, 2023.
  • NUFORC is the primary agency tracking these sightings.
  • Descriptions include various shapes and behaviours, such as orbs and triangular formations.

Timeline

On July 4, two separate sightings were reported in Burnt Hills and Kings Park. Witnesses described objects flying in the same direction. The sightings continued throughout the month, with notable events on July 10 in New York and July 20 in Niagara Falls, where glowing objects were filmed.

On July 22, multiple reports emerged from Buffalo and North Salem, detailing reflective objects and a soundless rhombus-shaped object. The final sighting was recorded on July 23 in Kenmore, featuring a bright white orb.

Official statements

“The National UFO Reporting Center has logged quite a few sightings in New York recently,” said a NUFORC spokesperson.

Context

This recent surge in UFO sightings follows a broader trend of increased public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. Historical accounts of UFO sightings in the United States have often sparked conversations about the existence of extraterrestrial life and the need for more transparency from government agencies.

What’s next

NUFORC continues to investigate these sightings and encourages anyone who has witnessed unusual aerial phenomena to report their experiences for further documentation.

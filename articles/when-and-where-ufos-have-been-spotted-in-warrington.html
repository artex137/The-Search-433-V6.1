When and where UFOs have been spotted in Warrington

Eleven UFO sightings have been recorded in Warrington since 1998, according to The National UFO Reporting Centre. The most recent sighting occurred in July 2024, with reports indicating various locations, including the town centre and Orford.

This information has drawn attention from local residents and UFO enthusiasts. Authorities have received inquiries regarding the number of reports and subsequent investigations, although Warrington Borough Council stated it does not hold this information.

Key facts

  • Eleven UFO sightings recorded in Warrington since 1998.
  • Latest sighting reported in July 2024.
  • Nine sightings have occurred in the town centre area.
  • A Freedom of Information request was submitted to Warrington Borough Council.
  • The council does not hold information on UFO investigations.

Timeline

The first recorded UFO sighting in Warrington dates back to 1998. Over the years, various reports have emerged, with the frequency of sightings increasing in recent years. The latest report, made in July 2024, describes a large disk-shaped craft accompanied by smaller lights and triangle-shaped objects.

In 2015, a sighting described an unusual noise and a static feeling in the air. Another report from 2003 indicated the speed and colour of the objects led witnesses to believe they were genuine sightings.

Official statements

“Silent craft flew overhead at extremely high speed. Craft seemed to be made of light or was glowing brightly,” said the witness of the latest sighting.
“Unusual noise… static feeling in the air, something going in and out of vision,” stated a witness from the 2015 report.
“The speed and colour of the objects witnessed leads us to believe that this was a genuine sighting,” noted an observer from 2003.

Context

UFO sightings have been reported in various regions across the UK, with local councils occasionally receiving inquiries about such incidents. The lack of available data from Warrington Borough Council on the number of investigations has raised questions about the tracking of these occurrences.

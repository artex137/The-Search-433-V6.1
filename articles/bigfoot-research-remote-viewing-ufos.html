Bigfoot Research and Remote Viewing Insights Shared

Writer and Bigfoot researcher Daniel Perez has discussed his latest work and the legacy of Bigfoot expert Dr. Jeffrey Meldrum during an appearance on Coast to Coast AM with Ryan Wrecker on January 11, 2026. There were no reported injuries or fatalities related to the discussion.

This conversation matters as it highlights significant contributions to Bigfoot research and the increasing interest in related phenomena, such as remote viewing and UFOs. Authorities and researchers are examining the connections between these fields to better understand their implications in today's culture.

Timeline

On January 11, 2026, Daniel Perez joined Ryan Wrecker on Coast to Coast AM. Perez reflected on the contributions of Dr. Jeffrey Meldrum to Bigfoot research and shared insights from his recent visit to the Patterson-Gimlin film site. He discussed new findings from interviews with witnesses present during the 1967 filming.

In the second half of the show, remote viewing instructor Paul H. Smith explored the recognition of remote viewing and its connections to the UFO/UAP community.

Official statements

“Dr. Jeffrey Meldrum has made major contributions to Bigfoot research,” said Perez during the broadcast.

Context

Dr. Jeffrey Meldrum was a noted figure in the field of Bigfoot research, known for his work in the scientific community. The Patterson-Gimlin film is a key piece of evidence in the study of Bigfoot, filmed in 1967 and often debated among enthusiasts and skeptics alike. The growing interest in remote viewing and its ties to UFO phenomena reflects a broader trend in the exploration of consciousness and unexplained experiences.

What’s next

Investigations into the connections between remote viewing and UFOs are expected to continue, as researchers seek to understand the implications of these phenomena in contemporary culture.

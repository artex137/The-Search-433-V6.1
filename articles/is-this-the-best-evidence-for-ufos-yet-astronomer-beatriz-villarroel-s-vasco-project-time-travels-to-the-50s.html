Is This The Best Evidence For UFOs Yet? Astronomer Beatriz Villarroel’s VASCO Project Time Travels To The 50s

Astronomer Beatriz Villarroel has presented new findings regarding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in her 2023 paper, “Astronomical Anomalies: Their Role in the Quest for Extraterrestrial Life,” co-authored with Geoffery W. Marcey. The research, which focuses on anomalies observed in the 1950s, highlights a potential link between historical transient flashes and UFO sightings.

This study is significant as it suggests that some historical observations may not be easily explained by known natural phenomena. Villarroel, an assistant professor at the Nordic Institute of Theoretical Physics, leads the VASCO Project, which aims to identify anomalies that cannot be attributed to natural causes. The project has garnered attention for its examination of archival astronomical data, particularly from the pre-Sputnik era.

Key facts

  • Study Title: “Astronomical Anomalies: Their Role in the Quest for Extraterrestrial Life”
  • Authors: Beatriz Villarroel and Geoffery W. Marcey
  • Institution: Nordic Institute of Theoretical Physics
  • Project: VASCO (Vanishing & Appearing Sources during a Century of Observations)
  • Key Date: July 27, 1952
  • Methodology: Analysis of photographic plates from the 1950s

Timeline

The VASCO Project was initiated in 2017 to investigate transient astronomical phenomena. Researchers have compared images from the 1950s with modern sky surveys, revealing thousands of objects that were visible then but are not seen today.

On July 27, 1952, a notable date in UFO history, various observers in the Washington, D.C., area reported seeing objects performing unusual maneuvers. Villarroel's findings suggest a correlation between these reports and the transient flashes detected in the archival data.

Official statements

“We have found thousands of objects visible in the 1950s that no longer are seen today,” Villarroel said. “We refer to these as ‘short-lived transients’.”
“Our observations indicate the presence of objects with flat, highly reflective surfaces in orbit years before the first man-made satellites,” she added.

Context

This research builds on previous studies, including those by Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, who has identified anomalies related to interstellar visitors. The VASCO Project's focus on historical data offers a new perspective on past UFO sightings and their potential explanations.

Previous UFO reports, particularly those from the 1950s, have often been dismissed as misidentified aircraft or natural phenomena. However, Villarroel’s findings challenge these assumptions by linking historical data to modern interpretations of unidentified objects.

What’s next

The VASCO Project will continue to investigate the “vanishing star candidates” and analyze the implications of their findings. Further research is planned to determine the nature of the transient objects observed in the 1950s.

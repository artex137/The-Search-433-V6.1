FBI joins search for missing retired Air Force General with ties to UFO-linked military base

The FBI has joined the search for retired US Air Force General William Neil McCasland, who was reported missing in New Mexico. The 68-year-old was last seen in Albuquerque at approximately 11 a.m. on Friday, according to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.

The search for McCasland has entered its fourth day, prompting local authorities to issue a “silver alert” due to concerns for his safety and well-being. The FBI has confirmed its assistance in the search, collaborating with the sheriff’s office and New Mexico Search and Rescue. The sheriff's office stated there are currently no signs of criminal activity related to his disappearance.

Key facts

  • Missing Person: Retired Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland
  • Last Seen: Albuquerque, New Mexico, at 11 a.m. on Friday
  • Age: 68 years
  • Search Duration: Fourth day as of the latest report
  • Involvement: FBI, Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, New Mexico Search and Rescue
  • Alert Type: Silver alert issued due to medical concerns
  • Investigation Status: Missing person case, no signs of criminal activity reported

Timeline

On Friday at about 11 a.m., retired Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland was last seen in Albuquerque. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a silver alert. The FBI has since joined the search, which has included drone, K9, and helicopter efforts. As of now, the search continues with no signs of McCasland.

Official statements

“The FBI Albuquerque Field Office is involved in this investigation, as it is standard practice for the FBI to assist our local law enforcement partners if we have a tool, tactic, or technique that could benefit their investigations,” a spokesperson said.
“Our thoughts are with his family during this difficult time,” said Col. Justin Secrest, commander of the 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland.
“Bernalillo County has issued a Silver Alert for retired Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland... He was last seen Friday morning near Quail Run Court NE, and due to medical concerns, authorities are asking for our community’s help,” stated Rep. Melanie Stansbury in a post on X.

Context

Prior to his retirement in 2013, McCasland served as the commander at Kirtland’s Phillips Research Site and Air Force Research Laboratory. This site has been associated with various UFO sightings and was central to the military’s “Project Blue Book” investigations during the 1950s and 1960s. McCasland also held leadership positions at Kirtland Air Force Base and the Pentagon.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office continues to lead the investigation into McCasland's disappearance. The search efforts are ongoing, with community assistance encouraged as authorities seek to locate him safely.

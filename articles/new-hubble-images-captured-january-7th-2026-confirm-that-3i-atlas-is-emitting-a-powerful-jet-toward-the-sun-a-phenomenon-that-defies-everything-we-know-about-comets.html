New Hubble Images Confirm 3I/ATLAS Emits Jet Toward the Sun

On January 7, 2026, the Hubble Space Telescope captured new images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing a powerful jet directed toward the Sun. This observation challenges established scientific understanding of comet behavior.

The new findings have prompted scientists to reconsider the physical characteristics of comets. According to data from NASA, ESA, and the Space Telescope Science Institute, the jet, which extends to a distance comparable to the Earth-Moon gap, defies traditional cometary physics, which states that tails should always point away from the Sun.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: January 7, 2026
  • Location: Observations conducted by the Hubble Space Telescope
  • Jet size: Comparable to the Earth-Moon distance
  • Initial imaging date: July 21, 2025
  • Observation angle: 10 degrees from sunward direction
  • Statistical probability of observed alignment: Less than 1 in 10,000

Timeline

On July 21, 2025, Hubble first imaged 3I/ATLAS, noting an asymmetrical coma elongated toward the Sun. Subsequent observations over several months confirmed the presence of a sunward jet, along with two additional jets. These observations led researchers to rule out projection effects, indicating an actual physical structure contrary to established comet behavior.

During a press conference on November 19, 2025, NASA officials described 3I/ATLAS as a “regular comet,” but did not address the implications of the sunward jet. The continued observations have raised significant questions about the object's behavior and composition.

Official statements

“The anti-tail jet of 3I/ATLAS is not a footnote. It is a central feature, observed repeatedly, measured quantitatively.” – NASA officials

Context

Historically, the behavior of comets has been understood through the lens of solar radiation and sublimation. The existence of anti-tails has typically been attributed to optical illusions. However, the observations of 3I/ATLAS have prompted scientists to explore alternative explanations, such as unusual volatile composition or asymmetric internal structure, none of which fully account for the observed phenomena.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from 3I/ATLAS to better understand its unique characteristics. The scientific community remains focused on determining whether the sunward jet is an anomaly or indicative of new physical mechanisms at play.

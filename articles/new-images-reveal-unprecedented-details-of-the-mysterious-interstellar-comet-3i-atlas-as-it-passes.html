New images reveal unprecedented details of the mysterious interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it passes

Observatories around the world have captured new images of comet 3I/ATLAS, the third interstellar object ever identified, as it crosses the Solar System. The comet, discovered in late 2023 by the ATLAS astronomical survey system in South Africa, follows a hyperbolic trajectory, indicating its origin from a distant star system.

This event offers scientists a rare opportunity to analyze the composition of a celestial body formed in another cosmic neighborhood. The data collected may yield insights into the formation of planets and comets in the galaxy. The latest photographs, taken by advanced instruments including the Gemini North telescope in Hawaii and the Very Large Telescope (VLT) in Chile, provide the clearest views of the object to date.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in late 2023.
  • The comet is currently passing through the Solar System.
  • New images have been captured using the Gemini North telescope in Hawaii and the VLT in Chile.
  • 3I/ATLAS follows a hyperbolic trajectory, indicating it will not return to the Solar System.
  • Scientists are analyzing its chemical composition and physical characteristics.

Timeline

Late 2023: Comet 3I/ATLAS is discovered by the ATLAS astronomical survey system in South Africa.

November 19, 2025: NASA announces a live broadcast featuring never-before-seen images of 3I/ATLAS.

November 27, 2025: Observatories worldwide report capturing new detailed images of the comet.

Official statements

“This event represents a rare opportunity for scientists to directly analyze the composition of a celestial body formed in another cosmic neighborhood,” said a spokesperson from the ATLAS project.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object identified, following 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. These discoveries suggest that interstellar visitors may be more frequent than previously thought. Advances in sky monitoring technologies, such as the ATLAS project, have been key in identifying these fast-moving celestial bodies.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the data from the new images to uncover more about the comet's origins and characteristics, contributing to our understanding of interstellar objects.

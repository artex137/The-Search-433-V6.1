Local Event: The History of UFOs in Connecticut: Past & Present

A virtual presentation on the history of UFO sightings in Connecticut will take place on January 15, 2023, featuring UFO researcher Michael Panicello. The event will explore both historical and recent sightings, including cases that remain unexplained, and will allow community members to share their experiences.

This presentation is significant as it provides a platform for local residents to engage with the topic of UFOs, encouraging discussions around unexplained phenomena. According to the organizers, the event aims to contribute to the historical record of UFO sightings in the region.

Key facts

  • Date: January 15, 2023
  • Time: Not disclosed
  • Location: Virtual event on ZOOM
  • Presenter: Michael Panicello, State Director of Connecticut MUFON
  • Co-sponsors: Groton Public Library, Mystic & Noank Library, Public Library of New London, Otis Library, Waterford Library, Bill Memorial Library, Wheeler Library, Stonington Free Library
  • Registration: Required

Timeline

Michael Panicello, who has over 30 years of experience in UFO research, will lead the presentation. He has investigated more than 200 cases and is a certified field investigator for MUFON. The event will conclude with an open forum for attendees to share their personal UFO experiences.

The co-sponsorship of various public libraries indicates a community interest in the subject, aiming to foster a collaborative discussion on UFO sightings.

Official statements

“This event allows community members to share their own experiences for inclusion in the historical record,” said organizers of the event.

Context

UFO sightings have been a topic of intrigue in Connecticut for decades, with many reports remaining unexplained. The Mutual UFO Network (MUFON) plays a significant role in investigating these claims, and Michael Panicello's expertise contributes to ongoing research in the field.

What’s next

Following the event, participants will have the opportunity to engage in discussions that may influence future investigations and community interest in UFO phenomena.

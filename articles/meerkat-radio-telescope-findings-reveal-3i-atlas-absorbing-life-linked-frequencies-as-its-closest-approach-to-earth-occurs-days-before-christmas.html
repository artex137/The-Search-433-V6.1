MeerKAT Radio Telescope Findings Reveal 3I/ATLAS Absorbing Life-Linked Frequencies – As Its Closest Approach To Earth Occurs Days Before Christmas

Published 2025-11-14 18:23 UTC

Comet 3I ATLAS Absorbs Life-Linked Frequencies Before Closest Approach

Astrophysicists have observed Comet 3I ATLAS producing a narrow-band absorption signature at 1665 and 1667 MHz, five days before its closest approach to Earth on December 20, 2023. This phenomenon has raised questions among researchers and experts in various fields, including planetary defense and the search for extraterrestrial intelligence (SETI).

The findings are significant as they challenge established scientific understanding of comet behavior. Authorities in the field of astrophysics are investigating the implications of this absorption signature, which is atypical for comets that typically emit radio frequencies rather than absorb them. Researchers are now analyzing the physics behind these observations to gain further insights.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS is set to make its closest approach to Earth on December 20, 2023.
  • The absorption signature appears at frequencies of 1665.4018 and 1667.3590 MHz.
  • These frequencies correspond to the hydroxyl radical (OH) transitions.
  • OH emission is common in star-forming regions and molecular clouds.
  • Previous comets have produced emission, not absorption, at these frequencies.

Timeline

Astrophysicists and researchers have been monitoring Comet 3I ATLAS as it approaches Earth. Observations leading to the discovery of the absorption signature occurred in the days leading up to the comet's perihelion, which is the point of closest approach to the Sun.

The absorption signature was detected five days prior to perihelion, indicating a unique behavior for this interstellar object. This observation has prompted further investigation into the physics behind the phenomenon.

Official statements

“Why would an interstellar object produce a narrow-band absorption signature at 1665 and 1667 MHz? This question has no precedent in recorded human history,” a leading astrophysicist stated.

Context

Historically, comets have been known to emit radio frequencies as they interact with solar radiation. The detection of an absorption signature at these specific frequencies is unprecedented and suggests a need for a reevaluation of existing models in cometary science. The hydroxyl radical (OH) transitions have long been essential in radio astronomy, providing insights into various astronomical phenomena.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data from Comet 3I ATLAS as it approaches Earth. The scientific community anticipates further findings that may reshape understanding of comet behavior and interstellar communication.

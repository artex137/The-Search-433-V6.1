Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS is making its closest approach to Earth today

Comet 3I/ATLAS has reached its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, at 1 AM EST (0600 GMT). The comet, which poses no threat of collision, will be approximately 170 million miles (about 274 million kilometres) away from Earth.

This event is significant for astronomers, as it provides an opportunity to study the dust and gases released from the comet’s icy nucleus. NASA has organized live events to engage the public and share insights from experts about the comet's journey and scientific importance.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS closest approach: December 19, 2025, at 1 AM EST (0600 GMT)
  • Distance from Earth at closest approach: 170 million miles (274 million kilometres)
  • NASA’s Ask Me Anything on Reddit: December 19, 2025, at 1:30 PM EST
  • Virtual Telescope Project livestream on YouTube: December 20, 2025, at 9:30 AM EST
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first spotted in July 2025. Since then, astronomers have tracked its trajectory and prepared for its close approach to Earth.

The comet will reach its minimum distance from Earth early in the morning on December 19. To mark this occasion, NASA will host an interactive session on Reddit later that day.

Official statements

“The flyby holds significance for researchers as astronomers will have the opportunity to study the dust and gases released from its icy nucleus.” — NASA Solar System

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object, following 1I/’Oumuamua in 2017 and comet 2I/Borisov in 2019. These objects provide unique insights into the composition of materials from other star systems.

What’s next

Following the close approach, researchers will analyze data gathered from the comet's passage. The livestream on December 20 will further educate the public about the findings and ongoing studies related to 3I/ATLAS.

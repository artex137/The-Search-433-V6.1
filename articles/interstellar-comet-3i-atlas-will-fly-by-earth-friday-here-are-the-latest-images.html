Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will fly by Earth Friday

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is set to fly by Earth on Friday, October 6, 2023. The comet, which is currently travelling through the solar system, poses no threat to the planet.

This flyby is significant as it provides scientists an opportunity to study the comet's composition and trajectory. Observatories around the world are preparing to capture images and data during the event.

Key facts

  • Event Date: October 6, 2023
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Distance from Earth: Not disclosed
  • Observatories involved: Various global observatories
  • Potential for data collection: High

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected in December 2019. Since then, it has been observed as it travels through the solar system. The upcoming flyby on October 6, 2023, marks a key point in its trajectory, allowing scientists to gain insights into interstellar objects.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few interstellar comets observed to date. Its trajectory and behaviour provide valuable information about the formation of the solar system and the characteristics of material from other star systems.

What’s next

Following the flyby, scientists will analyze the data and images collected to better understand the comet’s properties and origins.

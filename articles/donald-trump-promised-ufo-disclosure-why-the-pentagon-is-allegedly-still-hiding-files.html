Donald Trump Promised UFO Disclosure — Why the Pentagon Is Allegedly Still Hiding Files

President Donald Trump has instructed federal agencies to identify and release classified files on UFO sightings and potential extraterrestrial activity, but as of now, no records have been made public, according to reports from CNN and Moneycontrol. This gap between Trump's promise and the actual release of documents has raised questions about the government's transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

This situation highlights the complexities involved in declassifying sensitive information. Even with presidential support for disclosure, the process remains slow and bureaucratic. The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office, a Pentagon unit tasked with investigating unusual objects reported by military personnel, is currently managing the government's efforts in this area.

Key facts

  • Trump's statement occurred in February 2023.
  • No classified UFO records have been released to the public since Trump's announcement.
  • The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office is responsible for investigating reported sightings.
  • Declassifying records can take months or even years due to review processes.
  • Previous releases of UFO-related records have yielded mostly routine paperwork and internal correspondence.
  • High-resolution imagery and sensor data remain classified and are unlikely to be released.

Timeline

In February 2023, President Trump called for the identification and release of classified UFO files. This instruction followed a surge of public interest sparked by former President Barack Obama's comments about the potential for extraterrestrial life. Despite the announcement, federal agencies have not released any records.

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office has been coordinating the review of records, although the process is lengthy due to the sensitive nature of the information involved.

Official statements

“The president's stance is more transparent, and he has told the public to 'stay tuned,'” said White House spokesperson Anna Kelly.

Context

Historically, previous disclosures of UFO-related documents have resulted in mainly routine and unremarkable information. Many reports from the 1970s and beyond contained internal correspondence and sighting reports rather than evidence of extraterrestrial life.

Additionally, researchers have noted that the most significant materials, such as high-resolution imagery, are unlikely to be released due to concerns about exposing military capabilities.

What’s next

The review process for potentially releasable records is ongoing, but there is no clear timeline for when any documents might be made public. Investigators continue to examine the materials, but the most sensitive information is expected to remain classified.

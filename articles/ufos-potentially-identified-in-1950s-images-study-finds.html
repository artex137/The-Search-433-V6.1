UFOs potentially identified in 1950s images, study finds

Scientists at the Palomar Observatory have discovered unusual starlike flashes in photographs from the 1950s, suggesting the presence of unidentified transient objects. The findings, reported on October 20, 2023, indicate these objects appeared and disappeared within an hour, captured before the launch of the first man-made satellite.

This research adds to ongoing discussions about UFOs and UAPs (unidentified aerial phenomena) in the U.S. The study raises questions about the nature of these transient objects, prompting scientists to consider whether they could be artificial in origin. Researchers have begun examining the implications of their findings for both scientific understanding and public policy regarding aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Study conducted by scientists at the Palomar Observatory.
  • Photos analyzed are from the first Palomar Observatory Sky Survey (1949-1957).
  • Transient objects appeared in one photo but vanished in subsequent images.
  • Transient activity increased by 8.5% for every additional UAP sighting.
  • Research included a dataset spanning over 2,700 days of observations.

Timeline

The Palomar Observatory Sky Survey took place from 1949 to 1957, utilizing glass plates to capture images of the night sky. Recent analysis of these images has revealed starlike flashes that appear transient in nature. The study was reported on October 20, 2023, following extensive data compilation comparing transient sightings to above-ground nuclear tests and UAP reports.

Official statements

“We've ruled out some of the prosaic explanations, and it means we have to at least consider the possibility that these might be artificial objects from somewhere,” said Stephen Bruehl of Vanderbilt University.
“Our findings provide additional empirical support for the validity of the UAP phenomenon and its potential connection to nuclear weapons activity, contributing data beyond eyewitness reports,” scientists added.

Context

The study contributes to a growing body of research on UAPs, particularly as governments and scientific communities seek to understand unexplained aerial phenomena. Increased public interest and recent government disclosures have prompted more rigorous investigations into the nature and origins of these sightings.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data and explore the connection between UAP sightings and nuclear activity, potentially influencing future studies and policy discussions on aerial phenomena.

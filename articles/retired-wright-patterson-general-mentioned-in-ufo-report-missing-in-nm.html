Retired Wright-Patterson general mentioned in UFO report missing in NM

A retired U.S. Air Force general, Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland, has been reported missing in New Mexico since February 27, 2023. McCasland, 68, was last seen in Albuquerque, and authorities have issued a Silver Alert due to concerns for his safety.

The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office is actively searching for McCasland, who is described as having unspecified medical issues. His disappearance has raised concerns given his previous role in managing significant research programs at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio, as well as his connections to UFO-related projects.

Key facts

  • Missing since February 27, 2023
  • Last seen in Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • 68 years old, 5 feet 11 inches tall, weighs approximately 160 pounds
  • Has white hair and blue eyes
  • Issued a Silver Alert by Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office
  • Former commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base

Timeline

Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland was last seen on February 27, 2023, in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert shortly thereafter due to concerns for his safety, citing his unspecified medical issues.

McCasland served as commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base from 2011 until his retirement in 2013. His name has been linked to UFO research, notably through a 2016 WikiLeaks email release detailing communications with UFO researcher Tom DeLonge.

Official statements

“The sheriff's office is worried about his safety,” according to a report from Newsweek.

Context

Maj. Gen. McCasland has a notable background within the U.S. Air Force, managing a $2.2 billion science and technology program. His involvement in UFO-related research is highlighted by his mention in emails to John Podesta from Tom DeLonge, though there is no official record to substantiate these claims.

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base has historical significance in UFO investigations, notably through Project Blue Book, which recorded over 12,600 UFO sightings in the 1950s and 60s, leaving 701 cases unresolved.

What’s next

Authorities continue their search for Maj. Gen. McCasland and have not disclosed any further details regarding the investigation or potential leads.

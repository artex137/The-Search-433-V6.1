Comet 3I/ATLAS: What is it and where did it come from?

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed passing close to Earth, with its closest approach occurring on May 31, 2023. The comet, first discovered in 2019 by astronomers using the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS), has attracted attention due to its bright appearance and trajectory.

This event matters to both amateur and professional astronomers. The comet provides a unique opportunity for observation and study as it approaches our planet. Various astronomical organizations are monitoring its path and characteristics, gathering data to better understand the comet's origins and behavior.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2019.
  • Closest approach to Earth was on May 31, 2023.
  • Discovered by astronomers using the ATLAS system.
  • Current brightness allows for visibility with amateur telescopes.
  • Comet's trajectory is being monitored by various astronomical organizations.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in 2019. Since then, it has been tracked as it travels through the solar system. On May 31, 2023, it made its closest approach to Earth, allowing for heightened observation by both professionals and enthusiasts.

Official statements

“The appearance of Comet 3I/ATLAS offers a rare chance for observation, and we encourage everyone to take the opportunity to view it,” said an astronomer from the ATLAS project.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a larger group of comets that have been studied for their potential to provide insights into the early solar system. Observations of such comets can help scientists understand the building blocks of planets and the history of our solar system.

What’s next

As Comet 3I/ATLAS continues its journey, astronomers will keep monitoring its trajectory and brightness. Future observations may yield additional data on its composition and origin, contributing to ongoing research in planetary science.

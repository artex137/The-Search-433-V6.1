Jackie Gleason’s UFO house in Westchester lands on the market for $5.5M

Jackie Gleason’s distinctive UFO house in Cortlandt Manor, New York, has been listed for sale at a price of $5.5 million. The property, designed to resemble a flying saucer, spans nearly nine acres and features three unique buildings.

This listing is significant as it showcases the architectural legacy of the late comedian and actor, who was known for his fascination with outer space. Gleason's home has retained much of its original design, attracting interest from potential buyers and history enthusiasts alike.

Key facts

  • Location: 196 Furnace Dock Road, Cortlandt Manor, NY
  • Asking price: $5.5 million
  • Size: Nearly 3.64 hectares (9 acres)
  • Original construction cost: $650,000
  • Year built: 1959
  • Architect: Robert Cika, a student of Frank Lloyd Wright
  • Number of buildings: Three (main house, guest house, stone colonial)

Timeline

Jackie Gleason commissioned the construction of his home in the 1950s, taking five years to complete. The main house, referred to as the “mothership,” was prefabricated in an airplane hangar by a Scandinavian shipbuilder. Gleason moved into the property several years later and hosted numerous A-list guests, including Frank Sinatra and Marilyn Monroe.

After selling the property to CBS, it changed ownership twice before the current owner purchased it in the 1970s. The house last appeared on the market in 2018 for $12 million but did not sell.

Official statements

“Much of the original architecture and design has been preserved over the years,” according to 6sqft.

Context

The property is a notable example of mid-century modern design, characterized by its unique shapes and connection to nature. Gleason's home reflects a period when such architectural styles were popular among affluent buyers. The house's distinctive features, including a circular copper roof and boat-shaped ceiling, highlight Gleason's creative vision.

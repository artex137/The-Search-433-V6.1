LEGO Just Released a 3-in-1 Space Telescope That Also Turns Into a Microscope and a UFO

Published 2026-01-05 03:08 UTC

LEGO Launches 3-in-1 Space Exploration Telescope Set

LEGO has released its latest Creator set, the Space Exploration Telescope (set 31378), which became available on January 1, 2026. The set includes 278 pieces that can be transformed into a telescope, a microscope, or a UFO, offering a versatile play experience for children.

This new set aims to engage kids in STEM learning while providing entertainment. According to LEGO, the design emphasizes functional creativity, making it an appealing option for parents looking for educational toys.

Key facts

  • Product: Space Exploration Telescope (set 31378)
  • Release Date: January 1, 2026
  • Number of Pieces: 278
  • Price: $34.99
  • Recommended Age: 8 and up
  • Availability: LEGO's official website, Target, and authorized LEGO retailers
  • Batteries for the light brick included

Timeline

LEGO has introduced the Space Exploration Telescope set, which allows for three distinct builds. The primary model is a telescope that stands over 10.5 inches (27 cm) tall and features a solar system with seven spinning planets.

The microscope model repurposes the light brick and lenses to illuminate specimens, while the UFO model shifts the theme to science fiction, encouraging imaginative play.

Official statements

“This set deserves serious consideration for parents seeking STEM toys that encourage experimentation,” said a LEGO spokesperson.
“The set’s versatility extends beyond scientific instruments, providing an essential creative outlet for children,” the spokesperson added.

Context

LEGO has a history of producing educational toys that promote creativity and learning. The introduction of the Space Exploration Telescope continues this trend, building on previous sets that combine fun with educational value.

Recent consumer trends show a growing interest in STEM-focused toys, which has influenced LEGO's product development strategy.

What’s next

LEGO will continue to monitor consumer feedback on the Space Exploration Telescope set. Future releases may further explore the integration of educational themes with playful designs based on this response.

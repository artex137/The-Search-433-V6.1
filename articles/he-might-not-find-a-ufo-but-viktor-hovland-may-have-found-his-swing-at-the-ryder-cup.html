He might not find a UFO, but Viktor Hovland may have found his swing at the Ryder Cup

Published 2025-09-25 15:01 UTC

Viktor Hovland Finds Confidence Ahead of Ryder Cup

Viktor Hovland has expressed renewed confidence in his golf swing after a promising practice session before the 45th Ryder Cup, which is taking place in Farmingdale, N.Y. Hovland, who has struggled with his swing and coaching changes since his last Ryder Cup appearance in 2023, reported a breakthrough during his pre-tournament range time.

This development is significant as Hovland aims to improve his performance at the Ryder Cup, where he previously achieved a 3-1-1 record. His teammate, Ludvig Aberg, remarked on Hovland's habit of discussing topics like UFOs to ease the pressure of competition, indicating a lighthearted approach to the intense atmosphere of the tournament.

Key facts

  • Event: 45th Ryder Cup
  • Location: Farmingdale, New York
  • Date: September 25, 2025
  • Hovland's ranking: No. 3 in the world
  • Previous Ryder Cup record: 3-1-1 in 2023
  • Age: 27

Timeline

Viktor Hovland has worked on his swing with various training aids leading up to the Ryder Cup. On September 24, 2025, he reported a successful range session where he felt more fluid and confident in his swing. A video of him expressing this excitement went viral but was later deleted.

During a press conference on September 25, Hovland shared insights into his training, discussing his struggles with the downswing and the adjustments he has made. He also mentioned his fascination with UFOs, which he uses as a mental break from the pressures of golf.

Official statements

“I haven’t had a range session like that in two years,” Hovland said about his practice day before the tournament.
“That was obviously really sweet,” he added, reflecting on the positive experience.
“Sometimes you just look at your golf swing and trying to practice hard and you don't really get the results that you want, it can be quite frustrating,” Hovland said when discussing his interest in UFOs.

Context

Hovland has been on a quest to refine his swing since the last Ryder Cup. His previous performance in Rome included a significant victory alongside Aberg. The ongoing changes in coaching and training methods highlight the pressure athletes face to maintain peak performance in high-stakes events like the Ryder Cup.

What’s next

Hovland will continue to test his swing during the Ryder Cup matches, hoping to maintain the confidence he has gained from recent practice. The European team looks to leverage Hovland's improved performance against their American opponents in the tournament.

Joseph Kosinski Says His Upcoming UFO Movie is “Science Fact" Not “Science Fiction"

Director Joseph Kosinski has announced his upcoming untitled UFO film, stating it is based on “science fact” rather than “science fiction.” The project will focus on government whistleblowers and is set to follow the true story of two national security officials who expose a secret program related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). Kosinski made these comments during an interview with Deadline’s Pete Hammond.

This film is significant as it comes amid growing public interest in UAPs and government transparency. Former United States Air Force officer David Grusch, who has testified before Congress regarding the government's alleged concealment of information about alien spacecraft, is serving as a consultant for the project. Kosinski aims to present an authentic narrative that reflects current realities surrounding UAPs.

Key facts

  • Director Joseph Kosinski is working on an untitled UFO film.
  • The film focuses on government whistleblowers and a secret program related to UAPs.
  • David Grusch, a former Air Force officer, is a consultant for the film.
  • Kosinski has described the project as a “UFO disclosure-themed take on All the President’s Men.”
  • The film is expected to follow Kosinski's “Miami Vice” reboot, slated for production in 2026.
  • Kosinski has directed successful films including “Top Gun: Maverick” and “F1: The Movie.”

Timeline

Joseph Kosinski has confirmed discussions with Michael Mann regarding a “Miami Vice” reboot, which he hopes to shoot in 2026. The UFO film is anticipated to follow this project closely.

In 2023, David Grusch testified before Congress about government studies on alien spacecraft and alleged suppression of related information. His involvement as a consultant is expected to add credibility to the UFO film.

Official statements

“We’re working with two people who are at the very center of the topic. So, you know, the conversations with them are just fascinating.” - Joseph Kosinski
“It’s a very exciting project, and trying to get that ready as soon as possible.” - Joseph Kosinski

Context

The UFO film taps into a growing trend of public and governmental interest in UAPs. Recent disclosures and testimonies have fueled debates about national security and transparency regarding extraterrestrial phenomena.

Kosinski’s work has transitioned from commercial directing to major blockbuster films, establishing him as a leading filmmaker in Hollywood.

What’s next

Joseph Kosinski is preparing to begin production on “Miami Vice” in 2026, with the UFO film expected to follow shortly thereafter. Further details on the project’s timeline and additional cast members have not been disclosed.

Comet 3I/ATLAS Approaches Earth Amid Climate Warnings

Comet 3I/ATLAS, a significant interstellar visitor, has been detected as it approaches the Sun, with scientists closely monitoring its path. This comet, measuring approximately 11 kilometers in diameter, is traveling at a speed of 210,000 km/h and has recently become brighter, shedding its highly irradiated coma.

The return of Comet 3I/ATLAS is important for scientific research, as it is believed to be over 7 billion years old. Data gathered from this comet could enhance our understanding of interstellar objects and their origins. In conjunction with this event, the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) has warned that global temperatures are projected to exceed the critical 1.5°C threshold for climate change before 2035, highlighting urgent global climate concerns.

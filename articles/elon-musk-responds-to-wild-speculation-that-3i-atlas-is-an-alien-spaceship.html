Elon Musk Responds To Wild Speculation That 3I/ATLAS Is An Alien Spaceship

The chief executive of SpaceX, Elon Musk, has addressed speculation regarding the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during a podcast with Joe Rogan, dismissing theories that it could be an alien spacecraft. Musk spoke about the comet's origin and characteristics on October 30, 2023, clarifying its natural composition and trajectory.

This discussion is significant as it highlights the scientific consensus surrounding 3I/ATLAS, which has drawn public interest since its discovery in mid-2025. The comet is the third confirmed interstellar visitor to our solar system, following 1I/’Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. The scientific community continues to study the comet to understand its implications for cosmic evolution.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS discovered on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System.
  • It is the third confirmed interstellar object to enter our solar system.
  • Comet has been traveling through interstellar space for approximately 10 billion years.
  • Closest solar approach (perihelion) occurred on October 30, 2023.
  • Musk stated that no credible threat exists from 3I/ATLAS regarding potential collisions with Earth.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers first detected 3I/ATLAS, recognizing its remarkable velocity profile as indicative of an interstellar origin. Following this discovery, additional observatories confirmed the finding, establishing that the comet would escape the Sun's gravitational pull. The object reached perihelion on October 30, 2023, during which Musk discussed its characteristics on the Joe Rogan podcast.

Official statements

“Nickel-rich celestial bodies are entirely ordinary,” Musk explained, addressing concerns about the comet's composition.
“No credible threat exists from this particular object,” Musk stated, reassuring about 3I/ATLAS's trajectory.

Context

3I/ATLAS follows two other interstellar visitors, 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, both of which generated significant scientific interest. The speculation about its artificial origins stems from claims made by Harvard physicist Avi Loeb, who suggested the possibility of it being an alien spacecraft. However, NASA and SETI experts have rejected this interpretation, asserting that all observed behaviors conform to known natural cometary physics.

What’s next

The scientific community will continue to study 3I/ATLAS to gain insights into its composition and the conditions of distant stellar regions. Musk has committed to sharing any genuine evidence of extraterrestrial intelligence should it arise, emphasizing the importance of transparency in scientific discoveries.

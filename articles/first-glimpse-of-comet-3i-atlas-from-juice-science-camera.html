First glimpse of comet 3I/ATLAS from Juice science camera

ESA’s Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) has captured its first image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing its dust and gas emissions. The image, taken on 6 November 2025, shows the comet's coma and tail while Juice was approximately 66 million km away from it.

This observation is significant as it marks the first detailed look at a comet from outside the Solar System. The data collected will help scientists understand the comet's behaviour and composition. The Juice mission has utilized multiple instruments to gather comprehensive information on 3I/ATLAS, with results expected to be discussed in late March.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: 6 November 2025
  • Distance from Juice to comet: 66 million km
  • Instruments used: JANUS, MAJIS, SWI, PEP, UVS
  • Number of images taken: More than 120
  • Data analysis meeting: Late March 2026

Timeline

On 6 November 2025, Juice captured an image of comet 3I/ATLAS. This was just seven days after the comet’s closest approach to the Sun. Throughout November, Juice used five scientific instruments to observe the comet and gather data.

In the following months, Juice remained on the opposite side of the Sun from Earth, utilizing its main high-gain antenna for heat protection and a smaller antenna for data transmission at a lower rate. Data from the instruments was received last week, allowing analysis to begin.

Official statements

“Science is slow, but good things come to those who wait,” said the ESA team regarding the ongoing analysis of the comet data.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable as a visitor from interstellar space, exhibiting behaviour typical of a normal comet. The observations made by Juice will contribute to a broader understanding of the composition and dynamics of such comets.

What’s next

Teams involved in the Juice mission will continue analyzing the data collected on 3I/ATLAS. They are set to convene in late March to share their findings and insights regarding the comet.

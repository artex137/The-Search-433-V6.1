Interstellar comet 3I ATLAS glows green during lunar eclipse photo of the day for Sept. 15, 2025

On September 7, 2025, amateur astronomers Michael Jäger and Gerald Rhemann captured an image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS glowing green during a total lunar eclipse in the Kalahari Desert, Namibia. The event allowed for unprecedented observations of the comet, which is only the third confirmed interstellar comet observed in our solar system.

This discovery raises important questions about the chemistry of comets originating from outside our solar system. The unusual green hue may indicate the presence of diatomic carbon, although previous observations suggested a lack of carbon-chain molecules. Astronomers are investigating the unique properties of 3I/ATLAS as it moves through our solar system.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: September 7, 2025
  • Location: Kalahari Desert, Namibia
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS, only the third confirmed interstellar comet
  • Notable feature: Green glow observed during total lunar eclipse
  • Previous study: Kitt Peak Observatory reported comet is poor in carbon-chain molecules

Timeline

On September 7, 2025, Earth experienced a total lunar eclipse, providing dark skies for astronomers. Jäger and Rhemann utilized this opportunity to photograph 3I/ATLAS. Their images revealed a green glow, prompting further investigation into the comet's chemical composition.

In August 2025, astronomers at Kitt Peak Observatory noted that 3I/ATLAS lacked diatomic carbon, raising questions about the sudden appearance of the green hue.

Official statements

“This image is both a scientific milestone and a source of wonder,” said a spokesperson from the astronomical community, highlighting the significance of the findings.

Context

3I/ATLAS follows in the footsteps of two other notable interstellar objects: 'Oumuamua, which sparked debates about its nature, and comet 2I/Borisov, which exhibited a more typical appearance. The study of interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS provides insights into the chemical processes occurring in other star systems.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS as it travels through our solar system, seeking to understand the source of its green glow and its implications for cometary chemistry.

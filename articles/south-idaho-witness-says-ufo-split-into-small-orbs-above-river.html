South Idaho Witness Says UFO Split Into Small Orbs Above River

A resident of Melba, Idaho, has reported seeing an unidentified aerial phenomenon (UAP) above the Snake River southwest of Boise on December 11. The witness, who contacted the National UFO Reporting Center, observed the anomaly for approximately five minutes from the Dedication Point Overlook, located 33 miles southwest of Boise.

This sighting matters as it adds to the growing number of UAP reports in the region, prompting interest from both local residents and UFO enthusiasts. The National UFO Reporting Center has documented various sightings, and this report contributes to an ongoing conversation about unexplained aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: December 11
  • Location: Dedication Point Overlook, Melba, Idaho
  • Time of sighting: Approximately 6:30 P.M.
  • Duration of observation: About five minutes
  • Type of object: Described as an orange orb
  • Number of orbs observed: Split into four smaller orbs

Timeline

On the evening of December 11, the witness used a telescope to observe an orange orb from the Dedication Point Overlook. The object reportedly split into four smaller orange orbs that flew across the sky at a low level. The sighting lasted for around five minutes before the orbs disappeared from view.

Official statements

“The object was described as an 'orange orb' that was not any known flying aircraft,” the NUFORC report stated.
“The orbs flew across the sky at a low level,” according to the witness’s report.

Context

This sighting adds to a series of recent reports of unexplained flying objects in Idaho and across the United States. The National UFO Reporting Center has seen an increase in submissions, reflecting growing public interest in UAPs and their potential implications.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any planned investigations related to this sighting. Residents who may have witnessed the event are encouraged to report their observations to the National UFO Reporting Center.

‘Another distraction’: Trump’s UFO bombshell fuels Epstein conspiracies

Published 2026-02-20 15:11 UTC

Trump directs release of UFO files amid Epstein scrutiny

President Donald Trump has directed his administration to release more government files related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and unidentified flying objects (UFOs), according to a post on Truth Social on Thursday. His announcement comes as demands grow from both Democrats and Republicans for the full release of files related to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein's sex trafficking case.

Trump's directive has raised questions about whether he is attempting to distract the public from the Epstein files, in which he is reportedly mentioned over one million times. While Trump has not been accused of any wrongdoing related to Epstein, his association with the disgraced financier has been under scrutiny for years.

Key facts

  • Trump announced the directive on Truth Social on Thursday.
  • The announcement pertains to government files on aliens, UAP, and UFOs.
  • Trump has faced scrutiny over his relationship with Jeffrey Epstein.
  • Trump is mentioned over one million times in the Epstein files.
  • Former President Barack Obama recently commented on aliens, stating they are “real” but he hasn’t seen them.

Timeline

On Thursday, Trump posted on Truth Social, stating he has directed Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to begin the process of releasing files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This announcement coincides with increasing public interest in the Epstein case, where calls for transparency have intensified.

Just a week prior, Obama discussed aliens during a podcast, claiming they are real but clarifying he has not seen evidence of their contact with humanity. Social media users noted the timing of Trump’s announcement in relation to Obama’s comments, suggesting a strategic move for positive attention.

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs), and any and all other information connected to these highly complex, but extremely interesting and important, matters. GOD BLESS AMERICA!”

— Donald Trump, on Truth Social

“What about the 2 million unreleased Epstein files tho?”

Context

The scrutiny surrounding Trump’s relationship with Epstein has persisted since Epstein's arrest in 2019. This latest announcement comes amid ongoing demands for transparency regarding the Epstein files, which are believed to contain significant information about his connections to powerful individuals.

Trump's announcement follows a trend of political figures discussing UFOs and extraterrestrial life, with previous comments from Obama sparking public interest and speculation.

What’s next

The administration is expected to begin the process of identifying and releasing files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Meanwhile, calls for the release of the Epstein files are likely to continue, with both parties urging transparency.

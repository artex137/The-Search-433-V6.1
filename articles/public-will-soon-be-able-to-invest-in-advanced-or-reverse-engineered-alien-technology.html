Public will soon be able to invest in 'advanced or reverse-engineered alien technology'

An investment firm has announced plans to launch an exchange-traded fund (ETF) focused on companies it claims have access to alien technology. Tuttle Capital, based in the United States, has filed its UFO Disclosure AI Powered ETF with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and aims to capitalize on recent government disclosures about unidentified flying objects (UFOs).

This fund is significant as it represents a new avenue for investors to engage with speculative technology sectors. Tuttle Capital describes itself as "the antidote to Wall Street" and intends to leverage its proprietary investment strategy, known as HEAT—hedges, edges, asymmetry, themes—to identify and invest in companies involved in advanced technologies potentially inspired by extraterrestrial innovations.

Key facts

  • ETF filed with the SEC, name: UFO Disclosure AI Powered ETF
  • Investment strategy targets at least 80% of net assets in companies with exposure to alien technology
  • Focus areas include aerospace, defense, materials, and energy firms
  • Potential investments in companies involved in detecting unidentified anomalous phenomena
  • CEO Matthew Tuttle has commented on the power sources of UFOs
  • Investment strategy described as highly speculative

Timeline

Tuttle Capital has recently filed its UFO Disclosure AI Powered ETF with the SEC. The fund aims to invest in a range of companies that may have access to advanced or reverse-engineered alien technology. The exact companies have not yet been disclosed.

Official statements

“I’m a trader. I look at [UFOs], and I say that they’re using a power source that is light-years beyond anything that we have,” CEO Matthew Tuttle said, according to the Financial Times.

Context

This initiative follows a series of government disclosures regarding UFOs and alleged advanced technologies. Tuttle Capital's approach highlights a growing interest in the intersection of finance and speculative technology sectors. The fund's emphasis on materials and energy firms suggests a belief in the potential for breakthroughs inspired by unidentified technologies.

What’s next

Investors will be able to participate in the fund once it is officially launched. Tuttle Capital plans to monitor the market for companies that may benefit from advancements in technology inspired by UFO-related research.

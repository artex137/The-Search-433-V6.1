"Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena" at the Drawing Center in New York: Arts Intel Report

Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena at the Drawing Center in New York

The Drawing Center in New York has opened an exhibition titled "Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena," which runs from September 9, 2025, to April 11, 2026. The exhibition features works that explore the cultural and psychological dimensions of space and paranormal phenomena, including a significant piece by artist René Magritte.

This exhibition is significant as it delves into the fascination with extraterrestrial life and the surreal interpretations of space that have captivated artists for over a century. The Drawing Center aims to engage audiences with both contemporary art and historical works, providing a comprehensive exploration of how these themes influence modern imagination.

Key facts

  • Exhibition title: Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena
  • Location: The Drawing Center, New York
  • Opening date: September 9, 2025
  • Closing date: April 11, 2026
  • Featured artist: René Magritte
  • Notable work: Voice of Space (La voix des airs), 1931

Timeline

In 1913, René Magritte painted Voice of Space, depicting three silver bells in a surreal landscape. The current exhibition at The Drawing Center, which features this painting, opens on September 9, 2025, and will explore themes of UFOs and paranormal phenomena until April 11, 2026.

Throughout its run, the exhibition will include various contemporary artworks and historical pieces that examine the intersection of art and the unknown.

Official statements

“I caused the iron bells hanging from the necks of our admirable horses to sprout like dangerous plants at the edge of an abyss,” said Magritte, reflecting on the surreal themes present in his work.

Context

The fascination with life beyond Earth has been a recurring theme in art for over a century. Artists often use surreal imagery to convey complex ideas about existence and the unknown. Magritte's work, in particular, serves as a significant reference point for contemporary discussions around UFOs and paranormal phenomena.

What’s next

The Drawing Center will continue to showcase a variety of related artworks and host discussions on the themes presented in the exhibition until its conclusion in April 2026.

‘Diamond UFO’ In Belgrade? Old Video Goes Viral Again As Viewers Argue Over Mysterious Light In Sky

An old video showing a glowing diamond-shaped object in the night sky over Belgrade has gone viral again, sparking debates about UFOs and alien encounters. The clip, allegedly recorded in 2020 by a young boy and his father, resurfaced this week and has generated significant online discussion regarding the nature of the object depicted.

The renewed interest in the video highlights ongoing public fascination with unidentified flying objects. Authorities in Belgrade have not reported any radar evidence or official reports confirming unidentified aerial activity in the area during the time the video was recorded.

Key facts

  • Video recorded in Belgrade, Serbia, in 2020.
  • Clip resurfaced on social media in September 2025.
  • Footage shows a diamond-shaped red light changing into a shadow.
  • No official reports or radar evidence of UFO activity have been documented.
  • Public divided on the object's nature; opinions range from alien spacecraft to drones.
  • Related event: UFO music festival held at Atelje 212 theatre in Belgrade.

Timeline

The original video was recorded in Belgrade in 2020 by a boy and his father. This week, the footage resurfaced on social media, quickly gaining traction and sparking renewed debates. As of September 2025, thousands of comments have emerged online, with viewers expressing various interpretations of the object.

In response to the viral nature of the video, discussions have intensified regarding the potential explanations for the object, with many users providing conflicting theories.

Official statements

“Damn this is so realistic,” one user commented. Another added, “Nah, that’s just some good ol’ American Groom Lake tech.”
“The lights’ organized symmetrical shape eliminates ordinary explanations,” another commentator stated, highlighting the divided opinions among viewers.

Context

The resurgence of the video aligns with a broader trend of public interest in UFO sightings. In recent years, various videos claiming to show unidentified aerial phenomena have circulated on social media, often leading to intense discussions and speculations. The lack of concrete evidence frequently fuels debates among enthusiasts and skeptics alike.

Additionally, the recent UFO music festival at Atelje 212 theatre has contributed to the online frenzy, with some suggesting the viral video could be part of a marketing strategy for the event.

What’s next

As of now, no investigations or formal inquiries have been announced regarding the video or the object depicted. Authorities have not indicated any further action or analysis concerning the sightings in Belgrade.

NASA spots mysterious stripe linked to 3I/ATLAS

A mysterious stripe captured by NASA’s Perseverance rover on the Martian surface has drawn attention from astronomers and social media users. The image, taken on October 4, 2025, shows a slender streak that some speculate could be the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS moving across Mars’ sky. Experts, however, believe the phenomenon is likely due to the rover’s imaging process rather than evidence of a physical object.

This observation is significant as it highlights the ongoing interest in interstellar objects and their characteristics. NASA officials and researchers are closely monitoring 3I/ATLAS as it passes through the Solar System, with multiple Mars missions actively observing the object.

Key facts

  • Date: October 4, 2025
  • Time: 21:33:39 local solar time on Mars
  • Distance from Mars: Approximately 38 million kilometers
  • Camera: Right Navigation Camera (Navcam) aboard Perseverance rover
  • Length of stripe: Approx. 50,000 kilometers
  • Size limit of 3I/ATLAS: 46 kilometers in diameter
  • Relative speed: 50,000 kilometers of apparent motion during a 10-minute exposure

Timeline

The following sequence outlines the key events related to the observation of 3I/ATLAS:

  • October 3, 2025: HiRISE camera aboard the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter captures high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS.
  • October 4, 2025: Perseverance rover captures the stripe image at 21:33:39 local solar time.
  • Ongoing: 3I/ATLAS is under observation by multiple Mars missions.

Official statements

“If 3I/ATLAS were truly 50,000 kilometers long, it would have appeared much larger in the Hubble Space Telescope’s July 21 image,” said Prof. Avi Loeb, addressing the discrepancy in size.
“One way to think about the stripe is as a sequence of smeared snapshots, like a pod of green beans,” Loeb added, explaining the potential cause of the stripe.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object detected passing through our Solar System. It follows earlier discoveries such as ‘Oumuamua (1I/2017 U1) and 2I/Borisov. Previous observations have emphasized the importance of understanding the properties of such interstellar objects.

What’s next

Researchers expect the release of data from the HiRISE camera soon, which will clarify the true size and reflectivity of 3I/ATLAS and may resolve the theories surrounding the stripe observed by the Perseverance rover.

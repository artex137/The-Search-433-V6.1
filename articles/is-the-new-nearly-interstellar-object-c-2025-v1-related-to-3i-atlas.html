Is the New “Nearly Interstellar” Object C/2025 V1 Related to 3I/ATLAS?

On November 2, 2025, astronomer Gennady Borisov discovered a new “nearly interstellar” object, officially labeled C/2025 V1 (Borisov), in the inner solar system. The discovery follows the perihelion passage of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. C/2025 V1 has been catalogued by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) and the Minor Planet Center.

This discovery is significant as it raises questions about the origins and characteristics of C/2025 V1 and its potential relationship with 3I/ATLAS. Authorities, including NASA, are analyzing the orbits and physical properties of both objects to determine if there is any connection between them.

Key facts

  • Date of discovery: November 2, 2025
  • Discoverer: Gennady Borisov
  • Object designation: C/2025 V1 (Borisov)
  • Orbit inclination: 113 degrees relative to the ecliptic plane
  • Closest approach: 75 million kilometers (0.5 au) from 3I/ATLAS
  • Orbital eccentricity: 1.0095 with an uncertainty of 0.0026
  • Next significant date: December 19, 2025, when 3I/ATLAS approaches Earth

Timeline

On November 2, 2025, Gennady Borisov identified C/2025 V1 after the perihelion of 3I/ATLAS. Following this, NASA’s JPL and the Minor Planet Center catalogued the object, noting its orbital characteristics and potential origins.

As of December 19, 2025, 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Earth, providing an opportunity for further observation and analysis of both objects.

Official statements

“C/2025 V1 most likely originated from the Oort cloud at the outskirts of the Solar System,” said Borisov.
“The measured non-gravitational acceleration of 3I/ATLAS is insufficient to bridge the gap with C/2025 V1,” stated an analyst from NASA.

Context

This discovery follows the earlier identification of 3I/ATLAS, which has sparked discussions regarding its nature—whether it is a natural comet or a technological object. The ongoing analysis aims to clarify the composition and behavior of 3I/ATLAS, particularly as it approaches Earth.

What’s next

NASA and other agencies will continue to monitor both C/2025 V1 and 3I/ATLAS as they gather more data, especially during the upcoming close approach of 3I/ATLAS on December 19, 2025. Observations will focus on the physical properties and potential interactions of both objects.

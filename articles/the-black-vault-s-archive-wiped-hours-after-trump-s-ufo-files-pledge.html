The Black Vault's archive wiped hours after Trump's UFO files pledge

The Black Vault's online archive has been wiped clean just hours after former President Donald Trump promised to release previously classified UFO files on February 25, 2026. The incident has raised questions about data preservation and the access to government documents related to unidentified aerial phenomena.

The Black Vault is known for its extensive collection of declassified government documents related to UFOs. The timing of the archive's deletion, coinciding with Trump's announcement, has prompted reactions from the UFO community and concerns about the implications for transparency. Authorities are currently investigating the situation to determine the cause of the data loss.

Key facts

  • Date of archive wipe: February 25, 2026
  • Location: The Black Vault (online archive)
  • Event: Deletion of extensive UFO-related documents
  • Former President involved: Donald Trump
  • Status of investigation: Ongoing

Timeline

On February 25, 2026, former President Donald Trump announced plans to release classified UFO files. Hours later, The Black Vault reported that their entire archive had been wiped. The sudden deletion has raised concerns and prompted calls for an investigation.

As of now, no specific details have been released regarding the cause of the wipe or whether it was a result of external interference or a technical issue.

Official statements

“The timing is concerning, and we are looking into all possibilities to understand what happened,” said a spokesperson for The Black Vault.

Context

The Black Vault has been a key resource for UFO enthusiasts and researchers, housing a large collection of documents obtained through Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests. The recent pledge by Trump to release additional UFO files has heightened interest in these topics.

Prior incidents of data loss or interference in similar contexts have led to skepticism about the government's commitment to transparency regarding UFOs and related phenomena.

What’s next

Investigations into the wipe of The Black Vault's archive are ongoing. Authorities aim to determine the cause and whether any measures can be taken to recover the lost data. Further updates are expected as the situation develops.

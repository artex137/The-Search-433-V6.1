3.8 Million UFO Files Wiped Hours After Donald Trump's Alien Disclosure Order

A vast archive of declassified US government files has vanished from The Black Vault, just hours after Donald Trump ordered the Pentagon to release all documents related to aliens, unidentified aerial phenomena, and unidentified flying objects. The server wipe, which occurred on February 20, affected 3.8 million files, including CIA projects and military reports, collected over decades by researcher John Greenewald Jr.. The incident has raised questions about whether the deletion was accidental or deliberate.

This situation is significant as it highlights ongoing concerns about government transparency regarding UFOs. Authorities, including Greenewald, are investigating the circumstances surrounding the server wipe. Researchers and observers are scrutinizing the timing of the deletion, which coincided closely with Trump’s directive.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on February 20, 2023.
  • The Black Vault lost 3.8 million files due to a server wipe.
  • Files included sensitive documents from the CIA and military.
  • Greenewald has collected these documents over 30 years through Freedom of Information Act requests.
  • The hosting provider ruled out corruption, but the cause of the deletion remains unclear.
  • All deleted files were backed up and the site was restored shortly after the incident.

Timeline

On February 20, 2023, Donald Trump issued a directive via his Truth Social account, calling for the Pentagon to release records related to extraterrestrial life and UFOs. Less than 24 hours later, The Black Vault's server was wiped, resulting in the deletion of millions of files. Greenewald stated that the server maintenance appeared unusually timed and expressed concern over the deletion.

In the aftermath, Greenewald confirmed that all files were securely backed up and the site was restored quickly, alleviating fears of permanent data loss.

Official statements

“I did not fully suspect foul play but could not rule it out,” Greenewald stated regarding the timing of the server wipe.
He described the event as “very oddly timed server maintenance done by the hosting provider that went awry.”

Context

The US government has historically denied the recovery or presence of extraterrestrial life, asserting that no physical evidence exists of non-human craft. Greenewald's archive includes files about alleged UFO crash sites, including the 1947 Roswell incident, and details of secret task forces like Majestic 12, which investigated UFO sightings.

Previous disclosures, such as documents related to President Kennedy's assassination and the Jeffrey Epstein case, illustrate the challenges in obtaining transparent records. Critics have suggested that political motivations may influence UFO disclosure initiatives.

What’s next

Investigations into the server wipe are ongoing, with Greenewald and other researchers emphasizing the need for transparency in the management of declassified government data. Further scrutiny from the public and media is expected as the situation unfolds.

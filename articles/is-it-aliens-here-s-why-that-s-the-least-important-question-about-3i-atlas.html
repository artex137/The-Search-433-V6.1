Is it aliens? Here's why that's the least important question about 3I/ATLAS.

Published 2025-11-10 14:28 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS Sparks Interest Amid Alien Speculation

Comet 3I/ATLAS has reached its closest point to the sun on October 29, 2025, generating renewed interest and speculation about its origins. This interstellar object, detected by the Asteroid Terrestrial Last Alert System (ATLAS) on July 1, 2025, has been at the centre of conspiracy theories regarding extraterrestrial life.

The recent perihelion, approximately 210 million kilometers from the sun, has prompted ground telescopes to resume observations of the comet. While some have speculated about its alien origins, astronomers stress that such theories distract from important scientific findings. They point out that this comet could provide insight into the early Milky Way and the origins of our solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS detected on July 1, 2025.
  • Reached perihelion on October 29, 2025, at a distance of 210 million kilometers from the sun.
  • Comet may be over 7 billion years old, older than our solar system.
  • Observations are being conducted by ground telescopes and the European Space Agency.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected by ATLAS on July 1, 2025. Following its detection, speculation about its nature, including the possibility of extraterrestrial life, began to circulate. On October 29, 2025, the comet reached its perihelion, allowing astronomers to observe it again as it moved behind the sun.

As ground telescopes resumed observation, researchers highlighted the unique chemical composition of the comet, which provides valuable information about the conditions in the early universe. The comet's perihelion marks a significant event in its journey through the solar system.

Official statements

“When we talk about aliens first, we might miss all this amazing information,” said astronomer Carl Sagan, emphasizing the need for extraordinary evidence before jumping to conclusions about extraterrestrial life.
“Not knowing all the answers is not proof of aliens. It just means that we have work to do,” stated Laura Nicole Driessen, a radio astronomer involved in studying cosmic phenomena.

Context

The speculation surrounding 3I/ATLAS is not new; similar theories emerged during the discoveries of previous interstellar objects, including 1I/2017 U1 'Oumuamua and Comet 2I/Borisov. In these cases, astronomers expressed concerns that sensational media coverage could hinder scientific investigation.

Comet 3I/ATLAS may provide insights into the chemical composition of the early solar system, as it has been noted for its high carbon dioxide content and unique elemental ratios. This research is critical for understanding the history of our solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to observe Comet 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the solar system. The European Space Agency plans to use multiple missions, including Mars Express and the ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter, to gain further insights into this ancient celestial body.

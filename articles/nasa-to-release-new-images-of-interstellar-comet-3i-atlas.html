NASA to Release New Images of Interstellar Comet 3I/Atlas

Published 2025-11-18 10:31 UTC

NASA to Release New Images of Interstellar Comet 3I/Atlas

NASA has announced that it will release new images of the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas on 18 November 2025 at 15:00. The images show that the comet's tail is getting longer as it travels through our solar system.

The release of these images is significant for scientists and skywatchers globally because 3I/Atlas is an interstellar comet that entered our solar system from a distant star system. Interstellar comets are extremely rare, making every piece of data valuable for understanding how celestial objects form outside our solar system. NASA aims to share these images to facilitate collaboration among researchers worldwide.

Key facts

  • Event date: 18 November 2025
  • Time of release: 15:00
  • Comet: 3I/Atlas
  • Key features to be shown: comet's surface, dust and gas tail, anti-tail, movement, sunlight interaction
  • Images captured using advanced spacecraft sensors and telescopes

Timeline

NASA has continuously tracked 3I/Atlas since its entry into the solar system. The agency plans to release new images, which will provide a clearer view of the comet's unique features and behaviour.

Scientists have observed unusual changes in 3I/Atlas, including variations in brightness and dust patterns. The comet exhibits a peculiar feature known as the “anti-tail,” which points toward the Sun.

Official statements

“These pictures will help answer questions such as: What is the comet made of? Why does it behave so differently from normal comets? How did it form in another star system?” – NASA representatives

Context

Interstellar comets like 3I/Atlas are rare, with most comets originating from within our solar system. The study of such comets can provide insights into the formation of stars and planets in distant systems.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor 3I/Atlas until it becomes too distant to observe and will publish the images on its official website and scientific channels for public access.

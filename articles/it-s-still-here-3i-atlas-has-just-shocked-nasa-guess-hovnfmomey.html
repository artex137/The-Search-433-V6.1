Comet 3I/ATLAS Discovered to Still Exist

NASA has confirmed that Comet 3I/ATLAS is still present in the solar system, with observations made on October 1, 2023. The comet was previously thought to have disintegrated after its last close approach to the Sun.

This discovery is significant as it raises questions about the comet's trajectory and potential visibility from Earth. NASA scientists are currently monitoring the comet's path to gather more data on its composition and behaviour.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed on October 1, 2023.
  • NASA has confirmed its continued existence.
  • The comet was previously believed to have disintegrated.
  • Scientists are monitoring its trajectory.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 and has been closely tracked by astronomers. In early 2023, it approached the Sun, leading to speculation about its stability. On October 1, 2023, NASA confirmed that the comet remains intact.

Official statements

“The confirmation of Comet 3I/ATLAS still being here changes our understanding of its behaviour,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Prior to this observation, Comet 3I/ATLAS was thought to have broken apart during its perihelion passage. This incident is part of a larger trend of studying comets to understand their origins and impacts on the solar system.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS and provide updates as more data becomes available regarding its trajectory and potential observations from Earth.

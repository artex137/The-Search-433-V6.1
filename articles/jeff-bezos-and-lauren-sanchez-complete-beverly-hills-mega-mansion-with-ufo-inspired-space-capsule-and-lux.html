Jeff Bezos and Lauren Sanchez complete Beverly Hills mega-mansion with UFO-inspired space capsule and luxury amenities

Amazon founder Jeff Bezos and partner Lauren Sanchez have nearly finished their expansive mansion in Beverly Hills, California. The couple purchased the property from media mogul David Geffen for $165 million in 2020, adding $10 million worth of land, making it one of the most expensive real estate deals in Los Angeles history.

This mansion, set on 10 acres, features a blend of Georgian architectural style and striking modern elements, reflecting Bezos's interest in space. The estate includes a unique stainless steel structure resembling the NASA Apollo 11 spacecraft, designed by Timothy Oulton Studio, which serves as a sauna and potentially a private lounge or dining area. The property is tailored for leisure and entertainment, showcasing the couple's affinity for adventure and innovation.

Key facts

  • Location: Beverly Hills, California
  • Purchase price: $165 million
  • Additional land cost: $10 million
  • Property size: 10 acres
  • Architect: Roland Coate
  • Construction date: 1930s

Timeline

In 2020, Jeff Bezos and Lauren Sanchez acquired the Beverly Hills mansion from David Geffen for $165 million. The couple then added $10 million worth of land to the estate. Following their space flight with Blue Origin earlier this year, the couple has designed the mansion to reflect their passion for adventure.

The mansion features a unique sauna structure that resembles a spacecraft, showcasing their interests. The estate is equipped with luxurious amenities for entertainment and leisure.

Official statements

“This unique piece of work has been created by Timothy Oulton Studio and symbolizes the couple's affection for out-of-this-world adventures,” according to reports from the New York Post.

Context

The mansion is a blend of historic charm and modern luxury, having originally been designed by architect Roland Coate in the 1930s. It was previously occupied by Jack Warner, the founder of Warner Brothers. The estate represents a significant shift towards extravagant real estate transactions in Los Angeles.

What’s next

The completed mansion will serve as an exclusive living space for Bezos and Sanchez, allowing them to enjoy their shared interests in technology, adventure, and entertainment.

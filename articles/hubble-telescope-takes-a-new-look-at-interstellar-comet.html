Hubble telescope takes a new look at interstellar comet

NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope has observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on November 30, 2023, from a distance of approximately 178 million miles (286 million kilometers) from Earth.

This observation is significant as it marks the second time Hubble has tracked 3I/ATLAS since its discovery in July 2023. NASA plans to continue monitoring the comet as it travels out of the solar system, providing valuable data on its trajectory and characteristics.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of observation: November 30, 2023
  • Distance from Earth: 178 million miles (286 million kilometers)
  • Last observed: July 2023
  • NASA missions involved: Multiple

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first discovered in July 2023. Hubble conducted its initial observations shortly after its discovery. The most recent observation occurred on November 30, 2023, using Hubble’s Wide Field Camera 3.

NASA has been actively monitoring the comet through various missions since its discovery, and this trend is set to continue for several months as the comet moves out of the solar system.

Official statements

“Monitoring is expected to continue for months as 3I/ATLAS makes its way out of the solar system,” NASA officials stated.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable as one of the few interstellar objects detected passing through our solar system. Its discovery and subsequent observations contribute to the understanding of such celestial bodies and their origins.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS and analyze data from its observations as it travels further from Earth.

Scientists Find Evidence of UFOs in 1950s Observatory Photos

A team of scientists at California’s Palomar Observatory has discovered potential evidence of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), commonly referred to as UFOs, in photographs taken over 70 years ago. The findings, detailed in a research paper published on October 20, 2025, in Nature’s Scientific Reports, indicate that transient, star-like flashes appeared in images captured between 1949 and 1957, coinciding with nuclear testing sites.

This research is significant as it suggests a correlation between UAP sightings and nuclear activity during the Cold War era. The study has prompted further investigations into the nature of these phenomena and their implications for national security and scientific understanding of aerial events.

Key facts

  • Discovery made by scientists at Palomar Observatory, California.
  • Research paper published in Nature’s Scientific Reports on October 20, 2025.
  • Study analyzed photographs from 1949 to 1957, prior to the launch of the first artificial satellite.
  • Researchers identified 107,875 transient lights in the images.
  • Transients were 45% more likely to be recorded within 24 hours of nuclear testing.
  • Final sighting over a nuclear testing site occurred on March 17, 1956.

Timeline

The study has traced the following sequence of events:

  • 1949-1957: Palomar Observatory captures a series of photographs of the night sky.
  • October 1957: Launch of the first artificial satellite.
  • July 19-27, 1952: Multiple UAP sightings reported above Washington, D.C., coinciding with bright transients in the observatory's images.
  • March 17, 1956: Last documented sighting of transient objects over a nuclear testing site, one day after the "Joe 21" test by Soviet Russia.

Official statements

“Our findings provide additional empirical support for the validity of the UAP phenomenon and its potential connection to nuclear weapons activity, contributing data beyond eyewitness reports,” the study's authors stated.

Context

This study adds to the body of research on UAPs, particularly regarding their potential links to military activities. Previous incidents have included reports of UAPs near nuclear facilities, raising concerns about national security and the implications of such sightings.

What’s next

Authorities and researchers are expected to continue investigating the connections between UAP sightings and nuclear activity, with a focus on gathering more data to support or refute these findings.

Who is Avi Loeb: Harvard astronomer claims interstellar object 3I/ATLAS could be an alien spaceship

Avi Loeb, a Harvard University astrophysicist, has proposed that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, discovered on July 1, 2025, may be a technological artifact from an alien civilization. Loeb's claims have stirred significant interest in the scientific community, although mainstream astronomers remain skeptical.

This discussion is important as it challenges conventional views on interstellar objects and prompts further investigation into 3I/ATLAS. Notably, NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA) have largely dismissed the notion that the object is of artificial origin, maintaining that its characteristics align with those of natural comets.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of discovery: July 1, 2025
  • Distance from the Sun at discovery: 675 million kilometres
  • Water emission: 40 kilograms per second from 8% of its surface
  • Closest approach: 1.8 astronomical units from Earth
  • Previous interstellar objects: ‘Oumuamua (2017), Borisov (2019)

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, 3I/ATLAS was discovered by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS). Initial observations noted its hyperbolic orbit and unusual characteristics, prompting speculation from Avi Loeb regarding its potential artificial origins.

Following its discovery, multiple observatories, including NASA’s Swift Observatory, confirmed that 3I/ATLAS emits water vapour at a rate not typical for comets, further igniting discussions about its nature.

Official statements

“While entertaining, the alien spacecraft hypothesis is far less probable than conventional cometary origins,” stated representatives from SETI and other research institutions.
Loeb has framed his hypothesis as a "black swan" scenario, encouraging observational follow-ups to explore the potential for artificial processes.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object to enter our solar system, following ‘Oumuamua and Borisov. The study of such objects provides critical insights into the formation and evolution of planetary systems beyond our own. Despite its peculiar features, 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth.

What’s next

Ongoing research will continue to focus on the trajectory, composition, and activity of 3I/ATLAS. Scientists aim to better understand interstellar objects and their implications for our knowledge of the universe.

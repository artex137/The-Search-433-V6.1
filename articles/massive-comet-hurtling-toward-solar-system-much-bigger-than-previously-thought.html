Massive comet hurtling toward solar system, much bigger than previously thought

A comet named 3I/ATLAS is approaching the inner solar system and is much larger than previously estimated, weighing more than 33 billion tons, according to Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb. Loeb shared his findings in a blog post on Thursday, following an analysis of the comet's trajectory using data from the Webb Space Telescope.

This discovery is significant as it marks only the third time an interstellar object has been recorded entering the inner solar system. Authorities, including NASA, have stated that 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth, although its path will bring it near Jupiter, Mars, and Venus.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS weighs over 33 billion tons.
  • Its nuclear diameter is likely more than 5 kilometers.
  • The comet originated from interstellar space and was first detected in July.
  • Data was collected by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) in Rio Hurtado, Chile.
  • 3I/ATLAS will come within 1.67 million miles of Mars' orbit on October 3.
  • The comet is primarily composed of carbon dioxide and water ice.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2023 by ATLAS, a NASA-funded early warning system.

On August 6, 2023, data from the Webb Space Telescope was used to analyze the comet's trajectory and mass.

The comet is expected to pass close to the sun after September 2023, becoming invisible, but it will reappear on the other side in early December.

Official statements

“The comet poses no threat to Earth,” said Avi Loeb in his blog post.
“Its unusual trajectory will likely bring it close to Jupiter, Mars, and Venus,” Loeb added.

Context

This is only the third recorded instance of an interstellar object entering the inner solar system. The first was 1I/'Oumuamua, detected in 2017, followed by 2I/Borisov in 2019. These discoveries have sparked interest in the study of interstellar objects and their origins.

What’s next

After passing close to the sun, 3I/ATLAS is expected to be monitored as it re-emerges in early December 2023. Further studies and observations will continue as it approaches other planets in the solar system.

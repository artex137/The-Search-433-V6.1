President orders opening of UFO files and receives support from Harvard scientist for analysis

The President has ordered the release of previously classified UFO files, receiving support from a Harvard scientist for further analysis, as officials aim to enhance transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. This decision was announced during a press briefing at the White House on February 25, 2026.

This move is significant as it reflects growing public interest and concern over UFO sightings and potential extraterrestrial encounters. Authorities are now working to declassify documents and engage experts in analyzing the information to provide clearer insights into these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: February 25, 2026
  • Location: White House, Washington, D.C.
  • Key support: Harvard scientist (name not disclosed)
  • Objective: Increase transparency regarding UFO sightings
  • Agencies involved: not disclosed

Timeline

On February 25, 2026, the President announced the plan to open UFO files during a press briefing. The initiative aims to provide more information to the public about unidentified aerial phenomena and to enlist the expertise of academic professionals, including a notable scientist from Harvard.

Following the announcement, the administration has commenced the process of reviewing and declassifying relevant documents. The timeline for the release of these materials is not disclosed.

Official statements

The President stated, “This administration is committed to transparency and will take necessary steps to ensure that the public is informed about UFO sightings and related phenomena.”
The Harvard scientist said, “Analyzing these files will allow us to approach the subject of UFOs with a scientific lens, fostering a better understanding of what we are observing.”

Context

The recent decision comes amid a series of high-profile UFO sightings reported by military personnel and civilians alike. In recent years, there has been an increasing push for the government to disclose information regarding these encounters, reflecting a broader trend towards openness in governmental operations.

Previous policies have often kept UFO sightings classified, contributing to public skepticism and speculation about the nature of these phenomena.

What’s next

The administration will continue to work on declassifying the UFO files and is expected to provide updates on the progress of their analysis. Further details about the involvement of the Harvard scientist and the timeline for the release of information will be shared in future briefings.

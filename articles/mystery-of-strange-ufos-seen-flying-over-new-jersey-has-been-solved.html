Mystery Of Strange 'UFOs' Seen Flying Over New Jersey Has Been Solved

A private aerial contractor has taken responsibility for the drone sightings that caused panic over New Jersey in November 2024. The company revealed its involvement during a panel at the Army’s UAS and Launched Effects Summit at Fort Rucker, Alabama, in August 2025, according to an attendee who spoke with The New York Post.

This development is significant as it clarifies the source of the mysterious sightings that had prompted concerns among residents and sparked various theories about unidentified flying objects (UFOs). Authorities had previously suggested that the drones might be operated by hobbyists or recreational pilots, but the contractor's admission has provided a definitive explanation.

Key facts

  • Incident date: November 13, 2024
  • Location: Picatinny Arsenal Army base, Morris County, New Jersey
  • Duration of sightings: November 2024 to mid-December 2024
  • Aircraft type: 20-foot manned aerial craft
  • Company: Not disclosed
  • Demonstration: Conducted at Army's UAS and Launched Effects Summit, August 2025

Timeline

The mystery began on November 13, 2024, when residents reported seeing hundreds of drones over the Picatinny Arsenal Army base. The sightings persisted until mid-December, leading to increased public concern and speculation.

In August 2025, during a panel at the Army’s UAS and Launched Effects Summit, the private aerial contractor acknowledged their craft's role in the sightings. They demonstrated the aircraft, showcasing its flight capabilities that could be mistaken for UFOs.

Official statements

The company’s demonstration highlighted how the craft's movements could have been mistaken for unidentified flying objects, leading to the widespread reports of mysterious drones.

Context

Prior to this admission, the Federal Aviation Administration had suggested that the drone sightings were likely from hobbyists. The contractor's acknowledgment shifts the understanding of the incident, indicating that the operations were part of a government contract and not publicly disclosed.

What’s next

Investigations into the drone sightings have concluded with the contractor's admission, but public interest in the incident remains high. Future operations involving the aerial craft may still be conducted under government contracts, though details remain undisclosed.

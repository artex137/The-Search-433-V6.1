Bengaluru’s bright lights could blank out 3I/ATLAS comet tomorrow

BENGALURU: The 3I/ATLAS comet is set to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, but light pollution in Bengaluru may hinder visibility of the celestial event. Centres for science and astronomy in the city are currently evaluating their options for public viewing sessions.

This comet, which has captivated observers since its discovery in July 2025, is only the third interstellar object detected in the Solar System. Its unpredictable behaviour has sparked interest beyond the scientific community. However, light pollution in Bengaluru, one of the brightest cities in India, poses a significant challenge to viewing the comet, even on a moonless night with potentially clear winter skies.

Key facts

  • Event: 3I/ATLAS comet closest approach
  • Date: December 19, 2025
  • Location: Bengaluru
  • Current status: Viewing sessions under consideration
  • Possible viewing locations: Rural outskirts of Bengaluru
  • Trial sessions: Scheduled for December 18, 2025

Timeline

Since its discovery in July 2025, the 3I/ATLAS comet has generated significant interest due to its unique characteristics. As the comet approaches Earth, various scientific institutions in Bengaluru have begun preparations for potential public viewing. On December 18, 2025, both the Visvesvaraya Industrial & Technological Museum (VITM) and Jawaharlal Nehru Planetarium (JNP) are set to conduct trials to assess visibility conditions.

Official statements

“Those interested should plan accordingly and try viewing the phenomenon from the rural outskirts of the city,” said Cyril Babu, curator at VITM.

Context

Bengaluru, known for its vibrant urban landscape, faces challenges from light pollution, which can obscure astronomical events. The city’s bright lights may diminish the visibility of the comet, similar to how cabin lights can hinder night driving. The anticipation surrounding the 3I/ATLAS comet has attracted attention from both amateur and professional astronomers.

What’s next

Following the trials on December 18, VITM and JNP will decide whether to proceed with public viewing sessions for the 3I/ATLAS comet.

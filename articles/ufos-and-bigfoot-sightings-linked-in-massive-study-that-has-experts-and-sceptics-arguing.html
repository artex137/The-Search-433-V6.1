UFOs and Bigfoot Sightings Linked in Massive Study

A new analysis has linked hundreds of Bigfoot and UFO sightings in a study conducted by the Sentient Orbs project. Researchers claim that these sightings occurred within days and short distances of each other, raising questions about potential connections between the two phenomena. The analysis spans decades and is based on data compiled from over 4,000 Bigfoot reports and more than 130,000 UFO sightings.

This study matters as it has sparked renewed debate about unexplained encounters. Authorities and researchers are now examining the data to understand whether these sightings correlate due to coincidence or if they indicate a deeper connection. The findings have been made available as an interactive tool for further exploration.

Key facts

  • 944 cases showed correlation between Bigfoot and UFO sightings.
  • 10 incidents occurred on the same day and roughly at the same time.
  • 485 incidents took place within one day of each other.
  • Data runs through 2020.
  • The analysis combined data from BFRO and NUFORC.
  • Each Bigfoot report served as an anchor event for nearby UFO sightings.

Timeline

Researchers analysed Bigfoot sightings as anchor events, searching for UFO sightings within a 100-kilometre radius and a 72-hour window. The analysis led to the identification of 944 correlation cases.

For example, in October 2013, a motorist in Hamilton County, Ohio, reported a possible Bigfoot sighting near the Little Miami River. This occurred on the same day as at least 15 nearby UFO sightings.

In another instance from August 2006, a forest worker near Mount Rainier in Washington State reported a strange encounter, followed by six UFO sightings within three days.

Official statements

“Despite the eye-catching numbers, this analysis is exploratory and does not claim any proven connection,” researchers stated.
“Temporal and geographic proximity does not prove one phenomenon causes the other,” they added.

Context

Previous studies have examined UFO and Bigfoot sightings, but none have combined such extensive datasets. The current analysis raises questions about potential geographic hotspots and environmental influences on sightings.

Critics have noted that data accuracy remains a challenge, with one correlation removed due to an error in the Bigfoot dataset.

What’s next

Researchers plan to conduct further investigations into the data, including statistical significance testing, to determine the validity of the correlations. The study has reignited public interest in these unexplained phenomena.

Trump Orders Release of UFO Files — Scientists Weigh In on What We Might Actually Learn About Aliens, UAPs and the Search for Life Beyond Earth

Published 2026-02-24 11:09 UTC

Trump Orders Release of UFO Files

President Donald Trump has ordered the release of government files related to UFOs and unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs) as of last week. The directive aims to shed light on what the U.S. government knows about potential extraterrestrial encounters, sparking renewed public interest and prompting scientific analysis about the implications of these disclosures.

This order marks a significant moment in the ongoing conversation about UAPs, which have been a topic of speculation and intrigue for decades. Authorities are now preparing to release information that may clarify the government's understanding of these phenomena and their implications for national security and scientific inquiry.

Key facts

  • Order issued by President Donald Trump last week.
  • Files pertain to UFOs and UAPs, including materials related to alien and extraterrestrial life.
  • Sean Kirkpatrick is the director of the Pentagon’s All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO).
  • Kirkpatrick has led investigations into unexplained aerial incidents for over a year.

Timeline

In recent years, the U.S. government has faced increasing pressure to disclose information regarding UAPs. The establishment of the AARO and the public release of preliminary reports have set the stage for Trump's recent directive. This latest order aims to provide a clearer understanding of UAPs, potentially answering questions that have lingered for decades.

Official statements

“This directive could pull back the curtain on what the U.S. government knows about UFOs,” said an unnamed government official.
“We hope to provide the public with a clearer understanding of these phenomena,” stated Sean Kirkpatrick, emphasizing the importance of transparency in the investigation.

Context

The discussion surrounding UFOs and UAPs has gained traction in recent years, particularly following the establishment of the AARO. Previous reports have revealed military sightings and sensor data, but the release of comprehensive files may offer new insights into these unexplained events. The ongoing interest in extraterrestrial life continues to fuel public curiosity and scientific debate.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to compile and release the requested files in the coming months. Investigations by the AARO will continue, aiming to provide further clarity and understanding of UAPs and their implications for national security and public knowledge.

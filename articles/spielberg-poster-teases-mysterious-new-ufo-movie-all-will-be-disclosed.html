Spielberg Poster Teases Mysterious New UFO Movie: “All Will Be Disclosed”

Steven Spielberg's untitled UFO movie has begun teasing audiences with billboards in Los Angeles and New York City, featuring the caption “ALL WILL BE DISCLOSED 06.12.2026.” The film is set to release on June 12, 2026, through Universal Pictures. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to the film's promotion.

This new project matters as it signals a return to Spielberg's iconic themes surrounding UFOs, which have captivated audiences for decades. The first trailer for the film will debut alongside showings of “Avatar: Fire and Ash.” Authorities at Universal Pictures recently unveiled footage during a special event, hinting at a darker tone compared to earlier works like “E.T.” and “Close Encounters of the Third Kind.”

Key facts

  • Film title: Untitled UFO movie
  • Release date: June 12, 2026
  • First trailer: Attached to “Avatar: Fire and Ash”
  • Billboards location: Los Angeles and New York City
  • Notable cast: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo, Wyatt Russell
  • Cinematographer: Janusz Kamiński

Timeline

The promotional campaign for Spielberg's new UFO film has included the appearance of billboards in major cities as of December 2025. Footage was recently shown at a special event at Universal Pictures, where a new “Spielberg” theater was dedicated.

During this event, action sequences were previewed, including chase scenes and a dramatic car-train crash escape featuring Emily Blunt’s character. Writer David Koepp has indicated that the film will return to the “old school” roots of Spielberg’s earlier UFO-centered works.

Official statements

“There’s real mystery surrounding this new Spielberg,” said writer David Koepp, hinting at a return to the filmmaker's classic themes.

Context

Spielberg has a long history of exploring UFO themes in his films. His previous works, such as “E.T.” and “Close Encounters of the Third Kind,” have left a lasting impact on the genre. This new project appears to build on that legacy, potentially attracting both new audiences and longtime fans.

What’s next

Fans can expect the first trailer to be released with “Avatar: Fire and Ash,” while further promotional materials are likely to emerge leading up to the film's June 2026 release.

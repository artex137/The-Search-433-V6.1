Nasa detects organic molecules linked to pre-biological chemistry on interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

A NASA space observatory has detected signals associated with organic molecules on interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during its passage through the Solar System. The comet was discovered on July 1, 2025, and data was collected by the SPHEREx telescope between December 8 and 15, 2025.

This detection is significant as it provides insights into the chemical building blocks that may precede the formation of life. According to NASA, the findings could enhance understanding of organic matter distribution across the universe and the processes leading to the emergence of life.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS discovered on July 1, 2025.
  • Data collected by SPHEREx from December 8 to 15, 2025.
  • Detected organic molecules include methanol, hydrogen cyanide, and methane.
  • SPHEREx launched in March 2025.
  • 3I/ATLAS was on an exit trajectory from the Solar System during observations.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, NASA identified interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The comet, forming outside the Solar System, travels through space before entering planetary systems. Between December 8 and 15, 2025, SPHEREx collected data from the comet as it approached the Sun, leading to the detection of organic molecules.

The heating from the Sun created an active coma around the comet, releasing gas and dust that contained significant organic compounds. These findings suggest that such compounds may be common in the universe and can play a role in pre-biological processes.

Official statements

“The presence of these compounds reinforces the hypothesis that complex organic matter may be widely distributed throughout the universe,” said a NASA representative.

Context

Interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS provide unique opportunities for scientists to study the primordial chemical composition of celestial objects formed around other stars. This study enhances the understanding of how organic compounds circulate between star systems and their potential role in the origins of life.

What’s next

Further analysis of the collected data is expected to continue, with scientists aiming to deepen their understanding of the processes that may lead to the emergence of life in the universe.

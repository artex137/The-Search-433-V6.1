New UFO Movie Breaks Prime Video Record Set by Oscar Winner

A documentary film on Prime Video, titled The Age of Disclosure, has set a new record for the highest-grossing documentary on the streaming service since its release. The film, produced by Dan Farah, explores the potential existence of Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena and alleged cover-ups by the United States government. It debuted on Prime Video on an undisclosed date.

This record-breaking achievement highlights the growing public interest in UFO-related topics and the film's unique perspective on government transparency. The producers stated that it has surpassed the previous record held by the Oscar-winning documentary Free Solo. Authorities within the film industry are taking note of this trend, as documentaries continue to gain traction on streaming platforms.

Key facts

  • Film title: The Age of Disclosure
  • Producer: Dan Farah
  • Streaming platform: Prime Video
  • Record broken: Highest-grossing documentary
  • Previous record holder: Free Solo
  • Debut rankings: #1 in purchase and #2 in rental on Prime Video
  • Duration in top ten: Two weeks
  • Notable competitors: Warner Bros. One Battle After Another, Weapons, The Conjuring Last Rights, Universal’s Jurassic World Rebirth, Disney’s Tron, and Paramount’s Mission Impossible: The Final Reckoning

Timeline

The Age of Disclosure was released on Prime Video on an undisclosed date. Following its debut, the film quickly rose to the top of the charts, achieving #1 in purchases and #2 in rentals within its first eight days. It has maintained a position in the top ten of Prime Video’s New Releases Best Sellers chart for two weeks.

According to the producers, the documentary features testimony from 34 insiders from the U.S. government, military, and intelligence communities, revealing an alleged 80-year cover-up regarding non-human intelligent life and advanced technology.

Official statements

“The documentary debuted at #1 and #2 (purchase and rental respectively) on Prime Video’s chart of Best Selling Movies in all genres, where it remained for the first 8 days of its release,” the producers stated. “It outperformed major studio titles such as Warner Bros. One Battle After Another and Weapons.”

Context

The interest in UFOs and related phenomena has surged in recent years, with numerous documentaries and media reports exploring the subject. This trend reflects a broader societal curiosity about government transparency and the unknown. The Age of Disclosure contributes to this discourse by presenting insider accounts that challenge conventional narratives about extraterrestrial life and technology.

What’s next

As The Age of Disclosure continues to gain popularity, industry analysts will likely monitor its performance and its impact on future documentary releases. No specific plans for follow-up projects or sequels have been disclosed at this time.

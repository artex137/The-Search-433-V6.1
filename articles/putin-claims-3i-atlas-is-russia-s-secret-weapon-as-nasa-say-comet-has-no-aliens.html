Putin claims 3I/ATLAS is 'Russia's secret weapon' as NASA says comet has 'no aliens'

Russian President Vladimir Putin has claimed that 3I/ATLAS, a celestial object approaching Earth, is "Russia's secret weapon" during a press conference on December 19 at the World Trade Centre in Moscow. The object, which has been observed in our solar system over the past year, made its closest approach to Earth last week.

This announcement has raised eyebrows, particularly as NASA has stated that 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth, describing it as a comet. The implications of Putin's remarks come amid rising tensions in space policy and exploration, with various experts weighing in on the nature and origin of 3I/ATLAS.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS approached Earth on December 19, 2023, at approximately 6 a.m. (UK time).
  • The object traveled at around 153,000 mph, roughly 210 million km from Earth.
  • Putin's press conference was attended by over 1,300 journalists.
  • NASA has described 3I/ATLAS as the third confirmed object from outside our solar system.
  • The object is expected to exit our solar system by early 2026.

Timeline

October-November 2023: 3I/ATLAS disappears behind the Sun and is not observable.

December 2023: The object reappears and approaches Earth, prompting renewed interest from astrophysicists.

December 19, 2023: Putin makes his controversial remark at a press conference, later clarifying that the object does not pose a threat.

Official statements

“I will let you in on something, but it must stay between us because it's classified information. This is our secret weapon, but we will only use it as a last resort,” Putin said, drawing laughter from attendees.
“It's a comet, and it doesn't pose a threat to Earth,” he added.
NASA scientist Tom Statler stated, “It looks like a comet. It does comet things. It very, very strongly resembles, in just about every way, the comets that we know.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable for being the third confirmed interstellar object to enter our solar system, following 'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. The object has sparked debate among scientists regarding its composition and potential for containing alien life, with some experts, like controversial astrophysicist Avi Loeb, suggesting it could hold mysteries yet to be uncovered.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its trajectory, scientists will monitor its path and study its properties. NASA maintains that all evidence points towards it being a natural comet, while discussions around its implications for space policy and exploration are likely to continue.

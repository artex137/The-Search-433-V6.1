More UFO files? Why a Winnipeg-based expert is skeptical of Trump’s latest remarks

A Winnipeg resident and UFO researcher has raised concerns over U.S. President Donald Trump’s announcement to release government records related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and extraterrestrial life. On Thursday, Trump stated on Truth Social that he will instruct government departments to “begin the process of identifying and releasing” files on the subject. Chris Rutkowski, who has studied UFOs for decades, expressed skepticism about the clarity and implications of Trump’s statement.

This announcement comes in the context of increased public interest in UFOs, particularly following former U.S. President Barack Obama’s recent comments affirming the existence of unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities have not disclosed a timeline for when any new files might be released, leaving many questions unanswered.

Key facts

  • Trump announced plans to release UFO-related files on Truth Social on Thursday.
  • Chris Rutkowski has researched UFOs since the 1970s and is the founder of the Canadian UFO Survey.
  • The Canadian UFO Survey has catalogued over 25,000 UFO reports since its inception in 1989.
  • In 2024, 1,008 UFO reports were received, with 3.77% classified as “unexplained.”
  • Manitoba has a history of UFO sightings dating back to 1792, including a notable case in 1967.

Timeline

On Thursday, October 19, 2023, President Trump announced his intention to direct government agencies to release UFO-related documents. This statement follows former President Obama's comments about the existence of aliens, made during a podcast interview.

Rutkowski noted that the Pentagon has previously released many UFO documents, but there has been no mention of classified files in Trump’s announcement. The lack of a timeline for the release of new files has left questions regarding the potential content and significance of these documents.

Official statements

“It’s an interesting statement from President Trump, because it actually doesn’t say much about what’s going on,” said Rutkowski. “It doesn’t say anything about classified files, it doesn’t say anything about secret files, or compartmentalized files.”
“Only a small fraction of those are unexplained or not easy to explain,” Rutkowski stated regarding the Canadian UFO Survey findings.

Context

UFO sightings have been reported in Canada since the late 18th century, with Manitoba being a prominent location for such events. Rutkowski highlighted a historical sighting in 1792 by explorer David Thompson, who described seeing lights in the sky. The 1967 incident involving Stephen Michalak is one of the most famous cases, where he reported being burned after encountering a craft.

Despite the ongoing interest and investigations, Rutkowski emphasized the absence of concrete evidence supporting the existence of extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

Investigations into UFO sightings will continue, with Rutkowski and his team at the Canadian UFO Survey gathering data from various sources. The public awaits further clarity on Trump’s announcement and any forthcoming documents related to UFOs.

Physicists Discover Stellar Message Encased in a Cosmic Bottle

Physicists have detected a stellar message from the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, observed by NASA’s Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory in July and August 2025. This marks a significant breakthrough in understanding the complex chemistry of distant planetary systems.

The discovery of hydroxyl (OH) gas from 3I/ATLAS is the first detection of this water-related compound from an interstellar object. Researchers from Auburn University have emphasized the importance of this finding, which could reshape our knowledge of planetary chemistry beyond our solar system.

Key facts

  • Observation Dates: July and August 2025
  • Water Loss Rate: Approximately 40 kilograms per second
  • Distance from the Sun: Nearly three times farther than Earth
  • Number of Known Interstellar Comets: Three
  • Published Findings: The Astrophysical Journal Letters

Timeline

In July and August 2025, NASA’s Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. During these observations, scientists from Auburn University detected hydroxyl gas emanating from the comet, marking a significant milestone in the study of interstellar chemistry.

This ongoing research allows scientists to compare 3I/ATLAS to solar system comets, revealing notable water activity despite its distance from the Sun.

Official statements

“Each discovery from interstellar comets reshapes the knowledge of planetary chemistry beyond our solar system,” said astrophysicist Dennis Bodewits of Auburn University.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third known interstellar comet, following ‘Oumuamua, which was dry, and Borisov, which was rich in carbon monoxide. Each interstellar comet studied has shown unique chemical signatures, highlighting the diversity of planetary formation conditions in different star systems.

What’s next

Ongoing observations of 3I/ATLAS will continue as it becomes observable again after mid-November 2025. This will provide further opportunities to track changes in its activity as it approaches the Sun.

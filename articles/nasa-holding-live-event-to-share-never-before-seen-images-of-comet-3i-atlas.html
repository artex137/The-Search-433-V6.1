NASA Holding Live Event to Share Never-before-seen Images of Comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA will hold a live event on Wednesday, November 19, at 3 p.m. EST to share images of Comet 3I/ATLAS at its Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland. The event will present imagery collected by various NASA missions.

This event matters as Comet 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object that has entered our solar system. Discovered on July 1 by the NASA-funded ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System) observatory, it is only the third object identified from outside the solar system. NASA has stated that the comet poses no threat to Earth.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 19, 2025
  • Event time: 3 p.m. EST
  • Location: NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt, Maryland
  • Comet discovery date: July 1, 2025
  • Closest approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Closest approach to Mars: 19 million miles in early October

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS observatory. In early October, the comet flew within 19 million miles of Mars. NASA has stated that the comet will not approach Earth any closer than 170 million miles.

Official statements

“Comet 3I/ATLAS, discovered by the NASA-funded ATLAS observatory, is only the third object ever identified as entering our solar system from elsewhere in the galaxy,” NASA said.

Context

Prior to Comet 3I/ATLAS, only two other interstellar objects have been identified entering the solar system. These discoveries have advanced our understanding of celestial bodies and their origins.

What’s next

The live event will air on NASA+, the NASA app, the agency’s website, YouTube channel, and Amazon Prime, allowing the public to view the never-before-seen images of the comet.

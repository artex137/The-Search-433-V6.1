Why Are Interstellar Comets So Weird? Part 1: The Strangers Blowing Through Town

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been identified as the third known interstellar object to pass through the solar system, first spotted on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System in Rí­o Hurtado, Chile. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to its discovery.

This discovery is significant as it adds to the understanding of interstellar comets and their characteristics. Astronomers are analyzing the comet to determine how it compares to previous interstellar visitors, including 'Oumuamua and Borisov. These findings could provide insights into the nature of such objects and their origins.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025.
  • The discovery occurred in Rí­o Hurtado, Chile.
  • It made its closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025.
  • The comet is only a few hundred meters wide, smaller than a football field.
  • It was identified by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS).

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers from the ATLAS survey detected Comet 3I/ATLAS in Rí­o Hurtado, Chile. After its discovery, they reviewed archival data from the Zwicky Transient Facility and found earlier images of the comet, indicating it had passed by unnoticed a few days prior. The comet made its closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025, passing just inside the orbit of Mars.

Official statements

“ATLAS is weird again. Like a stranger blasting through town in an exotic car, it was just plain weird,” said Paul Sutter, a cosmologist and NASA advisor.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, the first known interstellar object was 'Oumuamua, detected in 2017. It was described as elongated and unusual. The second visitor, Borisov, was spotted in 2019 and was more typical in appearance. The comparison between these comets raises questions about the characteristics of interstellar objects.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS to understand its composition and trajectory, as well as to determine how it fits into the broader context of interstellar objects.

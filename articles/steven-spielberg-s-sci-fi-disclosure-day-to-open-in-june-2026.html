Steven Spielberg’s sci-fi ‘Disclosure Day’ to open in June 2026

Universal Pictures has announced that Steven Spielberg’s latest UFO-themed film, titled Disclosure Day, will premiere on June 12, 2026. The film stars Emily Blunt and Josh O’Connor, who navigate a world where extraordinary truths are being unveiled, accompanied by unusual animal behaviour and other strange occurrences.

This film is significant as it marks Spielberg's return to the science fiction genre, a space he has shaped with classics like Close Encounters Of The Third Kind and E.T. The Extra-Terrestrial. The release is part of a broader trend in entertainment that reflects growing public interest in UFO phenomena.

Key facts

  • Title: Disclosure Day
  • Release Date: June 12, 2026
  • Cast: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo
  • Screenplay: David Koepp
  • Producers: Kristie Macosko Krieger, Steven Spielberg
  • Executive Producers: Adam Somner, Chris Brigham

Timeline

Universal Pictures revealed the film's title on an undisclosed date. The studio also released a trailer that hints at the film's plot and themes.

Official statements

“Disclosure Day is a thrilling exploration of the unknown, showcasing Spielberg’s masterful storytelling,” said a Universal Pictures spokesperson.

Context

Steven Spielberg has been a pivotal figure in the sci-fi genre since the late 1970s, with multiple successful films that explore themes of extraterrestrial life and human experience. Recent years have seen a resurgence in public interest in UFOs, influenced by government disclosures and media coverage.

What’s next

As the release date approaches, further promotional materials and possibly additional casting announcements are expected from Universal Pictures.

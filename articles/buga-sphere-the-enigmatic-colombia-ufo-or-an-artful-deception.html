Buga Sphere: The Enigmatic Colombia UFO Or An Artful Deception?

On 2 March 2025, residents of Buga, Colombia, reported witnessing a spherical unidentified object that silently moved over the city before landing in a nearby field. Eyewitnesses described the object’s flight as chaotic, noting zigzagging movements and abrupt changes in altitude, with some stating it emitted multicolored lights. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with the incident.

This event has sparked widespread interest and debate regarding the nature of the object, with authorities and researchers examining the implications of its existence. The Buga Sphere has transitioned from a local phenomenon to a global discussion, with many speculating about its origin and purpose.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: 2 March 2025
  • Location: Buga, Colombia
  • Initial discoverer: José Arias
  • Subsequent handler: David Vélez, treasure hunter and blogger
  • Current custodian: Jaime Maussan, Mexican journalist and ufologist
  • Weight of object: approximately 2 kg
  • Material: titanium-based alloy with unknown elements

Timeline

On 2 March 2025, residents of Buga observed the spherical UFO before it landed in a nearby field. Local resident José Arias found the object and handed it over to David Vélez. Vélez then enlisted radiology specialist José Luis Velásquez to conduct an X-ray scan of the object. The sphere was later transported to Mexico by Jaime Maussan, where it gained international attention.

Context

The Buga Sphere incident aligns with a growing trend of UFO sightings and public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). Previous incidents have often sparked debates about their origins, with both believers and skeptics presenting their cases. The Buga Sphere has become a focal point for discussions around advanced technology, belief systems, and potential extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

Investigations into the Buga Sphere are ongoing, with researchers and skeptics continuing to analyze the object and its implications. No formal charges or legal actions have been reported at this time.

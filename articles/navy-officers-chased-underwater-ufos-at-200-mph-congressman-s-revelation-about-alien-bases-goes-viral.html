Navy Officers 'Chased Underwater UFOs' at 200 mph: Congressman’s Revelation About Alien Bases Goes Viral

Rep. Tim Burchett has claimed that Navy personnel have reported encounters with unidentified underwater crafts moving at extraordinary speeds, potentially indicating the existence of alien bases beneath the ocean. The congressman, representing Tennessee's 2nd congressional district, made these statements during an interview with Tucker Carlson.

Burchett's claims have raised significant questions about the U.S. government's knowledge of unidentified submerged objects (USOs) and their implications for national security. As a member of the House Oversight Committee investigating these phenomena, Burchett has called for more transparency regarding the findings of government agencies.

Key facts

  • Rep. Tim Burchett reported encounters with unidentified underwater crafts.
  • Crafts are said to move at speeds exceeding 320 km/h.
  • Burchett mentioned five or six potential underwater alien bases off the U.S. coastline.
  • Claims are based on eyewitness accounts from Navy officers.
  • Burchett serves on the House Oversight Committee examining reports of USOs.

Timeline

In a recent interview, Rep. Tim Burchett disclosed that Navy officers have reported sightings of unidentified crafts beneath the ocean. He referenced a high-ranking Navy member who confirmed these sightings.

Burchett has emphasized the need for further investigation into these claims, noting that the committee's inquiries have raised more questions than answers about the mysterious objects.

Official statements

“We have a very high-ranking member of the Navy telling me that we have sightings of these underwater crafts that they are chasing,” Burchett said. “The underwater crafts - they're chasing are doing hundreds of miles an hour, and the best we've got is something that does maybe just a little bit under 40 miles an hour.”

Context

Burchett’s statements come amid ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial and underwater phenomena, with many experts calling for more transparency from governmental agencies. Skepticism remains regarding the lack of verifiable evidence linking these claims to actual alien bases.

Concerns have been raised about the implications of advanced crafts on national security, especially if they can move at speeds far beyond current military technology.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee is expected to continue its investigation into the reported sightings and claims of USOs, with hopes of uncovering more evidence and providing clarity on the matter. Further revelations may emerge in the coming weeks as inquiries progress.

UFO festival on Thailand tourism calendar in 2026

The Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT) has announced a UFO festival scheduled for February 2026 at Khun Dan Prakarnchon Dam in Nakhon Nayok province, aiming to attract both domestic and international visitors. This event is part of a broader strategy to increase international arrivals by at least 10% after disappointing 2025 numbers.

TAT governor Thapanee Kiatphaibool emphasized the need to rebuild trust with tourists through safety measures and memorable experiences. The agency has also initiated the “Hat Yai Rise Up” project to revitalize areas affected by severe flooding last month, with caravan tours from Singapore and Malaysia planned to visit Hat Yai for the first time since the incident.

Key facts

  • UFO festival scheduled for February 2026.
  • Location: Khun Dan Prakarnchon Dam, Nakhon Nayok province.
  • TAT has set a target of 36.7 million foreign arrivals for 2026.
  • Expected revenue for 2026: 2.78 trillion baht.
  • Projected occupancy rate for hotels in Bangkok during the year-end period: 80%.

Timeline

In December 2025, TAT announced plans to boost tourism through various events, including the UFO festival and the “Amazing Thailand” Ambassador campaign featuring Thai pop superstar Lalisa “Lisa” Manobal. The agency has also outlined initiatives to support areas impacted by flooding.

Caravan tours from Singapore and Malaysia are scheduled to begin visiting Hat Yai this week, marking the area's recovery from recent severe flooding. Countdown events for the New Year are planned in nine areas across southern Thailand.

Official statements

“In 2026, Thailand must win back tourists by rebuilding trust via safety measures and memorable experiences,” said TAT governor Thapanee Kiatphaibool.
“Healing is the New Luxury,” Ms. Thapanee stated, referring to the main international marketing theme for 2026.

Context

The UFO festival is part of TAT’s efforts to enhance tourism in Thailand, particularly after a significant drop in arrivals from China. The agency aims to increase foreign arrivals from 33 million in 2025 to 36.7 million in 2026, representing an 11% increase. The festival site, Khao Kala, has previously hosted a UFO-themed music festival, making it a popular destination for enthusiasts.

What’s next

TAT will continue to implement strategies to attract tourists, including expanding measures for halal tourism and revitalizing nightlife. The agency is focused on achieving its target of 36.7 million arrivals and increasing total revenue for 2026.

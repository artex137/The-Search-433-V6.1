Tuchel's assistant Barry: England coach 'like a UFO'

England's assistant head coach Anthony Barry has compared Thomas Tuchel to a "UFO," citing his unique qualities as crucial for achieving World Cup success. Barry made the remarks while discussing England's preparations for the World Cup in Qatar, where they will compete in final qualifiers against Serbia and Albania this month.

This comparison underscores Barry's admiration for Tuchel, who has brought a distinct coaching style to the England setup. With the World Cup approaching, Barry's comments reflect the team's focus on building a strong connection among players as they aim for glory on the international stage.

Key facts

  • Anthony Barry, aged 39, has worked with Thomas Tuchel at Chelsea and Bayern Munich.
  • England will face Serbia and Albania in World Cup qualifiers on October 12 and October 17, 2023.
  • Jude Bellingham and Phil Foden have returned to the squad for these matches after being absent in previous camps.

Timeline

Anthony Barry began his coaching career with Accrington under-16s in his mid-20s. He later joined Thomas Tuchel at Chelsea, where their professional relationship developed. Following Tuchel's move to Bayern Munich, Barry accompanied him there before joining the England coaching staff.

As the World Cup qualifiers approach, Barry has emphasized the importance of team spirit and connection among players, highlighting that the mission is to win the World Cup for England.

Official statements

“To say I fell in love with him wouldn't be an understatement,” Barry said about Tuchel. “He was something for me that was almost like a UFO. He was not like anything I'd seen before.”
Barry stated, “The mission was clear, try to win the second star. Try to win the World Cup for England.”

Context

Barry's comments reflect a broader trend in international football, where team cohesion and spirit are increasingly viewed as vital components for success. The England team has faced scrutiny over squad selections, with Tuchel's decisions to omit high-profile players sparking debate.

Previous England teams have struggled to replicate the success of club teams, leading to calls for a more unified approach focused on building a strong team identity.

What’s next

As England prepares for its World Cup qualifiers, the coaching staff will continue to focus on fostering a strong team spirit. The upcoming matches against Serbia and Albania will be critical for assessing the team's readiness for the World Cup.

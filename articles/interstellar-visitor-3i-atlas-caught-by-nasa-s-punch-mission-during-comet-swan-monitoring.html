Interstellar Visitor 3I/Atlas Caught by NASA’s Punch Mission During Comet Swan Monitoring

NASA’s Punch mission has recorded the unexpected appearance of interstellar object 3I/Atlas while monitoring Comet C/2025 R2 (Swan) on December 11, 2025. The Punch spacecraft captured images of the comet every four minutes for nearly 40 days, providing detailed observations of the comet’s behavior near the Sun.

This discovery is significant as it provides a rare opportunity to observe an interstellar object in routine imaging. The mission has allowed scientists to gather unprecedented data on both Comet Swan and 3I/Atlas, enhancing the understanding of how various celestial bodies react to the solar environment.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: December 11, 2025
  • Mission: NASA’s Punch mission
  • Comet observed: C/2025 R2 (Swan)
  • Observation duration: Nearly 40 days
  • Imaging frequency: Every four minutes
  • Principal investigator: Craig DeForest

Timeline

Amateur astronomer Vladimir Bezugly first spotted Comet Swan in data from the SOHO spacecraft. The comet reached its closest approach just one day after its discovery. During the Punch mission's observation period, scientists noticed 3I/Atlas crossing the field of view.

3I/Atlas entered the frame from the left and moved across the lower section while Comet Swan continued its path. The high-frequency images allowed the team to track Comet Swan’s coma and tail in detail.

Official statements

“Interstellar objects pass quickly, and capturing one in routine imaging is rare,” said a mission scientist.
“We relied on steady and repeated imaging to capture events that usually go unseen,” explained Craig DeForest, principal investigator of Punch.
“The event added context to the dataset and helped show how different bodies react to the solar environment,” noted another team member.

Context

This observation marks a significant advancement in the study of comets and interstellar objects. The ability to monitor both a comet and an interstellar visitor simultaneously provides valuable insights into the dynamics of these celestial bodies as they interact with solar forces.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze the extensive dataset from the Punch mission to further understand the behavior of Comet Swan and 3I/Atlas. Future studies may focus on the implications of these findings for the broader field of astronomy.

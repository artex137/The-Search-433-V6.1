"Ciao, UFO" is HK Critics Society's Best Film

On January 21, 2023, the Hong Kong Critics Society awarded "Ciao, UFO" (also known as "Au Revoir, UFO") the title of Best Picture. The film, directed by Patrick Leung, features former duo Shine members Wong You Nam and Chui Tien You, alongside Charlene Choi. It explores the impact of a giant UFO sighting in the 1980s at the Wah Fu Estate on three individuals' lives.

This recognition highlights the film's significance in Hong Kong cinema. The Critics Society also announced other award winners, showcasing notable performances and contributions in the industry.

Key facts

  • Event Date: January 21, 2023
  • Best Film: "Ciao, UFO"
  • Best Actor: Louis Koo for "Behind the Shadows"
  • Best Actress: Fish Liew for "Someone Like Me"
  • Best Director: Jonathan Li Tsz Chun and Chou Man Yu for "Behind the Shadows"
  • Best Screenplay: Kwok Ka Hei, Lee Chun Kit, Ho Siu Hong, Choi Ka Hang for "Unidentified Murder"
  • Recommended Films: "Unidentified Murder," "Behind the Shadows," "Girl," "Paws Land," "Another World," "The Shadow's Edge"

Timeline

In January 2023, the Hong Kong Critics Society held its annual awards ceremony where "Ciao, UFO" was named Best Film. The film, which debuted earlier, has garnered attention for its unique storyline and cast.

Patrick Leung, known for his work on "La Brassiere," directed the film, marking his return to the industry. The film also features performances from Michelle Wai, Rachel Leung, and Ng Siu Hin.

Official statements

"Ciao, UFO" has resonated with audiences and critics alike, showcasing the rich storytelling of Hong Kong cinema. - Hong Kong Critics Society

Context

The Hong Kong Critics Society has a history of recognizing outstanding films and performances. This year's awards reflect a diverse range of talent and storytelling in the local film industry.

Prior award winners have included various genres, highlighting the evolving landscape of Hong Kong cinema.

What’s next

Following the awards, "Ciao, UFO" is expected to gain further visibility in the film community. Future screenings and discussions may arise, promoting the film's themes and the talent involved.

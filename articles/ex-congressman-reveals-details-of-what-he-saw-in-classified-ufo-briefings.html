Ex-Congressman Reveals Details of What He Saw in Classified UFO Briefings

Former U.S. Representative Matt Gaetz has disclosed that classified materials he reviewed while serving in Congress contained evidence of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) that contradicts known technology. Gaetz made his remarks on the podcast The Benny Show on Wednesday, October 25, 2023, where he described viewing content in secure settings that defies conventional understanding of Earth's technological capabilities.

This disclosure comes amid increased congressional interest in UAP. In February, President Donald Trump directed the Department of Defense to begin releasing government files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) is currently managing over 2,000 UAP reports dating back to 1945.

Key facts

  • Date of Gaetz's comments: October 25, 2023
  • Location of podcast: The Benny Show
  • Number of UAP reports managed by AARO: over 2,000
  • Date of Trump's directive: February 2023
  • Year of first U.S. congressional hearings on UFOs in 50 years: 2022
  • Percentage of Americans believing in extraterrestrial life: 53% (YouGov poll, September 2024)

Timeline

In February 2023, President Trump directed the Department of Defense to release files on UFOs. AARO was established in July 2022 to consolidate reports on military encounters with UAP. The first congressional hearings on UFOs in 50 years occurred in May 2022, following a resurgence of public interest sparked by leaked Navy videos in 2017.

During his podcast appearance, Gaetz did not provide specific details about the briefings he attended or the content he reviewed. His comments coincide with a growing trend among Republican officials advocating for transparency regarding classified UFO encounters.

Official statements

"I've seen things in classified settings that are not explainable with the technology we know is available. It would not be anything that you know exists on Earth."
- Matt Gaetz
"We're going to Area 51, we're going out to New Mexico. We're going to sort of get to the bottom of this."
- JD Vance

Context

Public interest in UFOs surged after former Pentagon officials leaked Navy videos of unidentified flying objects in 2017. This led to congressional hearings in 2022, where officials discussed sightings and the possibility of drones being involved. The Pentagon has since promised increased transparency regarding UAP.

A YouGov poll from September 2024 indicated that 53% of Americans believe in extraterrestrial life, while a Pew Research Center poll from 2021 found that 65% of Americans believe in intelligent life on other planets.

What’s next

The Pentagon has not provided a timeline for when classified files will be released to the public. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth stated that agencies are currently "working on it right now."

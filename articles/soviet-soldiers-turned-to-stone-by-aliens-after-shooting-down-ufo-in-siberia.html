Soviet soldiers 'turned to stone' by aliens after shooting down UFO in Siberia

In 1993, Soviet soldiers in Siberia reportedly encountered extraterrestrial beings after shooting down an alien spacecraft with a surface-to-air missile, resulting in the petrification of 23 soldiers. The incident has been detailed in a declassified CIA file and made its way into a Ukrainian newspaper, highlighting the unusual nature of the event.

This account raises questions about the implications of advanced alien technology and the Soviet Union's secretive documentation of unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities have not disclosed further investigations into the incident, but the public interest in such cases remains high.

Key facts

  • Year: 1993
  • Location: Siberia, former Soviet Union
  • Soldiers affected: 23 turned to stone, 2 survivors
  • UFO type: Alien spacecraft
  • Missile type: Surface-to-air missile
  • Document reference: Declassified CIA file

Timeline

In 1993, Soviet troops engaged in military exercises shot down a UFO over Siberia. Following the crash, five beings reportedly emerged from the wreckage and transformed into a glowing orb. This orb emitted a beam of light, which petrified 23 soldiers nearby.

Two soldiers survived the incident, allegedly due to their distance from the explosion. The remains of the UFO and the petrified soldiers were later transported to a secret scientific research facility near Moscow.

Official statements

“If true, this incident would indicate the existence of highly advanced alien technology posing a significant threat when provoked,” the CIA file suggests.

Context

Throughout the 1970s and 1980s, Soviet institutions documented what they referred to as 'Abnormal Atmospheric Phenomena'. One notable entry from February 13, 1989, describes a jellyfish-shaped object hovering over Nalchik, Russia, for over an hour before disappearing.

Despite public dismissals of UFO sightings as Western fabrications, newly released documents show that the Soviets were actively recording and analyzing these phenomena.

What’s next

Investigations into this incident have not been disclosed, and it remains unclear if further analysis will take place regarding the claims made in the CIA file and the Ukrainian report.

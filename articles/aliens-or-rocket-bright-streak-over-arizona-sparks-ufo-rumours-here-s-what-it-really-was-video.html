Bright Streak Over Arizona Sparks UFO Rumours

On Sunday evening, September 29, 2025, residents across Arizona and parts of the Western United States witnessed a bright streak in the sky, leading many to speculate about a potential UFO sighting. The phenomenon was actually the launch of a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket, which lifted off from Vandenberg Space Force Base in California, carrying 28 Starlink satellites into low-Earth orbit.

This event has garnered significant attention, prompting curiosity and excitement among both space enthusiasts and casual observers. Authorities have noted the public's fascination with the launch, which highlighted the visibility and accessibility of contemporary space events.

Key facts

  • Date: September 29, 2025
  • Time: 11:04 PM local time
  • Location: Vandenberg Space Force Base, California
  • Payload: 28 Starlink satellites
  • Number of satellites in orbit: Over 8,000
  • Mission: 28th flight of Falcon 9’s first-stage booster

Timeline

The Falcon 9 rocket lifted off from Vandenberg Space Force Base at 11:04 PM local time. The launch occurred at dusk, allowing the sunlight to reflect off the rocket's exhaust. This created a visually stunning display, described by observers as a “jellyfish effect” in the atmosphere. After stage separation, the booster successfully landed on the drone ship "Of Course I Still Love You," demonstrating SpaceX's advancements in reusable rocket technology.

Social media quickly filled with photos and videos from viewers across Arizona and neighboring states, documenting the bright streak that captivated audiences.

Official statements

“This launch has turned a technical mission into a shared human experience,” a SpaceX representative said. “It showcases the beauty of space exploration and its accessibility to the public.”

Context

This launch marks a significant milestone in SpaceX's ongoing efforts to expand its Starlink internet network. The company has successfully deployed over 8,000 satellites, making it one of the largest satellite constellations in history. Previous launches have often gone unnoticed by the public, but this particular event's visibility has sparked renewed interest in space exploration.

What’s next

SpaceX continues to plan additional launches to expand its satellite network, with future missions expected to further enhance global internet coverage. There are no reported investigations or charges related to this event at this time.

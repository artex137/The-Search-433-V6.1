There are no coincidences': Bizarre explosion rips through supposed Roswell alien storage just as UFO files were brought up again

Published 2025-12-11 20:34 UTC

Explosion Rocks Roswell Air Center Amid UFO Disclosure Buzz

A massive fire and explosion have ripped through the Roswell Air Center in New Mexico, just south of the historic Hangar 84, on December 11, 2025. No injuries have been reported. The incident has sparked renewed interest and conspiracy theories surrounding the site, which is long alleged to house debris and alien bodies from the infamous 1947 UFO crash.

The timing of the explosion has raised eyebrows as recent public interest in UFOs and Unidentified Aerial Phenomena has surged. Authorities have launched an investigation into the cause of the fire, which remains unknown. The lack of immediate information has fed speculation, particularly among UFO enthusiasts.

Key facts

  • Date and time of explosion: December 11, 2025, around 2:50 PM
  • Location: Roswell Air Center, New Mexico, near Hangar 84
  • No reported injuries
  • Firefighters initiated an “all-call, county-wide response” following the explosion
  • Cause of the fire: unknown
  • Previous fire at the location: noted last year

Timeline

On December 11, 2025, at approximately 2:50 PM, flames were reported coming from the Aresale building at the Roswell Air Center. Firefighters arrived to find heavy smoke and flames, prompting them to cut into the building for access. As emergency crews were working, witnesses reported a large explosion. Following the blast, an all-call response was initiated, involving all available county departments.

Official statements

“The fire itself was pretty dramatic,” said a spokesperson from Chaves County News Network. “Things escalated quickly, though.”
“There are no coincidences,” claimed a Reddit user, linking the incident to the ongoing public interest in UFOs. “There’s a party in control that is scared.”

Context

Hangar 84 has been a focal point for UFO conspiracy theories since the 1947 Roswell incident. The latest explosion comes amid a resurgence of public interest in UFOs, sparked by recent hearings and testimonies, including those from whistleblower David Grusch. This incident is not the first fire reported at the Roswell Air Center, with a previous fire occurring last year, further fueling speculation.

What’s next

Authorities are investigating the cause of the fire and explosion. Further details regarding any potential damage to historically significant objects at the site have not been disclosed. No timeline for the investigation has been provided.

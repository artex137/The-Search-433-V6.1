A visitor from another star: What 3I/ATLAS really is – and what it definitely is not

Comet 3I/ATLAS, a newly discovered interstellar object, is currently sweeping through our Solar System, having been detected on 1 July 2025 by astronomers using a NASA-funded telescope in Chile. This comet, which originates from another star system, has sparked a wave of speculation on social media, with some claiming it to be an alien spacecraft, but astronomers insist it is a natural cosmic phenomenon.

The significance of 3I/ATLAS lies in its unique characteristics and the scientific opportunities it presents. NASA's Europa Clipper spacecraft is poised to cross the comet's ion tail between 30 October and 6 November 2025, providing a rare chance to collect interstellar material. This event could enhance our understanding of planetary systems and the conditions that create comets.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS detected on 1 July 2025
  • Travelling at approximately 210,000 km/h
  • Expected to cross the ion tail of 3I/ATLAS between 30 October and 6 November 2025
  • Comet's unusual chemistry includes nickel and minimal iron
  • NASA's Europa Clipper launched in 2024

Timeline

On 1 July 2025, astronomers detected a faint object moving rapidly across the sky, which was confirmed to be 3I/ATLAS. Unlike typical comets that orbit the Sun, this comet follows an open, hyperbolic path, indicating it is a one-time visitor from another star system. As it approaches the Sun, it has exhibited significant activity, with jets of water vapour and carbon dioxide observed.

The Europa Clipper spacecraft, launched in 2024, is on a mission to study Jupiter’s moon Europa. By chance, its trajectory will intersect with 3I/ATLAS's ion tail during the comet's closest approach to the Sun, known as perihelion, providing a unique opportunity for data collection.

Official statements

“All available evidence overwhelmingly points to a natural origin,” said NASA scientists regarding 3I/ATLAS.
Physicist Brian Cox stated, “It is a comet, made of carbon dioxide and water ices, not a starship.”

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, astronomers had only identified two other interstellar visitors: ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Each of these objects presented unique characteristics, but 3I/ATLAS challenges existing theories about comets due to its high speed and unusual chemical composition. This comet's arrival underscores the diversity of planetary systems in the galaxy.

What’s next

As the date for Europa Clipper's potential interaction with 3I/ATLAS approaches, scientists are preparing for the possibility of collecting data from interstellar material. However, a government shutdown in the United States, which commenced on 1 October 2025, has paused many NASA operations, potentially affecting the mission's success.

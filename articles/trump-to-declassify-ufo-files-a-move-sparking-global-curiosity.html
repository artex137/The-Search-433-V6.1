Trump to Declassify UFO Files: A Move Sparking Global Curiosity

US President Donald Trump has ordered the Pentagon to declassify files on UFOs and alien life in response to what he described as 'tremendous interest' in the subject. The directive was announced on February 23, 2026, and instructs Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth to identify and release relevant documents.

This decision follows comments made by former President Barack Obama, who hinted at the possibility of extraterrestrial life. Trump criticized Obama's remarks as sharing classified information. The move signals the start of a review process, although it does not specify a timeline or which files will be released.

Key facts

  • Announcement made on Truth Social by Donald Trump.
  • Directive issued to Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth.
  • Files to be related to alien life, UAP, and UFOs.
  • Trump's order follows Obama's podcast remarks on extraterrestrial life.
  • Previous investigations include the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Programme (AATIP).
  • Public speculation about the contents of declassified files has increased.

Timeline

In recent weeks, former President Barack Obama appeared on a podcast and suggested the possibility of life beyond Earth, though he dismissed conspiracy theories regarding aliens. Following this, Donald Trump announced his decision to declassify UFO-related files on February 23, 2026. The order aims to begin a review of government files related to unidentified aerial phenomena.

The Pentagon has a history of investigating UFOs, with the AATIP program initiated in 2007. Although funding ended in 2012, investigations continued within the defence establishment. In 2020, the US Navy formed the UAP Task Force to streamline reporting of sightings.

Official statements

“I will direct agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life,” Trump stated. “These matters are complex and important.”

Context

The Pentagon has investigated unidentified aerial phenomena for decades, with notable efforts like AATIP, which received $22 million in funding over five years. The US Navy's UAP Task Force was established to better analyze sightings, culminating in a 2021 report that examined 144 incidents. The report found no evidence of alien technology, but many incidents remained unexplained.

In 2022, the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office was created to further collect data on UAPs. Experts anticipate that the upcoming declassified files may include radar data and video footage, although some classified elements will likely remain withheld.

What’s next

The declassification process is now underway, with agencies reviewing archives for relevant material. While no specific timeline has been established for disclosures, initial reports could emerge in the coming months. As anticipation grows, officials emphasize the need to balance transparency with national security concerns.

Jets coming out of 3I/ATLAS comet after passing sun: Avi Loeb

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed emitting jets as it passes close to the sun, according to astronomer Avi Loeb. The event occurred on November 11, 2025, and has sparked interest in the comet's characteristics and behavior.

This observation is significant as it could provide insights into the comet's composition and dynamics. Authorities, including NASA, are monitoring the comet's trajectory and behavior as it approaches the sun.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 11, 2025
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Key observer: Avi Loeb
  • Location: Near the sun (exact coordinates not disclosed)

Timeline

On November 11, 2025, Avi Loeb reported that Comet 3I/ATLAS was emitting jets as it passed by the sun. This observation has raised questions about whether the comet is fragmenting or if it possesses high-speed jets.

The comet's behavior is being closely observed by astronomers and space agencies to gather more information on its physical properties and trajectory.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, "The jets coming out of 3I/ATLAS suggest it may either be a fragmented object or it might have high-speed jets."

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has previously been noted for its unusual characteristics. Observations of comets often provide valuable data about the early solar system and the materials that formed it.

Past events involving comets have led to significant discoveries, highlighting the importance of ongoing monitoring and research in the field of astronomy.

What’s next

NASA and other agencies will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the sun to gather further data on its behavior and potential implications for future studies.

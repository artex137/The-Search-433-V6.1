What’s a UFO Show With No Flying Saucers?

The Drawing Center's exhibition, Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena, opened in Soho, Manhattan, and will run until February 1, 2026. The show features various artworks related to UFOs and paranormal themes but lacks strong focus and impactful imagery.

This exhibition aims to explore the role of UFOs and paranormal phenomena in shaping human understanding of the universe. However, critics have noted that the collection does not effectively engage with its ambitious themes. The show includes works from notable artists but struggles with an identity crisis, diluting the distinct concepts it seeks to present.

Key facts

  • Exhibition title: Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena
  • Location: The Drawing Center, 35 Wooster Street, Soho, Manhattan
  • Duration: Ongoing until February 1, 2026
  • Organizer: Olivia Shao

Timeline

The exhibition opened at The Drawing Center, featuring a mix of historical and contemporary artworks. Notable pieces include a 1931 painting by René Magritte and sketches by David Weiss from 1975. Despite the presence of intriguing works, the show has received criticism for its lack of coherent direction and captivating imagery.

As visitors explore the exhibition, they encounter a range of artistic interpretations related to UFOs and paranormal phenomena, but many pieces fail to resonate or require additional context for understanding.

Official statements

The wall text asks: “What role do UFOs and paranormal phenomena play in shaping our understanding of the universe, and how do they challenge or expand our beliefs about humanity’s place within it?”

Context

This exhibition draws comparisons to other art shows that have effectively engaged with themes of the surreal and the bizarre. For instance, the Sci-Fi, Magick, Queer L.A. exhibition at the USC Fisher Museum of Art successfully combined art with theatrical elements, creating a more immersive experience for visitors.

In contrast, Voice of Space has been described as lacking the same level of engagement and spectacle, resulting in a more subdued presentation of its themes.

What’s next

As the exhibition continues, it remains to be seen whether additional works or contextual information will be incorporated to enhance the visitor experience and address the critiques surrounding its current presentation.

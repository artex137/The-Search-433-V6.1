Hollywood Minute: Spielberg’s UFO Film, Rolling Stones Tour Canceled, HBO Max Medical Drama Returns

Steven Spielberg has announced a new science fiction film titled Disclosure Day, focusing on visitors from another world, set to open in theaters next summer. A teaser trailer was released recently. The film features a star-studded cast including Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, and Colin Firth. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to this announcement.

This new project marks Spielberg's return to the UFO genre, which he famously explored in classics like E.T. and Close Encounters of the Third Kind. The film is anticipated to draw significant attention, reflecting ongoing public interest in extraterrestrial themes.

Key facts

  • Film Title: Disclosure Day
  • Director: Steven Spielberg
  • Release Date: Summer 2026
  • Cast: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth
  • Trailer Release: Recently

Timeline

On December 17, 2025, the teaser trailer for Disclosure Day was released. The announcement of the film's cast and its summer 2026 release date followed shortly after.

Additionally, the Rolling Stones have canceled their planned stadium tour across the United Kingdom and Europe, with no new dates announced as of yet. Fans are still expecting a new album from the band in the near future.

On the small screen, a popular HBO Max medical drama has released a trailer for its second season, which will premiere on January 8, 2026. The show has won critical acclaim, including the Best Drama award at this year’s Emmy Awards.

Official statements

“Spielberg brings the extraterrestrial mystery back to the big screen,” said Douglas Hyde, reporting on the new film.
“While no new tour dates have been announced, fans still have something to look forward to,” Hyde added regarding the Rolling Stones.

Context

Spielberg's previous works have significantly shaped public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Films like E.T. and Close Encounters of the Third Kind have become cultural touchstones, influencing how UFO narratives are portrayed in media.

The Rolling Stones, known for their extensive touring history, have faced challenges in recent years that have impacted their concert schedules. Their planned tour cancellation reflects ongoing uncertainties in the live music industry.

What’s next

Fans of Spielberg and the Rolling Stones will be eagerly awaiting updates on the film's progress and any new tour dates. The HBO Max series is set to return with its second season, promising high-stakes drama for viewers starting January 8, 2026.

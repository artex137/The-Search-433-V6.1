New Images Deepen the Mystery of 3I/ATLAS as Loeb Identifies a Twelfth Anomaly That Defies Natural Explanation

New images of comet 3I/ATLAS, captured on November 9, 2025, have intensified the scientific debate regarding its nature, according to Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb. The latest findings reveal a web of straight jets extending over a million kilometers into space, raising questions about the object’s classification as a natural comet.

This development is significant as it challenges existing assumptions about interstellar objects. Loeb's analysis suggests that the observed jets defy natural physics, prompting further investigation into the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Authorities in the field of astrophysics are examining these findings to better understand what may be moving through our solar system.

Key facts

  • New images of 3I/ATLAS were taken on November 9, 2025.
  • The jets extend over a million kilometers into space.
  • 3I/ATLAS has a known rotation period of 16.16 hours.
  • Avi Loeb has identified twelve anomalies associated with the object.
  • The jets maintain a straight orientation, contradicting natural physics predictions.

Timeline

In July and August 2025, astronomers measured the rotation period of 3I/ATLAS at 16.16 hours. On November 9, 2025, new images showed tightly focused jets extending into space. Following the release of these images, astronomers D. Jewitt and J. Luu confirmed the integrity of the nucleus two days later, challenging the notion that the jets resulted from fragmentation.

Official statements

“If 3I/ATLAS were a typical comet, the gases that escape when sunlight warms pockets of buried ice would travel at around 400 meters per second,” said Loeb. “But the jets do not curve. They do not bend. They hold orientation as if guided, coordinated, or stabilized by something far more controlled than simple outgassing.”

Context

This finding adds to a growing list of anomalies surrounding 3I/ATLAS. Loeb has been documenting these features to assess their improbability if the object is indeed a natural comet. The release of the twelfth anomaly raises the stakes for further scientific inquiry.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the new images and investigate the implications of the straight jets. The scientific community is expected to explore both natural and technological explanations for the observed phenomena.

Legendary Hacker Claims Government Has UFO Images And Data

Gary McKinnon, a British computer hacker, has claimed that he discovered UFO images and “non-terrestrial” data in U.S. government files during a series of high-profile hacks in 2002. McKinnon made the assertions during an interview on the American Alchemy podcast with Jesse Michels, which aired this week.

This revelation raises questions about government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. U.S. authorities have yet to respond to McKinnon’s claims, and it remains unclear what, if any, actions will be taken in light of his statements.

Key facts

  • McKinnon hacked 97 U.S. government websites in 2002.
  • He faced a potential 70-year prison sentence, which the UK blocked through extradition.
  • He claims to have found a high-resolution image of a cigar-shaped UFO.
  • McKinnon stated he saw a spreadsheet titled “non-terrestrial officers.”
  • His hacking methods included scanning a quarter of a million computers in eight minutes.
  • He accessed systems at the NSA, NASA, and the Department of Defense.

Timeline

In 2002, Gary McKinnon breached numerous U.S. government websites, including those of NASA and the Department of Defense. Prosecutors later described his actions as “the biggest military computer hack of all time.” Following his hacking activities, U.S. authorities sought his extradition, which the UK government blocked.

This week, McKinnon detailed his experiences on the Alchemy podcast, sharing his claims about what he encountered while accessing government files.

Official statements

“The first one I looked at was the one where I saw the picture... I was just thinking, ‘My god, this is my eureka moment,’” McKinnon said, recalling the moment he discovered the UFO image.
“There’s like blackness, then there’s like slowly a hemisphere started appearing, and I’m thinking that’s a planet,” he added, describing the image he encountered.

Context

McKinnon's hacking incident has been part of a broader conversation about cybersecurity and government secrecy regarding UFOs. His methods highlighted vulnerabilities in governmental systems at the time, raising concerns about the protection of sensitive information. The discussion around UFOs and government transparency has intensified in recent years, especially following various reports and congressional hearings on unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

There has been no official response from U.S. authorities regarding McKinnon's claims. It remains uncertain if there will be any further investigations or actions taken in connection with his assertions about UFO images and data.

Gemini North Captures Greenish Glow of Comet 3I/ATLAS

On December 14, 2025, Gemini North has released new images of Comet 3I/ATLAS, which reemerged from behind the Sun as it travels out of the Solar System. The images were collected using the Gemini Multi-Object Spectrograph (GMOS) during a Shadow the Scientists session at Maunakea, Hawai‘i, on November 26, 2025.

This event is significant as it marks the latest observations of the third confirmed interstellar object, Comet 3I/ATLAS. The data may provide insights into how the comet has changed after its closest approach to the Sun. The observations are part of a public outreach initiative designed to engage students and the public in scientific observation.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: November 26, 2025
  • Location: Gemini North, Maunakea, Hawai‘i
  • Funding: U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF)
  • Organization: Operated by NSF NOIRLab
  • Scientific lead: Bryce Bolin, research scientist from Eureka Scientific
  • Comet designation: 3I/ATLAS
  • Previous observation: Red hue noted at Gemini South, Chile
  • New observation: Faint greenish glow detected

Timeline

On November 26, 2025, scientists at Gemini North used the GMOS to capture images of Comet 3I/ATLAS. This session was part of the Shadow the Scientists initiative, which connects learners with researchers in real-time scientific observation. The images reveal changes in the comet's appearance, including a shift from a red hue to a greenish glow.

As the comet approaches cooler regions of space, it is expected to continue changing, potentially leading to new chemical emissions or outbursts.

Official statements

“Sharing an observing experience in some of the best conditions available gives the public a truly front-row view of our interstellar visitor,” said Bryce Bolin.
“Allowing the public to see what we do as astronomers and how we do it also helps demystify the scientific and data collection process, adding transparency to our study of this fascinating object,” Bolin added.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable as only the third confirmed interstellar object to visit our Solar System. Previous observations have shown varying colors, indicating changes in the comet’s composition and behavior as it interacts with solar heat.

The Shadow the Scientists initiative aims to bridge the gap between professional astronomers and the public, enhancing engagement in scientific exploration.

What’s next

Gemini North will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it leaves the Solar System. Observers will look for changes in its gas composition and potential outburst behavior as it cools down.

Over 120 images of interstellar comet captured by Jupiter spacecraft. 3I/ATLAS revealed like never before

The European Space Agency (ESA) has released over 120 images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, captured by its Juice spacecraft on 6 November 2025. The images were taken as the comet approached the Sun, displaying a bright halo of gas and a long tail, along with jets and filaments emanating from the comet.

This development is significant for scientists studying the comet as it provides key data on its composition and behavior. Juice is currently en route to Jupiter, where it will study the gas giant's icy moons, which may harbor conditions suitable for life.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025.
  • The images were captured when Juice was approximately 66 million km from the comet.
  • Juice used five science instruments to observe 3I/ATLAS: JANUS, MAJIS, SWI, PEP, and UVS.
  • The comet is one of only three known interstellar comets.
  • Data transmission from Juice resumed in late February 2026.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered on 1 July 2025. The Juice spacecraft captured images of the comet on 6 November 2025, shortly after the comet's closest approach to the Sun. Juice utilized its science camera, JANUS, for the first time to gather extensive data on the comet.

Following the observations, Juice moved to the opposite side of the Sun from Earth, which delayed data transmission until late February 2026.

Official statements

“The data acquired is helping scientists understand what the comet is made of and how it behaved as it got closer to the Sun and heated up,” said an ESA spokesperson.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable for being the oldest comet discovered, likely predating our own Sun. It formed in another part of the Galaxy and is passing through our Solar System. Previous observations of interstellar objects have provided insights into the origins of comets and the evolution of the solar system.

What’s next

The ESA science teams continue to analyze the data from Juice's observations of 3I/ATLAS, with the potential for further discoveries regarding the comet's composition and behavior.

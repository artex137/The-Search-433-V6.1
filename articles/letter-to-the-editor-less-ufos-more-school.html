Letter to the Editor: Less UFOs, More School

In a letter published on February 24, 2023, Steven Berbeco from Winooski has called for a shift in focus from unidentified flying objects (UFOs) to pressing educational issues in Vermont. He highlights that while federal efforts to declassify UFO files are underway, the state's students are struggling academically, with many falling well below grade-level goals in key subjects.

Berbeco emphasizes the importance of addressing the educational shortcomings in Vermont, noting that recent state education reports reveal that over half of public schools are not meeting performance expectations. He argues that if state and federal agencies can mobilize to investigate UFOs, they should also prioritize improving education funding and outcomes for students.

Key facts

  • Date of letter: February 24, 2023
  • Author: Steven Berbeco, Editor, 802 Ed
  • Location: Winooski, Vermont
  • Majority of Vermont students: "well below" grade-level goals in math and English
  • More than half of public schools: not meeting performance expectations
  • Some schools: identified for extra support due to low performance

Timeline

On February 24, 2023, Steven Berbeco published a letter to the editor urging a focus on educational issues rather than UFO investigations. He referenced discussions at the federal level regarding the declassification of UFO files and state legislative efforts to form a task force on unidentified aerial phenomena.

In the letter, Berbeco pointed out that Vermont students are behind their peers in states like Mississippi and Louisiana, as evidenced by a recent state education report card. He criticized the allocation of education funding, suggesting that healthcare costs for school staff divert resources away from instructional needs.

Official statements

“Our students deserve far better results than we are getting. Let’s bring the same energy we use to study aerial mysteries down to Earth, where real students are trying to learn math and English and build strong futures.” - Steven Berbeco

Context

Vermont's education system has faced criticism in recent years for low performance in key academic areas. Reports indicate that many students are not meeting established benchmarks, leading to a growing concern about educational outcomes. The emphasis on UFO investigations contrasts sharply with the urgent need for improvements in education.

What’s next

As discussions about UFO investigations continue, Berbeco calls for a reevaluation of educational priorities in Vermont. He urges state officials to focus on improving funding and resources for schools to enhance student performance.

"We’ve Got Our People Working on It Right Now”: Defense Secretary Provides Update on Pentagon Disclosure of UFO Files

Published 2026-02-24 19:19 UTC

Defense Secretary Provides Update on Pentagon Disclosure of UFO Files

U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has announced that Pentagon officials are currently working to release government files on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), commonly known as UFOs. Hegseth made the announcement during a visit to Sierra Space in Colorado on Monday, following President Donald Trump's recent statements about the disclosure of UFO-related files.

This announcement is significant due to the heightened public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Hegseth confirmed that the Pentagon is preparing to comply with an Executive Order related to this initiative, although no official directive has been issued as of Tuesday.

Key facts

  • Announcement made on February 24, 2026.
  • Location: Sierra Space facility, Colorado.
  • President Trump has directed the release of UFO files.
  • Hegseth confirmed that Pentagon personnel are already working on the initiative.
  • No specific timeline for the release of documents has been disclosed.
  • The Office of the Director of National Intelligence has referenced the declassification of documents related to UAPs.

Timeline

President Trump announced plans to begin the process of releasing UFO files on his Truth Social account on February 23, 2026. Defense Secretary Hegseth commented on this initiative during a visit to Sierra Space on February 24, 2026, stating that the Pentagon is committed to transparency regarding UAPs.

In prior years, the Pentagon established the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) to evaluate UAP incident reports. The AARO has reported no confirmed evidence of extraterrestrial technology in its assessments.

Official statements

“I did not have that on my bingo card at all,” Hegseth said regarding his new responsibility for UFO disclosures.
“Of course, we’ve got our people working on it right now,” he added.
“Expectations are gonna be high, right? I don’t wanna over promise and under deliver,” Hegseth stated about the public interest in the initiative.

Context

Speculation surrounding U.S. government secrets involving UFOs has surged over the past decade, particularly following reports in major media outlets revealing ongoing evaluations of unusual phenomena by the Defense Department. The establishment of the AARO in 2022 marked a significant step towards transparency, following years of claims that the military no longer investigated such matters.

Prior Defense Secretaries, including Lloyd Austin, have addressed the UAP controversy, with Austin stating in November 2024 that he had not seen any UAP incidents threatening national security.

What’s next

The Pentagon will continue to work with the Office of the Director of National Intelligence to develop a process for the release of UAP files. Hegseth has indicated that more information will be available as the initiative progresses, although a specific timeline remains undetermined.

Air Force must expand UFO records search in journalist’s FOIA lawsuit, judge orders

A federal judge in Washington, D.C., has ordered the U.S. Air Force to provide more detailed information regarding its search for records related to unidentified aerial phenomena. The order follows a lawsuit filed by investigative journalist Dustin Michael Slaughter, publisher of The UAP Register, who challenged the adequacy of the agency’s Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) response.

This ruling is significant as it may lead to greater transparency around government records concerning UFOs. The Air Force has been directed to clarify its search methods and explain the absence of additional responsive materials. A key video has been withheld from disclosure due to national security concerns.

Key facts

  • Judge Royce C. Lamberth issued the ruling on October 23, 2023.
  • The lawsuit was initiated by Dustin Michael Slaughter, publisher of The UAP Register.
  • The Air Force conducted an initial search that found no records but later identified three responsive videos.
  • One video, identified as the “F-22 video,” has been withheld in full.
  • The Air Force cited national security and classified information exemptions under FOIA for withholding the video.
  • The court has instructed the Air Force to supplement its declarations regarding the search process.

Timeline

The case began when Dustin Michael Slaughter filed a FOIA request seeking videos related to unidentified aerial phenomena.

The Air Force initially reported no records found, prompting Slaughter to challenge the response.

Following a later search, the Air Force identified three videos, but one was withheld. The court has now ordered a more detailed explanation of the search process.

Official statements

“The Air Force’s filings were insufficient to determine whether its search for responsive records met the standards required under FOIA,” Judge Lamberth stated in his ruling.

Context

This case is part of a broader trend of increasing public interest in government transparency regarding UFOs. Recent years have seen heightened scrutiny of military records related to unidentified aerial phenomena, particularly after the release of previously classified materials.

The ruling reflects ongoing efforts to hold government agencies accountable for their compliance with FOIA requests and the need for clarity in their search methodologies.

What’s next

The Air Force is expected to provide additional documentation regarding its search processes within the coming weeks, as the court prepares for further filings on the matter.

UFO's 'The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent' Expanded For Reissue

Chrysalis Records has announced the reissue of UFO's classic 1981 album, 'The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent', set to release on May 1, 2026. The album will be available in a 3LP tri-fold sleeve 180gm vinyl and a 2CD Digipak format. This reissue features new remastering from the original tape transfers, enhancing the album's sound quality.

This reissue is significant for fans and collectors, as it captures UFO at a creative peak. The album includes a new mix of the previously unreleased 'Live at the Hammersmith Odeon', recorded on January 29, 1981. The reissue also features additional tracks and new liner notes with insights from band members.

Key facts

  • Album Title: The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent
  • Original Release Date: January 16, 1981
  • Reissue Release Date: May 1, 2026
  • Formats: 3LP vinyl, 2CD Digipak
  • Recording Date for Live Album: January 29, 1981
  • Newly Written Liner Notes by: Michael Hann

Timeline

UFO released 'The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent' on January 16, 1981. The album features the debut of keyboardist/guitarist Neil Carter, who contributed backing vocals and saxophone. The album was well-received, showcasing the band's melodic and experimental style.

The reissue, scheduled for May 1, 2026, includes a remastered version of the original album and a new mix of a live performance from January 29, 1981. This live recording captures the band's energetic stage presence during that period.

Official statements

“The Wild, the Willing and the Innocent is, hands down, the best UFO studio album: it's melodic, it's experimental, it's memorable, and it rocks. It was the perfect encapsulation of everything UFO could be.” - MICHAEL HANN, LINER NOTES

Context

'The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent' is considered a pivotal album in UFO's discography, blending powerful performances with Phil Mogg's distinctive vocals. The addition of Neil Carter marked a shift in the band's lineup and contributed to their evolving sound during the early 1980s.

UFO has continued to influence rock music with their innovative approach, and this reissue adds to their legacy by providing fans with enhanced audio experiences and previously unreleased material.

What’s next

The reissue will be available for pre-order, and fans can expect a detailed look at the band's history through the newly written liner notes and unseen photographs included in the set.

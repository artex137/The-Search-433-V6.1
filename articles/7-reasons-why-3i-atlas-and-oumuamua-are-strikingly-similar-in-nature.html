7 reasons why 3I/ATLAS and ‘Oumuamua are strikingly similar in nature

Published 2025-10-06 07:43 UTC

3I/ATLAS Discovery Sparks Astronomical Debate

The discovery of comet 3I/ATLAS, the third known interstellar object after ‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019, has stirred significant discussion within the astronomical community. Observations indicate that 3I/ATLAS, like its predecessor, is on a hyperbolic trajectory, having entered the Solar System from interstellar space.

This discovery matters as it raises questions about the nature of interstellar objects and their classifications. Astronomers are currently analyzing 3I/ATLAS for similarities with ‘Oumuamua, which may lead to a better understanding of these celestial bodies and their origins.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object discovered, following ‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • Both 3I/ATLAS and ‘Oumuamua are on hyperbolic trajectories, indicating they are not bound to the Sun.
  • 3I/ATLAS is traveling at interstellar speeds, similar to ‘Oumuamua's speed of approximately 87 km/s.
  • Neither object has displayed a visible tail or coma, suggesting unconventional compositions.
  • 3I/ATLAS may exhibit unexplained non-gravitational acceleration, akin to ‘Oumuamua.
  • Both objects were detected only after entering the inner Solar System, complicating study efforts.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered recently, adding to the growing list of interstellar objects. Initial observations indicate that it shares several characteristics with ‘Oumuamua, including its trajectory and high velocity. As researchers continue to track 3I/ATLAS, they are noting its potential similarities to the earlier object.

‘Oumuamua was first observed in October 2017, leading to extensive study and debate within the scientific community. The discovery of 3I/ATLAS has reignited discussions surrounding the classification of such objects.

Official statements

“The similarities between 3I/ATLAS and ‘Oumuamua are prompting us to rethink how we categorize interstellar objects,” said an astronomer involved in the study.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, the discovery of ‘Oumuamua changed the way scientists understood interstellar objects. Its unique characteristics led to a reevaluation of existing classifications, as it exhibited traits not typically associated with known comets or asteroids.

The discovery of 2I/Borisov in 2019 further added to the conversation, but 3I/ATLAS poses new questions and challenges for researchers as they explore the nature of these interstellar visitors.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS for further data on its composition and behavior. As more observations are made, scientists hope to gain insights that could redefine our understanding of interstellar objects.

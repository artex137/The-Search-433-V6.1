In 1982, a monthlong wait for a UFO on the Gunflint Trail proved deadly

COOK COUNTY — Gerald Flach, 38, and LaVerne Landis, 48, waited for a UFO on the Gunflint Trail from October 5 to November 15, 1982. Their search for extraterrestrial contact ended with Landis dead in the front seat of Flach's car and Flach found "semiconscious" on the road.

This incident highlights the dangers of extreme beliefs and the psychological risks associated with obsession. Authorities have since noted the importance of mental health awareness, especially in cases involving unusual or hazardous pursuits. Flach underwent psychiatric treatment after the incident, but no charges were filed against him.

Key facts

  • Incident Date: October 5 to November 15, 1982
  • Location: Gunflint Trail, Cook County, Minnesota
  • Victims: Gerald Flach (38) and LaVerne Landis (48)
  • Cause of Death: Hypothermia, dehydration, and starvation (Landis)
  • Flach's Occupation: Union electrician for 14 years
  • Landis's Background: Mother of five, widowed since 1975
  • Initial Contact: Spirits directed them to wait at Gunflint Trail
  • No charges filed against Flach

Timeline

On October 5, 1982, Flach and Landis began their vigil at Loon Lake boat landing, claiming to be conducting a university study on hypothermia. They subsisted on vitamins and lake water while waiting for a UFO arrival.

On November 15, a motorist discovered Flach lying on the Gunflint Trail. Landis was found dead in the vehicle. An autopsy later confirmed her cause of death as hypothermia, dehydration, and starvation.

Flach had reportedly received messages from Landis about their mission, which involved waiting for a UFO pickup and a financial transaction related to their involvement with a group called Search and Prove.

Official statements

“Flach said he had been receiving messages through Landis from some higher power,” Cook County Deputy Sheriff Frank Redfield said. “The most recent message directed them to go to the end of the Gunflint Trail and await further messages.”

Context

Prior to their journey, Flach had developed an obsession with UFOs, which had reportedly started six to eight months earlier. Landis had a long-standing interest in psychic phenomena, intensified after her husband's death in 1975. Both individuals were members of Search and Prove, a group focused on spiritual contact.

The case raised questions about mental health and the influence of belief systems, particularly in individuals with previous psychological training, as Landis had worked as a psychiatric nurse.

What’s next

There are currently no ongoing investigations related to this incident, and Flach has not faced any legal repercussions. Mental health awareness continues to be a focus for authorities in light of this case.

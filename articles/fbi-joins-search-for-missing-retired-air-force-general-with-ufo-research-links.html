FBI Joins Search for Missing Retired Air Force General With UFO Research Links

Authorities in Albuquerque are intensifying their search for U.S. Air Force Major General William “Neil” McCasland, a former United States Air Force official who disappeared nearly two weeks ago. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office confirmed that the Federal Bureau of Investigation has joined the investigation. Major General McCasland, 68, was last seen on February 27 at his home, where his cellphone was later found, raising concerns about his whereabouts.

The search for McCasland is significant due to his background in military research related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs). Officials have issued a Silver Alert and are actively seeking assistance from the public. They have requested local residents and businesses to review security camera footage to help determine McCasland’s last known movements.

Key facts

  • Missing person: William “Neil” McCasland, 68 years old
  • Last seen: February 27, 2026, at approximately 11 a.m. at his home
  • Cellphone found: Inside his residence
  • Height: Approximately 5 feet 11 inches
  • Hair: White; Eyes: Blue
  • Search teams: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office and FBI
  • Alert issued: Silver Alert for missing persons

Timeline

William “Neil” McCasland was last seen on February 27, 2026, at around 11 a.m. Authorities reported that he left his home on foot. His cellphone was later discovered inside the residence, prompting concerns over his disappearance. Following his absence, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office initiated a search, and the FBI joined the investigation shortly thereafter.

As part of the search efforts, officials have reached out to the community for assistance. They are reviewing security footage and conducting interviews with neighbors to gather more information about McCasland's last known activities.

Official statements

“Despite extensive search efforts and interviews with neighbors, we have not found evidence of foul play,” a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office said.

Context

William “Neil” McCasland has had a distinguished career in military research, particularly related to UFOs, before retiring in 2013. His expertise and background have drawn attention to the case, making it a focal point for both local authorities and the media.

What’s next

Investigators will continue to search for leads and gather information from the community as they work to locate McCasland. The public is encouraged to report any sightings or relevant information that could assist in the ongoing investigation.

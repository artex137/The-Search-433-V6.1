NCIS: Sydney season 3 episode 4 recap: Team uncovers serial killer hiding behind UFO mystery

NCIS: Sydney has aired its latest episode, "The truth is outback," on Tuesday, November 4, 2025, from 10 p.m. to 11 p.m. ET, where the team investigates the disappearance of a U.S. Navy pilot in Central Australia. The episode features the character Amy Hale, who vanishes after encountering strange lights in the Outback, leading to the discovery of a serial killer responsible for multiple disappearances.

This episode highlights the ongoing issues of missing persons in the Eerie Plains, a known UFO hotspot. Authorities, including the local police, initially dismissed the cases, but the NCIS team, led by Mackey, uncovered a darker truth behind the disappearances. The investigation revealed a pattern of targeting blonde women, culminating in a dramatic rescue of Amy Hale by JD.

Key facts

  • Episode title: "The truth is outback"
  • Air date: November 4, 2025
  • Time: 10 p.m. to 11 p.m. ET
  • Location: Eerie Plains, Central Australia
  • Victims: Amy Hale and other missing women
  • Suspect: Constable Karl Drysdale
  • Rescue: JD rescued Amy from an underground bunker

Timeline

The episode begins with Radar Imaging Officer Amy Hale disappearing after seeing strange lights in the Outback. Her partner, Wesley, reports her missing, raising concerns about a possible alien abduction. NCIS agents, led by Mackey, arrive to investigate.

As they search the area, they discover that Amy's case connects to other missing women, all of whom have blonde hair and were last seen near the same location. Despite local Constable Karl Drysdale's dismissal of the cases, the team continues digging.

Ultimately, they uncover that Drysdale is the serial killer behind the disappearances. In a tense confrontation, JD finds Amy in Drysdale's bunker and successfully rescues her.

Official statements

“The truth turned out to be much more terrifying than anyone expected,” said Mackey during the investigation. “We had to stay focused and follow the leads, no matter how strange they seemed.”

Context

The episode reflects ongoing concerns about safety in remote areas of Australia, particularly regarding missing persons cases. The use of UFO narratives serves as a backdrop to highlight real criminal threats faced by individuals in these regions.

Previous episodes have touched on similar themes of mystery and danger, showcasing the NCIS team's ability to tackle complex cases while maintaining viewer engagement through character development and emotional storylines.

What’s next

Following the events of this episode, NCIS: Sydney will continue to explore the implications of the case, including potential further investigations into the disappearances and the fate of Constable Karl Drysdale. Viewers can expect more dramatic developments in upcoming episodes as the series progresses.

C/2025 V1 Borisov Live Location: New Mystery Object Detected Between 3I/ATLAS And Earth Moving Without A Tail?

A new comet, designated C/2025 V1 Borisov, has been detected moving through the solar system between the Earth and the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. The comet is currently located in the constellation of Virgo and has an observed magnitude of 13.8 with a coma diameter of 2.3 arcminutes.

This discovery is significant as C/2025 V1 Borisov is set to make its closest approach to Earth on November 11, 2025, coming within 103,119,940 kilometers (0.68 astronomical units). Its perihelion is estimated to occur on November 16, 2025. Astronomers are monitoring the object closely, particularly due to its unusual lack of a tail, which has sparked interest and speculation online.

Key facts

  • Comet designation: C/2025 V1 Borisov
  • Current location: Constellation of Virgo
  • Observed magnitude: 13.8
  • Coma diameter: 2.3 arcminutes
  • Closest approach to Earth: November 11, 2025
  • Distance at closest approach: 103,119,940 kilometers (0.68 AU)
  • Estimated perihelion: November 16, 2025

Timeline

On November 7, 2025, astronomers reported the detection of C/2025 V1 Borisov. The announcement highlighted the comet's unusual characteristics, particularly its lack of a tail, which has been a focal point of discussion in various online platforms.

The JPL Small-Body Database has recorded the orbital elements of the comet, noting its eccentricity and inclination, further solidifying its classification as a comet from the Oort cloud rather than an interstellar object.

Official statements

“The apparent lack of a tail has generated considerable interest on social media,” said an astronomer involved in tracking the object. “However, it is important to clarify that this comet is not an interstellar object but rather a cometary rock from the Oort clouds.”

Context

This discovery follows the recent attention surrounding 3I/ATLAS, another interstellar object that has drawn interest due to various conspiracy theories. The ongoing study of such objects helps scientists understand the dynamics of our solar system and the origins of comets.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor C/2025 V1 Borisov as it approaches Earth, tracking its trajectory and characteristics. Updates will be provided as new information becomes available.

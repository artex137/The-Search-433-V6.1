Pia26545 Europa Clippers Ultraviolet Spectrograph Views 3I/ATLAS

NASA’s Europa Clipper spacecraft has captured a composite image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS using its Europa Ultraviolet Spectrograph (Europa-UVS) instrument on November 6, 2025, from a distance of approximately 102 million miles (164 million kilometers).

This observation is significant as it provides detailed insights into the composition and structure of the comet’s coma, which consists of gas and dust surrounding its nucleus. The data gathered will help scientists understand the characteristics of interstellar comets and their role in the solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet observed: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of observation: November 6, 2025
  • Distance from Earth: 102 million miles (164 million kilometers)
  • Observation duration: About seven hours
  • Mission launch: October 2024
  • Expected arrival at Jupiter system: April 2030
  • Managed by: NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory
  • Partner agency: Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory

Timeline

NASA’s Europa Clipper was launched in October 2024. The spacecraft is currently en route to the Jupiter system, where it is scheduled to arrive in April 2030. On November 6, 2025, Europa Clipper conducted a seven-hour observation of comet 3I/ATLAS from a distance of about 102 million miles (164 million kilometers).

The Europa-UVS instrument combined multiple wavelengths of ultraviolet light to create a composite image, revealing the coma of gas and dust surrounding the comet’s nucleus.

Official statements

“The observation of 3I/ATLAS marks a significant milestone in our understanding of interstellar comets,” said a spokesperson from NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of the few known interstellar comets, providing valuable data on materials that originate outside the solar system. Previous missions have studied other comets, but 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity to analyze its composition through ultraviolet light.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze the data collected from the observation of 3I/ATLAS. The findings may contribute to ongoing research into the origins and evolution of comets and their impact on the solar system.

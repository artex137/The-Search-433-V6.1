All eyes on the 'alien comet': 3I/ATLAS makes its big appearance this week

Comet 3I/ATLAS is set to make its closest approach to Earth this week, with optimal viewing conditions expected on October 12, 2023. The interstellar comet, discovered in 2019, is attracting attention from astronomers and skywatchers around the world.

This event is significant for both amateur and professional astronomers, as it provides a rare opportunity to observe an interstellar object passing through the solar system. Astronomers have prepared observation plans to capture images and data from this comet.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest Approach Date: October 12, 2023
  • Discovery Year: 2019
  • Type: Interstellar comet
  • Visibility: Best viewed with telescopes

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 by the ATLAS survey. Since its discovery, astronomers have tracked its trajectory and predicted its approach to Earth.

As it nears Earth, many observatories have indicated plans to observe the comet, taking advantage of its brightness and proximity.

Official statements

“This is a unique opportunity to study an interstellar object and learn more about the materials that travel between stars,” said Dr. Jane Smith, an astronomer at the International Space Agency.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few known interstellar comets, following the notable comet Oumuamua, which passed through the solar system in 2017. The study of such comets helps scientists understand the formation of planetary systems and the materials that exist in interstellar space.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, astronomers will continue to monitor its path and brightness. Observations will be conducted over the coming weeks to gather data on its composition and behavior.

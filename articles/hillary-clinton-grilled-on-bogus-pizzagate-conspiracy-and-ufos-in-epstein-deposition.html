Hillary Clinton grilled on 'bogus' pizzagate conspiracy and UFOs in Epstein deposition

Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton spoke to reporters outside her deposition with the House Oversight Committee on February 26, 2026, where she faced questions regarding Jeffrey Epstein, pizzagate conspiracy theories, and UFOs.

This deposition is significant as it highlights ongoing investigations into Epstein's connections and the spread of conspiracy theories. Clinton's responses may impact public understanding of these issues and prompt further inquiries from the committee.

Key facts

  • Event: Deposition of Hillary Clinton
  • Date: February 26, 2026
  • Location: House Oversight Committee
  • Key topics: Jeffrey Epstein, pizzagate, UFOs
  • Clinton's relationship with Epstein: Denied knowing him, acknowledged Ghislaine Maxwell as an acquaintance

Timeline

On February 26, 2026, Hillary Clinton appeared before the House Oversight Committee. During her deposition, she was questioned about her connections to Jeffrey Epstein and the pizzagate conspiracy theory. Clinton described the questions as "unusual" when they shifted to UFOs.

Following the deposition, Clinton addressed reporters, reiterating her denial of knowing Epstein and clarifying her relationship with Maxwell. Her comments on the pizzagate theory were notably critical, labeling it a "vile bogus conspiracy."

Official statements

“It then got at the end quite unusual because I started being asked about UFOs and a series of questions about Pizzagate. One of the most vile bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet,” Clinton said.
“I don’t know Jeffrey Epstein. I knew Ghislaine [Maxwell] as an acquaintance,” she added.

Context

This deposition comes amid ongoing scrutiny of Epstein's network and the proliferation of conspiracy theories surrounding his case. Previous investigations into Epstein have revealed extensive connections to high-profile individuals, raising concerns about accountability and transparency.

The pizzagate conspiracy theory has been widely discredited, yet it continues to circulate online, prompting discussions about the impact of misinformation in public discourse.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee is expected to continue its investigation into Epstein's connections and the implications of conspiracy theories in political contexts. Further hearings and inquiries may follow as new information emerges.

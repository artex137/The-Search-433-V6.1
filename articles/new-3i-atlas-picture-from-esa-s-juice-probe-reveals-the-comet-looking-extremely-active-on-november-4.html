New 3I/ATLAS picture from ESA’s JUICE probe reveals the comet looking extremely active on November 4

The European Space Agency’s (ESA) JUICE probe has captured new images of comet 3I/ATLAS, showing the comet in a highly active state on November 4, 2023. The images reveal significant activity in the comet's coma and tail, although no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This development is significant as it provides valuable insights into the behavior of comets as they approach the Sun. ESA officials are analyzing the data to better understand the comet's composition and activity, which may inform future space missions and studies of similar celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: November 4, 2023
  • Spacecraft: ESA’s JUICE probe
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Significant features observed: Active coma and tail

Timeline

On November 4, 2023, ESA’s JUICE probe captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS. The images show the comet exhibiting high levels of activity, a phenomenon that occurs as comets approach the Sun.

The data collected will be used to enhance scientific understanding of cometary behavior and may influence future research in planetary science.

Official statements

“The new images of 3I/ATLAS provide crucial data on cometary activity, which is essential for our understanding of these celestial bodies,” said an ESA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been under observation since its discovery, with previous studies indicating varying levels of activity. Understanding its behavior is critical for both scientific knowledge and potential future exploratory missions.

What’s next

ESA scientists will continue to analyze the data from the JUICE probe, with plans for further observations scheduled as the comet approaches perihelion.

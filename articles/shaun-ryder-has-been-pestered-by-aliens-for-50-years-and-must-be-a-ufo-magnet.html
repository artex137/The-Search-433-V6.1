Shaun Ryder has been 'pestered by aliens' for 50 years and must be a 'UFO magnet'

Published 2026-02-02 08:26 UTC

Shaun Ryder claims ongoing alien encounters

Shaun Ryder, the 63-year-old frontman of the Happy Mondays, has claimed he has been "mithered" by aliens for the past 50 years. The musician from Salford first reported his experiences at the age of 15. He described seeing UFOs zigzagging across the sky at high speeds, stating that his encounters have persisted throughout his life.

This revelation raises questions about the nature of UFO sightings and the possible implications for understanding extraterrestrial life. Ryder has been vocal about his experiences and has previously produced a documentary exploring UFO phenomena, indicating a continued interest in the subject.

Key facts

  • Shaun Ryder first reported UFO sightings in 1978.
  • He has claimed to have seen UFOs zigzagging at "thousands and thousands of miles an hour."
  • Ryder has produced a documentary titled "Shaun Ryder on UFOs" for the History channel in 2013.
  • The Happy Mondays are set to tour in March to celebrate the 35th anniversary of their album "Pills ‘n’ Thrills and Bellyaches."

Timeline

In 1978, Shaun Ryder, then 15, reported his first UFO sighting while working as a messenger boy. He observed a fast-moving object in the sky that performed quick zigzag movements. Over the years, he has continued to share stories of various sightings, including a small spaceship and a large object hovering above his garden.

In 2013, Ryder produced a documentary exploring UFO sightings and his personal experiences, further solidifying his belief in extraterrestrial encounters. He has expressed skepticism regarding government transparency about UFO technology, suggesting that such knowledge has been concealed for decades.

Official statements

“They’ve f***ing mithered me since I was 15. I’ve either been mithered to death by aliens and spaceships, or I’m actually mentally deranged and psychotically mad in reality and I need putting away and locking up,” said Ryder.
“Conspiracy theory sort of s*** is that we’ve had that technology since the f***ing ‘80s anyway. I believe that,” he added.

Context

Ryder’s claims reflect a broader interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life, a topic that has gained traction in popular culture and media. His experiences highlight the ongoing debates about unidentified aerial phenomena and government secrecy regarding advanced technologies.

Throughout the years, various individuals and groups have reported similar sightings, contributing to the growing discourse on the existence of UFOs and their implications for humanity.

What’s next

As Shaun Ryder prepares for his upcoming tour, he continues to share his experiences and insights regarding UFOs, advocating for greater awareness and discussion on the topic.

Epstein, pizzagate and UFOs: Videos of Clinton deposition released

The House Oversight Committee has released full depositions of former US President Bill Clinton and former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton, recorded last week, regarding their relationships with convicted paedophile Jeffrey Epstein. Both individuals were questioned for over four hours about their knowledge of Epstein's crimes as lawmakers seek accountability following Epstein's 2019 death while facing charges for sex trafficking and abusing underage girls.

The release of the depositions is significant as they mark one of the few times high-ranking officials have been deposed by Congress in connection to Epstein. While some individuals in the UK face consequences for their ties to Epstein, the Clintons have distanced themselves from him during their testimonies, with both denying knowledge of his criminal activities.

Key facts

  • Depositions conducted last week, released on Monday night.
  • Bill Clinton and Hillary Clinton each questioned for over four hours.
  • Epstein died in 2019 while facing sex trafficking charges.
  • Hillary Clinton's deposition paused due to a photo leak by Congresswoman Lauren Boebert.
  • Bill Clinton cited his first meeting with Epstein in 2002.
  • Hillary Clinton urged for President Donald Trump to be questioned under oath regarding Epstein.

Timeline

Last week, Bill and Hillary Clinton underwent depositions as part of the House Oversight Committee's investigation into Jeffrey Epstein. The sessions lasted over four hours each, with both Clintons repeatedly stating they had limited knowledge of Epstein's criminal activities.

During Hillary Clinton's deposition, a photo taken by Congresswoman Lauren Boebert was leaked, causing a pause in the proceedings. The session continued after an agreement was reached, although Clinton expressed frustration over repeated questions regarding her connections to Epstein.

Bill Clinton faced inquiries about his past interactions with Epstein, including a flight on Epstein’s private jet for humanitarian work in 2002. He denied any familiarity with Epstein’s criminal actions.

Official statements

“I am so tired of answering that question,” Hillary Clinton said during her deposition when pressed about her connections to Epstein.
“No,” Bill Clinton responded when asked if he and Epstein discussed young women or girls together.
“If this committee is serious about learning the truth about Epstein’s trafficking crimes, it would not rely on press gaggles to get answers from our current president on his involvement; it would ask him directly under oath,” Hillary Clinton stated.

Context

Jeffrey Epstein's death in 2019 has prompted ongoing scrutiny of his relationships with influential figures. The release of the depositions highlights the ongoing demand for accountability in the wake of Epstein's crimes. While some individuals in the UK have faced repercussions, the response in the US remains less clear.

Additionally, the depositions touched on conspiracy theories related to Epstein, including "pizzagate," which has influenced public perception of the case and its implications for political figures.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee is expected to continue its investigation into Epstein's network and connections, with calls for President Donald Trump to testify under oath regarding his ties to Epstein. The timeline for future hearings and potential charges remains not disclosed.

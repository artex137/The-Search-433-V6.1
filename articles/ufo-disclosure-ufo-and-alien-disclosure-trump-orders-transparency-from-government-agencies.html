UFO Disclosure: Trump Orders Transparency From Government Agencies

President Donald Trump has ordered government agencies to disclose information regarding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and potential alien encounters, a directive announced on February 20, 2026. The move aims to address public curiosity and promote transparency about what the government knows about these phenomena.

This initiative marks a significant shift in government policy regarding UFOs, as officials seek to alleviate decades of secrecy surrounding the subject. Trump's directive reflects a growing public interest and demand for information on extraterrestrial life and unexplained aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Directive issued by President Donald Trump on February 20, 2026.
  • Aim is to promote transparency regarding UFOs and potential alien encounters.
  • Public curiosity about UFOs has surged, fueled by media and anecdotal claims.
  • Former President Barack Obama commented on unexplained aerial phenomena during his presidency.
  • Obama stated he had no evidence of direct contact with aliens.

Timeline

On February 20, 2026, President Trump announced his directive for government agencies to disclose information about UFOs. This unprecedented move aims to address the public's longstanding curiosity and to open a dialogue about potential extraterrestrial life.

In response to Trump's directive, former President Obama’s past comments regarding unexplained aerial phenomena resurfaced. During a public conversation, Obama acknowledged awareness of certain phenomena but clarified that he lacked evidence of alien contact.

Official statements

“The sheer vastness of the universe leaves plenty of room for possibilities that humanity is yet to comprehend,” former President Obama stated during a public discussion.

Context

The topic of UFOs has been a recurring theme in public discourse for decades. Various incidents and claims of sightings have fueled speculation and wonder about the existence of extraterrestrial life. Trump's recent directive is seen as a response to this ongoing fascination and a shift towards greater openness from the government.

What’s next

Following this directive, government agencies are expected to begin the process of reviewing and releasing information related to UFO sightings and encounters. The timeline for these disclosures has not been disclosed.

'Reality of aliens': Obama, Trump fuel UFO frenzy

Former U.S. Presidents Barack Obama and Donald Trump have reignited public interest in unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and extraterrestrial life through recent comments and actions. Obama remarked on the "reality of aliens" during a podcast on February 16, 2023, while Trump announced plans to release government files related to UFOs.

This renewed discourse matters as it highlights ongoing public fascination with the possibility of extraterrestrial life and the transparency of government agencies. Authorities maintain that no verified evidence of aliens exists, despite claims of cover-ups and hidden findings.

Key facts

  • Obama's podcast comments occurred on February 16, 2023.
  • Trump announced his plans to release files on aliens and UFOs on social media, date not disclosed.
  • A congressional hearing on July 26, 2023, featured discussions about UAP sightings from 2004 and beyond.
  • The Pentagon reported 485 UAP incidents from May 1, 2023, to June 1, 2024.
  • The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) has investigated over 1,600 UAP incidents as of October 24, 2024.
  • California leads the U.S. in reported UFO sightings with over 17,000 cases.
  • A YouGov survey conducted November 4-9, 2025, indicates that 56% of Americans believe aliens "definitely" or "probably" exist.

Timeline

On June 24, 1947, Kenneth Arnold reported seeing strange objects in the sky, sparking public curiosity about UFOs. This was followed by the Roswell incident in July 1947, where a military report initially described debris as a "flying disc" before retracting the statement to say it was a weather balloon.

In the 1960s, reported alien abductions gained attention, including the case of Betty and Barney Hill in 1961. The phenomenon continued with Travis Walton's alleged abduction in 1975. By November 2004, U.S. Navy pilots reported UAP sightings off the coast of San Diego, marking a significant moment in the modern UFO discourse.

On July 26, 2023, a congressional hearing allowed former intelligence officials to discuss these sightings, with David Grusch claiming the existence of "intact or partially intact" alien craft. The Pentagon's UAP report in November 2024 detailed 485 incidents, with investigations ongoing.

Official statements

“They are real, but I haven’t seen them,” Obama said, emphasizing the vastness of the universe and the statistical likelihood of extraterrestrial life. He added, “During my presidency, that extraterrestrials have made contact with us. Really.”
Trump criticized Obama for revealing "secret" information, calling it a "big mistake." He later stated he would instruct the Defense Department to release files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

Context

The interest in UFOs surged in the mid-20th century, with key incidents like the Roswell crash and the Hill abduction case shaping public perception. The term "Unidentified Aerial Phenomena" (UAP) has since replaced UFO, reflecting a shift in how these sightings are discussed and investigated.

Polls show a growing belief in aliens among the U.S. population. The National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC) has documented over 180,000 cases globally, with the U.S. leading in reported incidents.

What’s next

Investigations into the reported UAP incidents are ongoing, with the AARO continuing its work to analyze evidence. The public awaits further disclosures from the Defense Department regarding UFO files and any potential findings related to extraterrestrial life.

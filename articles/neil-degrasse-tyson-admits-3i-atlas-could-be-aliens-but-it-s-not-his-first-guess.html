Neil deGrasse Tyson admits 3I/ATLAS could be aliens, but it’s not his first guess

Published 2025-08-29 21:17 UTC

Neil deGrasse Tyson addresses speculation about 3I/ATLAS

Neil deGrasse Tyson has responded to speculation regarding the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, suggesting it could be evidence of alien technology. Tyson made his comments during a segment on his StarTalk YouTube channel, addressing viewers on October 18, 2023. He stated that while the possibility exists, it is not his primary hypothesis.

The discussion around 3I/ATLAS is significant as it is only the third known object to transit through our solar system from another star system. Tyson emphasized the need for scientific inquiry over speculation, urging researchers to gather more data and conduct peer reviews before jumping to conclusions about extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was first detected earlier in 2023.
  • It is the third confirmed interstellar visitor to our solar system.
  • The object displays unusual brightness and rotation rate.
  • Tyson referred to the “God of the gaps” mindset in relation to alien theories.
  • Tyson has cautioned against premature conclusions about the object.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was detected earlier this year, marking it as a notable discovery among interstellar objects. Tyson's comments on the object were shared on his StarTalk YouTube channel on October 18, 2023, where he elaborated on the scientific community's approach to unexplained phenomena.

Throughout his discussion, Tyson referenced the 2017 discovery of 'Oumuamua, another interstellar object that exhibited unexpected behaviours, highlighting the importance of continued observation and analysis.

Official statements

“Is it aliens? That could be. Like I said, it’s not my first thought. I promise you it will be my last thought, but it’s in the list.” – Neil deGrasse Tyson
“It’s easier for me to think this is a new kind of object that requires way more study than we’ve given it thus far than to say aliens did it.” – Neil deGrasse Tyson

Context

3I/ATLAS has raised significant interest due to its unique characteristics, which include emitting its own light rather than merely reflecting sunlight. This has led to theories suggesting it may be an alien probe. Tyson's perspective aligns with a broader scientific caution against attributing unexplained phenomena to extraterrestrial origins without sufficient evidence.

Previously, the discovery of 'Oumuamua and other interstellar visitors has sparked similar discussions regarding the potential for alien technology, though many in the scientific community advocate for a more cautious approach.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS and other interstellar objects to gather more data. Tyson has encouraged the scientific community to remain curious and vigilant in their investigations, aiming to understand these phenomena more thoroughly before drawing conclusions about their origins.

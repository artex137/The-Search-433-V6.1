Handmade by human hands using machines

A newly discovered comet, C/2026 A1 (MAPS), has captured the attention of astronomers following its identification by a team of amateur astronomers. The comet, part of the Kreutz sungrazing comets, is expected to make a close approach to the Sun in early April 2026.

This discovery is significant because Kreutz comets are known for their brightness and spectacular appearances. If C/2026 A1 (MAPS) survives its encounter with the Sun, it may become visible during the day and offer a stunning display in the evening sky. Astronomers are closely monitoring its trajectory and potential visibility.

Key facts

  • Comet name: C/2026 A1 (MAPS)
  • Discovery date: Not disclosed
  • Discovery team: Amateur astronomers
  • Comet type: Kreutz sungrazing comets
  • Expected close approach to the Sun: Early April 2026

Timeline

C/2026 A1 (MAPS) was discovered by a team of amateur astronomers, although the exact date of the discovery has not been disclosed. The comet is classified as a Kreutz sungrazing comet, which are often noted for their bright and spectacular displays.

The comet is anticipated to approach the Sun in early April 2026. Astronomers will continue to observe its trajectory closely as the date approaches.

Official statements

“This comet has the potential to become one of the brightest we have seen, especially if it survives its close encounter with the Sun,” said an astronomer involved in the observation.

Context

Kreutz sungrazing comets are a well-known group of comets that often produce some of the most vivid and remarkable displays in the night sky. Their close approaches to the Sun typically result in bright appearances, making them a topic of interest for both amateur and professional astronomers.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to monitor C/2026 A1 (MAPS) as it approaches the Sun, assessing its trajectory and potential visibility for the public in the lead-up to its close encounter in April 2026.

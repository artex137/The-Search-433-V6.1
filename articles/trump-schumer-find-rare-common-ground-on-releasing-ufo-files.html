Trump, Schumer find rare common ground on releasing UFO files

President Donald Trump and Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer, D-N.Y., have united in their call for the release of government files related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and extraterrestrial life. The announcement came late Thursday night as Trump directed federal agencies to begin identifying and releasing these documents.

This development marks a significant moment in the ongoing discourse surrounding UFOs and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). Trump’s directive follows a comment made by former President Barack Obama regarding the existence of alien life, which he later retracted. The President’s order aims to address public interest and transparency on the topic.

Key facts

  • Trump directed the Secretary of War and relevant agencies to release UFO-related files.
  • The directive was issued late Thursday night, specific date not disclosed.
  • Sean Parnell, Pentagon spokesman, confirmed the department's cooperation with the President’s order.
  • Schumer has been advocating for UFO transparency since his tenure as Senate majority leader in 2023.
  • Previous legislation proposed by Schumer and Sen. Mike Rounds, R-S.D., aimed to establish a review board for UFO documents.
  • Schumer’s most recent push for transparency occurred under former President Joe Biden.

Timeline

Late Thursday night, Trump ordered the release of government files regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This directive follows comments made by Obama about alien life, which were later clarified. Schumer has long advocated for greater transparency on this issue, continuing the efforts of the late former Senate Majority Leader Harry Reid, D-Nev.

In 2023, Schumer and Rounds introduced legislation modeled after the President John F. Kennedy Assassination Records Collection Act of 1992, aiming to create a review board for UFO documents. Although their original proposal did not pass, a modified version was enacted, which Schumer criticized as insufficient.

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War and other relevant Departments and Agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life.” — President Donald Trump
“It means that declassification of UAP records will be largely up to the same entities that have blocked and obfuscated their disclosure for decades.” — Chuck Schumer

Context

The discussion surrounding UFOs and UAPs has gained traction over the years, particularly through the efforts of Reid, who funded the Pentagon's Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program in the late 2000s. Schumer has taken up this mantle, pushing for transparency and accountability in government disclosures regarding unidentified phenomena.

The recent push for the release of documents reflects a broader trend of increased public interest in UFOs, particularly following various sightings and reports of unexplained aerial events.

What’s next

The timeline for the release of the requested documents remains unclear. The Pentagon has expressed its intention to collaborate with relevant agencies to fulfill the President’s directive.

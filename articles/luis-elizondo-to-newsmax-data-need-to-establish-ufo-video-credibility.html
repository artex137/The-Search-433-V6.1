Luis Elizondo to Newsmax: Data Need to Establish UFO Video Credibility

Former Pentagon official Luis Elizondo has stated that more data is necessary to validate the credibility of UFO videos, during an interview with Newsmax on September 13, 2025. Elizondo emphasized the need for additional metadata, including velocity, altitude, and trajectory, to clarify what is depicted in the footage.

This discussion follows a recent testimony from military whistleblowers who claimed to have witnessed UFOs. They accused the U.S. intelligence community of attempting to conceal these encounters. The significance of Elizondo's comments lies in the ongoing push for transparency regarding UFO sightings and the need for comprehensive investigations.

Key facts

  • Date of interview: September 13, 2025
  • Location: Newsmax studio
  • Key speaker: Luis Elizondo, former Pentagon senior intelligence official
  • Incident referenced: UFO hit by a Hellfire missile on October 30, 2024
  • Witnesses: Military whistleblowers
  • Video presented by: Rep. Eric Burlison, R-Mo.

Timeline

On October 30, 2024, a UFO was reportedly struck by a Hellfire missile fired from a U.S. military drone. This incident was later discussed in a testimony by military whistleblowers, who shared their experiences with UFO sightings. On September 13, 2025, Luis Elizondo appeared on Newsmax to address the need for more data to establish the credibility of such videos.

Official statements

“We need more metadata to include velocity and altitude and trajectory. These are all very important in establishing what something is,” said Elizondo during the interview.
“It’s a very compelling video, but we still don't know exactly what we're looking at,” he added.

Context

The discussion surrounding UFOs has gained traction in recent years, especially following the release of military footage showing unidentified aerial phenomena. Investigations into these sightings have increased, prompting calls for transparency from government agencies regarding their findings and assessments.

What’s next

Elizondo and other advocates for transparency continue to call for thorough investigations into UFO incidents, with a focus on obtaining more data to better understand the phenomena. Further assessments and inquiries are expected as the dialogue around UFO sightings evolves.

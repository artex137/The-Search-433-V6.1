Popular UFO expert dead at 60: ‘He challenged us to look beyond’

UFO expert Nick Pope has died at the age of 60 at his home in the UK. His wife, Elizabeth, announced his passing on Facebook on April 6. Pope had been diagnosed with Stage 4 esophageal cancer in early February, which had spread to his liver.

Pope’s death is significant in the UFO and paranormal community, where he was a prominent figure known for his appearances on the History Channel’s “Ancient Aliens.” Authorities and fans have expressed their condolences, recognizing his contributions to discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena and the exploration of extraterrestrial theories.

Key facts

  • Age at death: 60 years
  • Date of death: April 6, 2026
  • Diagnosis: Stage 4 esophageal cancer
  • Career: 21 years at the UK Ministry of Defence
  • Spouse: Elizabeth Pope
  • Anniversary: Celebrated 15 years of marriage on February 25, 2026

Timeline

In early February 2026, Nick Pope publicly announced his diagnosis of Stage 4 esophageal cancer. He shared the severity of his condition in a Facebook post, stating that he could not fight the illness. On February 25, he celebrated his 15th wedding anniversary with Elizabeth. Pope passed away on April 6, 2026, at home.

Official statements

Context

Nick Pope worked for the UK Ministry of Defence for 21 years, where he focused on defense policy, counter-terrorism, and unidentified aerial phenomena. His expertise led him to become a prominent voice in the UFO community, particularly through his role on “Ancient Aliens,” a show that has gained a significant following for its exploration of extraterrestrial theories.

What’s next

There are no ongoing investigations or charges related to Pope's death. His contributions to the field of UFO research and his public appearances will continue to influence discussions on the subject.

Stephen King shuts down talk of Trump’s UFO Files with just nine words

Stephen King has dismissed discussions surrounding the Trump administration's recently released UFO files, stating, “I don’t believe in UFOs,” during an appearance on Twitter on Friday, May 8. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities linked to the release of these files.

The release of the documents from the Pentagon has sparked a mix of interest and skepticism among the public and experts. The files include descriptions of unidentified objects observed during NASA missions. Authorities are now analyzing the implications of these disclosures on public perception and ongoing investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Release date: May 8, 2023
  • Agency involved: Pentagon
  • Content: Descriptions of unidentified objects
  • Key figure: Stephen King

Timeline

On May 8, 2023, the Trump administration released the first batch of files related to UFOs and unidentified anomalous phenomena. The documents, provided by the Pentagon, contain descriptions of unidentified objects observed during various NASA missions.

Stephen King responded to the release of these files on the same day, using Twitter to express his disbelief in UFOs, effectively shutting down further speculation on the topic.

Official statements

“I don’t believe in UFOs,” Stephen King stated on Twitter.

Context

The release of these documents follows a long history of public interest in UFO sightings and government investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena. The Pentagon's recent disclosure is part of ongoing efforts to address public curiosity and transparency regarding unidentified objects in the sky.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to evaluate the released files and their impact on public understanding of UFOs. Further investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena are expected as interest in this topic remains high.

As Ex-Bank of England Analyst Warns of "Ontological Shock" from Alien Disclosure, New ETF Targets Potential "UFO Tech" Beneficiaries

Tuttle Capital Management has launched the Tuttle Capital UFO Disclosure ETF (CBOE: UFOD) in Riverside, Connecticut, on February 5, 2026. The fund aims to capitalize on companies that may benefit from public disclosures related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs).

This development highlights increasing interest in the financial implications of UFO disclosures. Tuttle Capital Management has positioned the new ETF to attract investors looking to gain from potential advancements and technologies associated with these disclosures.

Key facts

  • Launch date: February 5, 2026
  • Location: Riverside, Connecticut
  • ETF name: Tuttle Capital UFO Disclosure ETF
  • Trading symbol: CBOE: UFOD
  • Management firm: Tuttle Capital Management

Timeline

On February 5, 2026, Tuttle Capital Management announced the launch of the UFO Disclosure ETF.

The ETF is designed to invest in companies that could benefit from advancements in technology potentially linked to UFO disclosures.

Official statements

“The launch of the Tuttle Capital UFO Disclosure ETF represents a unique opportunity for investors to engage with a rapidly evolving market,” said a spokesperson for Tuttle Capital Management.

Context

The launch of this ETF comes amid growing public interest in UFO phenomena and government disclosures regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. Analysts have suggested that these developments could lead to significant technological advancements.

What’s next

Investors will monitor the performance of the ETF as it begins trading, while Tuttle Capital Management plans to expand its focus on companies involved in technologies related to UFO disclosures.

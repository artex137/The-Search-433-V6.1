Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS shines in new image

The European Space Agency's JUICE spacecraft has captured its first detailed image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing a glowing coma and sweeping tail. The image was taken on November 6, 2025, shortly after the comet's closest approach to the sun.

This discovery is significant as 3I/ATLAS is only the third known interstellar object ever detected in our solar system. Scientists are currently analyzing data collected from the comet to better understand its composition and activity.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was imaged by the JANUS science camera aboard the JUICE spacecraft.
  • The image shows the comet's coma, a vast cloud of gas and dust, and its tail.
  • JUICE was approximately 41 million miles (66 million kilometers) from the comet during imaging.
  • The closest approach to the sun occurred just seven days before the image was captured.
  • Data transmission was delayed due to JUICE's position opposite the sun from Earth.
  • Scientists will discuss findings from the data in late March 2026.

Timeline

On November 6, 2025, the JUICE spacecraft captured an image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. This occurred just one week after the comet made its closest approach to the sun. Throughout November, JUICE's five instruments observed the comet and collected data. However, data transmission was slower due to JUICE's position relative to Earth.

Official statements

“The bright, egg-shape glow at the center of the image is the comet's coma, sculpted by radiation from the sun and the prevailing solar wind,” said a spokesperson from the European Space Agency.

Context

The detection of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is part of ongoing efforts to study objects from outside our solar system. Previous interstellar objects, such as 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have provided valuable insights into the formation of planetary systems beyond our own.

What’s next

Researchers will analyze the data from JUICE and will gather in late March 2026 to discuss their findings regarding the comet's characteristics and behavior.

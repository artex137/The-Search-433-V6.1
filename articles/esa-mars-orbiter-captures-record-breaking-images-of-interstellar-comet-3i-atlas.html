ESA Mars Orbiter Captures Record-Breaking Images of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

The European Space Agency (ESA) has released the closest-ever spacecraft images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from the orbit of Mars, marking a significant advancement in planetary defense tracking. The images provide astronomers with a unique perspective of the comet, which has been under observation globally since its discovery on July 1, 2025.

This development is important as it enhances the understanding of the comet's trajectory and physical behavior. The ESA's Mars-based measurement campaign is the first of its kind, providing a second vantage point for researchers studying interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Type: Third confirmed interstellar object
  • Previous Interstellar Objects: 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov
  • Agency: European Space Agency (ESA)

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, and has since been monitored by various global observatories. The ESA initiated a measurement campaign from Mars to capture detailed images of the comet, which have now been released, providing critical data on its trajectory and behavior.

Official statements

“The release of these images marks a major step in planetary-defense tracking,” said a spokesperson from the European Space Agency.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar object confirmed by astronomers, following the discoveries of 1I/'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. The study of such comets is crucial for understanding the composition and behavior of objects originating outside the solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the images and data collected from the ESA's Mars-based campaign to further refine their understanding of 3I/ATLAS and its implications for planetary defense strategies.

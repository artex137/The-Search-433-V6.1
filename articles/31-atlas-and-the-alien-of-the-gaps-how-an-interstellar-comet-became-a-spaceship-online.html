31/ATLAS and the “Alien of the Gaps”: How an Interstellar Comet Became a Spaceship Online

In the summer of 2025, the discovery of interstellar comet 31/ATLAS has generated extensive online speculation regarding its origins and nature. Researchers at the University of Washington’s Center for an Informed Public have noted that this reaction aligns with a phenomenon they describe as the “Alien of the Gaps.” There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this discovery.

This matter is significant as it reflects how scientific uncertainty can lead to extraordinary claims, particularly in the realm of social media. Authorities, including NASA, have been monitoring the discussions surrounding 31/ATLAS, amidst concerns about the spread of misinformation.

Key facts

  • Discovery date: July 1, 2025
  • Object designation: C/2025 N1 (ATLAS)
  • Confirmed as the third interstellar object observed in the Solar System
  • Research conducted by the University of Washington’s Center for an Informed Public
  • Term "Alien of the Gaps" coined by Mert Can Bayar

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers funded by NASA, working with the ATLAS survey, logged a faint object that was later designated C/2025 N1 (ATLAS). Within days, this object was confirmed as the third interstellar object observed passing through the Solar System.

As news of the discovery spread, social media platforms saw a surge in speculation regarding the object's potential as alien technology. This led to claims that 31/ATLAS could be an alien “mothership,” a notion that researchers at the University of Washington have linked to the "Alien of the Gaps" phenomenon.

Official statements

“The reactions to 31/ATLAS reflect a broader pattern of how we fill scientific uncertainties with extraordinary explanations,” said Mert Can Bayar, a postdoctoral scholar at the Center.

Context

The "Alien of the Gaps" concept is a modern adaptation of traditional “god of the gaps” reasoning, where gaps in scientific knowledge are filled with extraordinary claims. This pattern has been observed in various scientific discussions, particularly where public understanding of complex topics is limited.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue monitoring the discourse surrounding 31/ATLAS, aiming to address misinformation while promoting accurate scientific understanding of interstellar objects.

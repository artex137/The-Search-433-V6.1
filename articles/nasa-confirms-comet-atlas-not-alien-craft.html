NASA Confirms Comet 3I/ATLAS, Not Alien Craft

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) has confirmed that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS is a comet, not an alien spacecraft, following speculation about its origins. NASA released a photograph of the comet on November 19, 2023, to clarify its identity amid rumors circulating on social media platforms.

This confirmation matters as it addresses public concern and misinformation regarding the nature of the object. NASA aims to educate the public about interstellar comets and dispel myths about extraterrestrial life. The agency has emphasized the scientific importance of 3I/ATLAS as the third interstellar object detected to date.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • First observed: July 2023
  • NASA image release date: November 19, 2023
  • Confirmed as a comet: Yes
  • Previous interstellar objects: Oumuamua (2017), Borisov (2019)

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first observed in July 2023. Following its observation, it garnered attention due to its sudden brightness, leading some to speculate that it might be an alien craft. On November 19, 2023, NASA released a photograph of the comet and issued a statement confirming its identity as a comet. This clarification aimed to dispel ongoing rumors about the object's extraterrestrial nature.

Official statements

"I will first address the rumors about Atlas’s identity. This object is a comet, and all data collected by NASA confirms that Atlas is a comet." — Amit Kshatriya, NASA’s deputy director
"Atlas is an interstellar comet. It might have originated from something that existed before our solar system formed. It’s truly fascinating." — Nicola Fox, NASA’s deputy administrator for the Science Mission Directorate

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS is significant as it adds to the understanding of interstellar objects. Previous interstellar objects, Oumuamua and Borisov, have provided insights into the composition and characteristics of celestial bodies from outside the solar system. NASA's clarification about 3I/ATLAS helps counteract misinformation and promotes scientific literacy regarding such phenomena.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system. The agency will share further findings and data related to the comet’s trajectory and characteristics as they become available.

JD Vance Reveals MAGA 'Best Friend' Who Is 'Very Interested' In UFOs

Published 2025-10-29 23:42 UTC

JD Vance Reveals Interest in UFOs with Marco Rubio

Vice President JD Vance has disclosed his friendship with fellow Republican Marco Rubio and their mutual interest in unidentified flying objects (UFOs) during an interview on the New York Post’s “Pod Force One” podcast on Wednesday. Vance's comments come amid speculation about the political future of both men as potential GOP presidential candidates for 2028.

This revelation is notable as it highlights the camaraderie within the Trump administration and introduces a lighter topic into the political discourse. Vance's acknowledgment of his interest in UFOs, or unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), reflects a growing curiosity about this subject among some political figures.

Key facts

  • Event: Interview on the “Pod Force One” podcast
  • Date: Wednesday
  • Location: Not disclosed
  • Participants: JD Vance, Marco Rubio, Miranda Devine
  • Topics discussed: Friendship, UFOs, potential presidential candidacy

Timeline

During the podcast, Vance stated that he has never experienced tension with Rubio, emphasizing their friendship and collaboration in the Trump administration. He mentioned that they often work together, contributing to the administration's efforts.

The discussion shifted to UFOs after Devine, identifying herself as a UFO enthusiast, prompted Vance about his previous comments on his interest in UFO videos. Vance admitted he has not yet delved deeply into the topic but expressed a desire to explore it further.

Official statements

“Marco is my best friend in the administration, and he and I work a lot together,” Vance said. “We really do, I think … a lot of the good work that we’ve been able to do in this administration is because we’re all able to work together.”
“I haven’t had the time to figure it out yet … I really want to sort of dig into it,” Vance added regarding his interest in UFOs.
“There’s certainly an interest there and an intrigue in the subject of extraterrestrial life,” Vance noted.

Context

The discussion surrounding UFOs has gained traction in recent years, with various government agencies acknowledging the need for transparency on the topic. Vance's comments reflect a broader trend among politicians who are beginning to take the subject seriously, often linking it to national security and scientific inquiry.

What’s next

Vance has indicated a commitment to further investigate UFO phenomena, though he has not provided a specific timeline for when he plans to do so. The political landscape will continue to evolve as the 2028 presidential election approaches, with both Vance and Rubio potentially playing significant roles.

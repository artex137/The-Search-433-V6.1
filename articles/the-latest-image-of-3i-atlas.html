The Latest Image of 3I/ATLAS

The latest image of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has been released today, taken on November 5, 2025, by the Lowell Discovery Telescope in Arizona. The image shows 3I/ATLAS as a bright ball of light, with no injuries or fatalities reported.

This image is significant as it provides new data about 3I/ATLAS, which is currently 1.38 astronomical units (au) from the Sun and 2.23 au from Earth. Authorities, including NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), are analyzing the lack of a cometary tail, which contradicts expectations for a natural comet near perihelion.

Key facts

  • Image taken on November 5, 2025.
  • Location: Lowell Discovery Telescope, Arizona.
  • Distance from the Sun: 1.38 au.
  • Distance from Earth: 2.23 au.
  • No evidence of a cometary tail observed.
  • Brightness detected with a green filter optimized for diatomic carbon molecules (C2).

Timeline

On July 21, 2025, the Hubble Space Telescope imaged 3I/ATLAS, capturing an elongation in the sunward direction, indicating an anti-tail. The angle between 3I/ATLAS and the Sun was 10 degrees at that time.

As of November 5, 2025, the object was about 13 degrees from the Sun in the sky. Future observations will allow astronomers to determine whether 3I/ATLAS has a cometary tail as it moves further away from the Sun.

Official statements

“This new image does not show evidence for a dust tail pointing away from the Sun, as expected from massive evaporation of a natural comet near perihelion,” said a spokesperson from NASA’s JPL.

Context

3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object, and its study has generated significant interest in the scientific community. Previous reports have indicated non-gravitational acceleration, raising questions about its composition and behavior.

The absence of a cometary tail, despite expectations, adds to the intrigue surrounding this object, which has been the subject of numerous studies and discussions.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS moves further from the Sun, astronomers anticipate new data that may clarify the presence of a cometary tail and provide insights into the object's nature and origin.

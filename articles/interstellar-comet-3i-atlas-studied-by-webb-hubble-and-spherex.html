Interstellar comet 3I ATLAS studied by Webb, Hubble and SPHEREx

NASA has conducted extensive observations of interstellar comet 3I ATLAS using the James Webb Space Telescope, the Hubble Space Telescope, and the SPHEREx mission from August 7 through 15. No injuries or fatalities have been reported as a result of these observations.

This collaborative effort is significant as it aims to create a comprehensive profile of the comet's composition, size, and physical features. According to NASA, coordinated observations from multiple telescopes enhance their ability to identify and track objects entering the solar system from beyond, supporting planetary defense and advancing understanding of solar system evolution.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I ATLAS
  • Observation Period: August 7 to August 15
  • Missions Involved: James Webb Space Telescope, Hubble Space Telescope, SPHEREx
  • Data Availability: Research note based on SPHEREx findings is publicly accessible
  • Threat Level: No danger to Earth

Timeline

NASA's observation efforts began on August 7 and continued until August 15, during which SPHEREx tracked the comet. The James Webb Space Telescope and Hubble Space Telescope have also contributed data to this ongoing research.

These observations are part of a broader initiative to deepen the understanding of interstellar objects and their characteristics.

Official statements

“While comet 3I/ATLAS presents no danger to Earth, the importance of coordinated telescope observations cannot be overstated,” NASA stated. “This approach supports planetary defense and deepens knowledge of the solar system's evolution.”

Context

Interstellar comets like 3I ATLAS are rare, providing valuable insights into the materials and conditions that existed in the early solar system. Previous studies of interstellar objects have highlighted the need for enhanced observational capabilities to track and understand these visitors.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze the data collected from these observations to further characterize comet 3I ATLAS and enhance understanding of interstellar objects. Further studies and findings are expected to be released in the future.

C/2025 V1 Borisov is racing toward the sun with 3I/ATLAS-style oddness, and the answers are nowhere in sight

Published 2025-11-12 14:31 UTC

C/2025 V1 Borisov Racing Towards the Sun

C/2025 V1 Borisov, a newly discovered comet, has passed Earth's orbit and is expected to reach the sun on November 16, 2025. The comet was discovered on November 2 by Gennadiy Borisov and passed Earth’s orbit at approximately 103 million kilometers on November 11.

Why This Matters

The observations of C/2025 V1 Borisov are significant for astronomers as they seek to gather more data to refine its orbit and understand its composition. Early images indicate a compact coma and a faint tail, leading to comparisons with the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The data collected will help clarify the comet's origins and activity as it approaches the sun.

Key facts

  • Discovery date: November 2, 2025
  • Closest approach to Earth: November 11, 2025, at about 0.689 AU
  • Perihelion date: November 16, 2025, at approximately 0.463 AU
  • Inclination: 112 to 113 degrees on a retrograde path
  • Eccentricity: Approximately 1.0096
  • Estimated visual magnitude: 13 to 14
  • Best viewing location: Before sunrise in Virgo with larger amateur telescopes

Timeline

The following key events have occurred regarding C/2025 V1 Borisov:

  • November 2, 2025: Comet discovered by Gennadiy Borisov.
  • November 11, 2025: Comet passes Earth's orbit at about 103 million kilometers.
  • November 16, 2025: Expected perihelion, passing closest to the sun at approximately 0.463 AU.

Official Statements

“The orbital eccentricity of C/2025 V1 is 1.0095 with an uncertainty of 0.0026,” said Harvard scientist Avi Loeb in a Medium post dated November 9, 2025.
“C/2025 V1 is not related to 3I/ATLAS if it did not employ non-gravitational propulsion,” Loeb added in a report dated November 10, 2025.

Context

C/2025 V1 Borisov is compared to the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS due to its unusual path and tail characteristics. However, while 3I/ATLAS is confirmed as interstellar, C/2025 V1 is believed to be a long-period comet originating from the Oort cloud. Scientists emphasize caution in interpreting the comet's behavior and characteristics, as early observations may change with more data.

What’s Next

Researchers will continue to monitor C/2025 V1 Borisov closely. The priority is to refine its orbit through continued astrometry, secure spectra to analyze its composition, and track brightness changes as it approaches the sun.

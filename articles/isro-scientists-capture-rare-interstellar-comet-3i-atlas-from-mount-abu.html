ISRO scientists capture rare interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS from Mount Abu

ISRO scientists capture rare interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS from Mount Abu

ISRO scientists have successfully captured images of the rare interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS from Mount Abu, India, on November 19, 2025. This significant achievement marks the first time the comet has been observed from the Indian subcontinent.

The capture of Comet 3I/ATLAS is important for astronomical research as it provides insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar objects. ISRO has stated that this observation will contribute to the understanding of the origins of comets and their trajectories in space.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: November 19, 2025
  • Location: Mount Abu, India
  • Agency involved: Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
  • Comet designation: 3I/ATLAS
  • Significance: First observation of the comet from the Indian subcontinent

Timeline

On November 19, 2025, ISRO scientists utilized advanced imaging techniques to observe Comet 3I/ATLAS from Mount Abu. The imaging was part of ongoing efforts to monitor interstellar objects and enhance India's capabilities in astronomical research.

The comet, identified as 3I/ATLAS, has been on a trajectory through the solar system, and its observation from India adds to the global efforts to study such celestial bodies.

Official statements

“The successful capture of Comet 3I/ATLAS demonstrates our commitment to advancing astronomical research in India,” said an ISRO spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is categorized as an interstellar object, which means it originates from outside the solar system. Observations of such comets are rare and can provide valuable data on the formation of planetary systems. ISRO's capabilities in this field have been growing, with previous observations of celestial phenomena enhancing India's reputation in space science.

What’s next

ISRO plans to analyze the data collected from the observation and share findings with the global scientific community to further research on interstellar objects and their implications for understanding the universe.

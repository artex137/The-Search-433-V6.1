'It's coming right for us!': Witness tells Congress of UFO sighting

Witnesses have testified before the U.S. Congress about their experiences with unidentified flying objects (UFOs) during a hearing on "Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena" held on October 10, 2023. Five witnesses, including military veterans, shared their accounts of UFO sightings, but there have been no reported injuries or fatalities.

This hearing is significant as it marks a continued effort by U.S. authorities to investigate and understand these phenomena. The testimonies could influence future policies regarding national security and aerial phenomena, as well as public perception of UFOs.

Key facts

  • Date of hearing: October 10, 2023
  • Location: U.S. Congress
  • Number of witnesses: Five
  • Notable sightings: Objects seen in 2003 and 2023
  • NASA statement: No credible evidence of extraterrestrial life

Timeline

During the hearing, witnesses recounted significant incidents involving UFO sightings. One witness described a 2003 event, recalling his friends screaming, “It's coming right for us!” Another witness reported observing a "self-luminous" object rising from the ocean while stationed on a U.S. Navy ship in 2023. This object joined three other similar objects before speeding away and disappearing.

Official statements

“It's coming right for us!”

— Witness recalling a 2003 UFO sighting

“NASA has not found any credible evidence of extraterrestrial life and there is no evidence that UAPs are extraterrestrial.”

— NASA statement

Context

This hearing on UAPs follows a series of previous incidents where military personnel have reported sightings of unidentified objects. The U.S. government has been increasingly transparent about these occurrences, reflecting a trend towards greater openness regarding national security and aerial surveillance.

What’s next

Following the testimonies, U.S. authorities are expected to continue investigating these reports and may consider further hearings or studies to better understand UAPs and their implications for national security.

Published 2026-01-06 09:58 UTC

NASA Discovers 'Cloud 9' Astronomical Object

NASA has announced the discovery of a new astronomical object, nicknamed Cloud 9, which challenges existing categories of celestial bodies. The discovery was made using the Hubble Space Telescope in February 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This finding is significant as it may reshape our understanding of cosmic structures. Scientists believe Cloud 9 could represent a failed or nascent galaxy, a remnant from the early Universe that did not develop enough mass to form stars. NASA and the European Space Agency have mobilized to study this object further.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: February 2025
  • Object Name: Cloud 9
  • Identified By: NASA's Hubble Space Telescope
  • Location: Not disclosed
  • Characteristics: Starless, gas-rich, dark-matter cloud
  • Potential Significance: May represent a remnant from the early Universe

Timeline

In mid-2025, astronomers detected the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, designated C/2025 N1 (ATLAS). The object was identified by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System telescope in Chile. Observations confirmed its cometary nature and unique composition.

In February 2025, researchers using the Hubble Space Telescope discovered Cloud 9, a starless gas cloud that challenges known classifications of celestial objects.

Official statements

“Cloud 9 may represent a failed or nascent galaxy, a remnant from the early Universe that never gathered enough mass to ignite stellar formation,” said scientists from NASA and the European Space Agency.

Context

Prior to the discovery of Cloud 9, 3I/ATLAS had captured public attention due to its unusual features and the surrounding conspiracy theories regarding its origin. While many speculated about its nature, scientists maintained that it is a natural interstellar comet.

Cloud 9's discovery adds to the growing body of research on dark matter and its role in the formation of cosmic structures, providing a potentially new avenue of exploration in astronomy.

What’s next

NASA and the European Space Agency plan to conduct further observations and studies of Cloud 9 to better understand its properties and implications for our knowledge of the Universe.

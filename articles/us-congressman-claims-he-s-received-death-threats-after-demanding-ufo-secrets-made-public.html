US Congressman Claims He's Received Death Threats After Demanding UFO Secrets Made Public

Rep. Eric Burlison from Montana has reported receiving death threats after advocating for the disclosure of information regarding Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP), commonly known as UFOs. Burlison made these claims during an interview with The Anomalous Coalition, a YouTube channel focused on UAP discussions, alongside filmmaker James Fox and researcher Leslie Lean.

This situation has raised concerns about the safety of those pushing for transparency in government dealings with UAPs. Authorities, including the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), are reportedly coordinating with the White House to manage existing UAP records and expedite the release of new information related to these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Rep. Eric Burlison is a member of Congress from Montana.
  • Burlison has received death threats from unnamed Army Special Force and Intelligence Community staff.
  • He has joined other Congress members in urging the White House to release specific UAP files.
  • The letter to White House officials has not been publicly released.
  • Notable signatories include Anna Paulina Luna, Tim Burchett, Nancy Mace, and others.
  • The Pentagon’s AARO is working to consolidate UAP records.
  • Former President Donald Trump has initiated an investigation into UAPs.

Timeline

In September 2022, Rep. Eric Burlison participated in a House Oversight Committee hearing where he showcased a video of an "orb" that appeared unaffected after being struck by an MQ-9 hellfire missile off the coast of Yemen. This event marked a significant moment in his ongoing campaign for UAP transparency.

In the following months, growing public interest in UAPs led to former President Donald Trump announcing an investigation into UAPs on his Truth Social platform. This announcement aimed to direct federal agencies to identify and release pertinent government files.

Recently, during an interview with The Anomalous Coalition, Burlison revealed the death threats he has received, stating they came from unnamed staff members associated with U.S. military intelligence.

Official statements

“They said, ‘You need to take those 2 names off your list and never talk about [them] again. They'd have no problem having you killed,’” Burlison stated regarding the threats he received.
“Trump is the disclosure President,” Burlison told The Anomalous Coalition, emphasizing the importance of transparency in government dealings with UAPs.
“The AARO is working in close coordination with the White House and across federal agencies to consolidate existing UAP records collections and facilitate the expeditious release of never-before-seen UAP information,” a Pentagon representative said.

Context

Interest in UAPs has surged in recent years, particularly following public statements from former officials and the establishment of the AARO. The recent push for transparency is partly a reaction to comments made by former President Barack Obama, who suggested the existence of unknown objects during a podcast appearance.

Rep. Burlison has been a vocal advocate for UAP whistleblower protection, providing a platform for individuals to share their experiences related to unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue their investigations into UAPs, with the AARO working on consolidating records and facilitating the release of new information. The ongoing situation regarding Burlison’s safety and the implications of his claims remain under review.

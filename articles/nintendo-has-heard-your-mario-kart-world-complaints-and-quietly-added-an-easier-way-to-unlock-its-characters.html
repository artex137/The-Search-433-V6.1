Nintendo Has Heard Your Mario Kart World Complaints And Quietly Added An Easier Way To Unlock Its Characters

Published 2025-09-25 14:48 UTC

Nintendo Addresses Mario Kart World Player Concerns with New Update

Nintendo has released a significant update for Mario Kart World on Wednesday, enhancing gameplay and addressing player feedback from the game's first three months. The update, version 1.3.0, introduces new mechanics for unlocking characters and increases the frequency of UFOs in the game.

This update is crucial as it responds to player frustrations regarding character unlocks and race formats. The changes aim to improve user experience by making it easier to access characters and increasing the variety of race types available to players.

Key facts

  • Update version: 1.3.0
  • Release date: Wednesday
  • New character unlock method: Abducting characters using UFOs
  • Increased UFO appearance rates: Confirmed by player reports
  • More frequent three-lap races: Player feedback prompted this change

Timeline

Mario Kart World launched three months ago, prompting player feedback regarding character unlocks and race formats. On Wednesday, Nintendo released the 1.3.0 update, which included changes to UFO mechanics and race frequency. Players quickly began testing the new features, confirming increased UFO appearances and the new character unlock method.

Official statements

Nintendo has made a concerted effort to enhance player experience by addressing concerns regarding character accessibility and race variety.

Context

Prior to this update, unlocking certain characters in Mario Kart World was largely dependent on chance, involving the Kamek item, which transformed racers into random characters. The introduction of the UFO method allows for a more strategic approach to character unlocks.

What’s next

Players will likely continue to provide feedback on the effectiveness of the new features. Nintendo may implement further updates based on user experiences and reactions to the recent changes.

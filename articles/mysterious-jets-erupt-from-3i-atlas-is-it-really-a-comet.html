Mysterious Jets Erupt from 3I/ATLAS—Is It Really a Comet?

A mysterious interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS has sparked speculation about its origins after new images revealed complex jets emanating from its surface. The latest images were captured on November 8, 2025, and show at least seven distinct jets, some directed toward the Sun, challenging conventional comet behavior.

This discovery is significant as it raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS, the third known interstellar object to pass through our solar system since its discovery in July 2025. Notably, Harvard astrophysicist Dr. Avi Loeb has suggested the possibility of artificial propulsion, increasing public interest and scientific debate. With 3I/ATLAS making its closest approach to Earth in December 2025, scientists are preparing for further observations to better understand this object.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery: July 2025
  • Images captured: November 8, 2025
  • Number of jets observed: At least seven
  • Distance of jets: More than half a million kilometers
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 2025
  • Primary theorist: Dr. Avi Loeb, Harvard University

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025, quickly capturing the attention of astronomers. Initial observations indicated it was a typical comet. However, the latest images taken on November 8, 2025, revealed unusual jets, prompting renewed speculation about its nature.

As the object approaches Earth in December 2025, scientists plan to gather more data to investigate its characteristics further.

Official statements

“The latest developments have pushed the debate into the public eye,” said Dr. Avi Loeb, emphasizing the need for more high-resolution images to determine the object's nature.
NASA has stated that 3I/ATLAS is most likely a natural object and poses no threat to Earth.

Context

The debate surrounding 3I/ATLAS is not unprecedented; other interstellar objects have also raised questions about their origins. The possibility of extraterrestrial technology has been a topic of interest within the scientific community, though skepticism remains prevalent.

Dr. Loeb's arguments have captivated both scientists and the public, highlighting the need for continued observation and analysis of such objects.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches Earth, scientists will have more opportunities to gather data and conduct observations. The results of these studies may help clarify whether 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet or something more extraordinary.

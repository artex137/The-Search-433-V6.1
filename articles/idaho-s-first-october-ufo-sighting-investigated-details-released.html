Idaho's First October UFO Sighting Investigated, Details Released

The National UFO Reporting Center has investigated a reported sighting of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) near Caldwell, Idaho, that occurred on October 4 at approximately 5 A.M. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this sighting.

This sighting is significant as it adds to a growing list of UAP reports in southern Idaho, a region known for its high number of sightings, which some attribute to the proximity of Mountain Home Air Force Base. Authorities are currently reviewing the details of the incident and have encouraged any witnesses to come forward.

Key facts

  • Incident date: October 4, 2023
  • Time of sighting: Approximately 5 A.M.
  • Location: Near Caldwell, Idaho, 27 miles west of Boise
  • Reported to: National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC) on October 5
  • Witness description: Object with a 'haze' and emitting 'other objects'
  • Photographic evidence: Included in the report

Timeline

On October 4, 2023, at around 5 A.M., a solo witness observed a UAP near Caldwell, Idaho. The sighting was reported to the National UFO Reporting Center the following day, October 5. The witness provided a description of the object and included a photograph in the report.

Official statements

“Southern Idaho has a rich history of reported UAP sightings,” a representative from the NUFORC stated. “Many believe the high number of witness reports in the region has something to do with Mountain Home Air Force Base.”

Context

Southern Idaho has seen numerous reports of UAPs over the years, contributing to ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena. The recent surge in sightings has coincided with the release of previously confidential military footage and documents regarding UAPs, drawing public attention to the topic.

What’s next

The National UFO Reporting Center will continue its investigation into the Caldwell sighting, and authorities are urging anyone who may have seen anything unusual in the sky on the morning of October 4 to report their experiences.

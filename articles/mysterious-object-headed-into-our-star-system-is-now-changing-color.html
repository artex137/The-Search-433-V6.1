Mysterious Object Headed Into Our Star System Is Now Changing Color

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS is nearing its closest point to the Sun next month, captivating astronomers with its evolving characteristics. The comet, believed to originate from outside the solar system, is exhibiting changes in shape, including a longer tail and a more pronounced coma as it approaches the Sun.

This phenomenon is significant as it provides scientists with a unique opportunity to study a rare visitor from beyond our solar system. Astronomers have observed unusual properties of 3I/ATLAS that may take years to fully understand. Recent observations during a total lunar eclipse have revealed that the comet's coma has shifted from emitting red light to green, suggesting a possible increase in cyanide production.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Current location: Approaching the Sun
  • Next closest point to the Sun: Next month
  • Observation location: Namibia
  • Key observers: Michael Jäger and colleagues
  • Composition: 87% carbon dioxide

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first identified by the ATLAS telescope team, which led to its naming. As it approached the Sun, astronomers noted changes in its tail and coma. Recently, during a total lunar eclipse, researchers in Namibia captured detailed images of the comet, revealing its color shift from red to green.

Official statements

“The coma has now transitioned from giving off red light to green, yet another fascinating wrinkle in the rare interstellar visitor's odyssey through our solar system,” said Michael Jäger in a forum exchange.
“While Loeb has repeatedly raised the possibility that 3I/ATLAS might be an extraterrestrial artifact, observations are building a stronger case that it's merely a comet,” noted Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb.

Context

The study of interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS is rare and provides insights into the composition and behavior of celestial bodies from outside our solar system. Previous observations of such objects have led to significant discoveries, but 3I/ATLAS presents unique challenges and characteristics that set it apart from familiar comets.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey through the solar system, scientists will monitor its behavior and composition closely, with further studies planned to understand its unusual properties and trajectory.

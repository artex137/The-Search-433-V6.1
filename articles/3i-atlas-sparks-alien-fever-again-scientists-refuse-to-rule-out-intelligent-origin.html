3I/ATLAS Sparks Alien Fever Again: Scientists Refuse to Rule Out Intelligent Origin

Astronomers have detected an unusual interstellar object named 3I/ATLAS, which passed closest to Earth on December 19, 2023, at a distance of 267 million kilometres. While most scientists believe it is a natural comet, some researchers have not ruled out the possibility that it could be artificial in origin.

The arrival of 3I/ATLAS has prompted significant interest and debate within the scientific community. The Breakthrough Listen project, a global initiative aimed at detecting signs of intelligent life, has focused on the object, deploying the Green Bank Telescope to search for any potential electronic signals. However, the results have so far indicated no evidence of artificial emissions.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2023
  • Distance at closest approach: 267 million kilometres
  • Telescope used: Green Bank Telescope
  • Project: Breakthrough Listen
  • Findings: No artificial radio emissions detected

Timeline

In July 2023, astronomers identified 3I/ATLAS as it entered the solar system. Following its discovery, it quickly became a topic of discussion among scientists regarding its nature and origin.

On December 18, 2023, the Green Bank Telescope conducted a listening session aimed at detecting any possible signals from the object. However, results published in a preliminary paper indicate that no candidate signals were found.

The Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence Institute (SETI) confirmed the lack of evidence, stating the object appears to behave as expected for a natural astrophysical object.

Official statements

No artificial radio emission localised to 3I/ATLAS was detected. The data gathered suggests that the object is silent, cold, and likely lifeless.
It remains an extremely interesting target for observation given the overall rarity of interstellar objects.
I have painstakingly documented what I term 'anomalies' in the object's behaviour and appearance, including its suspected large size and finely-tuned trajectory.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar object detected in our solar system. Previous objects such as 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov have also sparked discussions about the possibility of extraterrestrial life. The ongoing exploration of interstellar objects continues to fuel interest in the search for technosignatures.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyse data from 3I/ATLAS, with a full dataset expected to be released in the coming months. The scientific community remains engaged in monitoring and studying this unique object.

NASA’s SPHEREx Space Telescope Observes Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA's SPHEREx (Spectro-Photometer for the History of the Universe, Epoch of Reionization and Ices Explorer) has observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from August 7 to August 15. There have been no reports of injuries or fatalities related to this observation.

The observations are significant as they provide insights into the comet's size, physical characteristics, and chemical compositions. The mission team is analyzing the data as part of a collaborative effort with other NASA space telescopes, including Webb and Hubble. This research enhances understanding of 3I/ATLAS while confirming that it poses no threat to Earth.

Key facts

  • Observation period: August 7 to August 15
  • NASA's SPHEREx is part of a larger observational campaign with Webb and Hubble
  • No threat to Earth from comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Data includes insights into the comet's size, characteristics, and compositions
  • A research note with preliminary data is now accessible online

Timeline

NASA's SPHEREx began its observations of 3I/ATLAS on August 7 and concluded them on August 15. The mission team has since started analyzing the collected data.

As part of a wider collaborative effort, SPHEREx's observations complement findings from other NASA telescopes like Webb and Hubble.

Official statements

“The insights gained from the observations contribute to NASA's ongoing mission to find, track, and enhance our understanding of celestial objects within our solar system,” said a NASA representative.

Context

This observation of 3I/ATLAS is part of a broader initiative by NASA to study interstellar objects. Previous observations of comets have provided vital information regarding their origins and compositions, enhancing our understanding of the solar system.

What’s next

The scientific community anticipates further analysis of the data collected from SPHEREx, with expectations of new knowledge regarding interstellar objects and their implications for our understanding of the universe.

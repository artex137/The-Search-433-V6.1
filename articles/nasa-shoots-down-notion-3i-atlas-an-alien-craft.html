NASA shoots down notion 3I/Atlas an alien craft

NASA has confirmed that comet 3I/ATLAS is not an alien spacecraft, following speculation about its origins. The announcement was made on November 21, 2025, amid growing interest in the comet's unusual trajectory.

This clarification is significant as it addresses public curiosity and concerns about unidentified objects in space. NASA's statement aims to provide accurate information and dispel myths surrounding the comet.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: November 21, 2025
  • Agency involved: NASA
  • Object in question: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Location of observation: Not disclosed
  • Public interest level: High

Timeline

Speculation about comet 3I/ATLAS began shortly after its discovery. Observers noted its unusual path and raised questions about its nature.

On November 21, 2025, NASA officially stated that the comet is not an alien craft, clarifying its composition and trajectory.

Official statements

“We can confirm that 3I/ATLAS is a comet, not an alien spacecraft. Our observations have shown its natural characteristics,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

The discussion around 3I/ATLAS is part of a broader public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena and space exploration. NASA has been proactive in addressing such inquiries to promote scientific literacy.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system, providing updates to the public as necessary.

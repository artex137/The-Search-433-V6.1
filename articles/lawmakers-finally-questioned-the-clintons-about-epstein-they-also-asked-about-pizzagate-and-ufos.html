Lawmakers finally questioned the Clintons about Epstein. They also asked about pizzagate and UFOs

Published 2026-03-03 04:59 UTC

Lawmakers question Clintons about Epstein and UFOs

WASHINGTON (AP) — A House committee has questioned former President Bill Clinton and former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton about their connections to Jeffrey Epstein during depositions last week. The sessions took place after a six-month investigation into Epstein's activities and revealed unexpected topics, including the pizzagate conspiracy theory and government information on UFOs.

This inquiry is significant as it reflects the ongoing investigation into Epstein's alleged abuse and the broader implications regarding high-profile individuals and their connections. The House Oversight Committee has released videos of the depositions, showcasing the Clintons' responses and the committee's line of questioning.

Key facts

  • Depositions held last week in Washington, D.C.
  • Investigation led by the House Oversight Committee
  • Bill Clinton distanced himself from Epstein's activities
  • Hillary Clinton stated she did not recall meeting Epstein
  • Sessions included discussions on pizzagate and UFO disclosures

Timeline

The House committee has been investigating Jeffrey Epstein for six months. During this time, they sought to question Bill and Hillary Clinton regarding their past interactions with Epstein.

The depositions took place last week, where both Clintons faced inquiries about their connections to Epstein and other unrelated topics, which shifted the focus of the sessions.

Videos of the depositions were released by the House Oversight Committee on Monday, illustrating the discussions and the dynamics between the committee members and the Clintons.

Official statements

“It is important for anyone with information about Epstein's abuse to come forward,” Bill Clinton stated during the questioning.
Hillary Clinton said, “I do not recall meeting Epstein,” in response to multiple inquiries about her past interactions.

Context

This inquiry into Jeffrey Epstein has drawn significant public attention due to Epstein's high-profile connections and the serious nature of the allegations against him. Past incidents involving Epstein have raised questions about accountability and transparency among influential figures.

The inclusion of topics such as pizzagate and UFOs in the questioning reflects the committee's broader mandate and the varied interests of lawmakers in addressing conspiracy theories and government disclosure policies.

What’s next

The House Oversight Committee will continue its investigation into Epstein and related matters, with further hearings and potential follow-up questions planned for both Clintons and other witnesses.

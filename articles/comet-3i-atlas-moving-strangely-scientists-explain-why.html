Comet 3I/ATLAS moving strangely: scientists explain why

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been moving off its expected path in the inner solar system, according to astronomers observing the comet since December 17, 2025. The unusual trajectory has prompted researchers to investigate the forces affecting its motion.

This phenomenon is significant as it challenges existing models of comet behavior and provides insights into the comet's composition and origin. Scientists from multiple observatories are collaborating to analyze the comet's movements and the internal processes driving them.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has shown deviations from a standard gravitational orbit.
  • Observations indicate outgassing, changes in brightness, and the formation of a coma.
  • Telescopes worldwide are monitoring the comet's behavior.
  • Researchers have identified persistent extra acceleration affecting the comet's trajectory.
  • Active processes on the comet include the release of gas and dust.

Timeline

On December 17, 2025, astronomers reported that Comet 3I/ATLAS is veering off its predicted path. Over several weeks, teams have monitored its position, leading to the discovery of systematic deviations from gravitational predictions. Observations have indicated changes in brightness and the emergence of a coma, suggesting active processes on the comet.

Further analysis revealed asymmetric features in images, pointing to localized activity on the comet's surface. These findings have led scientists to conclude that internal and external forces are influencing the comet's motion.

Official statements

“These data show the comet isn’t simply coasting under gravity; internal processes and external forces are changing its motion in measurable ways,” said an astronomer involved in the study.

Context

Comets are known to behave unpredictably as they approach the Sun, often displaying outgassing and changes in brightness. Previous studies have shown that such behavior can result from various factors, including the comet's spin, shape, and fragmentation. The current observations of 3I/ATLAS add to a growing body of research aimed at understanding these dynamics.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS to gather more data on its movements and the forces at play. This ongoing investigation could provide further insights into the comet's structure and origin as it travels through the solar system.

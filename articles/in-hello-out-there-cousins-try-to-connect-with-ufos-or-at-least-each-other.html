In Hello Out There, Cousins Try to Connect With UFOs — or At Least Each Other

Director Otis Blum's film Hello Out There examines the search for connection with aliens and the alienation felt on Earth. The film stars Chloe Bennet as Minnie, a journalist seeking access to Area 51, and Phil Dunster as Rex, her cousin and a punk-rock guitarist. The film screens Friday at the Santa Fe International Film Festival in New Mexico.

This film is significant as it taps into themes of connection during a time when many feel isolated. Blum noted an increase in stories about aliens during the pandemic, suggesting a collective yearning for connection. The film's setting in New Mexico also highlights the state's unique landscapes and culture.

Key facts

  • Film Title: Hello Out There
  • Director: Otis Blum
  • Screening Date: Friday at the Santa Fe International Film Festival
  • Main Cast: Chloe Bennet, Phil Dunster, Jennifer Beals
  • Filming Locations: Roswell, White Sands National Park, Albuquerque, Santa Fe
  • Notable Scene: Gates of Area 51 (filmed at a private airport outside Albuquerque)

Timeline

Otis Blum has expressed a lifelong interest in New Mexico, where he has family ties. He wrote the script with the intent to showcase the beauty of the state. The production team faced logistical challenges while filming across multiple locations, but they successfully completed the shoot with only one weather-related delay. The film incorporates themes of connection and alienation, drawing parallels between human relationships and the search for extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

“I wanted to explore the idea of connection. Is it possible to connect with other beings, and if so how?” – Otis Blum
“During the depths of the pandemic, I noticed an uptick in stories about aliens in traditional publications.” – Otis Blum
“I love New Mexico so much. I’ve been visiting the state since I was a child.” – Otis Blum

Context

The film emerges during a cultural moment where interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life has surged. Blum's observations about the pandemic's impact on storytelling reflect broader societal themes of isolation and the quest for connection.

What’s next

Hello Out There will be showcased at the Santa Fe International Film Festival, where it will reach audiences seeking new cinematic experiences. Future screenings and distribution details are not disclosed.

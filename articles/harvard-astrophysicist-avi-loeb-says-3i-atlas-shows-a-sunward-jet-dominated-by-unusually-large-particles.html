Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb says 3I/ATLAS shows a sunward jet dominated by unusually large particles

Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has published an essay on December 25, 2025, analyzing the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS and its unusual features, particularly its anti-tail. In his essay, published on Medium, Loeb discusses the comet's long, tightly collimated jet, which is directed toward the sun and contains unusually large particles.

This analysis is significant because it challenges existing theories about comet behavior. Loeb's findings may prompt further investigation by the scientific community to understand the physical characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, which do not align with typical comet physics.

Key facts

  • Date of publication: December 25, 2025
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Length of anti-tail: up to 400,000 kilometers
  • Particle size range: larger than 1 micron and smaller than 100 microns

Timeline

Avi Loeb has conducted ongoing research into the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, focusing on its anti-tail and jet structure. His latest findings, published on December 25, 2025, highlight the comet's unusual particle composition and jet behavior.

Loeb has observed that the anti-tail of 3I/ATLAS is not only longer than typical comets but also exhibits a focused stream of material directed toward the sun. This observation has led him to conclude that the particles in the jet are likely larger than those found in conventional comets.

Official statements

“The physical features of 3I/ATLAS are strange and worth looking into,” said Loeb, emphasizing the need for skepticism and further study of the comet.
“Standard physics fails to explain the physical features of the visitor,” Loeb concluded in his essay.

Context

The behavior of comets has been studied extensively, with typical anti-tails being shorter and less focused than that of 3I/ATLAS. Loeb's observations challenge the established understanding of comet physics and suggest that further investigation is necessary to explain the anomalies observed in this interstellar object.

What’s next

Loeb hopes that his findings will encourage other scientists to remain skeptical and continue to explore the unique characteristics of 3I/ATLAS as research into the comet progresses.

ESA’s Juice Probe Captures Active Comet 3I/ATLAS on November 4

The European Space Agency (ESA) has released a new image of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, captured by its Juice probe on November 4, 2025. There are no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this event.

This capture is significant as it occurred shortly after the comet's closest approach to the Sun, a period when comets typically exhibit heightened activity. ESA plans to release the full image in February, which will provide further insights into the behavior of interstellar comets.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 4, 2025
  • Distance of comet from Earth: approximately 66 million kilometers
  • Type of image captured: one-fourth of the Navigation Camera (NavCam) image
  • Expected release of full image: February 2026
  • Juice probe's destination: Jupiter in 2031

Timeline

On November 4, 2025, the Juice probe captured an image of 3I/ATLAS. This event followed the comet's closest approach to the Sun, which typically triggers increased activity in comets.

The image shows a bright coma and two faint tails, indicating the comet's active release of gas and dust. The NavCam, primarily a navigation tool for the probe, managed to capture a scientifically useful image despite the distance.

Official statements

“This new photograph will provide valuable insights into the behavior of interstellar comets,” ESA stated in their announcement.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is of particular interest because it originated outside our solar system. Each new image helps researchers understand how interstellar comets differ from typical comets found within the solar system.

What’s next

ESA will release the full-resolution image of 3I/ATLAS in February 2026, allowing scientists to further analyze the comet's characteristics and behavior.

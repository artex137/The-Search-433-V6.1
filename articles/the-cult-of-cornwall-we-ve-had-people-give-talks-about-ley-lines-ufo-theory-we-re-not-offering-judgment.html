The cult of Cornwall: ‘We’ve had people give talks about ley lines, UFO theory – we’re not offering judgment’

A growing movement in Cornwall, known as Stone Club, has attracted over 4,000 members since its inception in 2021. Founded by artists Lally MacBeth and Matthew Shaw, Stone Club offers a space for enthusiasts of ancient stone sites and alternative theories, including UFOs and ley lines. The club aims to create an inclusive environment for those interested in Cornwall's prehistoric and mystical heritage.

The emergence of Stone Club highlights a resurgence of interest in Cornwall’s ancient history, prompting local authorities and communities to engage with this cultural phenomenon. Events organized by the club, including monthly gatherings in London and pop-up arts events, have drawn participants from across the globe.

Key facts

  • Founded in 2021 by Lally MacBeth and Matthew Shaw
  • Membership has reached over 4,000 individuals worldwide
  • Monthly events held in London
  • Pop-up arts events organized across Cornwall and the UK
  • Focus on prehistoric sites and alternative theories

Timeline

In 2021, Lally MacBeth and Matthew Shaw established Stone Club as a gathering for stone enthusiasts. The club quickly gained popularity, attracting a large membership base. Monthly events were initiated in London, along with various pop-up events in Cornwall, fostering a community interested in exploring the region's ancient stones and related theories.

As interest grew, the club welcomed archaeologists, enhancing its credibility and legitimacy. The discussions have included topics such as ley lines and UFO theories, allowing for a diverse range of perspectives without judgment.

Official statements

“We want to make a space that’s inclusive and welcoming to all,” said MacBeth, reflecting on the club’s mission.

Context

Cornwall has long been a site of interest for those drawn to its rich history and folklore. The region is home to numerous ancient stone sites, which have attracted visitors for centuries. In recent years, there has been a noticeable increase in the popularity of alternative cultural events, as seen with the establishment of Stone Club.

What’s next

Stone Club plans to continue expanding its events and reach, aiming to engage more individuals in discussions about Cornwall’s prehistoric past and its cultural significance. Further collaborations with archaeologists and cultural institutions are anticipated as the movement grows.

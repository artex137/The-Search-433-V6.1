Top secret Air Force jet spotted on mysterious trip to Area 51

A top-secret Air Force jet has landed at Area 51 in the Nevada desert on December 22, 2025, at 8:42 AM PT. The aircraft is part of the military's Janet fleet, which transports contractor employees and military personnel to secure facilities. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This flight is significant because it highlights ongoing military operations at Area 51, a site historically associated with classified aircraft testing and rumored extraterrestrial technologies. Authorities have not disclosed the specific purpose of this flight, but it is one of several recent trips to the facility.

Key facts

  • Flight departed from Harry Reid International Airport, Las Vegas, at 8:25 AM PT.
  • Flight landed at Area 51 at 8:42 AM PT.
  • This flight is one of six Janet flights to Area 51 in the past week.
  • The Janet fleet began operations in 1972, with Area 51 established in 1955.
  • Area 51 features six runways, including a 12,000-foot-long strip.

Timeline

On December 22, 2025, a Janet flight departed from Las Vegas at 8:25 AM PT and arrived at Area 51 by 8:42 AM PT. This flight follows a pattern, as data shows similar flights have taken place daily over the past week, all departing from Las Vegas in the same time frame.

Historically, Area 51 has served as a testing ground for various aircraft, including the U-2 spy plane during the Cold War, and it continues to play a role in modern military operations.

Official statements

“There are a lot of things going on there that I won't be able to tell you until the year 2025,” said Jim Goodall, an aviation journalist, referring to information about Area 51.
“We have things out there that are literally out of this world… better than Star Trek, or anything you can see in the movies,” Goodall recalled from a conversation with a former safety specialist at the site.

Context

Area 51 gained notoriety during the Cold War for its role in testing advanced aircraft, and it has been the subject of numerous conspiracy theories regarding extraterrestrial technologies. The site has been linked to the development of classified military projects, and many believe that significant secrets remain hidden.

In a resurfaced interview, Goodall noted that a conversation with a source indicated that certain government secrets might be declassified following a 25-year timer established by an executive order from former President Bill Clinton.

What’s next

No specific investigations or charges have been announced regarding the recent flights. However, the ongoing secrecy surrounding Area 51 suggests that further developments related to its operations and potential declassifications may unfold in the coming years.

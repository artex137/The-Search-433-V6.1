3I/ATLAS: Europe Activates Largest Planetary Defence Drill for Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

Europe has activated its largest planetary defence drill in response to the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which is currently moving through the solar system. The drill commenced on December 11, 2025, and involves multiple space agencies and scientists coordinating efforts for planetary protection. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the drill.

This drill is significant as it tests readiness for potential threats from space objects. Authorities aim to enhance coordination among international space agencies to prepare for possible future threats. The involvement of various scientific communities underscores the importance of global collaboration in planetary defence initiatives.

Key facts

  • Date of drill activation: December 11, 2025
  • Location: Europe, involving multiple space agencies
  • Focus: Response to interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Objective: Test readiness and coordinate international planetary protection efforts

Timeline

On December 11, 2025, Europe activated its planetary defence drill as 3I/ATLAS approached the solar system. The drill aims to assess the readiness of various space agencies and to enhance international cooperation in dealing with potential cosmic threats.

Official statements

“The drill is designed to test our capabilities and ensure we are prepared for any threats from space,” a spokesperson for the European Space Agency stated.

Context

This drill marks Europe’s largest effort in planetary defence to date. Previous initiatives have focused on assessing threats from asteroids and comets, but this is the first drill of its magnitude specifically addressing interstellar objects.

What’s next

Following the drill, authorities will evaluate the outcomes and effectiveness of the coordinated efforts, with plans for future drills and improvements based on the findings.

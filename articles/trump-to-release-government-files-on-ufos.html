Trump to Release Government Files on UFOs

Former U.S. President Donald Trump has announced plans to release previously classified government files related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs) on February 20, 2026. The announcement was made during a press conference held in Washington, D.C. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities connected to this release.

This move comes amid growing public interest in UFO phenomena and increasing calls for transparency from government agencies. Authorities are currently reviewing the documents to ensure that sensitive information is appropriately redacted before they are made public.

Key facts

  • Event: Release of classified UFO files
  • Date: February 20, 2026
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Involved Agency: U.S. government
  • Current Status: Files under review for redaction

Timeline

In recent years, there has been a notable increase in UFO sightings reported across the United States. In 2020, the Pentagon established the Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP) Task Force to investigate these incidents. Following a series of public reports and government hearings, the demand for more transparency has intensified.

The announcement by Trump marks a significant moment in this ongoing dialogue, as it promises to shed light on previously hidden government information regarding UFOs.

Official statements

“This release will provide the public with insight into what the government knows about UFOs. We believe in transparency,” Trump stated during the press conference.

Context

The issue of UFOs has gained traction in recent years, with various government and military personnel coming forward to discuss their experiences. In 2021, a report released by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence mentioned several unexplained aerial phenomena, further fueling public curiosity.

Public interest in UFOs has historically fluctuated, but recent developments indicate that this topic may remain in the spotlight as more information comes to light.

What’s next

The U.S. government is expected to finalize the review of the classified files before their release. Further announcements regarding the content and implications of these files will follow as the release date approaches.

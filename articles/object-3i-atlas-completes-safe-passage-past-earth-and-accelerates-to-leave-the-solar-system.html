Object 3I/ATLAS completes safe passage past Earth and accelerates to leave the Solar System

Object 3I/ATLAS has successfully passed Earth without incident and is now on a trajectory to leave the Solar System, according to astronomers. The comet made its closest approach to Earth on February 28, 2026, at approximately 10:30 UTC.

This event is significant for the scientific community as it provides an opportunity to study the comet’s composition and trajectory. Researchers from various space agencies are analyzing data collected during the flyby to enhance understanding of cometary behavior and the formation of the Solar System.

Key facts

  • Object 3I/ATLAS passed Earth on February 28, 2026, at 10:30 UTC.
  • The closest approach distance was approximately 5.1 million kilometers.
  • The comet's speed increased as it accelerated away from the Sun.
  • Data is being analyzed by astronomers from NASA and ESA.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported during the event.

Timeline

Object 3I/ATLAS was first observed by astronomers in 2021. After a series of calculations, its trajectory was confirmed, leading to predictions of its closest approach to Earth.

On February 28, 2026, the comet reached its closest point to Earth, allowing scientists to gather valuable data as it passed. This flyby marks a key moment in its journey out of the Solar System.

Official statements

“The successful passage of 3I/ATLAS provides us with a unique opportunity to learn more about these celestial objects,” said Dr. Emily Chen, a leading astronomer at NASA.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a larger group of comets that have been studied as they approach the inner Solar System. Previous encounters with comets have led to significant discoveries about their structures and origins.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from the flyby over the coming months to draw conclusions about the comet's physical and chemical properties. Further studies may lead to new insights into the formation of comets and the early Solar System.

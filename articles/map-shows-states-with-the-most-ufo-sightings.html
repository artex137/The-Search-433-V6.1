Map Shows States With the Most UFO Sightings

California, Florida, and Texas have reported the highest number of UFO sightings in the United States, according to data from Enigma Labs. The data, which reflects crowdsourced reports of unidentified flying objects, shows California leading with over 30,000 sightings, followed by Florida with more than 16,000 and Texas with over 14,000. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these sightings.

This data is significant as interest in UFOs, now referred to as unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) by the federal government, has surged in recent years. Congressional hearings, Pentagon reports, and the release of previously classified military footage have contributed to this increased interest. Researchers indicate that the heightened attention may be driving more public reports, especially in populous states with active online communities.

Key facts

  • California: more than 30,000 sightings
  • Florida: over 16,000 sightings
  • Texas: more than 14,000 sightings
  • New York: over 11,600 sightings
  • Enigma Labs has published more than 33,000 U.S. sightings
  • Top 10 states also include Washington, Pennsylvania, Arizona, Ohio, Illinois, and Michigan
  • States with the fewest sightings include Wyoming, South Dakota, North Dakota, and Delaware

Timeline

Enigma Labs launched its website and mobile app in 2022 and 2023, allowing the public to submit reports of UFO sightings. The database has since compiled thousands of reports, reflecting ongoing public interest and engagement. The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office is currently examining over 2,000 UAP cases.

In recent years, federal officials have acknowledged the need for further investigation into unidentified anomalous phenomena. The government continues to analyze reports and plans to release additional records related to UAPs.

Official statements

“I am convinced that UFOs exist because I have seen one,” said former President Jimmy Carter about a 1969 sighting.
“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs),” stated President Donald Trump on Truth Social in February.

Context

While populous states report higher numbers of UFO sightings, researchers caution that many reports are ultimately identified as aircraft, drones, satellites, weather balloons, or rocket launches. This trend is particularly evident in states with significant military installations or active air traffic.

Enigma Labs has stated that not every report is considered credible or verified. The platform reviews submissions, filtering out hoaxes and duplicate entries, but the public totals reflect published sightings rather than confirmed unidentified phenomena.

What’s next

The Pentagon's examination of UAP cases is ongoing, with more reports under analysis. Additional records related to unidentified anomalous phenomena are expected to be released publicly in the future.

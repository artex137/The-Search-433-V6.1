Mysterious 'crashed disc' spotted on Google Earth fuels UFO cover-up theories in New Mexico

Images of a mysterious disc found on Google Earth have sparked new theories related to alien activity in New Mexico. The images, originating from the state's high desert, appear to show a luminous white or silver object wedged in a hillside. Recent comparative studies indicate that the object is no longer visible, leading to speculation about possible government efforts to obscure genuine alien-related imagery.

The apparent disappearance of the disc has prompted discussions online regarding potential cover-ups. Observers have noted that the pattern of vegetation surrounding the object remains consistent in earlier images, which some argue suggests alterations have been made to the location. Reports of similar altered images have emerged beyond New Mexico, with satellite enthusiasts identifying instances in California and Baja Norte.

  • Images of a disc object first identified in New Mexico's high desert.
  • Recent images show the object is no longer visible, replaced by buildings or blurred areas.
  • Speculation of government cover-ups has arisen from the object's disappearance.
  • Similar altered imagery has been reported in California and Baja Norte.
  • Changes in Google Earth imagery can result from various factors, including security adjustments.

Timeline

Images of the mysterious disc first appeared on Google Earth, drawing attention from UFO enthusiasts. As users began to analyze the images, they noted the object's apparent disappearance in newer imagery. This has led to increased speculation about potential alterations made by government entities. Concurrently, reports of similar occurrences in other regions have surfaced, broadening the discussion around altered satellite imagery.

Official statements

“Changes in Google Earth are typical and can stem from several factors, from newer satellite data to image resolution adjustments to security-related redactions,” said an analyst.
“The frenzy among alien believers has not subsided,” noted a commenter in an online forum.

Context

Prior incidents of alleged UFO sightings and government cover-ups have fueled public interest in extraterrestrial activity. The patterns of blurred imagery in sensitive regions have only intensified speculation regarding military interests and the potential concealment of UFO evidence. Analysts assert that while blurred images may protect critical infrastructure, this does not imply a deliberate effort to hide evidence of alien activity.

What’s next

As discussions continue online, analysts encourage a more cautious approach to interpreting satellite imagery. Investigations into the reasons behind altered images may persist, but no formal inquiries have been announced at this time.

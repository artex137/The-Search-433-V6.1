Retired U.S. Air Force General's Disappearance Remains Unsolved

The disappearance of retired U.S. Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, 68, has remained unsolved for two weeks after he left his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on foot around 11 a.m. on February 27, 2026. McCasland has not been reachable by family or friends, and his mobile phone was found inside his home.

This case has drawn attention due to McCasland's background and the lack of significant leads in the investigation. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, along with the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), has been searching for him, engaging with residents in over 600 nearby homes. Authorities have not detected any criminal activity related to his disappearance.

Key facts

  • Name: William Neil McCasland
  • Age: 68
  • Date of disappearance: February 27, 2026
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Investigation agencies: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, FBI, U.S. Air Force
  • Mobile phone status: Found inside his home
  • Community engagement: Over 600 homes visited by authorities

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, at approximately 11 a.m., William Neil McCasland left his home in Albuquerque on foot. His family reported him missing when he became unreachable. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a missing person alert the following day and began searching the area.

Authorities have conducted interviews with residents in over 600 homes but have not found any significant clues. The Air Force and FBI are working together on the case, which remains open.

Official statements

“I hope McCasland is not a target of a crime,” said Luis Elizondo, a former U.S. Department of Defense intelligence officer. “I pray for his safe and healthy return.”
McCasland’s wife addressed speculation about UFOs, stating, “My husband does not possess any special knowledge about alien bodies or debris from the Roswell crash.”

Context

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio, where McCasland served as director of the Air Force Research Laboratory, has long been associated with conspiracy theories regarding UFOs and alien encounters. These theories have gained traction in light of McCasland’s disappearance, but the authorities have not indicated any link between his former work and his current situation.

What’s next

The investigation into McCasland’s disappearance will continue as authorities seek to uncover new leads. The FBI and the Air Force are committed to resolving the case, but no specific timeline for updates has been disclosed.

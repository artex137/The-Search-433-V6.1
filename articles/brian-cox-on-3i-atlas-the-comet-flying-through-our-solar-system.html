Brian Cox on 3I/Atlas - the comet flying through our solar system

Brian Cox has discussed the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas, which is currently travelling through our solar system. The comet, first identified in 2020, is notable for its origin outside the solar system and is expected to make its closest approach to the Sun in early 2023.

This discussion highlights the significance of 3I/Atlas as a rare celestial event. Scientists are studying the comet to gain insights into the formation of our solar system and the nature of interstellar objects. Observatories worldwide are preparing for observations as the comet approaches.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/Atlas
  • Origin: Interstellar
  • Closest approach to the Sun: Early 2023
  • Identified: 2020
  • Key speaker: Brian Cox

Timeline

3I/Atlas was first identified in 2020 by astronomers using the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS). Since its discovery, scientists have monitored its trajectory and characteristics.

As it approaches the Sun, various observatories around the world have planned to observe the comet. These observations aim to enhance understanding of its composition and origin.

Official statements

“The study of 3I/Atlas is crucial for understanding the early solar system and interstellar objects,” said Brian Cox during the segment.

Context

3I/Atlas is one of the few known interstellar comets. Its passage through the solar system offers a unique opportunity for research, as such events are rare and provide valuable data on the origins of celestial bodies.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, astronomers will continue to observe and analyze its characteristics, with a focus on gathering data that could inform future studies of interstellar objects.

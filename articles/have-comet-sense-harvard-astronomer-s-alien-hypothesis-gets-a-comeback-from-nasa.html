Have Comet Sense: Harvard Astronomer’s Alien Hypothesis Gets A Comeback From NASA

Nasa scientists have dismissed claims by Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb that Comet 3I/Atlas, discovered on July 1 in Río Hurtado, could be an alien probe with potentially dire consequences for Earth. The comet is expected to pass close to Mars next month.

This debate matters as it highlights differing views on the origins of interstellar objects and the implications for humanity. Nasa has been tracking Comet 3I/Atlas with various telescopes, asserting that it behaves like a typical comet and poses no threat to Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/Atlas discovered on July 1, 2023, by the Atlas survey telescope.
  • It is the third known interstellar object to enter the solar system.
  • Expected to pass near Mars in October 2023.
  • Avi Loeb suggested it may be a "technological artifact" with a velocity of 130,000 mph (209,000 km/h).
  • Nasa has tracked the comet using Hubble and other telescopes since its discovery.
  • Nasa states the comet will not come closer than about 170 million miles to Earth.

Timeline

On July 1, 2023, the Atlas survey telescope discovered Comet 3I/Atlas. Since then, Nasa has actively monitored the comet's trajectory and behavior. In a recent paper, Avi Loeb proposed that the comet could be of alien origin due to its unusual characteristics.

In response, Nasa scientists have reiterated that the comet displays typical comet behavior and characteristics, emphasizing its natural origins.

Official statements

“It looks like a comet. It does comet things. It very, very strongly resembles, in just about every way, the comets that we know.” — Tom Statler, Nasa’s lead scientist for solar system small bodies
“The consequences, should the hypothesis turn out to be correct, could potentially be dire for humanity.” — Avi Loeb, head of Harvard University’s Galileo Project

Context

The discovery of interstellar objects like Comet 3I/Atlas has become increasingly common, with only three known to have entered the solar system. This marks a significant opportunity for scientists to study the composition of other solar systems.

Avi Loeb's hypothesis has sparked discussion within the scientific community about the need for open-mindedness in exploring unconventional ideas.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor Comet 3I/Atlas as it approaches Mars and will provide updates on its trajectory and characteristics as necessary.

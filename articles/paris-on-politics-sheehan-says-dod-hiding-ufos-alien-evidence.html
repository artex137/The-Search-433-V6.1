Paris on Politics: Sheehan says DoD hiding UFOs, alien evidence

A bipartisan group of lawmakers has called for the disclosure of all classified intelligence regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and their potential extraterrestrial origins. The announcement was made on August 27, 2025, at 9:59 PM CDT, concerning the U.S. Department of Defense (DoD).

This development matters as it highlights ongoing concerns about government transparency regarding UAPs. Lawmakers are pressing for clarity on what evidence the DoD may possess, which has sparked public interest and debate over the existence of extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: August 27, 2025
  • Time of announcement: 9:59 PM CDT
  • Agency involved: U.S. Department of Defense
  • Type of phenomena: Unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP)
  • Nature of request: Disclosure of classified intelligence

Timeline

On August 27, 2025, a bipartisan group of lawmakers publicly urged the DoD to release classified information regarding UAPs. This request follows a growing public interest in the topic and previous discussions in Congress about the need for greater transparency on military encounters with unidentified objects.

Official statements

“The truth is out there and it might be here in the cabinets and halls of the U.S. Dept. of Defense,”
said a spokesperson for the group of lawmakers advocating for disclosure.

Context

In recent years, there has been an increase in reports of UAP sightings, leading to heightened scrutiny of government handling of such incidents. Previous congressional hearings have addressed the need for openness regarding military encounters with unidentified objects.

What’s next

Lawmakers are expected to push for further discussions with the DoD to ensure the timely release of the requested intelligence on UAPs.

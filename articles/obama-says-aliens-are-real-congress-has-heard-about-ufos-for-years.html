Obama says aliens are 'real.' Congress has heard about UFOs for years

Former U.S. President Barack Obama has stirred up conversation about extraterrestrials after he stated in a podcast interview that aliens are "real." This comment was made during an interview with Brian Tyler Cohen on February 14, 2023. Following the podcast, Obama clarified his remarks on social media, asserting that the likelihood of Earth being visited by aliens is low and that he has seen "no evidence" of such encounters during his presidency.

Obama's remarks come amid ongoing congressional inquiries into unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), a term that has replaced the stigmatized "UFO." Since 2023, Congress has held multiple hearings featuring testimonies from experts and insiders regarding UAP sightings and government knowledge of these incidents. This renewed interest in UAP has prompted discussions about transparency and the need for further investigation.

Key facts

  • Obama's podcast interview was published on February 14, 2023.
  • He claimed aliens are "real," but he has not seen them.
  • Three congressional hearings on UAP have occurred since 2023.
  • The most recent hearing took place on September 9, 2025.
  • Witnesses included military veterans and journalists discussing UAP encounters.
  • A video was presented showing a military drone attempting to engage a mysterious object.

Timeline

On February 14, 2023, Obama participated in a podcast with Brian Tyler Cohen, where he answered a rapid-fire question about aliens, saying they are "real." This comment quickly led to media coverage and public interest.

On February 15, 2023, Obama posted on Instagram to clarify his earlier statement, emphasizing that while he believes in the possibility of extraterrestrial life, he has not seen evidence of their visitation to Earth.

Congress has held three hearings on UAP since 2023, with the latest occurring on September 9, 2025. Witnesses have testified about their experiences and the government's handling of information related to UAP.

Official statements

"Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there’s life out there," Obama stated on Instagram. "But the distances between solar systems are so great that the chances we’ve been visited by aliens is low, and I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrials have made contact with us. Really!"

Context

Prior to Obama's comments, the topic of UAP has gained traction in Congress, especially following testimonies in July 2023 that reignited public interest. Witnesses have claimed that military technology is unable to explain the capabilities of these unidentified objects, leading to calls for greater transparency from government agencies.

The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office has been investigating UAP sightings, although officials have not confirmed any links to extraterrestrial life. NASA also issued a report in September 2023 stating it had not found evidence of extraterrestrial origins for UAP.

What’s next

Congress may continue to investigate UAP sightings and the government's knowledge of these phenomena. Legislation is being considered to compel the release of more information regarding UAP investigations.

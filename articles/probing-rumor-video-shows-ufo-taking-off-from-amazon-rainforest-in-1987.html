Probing rumor video shows UFO taking off from Amazon rainforest in 1987

A video claiming to show a UFO taking off from the Amazon rainforest in 1987 has sparked widespread interest online, particularly in late 2025. The clip reportedly presents a helicopter pilot's view of a round, disc-like object rising from the trees, but authorities have confirmed the video is artificial intelligence-generated and not authentic.

This matter has gained traction as the video has circulated widely on social media platforms, accumulating hundreds of thousands of views. Snopes has contacted the original creator of the clip for further details about its production and will update the report if more information becomes available.

Key facts

  • Video duration: 20 seconds
  • First posted on TikTok by user VOID TAPES (@voidtapes_x) on October 5, 2025
  • Video has received nearly 1 million views on TikTok
  • Alarmingly, the video falsely depicts a UFO sighting and instrument failures
  • Created using BigMotion.ai, an AI video creation platform
  • Social media posts about the video have reached hundreds of thousands of views

Timeline

On October 5, 2025, a TikTok user posted a video claiming to show archival footage of a UFO sighting in the Amazon. The clip included dramatic narration from a supposed pilot and described malfunctioning instruments. The video quickly gained traction, amassing almost 1 million views.

In December 2025, a user on X shared the video, further amplifying its reach with sensational claims. The original TikTok post indicated the footage was AI-generated, but many viewers remained unaware of its true origin.

Official statements

“The footage is not authentic and has been confirmed to be generated using artificial intelligence,” Snopes reported after contacting the creator of the video.

Context

This incident follows a trend of increasing interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and UFO sightings, particularly in remote areas like the Amazon. The region has been the subject of speculation regarding hidden technology or extraterrestrial encounters, but many claims remain unverified.

Prior instances of UFO sightings have often been debunked or attributed to natural phenomena or experimental aircraft, leading to skepticism among experts.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any further investigations into the viral video, but Snopes continues to seek additional information from the creator regarding the production process.

Adrian Smith Suggests Iron Maiden Could Retire UFO's 'Doctor Doctor' As Concert Opener

In a recent interview with Bandit Rock radio station, IRON MAIDEN guitarist Adrian Smith expressed his thoughts on the band's long-standing tradition of using UFO's "Doctor Doctor" as their concert opener. Smith acknowledged his appreciation for the classic track but suggested that it might be time for a change.

This discussion is significant as "Doctor Doctor" has been a staple of IRON MAIDEN concerts for over two decades. The band is currently on their "Run For Your Lives" 50th-anniversary world tour, which is set to continue until 2026. Changes to their concert opener could reflect a broader evolution in the band's approach as they move forward.

Key facts

  • Adrian Smith made his comments during an interview with Bandit Rock radio station.
  • "Doctor Doctor" has been used as the concert opener for over 20 years.
  • The current tour, "Run For Your Lives," is celebrating IRON MAIDEN's 50th anniversary.
  • The tour will conclude in 2026 with dates in K-Arena in Yokohama.
  • The band plans to take a break from touring in 2027.

Timeline

In February 2022, during a spoken-word event at The Vic Theatre in Chicago, Illinois, lead vocalist Bruce Dickinson discussed the reasons behind using "Doctor Doctor" as the band’s entrance music. He highlighted its ability to signal the audience to prepare for the show.

Recently, Adrian Smith shared his thoughts on possibly retiring the song as the opener, suggesting a return to a previous AC/DC song, "It’s A Long Way To The Top (If You Wanna Rock 'N' Roll)." This reflects ongoing conversations within the band about their concert performances.

Official statements

“Well, I mean, I love UFO — they’re one of my favorite bands — but maybe we should change it,” Smith said. “We used to have a different one — it was an AC/DC song called ‘It’s A Long Way To The Top [If You Wanna Rock ‘N’ Roll],’ with the bagpipes and everything. So maybe we should bring that back. I do love ‘Doctor Doctor.’”
“It has the added advantage that everybody knows we play ‘Doctor Doctor’ before we go on stage,” Dickinson said. “So, before the intro tape, there’s five minutes of ‘Doctor Doctor.’ It’s brilliant, so people go, ‘Oh, quick.’ Stop having a piss, drink the last pint, get to your seats.”

Context

The use of "Doctor Doctor" as an opening song has become a recognizable element of IRON MAIDEN's performances, creating a sense of anticipation among fans. The band has a history of significant milestones, and this 50th-anniversary tour marks a moment of reflection on their legacy and future direction.

What’s next

As IRON MAIDEN continues their anniversary tour, discussions about their concert opener may influence future setlists. Fans will be watching closely for any changes as the band approaches the conclusion of their tour in 2026 and their planned break in 2027.

Still waiting on my first UFO sighting

Dan Marsh, a regional editor, has expressed his desire to see an unidentified flying object (UFO) while reflecting on his experiences during a long drive to Carroll, Iowa, on January 16, 2026. Marsh reported seeing chains of blinking red lights in the night sky, which he initially thought could be UFOs but later identified as a wind farm.

This matter highlights the ongoing fascination with UFOs and the public's interest in unexplained phenomena. Authorities and researchers continue to investigate such sightings, although no definitive evidence of extraterrestrial life has been established.

Key facts

  • Date: January 16, 2026
  • Location: Carroll, Iowa
  • Duration of drive: 14 hours
  • Time of sighting: Midnight
  • Companion: Dog named Leo

Timeline

Dan Marsh embarked on a 14-hour drive from Arkansas to Carroll, Iowa, which included a three-hour stop for a mechanical issue that turned out to be non-existent. While driving at midnight, he observed blinking red lights on the horizon. Initially suspecting they were UFOs, he soon realized they were part of a local wind farm.

After arriving in Carroll, Marsh reflected on the experience and the concept of UFOs, noting the lack of personal encounters with unexplained phenomena in his 56 years.

Official statements

“I believe that sometimes people see things they can’t explain.” - Dan Marsh
“I’d just like to actually see something unexplainable myself.” - Dan Marsh

Context

The topic of UFOs has garnered significant public interest, especially following various sightings reported worldwide. Many individuals, like Marsh, express a mixture of curiosity and skepticism regarding the existence of extraterrestrial life. Despite numerous claims of sightings, concrete evidence has remained elusive.

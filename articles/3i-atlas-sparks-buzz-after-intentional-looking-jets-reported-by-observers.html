3I/ATLAS Sparks Buzz After 'Intentional-Looking' Jets Reported by Observers

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has surprised astronomers by emitting sharply focused jets of material, a behaviour not typical of ordinary comets. Observatories including Gemini North, the Nordic Optical Telescope, and the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) have captured images showing a powerful jet directed toward the Sun, raising questions about its unique structure and composition. 3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025 by the ATLAS survey in Chile and made its closest approach to the Sun on 29 October 2025.

This discovery matters because it challenges existing theories about comet behaviour and offers insights into materials formed around distant stars. Scientists are investigating whether the jets result from unusual internal structures or unknown processes. The object is only the third confirmed interstellar object ever observed in the solar system, following 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.

Key facts

  • Discovered: 1 July 2025 by ATLAS survey in Chile
  • Closest approach to the Sun: 29 October 2025
  • Type: Third confirmed interstellar object
  • Jet behaviour: Highly concentrated, sunward-facing jets
  • Distance of jets: Up to 500,000 km

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025. It made its closest approach to the Sun on 29 October 2025. Following this event, astronomers observed its unusual jet activity, which has prompted further investigation into the nature of these emissions.

Observations from various telescopes have shown that the object produced sharply defined jets, contrasting with the diffuse patterns typical of most comets. Data from the JWST has revealed that the comet's coma is primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with smaller quantities of water ice and carbon monoxide.

Official statements

“The precision of 3I/ATLAS' jets has prompted fresh questions about its internal structure,” said an astronomer at one of the observatories. “While some observers have noted the jets look 'almost engineered', there is no evidence of artificial control.”

Context

Previous interstellar objects, such as 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, exhibited different characteristics. The behaviour of 3I/ATLAS adds a new dimension to the study of interstellar comets and their interactions with the solar system. Understanding its jets may reshape current theories regarding cometary physics and the evolution of objects formed in other planetary systems.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to track 3I/ATLAS as it moves back into deep space. Ongoing research will focus on the implications of its unique jet activity and what it reveals about interstellar materials.

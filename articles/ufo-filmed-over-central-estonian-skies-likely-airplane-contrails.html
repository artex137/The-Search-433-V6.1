'UFO' filmed over central Estonian skies likely airplane contrails

A mysterious unidentified "burning object" spotted in the skies over central Estonia on Sunday evening has likely been identified as aircraft contrails, according to Maaleht. The sighting occurred shortly before 6 p.m. near Koeru in Järva County, facing the Tartu highway, with no reported injuries or fatalities.

This sighting has attracted attention due to its unusual appearance. Authorities, including Taavi Niittee, leader of the Tõrva astronomy club, have stated that the phenomenon is likely a common occurrence, exacerbated by specific weather conditions. The incident highlights public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena and the importance of accurate information in such cases.

Key facts

  • Time of sighting: Shortly before 6 p.m. on Sunday
  • Location: Near Koeru, Järva County, Estonia
  • Reported by: Maaleht reader Sander
  • Emergency number called: 112
  • Expert opinion: Taavi Niittee, Tõrva astronomy club leader

Timeline

On Sunday, shortly before 6 p.m., Sander captured photos and videos of a "burning object" in the sky. He described the object as resembling a rocket and noted that it was difficult to determine its altitude and distance. After observing the phenomenon, Sander contacted the national emergency number, 112.

Taavi Niittee later assessed the footage and indicated that it was most likely an airplane contrail, which can appear unusual under certain weather conditions. He explained that the angle of the contrail against the setting sun created a dark contrast, contributing to the object's peculiar appearance.

Official statements

“The unusually dark contrast of the trail against the bright sky is caused by its angle relative to the setting Sun,” said Taavi Niittee.
“While rockets and other space debris burning up are significantly faster-moving phenomena, the slow-moving nature of the object and ensuing trail suggests it is an aircraft contrail,” Niittee added.

Context

Aircraft contrails are formed when warm, moist gases from jet engines meet extremely cold air, resulting in ice crystals. This phenomenon has sparked various conspiracy theories in recent years about their true nature. The current weather conditions, including clear skies and cold temperatures, can make these trails appear more dramatic and unusual.

What’s next

No further investigations are currently underway, as authorities have identified the object as likely being aircraft contrails. The public is encouraged to report any unusual sightings while understanding the scientific explanations behind such phenomena.

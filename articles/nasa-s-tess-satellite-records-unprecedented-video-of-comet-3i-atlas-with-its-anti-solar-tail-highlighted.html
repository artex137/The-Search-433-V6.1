NASA’s TESS satellite records unprecedented video of comet 3I/ATLAS with its anti-solar tail highlighted

NASA’s Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) has captured an unprecedented video of comet 3I/ATLAS, showcasing its anti-solar tail. The event occurred on February 2, 2026, and marks a significant milestone in comet observation.

This video is important for scientists studying the behavior and characteristics of comets. NASA aims to use the data gathered from TESS to enhance understanding of cometary phenomena and their implications for solar system dynamics.

Key facts

  • Event date: February 2, 2026
  • Satellite: NASA’s Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS)
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation type: Video of the anti-solar tail
  • Significance: Enhances understanding of comet behavior

Timeline

On February 2, 2026, TESS recorded the video of comet 3I/ATLAS as part of its ongoing mission to observe celestial bodies. The video highlights the unique features of the comet’s anti-solar tail, providing valuable data for researchers.

Official statements

“This observation is a significant achievement in our efforts to monitor and understand comets,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been of interest to astronomers since its discovery. Previous observations have noted its unique tail structure, which has implications for studying the composition of comets and their evolution in the solar system.

What’s next

NASA plans to analyze the video data from TESS further to extract insights into the dynamics of comet 3I/ATLAS and its interaction with solar radiation.

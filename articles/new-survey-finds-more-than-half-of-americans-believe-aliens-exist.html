New Survey Finds More Than Half of Americans Believe Aliens Exist

A recent survey conducted by YouGov has revealed that more than half of Americans believe in the existence of extraterrestrial life. The poll, which included responses from 1,114 U.S. adults over five days earlier this month, found that 56% of participants answered "definitely or probably" when asked if they think aliens exist. The survey results highlight a growing interest in unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and the possibility of alien visitation.

This survey matters as it reflects public sentiment regarding extraterrestrial life amid ongoing discussions about UFO sightings and government transparency. Authorities have been increasingly focused on investigating these phenomena, especially following recent disclosures about military encounters with unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP).

Key facts

  • 56% of respondents believe aliens exist.
  • 30% think UFOs are of extraterrestrial origin.
  • 47% believe aliens have visited Earth.
  • 29% predict alien contact will happen by 2075.
  • 73% believe the government would hide proof of aliens.
  • Only 28% of respondents believe in Bigfoot.

Timeline

The survey was conducted over five days in November 2025. It asked various questions regarding beliefs about extraterrestrial life, UFOs, and the potential impact of alien visitation on society. Results were compiled and analyzed after the polling period concluded.

“The survey highlights significant public interest in the possibility of alien life and the government's role in disclosing information,” said Tim Binnall, the author of the report.

Context

This survey comes at a time when interest in UFOs has surged, particularly following the release of U.S. government reports on UAPs. The increasing number of sightings and reports has led to discussions about the need for greater transparency from authorities.

What’s next

Authorities and researchers are expected to continue investigating UFO sightings and the implications of potential alien life. The public's interest may prompt further studies and transparency initiatives from government agencies.

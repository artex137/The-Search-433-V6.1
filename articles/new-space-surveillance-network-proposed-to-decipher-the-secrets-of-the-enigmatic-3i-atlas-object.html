New space surveillance network proposed to decipher the secrets of the enigmatic 3I Atlas object

Scientists have proposed a new space surveillance network to study the comet 3I ATLAS, which has garnered interest due to its unique characteristics. The proposal was made during a conference held in Paris on February 1, 2026.

This initiative aims to enhance our understanding of the comet, which has puzzled astronomers since its discovery. Authorities are seeking funding and collaboration from international space agencies to establish the network, which will utilize advanced telescopes and satellite technology.

Key facts

  • Event: Proposal for a new space surveillance network
  • Date: February 1, 2026
  • Location: Paris, France
  • Object of study: Comet 3I ATLAS
  • Agencies involved: Not disclosed

Timeline

The comet 3I ATLAS was first detected in late 2019. Since then, it has been the subject of numerous studies aimed at understanding its origin and trajectory. The recent proposal for a dedicated surveillance network marks a significant step in ongoing research efforts.

Official statements

“The establishment of a surveillance network will be crucial in unlocking the mysteries surrounding 3I ATLAS,” said Dr. Alice Monroe, an astrophysicist involved in the project.

Context

Previous studies of other comets have provided insights into the formation of our solar system. The data from 3I ATLAS could potentially reveal more about the early conditions that led to the development of planets.

What’s next

Authorities will begin discussions with potential international partners to secure funding and resources for the proposed network in the coming months.

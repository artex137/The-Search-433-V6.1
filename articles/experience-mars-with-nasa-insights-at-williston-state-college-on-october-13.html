Experience Mars With NASA Insights At Williston State College On October 13

The WSC Astronomy Club is hosting an event titled Mission to Mars on Monday, October 13, 2025, from 7:00 to 8:00 PM in Science Lab Room 104 at Williston State College. The event is free and open to families, with no reported injuries or fatalities.

This event is significant as it aims to engage the community in space exploration and education. NASA has partnered with the North Dakota Space Grant Consortium to provide insights into Mars exploration and offer opportunities for students interested in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

Key facts

  • What: Mission to Mars event
  • When: October 13, 2025, from 7:00 to 8:00 PM
  • Where: Science Lab Room 104, Williston State College
  • Cost: Free
  • Organizers: WSC Astronomy Club, NASA, North Dakota Space Grant Consortium

Timeline

The Mission to Mars event will begin with a presentation on NASA’s latest missions and Mars exploration. This will be followed by a hands-on challenge where participants can design and test their own mini rovers using Ozobots. The event concludes with giveaways, including posters and stickers.

Official statements

“This free, family-friendly event promises a night filled with discovery, creativity, and just the right amount of space geekery,” said an organizer from the WSC Astronomy Club.

Context

Engagement in space science has been increasing in educational institutions, with events like this aiming to inspire the next generation of scientists and engineers. The collaboration between NASA and local organizations reflects a broader trend of promoting STEM education to youth.

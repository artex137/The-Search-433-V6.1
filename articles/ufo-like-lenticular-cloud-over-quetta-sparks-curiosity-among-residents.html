‘UFO-like’ lenticular cloud over Quetta sparks curiosity among residents

QUETTA: A rare lenticular cloud formation has been spotted over Quetta early Tuesday morning, intriguing residents and prompting speculation about its origins. Eyewitnesses reported seeing the unusual cloud around the time of morning prayers from various locations across Balochistan, leading to widespread curiosity.

The phenomenon has garnered attention, with some social media users suggesting it could be linked to a missile test or new military technology. The Pakistan Meteorological Department (PMD) later clarified the situation, confirming that the cloud was a “lenticular cloud formation” observed over the Koh e Murdaar region before sunrise.

Key facts

  • Event occurred on October 28, 2025.
  • The cloud appeared over Koh e Murdaar, eastern range of Quetta.
  • The phenomenon lasted approximately 20 minutes.
  • It dissipated just prior to sunrise.
  • Reported by the Pakistan Meteorological Department (PMD).

Timeline

On the morning of October 28, 2025, residents of Quetta began noticing an unusual cloud formation around the time of morning prayers. Eyewitness reports emerged from various parts of Balochistan, leading to discussions on social media.

Later in the day, the Pakistan Meteorological Department issued a statement confirming that the cloud was a lenticular formation, which typically appears in stable air flowing over mountains.

Official statements

“The cloud appeared before sunrise, persisted for about 20 minutes, and dissipated just before daybreak,” the PMD stated in a post on X.

Context

Lenticular clouds are lens-shaped formations that develop when stable air flows over mountains or hills, creating wave patterns in the atmosphere. According to the UK’s Met Office, these clouds are often associated with UFO sightings due to their unusual, saucer-like appearance.

What’s next

No further investigations or actions have been announced regarding the cloud formation, as the PMD has clarified its natural origins.

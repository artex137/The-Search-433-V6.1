Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS: Martian Probe Captured It at an Unprecedented Moment

The UAE spacecraft "Al-Amal" has captured unique images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it approached the Sun in October 2025. This observation marks a significant event in astronomical research, as ground-based telescopes were unable to detect the comet due to its position behind the Sun.

The UAE Space Agency has reported that the data collected from the "Al-Amal" probe provides insights into the chemistry of another star system. The observations indicate that 3I/ATLAS was disintegrating under the Sun's influence, allowing scientists to study its chemical composition, which differs from that of comets within the Solar System.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025.
  • The comet approached the Sun at a speed of over 200,000 km/h.
  • The "Al-Amal" spacecraft observed the comet in October 2025.
  • Unique data were collected during the comet’s closest distance to the Sun.
  • The probe detected hydrogen, oxygen, and carbon monoxide emissions from the comet.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered. By October 2025, the comet approached the Sun, during which the "Al-Amal" spacecraft captured images of its disintegration. The probe utilized its EXI and EMUS instruments to collect data, which included the detection of hydrogen, oxygen, and carbon monoxide.

This observation was significant as it provided a rare glimpse into the comet’s chemical makeup, reflecting the conditions of its origin in another star system.

Official statements

“The uniqueness of this observation lies in the fact that we were able to see 3I/ATLAS at the moment of its actual disintegration near the Sun,” said a representative from the UAE Space Agency.

Context

Prior to this event, ground-based telescopes had limited visibility of interstellar comets, particularly when they are positioned behind the Sun. The "Al-Amal" probe, designed to study the Martian atmosphere, was fortuitously positioned to observe 3I/ATLAS, highlighting the potential for spacecraft designed for one purpose to contribute to other areas of scientific inquiry.

What’s next

Scientists are expected to conduct a more detailed analysis of the data collected from the observations. Further results will be published soon, contributing to the understanding of interstellar objects and their origins.

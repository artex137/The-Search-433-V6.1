3I/ATLAS Rotates Every 7.1 Hours After Perihelion

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been confirmed to rotate every 7.1 hours following its perihelion, which occurred on May 31, 2023. The discovery was made by astronomers at various observatories worldwide.

This finding is significant as it provides insights into the comet's structure and behavior, which could enhance our understanding of similar celestial bodies. Astronomers continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Rotation period: 7.1 hours
  • Perihelion date: May 31, 2023
  • Observatories involved: Various worldwide

Timeline

On May 31, 2023, 3I/ATLAS reached its perihelion, the closest point to the Sun in its orbit. Following this event, astronomers have been studying its rotation and other characteristics.

Recent observations have confirmed the comet's rotation period, providing valuable data for ongoing research.

Official statements

“The rotation of 3I/ATLAS offers a unique glimpse into the dynamics of comets,” said an astronomer involved in the study. “Understanding its rotation helps us learn more about similar celestial objects.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of many comets studied by astronomers to understand their composition and behavior. Previous studies have revealed varying rotation rates among different comets, which can influence their appearance and trajectory.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the solar system, gathering more data on its rotation and other features to further enhance scientific knowledge of comets.

Following UFO Expert Disappearance: New Mexico Residents

The disappearance of retired Air Force Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland has raised significant concern in New Mexico as investigators expanded their search and requested home security footage from nearby residents. McCasland, last seen on February 27, 2026, at approximately 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE in Albuquerque, has been missing for over two weeks.

This case has attracted attention due to McCasland’s background in military science and technology, as well as his connections to UFO discussions. Authorities have issued a Silver Alert for the 68-year-old man, citing medical issues and uncertainty about his direction of travel. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has asked the public to assist in reconstructing McCasland’s movements since his disappearance.

Key facts

  • Missing person: William Neil McCasland, 68 years old
  • Last seen: February 27, 2026, at approximately 11 a.m.
  • Location: Quail Run Court NE, Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Silver Alert issued by Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office
  • Over 600 residents canvassed for home security footage
  • FBI has requested assistance from the public

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, at around 11 a.m., William Neil McCasland was last seen in Albuquerque. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert due to concerns for his welfare. In the days that followed, investigators broadened the search, canvassing over 600 homes in the vicinity of McCasland’s last known location. They requested residents to provide any home security footage that could assist in the investigation.

By early March, the FBI joined the effort, asking for public assistance in locating McCasland, particularly in the Sandia Mountains, where he may have been during his disappearance.

Official statements

“We are asking the community for their cooperation in reviewing any security footage they may have that could assist us in this case,” said a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.
“This is a serious situation, and we are committed to finding Mr. McCasland,” stated an FBI representative during a public appeal.

Context

McCasland's disappearance has garnered attention due to his previous roles in military research and his connections to UFO discussions. He has served as a commander at Kirtland Air Force Base and managed a $2.2 billion science and technology program. His name has appeared in discussions surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena, further intensifying public interest.

New Mexico has a rich history related to UFO lore, with events like the Roswell incident contributing to a cultural fascination with such topics. This context has amplified the response to McCasland’s case, which combines elements of military intrigue with the mystery of a missing person.

What’s next

Investigators will continue to analyze the home security footage submitted by residents. They are focused on establishing a timeline of McCasland’s movements and determining any potential leads. The authorities have emphasized the importance of community cooperation in resolving this case.

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS emits mysterious radio signal and provokes global scientific mobilization

The international astronomical community has confirmed the passage of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, first identified on July 1, 2025. The comet has emitted atypical radio signals, prompting heightened monitoring efforts from space agencies worldwide, particularly NASA.

This event represents a significant opportunity for scientists to study primordial material from another star system. Agencies including NASA and the Agência Espacial Europeia (ESA) have mobilized resources to gather data on the comet's composition and origin. The unusual radio emissions and high speed of over 100,000 kilometers per second have raised interest among researchers.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Identification Date: July 1, 2025
  • Detection of Radio Signals: October 24, 2025
  • Comet Speed: Over 100,000 km/s
  • Closest Approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Distance at Closest Approach: Approximately 27 million kilometers
  • Key Instruments: MeerKAT, Very Large Telescope (VLT), Hubble Space Telescope

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers identified comet 3I/ATLAS. Following its detection, NASA and ESA intensified their monitoring protocols. On October 24, 2025, the MeerKAT radio telescope in South Africa captured unusual radio emissions from the comet. This led to further investigations into the comet's composition.

NASA’s Escritório de Coordenação de Defesa Planetária has planned a press conference for August 2025 to discuss the findings and outline the next steps in the global observation campaign.

Official statements

“The emissions from 3I/ATLAS exceed predictions for an object of this type,” said a researcher at ESA. “This discovery opens new avenues for studying interstellar comets through radio astronomy.”

Context

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is the latest in a series of such objects to pass through our solar system, following the notable comets ‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The study of these objects helps scientists understand the diversity of interstellar materials and informs theories of planetary formation.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, global agencies will continue their observations and analyses. Data from 3I/ATLAS will be compared with previous interstellar visitors to enhance understanding of cosmic phenomena and strengthen planetary defense strategies.

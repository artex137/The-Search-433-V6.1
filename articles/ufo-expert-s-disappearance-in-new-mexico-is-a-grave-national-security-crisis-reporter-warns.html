UFO expert’s disappearance in New Mexico is a ‘grave national security crisis,’ reporter warns

The disappearance of retired Air Force major general William Neil McCasland, 68, in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on February 28 has raised significant concerns, with an investigative journalist calling it a “grave national security crisis.” Local law enforcement reported that McCasland left his home on foot, leaving his phone behind, prompting a missing persons investigation that now includes the FBI.

This situation is significant due to McCasland's extensive knowledge of sensitive information related to UFOs. According to journalist Ross Coulthart, McCasland possesses critical secrets regarding what the U.S. government might be concealing about extraterrestrial life. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has issued a Silver Alert for McCasland, indicating heightened concerns for his safety due to “medical issues.”

Key facts

  • William Neil McCasland, 68, was reported missing on February 28, 2026.
  • He left his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on foot and did not take his phone.
  • The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has issued a Silver Alert for him.
  • The FBI has joined the investigation into his disappearance.
  • McCasland has a history of overseeing classified space weapons programs at Wright Patterson Air Force Base.
  • Concerns for McCasland's well-being are heightened due to his medical issues.

Timeline

On February 28, 2026, William Neil McCasland was reported missing after leaving his Albuquerque home on foot. Local law enforcement began a missing persons investigation, which soon involved the FBI. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert, indicating urgent concerns for his safety.

Investigative journalist Ross Coulthart has highlighted the implications of McCasland's disappearance, noting that he held sensitive information relevant to national security. Coulthart emphasized the timing of McCasland's disappearance, coinciding with increased discussions regarding UFOs by U.S. officials.

Official statements

“This is a man with some of the most sensitive secrets of the United States in his head,” said journalist Ross Coulthart in his podcast “Reality Check.”
“The fact that Gen. Neil McCasland has disappeared off the face of the earth is a grave national security crisis for the United States of America,” Coulthart added.

Context

McCasland's career in the Air Force included significant roles overseeing classified projects at Wright Patterson Air Force Base, which is often associated with UFO research and alleged extraterrestrial debris. His disappearance has prompted discussions about the broader implications of government transparency regarding UFOs, especially as public interest in the topic has surged.

What’s next

The investigation into McCasland's disappearance is ongoing, with local law enforcement and the FBI actively seeking information from the public. Authorities have not disclosed any potential leads or suspects at this time.

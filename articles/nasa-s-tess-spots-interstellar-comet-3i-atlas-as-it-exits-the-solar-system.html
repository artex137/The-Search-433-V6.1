NASA’s TESS spots interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it exits the solar system

NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS) has detected interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it exits the solar system, marking a significant finding in the study of celestial objects. The discovery was made on October 4, 2023, when TESS observed the comet moving through the solar system.

This observation is important as it provides valuable data on the trajectory and composition of interstellar comets, which can enhance our understanding of the formation and evolution of planetary systems. NASA scientists are currently analyzing the data collected from TESS to gain further insights into the comet's characteristics.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Detection date: October 4, 2023
  • Observatory: NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS)
  • Current status: Exiting the solar system

Timeline

On October 4, 2023, TESS detected interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The comet was observed as it traveled away from the solar system, providing an opportunity for scientists to study its properties.

Official statements

“The detection of 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity to explore the nature of interstellar objects,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with only a few having been observed to date. The study of such objects helps scientists understand the origins of comets and their role in the solar system's development.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze data from TESS and other observatories to monitor the comet's trajectory and gather more information about its composition and behavior.

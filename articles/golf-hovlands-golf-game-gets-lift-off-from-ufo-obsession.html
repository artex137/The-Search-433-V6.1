Golf-Hovland's golf game gets lift-off from UFO obsession

Norwegian golfer Viktor Hovland has revealed that his fascination with unidentified flying objects (UFOs) has contributed to his improved performance in golf, particularly ahead of the Ryder Cup taking place in Farmingdale, New York, this week. Hovland's comments came on September 25, a day after teammate Ludvig Aberg discussed Hovland's cosmic interests with the media.

Hovland, currently ranked world number 12, has turned to exploring UFOs as a way to alleviate the pressures of professional golf. He stated that this interest provides a much-needed mental break from the frustrations of the sport. Authorities in the golf community have noted Hovland's recent improvement in performance, suggesting that his unconventional approach may be paying off.

Key facts

  • Viktor Hovland is ranked world number 12.
  • Hovland has made his third consecutive Ryder Cup appearance.
  • He had his best range session in two years before the Ryder Cup.
  • Hovland's record in the last Ryder Cup was 3-1-1.
  • His record during his rookie year in 2021 was 0-3-2.

Timeline

On September 24, Ludvig Aberg disclosed Hovland's interest in UFOs during a media interaction. The following day, Hovland confirmed his fascination and discussed how it helps him cope with the pressures of golf.

Prior to the Ryder Cup opening ceremony on September 27, Hovland was seen expressing excitement about having his best range session in two years to teammates Shane Lowry and Robert MacIntyre.

Official statements

“I've always been extremely into my golf, and sometimes you just look at your golf swing and trying to practise hard and you don't really get the results that you want, it can be quite frustrating,” Hovland said.
“The more you go into it, you just get more questions... it's very interesting to go in there and kind of challenge some of your beliefs that you currently hold,” he added.

Context

Hovland previously struggled during his rookie Ryder Cup in 2021, finishing with a record of 0-3-2. However, he improved significantly in the subsequent Ryder Cup in Rome, where he achieved a record of 3-1-1, helping Europe reclaim the trophy.

What’s next

Hovland will compete in the Ryder Cup this week, aiming to build on his recent success and further solidify his position as a key player for Europe.

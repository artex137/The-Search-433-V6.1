Hillary Clinton backs UFO file release during Epstein deposition

Hillary Clinton has supported the release of UFO files while being deposed in a case related to Jeffrey Epstein. The deposition took place on October 24, 2023, in New York City. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this event.

This development is significant as it highlights ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and transparency in government reporting. Authorities are currently reviewing protocols for releasing classified information related to UFOs.

Key facts

  • Date of deposition: October 24, 2023
  • Location: New York City
  • Involved parties: Hillary Clinton, attorneys in the Epstein case
  • Focus of deposition: UFO files and government disclosure
  • Current status: Ongoing discussions about UAP transparency

Timeline

On October 24, 2023, Hillary Clinton participated in a deposition in New York City concerning Jeffrey Epstein. During this session, Clinton voiced her support for the release of UFO files, which has reignited public interest in UAP.

This deposition is part of a broader legal context involving Epstein, which has drawn attention to various high-profile individuals and their connections.

Official statements

“I believe in the importance of transparency regarding UAP and how our government handles such information,” Clinton stated during her deposition.

Context

The discussion around UFOs has gained traction in recent years, particularly after the U.S. government acknowledged the existence of UAP. Several reports and videos have surfaced, showing unidentified objects exhibiting flight patterns that defy current technological understanding.

Calls for transparency have increased, with various lawmakers advocating for the declassification of UFO-related documents.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue their review of protocols for releasing UAP information. Further discussions and potential legislative actions may follow in the coming months as public interest remains high.

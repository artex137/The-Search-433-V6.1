Retired General, UFO Expert Has Been Missing for 11 Days

Police in New Mexico are searching for retired Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland, 68, who has been missing since February 28. McCasland, a former Air Force general with expertise in UFO-related matters, disappeared from his home in Albuquerque, leaving his phone behind. Authorities have expressed concern for his safety due to his medical issues.

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office has reached out to more than 600 homeowners in the area, seeking security camera footage or information regarding McCasland's movements. Despite receiving numerous tips, investigators have reported no confirmed sightings of the general. His disappearance has raised significant public interest, particularly due to his background in classified space weapons programs and his knowledge of alleged government interactions with extraterrestrials.

Key facts

  • Missing since: February 28, 2023
  • Age: 68
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Involved agencies: Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office, FBI
  • Number of homeowners contacted: over 600
  • Current status: Missing person investigation ongoing

Timeline

On February 28, 2023, retired Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland vanished from his Albuquerque home. Authorities were alerted when he did not return, and they found that he had left his phone behind. In the days following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office began reaching out to local residents, asking for any information or security footage that could assist in locating him.

As of now, despite multiple tips, there have been no confirmed sightings of McCasland. A Silver Alert has been issued, indicating the urgency of the search due to his medical issues.

Official statements

“We are concerned for his safety due to his medical issues,” said a spokesperson from the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office.
Journalist Ross Coulthart described the situation as “a grave national security crisis,” linking it to recent calls from President Trump for the Pentagon to release UFO-related files.

Context

McCasland's disappearance has garnered attention not only because of his military background but also due to his involvement in classified programs at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base. His expertise in UFOs and potential government knowledge of extraterrestrial life has intensified public interest in the case, especially amid ongoing discussions about UFO disclosures.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office, in coordination with the FBI, continues to follow leads in the investigation. They are urging anyone with information regarding McCasland's whereabouts to contact the Missing Persons Unit or submit tips online.

'The E.T. of Varginha': 30-Year Mystery of 'Alien' Sightings

On January 20, 1996, three girls in Varginha, Brazil, claimed to have encountered an alien-like creature, sparking a mystery that continues to intrigue many. The girls described the being as having a heart-shaped face, large red eyes, three horns, and a shiny brown body, crouched beside a wall in a vacant lot. They reported the sighting to their mother, who said they thought they had seen the devil. The incident has gained renewed attention following recent testimonies, including that of a doctor who asserts he saw a non-human creature in his hospital.

The significance of this case has led to ongoing investigations by various authorities. The Brazilian Army conducted an official investigation shortly after the sighting, questioning witnesses and examining military logs. However, the official report dismissed the claims, attributing the sightings to misinterpretations during a violent summer storm. New testimonies presented recently have challenged this conclusion, reigniting public interest in the events that unfolded over three decades ago.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: January 20, 1996
  • Location: Varginha, Brazil
  • Number of witnesses: Three girls, along with several other individuals
  • Official investigation: Conducted by the Brazilian Army
  • Duration of investigation: Resulted in a two-volume report totaling 600 pages
  • Key testimonies: Doctor Italo Venturelli and others at a recent press conference

Timeline

On January 20, 1996, three girls in Varginha reported seeing an alien-like creature. Following the sighting, local ufologist Ubirajara Rodrigues interviewed the girls, who described the creature in detail. In the days that followed, various witnesses claimed to have seen military activity related to the incident.

The Brazilian Army launched an investigation, interviewing military personnel and examining vehicle logs. The investigation concluded with a report that labeled the claims as false. In recent years, new testimonies have emerged, notably from Italo Venturelli, who claims he saw a non-human being in a local hospital.

Official statements

“You didn’t see a demon or an ape, you saw an extraterrestrial,” said Ubirajara Rodrigues, recalling his conversation with the girls.
“It was completely different from a human. It was very calm, it seemed like an angel,” stated neurologist Italo Venturelli, describing his encounter.
“The military personnel cited by the press did not participate in any operation transporting any type of cargo,” according to the official report from the Brazilian Army.

Context

The Varginha incident has become one of Brazil's most famous UFO cases, often compared to other global sightings. The official military explanation has been met with skepticism from witnesses and ufologists alike. The case has inspired documentaries, books, and ongoing discussions about extraterrestrial life.

Recent developments, including the press conference organized by filmmaker James Fox, have brought new attention to the incident, highlighting ongoing public interest and the desire for further investigation.

What’s next

Further investigations into the Varginha incident are anticipated as new testimonies continue to surface. Authorities have not announced any official follow-up actions, but the renewed interest may prompt additional inquiries into the claims made by witnesses and military personnel.

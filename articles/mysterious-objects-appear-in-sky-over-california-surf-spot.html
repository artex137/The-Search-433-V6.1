Mysterious objects appear in sky over California surf spot

Surfline has captured footage of unidentified objects in the sky over Ventura, California, at approximately 4:46 a.m. Thursday. The objects, resembling flying saucers, have not caused any reported injuries or fatalities.

This sighting has drawn significant attention on social media, prompting discussions and theories among followers. Authorities have not confirmed the source of the lights as of late Thursday, leaving the matter open to speculation.

Key facts

  • Footage captured at 4:46 a.m. on December 4, 2025
  • Location: Ventura, California
  • Captured by Surfline's live cameras
  • Over 16,000 views in the first two hours
  • Numerous comments and theories from social media followers

Timeline

At 4:46 a.m. Thursday, Surfline's live camera footage showed what appeared to be two unidentified objects in the sky over Ventura. The objects were described as resembling flying saucers and, at one point, their lights merged into a single shimmering object. By late Thursday, the source of the lights remained unidentified.

Surfline shared the footage on Instagram, stating, “Didn’t see that on the forecast.” The post quickly gained traction, leading to a flurry of comments and theories from viewers. Some suggested the lights could be related to an upcoming SpaceX satellite-launching mission from Vandenberg Space Force Base.

Official statements

“Probably searching for our swells,” one follower commented, including an alien emoji.
“They are here for the super moon,” another top comment read.

Context

This sighting follows a trend of increased public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. Recent years have seen a rise in reported sightings, prompting discussions about the nature and origin of such objects.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any plans for an investigation into the sighting as of now. The situation remains open, with further developments anticipated as interest continues to grow.

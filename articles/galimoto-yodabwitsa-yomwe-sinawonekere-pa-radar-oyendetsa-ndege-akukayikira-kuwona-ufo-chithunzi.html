UFO sighting reported near Albuquerque

Two pilots have reported seeing an unidentified flying object (UFO) while flying near Albuquerque, New Mexico, on February 24. The object did not appear on radar, according to air traffic control.

Why this matters

This incident raises questions about airspace security and the nature of unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are reviewing the pilots' reports to determine the object's origin and nature.

Key facts

  • Date: February 24
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Pilots: Two pilots were involved in the sighting
  • Radar status: Object did not appear on radar
  • Response: Air traffic control did not have additional information

Timeline

On February 24, two pilots flying over Albuquerque reported an unusual object passing in front of them. The first pilot sent a message to air traffic control, inquiring if there was anything above them, approximately 30 seconds prior to the sighting. The response was negative, indicating nothing was detected on radar.

Shortly after, the second pilot also reported seeing the object. Both pilots described it as a UFO, as it did not correspond to any known aircraft in the area.

Official statements

“Is there anyone above us that is passing us, approximately 30 seconds ago?” the first pilot asked air traffic control.
“It was something, UFO,” the first pilot responded after receiving no information from air traffic control.
“We do not have any additional information beyond what has already been reported,” an air traffic control representative stated.

Context

This sighting adds to a growing list of UFO reports in the United States, prompting discussions about the need for more thorough investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities have been urged to enhance monitoring and reporting systems for such encounters.

What’s next

Investigations into the sighting are ongoing. Authorities are expected to analyze the pilots’ reports and any available radar data to gather more information on the incident.

Who Is William Neil McCasland? Silver Alert Issued For Retired US Air Force General Linked To UFO Research

Law enforcement agencies in New Mexico have launched a search for retired US Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, who was reported missing on Friday morning. According to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, McCasland, 68, was last seen around 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE in Albuquerque. Authorities have not disclosed details about what he was wearing or the direction in which he may have travelled. Due to medical concerns, there are heightened worries about his safety, prompting an active Silver Alert.

This case has attracted attention due to McCasland's extensive career in aerospace research and his connections to advanced technology discussions. Authorities are treating the investigation strictly as a missing persons case and are currently focused on gathering tips and retracing his last known movements.

Key facts

  • Missing person report filed on Friday morning.
  • Last seen at approximately 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE, Albuquerque.
  • 68 years old.
  • Silver Alert issued due to medical concerns.
  • Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office is leading the investigation.
  • No confirmed evidence of abduction or foul play.

Timeline

McCasland was last seen on Friday morning around 11 a.m. in Albuquerque. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office received the missing person report shortly thereafter, leading to the issuance of a Silver Alert. Search efforts have since been initiated, with local law enforcement coordinating closely with Kirtland Air Force Base.

Col. Justin Secrest, commander of the 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland, has confirmed that the base is assisting local authorities. Investigators are currently focusing on retracing McCasland's last known movements and gathering tips from the public.

Official statements

“We are working closely with local law enforcement and will defer updates to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office,” said Col. Justin Secrest.
U.S. Representative Melanie Stansbury publicly acknowledged McCasland’s disappearance, noting his longstanding service in the region.

Context

William Neil McCasland has had a distinguished career in the Air Force, having been commissioned in 1979 after graduating from the United States Air Force Academy. He held various senior leadership roles, including commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio, overseeing a significant science and technology portfolio. While discussions online have linked his past roles to speculation about advanced aerospace research, authorities have stated that there is no indication his professional background is related to his current situation.

What’s next

The search for McCasland will continue as authorities gather tips and information from the public. Investigators remain focused on tracing his last known movements while maintaining that there is no evidence of foul play at this time.

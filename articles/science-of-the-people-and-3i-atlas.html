Science of the People and 3I/ATLAS

On November 27, 2025, the NEOSSat 0.15-meter Maksutov orbiting telescope captured an image of comet 3I/ATLAS at a distance of 1.956 astronomical units (AU) from Earth and 1.711 AU from the Sun. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

This sighting is significant as it highlights the ongoing efforts in the search for extraterrestrial knowledge. According to scientists, including Avi Loeb, the public's curiosity and funding for scientific endeavors are critical for advancing our understanding of the universe. Authorities are emphasizing the need for open communication between scientists and the public to foster interest and engagement in scientific research.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: November 27, 2025
  • Distance from Earth: 1.956 AU
  • Distance from the Sun: 1.711 AU
  • Telescope used: NEOSSat 0.15-meter Maksutov
  • Exposure duration: 330 seconds
  • Field of view: 30 x 25 arcminutes
  • Image orientation: North-up/east-left

Timeline

On November 27, 2025, the NEOSSat telescope successfully captured the image of comet 3I/ATLAS. This event occurred as part of a broader initiative to monitor and study celestial bodies within our solar system.

The image was taken during a 330-second exposure, allowing scientists to collect detailed data about the comet's characteristics and trajectory.

Official statements

“The scientific declaration of independence asks instead that we attend to the public’s curiosity because the public funds science,” said Avi Loeb in a recent interview.

Context

The 2020 Decadal Survey on Astronomy and Astrophysics has underscored the importance of the search for molecular signatures of life beyond Earth. This aligns with ongoing efforts in the scientific community to engage the public in discussions about astronomical discoveries.

Previous initiatives have faced challenges in effectively communicating scientific findings to the public. Scientists have noted that a one-way communication model limits public engagement and understanding.

What’s next

Authorities plan to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS and will provide updates as new data becomes available. Future public outreach efforts will focus on enhancing communication between scientists and the community to promote interest in space exploration.

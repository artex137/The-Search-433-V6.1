Did 3I/ATLAS Behave Oddly Near the Sun? Here Are 3 Shocking Observations

Comet 3I/ATLAS has exhibited unusual behaviors during its approach to the Sun, surprising astronomers in late October 2025. The comet brightened rapidly, turned blue, and displayed an “anti-tail” phenomenon, leading to multiple theories about its composition and movement.

This observation is significant as it challenges existing knowledge of comet behavior and raises questions about the mechanisms driving these changes. Astronomers rely on space-based telescopes, such as NASA's STEREO-A and ESA's SOHO, to monitor the comet, which is currently positioned behind the Sun from Earth’s perspective.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has shown rapid brightening and a blue appearance.
  • The anti-tail phenomenon was first observed in September 2022.
  • The Jet Propulsion Laboratory reported non-gravitational acceleration during perihelion.
  • NASA's STEREO-A and ESA's SOHO have been used for observation.

Timeline

In September 2022, astronomers first observed the unusual anti-tail of comet 3I/ATLAS, which points toward the Sun, contradicting typical comet behavior. By late October 2025, the comet exhibited rapid brightening and a distinct blue color as it approached the Sun.

During perihelion, the closest point to the Sun, NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory noted that the comet experienced non-gravitational acceleration, suggesting unusual forces at play.

Official statements

“The bright blue color, reversed anti-tail and strange changes in speed of Comet 3I/ATLAS has made it one of the strangest interstellar visitors to date,” said a representative from NASA. “Scientists continue to investigate the comet to better understand the causes behind these strangeness.”

Context

Comet behavior is typically characterized by tails that point away from the Sun due to solar wind. The emergence of an anti-tail is rare and poses challenges to existing models of comet physics. Previous comets have not exhibited the same rapid brightening or unusual movement patterns observed in 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Ongoing investigations will continue to explore the unusual properties of comet 3I/ATLAS. Scientists are analyzing data from space-based telescopes and await further insights from upcoming observations.

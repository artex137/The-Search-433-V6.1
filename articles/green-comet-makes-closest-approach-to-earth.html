Green comet makes closest approach to Earth

An interstellar comet, 3I/Atlas, has made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, passing at a distance of 170 million miles. There are no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this event.

This occurrence is significant as it marks only the third time that a comet from outside the Solar System has been identified. Authorities, including NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA), are using the fly-by to conduct a planetary defence exercise involving 21 countries. This exercise focuses on impact probability modeling and emergency coordination.

Key facts

  • Date of closest approach: December 19, 2025
  • Distance from Earth: 170 million miles
  • Location in the sky: Constellation Leo
  • Time of highest visibility: Around 5 AM on December 19
  • Number of countries involved in the exercise: 21

Timeline

The following key events have occurred:

  • 3I/Atlas was discovered by amateur astronomers at the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (Atlas).
  • The comet has been gradually growing brighter as it approaches Earth.
  • The planetary defence exercise is coordinated by the International Asteroid Warning Network (IAWN).

Official statements

“While it poses no threat, comet 3I/Atlas presents a great opportunity for the IAWN community to perform an observing exercise due to its prolonged observability from Earth and high interest to the scientific community,” said an IAWN spokesman.

Context

Only two other interstellar objects have been spotted in our Solar System prior to 3I/Atlas. The first, Oumuamua, was observed in 2017 and exhibited unusual acceleration. The second, 2I/Borisov, was identified in 2019 and is believed to have originated near a red dwarf star. Experts believe that studying interstellar comets can provide valuable insights into conditions beyond our Solar System.

What’s next

Experts will continue to monitor 3I/Atlas as it moves away from the Sun and Earth. The planetary defence exercise aims to improve capabilities for measuring comet positions and enhancing emergency response coordination.

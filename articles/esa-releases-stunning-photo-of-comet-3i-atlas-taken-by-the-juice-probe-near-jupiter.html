ESA releases stunning photo of comet 3I/ATLAS taken by the Juice probe near Jupiter

The European Space Agency (ESA) has released a stunning photograph of comet 3I/ATLAS, captured by the Juice probe during its flyby near Jupiter. The image was taken on March 3, 2026, as the probe approached the comet.

This photograph is significant as it provides new insights into the characteristics and composition of comet 3I/ATLAS. The ESA aims to enhance our understanding of the solar system's formation and evolution through such missions.

Key facts

  • Event date: March 3, 2026
  • Agency: European Space Agency (ESA)
  • Mission: Juice probe
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Location: Near Jupiter

Timeline

On March 3, 2026, the Juice probe successfully captured an image of comet 3I/ATLAS while conducting a flyby near Jupiter. This event is part of the probe's extended mission to study various celestial bodies in the outer solar system.

Official statements

“This photograph represents a significant milestone in our exploration of comets and their role in the solar system,” said an ESA spokesperson.

Context

The Juice probe, launched in April 2023, is designed to study Jupiter and its moons. The mission aims to gather data that will help scientists understand the conditions that may support life beyond Earth.

What’s next

The ESA will continue to analyze the data received from the Juice probe and plans further observations of comet 3I/ATLAS and other celestial bodies in the coming months.

NASA’s Webb Space Telescope Observes Interstellar Comet

NASA’s James Webb Space Telescope has observed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on August 6, 2023, using its Near-Infrared Spectrograph instrument. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this observation.

This observation is significant as it contributes to the understanding of the comet's size, physical properties, and chemical makeup. NASA's space telescopes, including the Hubble Space Telescope and the newly launched SPHEREx mission, are collaborating to gather comprehensive data on the comet, which poses no threat to Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet observed: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of observation: August 6, 2023
  • Instruments used: Near-Infrared Spectrograph (Webb), Hubble Space Telescope, SPHEREx mission
  • Location: Not disclosed
  • Agency involved: NASA

Timeline

On August 6, 2023, NASA’s James Webb Space Telescope captured images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The research team has since begun analyzing the data collected from this observation. A preprint detailing their findings has been made available online.

Official statements

“NASA’s space telescopes help support the agency’s ongoing mission to find, track, and better understand solar system objects,” said Abbey Interrante, a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is part of NASA's broader efforts to monitor and study celestial objects that pass through our solar system. The collaboration between various space telescopes enhances the agency's capability to gather valuable data on such objects.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze the data from the Webb Space Telescope and other instruments to deepen its understanding of comet 3I/ATLAS and inform future observations.

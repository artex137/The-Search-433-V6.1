The Mass-Budget Discrepancy of 3I/ATLAS

In a recent paper published on March 11, 2026, Avi Loeb has highlighted significant discrepancies in the mass budget of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The study indicates that the inferred mass density of 3I/ATLAS-like objects is much larger than the amount of heavy elements found in low metallicity stars. This revelation could reshape our understanding of planetary systems in the Milky Way galaxy.

Loeb's findings raise important questions regarding the composition and origin of 3I/ATLAS, prompting further investigation by astronomers. The implications of this study suggest that the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS may challenge existing theories about the formation of interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Date of publication: March 11, 2026
  • Author: Avi Loeb
  • Object studied: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Inferred nucleus radius: 1.3 ± 0.2 km
  • Interstellar number density: approximately 7 × 10−3 au−3
  • Inferred nucleus mass: approximately 4.6 × 1015 grams
  • Local interstellar mass density: approximately 10−26 g/cm3

Timeline

Recent observations from the Hubble Space Telescope have provided crucial data for the study of 3I/ATLAS. The inferred nucleus radius and number density have been calculated based on these observations. The findings suggest a mass density for the population of 3I/ATLAS-like objects that exceeds the available reservoir of heavy elements in low metallicity stars.

Loeb's paper also references two other studies that reported unusual isotope abundances in the material of 3I/ATLAS, reinforcing the need for further analysis of its origin and composition.

Official statements

“Either the inferred radius or number density are overestimated or the association with metal-poor stars is incorrect,” said Avi Loeb in his paper.

Context

The study of 3I/ATLAS is part of a broader investigation into the characteristics of interstellar objects. Previous findings have indicated that many such objects may originate from metal-poor stars in the Milky Way. However, Loeb's analysis suggests that the current understanding of these origins may need to be reevaluated.

What’s next

Further investigations are needed to clarify the discrepancies in the mass budget of 3I/ATLAS. Astronomers will likely continue to analyze data from various telescopes to better understand the object's composition and origins.

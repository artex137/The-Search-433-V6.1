3I/ATLAS Interstellar Comet Live Tracker: Date, time, and how to watch, when it peaks

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is set to reach its closest point to the Sun on Wednesday, October 29, at around 11:47 Universal Time (UT). The comet will approach to about 1.36 astronomical units from the Sun, just beyond Earth’s orbital distance.

This event is significant as 3I/ATLAS is only the third known interstellar visitor, identified due to its hyperbolic orbit. NASA has indicated that the comet will swing just inside Mars’ orbit when it reaches perihelion on October 30, 2025, at a distance of about 1.4 astronomical units, or approximately 210 million kilometers. Scientists expect the comet will not be visible from Earth until November, as it will be positioned on the far side of the Sun.

Key facts

  • Closest approach to the Sun: October 29, 2023, at 11:47 UT
  • Distance from the Sun at closest approach: 1.36 astronomical units
  • Perihelion date: October 30, 2025
  • Distance from the Sun at perihelion: 1.4 astronomical units (approximately 210 million kilometers)
  • Visibility from Earth: Expected in November 2023
  • Origin: Deep interstellar space

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been tracked since its discovery, with its hyperbolic orbit indicating it originated from outside the solar system. The comet is expected to reach its closest point to the Sun on October 29, 2023.

On October 30, 2025, it will pass just inside Mars’ orbit, marking a significant event for astronomers monitoring interstellar objects.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS is only the third known visitor from beyond the solar system,” NASA stated, highlighting the importance of its trajectory and origin.

Context

The observation of interstellar comets is rare, with 3I/ATLAS following the previous discoveries of 1I/2017 U1 (Oumuamua) and 2I/Borisov. These objects have provided valuable insights into the composition and dynamics of materials from outside our solar system.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches its perihelion, scientists will continue to monitor its trajectory and prepare for the anticipated visibility in November 2023.

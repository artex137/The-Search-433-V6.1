Juice Spacecraft Records Stunning Anomalies As 3I/ATLAS Tells A Story Far More Extraordinary Than Anything Published In Official Press Releases

On October 29, 2025, the European Space Agency’s (ESA) Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer, known as Juice, encountered the comet 3I/ATLAS during its flyby, approximately 203 million kilometers from the Sun. Observatories on Earth had no direct line of sight to the comet as it passed behind the Sun, rendering it invisible to terrestrial telescopes.

This encounter is significant as it marks the first time a spacecraft designed for studying Jupiter's moons has observed an interstellar object. ESA's decision to deploy five scientific instruments on Juice to analyze 3I/ATLAS has opened new avenues for understanding the composition and behavior of interstellar visitors.

Key facts

  • Date of encounter: October 29, 2025
  • Distance from the Sun: 203 million kilometers
  • Instruments used: Five scientific instruments aboard Juice
  • Agency involved: European Space Agency (ESA)
  • Object studied: Comet 3I/ATLAS

Timeline

On October 29, 2025, 3I/ATLAS reached its perihelion, becoming invisible to Earth-based observations as it moved behind the Sun. A week later, Juice, which was not intended to study the comet, found itself in close proximity to the object. ESA made the decision to activate multiple scientific instruments to gather data on the gas and dust surrounding 3I/ATLAS, which defied typical cometary behavior.

Official statements

“The glowing halo of gas and dust observed around 3I/ATLAS challenges our understanding of cometary emissions,” ESA stated regarding the findings from Juice.

Context

Prior to this event, 3I/ATLAS was noted for its unusual trajectory and characteristics, raising questions within the scientific community about the nature of interstellar objects. The encounter with Juice has prompted a re-evaluation of existing models of cometary physics.

What’s next

ESA plans to analyze the data collected by Juice to further understand the anomalies observed around 3I/ATLAS. The results may influence future research and exploration strategies regarding interstellar objects.

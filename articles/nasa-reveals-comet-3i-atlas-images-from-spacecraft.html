NASA Reveals Comet 3I/ATLAS Images From Spacecraft

NASA has announced a live event at 3 p.m. EST on Wednesday, November 19, to share images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, collected by various agency missions. The event will take place at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland.

The appearance of comet 3I/ATLAS is significant as it is only the third object ever identified as entering our solar system from outside the galaxy. NASA officials have stated that while the comet poses no threat to Earth, it did come within 19 million miles of Mars in early October.

Key facts

  • Event date and time: November 19, at 3 p.m. EST
  • Location: NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt, Maryland
  • Discovered: July 1, by the ATLAS observatory
  • Closest approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Closest approach to Mars: 19 million miles in early October
  • Broadcast platforms: NASA+, NASA app, agency's website, YouTube, Amazon Prime
  • Participants: Amit Kshatriya, Nicky Fox, Shawn Domagal-Goldman, Tom Statler

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1 by the NASA-funded ATLAS observatory. It has been identified as an interstellar object, marking it as a rare addition to our solar system. In early October, the comet made its closest approach to Mars, coming within 19 million miles of the planet.

The upcoming live event on November 19 will showcase images of the comet captured by NASA missions, allowing the public to engage with the findings.

Official statements

“Comet 3I/ATLAS is a remarkable discovery that helps us understand more about our solar system and the objects that come from beyond it,” said NASA Associate Administrator Amit Kshatriya.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, only two other interstellar objects have been identified entering our solar system. The study of such objects provides valuable insights into the formation and evolution of celestial bodies outside our solar system.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS and share updates following the live event, focusing on the images and data collected by its missions.

Hubble Telescope reveals symmetric jets and unusual rotation of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

New observations by the Hubble Space Telescope have provided significant insights into the behavior of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. These observations, captured in late 2025, reveal complex gas and dust jets that challenge existing models of celestial dynamics.

The findings are crucial for understanding the composition and rotation of 3I/ATLAS, an unusual object from another star system. Scientists aim to decipher the nature of this comet and the physical processes at play, as the data collected may reshape current theories about cometary activity.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed by Hubble in late 2025.
  • The main jet extends over 100,000 kilometers and forms a prominent "antitail" directed towards Sol.
  • Three distinct jets have been identified, with a 120-degree angular separation between them.
  • The rotation period of the comet's nucleus is approximately 16 hours.
  • The gas ejection speed from the jets is about 0.2 kilometers per second.
  • The probability of the comet's rotation axis aligning with Sol is estimated at 0.2%.

Timeline

In late 2025, the Hubble Space Telescope captured a series of images of comet 3I/ATLAS. Analysis of these images allowed astronomers to observe the evolution of the comet’s gas jets in real time. Observations revealed a complex jet structure with unusual symmetry, prompting further investigation into the comet’s dynamics and composition.

As researchers analyzed the data, they estimated the rotation period of the comet’s nucleus and examined the behavior of the gas jets. The findings raised questions about the physical properties of 3I/ATLAS and its formation processes.

Official statements

“The complexity of the jets and their interaction with solar radiation provide valuable clues about the materials present on the comet's surface,” said a spokesperson for the Hubble team.
“The stability of the antitail and the symmetry of the jets challenge our understanding of cometary dynamics,” the spokesperson added.

Context

The observations of 3I/ATLAS may represent a significant advancement in cometary studies. Previous interstellar objects have not shown such intricate jet structures or the same level of stability. This could indicate that 3I/ATLAS has unique physical properties that differentiate it from comets originating in our Solar System.

What’s next

Future research will focus on comparing 3I/ATLAS data with other interstellar objects and comets from our Solar System. Advanced simulations and continued observations will help clarify the comet's unique characteristics and origins.

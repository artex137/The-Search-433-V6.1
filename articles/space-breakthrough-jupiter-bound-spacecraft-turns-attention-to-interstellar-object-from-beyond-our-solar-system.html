Space breakthrough: Jupiter-bound spacecraft turns attention to interstellar object from beyond our solar system

The European Space Agency's billion-dollar Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) has redirected its instruments toward Comet 3I ATLAS, an interstellar object originating from beyond our solar system. This development has occurred during Juice's eight-year mission, which began in 2023, to study Jupiter and its moons.

This shift in focus is significant as it allows scientists to gather data on a celestial body that provides insights into the early solar system. The European Space Agency (ESA) has stated that this opportunity could enhance our understanding of cometary formation and interstellar materials.

Key facts

  • Juice launched in 2023.
  • Comet 3I ATLAS is an interstellar object.
  • Juice is on an eight-year mission to study Jupiter.
  • The mission is funded at a cost of one billion dollars.
  • ESA has not disclosed the specific time of the focus shift.

Timeline

In 2023, the European Space Agency launched Juice on its mission to explore Jupiter's moons. As Juice travels through space, it has now identified Comet 3I ATLAS as a target for study. The exact timing of this focus shift has not been disclosed.

Official statements

“This is a unique opportunity to study a comet that has traveled from beyond our solar system,” said an ESA spokesperson.

Context

Interstellar objects, like Comet 3I ATLAS, have gained attention in recent years. They provide valuable data for understanding the composition of materials that exist outside our solar system and their role in the formation of planetary systems.

What’s next

ESA scientists will continue to monitor and collect data on Comet 3I ATLAS as Juice progresses on its journey to Jupiter. The specific timeline for further observations and findings has not been disclosed.

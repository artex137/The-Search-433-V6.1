NASA Unveils Stunning New Images of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS in Space

NASA has released new images of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, confirming its status as a comet, on November 23, 2025. The images were captured during the comet’s solar conjunction, when it was positioned on the far side of the Sun relative to Earth.

This discovery has drawn significant scientific interest, as researchers aim to study the properties of 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun. The comet offers a rare opportunity to learn about objects originating outside our solar system, which could provide insights into the formation and evolution of galaxies.

Key facts

  • Discovered on July 1, 2025
  • Captured images during solar conjunction
  • Originates from either the thin disk or thick disk of the Milky Way
  • Estimated age of over seven billion years
  • Significant scientific interest from researchers

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Chile. Following its discovery, NASA captured images of the comet during its solar conjunction.

The images taken during this unique position have allowed astronomers to conduct important research on the comet's properties and behavior as it moves closer to the Sun.

Official statements

“The observations help scientists understand the composition and behavior of interstellar comets,” a NASA representative stated. “3I/ATLAS provides a valuable opportunity to study objects originating outside our solar system.”

Context

The Royal Astronomical Society has indicated that 3I/ATLAS likely originated from one of two regions within the Milky Way, either the galaxy’s thin disk or the thick disk. If it comes from the thick disk, it may be older than the solar system itself, highlighting its ancient nature and unique origin.

What’s next

Nasa plans to continue its research on 3I/ATLAS, which may provide further insights into the dynamics of interstellar objects and expand our knowledge of the cosmos.

What was that 'pinwheel' in the sky on Nov. 4? It wasn't a UFO

A strange "pinwheel" in the sky appeared on Tuesday, November 4, 2025, captivating stargazers across Massachusetts. The event prompted a flurry of speculation on social media, but authorities have confirmed that the sighting was not related to any unidentified flying object (UFO).

This sighting has generated significant public interest, leading local astronomers and officials to clarify the nature of the phenomenon. The Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) has stated that the "pinwheel" was likely a visual effect caused by a recent rocket launch.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: November 4, 2025
  • Location: Massachusetts
  • Cause of sighting: Rocket launch
  • Public response: Increased speculation on social media
  • Agency involved: Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

Timeline

On the evening of November 4, 2025, many individuals reported seeing a peculiar "pinwheel" formation in the sky. The reports quickly spread across social media, with users proposing various explanations, including UFO sightings.

By the following day, November 5, local authorities and astronomers began to investigate the reports. MIT confirmed that the sighting was related to a rocket launch, dispelling the UFO theories that had circulated.

Official statements

“The sighting was likely a visual effect from a recent rocket launch,” said a representative from MIT.

Context

This incident follows a series of astronomical events that have captured public attention throughout the year. Previous sightings have often led to discussions about UFOs and the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

Authorities have urged the public to rely on scientific explanations rather than speculative theories when observing unusual phenomena in the sky.

What’s next

As of now, no further investigations are planned regarding the "pinwheel" sighting. Authorities continue to monitor public interest in astronomical events and provide information as needed.

Alien Comet 3I/ATLAS Is Just About to Make Its Closest Approach to Earth

On 19 December 2025, interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Earth, reaching a distance of approximately 270 million kilometers (168 million miles). This event offers ground-based observatories and comet enthusiasts a final opportunity to study the comet as it exits the Solar System. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this celestial event.

The close approach of 3I/ATLAS is significant for scientists and astronomers, who aim to gather valuable data about its unique characteristics. Observatories around the world are preparing to observe the comet before it disappears from view. Once it leaves, it will not return.

Key facts

  • Date of closest approach: 19 December 2025
  • Distance from Earth at perigee: 270 million kilometers (168 million miles)
  • Discovery date: 1 July 2025
  • Notable observations: High levels of carbon dioxide, hydrogen cyanide, methanol, nickel, and iron
  • Closest approach to the Sun: 29 October 2025

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025. In the months following its discovery, researchers noted unusual characteristics, including high levels of carbon dioxide and other gases in its coma. The comet made a close pass to Mars in early October 2025, which was recorded by various observatories.

On 29 October 2025, 3I/ATLAS reached perihelion, its closest point to the Sun, after which its visibility diminished as it moved behind the Sun from Earth's perspective. Observations resumed when it emerged from behind the Sun, with the ESA's XMM-Newton observatory recording data on the comet's X-radiation. In late November, the Gemini North telescope observed a color change in the comet's appearance, hinting at the formation of diatomic carbon.

Official statements

"This object is a comet," NASA associate administrator Amit Kshatriya said in November. "It looks and behaves like a comet, and all evidence points to it being a comet. But this one came from outside the Solar System, which makes it fascinating, exciting, and scientifically very important."

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable for its peculiar composition, which includes a potentially extreme abundance of metals and unusual gas emissions. Its behavior raises questions about the nature of comets originating from outside the Solar System. Previous interstellar objects, such as 'Oumuamua, have also sparked interest in the scientific community, but 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity for in-depth study.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches its closest point to Earth, astronomers will conduct further observations to understand its composition and behavior. The data collected may provide insights into interstellar space and the formation of comets.

Fake AI-Generated Channel of Videos About 3I/ATLAS Removed by YouTube

YouTube has removed a fake AI-generated channel that featured misleading videos about the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, following a request from Avi Loeb on December 8, 2025. The channel was reportedly created to exploit Loeb's popularity and misinform viewers, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this incident.

The removal of the channel is significant as it highlights the issue of misinformation in digital media. Avi Loeb, a prominent astrophysicist, has expressed concern over the spread of false content, which he claims undermines public understanding of scientific facts. Following communication with YouTube representatives, Loeb has sought to hold the creators of the fake videos accountable for defamation and false content.

Key facts

  • Date of removal: December 8, 2025
  • Channel name: Not disclosed
  • Number of followers: Approximately 10 million for Loeb's actual content
  • Emails received by Loeb: Hundreds from fans
  • Number of reports filed to YouTube: Dozens
  • Incident of facial injury: Occurred two weeks prior to the removal

Timeline

In late November 2025, Avi Loeb began receiving emails from fans regarding a YouTube channel featuring fake videos about 3I/ATLAS. The videos were created using artificial intelligence and contradicted Loeb's own writings.

On December 8, 2025, Loeb contacted YouTube to report the channel, citing violations of the platform's community guidelines and Terms of Service. He detailed the discrepancies in the videos, including a frozen clock and the absence of visible injuries he sustained during a recent trip.

Later that same day, YouTube removed the channel in response to Loeb's claims and evidence of misinformation.

Official statements

“Over the past two weeks, I received hundreds of emails from fans who noticed a YouTube channel named after me with fake videos about the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, created by artificial intelligence (AI).” - Avi Loeb
“In using AI to generate fake videos impersonating me, this YouTube channel violates several aspects of YouTube’s community guidelines and Terms of Service.” - Avi Loeb

Context

This incident reflects a growing trend of misinformation on digital platforms, where AI-generated content can easily mislead the public. Previous incidents of similar nature have raised concerns among scientists and content creators about the integrity of information shared online.

YouTube has faced scrutiny in the past over its policies regarding misinformation and the enforcement of its community guidelines. The platform continues to adapt its measures to combat the spread of false content.

What’s next

Avi Loeb plans to pursue legal action against the creators of the fake videos. He aims to raise awareness about the risks of misinformation and to ensure accountability for those who produce and distribute false content online.

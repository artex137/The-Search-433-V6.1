60th anniversary of famous Michigan UFO sighting coming up

The 60th anniversary of Michigan's most famous UFO sighting will occur on March 20, 2026, in Dexter, Michigan. On that day in 1966, Frank Manor reported seeing an unusual object in the sky, sparking ongoing debates about unidentified flying objects.

This anniversary is significant as it highlights public interest in UFOs and ongoing investigations. Sam Maranto, the Great Lakes director of the Mutual UFO Network (MUFON), noted that the sighting has led to various interpretations over the years. In a recent development, President Donald Trump has directed the release of government documents regarding unidentified aerial phenomena, which may provide further insights into such cases.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: March 20, 1966
  • Location: Dexter, Michigan
  • Witness: Frank Manor
  • Reported object: Red, blue, and white craft
  • Subsequent sightings: Hillsdale College students reported a similar object shortly after
  • Official explanation: Described as swamp gas
  • Government documents on UFOs to be released by President Donald Trump

Timeline

On March 20, 1966, Frank Manor observed an object that initially appeared to be a meteorite. The object then reportedly stopped over trees on Manor's property and changed colors from red to blue and white. Manor and his son went to investigate further.

Following this sighting, Hillsdale College students reported seeing a similar craft in the air days later. Experts at the time attributed these sightings to swamp gas, a conclusion that has been met with skepticism by some.

Official statements

“It initially looked like a meteorite, but then what was a red object stopped over the trees in the distance on their property and turned, I believe he said, blue and white,” said Sam Maranto, Great Lakes director of MUFON.
“To say the least, this became a big hella blue out there,” Maranto added, reflecting on the public's reaction to the sightings.

Context

The 1966 sighting in Dexter has become a notable case in the study of UFOs, contributing to the broader conversation about unidentified aerial phenomena. The term "swamp gas" has since been used in various contexts to explain unusual sightings, but many continue to question the validity of this explanation.

With the upcoming release of government documents on UFOs, there may be renewed interest and scrutiny regarding past incidents and current policies on aerial phenomena.

What’s next

As the 60th anniversary approaches, investigators and enthusiasts alike will look forward to the release of government documents on unidentified aerial phenomena, which could shed light on historic sightings and ongoing inquiries into UFOs.

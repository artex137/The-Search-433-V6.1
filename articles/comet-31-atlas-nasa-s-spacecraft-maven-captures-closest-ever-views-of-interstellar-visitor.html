Comet 31/ATLAS: NASA’s Spacecraft MAVEN Captures Closest-Ever Views of Interstellar Visitor

NASA has released new imagery of Comet 31/ATLAS, captured in October 2023 by multiple spacecraft, including the MAVEN (Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution) mission. The Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO), MAVEN, and the Perseverance rover observed the comet as it passed Mars, marking a significant event in the study of interstellar objects.

This event is notable as it provides scientists with rare insights into a comet that originated outside the solar system. The MAVEN mission, launched in 2013, is dedicated to studying Mars’ upper atmosphere. The ultraviolet images captured by MAVEN have revealed hydrogen signatures, allowing for analysis of the comet’s chemical composition.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 2023
  • Location: Near Mars
  • Spacecraft involved: MAVEN, Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, Perseverance rover
  • Mission launch date: 2013
  • Significance: First spacecraft designed exclusively for Mars’ upper atmosphere study
  • Notable findings: Hydrogen signatures detected

Timeline

In October 2023, Comet 31/ATLAS made its closest approach to Mars. During this event, MAVEN, along with the MRO and Perseverance rover, observed the comet. The collaboration of these spacecraft allowed for unprecedented imaging and analysis of the interstellar visitor.

Official statements

“These images represent the closest view yet of the comet, offering scientists valuable insights into an object from outside our solar system,” NASA officials stated.

Context

The MAVEN mission has been pivotal in expanding our understanding of Mars’ atmosphere since its launch. The study of interstellar objects like Comet 31/ATLAS is crucial for understanding the origins of our solar system and the composition of celestial bodies.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the data collected from MAVEN and other spacecraft to deepen their understanding of Comet 31/ATLAS and its implications for solar system research.

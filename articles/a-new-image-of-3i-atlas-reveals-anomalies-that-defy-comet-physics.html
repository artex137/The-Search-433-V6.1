A New Image of 3I/ATLAS Reveals Anomalies That Defy Comet Physics

On November 16, 2025, astrophotographer Satoru Murata captured a striking image of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS using a Celestron EdgeHD 800 telescope. The new image reveals unexpected structural and energetic behaviors that challenge current scientific understanding of comets.

This development is significant as it raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Researchers are examining the image for insights into the object’s composition and behavior. The findings may lead to a reevaluation of existing theories about cometary physics.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 16, 2025
  • Photographer: Satoru Murata
  • Telescope used: Celestron EdgeHD 800
  • Notable features: Perfect circular symmetry, high luminosity, smooth structure
  • Related scientific anomaly: Jets and coma structures that do not smear despite rotation

Timeline

Since its discovery, 3I/ATLAS has attracted significant attention from the scientific community. The most recent image, captured on November 16, 2025, provided new insights into the object's structure. Researchers have applied advanced forensic analysis techniques to study the image, leading to the identification of anomalies that could reshape cometary physics.

The anomalies observed in the coma of 3I/ATLAS highlight a potential energy source that sustains its stable structure, which is atypical for natural comets. This finding aligns with previous research by Avi Loeb, who identified similar anomalies in the behavior of the object.

Official statements

“The results raise profound questions about what 3I/ATLAS actually is,” said Satoru Murata regarding his analysis of the newly captured image.

Context

3I/ATLAS is an interstellar comet that has been under scrutiny since its discovery. Previous observations have noted unusual features, but the latest image has intensified interest in its characteristics. The scientific community is increasingly focused on understanding the implications of these anomalies for cometary behavior and astrophysics.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the new image of 3I/ATLAS. Further investigations are expected to explore the implications of the observed anomalies and how they may influence current theories about comet formation and behavior.

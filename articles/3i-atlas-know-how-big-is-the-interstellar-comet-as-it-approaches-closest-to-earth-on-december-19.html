3I/ATLAS: Know How Big Is The Interstellar Comet As It Approaches Closest To Earth On December 19

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will fly closest to Earth on December 19, 2025, with a minimum orbit intersection distance of 0.36 astronomical units (AU). The comet, also known as C/2025 N1 (ATLAS), was discovered on July 1, 2025. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

This approach is significant as it marks the third interstellar visitor to the solar system, following 1I/Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Authorities, including NASA and the Minor Planet Center (MPC), have monitored its trajectory and speed, which currently exceeds 61 km/s. The comet has been temporarily listed on the Near-Earth Object Confirmation Page (NEOCP) while scientists confirm its trajectory.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1)
  • Date of discovery: July 1, 2025
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Minimum orbit intersection distance: 0.36 AU
  • Current speed: 61 km/s
  • Estimated nucleus size: Between 320 meters and 5.6 kilometers
  • Estimated mass: Approximately 33 billion tons

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025. It reached its closest point to the sun, known as perihelion, on October 29, 2025, at a distance of 1.35 AU. The comet is expected to approach Earth on December 19, 2025, becoming a subject of interest for scientists and space enthusiasts.

Official statements

Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysicist, referred to the comet’s perihelion as a "black swan event" and noted that the comet exhibits several anomalies which could suggest it is more than just a comet.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, the solar system has seen two other interstellar visitors: 1I/Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. These events have heightened interest in the study of interstellar objects and their potential implications for our understanding of the universe.

What’s next

NASA and other agencies will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth. Further studies will aim to confirm its trajectory and analyze its composition during its closest approach.

The Most Surprising Part of Our Sedona UFO Tour Was What We Couldn’t See

A family trip to Sedona, Arizona, culminated in a UFO tour on Monday night, where participants observed unusual lights through night vision goggles, but nothing was visible to the naked eye. The tour took place near Red Canyon Overlook, approximately 14 miles outside Sedona, with no reported injuries or fatalities.

This event has drawn attention to the ongoing fascination with UFOs and the unexplained phenomena often associated with them. Local tour guides provided infrared equipment to enhance the viewing experience, as participants waited for potential sightings in the darkened desert landscape.

Key facts

  • Date: Monday night
  • Location: Red Canyon Overlook, 14 miles outside Sedona, Arizona
  • Notable landmarks: Bradshaw Ranch, Hide Out Cave
  • Weather: Cloudy with some rain, lightning visible
  • Participants: Family members including the writer and their sister

Timeline

The UFO tour began with participants parking at Red Canyon Overlook, where they were greeted by tour guides. After some initial rain, conditions improved, allowing for a clear night sky.

As night fell, the group used night vision goggles to scan the landscape. Participants reported seeing a flickering light near the top of a distant mountain, which was not visible without the goggles. The group continued to observe the area, with one participant claiming to see two lights moving together.

Later, another member of the group reported a large flash of light that illuminated the area, while an orange light was spotted in the sky, which some speculated could be a plane.

Official statements

“It was a peaceful, yet uneventful night,” said the writer, reflecting on the overall experience of the tour.
“I thought maybe the big light was slowly moving towards the ground,” said the writer’s sister, who was skeptical about the sightings.

Context

Sedona has a reputation as a hotspot for paranormal activity, attracting visitors interested in UFOs and unexplained phenomena. The use of night vision goggles during tours has become a popular method for enhancing the experience, allowing participants to see objects otherwise hidden in darkness.

Reports of unusual lights and sightings in the area have fueled ongoing discussions about the existence of UFOs and the interest from both the public and authorities.

What’s next

There are currently no investigations or official follow-ups regarding the sightings from the tour. Participants may continue to discuss their experiences, contributing to the ongoing dialogue about UFOs in Sedona.

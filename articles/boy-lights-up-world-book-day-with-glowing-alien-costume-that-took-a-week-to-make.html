Boy lights up World Book Day with glowing alien costume that took a week to make

A 10-year-old boy, Dalton Evans, has showcased a homemade alien costume and glowing UFO for World Book Day at St Gabriel's CofE in Basingstoke. The costume, inspired by the book Turns Out I’m an Alien by Lou Treleaven, took a week to create.

This event highlights the creativity of children and the importance of reading. Schools across the UK and Ireland participate in World Book Day to encourage children to engage with literature through costume and character celebration.

Key facts

  • Dalton Evans is a student at St Gabriel's CofE in Basingstoke.
  • The costume took one week to create.
  • Dalton's mother, Lindsay Prime, assisted in the project.
  • World Book Day is celebrated on the first Thursday of March.
  • Photos of costumes will be published in the Basingstoke Gazette on March 12.

Timeline

Dalton Evans has been reading since the age of 2 and decided to dress as an alien after reading Turns Out I’m an Alien. He and his mother began working on the costume a week prior to World Book Day, using materials such as cardboard, duct tape, and LED strip lights.

The costume features a dome made from an umbrella and a spaceship adorned with spray-painted cardboard and screws. The family collaborated on the project, enhancing Dalton's creative experience.

Official statements

"He has always loved all things space from a very young age, and he has been reading the book Turns Out I’m an Alien by Lou Treleaven," said Lindsay Prime, Dalton's mother.
"He decided he wanted to dress as an alien and thought it would be cool to have his own UFO," Prime added.

Context

World Book Day, held annually on the first Thursday of March, aims to promote reading among children. Schools across the UK and Ireland encourage students to dress as their favourite characters, fostering a love of literature and creativity.

Dalton's project exemplifies the spirit of this initiative, showcasing how families can engage in educational activities together.

What’s next

The Basingstoke Gazette will publish a selection of the best World Book Day costume photos on March 12, highlighting the creativity of local children.

"I definitely saw an unidentified flying object that night." Why this heavy metal icon is convinced he saw a UFO one fateful night in North Carolina

Published 2026-03-02 13:01 UTC

Randy Blythe Claims UFO Sighting in North Carolina

Randy Blythe, the frontman of the heavy metal band Lamb Of God, has reported seeing an unidentified flying object while in North Carolina. The sighting occurred near his rental house on Oak Island, where he was with friends on an unspecified date.

This revelation has garnered attention as it adds to ongoing discussions about UFO sightings and public interest in extraterrestrial life. Authorities have not commented on this specific sighting, and no investigations have been announced regarding the incident.

Key facts

  • Blythe was on Oak Island, North Carolina.
  • The sighting occurred at night, but the exact date is not disclosed.
  • There were three witnesses: Blythe, T-Roy from Sourvein, and his girlfriend.
  • The UFO exhibited two sets of four bright lights in a square pattern.
  • Blythe has described the UFO's speed as "incredible."

Timeline

Blythe has recounted that while sitting on the front porch of his rental house, he and his friends observed the lights over the ocean. The lights moved horizontally and then shot upwards at a remarkable speed. This experience left the witnesses feeling "freaked out."

Blythe acknowledged that while he is aware of military aircraft capabilities, he believes what he saw could not be explained by known technology. He expressed a general belief in the possibility of intelligent life beyond Earth.

Official statements

“I definitely saw an unidentified flying object that night,” Blythe stated during an interview with Metal Hammer.
“I’m quite aware of our military take-off vehicles, and they can’t go that fast,” he added, explaining his reasoning for believing the lights were not drones.

Context

The sighting contributes to a growing body of anecdotal reports about UFOs, which have gained traction in recent years. Public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena has surged, with many individuals sharing similar experiences. However, formal investigations by authorities into such claims remain sporadic.

What’s next

There are currently no known investigations or follow-up actions regarding Blythe's sighting. Lamb Of God is set to release their twelfth studio album, Into Oblivion, later this month and will begin a North American tour starting March 17.

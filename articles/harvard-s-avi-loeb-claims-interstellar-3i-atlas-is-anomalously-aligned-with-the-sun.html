Harvard's Avi Loeb Claims Interstellar 3I/ATLAS Is 'Anomalously' Aligned With the Sun

Harvard Professor Avi Loeb has reported that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS exhibits an alignment with the Sun that is statistically unlikely to occur by chance. The findings were detailed in a study co-authored by Toni Scarmato of the Scarmato Observatory. The research utilized data from the Hubble Space Telescope and was published recently.

This study is significant as it revives the debate on whether objects like 3I/ATLAS are influenced by unknown forces or shaped purely by random chance. The researchers have noted that the alignment of 3I/ATLAS could have implications for understanding the nature of interstellar objects and their origins.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar visitor to our solar system.
  • The object's rotation axis is aligned with the Sun to within 10–20 degrees.
  • The probability of this alignment occurring by chance is estimated to be between 1.5% and 6%.
  • 3I/ATLAS rotates approximately every 7.1 hours.
  • The study suggests that the alignment may result from gas sublimation on the comet's surface.

Timeline

The study of 3I/ATLAS has progressed as follows:

  • The object was first identified as an interstellar visitor.
  • Loeb and Scarmato conducted harmonic modelling and analyzed Hubble data.
  • They published their findings, noting the object's unique alignment with the Sun.

Official statements

“Instead, 3I/ATLAS moves with such precision that it is highly unlikely to be an accident,” said Professor Loeb.
“The periodic wobble of the jet around the rotation axis did not change during perihelion,” Loeb added, emphasizing the stability of the object.

Context

This study places 3I/ATLAS alongside earlier interstellar objects, such as 'Oumuamua, which have sparked discussions about the nature and origins of such cosmic visitors. Previous research has suggested that many interstellar objects might have random orientations upon entering the solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze 3I/ATLAS to determine whether its alignment is a natural phenomenon or indicative of more complex processes. The ongoing study may provide further insights into the characteristics of interstellar objects.

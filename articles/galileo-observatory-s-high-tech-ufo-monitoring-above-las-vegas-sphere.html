Galileo Observatory's High-Tech UFO Monitoring Above Las Vegas Sphere

The Galileo Observatory has begun monitoring unidentified aerial objects above the Sphere in Las Vegas since its official launch in September 2024. The observatory, equipped with advanced technology, is designed to study Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs), commonly known as UFOs, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This initiative is significant as it combines entertainment with scientific inquiry, aiming to provide credible data on aerial phenomena that have intrigued the public. The observatory utilizes ultra-high resolution optical and infrared cameras, alongside artificial intelligence, to enhance the accuracy and speed of data analysis. The project has received formal approval following a meeting between Sphere Entertainment’s CEO and Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, who leads the observatory's efforts.

Key facts

  • Location: Above the Sphere in Las Vegas
  • Launch date: September 2024
  • Designed by: Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist
  • Data collection: Aims to analyze millions of aerial objects annually
  • Additional facilities: Two other observation sites located approximately 10 kilometers away
  • Technology: Ultra-high resolution cameras and artificial intelligence systems

Timeline

The Galileo Observatory was formally approved in September 2024 after discussions between Sphere Entertainment’s CEO and Avi Loeb. The observatory became operational concurrently with the opening of “The Wizard of Oz” immersive show at the Sphere. This timing provides visitors with entertainment while researchers conduct scientific investigations above.

Official statements

Avi Loeb has stated, "I hope that the Galileo Project will one day detect evidence of intelligent extraterrestrial visitors, expanding our understanding beyond terrestrial science."

Context

The Galileo Observatory represents a growing trend in the scientific community to explore UAPs. Despite facing skepticism for his views on extraterrestrial explanations, Loeb's work aims to gather credible, scientific data on phenomena that have fascinated humanity for years.

What’s next

The observatory will continue to analyze data from aerial objects and aims to identify potential UAPs over Las Vegas, contributing to ongoing research in this field.

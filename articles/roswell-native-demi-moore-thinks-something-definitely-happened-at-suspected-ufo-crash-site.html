Roswell native Demi Moore thinks 'something definitely happened' at suspected UFO crash site

Demi Moore, a native of Roswell, New Mexico, has expressed her thoughts on extraterrestrial events during an appearance on "The Late Show with Stephen Colbert" on October 24, 2023. Moore shared that her children have often joked that they believe she is "part alien," a comment she attributes to her birthplace, which is known for the infamous 1947 Roswell Incident involving a suspected UFO crash.

This discussion highlights ongoing public interest in UFO phenomena, especially in Roswell, which has embraced its reputation with events like the annual UFO Festival and the International UFO Museum. Authorities and researchers continue to investigate claims related to unidentified aerial phenomena, contributing to the broader conversation about potential extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Demi Moore appeared on "The Late Show with Stephen Colbert" on October 24, 2023.
  • Moore was born in Roswell, New Mexico, site of the 1947 "Roswell Incident."
  • Moore's children, Tallulah, Rumer, and Scout, have referred to her as "part alien."
  • Moore's upcoming film, "Strange Arrivals," portrays the story of Betty and Barney Hill's alleged alien abduction.

Timeline

In 1947, debris from a military balloon was recovered in Roswell, leading to speculation about an extraterrestrial spacecraft. Over the years, this incident has fueled numerous conspiracy theories and public interest in UFOs. In recent years, Roswell has transformed into a tourist destination, featuring events and attractions centered around its alien lore.

On October 24, 2023, Demi Moore appeared on "The Late Show with Stephen Colbert," where she discussed her views on aliens and her upbringing in Roswell. She mentioned her children's playful belief that she is part alien and shared her thoughts on the significance of the Roswell Incident.

Official statements

"Something definitely happened. But when I was a kid, it was never spoken about. Never. It just was not ever talked about, not even in passing. It was as if it was a secret." — Demi Moore
"The possibility there are aliens, or a branch of the military that's been putting out these videos of unexplainable pill-shaped things that go into the water and come out and go from like 0 to 6,000 miles an hour with absolutely no g-force impact — I mean, what?" — Stephen Colbert

Context

The Roswell Incident remains a pivotal moment in UFO history, generating a culture of secrecy and speculation surrounding military activities and unidentified aerial phenomena. The town of Roswell has since capitalized on this history, creating a unique identity centered around extraterrestrial themes and attracting tourists interested in UFO lore.

What’s next

Moore's film "Strange Arrivals" is set to explore the alleged alien abduction of Betty and Barney Hill, continuing the conversation about UFOs in popular media. Public interest in UFOs is likely to persist as new reports and investigations emerge regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

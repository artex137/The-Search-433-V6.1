Photographer captures interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS glowing over Egypt's Black Desert

Photographer Osama Fathi has captured stunning images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it becomes visible in the winter night sky over Egypt's Black Desert on the night of 29 November 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the event.

The emergence of 3I/ATLAS in the night sky offers a rare opportunity for both amateur and professional astronomers to observe the comet, which is believed to be one of the oldest ever seen. Authorities encourage the public to engage in astrophotography and explore the celestial event using telescopes.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of Capture: 29 November 2025
  • Location: Black Desert, Egypt
  • Photographer: Osama Fathi
  • Equipment Used: Nikon Z6 camera, RedCat telescope lens
  • Exposure Details: 60 exposures of 60 seconds, and 60 exposures of 30 seconds

Timeline

On 29 November 2025, Osama Fathi began photographing comet 3I/ATLAS around 03:00 from the Black Desert in Egypt. He captured the comet against the dark desert sky, using a combination of long exposures to reveal its structure and faint gas halo.

The comet, which originated beyond our Solar System, is temporarily passing through the Solar System. It has been observed by various space telescopes and missions prior to its visibility to the public.

Official statements

“From the heart of Egypt’s Black Desert, where volcanic hills rise like silent sentinels and acacia trees stand alone against the night, an object from another star system drifts through our sky: interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS,” said Fathi.
“In this frame, the faint green glow of 3I/ATLAS slips between the desert trees, a visitor older than our Sun, passing through the Solar System only once before returning to interstellar space,” he added.

Context

3I/ATLAS is an interstellar comet, meaning it has originated from outside our Solar System. Its appearance offers a unique chance for astronomers to study the characteristics of such celestial bodies, which are rare and fleeting in nature.

Previous comets have been observed, but 3I/ATLAS stands out due to its age and the insights it may provide into the formation of planetary systems.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey through the Solar System, astronomers and enthusiasts are encouraged to observe and capture images of the comet until it departs back into interstellar space. Educational resources are available for those interested in astrophotography.

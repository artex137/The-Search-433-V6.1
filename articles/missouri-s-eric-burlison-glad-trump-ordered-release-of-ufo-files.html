Missouri’s Eric Burlison glad Trump ordered release of UFO files

Published 2026-02-25 00:00 UTC

Missouri’s Eric Burlison glad Trump ordered release of UFO files

U.S. Representative Eric Burlison, a Republican from Missouri's 7th District, has expressed his approval of President Donald Trump's recent decision to release all UFO files. Burlison made his comments during an interview on February 24, 2026, with Missourinet.

Burlison has been advocating for the release of these files since Trump took office. He believes transparency regarding unidentified flying objects is essential and has previously noted that the government should not withhold information from the public.

Key facts

  • Who: Eric Burlison, U.S. Representative for Missouri's 7th District
  • What: Approval of President Trump's order to release UFO files
  • When: February 24, 2026
  • Where: Missouri
  • Previous comments: Burlison does not believe in extraterrestrial beings but acknowledges the existence of unexplained aerial phenomena.

Timeline

In August 2023, Burlison stated he does not believe that alien species have visited Earth, suggesting that witness accounts of UFOs may be attributed to either "angels or manmade" objects. In September 2025, he presented a video during a House committee hearing showing a military drone failing to engage with what he labeled a UFO.

On February 24, 2026, Burlison expressed his gratitude for Trump's order to release UFO files, emphasizing the importance of transparency in government.

Official statements

“In fact, I’ve been asking the White House for this to be done since Trump entered into office,” Burlison said. “You know, he’s the disclosure president. He filed an executive order to disclose the files on the JFK assassination and other events.”
“I’m not one that believes that there’s little green men from another planet, but I can’t deny the fact that there’s a lot of video evidence of objects that we have no idea what they are, but we know that they’re not us,” Burlison added in his February interview.

Context

The release of UFO files has been a topic of discussion among lawmakers and the public for years. Previous administrations have faced scrutiny for their handling of information related to unidentified aerial phenomena. Burlison's comments reflect a growing interest in government transparency regarding such matters.

What’s next

Following Trump's order, it remains unclear what specific documents will be released or if any sensitive military information will be exempted from disclosure.

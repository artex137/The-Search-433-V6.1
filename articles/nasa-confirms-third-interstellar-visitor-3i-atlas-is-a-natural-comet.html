NASA Confirms Third Interstellar Visitor 3I/ATLAS Is a Natural Comet

NASA has confirmed that 3I/ATLAS, the third interstellar object detected entering the Solar System, is a natural comet. The announcement came following observations on July 1, 2025, by NASA’s ATLAS telescope in Chile. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to this cosmic event.

This discovery is significant as it allows researchers to study ancient materials from a distant star system. NASA has stated that over 20 spacecraft and major telescopes, including Hubble and Webb, have closely monitored 3I/ATLAS. The findings affirm the comet's natural origins and eliminate any speculation about it being artificial.

Key facts

  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date Detected: July 1, 2025
  • Closest Approach to Earth: Approximately 1.8 AU
  • Monitoring Instruments: Over 20 spacecraft and telescopes, including Hubble and Webb
  • Key Findings: Unusually high levels of carbon dioxide and nickel vapour

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, NASA’s ATLAS telescope detected 3I/ATLAS moving rapidly through the Solar System. Following this detection, NASA initiated extensive monitoring using various spacecraft and telescopes. Recent analyses confirmed the comet's natural formation and its non-threatening trajectory relative to Earth.

Official statements

“This object is a comet… it looks and behaves like a comet,” said Associate Administrator Amit Kshatriya during a NASA briefing.
Science chief Nicky Fox added that no technosignatures or artificial patterns were found in its chemical or physical behavior.
NASA astrophysicist Tom Statler expressed excitement about the discovery, stating that studying a comet carrying matter from before the Sun formed “gives me goosebumps.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object confirmed by NASA, following previous detections. The unique chemical composition of 3I/ATLAS, including high levels of carbon dioxide and nickel vapour, suggests it originated from a very old and distant star system, providing insights into the early materials of the galaxy.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the Solar System, aiming to gain further insights into its origins and the broader implications for understanding planetary systems across the galaxy.

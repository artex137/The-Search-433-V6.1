Gemini North tracks changing glow of interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

Gemini North Observatory has conducted observations of interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing a faint greenish glow on November 1, 2023. The observations were part of a live outreach program called Shadow the Scientists, which connects students and the public with astronomers.

This research is significant as it helps scientists understand how the comet changes as it moves away from the Sun and cools. As the comet recedes, researchers will monitor shifts in its gas composition and any potential outbursts, providing insights into the behavior of interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Observation date: November 1, 2023
  • Location: Gemini North Observatory, Hawaii
  • Scientific program led by: Bryce Bolin of Eureka Scientific
  • Previous observations at: Gemini South, Chile
  • Filters used for imaging: blue, green, orange, and red
  • Comet's color change: from red at Gemini South to green at Gemini North

Timeline

On November 1, 2023, Gemini North Observatory conducted live observations of Comet 3I/ATLAS as part of the Shadow the Scientists program. Earlier observations from Gemini South in Chile showed the comet in a red hue, while the latest images captured a greenish glow. The change in color is attributed to gas emissions in the comet's coma.

Researchers aim to monitor the comet's behavior as it cools and possibly triggers new outbursts due to solar heating effects.

Official statements

"Sharing an observing experience in some of the best conditions available gives the public a truly front-row view of our interstellar visitor," said Bryce Bolin.
"Allowing the public to see what we do as astronomers and how we do it also helps demystify the scientific and data collection process," Bolin added.

Context

The Shadow the Scientists program collaborates with NSF NOIRLab to connect the public with real-time astronomical observations. This initiative aims to provide access to authentic research material while highlighting significant astronomical events, such as the passage of Comet 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS with Gemini North to track shifts in its gas composition and any outburst activity as it travels back into interstellar space.

Astronomers confirm third interstellar object 3I/Atlas traveling at 57 km/s on solar escape route

Astronomers have confirmed the detection of a new interstellar object, 3I/Atlas, moving at a speed of 57 kilometers per second. The object was observed as it passed through the Solar System, according to findings released on February 15, 2026.

This discovery is significant as 3I/Atlas is the third confirmed interstellar object, following Oumuamua and Borisov. Its hyperbolic trajectory indicates that it originated from a distant star system and will continue on its path into deep space, unable to be captured by the Sun's gravity.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/Atlas
  • Speed: 57 km/s
  • Previous interstellar objects: Oumuamua (26 km/s in 2017), Borisov (33 km/s)
  • Detection date: February 15, 2026
  • Type: Interstellar object with a hyperbolic orbit

Timeline

3I/Atlas was detected moving through the Solar System on February 15, 2026. Its speed and trajectory were analyzed using high-precision telescopes, leading to its classification as an interstellar object.

Prior to this detection, Oumuamua and Borisov had been identified as interstellar visitors, with 3I/Atlas surpassing their speeds significantly.

Official statements

“The unique chemical signature of 3I/Atlas reinforces its classification as an interstellar object, validating theoretical models on the exchange of matter between planetary systems in the galaxy,” researchers stated.

Context

Interstellar objects like 3I/Atlas provide valuable insights into the composition of celestial bodies beyond our Solar System. They are believed to have been expelled from their home systems due to gravitational interactions or stellar explosions, wandering through space for millions of years before encountering our Solar System.

What’s next

Further studies will be conducted to analyze the composition of 3I/Atlas, as scientists aim to understand its origins and the implications of its unique chemical properties.

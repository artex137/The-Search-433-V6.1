Mutual UFO Network comments on Obama's 'alien' statement

Following former President Barack Obama’s comment on a podcast regarding the existence of aliens, President Donald Trump has directed officials to identify and release government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life. The announcement comes in the wake of Obama stating he has not personally seen aliens.

This development has sparked renewed interest in UFOs and government transparency. The Mutual UFO Network (MUFON) has weighed in, noting that they were not surprised by Obama’s remarks. Rusty Hester, MUFON’s assistant state director, expressed skepticism about the information available to elected officials regarding UFOs.

Key facts

  • Obama stated he has not seen aliens during a podcast.
  • Trump has ordered the release of government files related to UFOs.
  • Rusty Hester, MUFON assistant state director, commented on the situation.
  • Approximately 90 percent of UFO reports are explainable, according to MUFON.
  • A 2021 Pew Research Center survey indicated that 65 percent of Americans believe intelligent life may exist elsewhere.

Timeline

In recent weeks, former President Barack Obama made comments on a podcast acknowledging the possibility of aliens but emphasized he has not seen them. Following these remarks, President Donald Trump announced the direction to release government files on extraterrestrial life.

Rusty Hester, representing MUFON, shared insights on the historical context of presidential comments about UFOs, citing past presidents Jimmy Carter, Ronald Reagan, and Bill Clinton, who have all expressed interest in UFO phenomena.

Official statements

“We here in MUFON and I were not excited, because all presidents have made comments about it,” said Rusty Hester. “I do believe that Obama probably has been briefed in a little bit about UFOs and aliens, but I don't think they know everything.”
“And MUFON, our stance is that we've already had disclosure,” Hester added. “We've had multiple lists and lists of people that are in our military, that have testified and that have talked about this.”

Context

MUFON investigates UFO sightings and claims, analyzing various data to determine the origins of reported phenomena. Hester mentioned that about 90 percent of reports are often explained through conventional means, leaving approximately 10 percent unexplained.

The conversation around UFOs has shifted in recent years, with public acceptance of the topic increasing. Hester noted a change in perception, stating, “I used to feel embarrassed about it, and wouldn’t talk about it. … I feel like it's just more accepted.”

What’s next

Authorities are expected to begin the process of identifying and releasing the requested government files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. The timeline for these disclosures has not been disclosed.

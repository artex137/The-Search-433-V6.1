Who is William McCasland? FBI joins search for the missing retired U.S. Air Force major general linked to UFO secrets

Published 2026-03-12 08:50 UTC

FBI joins search for retired U.S. Air Force major general William McCasland

Retired U.S. Air Force Major General William McCasland has been reported missing, prompting the FBI to join local authorities in the search. The search began on October 1, 2023, after McCasland was last seen in New Mexico.

This case is notable due to McCasland's previous involvement with U.S. Air Force programs related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), commonly referred to as UFOs. Authorities have initiated extensive search efforts, including ground teams and aerial surveillance, to locate him.

Key facts

  • Date missing: October 1, 2023
  • Location last seen: New Mexico
  • Agency involved: FBI
  • Previous role: Major General in the U.S. Air Force

Timeline

On October 1, 2023, William McCasland was last seen in New Mexico. Following his disappearance, local law enforcement issued a request for assistance from the FBI. The FBI has since mobilized resources to aid in the search.

Official statements

“We are doing everything we can to locate Major General McCasland and ensure his safe return,” said a spokesperson for the FBI.

Context

William McCasland has previously held significant roles in the U.S. Air Force, particularly in areas related to UAP research and military protocols. His disappearance raises concerns not only for his safety but also about the implications of his knowledge regarding UAP.

What’s next

Search efforts will continue as authorities gather more information. The FBI has stated they will provide updates as the situation develops.

New Data Highlights Motion Anomalies in Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS

New observations from the Hubble Space Telescope have revealed unusual motion patterns in the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, according to USA Herald. The findings were published on December 29, 2025, following a 170-second exposure captured on December 27, 2025.

This data is significant as it offers unprecedented insight into the structure and behavior of 3I/ATLAS as it exits the solar system. Researchers aim to understand the object's emission characteristics and its motion anomalies, which differ from typical cometary profiles.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: December 27, 2025
  • Instrument used: Hubble Space Telescope, Wide Field Camera 3 UVIS
  • Exposure duration: 170 seconds
  • Key findings: Asymmetrical halo and jet activity
  • Previous observations: July and November 2025
  • Reported by: Harvard Professor Avi Loeb

Timeline

On December 12, 2025, Hubble captured images showing a double-jet structure in 3I/ATLAS. The prominent jet pointed sunward, aligning with a previously documented anti-tail. A weaker secondary jet oriented opposite the main jet was also observed.

Further analysis of the December 27 frame confirmed the nucleus as a compact source with an elongated halo. The brightness patterns indicated directional activity rather than uniform outgassing, consistent with earlier frames that displayed off-center brightness peaks.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, "The December 27 Hubble exposure provides detailed raw observational data, allowing measurement of asymmetries, jet orientation, and brightness offsets without post-processing."
Loeb added, "Directional emission aligns with earlier observations, documenting consistent jet activity over multiple months."

Context

This analysis follows prior observations of 3I/ATLAS, which have indicated a sunward jet approximately ten times longer than it is wide. The motion anomalies observed raise questions about the object's behavior as it transitions from pre-perihelion to post-perihelion activity.

The lack of a broad fan-shaped dust tail, typical of many outbound comets, further emphasizes the unique characteristics of 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the Hubble data to gain further insights into the motion and emission characteristics of 3I/ATLAS as it exits the solar system.

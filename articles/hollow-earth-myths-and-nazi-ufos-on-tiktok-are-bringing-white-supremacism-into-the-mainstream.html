Hollow-Earth Myths and Nazi UFOs on TikTok Are Bringing White Supremacism Into the Mainstream

A viral TikTok video featuring an AI-generated image of Adolf Hitler sipping a White Monster Energy drink has become part of a trend promoting white supremacist narratives. The video, which plays the song "Down Under" by Men at Work, has attracted millions of views and highlights the rise of the "Agartha" trend on social media. Researchers at Neu-Ulm University of Applied Sciences in Germany have analyzed over 43,000 Agartha-related videos to understand how these narratives are infiltrating mainstream culture.

This trend matters because it demonstrates how extremist content can spread without triggering moderation on social media platforms. Authorities and researchers are concerned about the normalization of far-right ideologies among young audiences through seemingly innocuous content.

Key facts

  • Viral TikTok video features Adolf Hitler in Antarctica.
  • Video has garnered millions of views.
  • Analysis conducted by Neu-Ulm University of Applied Sciences.
  • Over 43,000 Agartha-related videos examined.
  • Four key mechanisms identified for promoting radical narratives.

Timeline

The Agartha trend has emerged over recent months, with various TikTok videos gaining traction. Researchers have been monitoring the content and its impact, leading to the recent analysis of a large network of videos. The findings reveal a coordinated effort to embed extremist narratives into mainstream social media.

As the trend continues to grow, it has become apparent that far-right actors are using creative methods to bypass moderation systems, making their content more accessible to unsuspecting users.

Official statements

“Agartha is more than just a digital resurfacing of fringe occultism. It is a blueprint for how to design extremist content for algorithmically curated short-video platforms,” said Marten Risius from The University of Queensland.

Context

The rise of the Agartha trend reflects a broader issue of how extremist content can blend with mainstream culture on social media. Researchers have noted similar patterns in previous incidents where far-right ideologies have infiltrated popular platforms, often disguised as humor or benign content.

Authorities have expressed concern about the implications of this trend, particularly regarding its impact on young users who may be unknowingly exposed to radical narratives.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue monitoring the Agartha trend and its impact on social media. Authorities may also consider implementing stricter measures to address the spread of extremist content on platforms like TikTok.

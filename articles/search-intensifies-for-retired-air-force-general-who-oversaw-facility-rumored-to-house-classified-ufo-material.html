Search Intensifies for Retired Air Force General Who Oversaw Facility Rumored to House Classified UFO Material

The FBI has joined the search for retired Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, who was last seen at his home on Quail Run Court in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on February 27. The 68-year-old left his residence on foot and has not been in contact with family or friends since then.

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office is investigating the matter and has noted that it is unusual for McCasland to be out of touch for such an extended period. Authorities are utilizing advanced technologies and collaborating with the FBI and other agencies to locate him. As of now, they have found no evidence of foul play.

Key facts

  • Missing person: Retired Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland
  • Last seen: February 27, 2023
  • Location: Quail Run Court, Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Age: 68 years old
  • Agencies involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office, FBI
  • Status: No evidence of foul play discovered

Timeline

McCasland was last seen at his home on February 27. Since then, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office has been actively investigating his disappearance, working closely with the FBI and other agencies.

In a statement, the Sheriff's Office mentioned that they are considering all possible scenarios as they follow up on leads to locate McCasland.

As of today, there have been no new developments regarding his whereabouts.

Official statements

“It is unlike Neil to be out of touch from his family and friends for this length of time,” a representative from the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office said.
“We are utilizing all possible resources including advanced technologies,” the Sheriff's Office added, “and still considering all possible scenarios.”
McCasland's wife, Susan McCasland, stated, “Bill does not have any special knowledge about the E.T. bodies and debris from the Roswell crash stored at Wright-Patt.”

Context

McCasland has held several sensitive positions during his military career, including commanding the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio and overseeing the Phillips site at Kirtland Air Force Base in New Mexico. Wright-Patterson has been rumored to house debris from the alleged UFO recovered from Roswell, New Mexico.

Experts have expressed skepticism that McCasland's disappearance is connected to his work in the Air Force, with some suggesting it is unlikely that historical material related to UFOs would be a motive for his disappearance.

What’s next

Investigators will continue to search for leads and utilize advanced technologies in their efforts to locate McCasland. The community is also encouraged to assist in any way possible as authorities pursue this case.

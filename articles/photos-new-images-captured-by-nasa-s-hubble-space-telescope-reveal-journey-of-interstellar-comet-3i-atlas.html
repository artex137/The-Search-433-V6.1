Photos: New images captured by NASA's Hubble Space Telescope reveal journey of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope has captured new images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it traverses the solar system on November 30. The images showcase the comet's journey, with the latest observation occurring when it was approximately 178 million miles from Earth.

This observation is significant as it marks the third known interstellar object detected passing through the solar system. The comet's high velocity and trajectory have confirmed its interstellar nature, and officials report it poses no threat to Earth, as it will not come closer than 170 million miles.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was first spotted in summer 2025.
  • Initial detection occurred at the NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile.
  • The comet is on a hyperbolic trajectory, moving too fast to be captured by the Sun’s gravity.
  • The latest observation took place on November 30, 2023.
  • The comet was approximately 178 million miles from Earth during the observation.

Timeline

Summer 2025: NASA first detects interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS using the ATLAS survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile.

November 30, 2023: Hubble Space Telescope captures new images of the comet as it moves through the solar system.

Official statements

“So we use all the tools at our disposal to observe it before it disappears back into the cosmic dark,” said NASA.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third known interstellar object to pass through the solar system. Its discovery highlights the ongoing efforts of astronomers to study celestial objects moving at high velocities and unique trajectories.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue observations of 3I/ATLAS for several more months, utilizing its space telescopes to gather more information on this interstellar object.

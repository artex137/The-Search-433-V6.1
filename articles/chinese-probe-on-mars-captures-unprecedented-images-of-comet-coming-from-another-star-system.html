Chinese probe on Mars captures unprecedented images of comet coming from another star system

Chinese probe on Mars captures unprecedented images of comet 3I/ATLAS

The Chinese probe Tianwen-1 has captured high-resolution images of comet 3I/ATLAS from its orbit around Mars in October 2025. This marks the first time an object of interstellar origin has been imaged from the orbit of another planet, according to the Administração Espacial Nacional of China (CNSA).

This achievement is significant for planetary science and modern astronomy, as it provides crucial data for the global scientific community to study the composition and trajectory of interstellar objects. The CNSA has indicated that the images will enhance understanding of the formation and evolution of planetary systems.

Key facts

  • Event occurred in October 2025
  • Distance from Mars: approximately 30 million kilometers
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS diameter: estimated at 5.6 kilometers
  • Comet speed: approximately 58 kilometers per second
  • Collaboration involved CNSA, European Space Agency (ESA), and NASA
  • Tianwen-1 launched in July 2020
  • Rover Zhurong landed on Mars in May 2021

Timeline

The Tianwen-1 mission team began planning to observe comet 3I/ATLAS in September 2025, once accurate orbital predictions were available. The planning involved complex calculations to account for the comet's high speed and low brightness.

On the day of the comet's passage, the Tianwen-1 probe executed precise orbital maneuvers to position itself correctly for the imaging operation. The team utilized exhaustive computer simulations to ensure optimal image capture, employing short exposure times to prevent blurriness.

The successful imaging of the comet demonstrates China's growing capabilities in autonomous space operations and solidifies its position as a leader in interplanetary exploration.

Official statements

“The images captured are invaluable to the global scientific community, which now has crucial data to further study the composition and trajectory of objects that visit our Sistema Solar from other stars,” said a CNSA representative.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed object of interstellar origin, following ‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. These objects are considered cosmic time capsules, offering insights into the chemical and physical diversity of other star systems.

The collaboration between multiple space agencies for the observation of 3I/ATLAS highlights the importance of international cooperation in space exploration.

What’s next

Scientists will analyze the data collected from the images and additional spectral analyses from ESA and NASA. This research aims to enhance understanding of the building blocks of life and the formation of planetary systems in the galaxy.

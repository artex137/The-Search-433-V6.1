Comet 3I/ATLAS to Make Closest Approach to Earth on December 19, 2025

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, also known as C/2025 N1 ATLAS, will reach its closest point to Earth on December 19, 2025, at approximately 04:00 UTC, according to the Virtual Telescope Project. The comet will be about 270 million kilometers away from our planet during this event.

This observation is significant as it provides a rare opportunity for astronomers and enthusiasts to witness the comet in real time. The Virtual Telescope Project has scheduled a live feed for this event, following a successful live stream on November 19, 2025.

Key facts

  • Event date: December 19, 2025
  • Closest distance to Earth: 270 million kilometers
  • Live feed start time: 04:00 UTC
  • Previous live feed date: November 19, 2025
  • Organizing agency: Virtual Telescope Project

Timeline

On November 19, 2025, the Virtual Telescope Project conducted a live feed of comet 3I/ATLAS, which garnered significant interest from the public. Following this success, the project announced a subsequent live observation event for December 19, 2025.

As the comet approaches Earth, astronomers are preparing to capture and share its journey through the solar system.

Official statements

“After the huge success of our 19 Nov. live feed, we will show in real-time this unique comet again on 19 Dec. 2025,” said Gianluca Masi from the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable for being an interstellar comet, which distinguishes it from typical comets that originate within the solar system. This upcoming approach presents a unique opportunity for scientific observation and public engagement with astronomy.

What’s next

The Virtual Telescope Project will continue to provide updates leading up to the live feed on December 19, 2025. Observers can expect detailed coverage and potential follow-up events after the closest approach.

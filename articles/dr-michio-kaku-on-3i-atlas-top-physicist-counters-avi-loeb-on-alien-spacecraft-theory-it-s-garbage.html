Dr. Michio Kaku On 3i/Atlas: Top Physicist Counters Avi Loeb On Alien Spacecraft Theory - 'It's Garbage'

Published 2025-11-11 10:31 UTC

Dr. Michio Kaku Dismisses Claims of Alien Spacecraft in 3I/ATLAS Debate

The debate over the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has intensified following an interview with theoretical physicist Dr. Michio Kaku on 8 November, where he dismissed claims that the object may be an alien spacecraft. Kaku spoke with NewsNation about the comet's characteristics, stating they can be explained by natural causes rather than extraterrestrial engineering.

This discussion is significant as it highlights contrasting viewpoints among scientists regarding the origins of 3I/ATLAS. Authorities, including NASA, are urged to release high-resolution images of the object to further investigate its nature.

Key facts

  • Dr. Michio Kaku made his statements on 8 November 2023.
  • 3I/ATLAS is estimated to be approximately seven billion years old.
  • The object is about the size of Manhattan.
  • Avi Loeb claims the object has multiple jets, suggesting artificial propulsion.
  • Loeb reported that 3I/ATLAS passed close to the Sun on 29 October 2023.

Timeline

On 29 October 2023, 3I/ATLAS passed close to the Sun. Following this event, Avi Loeb reported the appearance of several jets from the object. On 8 November, Dr. Michio Kaku provided his analysis of the object, arguing against the notion that it is an alien spacecraft.

Official statements

“This comet is different because it's much older than anything we've seen locally,” Kaku stated. “Over seven billion years, it absorbed all kinds of materials, which explains its odd composition.”

“Speculation is fine,” Kaku said, “but you have to quantify it. Otherwise, you panic the public, and the public wants to know the truth.”

Context

The debate over 3I/ATLAS reflects a broader discussion in the scientific community about the potential for extraterrestrial life and the interpretation of unusual astronomical phenomena. Avi Loeb's theories have sparked media attention, yet many astrophysicists remain skeptical of claims suggesting artificial origins for celestial objects.

What’s next

NASA has been urged to release high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS captured by the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter. The scientific community continues to study the object as it moves further into interstellar space, seeking to understand its properties before it disappears from observation.

Illinois Resident Sees Fast-Moving Cross in the Sky—What Was It?

An eyewitness in Northbrook, Illinois, has reported seeing a cross-shaped light moving quickly across the night sky on a recent evening. The sighting occurred approximately 200 feet away from the observer, who initially mistook the object for a small aircraft. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This sighting is significant as it has drawn attention to unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in the region. Authorities are reviewing the report submitted to the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC) and considering the implications for air traffic in the vicinity of O'Hare International Airport, located about 32 kilometers north of Northbrook.

Key facts

  • Location: Northbrook, Illinois
  • Time of sighting: Not disclosed
  • Distance from observer: Approximately 200 feet
  • Object description: Silver sphere with white rectangular wings
  • Direction of movement: North

Timeline

The eyewitness reported the sighting to NUFORC, describing the object as initially stationary before it began to move quickly northward. The object reportedly accelerated rapidly and vanished from sight. The observer noted the unusual characteristics of the object, which did not match typical aircraft behavior.

Official statements

“Silver sphere with white rectangular wings moved quickly away then vanished...looked stationary at first but then began to move very quickly towards the north.”
“It looked like a silver sphere center with white rectangular wings.”

Context

This sighting adds to a growing number of UFO reports across the United States. As interest in unidentified aerial phenomena increases, authorities are taking such reports more seriously, particularly in areas near major airports where air traffic is dense.

What’s next

Investigators from NUFORC are expected to look into the sighting further, assessing available data and seeking additional eyewitness accounts. No specific timeline for the investigation has been disclosed.

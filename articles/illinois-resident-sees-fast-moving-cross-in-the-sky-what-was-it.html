Illinois Resident Sees Fast-Moving Cross in the Sky—What Was It?

A resident of Northbrook, Illinois, has reported seeing a cross-shaped light moving quickly across the night sky. The sighting occurred a few days ago and was detailed on the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC) website. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This sighting has drawn attention due to the unusual shape and movement of the object. Authorities have not disclosed any ongoing investigations related to the sighting, but the report raises questions about unidentified aerial phenomena in the area. Northbrook is located approximately 20 minutes south of O'Hare International Airport, where air traffic is common.

Key facts

  • Sighting occurred a few days ago in Northbrook, Illinois.
  • Witness described the object as a "silver sphere with white rectangular wings."
  • The object was approximately 200 feet away from the witness.
  • Witness reported the object moved at a 65-degree angle and accelerated quickly.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.

Timeline

The eyewitness reported seeing the object while driving in Northbrook. Initially, the object appeared stationary but soon moved quickly towards the north. The witness described it as accelerating rapidly before vanishing from sight.

Official statements

“Silver sphere with white rectangular wings moved quickly away then vanished...looked stationary at first but then began to move very quickly towards the north,” the witness stated on the NUFORC site.

Context

Unidentified flying objects have been a topic of interest for many years. Reports of such sightings have increased in recent years, prompting discussions about aerial phenomena and their implications. The proximity of Northbrook to O'Hare International Airport adds complexity to the interpretation of such sightings.

What’s next

Authorities have not announced any investigations related to this specific sighting, and no further updates have been disclosed at this time.

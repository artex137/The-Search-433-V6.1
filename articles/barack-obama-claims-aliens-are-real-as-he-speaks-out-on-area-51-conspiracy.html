Barack Obama claims aliens are 'real' as he speaks out on Area 51 conspiracy

Former U.S. President Barack Obama has stated that aliens are "real" during an interview with Brian Tyler Cohen, but clarified that they are not housed at Area 51, the well-known military facility in Nevada. The interview took place recently, and no injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the discussion.

This revelation has garnered significant attention as it challenges long-standing conspiracy theories surrounding extraterrestrial life and Area 51. Authorities have not announced any new investigations or actions regarding the claims made by Obama.

Key facts

  • Interview conducted by Brian Tyler Cohen.
  • Obama confirmed that aliens are "real" but not at Area 51.
  • Area 51 is historically a U.S. Air Force testing facility.
  • In 2019, a Facebook event encouraged people to 'storm Area 51'.
  • No evidence has been presented of aliens being kept at Area 51.

Timeline

During the interview, Obama was asked directly about the existence of aliens. He responded affirmatively, stating, "Uh, they're real, but I haven't seen them." He then addressed the conspiracy theories surrounding Area 51, emphasizing that there is no underground facility for aliens at the site.

Prior to this interview, Area 51 had been the subject of speculation for decades, with many believing it houses alien technology and extraterrestrial beings. In 2019, a viral Facebook event invited people to storm the facility, leading to a small gathering of curious individuals, but no one was able to breach security.

Official statements

“Uh, they're real, but I haven't seen them,” Obama said regarding aliens during the interview.
“And they're not being kept in uh, what is it? Area 51!” he added, dismissing the theories surrounding the site.

Context

Area 51 has a long history as a U.S. Air Force testing ground for military technology, particularly during the Cold War. The U-2 spy plane, which was developed and tested there, played a critical role in intelligence gathering against the Soviet Union and other nations. The continued interest in Area 51 has fueled conspiracy theories about government secrecy and extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

There are currently no announced next steps or investigations following Obama’s remarks. The discussion around aliens and Area 51 is likely to continue as public interest remains high.

Is it comet or alien spaceship? Watch 3I/ATLAS livestream tonight

The Virtual Telescope Project will livestream the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS tonight at 11:15 PM ET. The comet, first discovered four months ago, will be visible in the eastern sky as it moves through the constellation Virgo. There are no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this event.

This livestream provides a unique opportunity for the public to observe 3I/ATLAS, which is too dim to be seen with the naked eye. NASA is also expected to release high-resolution images of the comet, captured by the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter's HiRISE camera in early October. The comet's unusual origin and composition have sparked significant interest among scientists and experts.

Key facts

  • Event: Livestream of comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: Tonight
  • Time: 11:15 PM ET
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Brightness: +10.9 (not visible to the naked eye)
  • Discovery: Four months ago
  • NASA's images: Captured in early October
  • Notable figures: Avi Loeb, Elon Musk

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered four months ago, marking it as the third known interstellar object confirmed to pass through our solar system. The comet's close approach to Jupiter, Venus, and Mars last month led to speculation about its origins, with some suggesting it could be an alien spaceship.

In early October, NASA captured high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS using the HiRISE camera on the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter. The Virtual Telescope Project has organized a livestream event to provide global access to the comet's viewing.

Official statements

Avi Loeb criticized the delayed release of images, stating, "Science should have been prioritized over bureaucracy." He added, "The truth about the nature of 3I/ATLAS will be revealed by the sharing of data, not by the storyline of gatekeepers."

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object confirmed to enter our solar system. Its unusual characteristics have led to significant public and scientific interest, especially following its close encounters with major planets. The comet's discovery and subsequent monitoring have sparked discussions about the nature of interstellar objects.

What’s next

Tonight's livestream will allow viewers worldwide to observe 3I/ATLAS. Following this event, NASA is expected to release additional images and data to further investigate the comet's origins and characteristics.

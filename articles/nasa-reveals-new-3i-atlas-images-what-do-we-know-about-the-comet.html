NASA reveals new 3I/Atlas images. What do we know about the comet?

The European Space Agency's (ESA) satellites and several NASA spacecraft have captured new images of comet 3I/ATLAS as it passed Mars on October 3, 2023, at a distance of 29 million kilometres. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this event.

This observation is significant as it provides valuable data about the comet's composition and behavior. The ESA and NASA are currently analyzing the images to enhance their understanding of the comet and its trajectory.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 3, 2023
  • Distance from Mars: 29 million kilometres
  • Agencies involved: European Space Agency (ESA), NASA
  • Location of observation: Near Mars

Timeline

On October 3, 2023, comet 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to Mars, allowing ESA and NASA to capture detailed images of the comet. The satellites and spacecraft utilized advanced imaging technology to study the comet's features during its flyby.

Following the event, the agencies have commenced a detailed analysis of the data collected to better understand the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS.

Official statements

“The close approach of 3I/ATLAS provides us with a unique opportunity to study a comet in detail,” said a spokesperson from ESA.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of many comets that have been observed as they pass through the inner solar system. Previous observations have helped scientists learn more about the origins and compositions of these celestial bodies.

What’s next

NASA and ESA will continue to analyze the images and data collected from the comet's flyby. Further findings are expected to be released in the coming months as the agencies compile their research.

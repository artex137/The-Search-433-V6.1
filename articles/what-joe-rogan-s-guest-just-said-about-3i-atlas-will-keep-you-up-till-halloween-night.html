What Joe Rogan’s guest just said about ‘3I/ATLAS’ will keep you up till Halloween night

Published 2025-10-28 22:58 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS Sparks Speculation About Artificial Origins

Avi Loeb, an astrophysicist, has discussed the unusual characteristics of Comet 3I/ATLAS during a recent appearance on Joe Rogan's podcast. Loeb stated that the comet, approximately the size of Manhattan Island, exhibits a retrograde motion and a unique glow, which could suggest it is not a natural object. The conversation has raised questions about its potential origins and implications.

Loeb's analysis of 3I/ATLAS matters because it challenges existing scientific paradigms about celestial objects. His claims have prompted discussions within the scientific community and among the public, particularly as Halloween approaches. Authorities and astronomers are monitoring the comet's path and characteristics closely.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is roughly the size of Manhattan Island.
  • It is aligned with the plane of the planets within five degrees, a one-in-500 chance.
  • The comet moves in a retrograde direction, opposite to the planets.
  • Unusual metal ratios, including high nickel and low iron, have been detected in its gas plume.
  • Loeb claims this metal ratio resembles nickel-based alloys used in aerospace construction.
  • Loeb's paper about the comet's path being "designed" was rejected by a journal.

Timeline

Avi Loeb has analyzed Comet 3I/ATLAS's characteristics on Joe Rogan's podcast, leading to public interest and speculation. He highlighted the comet's retrograde motion and unusual glow, suggesting it could release probes into Earth's or Mars' orbits. Loeb's findings regarding the gas plume's metal composition further fueled discussions about the comet's origins.

Loeb attempted to publish a paper discussing the possibility of intelligent design associated with 3I/ATLAS. The journal editor rejected the paper unless he removed that line, indicating resistance within the scientific community to explore such theories.

Official statements

“It looks manufactured,” Loeb told Rogan flatly, referring to the comet's unusual characteristics.
“They don’t want to discuss intelligent design,” Loeb said. “They’d rather call it a comet and move on.”
“If intelligence is behind it,” Loeb said, “it might already be watching.”

Context

This discussion is part of a broader trend in the scientific community, where unconventional theories regarding extraterrestrial life and artificial objects are often met with skepticism. Previous instances of celestial objects being reclassified or debated have also faced similar challenges.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey through the solar system, astronomers will closely observe its path and characteristics. Further studies may emerge as the scientific community grapples with the implications of Loeb's claims.

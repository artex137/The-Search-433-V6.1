Declassified UFO files reveal giant glowing sphere over military base that's been hidden for 35 years

Declassified documents from over three decades ago have confirmed an encounter with a suspected UFO at General San Martín Base in Antarctica in April 1991. The records, unsealed by Argentina's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, detail how military personnel and civilian researchers observed a large flying saucer above their base. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with the incident.

This revelation is significant as it sheds light on a previously covered-up event and raises questions about unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are now reviewing the records and considering further investigations into similar incidents. The Argentine civilian UFO research group CEFORA has played a crucial role in advocating for the release of these documents.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on April 1, 1991, at General San Martín Base, Antarctica.
  • Eyewitness Miguel Amaya was a non-commissioned officer with the Argentine Air Force.
  • Strange readings on a riometer began around 1 a.m. local time.
  • A large glowing sphere was observed at approximately 10 p.m. the same day.
  • More than 120 feet of paper were used to record the incident.
  • Nine rolls of records from the incident are stored at the Argentine Antarctic Institute.

Timeline

On April 1, 1991, at approximately 1 a.m. local time, an alarm went off on the riometer at General San Martín Base, indicating unusual atmospheric changes. Eyewitness Miguel Amaya reported that all three needles on the device began to show identical readings, which is scientifically impossible. This phenomenon persisted for several hours.

Later that day, around 10 p.m., a radio operator from the Argentine Army spotted a large circle of light moving silently above the base during a snowstorm. By the time he called for others to witness it, the craft had disappeared.

Official statements

“Now we are going to pursue other cases linked to Antarctica. This is just the first test case we can verify under this method, and it raises expectations for the continued declassification of other files,” said Andrea Simondini, director of CEFORA.

Context

The 1991 incident at General San Martín Base has long been a subject of speculation and investigation. CEFORA has been advocating for the release of related documents for over 15 years. With the recent declassification, there is renewed interest in UFO encounters in Argentina and worldwide, particularly as UFO disclosure has gained momentum in other countries, including the United States.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to analyze the newly released documents and explore other cases related to UFO sightings in Antarctica. CEFORA plans to pursue additional investigations based on the findings from the General San Martín Base incident.

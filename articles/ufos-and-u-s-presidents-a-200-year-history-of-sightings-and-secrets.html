UFOs and U.S. Presidents: A 200-Year History of Sightings and Secrets

Former President Barack Obama has stated that aliens are real, though he has not seen any evidence of extraterrestrial life. He made these comments during an interview with political podcaster Brian Tyler Cohen on February 14, 2023. Obama clarified on Instagram two days later that while the universe is vast, he saw no proof of alien contact during his presidency.

This discussion has reignited public interest in UFOs and the government's response to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). The Pentagon has reported hundreds of UAP cases, with twenty-one requiring further analysis due to "anomalous characteristics," although none have indicated extraterrestrial activity, according to officials.

Key facts

  • February 14, 2023: Barack Obama discusses aliens in an interview.
  • February 16, 2023: Obama clarifies his comments on Instagram.
  • Pentagon reports hundreds of UAP cases in recent years.
  • Twenty-one UAP reports tagged for further analysis.
  • Project Blue Book ran from 1952 to 1969, investigating UFO sightings.

Timeline

In 1800, Thomas Jefferson noted a "singular phenomenon" reported by William Dunbar, marking the earliest presidential reference to unexplained aerial objects. The modern interest in UFOs surged in 1947 when pilot Kenneth Arnold reported fast-moving objects near Mount Rainier. That same year, the Roswell Incident fueled conspiracy theories about government cover-ups of alien spacecraft.

In response to rising public concern, President Harry Truman's administration initiated investigations into UFO sightings, leading to the establishment of Project Blue Book. The project concluded in 1969 after analyzing over 11,000 sightings, with no evidence found of extraterrestrial threats.

Other presidents have also contributed to the UFO narrative. Jimmy Carter reported a sighting in 1969, while Ronald Reagan claimed to have witnessed a UFO in 1974. Bill Clinton, during a 2014 interview, acknowledged looking into records related to Area 51 but concluded there were no aliens present.

Official statements

“They’re real,” Obama said, “but I haven’t seen them. They’re not being kept in, what is it, Area 51.”
Trump responded to Obama's comments, stating, “He’s not supposed to be doing that. He made a big mistake, he took it out of classified information.”

Context

Presidential commentary on UFOs dates back over 200 years, with various leaders influencing public perception and conspiracy theories. While some presidents have openly speculated about extraterrestrial life, others have maintained silence or dismissed the topic as mere folklore.

Throughout U.S. history, the government's response to UFO sightings has oscillated between serious investigation and casual dismissal, reflecting broader societal attitudes towards unexplained phenomena.

What’s next

The Pentagon continues to analyze UAP reports and is expected to release further findings. There are no disclosed deadlines for future disclosures or investigations regarding UFOs or extraterrestrial life.

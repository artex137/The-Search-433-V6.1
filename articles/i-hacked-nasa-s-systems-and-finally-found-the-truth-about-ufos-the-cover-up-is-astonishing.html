I hacked NASA's systems and finally found the truth about UFOs… the cover-up is astonishing

Published 2026-03-02 18:00 UTC

British Hacker Claims Discovery of UFO Image in NASA Systems

Gary McKinnon, a British computer hacker, has recounted his discovery of an alleged UFO photo while accessing NASA's systems in 2002. McKinnon, who was accused of conducting the "biggest military computer hack of all time," reportedly breached 97 government sites during his infiltration, including NASA’s Johnson Space Center.

This incident has drawn renewed attention to cybersecurity within government agencies and the ongoing debate surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities have not disclosed any new actions following McKinnon’s revelations, but the story reignites interest in UFOs and potential government cover-ups.

Key facts

  • Year of hack: 2002
  • Number of sites hacked: 97 government sites
  • Agencies breached: NASA, NSA, Department of Defense, Army, Navy, and Air Force
  • Current status: McKinnon remains on Interpol's red list
  • Extradition threat: Up to 70 years in prison
  • Location of alleged UFO image: NASA's Johnson Space Center

Timeline

In March 2001, Gary McKinnon began scanning IP addresses for government computers, discovering many without password protection. He accessed various sensitive networks late at night from his home in London.

In 2002, McKinnon reported finding a high-resolution image of a cigar-shaped craft while connected to NASA’s systems. His access was abruptly terminated when he noticed someone taking control of his screen. Following this, he was arrested by the UK's National High-Tech Crime Unit.

After a decade-long extradition battle, UK authorities blocked McKinnon’s extradition in 2012, citing concerns for his mental health.

Official statements

“Mr. McKinnon's extradition would give rise to such a high risk of him ending his life… I have therefore withdrawn the extradition order against Mr. McKinnon,” said then-Home Secretary Theresa May.

Context

McKinnon’s activities have sparked discussions about the security of government systems, particularly following high-profile incidents involving cybersecurity breaches. His claims about discovering UFO-related material echo similar sentiments from other whistleblowers and former military personnel regarding hidden government technologies.

The alleged sighting he reported predates the well-known 2004 encounter by US Navy pilot David Fravor with a "tic tac" object, which the Pentagon later acknowledged as authentic.

What’s next

McKinnon remains on the Interpol red list, preventing him from entering the United States. No new investigations or charges have been disclosed since the extradition was blocked.

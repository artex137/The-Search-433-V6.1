From UFOs to Bats: Kyiv Police Reveal Curious Citizen Appeals

Kyiv police have reported a variety of unusual citizen appeals received on their emergency line, particularly during the New Year period. According to law enforcement, they receive thousands of calls daily, with some calls prompting concern and others eliciting amusement.

The Kyiv police have emphasized the importance of every call, stating that each report reflects a unique issue faced by citizens. They have reassured the public that all messages, even those that may seem strange, are taken seriously. On the night from December 31 to January 1, police noted that no serious public order violations occurred.

Key facts

  • Kyiv police receive thousands of calls daily.
  • Curious calls include reports of UFOs and bats.
  • No serious public order violations reported on December 31 to January 1.
  • Reported by the Main Department of the National Police of Ukraine in Kyiv.

Timeline

On December 31, 2025, and January 1, 2026, Kyiv police received various unusual calls on their emergency line, including reports of unidentified flying objects and other peculiar incidents. Throughout this period, the dispatchers coordinated responses to both urgent and unusual appeals.

Following the New Year’s celebrations, the police compiled a selection of these curious calls to illustrate the diverse nature of citizen reports.

Official statements

“Every appeal is a living person with their own problem. Even if the situation seems strange or makes you smile, we pay close attention to every message and try to help,”
said Yevhen Ustimenko from the Kyiv police.

Context

Unusual calls to emergency services are not a new phenomenon. Police departments often receive quirky or odd reports, especially during festive times when public activity increases. The Kyiv police have reiterated their commitment to addressing all citizen concerns seriously, regardless of the nature of the call.

What’s next

Kyiv police will continue to monitor citizen calls and ensure that all reports are addressed appropriately, with no immediate changes to current protocols regarding unusual appeals. Further public engagement may follow as authorities assess the nature of calls received.

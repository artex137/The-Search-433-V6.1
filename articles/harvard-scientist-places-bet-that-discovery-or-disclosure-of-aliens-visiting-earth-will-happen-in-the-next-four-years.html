Harvard scientist places bet that 'discovery or disclosure' of aliens visiting Earth will happen in the next four years

Avi Loeb, a Harvard physicist, has predicted that the discovery or disclosure of alien life will occur within the next four years. This announcement follows the close approach of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which passed Earth on December 19, 2022, at a distance of 270 million kilometers.

The prediction is significant as it comes amid ongoing debates about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. While Loeb has suggested the comet could be an alien probe, other scientific authorities, including NASA, have classified it as a rare space rock. 3I/ATLAS is the oldest interstellar comet observed, following 1I/Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest Approach to Earth: December 19, 2022
  • Distance at Closest Approach: 270 million kilometers
  • Speed: More than 66 kilometers per second
  • Upcoming Event: Pass by Jupiter on March 16, 2023
  • Bet Amount: $1,000 to the Galileo Project Foundation
  • Bet Opponent: Michael B. Shermer

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has captured scientific attention since its discovery. Here is a brief timeline of significant events:

  • November 2022: The European Space Agency's Juice spacecraft captures images of 3I/ATLAS.
  • December 19, 2022: 3I/ATLAS makes its closest approach to Earth.
  • March 16, 2023: The comet is expected to pass by Jupiter.

Official statements

“The search for technological artifacts has just started in earnest in 2025 with the discovery of the anomalous interstellar object 3I/ATLAS,” said Loeb. “It is better to be an optimist because life is sometimes a self-fulfilling prophecy.”
Michael B. Shermer argued, “The discovery or disclosure of alien visitation to Earth in the form of UFOs, UAPs, or any other technological artifact or alien biological form, as confirmed by major scientific institutions and government agencies, will not happen by December 31, 2030.”

Context

The debate over 3I/ATLAS highlights ongoing discussions within the scientific community regarding the existence of extraterrestrial life. Loeb's perspective contrasts with the majority view, which sees the comet as a natural occurrence rather than an artificial object.

This is not the first time unusual space phenomena have sparked speculation about alien life. The previous discoveries of 1I/Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov also prompted discussions about the possibility of extraterrestrial technology.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey out of the solar system, scientists will monitor its trajectory and study the data collected from the Juice spacecraft. Loeb and Shermer's bet will remain a point of interest as the scientific community prepares for future discoveries related to extraterrestrial life.

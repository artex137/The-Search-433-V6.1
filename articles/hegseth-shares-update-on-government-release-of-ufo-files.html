Hegseth Shares Update on Government Release of UFO Files

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has confirmed that the Pentagon is actively working on identifying and releasing government files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life, following a directive from President Donald Trump. Hegseth made this announcement during a visit to Sierra Space in Louisville, Colorado, on February 23, 2026.

This initiative is significant as it responds to growing public interest in government information about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and extraterrestrial life. Trump has instructed federal agencies to begin the process of releasing relevant files, highlighting the importance of transparency on these complex matters.

Key facts

  • Event Date: February 23, 2026
  • Location: Sierra Space, Louisville, Colorado
  • President: Donald Trump
  • Defense Secretary: Pete Hegseth
  • Trump's Directive Date: February 19, 2026
  • Public Interest: Increased following Barack Obama's comments about aliens

Timeline

On February 19, 2026, President Trump announced his intention to direct federal agencies to identify and release files related to UAP and UFOs. This directive followed a surge of public interest after former President Barack Obama stated in a podcast interview that aliens are "real."

During Hegseth's visit to Sierra Space four days later, he confirmed that the Pentagon is in compliance with Trump’s instructions and is working on the release of the files. Hegseth noted that the timeline for declassification is uncertain but assured reporters that efforts are underway.

Official statements

“We’ve got our people working on it right now,” Hegseth said regarding the declassification efforts. “I don’t want to oversell how much time it will take, but we’re digging in.”
“We’ll see. I get to do the review and find out along with you!” Hegseth responded when asked about the existence of aliens.

Context

The renewed focus on UFOs and extraterrestrial life follows a period of heightened speculation after Obama’s remarks. In response to questions about his comments, Obama clarified that while the universe is vast, the chances of contact with extraterrestrials during his presidency were low.

Trump's directive indicates a shift towards greater transparency regarding UAP, reflecting public curiosity and a desire for more information on the subject.

What’s next

The Pentagon will continue to work on identifying and releasing the relevant files as directed by Trump. Further updates are expected as the review progresses.

UFO sighting over the M27 near Eastleigh

A driver has reported seeing a yellow-orange fireball near Southampton Airport at approximately 5:30 PM on January 4, 2026. The object reportedly vanished almost instantly and has drawn attention due to its unusual characteristics.

This sighting is significant as it has prompted the driver to submit dashcam footage to the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC). The centre collects and analyzes reports of unexplained aerial phenomena. Investigators will review the evidence to explore possible explanations, including atmospheric conditions or space debris.

Key facts

  • Time of sighting: 5:30 PM, January 4, 2026
  • Location: Near Southampton Airport
  • Witness: Motorist (identity not disclosed)
  • Evidence: Dashcam footage submitted to NUFORC
  • Agency involved: National UFO Reporting Center

Timeline

The sighting occurred on January 4, 2026, when a driver observed a yellow-orange fireball while driving. The object appeared for a brief moment before disappearing. Following the incident, the driver reviewed dashcam footage, which seemed to show a fast-moving white object streaking across the sky. The driver noted that there were no aircraft movements in the area at the time and expressed doubt that it was a drone due to the proximity to the airport.

The driver has since submitted the footage and details of the sighting to the NUFORC for further investigation. No official explanation has yet been confirmed, and the sighting remains under review.

Official statements

“The footage and report have now been submitted to the National UFO Reporting Center, which collects and analyses reports of unexplained aerial phenomena from around the world,” a spokesperson for the NUFORC said.

Context

Reports of unidentified flying objects are not uncommon, with various sightings documented over the years. Authorities typically investigate these claims to determine if there are conventional explanations. The review process often includes looking into atmospheric conditions, space debris, or meteors as potential sources of confusion.

What’s next

Investigators at the National UFO Reporting Center will assess the evidence submitted by the witness to determine if there is a conventional explanation for the sighting. Further updates will be provided as the investigation progresses.

PPG Wavetables, Dada & UFOs

Synthesizer enthusiasts have welcomed the announcement of new PPG-branded instruments, including a reissue of the iconic 1002 monosynth and a Eurorack module based on the PPG Wave 2.2, made by the Liquid Sky Artist Collective. This announcement was made in 2025, marking a significant return of PPG (Palm Products GmbH) after the original company ceased operations in 1987. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this announcement.

The reintroduction of PPG instruments is noteworthy for both fans of electronic music and the synthesizer community. Authorities in the music technology sector have expressed enthusiasm for the collaboration between Liquid Sky and Cornel Hecht of Hell Modular, which aims to deliver unique sounds while emphasizing environmental sustainability.

Key facts

  • Announcement Date: 2025
  • Company: PPG (Palm Products GmbH)
  • New Instruments: PPG 1002 monosynth, W2.2x4 Eurorack module
  • Collaborating Entity: Liquid Sky Artist Collective
  • Environmental Commitment: 75% of profits directed to reforestation efforts
  • Founding Year of Liquid Sky: 1986

Timeline

In 1987, PPG ceased operations, leaving a legacy in synthesizer design. In 2025, the company announced a revival with new products aimed at modern musicians. The Liquid Sky Artist Collective has taken the lead in producing these instruments, leveraging their artistic and environmental ethos.

The Liquid Sky Artist Collective, founded in 1986 in São Paulo, has a rich history that includes significant contributions to underground music culture. After relocating to Cologne, Germany, the collective has continued to innovate in both music and instrument design.

Official statements

“I'm against expansion just for the sake of expansion, and feel it's not 'greenwashing' - it’s an essential part of all our activities.” — Ingmar Koch, Liquid Sky Artist Collective

Context

The reintroduction of PPG instruments comes at a time when there is a growing interest in synthesizers and modular music production. The Liquid Sky Artist Collective has built a reputation for integrating environmental initiatives into their business model, which resonates with a community increasingly focused on sustainability.

What’s next

Looking ahead, the Liquid Sky Artist Collective plans to release additional modules that complement the PPG W2.2x4 and GLITHc, continuing their commitment to innovative design and environmental responsibility. Further announcements regarding product releases are expected in the coming months.

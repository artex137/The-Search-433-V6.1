Dierks Bentley Asks Fans To Help Him Identify UFOs

Dierks Bentley has taken to Instagram to share a video of what he believes is a UFO, prompting a flurry of reactions from fans. The country music star posted the video recently, inviting his followers to help him identify the unidentified flying objects he has observed in the night sky.

This call for assistance comes as Bentley continues his successful music career, known for hits like "Drunk On A Plane" and "What Was I Thinkin'." The singer's interest in UFOs has generated significant engagement among his fans, who have shared their own experiences and theories in the comments section of his post.

Key facts

  • Dierks Bentley shared the video on Instagram during his ongoing tour.
  • The video shows a small light moving in the night sky.
  • Bentley has described the objects as appearing to be at an altitude of approximately 21,300 metres (70,000 feet).
  • Fans have responded with various theories, including input from a pilot’s spouse.
  • No official investigations into the sightings have been disclosed.

Timeline

Dierks Bentley posted the video on Instagram amid his busy touring schedule. He has been observing the lights in the sky for several years but decided to share his findings recently. Following the post, fans quickly began commenting with their own insights and experiences related to UFOs.

In his post, Bentley mentioned that the lights appear to remain stationary despite traveling across the country and are not satellites, Starlink, or meteors.

Official statements

“See this all the time flying east to west in the NW sky. Been meaning to post about it for a few years. They light up, move around for a bit, and then go dark.”
“I hear other pilots, some that have been flying for 20 years, talk about it on tour 'guard' frequency. No one seems to know...”

Context

Bentley's fascination with UFOs reflects a growing public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. Recent years have seen an increase in sightings reported by both civilians and aviation professionals, prompting discussions about the nature of these objects.

While Bentley's post has attracted attention, no formal investigations or official statements have been made regarding the specific sightings he described.

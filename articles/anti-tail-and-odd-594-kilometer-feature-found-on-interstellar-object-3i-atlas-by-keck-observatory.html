Anti-Tail And Odd 594-Kilometer Feature Found On Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS By Keck Observatory

Astronomers have discovered unusual features of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, including a rare "anti-solar tail," during observations at the W. M. Keck Observatory. The findings were reported on August 24, 2025, as researchers analyzed the comet's spectra to identify its elemental composition.

This discovery is significant because it enhances understanding of interstellar objects, which could provide insights into the primordial composition of the Solar System. The international team of researchers has confirmed that 3I/ATLAS is a comet, due to its outgassing, and has been tracking it since its initial sighting by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) on July 1, 2025.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Observation Date: August 24, 2025
  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Eccentricity: Between 6.1 and 6.2
  • Distance from Sun: Observed at 3.8 astronomical units (au)
  • Nickel Emission Rate: Higher than 2I/Borisov and above the solar system comet median
  • Anti-Solar Tail Type: Not explicitly disclosed

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers at ATLAS discovered the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. The comet was confirmed to be the fastest object of its type ever observed, with an eccentricity that indicates it is a visitor from outside the Solar System. Following this, researchers analyzed the object using the Keck Cosmic Web Imager on August 24, 2025.

During their analysis, the team identified an "anti-solar tail" and reported unusual elemental ratios, including an extreme abundance of nickel compared to iron. The findings contribute to a growing understanding of the comet's unique characteristics and its long journey through interstellar space.

Official statements

“One avenue to understanding these objects is from cometary activity or lack thereof,” the research team stated in their paper. “When a comet warms, its volatile ices sublimate, lifting dust off the surface and creating the coma.”
“The presence of these lines in cometary spectra is unexpected because the surface blackbody equilibrium temperature is too low to allow the sublimation of refractory minerals containing these metals,” the team noted.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object discovered, following previous encounters with other interstellar visitors. The unusual features of this comet, including the anti-tail, have sparked interest among astronomers and the public, as they may provide clues about the formation and evolution of celestial bodies in the universe.

Previous observations of other comets have shown similar phenomena, but the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS present new puzzles for researchers to explore. The study of interstellar objects is crucial for understanding the origins of materials in the Solar System.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue observing 3I/ATLAS and other interstellar objects to gather more information about their properties and origins. Further studies may be enhanced with the upcoming launch of the Vera C. Rubin Telescope, which is expected to facilitate the discovery of additional interstellar visitors.

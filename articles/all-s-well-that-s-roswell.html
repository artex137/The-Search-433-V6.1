All’s well that’s Roswell

On February 19, 2023, the president announced that the government will begin releasing files related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and unidentified flying objects (UFOs). No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these announcements.

This development is significant as it follows decades of speculation about the Roswell Incident of 1947, which has become a focal point in UFO lore. Authorities have stated that the new efforts aim to open the historical record related to extraterrestrial life and aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: February 19, 2023
  • Location: Roswell, New Mexico
  • Initial incident: 1947
  • Previous reports: Two major reports released in the mid-1990s
  • Key programs mentioned: Project Mogul
  • Historical context: Increased declassification of Cold War-era information in the 1990s

Timeline

In 1947, a rancher near Roswell, New Mexico, reported finding debris, which was initially claimed by the military to be a “flying saucer.” This assertion was quickly retracted, with the military stating it was a weather balloon. The narrative shifted again in 1994 when the Air Force revealed the debris was from Project Mogul, a top-secret operation designed to detect nuclear explosions.

The Air Force published two reports in the mid-1990s, including “The Roswell Report – Fact vs. Fiction in the New Mexico Desert” in 1995 and “The Roswell Report: Case Closed” in 1997. These reports aimed to clarify misconceptions surrounding the incident, including claims of recovered alien bodies.

Official statements

“The new efforts will also be productive in opening up the history,” the president said regarding the recent directive.
“The two Air Force reports of the mid-1990s were important contributions to historical scholarship,” a government official noted.

Context

The Roswell Incident has been a part of the public consciousness for decades, particularly following the 1980 publication of the book “The Roswell Incident” by Charles Berlitz and William L. Moore. This book played a significant role in popularizing theories about alien encounters and government cover-ups.

In the 1990s, there was a notable increase in the declassification of Cold War-era documents, driven by both presidential and congressional pressure. This trend has allowed for greater transparency regarding military and intelligence operations, including the acknowledgment of various aerial reconnaissance programs.

What’s next

Authorities have begun the process of identifying and releasing additional government files related to UFOs and UAPs. Further investigations and public disclosures are expected as part of this initiative.

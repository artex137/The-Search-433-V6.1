An Extraterrestrial Wager

Dr. Michael Shermer, publisher of _Skeptic_ magazine, has placed a $1,000 wager regarding the discovery of extraterrestrial life, challenging Harvard astronomer Dr. Avi Loeb to prove the existence of alien visitation by December 31, 2030. The wager was announced on November 25, 2025, and is part of the Long Now Foundation’s Long Bets program.

This bet is significant as it highlights the ongoing debate surrounding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). The wager aims to encourage accountability and evidence-based discussions about extraterrestrial life through major scientific institutions and government agencies.

Key facts

  • Wager amount: $1,000
  • Deadline: December 31, 2030
  • Judging organizations: NASA, National Science Foundation, American Astronomical Society
  • Wager details: Confirmation of extraterrestrial intelligence or biological life by two of the three organizations
  • Wager platform: Long Now Foundation’s Long Bets program

Timeline

On November 25, 2025, Dr. Michael Shermer announced the wager with Dr. Avi Loeb regarding the existence of extraterrestrial life. The wager is set to be evaluated by the end of 2030, based on the confirmation of findings by recognized scientific institutions.

The Long Now Foundation’s Long Bets program, which facilitates such wagers, aims to promote long-term thinking and accountability in scientific predictions.

Official statements

“By Dec 31st 2030, at least two of these three scientific organizations will affirm that discovery of extraterrestrial intelligence has been made.” - Dr. Michael Shermer
“It is better to be an optimist because life is sometimes a self-fulfilling prophecy.” - Dr. Avi Loeb

Context

The wager comes amid a growing interest in UAPs and UFOs, particularly following recent testimonies before the U.S. Congress by proponents claiming evidence of alien encounters. The historical skepticism surrounding such claims has led to calls for more rigorous scientific examination and accountability.

Dr. Loeb points to recent advancements in astronomy and scientific tools, such as the discovery of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS and the launch of new observatories, as reasons to remain optimistic about the search for extraterrestrial artifacts.

What’s next

As the deadline approaches, both Dr. Shermer and Dr. Loeb will prepare for potential outcomes based on the advancements in the search for extraterrestrial life and the responses from the scientific community. The results of this wager could influence future discussions on UFOs and UAPs.

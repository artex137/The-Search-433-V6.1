Tennessee Congressman Doubles Down On Notion That There Are 'Five Or Six' Underwater Alien Bases

Tennessee Congressman Tim Burchett has reiterated his claims that the United States government possesses knowledge of underwater alien bases. Burchett made these remarks on Thursday in Washington D.C., following a hearing on Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAPs).

This assertion raises questions about government transparency regarding UAPs and possible extraterrestrial activity. Burchett suggests that there are “entities” located at five or six deep-water sites on Earth, indicating that the government may be withholding information from the public.

Key facts

  • Congressman Tim Burchett has claimed the existence of underwater alien bases.
  • Burchett stated there are “five or six” deep-water sites with entities.
  • He emphasized the need for transparency regarding UAP sightings.
  • Burchett has previously alleged government cover-ups related to UFOs since at least 2023.
  • Burchett expressed skepticism about the Pentagon's openness regarding military contractors.

Timeline

On Thursday, following a hearing on UAPs, Congressman Burchett made his latest claims about underwater alien bases in Washington D.C. He highlighted the discrepancy between reported UAP speeds and conventional military capabilities.

Burchett has been vocal about his beliefs regarding UAPs and government secrecy since at least 2023, suggesting that the government has been concealing significant findings related to UFOs.

Official statements

“What if these are entities that are here on this Earth, have been on this Earth for who knows how long?” Burchett asked. “We have a higher propensity of sightings around these five or six, I believe, deep-water areas.”
On the Pentagon’s handling of information, Burchett stated, “Oh, it’s 100% what they do. They pass it on, and we can’t FOIA it because it’s just outside of our purview.”

Context

Burchett has a history of making controversial claims regarding UAPs, alleging that the government is not forthcoming about its findings. His assertions about underwater alien bases tap into a long-standing narrative about extraterrestrial life and government cover-ups.

Previous discussions around UAPs have highlighted the need for greater transparency from military and government agencies regarding unidentified sightings and encounters.

What’s next

Burchett's claims may prompt further discussions in Congress about the need for transparency regarding UAP investigations and military operations related to unidentified aerial phenomena.

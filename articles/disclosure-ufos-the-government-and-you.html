Disclosure: UFOs, the Government, and You

The UFO phenomenon has engaged the American public for nearly eight decades, with recent developments in the Disclosure Movement calling for governments to release their intelligence on extraterrestrial life. A survey conducted by Armstrong State University found that 87.2% of 47 students believe in life beyond Earth, while 68.1% suspect that the U.S. government has withheld evidence related to extraterrestrial beings.

This issue holds significance as authorities, including Congress, have begun to examine UFOs and related phenomena more closely. Multiple hearings have taken place since 2022, featuring testimonies from various witnesses regarding national security concerns related to unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • 87.2% of surveyed students believe in extraterrestrial life.
  • 68.1% suspect government concealment of evidence.
  • Congress has held multiple hearings on UFOs since 2022.
  • Former President Barack Obama acknowledged the existence of unidentified phenomena.
  • Notable historical sightings include those reported by former President Jimmy Carter in 1969.

Timeline

Modern UFO sightings began during World War II, when pilots reported strange lights, termed “foo-fighters.” In 1952, the U.S. Air Force initiated “Project Blue Book,” which lasted until 1969. Despite the program's end, sightings continued to be reported.

In 1969, former President Jimmy Carter witnessed a luminous object in Leary, GA. In 1973, a surge of UFO sightings occurred across Georgia, including reports from military personnel at Hunter Army Airfield. In September 1977, a supposed mass abduction incident was reported at Fort Benning, although its validity remains disputed.

Official statements

“The data points, quite empirically, [that] we are not alone,” said congressional whistleblower and former Air Force Officer David Grusch.
Former President Barack Obama stated, “They’re real, but I haven’t seen them.”

Context

The UFO phenomenon has evolved significantly since the mid-20th century. Government programs, such as Project Blue Book, aimed to investigate potential extraterrestrial threats. Despite the official closure of such programs, public interest has surged, particularly with recent congressional actions and the acknowledgment of unidentified phenomena by former officials.

What’s next

Congress will continue to investigate UFOs and related national security issues, with further hearings and testimonies expected in the coming months.

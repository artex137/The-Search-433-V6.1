Belgian UFO Monument Removed at Behest of Late Witness’s Family

A monument commemorating a famous Belgian UFO sighting has been removed following a request from the family of the primary witness, Marin Vandercruyssen. The removal took place in Lotenhulle, Belgium, after the family petitioned for the monument's dismantling. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the monument's removal.

This development highlights ongoing discussions about the legacy of UFO sightings and the perspectives of those involved. Local authorities have complied with the family's wishes, and the monument is no longer present in the community.

Key facts

  • Monument location: Lotenhulle, Belgium
  • Original sighting date: July 1955
  • Witness: Marin Vandercruyssen
  • Objects described: Five silver disc-shaped UFOs
  • Witness account: Objects moved "at the speed of a zeppelin"

Timeline

In July 1955, Marin Vandercruyssen reported observing five UFOs moving in formation across the sky in Lotenhulle. The event attracted dozens of onlookers who witnessed the sighting. The monument was later erected to commemorate the incident.

Recently, the family of Vandercruyssen petitioned for the removal of the monument, citing their wishes to no longer have the structure in place. Authorities have acted upon this request, and the monument has been dismantled.

Official statements

“The family has expressed their desire for the monument to be removed, and we have respected their wishes,” a local official stated.

Context

The 1955 sighting by Vandercruyssen has been a notable part of Belgium's UFO history. Over the years, various accounts and reports have emerged regarding unidentified aerial phenomena, contributing to a broader discussion about the nature of such sightings.

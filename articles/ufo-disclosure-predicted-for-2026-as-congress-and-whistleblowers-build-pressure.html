UFO disclosure predicted for 2026 as Congress and whistleblowers build pressure

Speculation has intensified that the United States government may reveal authentic evidence related to UFOs in 2026. Reports indicate that whistleblower activity, political pressures, and revised laws have created a climate for significant disclosure regarding how the White House has been documenting information on extraterrestrial spacecraft.

This potential unveiling is significant as it could reshape public understanding of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) and influence international relations. Congress has already begun taking action, mandating new briefings on UAP encounters dating back to 2004 through the National Defense Authorization Act for fiscal year 2026.

Key facts

  • Speculation about UFO disclosure has increased in recent weeks.
  • The National Defense Authorization Act for fiscal year 2026 requires briefings on UAP encounters since 2004.
  • The documentary The Age of Disclosure released in November 2025 features testimonies from 34 current and former government officials.
  • Whistleblower David Grusch has alleged covert programs for recovering extraterrestrial crafts.
  • High-ranking officials, including Secretary of State Marco Rubio, have acknowledged UAP incidents over sensitive areas.

Timeline

In November 2025, the documentary The Age of Disclosure premiered, featuring insights from numerous military and intelligence figures, which reignited public interest and political discourse on UFOs. Following this, Congress has taken steps to address the classification of UAP-related information, culminating in the National Defense Authorization Act for fiscal year 2026.

As of 2026, the legislative push is expected to intensify, fueled by ongoing whistleblower activity and proposed amendments to the UAP Disclosure Act.

Official statements

“With ongoing efforts like the proposed UAP Disclosure Act amendments and whistleblowers such as David Grusch continuing to advise congressional caucuses into 2026, the legislative push is intensifying,” said Mark Christopher Lee, a filmmaker and researcher.
“We've had repeated instances of something operating in the airspace over restricted nuclear facilities - and it's not ours,” stated Secretary of State Marco Rubio in the documentary.
“The United States government appears to be partway through a multi-year process to declassify and disclose information on the existence of a technologically advanced non-human intelligence responsible for Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs),” cautioned Helen McCaw, a former Bank of England analyst.

Context

The recent surge in interest surrounding UFOs is part of a broader trend of increasing transparency from government agencies. Previous incidents of UAP sightings and encounters have often been met with skepticism and secrecy. However, recent whistleblower accounts and public demands have shifted the narrative towards accountability and disclosure.

As governments around the world grapple with the implications of UAPs, the potential for international ramifications grows, particularly if evidence of non-human intelligence emerges.

What’s next

Investigations into UAP encounters will continue as Congress seeks to enforce transparency through new legislation. The pressure from whistleblowers and public interest may lead to substantial disclosures in 2026, although the specifics remain to be seen.

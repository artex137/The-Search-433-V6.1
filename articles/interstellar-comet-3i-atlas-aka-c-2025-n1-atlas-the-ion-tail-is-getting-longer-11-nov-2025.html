Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS shows longer ion tail

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, also known as C/2025 N1 ATLAS, has displayed a longer ion tail as observed on 11 November 2025, from Manciano, Italy. The comet was imaged at 14 degrees above the eastern horizon, with a bright Moon positioned approximately 70 degrees away.

This observation is significant as it highlights the improving visibility of the comet's ion tail, which is essential for ongoing astronomical studies. The Virtual Telescope Project, which operates the imaging equipment, has committed to continuing monitoring this exceptional object.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1 ATLAS)
  • Date of observation: 11 November 2025
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Observation equipment: ARTEC250+Paramount ME+C3Pro61000EC
  • Moon phase: 61%
  • Ion tail length: at least 0.7 degrees

Timeline

On 11 November 2025, astronomers captured images of 3I/ATLAS using a robotic unit as part of the Virtual Telescope Project. The comet's ion tail was prominently visible, extending in a northeastern direction.

The imaging involved a sigma-clipping combination of 18 exposures, each lasting 120 seconds. The results showed a clear view of both the ion tail and the anti-tail, which pointed southeast.

Official statements

“We see how the ion tail of 3I/ATLAS is clearly showing better and better, thanks to the improving perspective,” said Gianluca Masi, the lead astronomer for the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable as an interstellar comet, a rare occurrence that provides valuable insights into the composition and behavior of celestial bodies from outside our Solar System. Observations of such comets help astronomers understand the dynamics of these objects and their interactions with solar radiation.

What’s next

The Virtual Telescope Project plans to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS to capture further developments in its tail and other characteristics as the comet approaches perihelion.

Trump Directs Pentagon to Release UFO Files as Public Curiosity Surges

U.S. President Donald Trump has ordered the Pentagon to declassify government files related to UFOs and extra-terrestrial life, citing "tremendous public interest" in the topic. The directive was announced on Truth Social on 23 February 2026 and instructs Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth to identify and release documents linked to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), UFOs, and potential alien life.

This move is significant as it marks the start of a formal review process, although no timeline or scope for the disclosures has been confirmed. Trump’s announcement follows renewed discussion after former President Barack Obama commented on the possibility of extra-terrestrial life in a recent podcast interview. Trump criticized Obama’s remarks, stating that releasing files would help clarify what is known.

Key facts

  • Announcement made by President Trump on 23 February 2026.
  • Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth is tasked with identifying and releasing documents.
  • Previous investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena have been conducted for decades.
  • Programs include the Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Programme (AATIP), launched in 2007.
  • No confirmed evidence of extra-terrestrial life has been presented to date.

Timeline

On 23 February 2026, President Trump announced the directive to declassify UFO-related files on Truth Social. This followed remarks by former President Obama regarding extra-terrestrial life, which prompted Trump to advocate for transparency.

The Pentagon has a history of investigating unidentified aerial phenomena, with programmes like AATIP and the UAP Task Force assessing hundreds of unexplained sightings over the years. However, previous reports have found many incidents unresolved and have not provided evidence of alien technology.

Official statements

“Releasing files would help clarify what is known,” Trump stated in his announcement.

Context

The Pentagon has been examining unidentified aerial phenomena for decades, with various programs aimed at understanding these occurrences. The AATIP, established in 2007, and subsequent initiatives have looked into numerous sightings, but experts have cautioned against sensationalism, emphasizing the need for careful assessment of data.

What’s next

As agencies review extensive archives, initial disclosures could emerge in the coming months. Transparency advocates have welcomed the initiative, while officials remind the public that no confirmed evidence of extra-terrestrial life exists at this time.

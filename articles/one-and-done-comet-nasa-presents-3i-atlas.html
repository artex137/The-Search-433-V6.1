One-and-done comet: Nasa presents 3I/Atlas

Nasa has unveiled close-up images of the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas, which made its closest approach to Mars last month. The comet passed harmlessly by at a distance of 29 million kilometers (18 million miles) from the planet.

This discovery is significant as 3I/Atlas is only the third confirmed object to visit the solar system from another star. Nasa and the European Space Agency have mobilized their spacecraft and telescopes to observe the comet, which is currently about 307 million kilometers (190 million miles) from Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/Atlas discovered in summer 2022
  • Closest approach to Mars occurred last month
  • Distance from Mars during closest approach: 29 million kilometers (18 million miles)
  • Current distance from Earth: 307 million kilometers (190 million miles)
  • Three Nasa spacecraft and two European Space Agency satellites observed the comet

Timeline

Nasa discovered comet 3I/Atlas during the summer of 2022. It passed Mars in October 2022, where it was observed by multiple spacecraft. As of now, astronomers continue to monitor its trajectory and appearance from Earth.

“The comet zipped harmlessly past Mars last month,” Nasa officials stated.

Context

3I/Atlas is significant as it marks one of the few interstellar visitors to the solar system, following the discovery of similar objects in recent years. This highlights advancements in astronomical observation and the ongoing exploration of our cosmic neighborhood.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to observe 3I/Atlas with various telescopes, including the Webb Space Telescope, as the comet travels through the solar system. Ground-based astronomers are also set to capture additional images in the coming weeks.

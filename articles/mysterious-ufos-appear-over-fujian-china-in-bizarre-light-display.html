Mysterious UFOs Appear Over Fujian, China in Bizarre Light Display

On February 10, 2025, around 8:00 p.m., residents of Zhangzhou City in Fujian Province reported seeing bright unidentified flying objects (UFOs) moving rapidly in the sky. Witnesses described the objects initially as point-like before they formed a straight-line formation, eventually multiplying to over ten and forming a circle before disappearing completely.

The sighting has gained significant attention, with videos and photos circulating on social media. Local authorities, including the Zhangzhou city government, have stated they are unaware of the phenomenon. The incident has sparked various speculations online, with some attributing the lights to drones or Starlink satellites.

Key facts

  • Event date: February 10, 2025
  • Time of sighting: Approximately 8:00 p.m.
  • Location: Zhangzhou City, Fujian Province, China
  • Duration of display: About ten seconds
  • Witness reports: Over ten objects observed
  • Local government response: Unaware of the incident

Timeline

Witnesses first observed the UFOs around 8:00 p.m. on February 10. Initially, the objects appeared as bright points in the sky. They quickly organized into a straight line, then multiplied and formed a circle before vanishing within ten seconds. Videos captured during the event show the objects moving in an orderly fashion.

Following the sighting, the phenomenon became a trending topic on social media, leading to various theories and discussions among netizens. Some suggested the lights were related to known satellite systems, while others speculated about the possibility of extraterrestrial activity.

Official statements

“The object formed a point in the sky, then spread and lined up in a straight line. At first, I thought it was a drone—it was very bright.” - Mr. Zhang, local witness
“I looked up and suddenly saw two rows of red dot-like things in the sky. They flashed for less than a minute. It was very shocking.” - Another witness

Context

This sighting is part of a broader trend, as UFO reports have increased across various regions in China, including Tianjin and Beijing. Notably, a UFO sighting at Tianjin Airport on September 11, 2024, caused significant flight delays, affecting over 3,000 travelers. Despite public interest, local governments have often claimed ignorance regarding these occurrences.

In recent years, interest in UFOs has surged globally, with discussions about extraterrestrial life becoming more prominent. U.S. Congressman Mike Burchett has raised concerns about government transparency regarding UFOs, while various researchers have suggested that evidence of extraterrestrial life may already exist.

What’s next

As of February 11, 2025, the exact nature of the UFOs remains unknown. Local authorities have not announced plans for further investigation, and it remains unclear if any follow-up actions will be taken regarding the event.

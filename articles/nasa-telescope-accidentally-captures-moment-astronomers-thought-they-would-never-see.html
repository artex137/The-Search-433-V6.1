NASA telescope accidentally captures moment astronomers thought they would never see

Astronomers have observed Comet C/2025 K1 (ATLAS) breaking apart, a rare event captured by the Hubble Space Telescope in November 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery is significant as it provides insight into the composition and evolution of comets, which are remnants from the formation of the solar system. Researchers have reported their findings in a journal earlier this week and plan further investigations to understand the implications of this event.

Key facts

  • Event occurred in November 2025
  • Comet C/2025 K1 (ATLAS) was observed breaking apart
  • Captured by the Hubble Space Telescope
  • Research led by John Noonan and Dennis Bodewits of Auburn University
  • Findings published in a journal earlier this week

Timeline

In November 2025, astronomers initially targeted another comet for observation. Due to technical constraints, they redirected their efforts to Comet C/2025 K1 (ATLAS). During this observation, the comet unexpectedly broke apart.

Scientists were able to analyze the data shortly after the event, revealing four comets in the images instead of the anticipated one. This unexpected finding has sparked significant interest in further research.

Official statements

“Sometimes the best science happens by accident,” said John Noonan, a research professor at Auburn University. “We had to find a new target – and right when we observed it, it happened to break apart, which is the slimmest of slim chances.”
“The irony is now we’re just studying a regular comet and it crumbles in front of our eyes,” said principal investigator Dennis Bodewits, also of Auburn University.
“This is telling us something very important about the physics of what’s happening at the comet’s surface,” Noonan added. “We may be seeing the timescale it takes to form a substantial dust layer that can then be ejected by the gas.”

Context

Comets are believed to be remnants from the early solar system, composed of primordial materials. Observing the breakdown of a comet can provide scientists with valuable information about its original composition and evolutionary processes. Previous studies have focused on the general characteristics of comets, but witnessing one disintegrate offers a unique opportunity for research.

What’s next

Further investigations are planned to explore the composition of Comet C/2025 K1 (ATLAS) and to gain insights into the origins of our solar system. Researchers aim to analyze the data collected from the Hubble Space Telescope to deepen their understanding of cometary physics.

Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Defies Comet Physics as NASA Confirms Coherent Anti-Tail Structure

Nasa has confirmed that interstellar object 3I/ATLAS displays an unusual anti-tail structure in a new image captured by the Hubble Space Telescope on November 30, 2025. The image shows a spherical glow extending 40,000 kilometres around the object's nucleus, as well as a narrow, sunward extension reaching approximately 60,000 kilometres. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery matters as it challenges existing theories about comet behavior and may offer new insights into the composition and dynamics of interstellar objects. NASA scientists are conducting further analysis to understand the implications of this finding on comet physics.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 30, 2025
  • Distance of spherical glow: 40,000 kilometres
  • Length of sunward extension: 60,000 kilometres
  • Instrument used: Hubble's Wide Field Camera 3
  • Filter used: F350LP

Timeline

On November 30, 2025, NASA captured an image of 3I/ATLAS using the Hubble Space Telescope, which revealed a distinct anti-tail structure. This finding has prompted significant interest and investigation from the astrophysics community.

Official statements

“The alignment of the anti-tail defies standard comet physics,” said a NASA spokesperson. “This raises important questions about the behavior of interstellar objects.”

Context

Previous studies of comets have generally not observed such coherent anti-tail structures. This new information may influence future research and understanding of how interstellar objects interact with solar radiation.

What’s next

NASA plans to conduct further observations and analyses of 3I/ATLAS to explore the underlying mechanisms that could explain the observed anti-tail structure.

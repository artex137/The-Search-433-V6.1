Alien Crash Rumours, Secret RAF Police Units And Cold War Bunkers: Mystery Of ‘Area 51’

RAF Rudloe Manor, located in Wiltshire, has become a focal point for UFO enthusiasts due to its history of secretive military operations and alleged extraterrestrial investigations. The abandoned base, taken over by the Royal Air Force in 1950, has been linked to various conspiracy theories surrounding UFO sightings and military cover-ups, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The significance of RAF Rudloe Manor lies in its past as a Cold War relic where RAF police and secret service personnel conducted operations away from public view. Authorities have confirmed the base's role in UFO investigations, which has attracted renewed interest from historians and paranormal investigators.

Key facts

  • Location: Wiltshire, UK
  • Established: 1950
  • Formerly operated by: Royal Air Force
  • Documented UFO involvement: Since at least 2010
  • Capacity of underground complex: Up to 4,000 people

Timeline

RAF Rudloe Manor was established in 1950 as a Royal Air Force base. During the early Cold War, it became a hub for espionage and counterintelligence operations. In 2010, declassified government documents confirmed the base's involvement in investigating UFO sightings, revealing that the RAF Police’s “Flying Complaints Flight” collected reports of unexplained aerial phenomena.

Documents from this period indicate that ministers received weekly briefings on UFO sightings. Notably, one document disclosed that Winston Churchill ordered details of a specific UFO encounter to be kept secret for 50 years to avoid public panic.

Official statements

“The atmosphere is chilling,” said urban explorer Gareth Smith, who has visited the site multiple times. “Nature is taking it back,” he added, as he pointed to brambles growing through walls and overturned furniture.

Context

RAF Rudloe Manor has been compared to the US Air Force's Area 51, known for its associations with alleged alien crash recoveries and government secrecy. The base's reputation has grown over the years, particularly with the release of declassified files that highlight its role in UFO investigations.

What’s next

Investigations into the historical significance of RAF Rudloe Manor are ongoing, with historians and enthusiasts seeking to uncover more about its operations and the nature of its secretive activities during the Cold War.

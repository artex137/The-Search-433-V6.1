Space agencies intensify studies on interstellar comet after detection of atypical radio signals

Space agencies have increased their investigations into comet 3I ATLAS following the detection of unusual radio signals on February 20, 2026. The signals were detected by astronomers at the Arecibo Observatory in Puerto Rico.

This development is significant as it may provide insights into the origins and characteristics of interstellar objects. Authorities, including NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA), are coordinating efforts to analyze the comet's trajectory and the nature of the signals.

Key facts

  • Date of signal detection: February 20, 2026
  • Location of detection: Arecibo Observatory, Puerto Rico
  • Agencies involved: NASA, European Space Agency (ESA)
  • Comet designation: 3I ATLAS

Timeline

On February 20, 2026, astronomers at the Arecibo Observatory detected atypical radio signals from comet 3I ATLAS. This prompted immediate interest and analysis from space agencies.

Following the detection, NASA and ESA announced plans to collaborate on further studies to understand the comet's composition and trajectory.

Official statements

“The detection of these signals is an exciting opportunity to learn more about interstellar objects,” said a NASA spokesperson.
“We are committed to working with our international partners to investigate these findings,” stated an ESA representative.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is notable as one of the few interstellar objects observed entering the solar system. Previous studies of interstellar comets have provided valuable data about the formation of the solar system.

What’s next

NASA and ESA will continue their investigations into the radio signals and the comet's characteristics. Further updates are expected as analysis progresses.

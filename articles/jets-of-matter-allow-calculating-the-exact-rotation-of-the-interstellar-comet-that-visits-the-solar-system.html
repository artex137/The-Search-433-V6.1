Jets of matter allow calculating the exact rotation of the interstellar comet that visits the solar system

Scientists have used jets of matter from comet 3I ATLAS to calculate its exact rotation as it approaches the solar system. The calculations were made public on February 21, 2026, by researchers at an undisclosed institution.

This discovery is significant as it enhances our understanding of interstellar comets and their behavior. Authorities in the scientific community are now focused on further analysis of the comet's trajectory and composition.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I ATLAS
  • Calculation date: February 21, 2026
  • Research institution: Not disclosed
  • Discovery significance: Improved understanding of interstellar comets

Timeline

On February 21, 2026, researchers successfully calculated the rotation of comet 3I ATLAS using jets of matter. This method allowed for precise measurements of the comet's motion as it travels through the solar system.

Official statements

“This calculation provides valuable insights into the dynamics of interstellar objects,” said a representative from the research team.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is one of the few interstellar comets observed in our solar system. Previous studies have focused on its composition and trajectory, but this new calculation adds a crucial layer to understanding its rotation.

What’s next

Researchers plan to conduct further observations of comet 3I ATLAS to gather more data on its characteristics and trajectory as it continues its journey through the solar system.

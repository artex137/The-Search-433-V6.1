SPHEREx watches an interstellar comet release a buried chemical mix

The SPHEREx space observatory has observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it releases a buried mix of chemicals, according to NASA officials. The event occurred as the comet passed through the solar system, but specific details about the timing and location have not been disclosed.

This observation is significant as it provides insights into the composition of interstellar objects and their potential origins. NASA scientists are analyzing the data collected by SPHEREx to understand more about the chemistry of comets and their implications for planetary formation.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object.
  • The SPHEREx mission is led by NASA.
  • The exact timing of the comet’s release of chemicals is not disclosed.
  • SPHEREx aims to study the formation of stars and planets.

Timeline

SPHEREx has been monitoring various celestial bodies since its launch. Recently, it focused on comet 3I/ATLAS as it traversed the solar system. The observatory has collected data that indicates the comet is releasing a mix of buried chemicals, which could provide valuable information about its structure and history.

Official statements

“The data from SPHEREx will help us understand the origins of comets and their role in the solar system,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects. Previous studies have revealed that these comets can carry significant information about the early solar system and the materials that formed planets. SPHEREx aims to enhance our understanding of such celestial phenomena.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue analyzing the data from SPHEREx to uncover more details about the composition of comet 3I/ATLAS and other interstellar objects in the future.

NASA Confirms Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS on Stable Course, No Threat to Earth

NASA has confirmed that the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is on a stable path through the Solar System as of October 30, 2025, and poses no risk to Earth. This announcement follows weeks of speculation on social media regarding potential changes in its trajectory.

The Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) has released data indicating that the comet's velocity and direction have remained constant since its discovery in July 2025. This confirmation reassures both scientists and the public that the comet will not impact Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet designation: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery date: July 2025
  • Confirmation date: October 30, 2025
  • Data source: NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)
  • Current status: Stable path, no risk to Earth

Timeline

In July 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered, prompting interest and analysis from astronomers. Speculation regarding its trajectory began circulating on social media in the following weeks. On October 30, 2025, NASA confirmed through data from JPL that the comet remains on a stable trajectory and poses no threat to Earth.

Official statements

“The data shows that the comet's velocity and direction have remained constant,” said a spokesperson from NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

Context

Prior to this announcement, public concern had grown over the potential for interstellar objects to alter their paths unexpectedly. NASA has previously issued statements on the importance of monitoring such objects to ensure planetary safety.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the Solar System, ensuring that any changes in its trajectory are promptly reported.

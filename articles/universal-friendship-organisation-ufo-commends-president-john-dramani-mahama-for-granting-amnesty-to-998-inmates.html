Universal Friendship Organisation (UFO) commends President John Dramani Mahama for granting amnesty to 998 inmates

The Universal Friendship Organisation (UFO) has commended President John Dramani Mahama for granting amnesty to 998 inmates in a ceremony held on October 28, 2023. The amnesty applies to inmates across various prisons in the country, aimed at reducing overcrowding and promoting rehabilitation.

This decision has significant implications for the criminal justice system in Ghana. The UFO has expressed its support, highlighting the importance of second chances for individuals who have served their time. The amnesty is part of a broader initiative to reform the prison system and improve inmate welfare.

Key facts

  • 998 inmates have been granted amnesty.
  • The decision was announced on October 28, 2023.
  • The amnesty affects inmates in various prisons across Ghana.
  • The initiative aims to reduce prison overcrowding.
  • The Universal Friendship Organisation has publicly supported this decision.

Timeline

On October 28, 2023, President John Dramani Mahama announced the amnesty for 998 inmates. This initiative has been framed as part of efforts to reform the prison system in Ghana.

In the lead-up to this announcement, discussions regarding prison reforms and overcrowding have been ongoing, with various advocacy groups, including the UFO, calling for action.

Official statements

“We commend President Mahama for this humane decision. It reflects a commitment to rehabilitation and the belief that everyone deserves a second chance,” said a spokesperson from the Universal Friendship Organisation.

Context

Overcrowding in Ghana's prisons has been a longstanding issue, with many facilities operating above their intended capacity. The government has faced pressure from human rights organizations to improve conditions and provide alternatives to incarceration.

This amnesty follows previous initiatives aimed at reforming the criminal justice system, including earlier amnesty grants and discussions about reducing sentences for minor offences.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to monitor the reintegration of the released inmates into society. Further discussions regarding ongoing prison reforms and potential legislative changes are anticipated as part of the government’s commitment to improving the justice system.

NASA’s Mars fleet captures unprecedented views of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

On 25th November 2025, NASA announced that its Martian assets have successfully captured detailed observations of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The imaging campaign took place in October 2025 while the comet was positioned behind the Sun, making it unobservable from Earth.

This observation marks a significant milestone in astrophysical research. The data collected from multiple spacecraft provides scientists with a unique opportunity to study material from another star system, as 3I/ATLAS will not return once it departs our solar system.

Key facts

  • NASA’s Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter captured detailed images on 2nd October 2025.
  • The orbiter was approximately 30 million kilometres from the comet during imaging.
  • MAVEN spacecraft began its imaging campaign on 27th September 2025.
  • The Perseverance rover captured images on 4th October 2025.
  • The European Space Agency’s Trace Gas Orbiter participated in the observational effort.

Timeline

NASA's coordinated imaging campaign began with MAVEN's ultraviolet observations on 27th September 2025. The Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter followed with detailed imaging on 2nd October, utilizing its High Resolution Imaging Science Experiment (HiRISE) camera. The Perseverance rover contributed on 4th October, capturing a long-exposure image from the Martian surface.

The campaign was designed to gather crucial data about the comet’s characteristics while it was hidden from Earth-based observations.

Official statements

“These multi-platform observations provide a rare opportunity to study material from another star system,” NASA officials stated.

Context

3I/ATLAS represents a unique scientific opportunity due to its interstellar origin. The comet's trajectory indicates it has traveled from beyond our solar system, making these observations crucial for understanding its composition and characteristics.

What’s next

Following the successful imaging campaign, scientists will analyze the data to gain insights into the comet's nucleus size and coma particle characteristics. This analysis may yield valuable information about the formation of celestial bodies in other star systems.

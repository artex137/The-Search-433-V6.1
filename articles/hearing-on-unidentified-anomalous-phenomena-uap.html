Hearing on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP)

A hearing on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) took place on Wednesday, October 25, 2023, in Washington, D.C. The session featured Rep. Nancy Mace (R-NC) questioning witness David Grusch about UAP recovery efforts.

This hearing is significant as it addresses ongoing concerns about UAPs and the government's transparency regarding potential extraterrestrial encounters. Grusch indicated that while he cannot publicly confirm contact with extraterrestrial crafts, he revealed that non-human biologics were found during some UAP crash recoveries.

Key facts

  • Date: October 25, 2023
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Witness: David Grusch
  • Questioned by: Rep. Nancy Mace (R-NC)
  • Findings: Non-human biologics discovered during UAP recovery efforts

Timeline

On October 25, 2023, the U.S. House held a hearing focused on UAPs. Rep. Nancy Mace questioned David Grusch about the government's efforts in recovering UAPs. Grusch discussed findings related to non-human biologics during these recovery operations.

Official statements

“I can't publicly discuss if the government has made contact with extraterrestrial crafts,” said David Grusch during the hearing.

Context

This hearing is part of a broader inquiry into UAPs, which have gained increased attention from both lawmakers and the public in recent years. Concerns about national security and unexplained aerial phenomena have prompted calls for more transparency from government agencies.

What’s next

Further investigations into UAP recovery efforts are expected as lawmakers continue to seek clarity on the government's findings and policies regarding these phenomena.

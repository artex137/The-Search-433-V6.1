NASA Puts Claims That Interstellar Object Coming Our Way Is Just A Comet And Not An Alien Spacecraft As Other Astronomers Have Claimed

Published 2025-12-09 15:32 UTC

NASA Addresses Claims About Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS

NASA has confirmed that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, entering our solar system, is a comet, countering claims from some astronomers that it may be an alien spacecraft. The object is currently traveling toward the center of the solar system and is expected to continue its journey before returning to interstellar space.

This clarification is significant as 3I/ATLAS is only the third confirmed interstellar object to be detected. Authorities, including NASA's lead scientist for solar system small bodies, Tom Statler, are monitoring the comet closely to learn more about its properties and composition.

Key facts

  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Type: Comet
  • Discovery: Third confirmed interstellar object
  • NASA's Lead Scientist: Tom Statler
  • Claimant of Alien Origin: Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb
  • Status: Currently traveling toward the center of the solar system

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected entering the solar system, sparking excitement among astronomers. Early observations indicated the object was traveling at high speeds and exhibited unusual dust behavior. Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysicist, claimed it could be an alien spacecraft, a notion that has been met with skepticism from other scientists.

NASA's Tom Statler has since stated that the object exhibits characteristics typical of comets, reinforcing the view that it is not an alien craft. Further analysis is ongoing as the object continues its journey through the solar system.

Official statements

“It looks like a comet. It does comet things. It very, very strongly resembles, in just about every way, the comets that we know. It’s a comet,” said Tom Statler.
“This gives us a window we’ve never had before, directly into the composition of other solar systems,” Statler added, highlighting the importance of studying such interstellar objects.

Context

3I/ATLAS follows the detection of other interstellar objects, which have become increasingly common with advancements in detection technology. The scientific community remains cautious but intrigued by the implications of studying such objects, as they offer insights into the composition of distant solar systems.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to gather data on 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the inner solar system, aiming to further understand its characteristics and origins before it moves back into interstellar space.

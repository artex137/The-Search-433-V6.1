Pentagon investigating mysterious UAP event reported by the Navy near Virginia’s coast

Experts in the Pentagon’s All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) have begun an investigation into a report from the U.S. Navy involving approximately 100 unidentified airborne objects and watercraft operating off the coast of Virginia. The incident was disclosed in AARO’s Fiscal Year 2025 Consolidated Annual Report on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena, released on July 21, 2026. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This investigation is significant as it reflects ongoing concerns about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) reported near U.S. military installations. AARO officials have noted that a lack of timely sensor data continues to hinder their ability to resolve such cases. The office, established under the Biden administration in 2022, is tasked with detecting UAP that could threaten national security or military operations.

Key facts

  • Incident reported off the coast of Virginia.
  • Approximately 100 airborne UAP and two likely uncrewed surface systems involved.
  • AARO resolved 114 of 319 UAP reports in the past fiscal year.
  • AARO received a total of 319 UAP reports between June 2, 2024, and May 30, 2025.
  • 284 of the reports were from the 2024-2025 reporting period; 25 were from 2010-2024.
  • As of May 30, 2025, AARO's case holdings included 1,870 reports.

Timeline

In June 2024, AARO began collecting reports of UAP incidents. The reporting period lasted until May 30, 2025, during which the office received 319 reports. The report detailing the UAP incident off the Virginia coast was included in AARO's annual review released on July 21, 2026.

In the latest report, AARO confirmed that the single maritime UAP case involved U.S. Navy assets and included descriptions of approximately 100 airborne objects. This incident is part of a broader trend of UAP sightings near military and critical infrastructure sites.

Official statements

“AARO is actively investigating this event in coordination with the reporting unit,” officials stated in the report.
“If validated, the attendant phenomena underlying these reports may represent a currently unmitigated threat vector,” officials noted regarding the potential implications of UAP sightings.

Context

AARO was established in 2022 to address mounting concerns regarding UAP, particularly in relation to national security. The office's findings reflect a continued pattern of UAP sightings near U.S. military bases and other sensitive areas. In past reports, officials have indicated that none of the resolved UAP cases have suggested advanced foreign adversarial capabilities.

The Intelligence Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2023 mandates that the director of national intelligence and the secretary of defense submit unclassified reports on UAP incidents to Congress, ensuring ongoing oversight of UAP investigations.

What’s next

AARO will continue its investigation into the recent UAP event off the Virginia coast while implementing new initiatives to enhance sensor capabilities and improve data analysis for UAP-related incidents. Further updates are expected as the investigation progresses.

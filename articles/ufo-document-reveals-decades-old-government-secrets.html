UFO Document Reveals Decades-Old Government Secrets

Documents from three decades ago have shed light on the Soviet Union's investigation of unidentified flying objects (UFOs). According to News Nation, these documents, smuggled out of Russia, detail how the Soviet Union tracked thousands of reports of strange craft, close encounters, and claimed abductions while publicly dismissing UFOs as American propaganda.

This revelation is significant as discussions around UFOs have intensified in recent years. Authorities are now examining historical data, which could impact current understandings of unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Documents date back to three decades ago.
  • Soviet Union operated multiple UFO investigation programs.
  • Thousands of reports of UFO sightings were tracked.
  • Notable mass sighting event occurred in the 1980s.
  • Ben Hansen, former FBI agent, has commented on these findings.

Timeline

Thirty years ago, documents regarding UFO investigations were smuggled out of Russia. These documents reveal the Soviet Union's extensive tracking of UFO reports while publicly dismissing the phenomenon. In the 1980s, a significant mass sighting event occurred, where a large jellyfish-shaped object was reported to have come within hundreds of feet of observers.

Official statements

“The most amazing thing is they had mass sighting events, one in particular that took place in the ’80s…there was a 450-foot-sized jellyfish-looking object that came within hundreds of feet above the people,” said Ben Hansen, a former FBI agent and host of “UFO Witness” on Discovery+.

Context

The interest in UFOs has grown over the years, with various governments revisiting past investigations. The Soviet Union's secretive approach contrasts with the current climate of openness regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue analyzing these historical documents to better understand past UFO investigations and their implications for current reports of unidentified aerial phenomena.

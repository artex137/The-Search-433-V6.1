Fetterman: Trump declassifying UFO files would be ‘incredible,’ ‘bipartisan’

Senator John Fetterman (D-Pa.) has praised President Trump’s announcement on Thursday that he will direct his administration to release all classified files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Fetterman described the move as “incredible” and a demonstration of bipartisanship during an appearance on Fox News.

This announcement is significant as it reflects growing public and governmental interest in unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs), previously known as UFOs. Fetterman expressed that Americans deserve transparency concerning these matters, particularly in light of former President Obama’s recent comments on the subject.

Key facts

  • Sen. John Fetterman praised Trump’s UFO file declassification on Fox News.
  • Trump announced plans to release documents about UAPs and alien life.
  • Fetterman supports the idea, referencing his interest in "The X Files."
  • Trump accused Obama of releasing classified information and referenced the Epstein Files Transparency Act.
  • The House Intelligence Subcommittee held its first UFO hearing in over 50 years in 2022.

Timeline

On Thursday, President Trump announced his intention to direct the Defense Department and other agencies to release classified documents concerning UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This declaration follows a series of congressional hearings on UFOs, including one in 2022 where former military intelligence officer David Grusch testified about a shadow program related to nonhuman technology.

Fetterman’s comments came shortly after Trump’s announcement, highlighting the potential for bipartisan support on the issue of UFO transparency.

Official statements

“Now if President Trump is going to release everything, I think that’s fantastic because I think America and the world would deserve this,” said Fetterman.
Trump stated, “I never talk about it. A lot of people do,” in response to questions about alien existence.
Rep. Thomas Massie (R-Ky.) criticized Trump’s UFO discussion, stating, “They’ve deployed the ultimate weapon of mass distraction, but the Epstein files aren’t going away … even for aliens.”

Context

Interest in UFOs has surged in recent years, leading to congressional hearings aimed at investigating the government's knowledge and handling of UAPs. In 2022, the House Intelligence Counterterrorism, Counterintelligence and Counterproliferation Subcommittee held its first hearing on UFOs in over five decades. The hearings continued into 2023, with testimony from whistleblowers claiming the government has engaged in reverse engineering of technology from unidentified sources.

The Pentagon has denied claims of a shadow program related to nonhuman technology, and conclusive evidence of extraterrestrial life remains undisclosed.

What’s next

As the situation develops, the public will be waiting for the release of the classified documents. The implications of these findings could influence ongoing discussions about transparency and accountability concerning UAPs and other related matters.

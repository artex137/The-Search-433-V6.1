NASA Prepares to Unveil Rare Views of Comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA is set to release new images and videos of comet 3I/ATLAS during a live broadcast from the Goddard Space Flight Center in Maryland. The event is scheduled for October 2023, with no injuries or fatalities reported.

The unveiling of these visuals is significant as comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third known comet to enter our solar system from interstellar space. NASA has confirmed that the comet poses no danger to Earth, maintaining a distance of 170 million miles from the planet during its closest approach.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 2023
  • Location: Goddard Space Flight Center, Maryland
  • Closest approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Closest approach to Mars: 19 million miles
  • Participants: Amit Kshatriya, Nicky Fox, Shawn Domagal Goldman, Tom Statler
  • Streaming platforms: NASA+, NASA app, agency website, YouTube, Amazon Prime

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected by the ATLAS observatory on July 1, 2023. It has traveled from deep interstellar space into our solar system, becoming a subject of scientific interest.

In early October 2023, the comet made a close pass to Mars, coming within approximately 19 million miles of the planet.

The live event from NASA is anticipated to provide insights into the behavior of interstellar comets through various observational perspectives.

Official statements

“Since these missions look at the comet from different angles, they can give scientists a fuller picture of its movement and activity,” says NASA.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable for being one of the few comets originating from outside our solar system. Its study offers a rare opportunity for scientists to understand interstellar objects and their characteristics.

The event highlights NASA’s ongoing commitment to public engagement and education in space science.

What’s next

Following the live broadcast, scientists will analyze the data collected from various missions to deepen understanding of comet 3I/ATLAS and its implications for the study of interstellar bodies.

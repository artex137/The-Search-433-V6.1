New NASA Photos Show 3I/ATLAS Passing Inner Planets as Experts Reject ‘Alien Craft’ Speculation

NASA has released new images of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which has sparked scientific interest as it approaches the inner planets. The photographs, taken by multiple spacecraft, were published in early October 2023.

This release is significant as it counters recent online speculation regarding the object's origins. NASA has confirmed that 3I/ATLAS, the third confirmed interstellar object ever observed, exhibits characteristics typical of active comets. The agency has emphasized its commitment to scientific integrity in the face of misinformation.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation date: Early October 2023
  • Spacecraft involved: Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, Lucy, Psyche
  • Classification: Third confirmed interstellar object
  • Characteristics: Diffuse halo, trailing dust and gas emissions

Timeline

NASA's observations of 3I/ATLAS have occurred over several months, culminating in the release of new photographs in early October 2023. These images illustrate typical cometary features as the object approaches the Sun.

The agency has continuously monitored 3I/ATLAS since its discovery, providing updates to the scientific community and the public.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS aligns fully with known cometary behaviour,” a NASA spokesperson stated, addressing the speculation surrounding the object's origins.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable as only the third interstellar object confirmed by astronomers. Its appearance follows previous observations of similar objects, which have provided valuable insights into the composition and behaviour of comets originating from outside our solar system.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it progresses through the inner solar system, providing updates and further analysis of the object’s characteristics and behaviour.

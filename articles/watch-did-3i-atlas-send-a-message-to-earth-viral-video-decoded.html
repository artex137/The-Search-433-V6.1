WATCH: Did 3I/ATLAS Send A Message To Earth? Viral Video Decoded

A viral video has sparked claims that Earth has received a message from the 3I/ATLAS comet, with some suggesting that a response was sent back. The post, widely shared on social media, alleges that the comet transmitted an organized signal that did not match any natural source. There have been no injuries or fatalities reported in relation to this claim.

The story has gained traction due to its sensational nature and the involvement of high-profile figures like Elon Musk, who warned that humanity might not be ready for a potential response from 3I/ATLAS. Authorities and scientists have since stepped in to debunk the claims, emphasizing that there is no credible evidence supporting the idea of intelligent communication from the comet.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS comet discovered on 1 July 2025 by the Asteroid Terrestrial Impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) in Chile.
  • 3I/ATLAS is classified as an interstellar comet, the third of its kind after ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • Infrared spectroscopy from the James Webb Space Telescope revealed a coma dominated by carbon dioxide, water, and dust.
  • On 24 October 2025, the South African radio telescope MeerKAT detected radio absorption lines corresponding to hydroxyl radicals.
  • Scientists have stated that the signals detected are natural, not artificial.

Timeline

The claims regarding 3I/ATLAS began circulating following a tweet that suggested the comet emitted a signal interpreted as intelligent communication. The tweet claimed that ground-based instruments automatically replied to the comet.

On 24 October 2025, MeerKAT detected radio absorption lines, which scientists later confirmed were related to natural emissions from the comet. This finding contradicted the claims of an alien signal.

Official statements

“There is no evidence of a technological signal from 3I/ATLAS,” said the director of the South African Radio Astronomy Observatory, emphasizing that the detected signals are natural features.

Context

3I/ATLAS has been observed intensively since its discovery, with multiple telescopes, including the Hubble Space Telescope and the Neil Gehrels Swift Observatory, monitoring its activity. Despite the viral speculation, scientific analysis has consistently shown that the comet behaves like a typical natural comet, with no signs of intelligent life.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS and further analyze its emissions. There are no ongoing investigations or charges related to the claims of alien communication, as the scientific community remains focused on understanding the comet’s natural phenomena.

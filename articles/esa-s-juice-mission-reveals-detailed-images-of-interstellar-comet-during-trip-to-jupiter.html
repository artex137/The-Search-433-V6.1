ESA’s JUICE mission reveals detailed images of interstellar comet during trip to Jupiter

The European Space Agency's (ESA) JUICE mission has captured detailed images of the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS during its journey to Jupiter. The mission, launched in April 2023, is currently en route to the gas giant, with the comet sighting occurring in early March 2026.

This discovery is significant as it enhances our understanding of interstellar objects and their characteristics. ESA officials have stated that the data collected will aid in studying the formation of the solar system and the nature of comets originating from outside it.

Key facts

  • Mission: ESA's JUICE (Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer)
  • Comet observed: 3I ATLAS
  • Launch date: April 2023
  • Observation date: Early March 2026
  • Location: En route to Jupiter

Timeline

In April 2023, ESA launched the JUICE mission to study Jupiter and its moons. The spacecraft has been traveling through space for nearly three years. In early March 2026, it successfully captured images of the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS, marking a milestone in the mission.

The JUICE mission aims to arrive at Jupiter in 2031, where it will conduct detailed studies of the planet and its icy moons.

Official statements

“The observation of 3I ATLAS is a groundbreaking moment for the JUICE mission and for our understanding of interstellar comets,” said ESA mission director. “The data we gather will provide insights into the early solar system.”

Context

The JUICE mission follows ESA's successful Rosetta mission, which studied a different comet, 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko, in 2014. Both missions aim to deepen our knowledge of cometary bodies and their role in the solar system's evolution.

What’s next

The JUICE mission will continue its journey to Jupiter, with upcoming observations and analyses planned as it approaches its destination in 2031.

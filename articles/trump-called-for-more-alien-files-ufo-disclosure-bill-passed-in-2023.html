Trump calls for more alien files; UFO disclosure bill passed in 2023

Former President Donald Trump has pledged to release government records on aliens and UFOs if he is reelected. Trump made this promise on February 19, 2026, following his criticism of former President Barack Obama for discussing his belief in the existence of extraterrestrial life.

This commitment comes in the wake of a 2023 law requiring the National Archives and Records Administration to collect and release documents related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). Trump's announcement has reignited public interest in UFOs and the government’s handling of information regarding extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Trump's post on Truth Social was made on February 19, 2026.
  • The 2023 law was signed by President Joe Biden.
  • The National Archives must establish a collection of government documents about UAPs.
  • Trump's directive aims to release records on "alien and extraterrestrial life, UAPs, and UFOs."
  • Congress has held multiple hearings on UFOs in recent years.
  • The National Archives began releasing some records related to UFOs in late April 2025.

Timeline

On February 19, 2026, Donald Trump criticized Barack Obama’s comments on aliens and pledged to release government documents related to UFOs if elected. This followed a series of congressional hearings on UAPs, with witnesses testifying about their experiences and knowledge of potential government cover-ups.

In 2023, President Joe Biden signed legislation that mandated the National Archives to collect and disclose information about UAPs. The law reflects an ongoing effort by Congress to enhance transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

On September 9, 2025, multiple witnesses testified under oath before Congress about UAPs, further fueling public interest and calls for greater government transparency.

Official statements

“He’s not supposed to be doing that,” Trump said regarding Obama’s comments on aliens.
White House spokesperson Anna Kelly stated, “Trump is more transparent than Biden, and ‘stay tuned!’”

Context

The interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life has grown significantly over the past few years, with Congress convening multiple times to discuss UAPs. The 2023 legislation aimed to create a framework for transparency, although it allows government agencies to maintain certain records as classified under specific circumstances.

Trump’s call for more disclosures follows a series of congressional hearings, which have highlighted the government's alleged reluctance to share information on UAPs and the potential existence of non-human intelligence.

What’s next

Investigations into UAPs are ongoing, with legislation being considered to compel the federal government to release further information. The National Archives will continue to process and disclose records as required by the 2023 law.

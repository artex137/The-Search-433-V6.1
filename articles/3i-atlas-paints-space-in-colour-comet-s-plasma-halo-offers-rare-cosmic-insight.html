3I/ATLAS Paints Space in Colour: Comet's Plasma Halo Offers Rare Cosmic Insight

A remarkable celestial event involving the comet 3I/ATLAS has provided astronomers with an unprecedented view of the universe, revealing a vibrant plasma halo. This event occurred on 9 December, when a photograph was taken using the Canary One 0.5-metre telescope, showcasing the comet's charged particles and their interaction with solar light.

The discovery is significant as it enhances understanding of the mechanics of space and the dynamic processes within our solar system. Avi Loeb, who authored a piece titled "Charged Particles Map of 3I/ATLAS," noted that the new image offers a nearly complete depiction of the comet's charged particles, marking a notable advancement in astronomical observation.

Key facts

  • Event date: 9 December
  • Telescope used: Canary One 0.5-metre
  • Comet speed: 60–70 km/s
  • Photograph revealed distinct colour zones: blue-violet, yellow-orange, and green
  • Avi Loeb is the author of the analysis on the comet.

Timeline

On 9 December, astronomers captured an image of comet 3I/ATLAS using the Canary One 0.5-metre telescope. The photograph displayed a vibrant plasma halo and distinct colour zones, leading to further analysis by Avi Loeb.

Loeb highlighted the significance of the image, stating it provided a clear view of the charged particles surrounding the comet. He also discussed the implications of the colour variations resulting from the interactions between solar light and the comet's gaseous envelope.

Official statements

“I have never seen clearly separated blue or orange-red regions around 3I/ATLAS until today,” said Avi Loeb. “What we see here is incredible.”

Context

This observation contributes to ongoing research into the interactions between solar winds and comets. Previous studies have primarily revealed yellow-green shades within the inner comas of comets, making this discovery particularly notable for its clarity and colour diversity.

What’s next

Avi Loeb plans to continue his research into the implications of the charge-exchange interactions observed in 3I/ATLAS, potentially leading to further insights into the behaviour of interstellar objects and their interactions with solar phenomena.

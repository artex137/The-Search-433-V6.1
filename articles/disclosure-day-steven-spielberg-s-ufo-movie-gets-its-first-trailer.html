‘Disclosure Day’: Steven Spielberg’s UFO movie gets its first trailer

Universal Pictures has released the first trailer for Steven Spielberg’s upcoming UFO movie, “Disclosure Day,” which will hit theatres on June 12. The film features Emily Blunt as a meteorologist who encounters a mysterious extraterrestrial force during a live weather report.

This release marks Spielberg's return to the science fiction genre, a field that has defined much of his career. The film aims to explore themes of alien existence and societal impact, drawing interest from audiences and industry experts alike.

Key facts

  • Film title: Disclosure Day
  • Director: Steven Spielberg
  • Release date: June 12
  • Lead cast: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo, Wyatt Russell, Henry Lloyd-Hughes
  • Screenwriter: David Koepp
  • Production: Spielberg and Kristie Macosko Krieger
  • Genre: Science fiction
  • Notable works by Spielberg: E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial, Close Encounters of the Third Kind, Minority Report

Timeline

Universal Pictures has unveiled the trailer for “Disclosure Day,” which showcases a world on the brink of change due to the revelation of extraterrestrial life. The trailer features Emily Blunt's character caught in a critical moment, emphasizing the film's tension and themes of truth and fear.

Spielberg has a long history with science fiction, starting with his first feature film, “Firelight,” in 1964. This upcoming project is his 37th feature film, continuing his legacy in the genre.

Official statements

“If you found out we weren’t alone... would that frighten you?”

Context

“Disclosure Day” marks a significant addition to Spielberg's filmography, which has grossed over $10 billion worldwide. The film's release is highly anticipated, given Spielberg's previous successes in the science fiction domain.

With a formidable cast and a talented production team, expectations are high for the film to resonate with both audiences and critics.

What’s next

As the release date approaches, promotional activities are expected to ramp up, including further trailers and marketing efforts to engage potential viewers.

'The aliens are crypto bros!' Mockery on Capitol Hill over Trump's UFO disclosure push

WASHINGTON — Congress has reacted with skepticism and curiosity as President Donald Trump has promised to release classified UFO files, while the Pentagon has missed its April 14 deadline to respond to lawmakers' demands for disclosure. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to these events.

This matter is significant as it touches on national security and public interest regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. Lawmakers have expressed frustration over the lack of transparency from the Pentagon, which has yet to provide the requested information.

Key facts

  • Date: April 14, 2023
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Key figure: President Donald Trump
  • Agency: Pentagon
  • Response status: Pentagon missed deadline

Timeline

On April 14, 2023, the Pentagon was expected to respond to Congress regarding classified UFO files. However, the deadline came and went without a response, prompting a wave of reactions from lawmakers.

Following the missed deadline, President Trump announced plans to release these files, which sparked both skepticism and curiosity among members of Congress.

Official statements

“The aliens are crypto bros!”

Lawmakers have expressed varying degrees of optimism and disbelief regarding the potential release of the UFO files. Some have called for greater accountability and transparency from the Pentagon.

Context

This event follows a growing trend of interest in UFO sightings and government disclosures about unidentified aerial phenomena. Previous incidents have led to increased scrutiny of military protocols regarding such sightings.

What’s next

Lawmakers are expected to continue pressing the Pentagon for answers and accountability regarding UFO disclosures in the coming weeks.

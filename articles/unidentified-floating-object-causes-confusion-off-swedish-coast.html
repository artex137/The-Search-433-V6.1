‘Unidentified Floating Object’ Causes Confusion off Swedish Coast

A giant piece of metal from an offshore wind turbine has been identified as an “unidentified floating object” (UFO) by the Swedish Coast Guard near the country's border with Norway. The object, large enough to obstruct shipping operations, was reported on August 24, 2023, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident follows a similar sighting just a week earlier, raising concerns about the safety of maritime navigation in the area. Authorities are currently investigating how the wind turbine blade ended up in the ocean, as it poses a potential hazard to seafaring activities.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: August 24, 2023
  • Location: Near the border of Sweden and Norway
  • Object size: Approximately 15 meters by 5 meters (50 feet by 16 feet)
  • Agency involved: Swedish Coast Guard
  • Previous UFO sighting: August 17, 2023 (hot water heater)
  • Status: Investigation ongoing

Timeline

On August 17, 2023, the Swedish Coast Guard responded to a report of a UFO, which turned out to be a hot water heater. A week later, on August 24, the Coast Guard received another tip about a large object in the water. Patrol vessel KBV 310 was dispatched to investigate, leading to the discovery of the wind turbine blade.

The Coast Guard reported that the object was large enough to pose a hazard to shipping and subsequently dragged it from the water for further examination. Investigators are now working to determine the origin of the wind turbine blade.

Official statements

“The size of the object was unclear but large enough to pose a hazard to seafaring,” the Coast Guard reported in a Facebook post.

Context

Sweden has a strong commitment to renewable energy, with more than half of its energy coming from sustainable sources. However, the government recently canceled a plan to build 13 wind farms amid geopolitical tensions with Russia. This has led to a re-evaluation of the country’s relationship with wind energy.

The recent discoveries of floating objects may serve as reminders of these ongoing challenges in the renewable energy sector.

What’s next

The Swedish Coast Guard will continue its investigation to determine how the wind turbine blade ended up in the ocean. Further assessments regarding the safety of maritime navigation in the area will also be conducted.

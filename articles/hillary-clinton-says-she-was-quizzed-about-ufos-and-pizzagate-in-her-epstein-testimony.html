Hillary Clinton says she was 'quizzed about UFOs and Pizzagate' in her Epstein testimony

Hillary Clinton testified for seven hours on February 27, 2026, before the House Oversight Committee in Chappaqua, New York, regarding her knowledge of Jeffrey Epstein's activities. Clinton stated she has no recollection of ever meeting Epstein and did not provide any information about him. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to this testimony.

This testimony is significant as it reflects ongoing investigations into Epstein's network and the allegations surrounding him. The House Oversight Committee is looking into various high-profile individuals associated with Epstein, including Clinton and her husband, former President Bill Clinton, who is set to testify later the same day. James Comer, chairman of the committee, emphasized that no accusations of wrongdoing have been made against the Clintons.

Key facts

  • Clinton testified for seven hours on February 27, 2026.
  • The hearing took place in Chappaqua, New York.
  • Clinton stated she has never met Jeffrey Epstein.
  • Former President Bill Clinton is scheduled to testify later on the same day.
  • Ghislaine Maxwell, Epstein's associate, is serving a 20-year sentence for sex trafficking.
  • Clinton faced unusual questions about UFOs and the Pizzagate conspiracy theory.

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, Hillary Clinton provided testimony before the House Oversight Committee regarding Jeffrey Epstein. During the seven-hour session, Clinton repeatedly denied any knowledge of Epstein, stating, “I do not recall ever encountering Mr. Epstein.” The hearing included questions on various topics, including UFOs and Pizzagate.

Clinton's husband, Bill Clinton, is expected to give his deposition later that day. The Clintons had initially resisted testifying, citing political motivations behind the hearings but later agreed to participate.

Official statements

“I don’t know how many times I had to say that I never knew Jeffrey Epstein, I never went to his island, I never went to his homes, I never went to his offices. So it’s on the record numerous times.”
“No one is accusing the Clintons of any wrongdoing. We just have a lot of questions.”
— James Comer, chairman of the House Oversight Committee

Context

The testimony comes in the wake of ongoing investigations into Epstein's extensive network of high-profile associates. Epstein was convicted in 2008 for soliciting sex from a 14-year-old girl and died in an apparent suicide in 2019 while awaiting trial on new charges. The Pizzagate conspiracy theory, which falsely implicated Clinton in a child sex crime ring, gained traction during the 2016 presidential election and has been widely debunked.

What’s next

Investigations into Jeffrey Epstein's network are ongoing, with additional testimonies expected. The House Oversight Committee may continue to question other individuals linked to Epstein, including Donald Trump, who has denied any wrongdoing.

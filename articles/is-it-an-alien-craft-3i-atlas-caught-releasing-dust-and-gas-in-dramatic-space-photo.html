Is it an alien craft? 3I/ATLAS caught releasing dust and gas in dramatic space photo

A newly released image of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has sparked interest among scientists after it was captured ejecting dust and gas. The image was taken on November 6, 2025, by the Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer, operated by the European Space Agency (ESA), shortly after the comet's closest approach to the Sun.

The significance of this observation lies in the ongoing debate about the true nature of 3I/ATLAS. While most scientists classify it as a natural comet, some researchers, including noted astrophysicist Avi Loeb, have raised questions about its origin due to its unusual characteristics. The discussion has garnered attention across the scientific community, with new data continuing to fuel curiosity about the object.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was photographed on November 6, 2025.
  • The image was captured at a distance of approximately 66 million kilometers from the comet.
  • The spacecraft recorded over 120 images of the comet across different wavelengths.
  • Avi Loeb has suggested the possibility of the object being artificial.
  • The comet's trajectory follows a retrograde orbit, raising questions about its natural formation.
  • 3I/ATLAS has been observed to have an "anti-tail," extending over 310,000 miles.

Timeline

On November 6, 2025, the Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer captured a striking image of comet 3I/ATLAS as it traveled through the solar system. This observation came just seven days after the comet's closest approach to the Sun. The ESA noted that the comet's nucleus was not visible in the image, but its surrounding features, including a bright halo of gas and a long dust tail, were clearly documented.

Following the image capture, scientists have analyzed the data to better understand the comet's behavior and characteristics. Research continues as new findings emerge from the observations.

Official statements

“The tiny nucleus of the comet (not visible) is surrounded by a bright halo of gas known as the coma. A long tail stretches away from the comet, and we see hints of rays, jets, streams, and filaments,”

ESA stated regarding the features observed in the image of 3I/ATLAS.

Context

3I/ATLAS is classified as an interstellar object that passed through our solar system after its discovery in 2025. The comet has been a subject of interest due to its peculiar trajectory and chemical characteristics. While some scientists, including Avi Loeb, have speculated about its potential artificial origins, the majority of researchers maintain that it aligns with known cometary behavior.

Early observations indicated an absence of typical comet activity, prompting further investigation into the object's features.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the data and images collected from the Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer to gain a deeper understanding of 3I/ATLAS. Ongoing research aims to clarify the comet's nature and behavior as it travels through the solar system.

Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena at The Drawing Center in NYC

The Drawing Center in New York City has launched an exhibition titled "Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena," which runs from October 17, 2025, to February 1, 2026. The exhibition explores the cultural and psychological implications of UFOs and paranormal phenomena through contemporary and historical artworks. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to the event.

This exhibition is significant as it responds to growing public interest in extraterrestrial activity, fueled by declassified government reports and increased media coverage. It aims to engage viewers in discussions about humanity's understanding of the universe and the role of these phenomena in shaping beliefs.

Key facts

  • Exhibition Duration: October 17, 2025, to February 1, 2026
  • Location: The Drawing Center, 35 Wooster Street, New York City
  • Featured Artists: Includes Shusaku Arakawa, Roy Ascott, René Magritte, and others
  • Curators: Organized by Olivia Shao, Burger Collection and TOY Meets Art Curator

Timeline

The exhibition opened on October 17, 2025, as part of a series focusing on the intersections of art and the unknown. Visitors can view a variety of artistic responses that engage with themes of belief and the unexplainable.

Throughout its run, the exhibition will showcase works that interpret religious iconography, psychological themes, and technological advancements related to UFOs and paranormal phenomena.

Official statements

“Voice of Space invites viewers to consider the ways in which these themes both live in our collective memory and influence our individual imaginations,” said Olivia Shao, the exhibition's curator.

Context

This exhibition is part of a broader cultural trend where UFOs and paranormal activities have gained traction in public discourse. The rise of declassified government documents and media attention has led to a renewed interest in these topics, prompting discussions about their implications for society.

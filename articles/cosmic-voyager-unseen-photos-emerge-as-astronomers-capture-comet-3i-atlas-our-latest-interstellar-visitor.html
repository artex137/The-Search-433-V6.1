Cosmic Voyager: Unseen Photos Emerge As Astronomers Capture Comet 3I-ATLAS, Our Latest Interstellar Visitor

Astronomers have captured rare photos of Comet 3I/ATLAS, an interstellar visitor passing through our solar system, from Sabadell Observatory in Spain. This historic sighting offers new insights into cosmic mysteries and the origins of celestial objects beyond our galaxy.

This discovery is significant as 3I/ATLAS becomes the third known interstellar object to visit our solar system, following ‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. The comet, which made its closest approach to the Sun on October 30, 2025, will safely pass near Earth in December 2025, at a distance of about 1.8 astronomical units (AU), or approximately 170 million miles.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was photographed at Sabadell Observatory, Catalonia, Spain.
  • Closest approach to the Sun occurred on October 30, 2025.
  • Expected to pass near Earth in December 2025 at a distance of 1.8 AU (170 million miles).
  • Comet features a dense nucleus over 5 km wide and dual anti-tail jets.
  • Astronomers recommend using an 8-inch or larger telescope to view the comet after November 11, 2025.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been tracked as it approached the Sun, culminating in its closest encounter on October 30, 2025. Following this, it will safely pass near Earth in December 2025, allowing for further observations.

Recent images captured by Spanish astronomer Joan Miquel González Navarra reveal the comet's structure and composition, intriguing scientists about its origins and journey through space.

Official statements

“It is still amazing to see,” said Spanish astronomer Joan Miquel González Navarra, describing the comet's appearance despite some blurriness due to its low altitude.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS highlights the ongoing interest in interstellar objects, which provide valuable insights into the evolution of celestial bodies and the cosmos. Prior to this, only two other interstellar objects have been confirmed to have passed through our solar system, marking 3I/ATLAS as a significant addition to the list.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to study Comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system. Observations will focus on analyzing images and data points to enhance our understanding of interstellar phenomena.

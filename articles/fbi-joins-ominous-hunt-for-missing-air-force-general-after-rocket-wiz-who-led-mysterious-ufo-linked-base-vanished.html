FBI joins ominous hunt for missing Air Force general after rocket wiz who led mysterious UFO-linked base vanished

Published 2026-03-03 20:40 UTC

FBI joins search for missing Air Force general

The FBI has joined the search for retired Major General William Neil McCasland, 68, who was last seen on Friday in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Authorities have issued a Silver Alert for McCasland, who has known medical issues, raising concerns for his safety.

The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office (BCSO) is leading the investigation and has called for public assistance. The FBI stated it is providing support as part of standard practice in missing person cases. Officials have confirmed that no signs of criminal activity have been detected related to McCasland's disappearance.

Key facts

  • Last seen: Friday, location not disclosed.
  • Age: 68 years old.
  • Current status: Missing person case, no signs of criminal activity.
  • Involved agencies: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, FBI Albuquerque Field Office, New Mexico Search and Rescue.
  • Search methods: K9s, drones, helicopters.

Timeline

On Friday, William Neil McCasland was reported missing in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert the same day, citing concerns for his safety due to medical issues. As the search has continued into its fourth day, the FBI has confirmed its involvement, assisting local authorities with resources.

Search efforts have included the deployment of K9 units, drones, and helicopters. Officials have stated that there are currently no indications of foul play.

Official statements

“The FBI Albuquerque Field Office is involved in this investigation, as it is standard practice for the FBI to assist our local law enforcement partners if we have a tool, tactic, or technique that could benefit their investigations,” a spokesperson said.
“His disappearance is only being investigated as a missing person/silver alert at this time,” a BCSO spokesperson added.

Context

William Neil McCasland is a retired Air Force general known for his roles in significant national defense technology and space initiatives. He commanded the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, which has historical ties to UFO investigations, particularly through Project Blue Book, which examined over 12,000 UFO sightings from the 1950s to the 1960s.

McCasland's name has also appeared in discussions related to UFO projects, suggesting his involvement in research that has attracted public interest.

What’s next

The search for McCasland will continue, with authorities urging residents to remain vigilant and report any sightings. Investigators will utilize all available resources to locate him safely.

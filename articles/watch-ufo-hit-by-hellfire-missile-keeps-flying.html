WATCH: UFO Hit By Hellfire Missile, Keeps Flying

Published 2025-09-09 19:27 UTC

UFO Hit By Hellfire Missile Off Yemen Coast

During a House Oversight Committee hearing on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs) on September 9, 2024, Representative Eric Burlison (R-MO) presented drone footage showing a flying object, described as "an orb," being struck by a projectile from an off-screen drone. The object appeared damaged but quickly resumed its original flight path. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident raises questions about UAPs and their potential implications for national security. Authorities are now considering the need for further investigation and transparency regarding such sightings. Burlison and journalist George Knapp, who has researched UAPs, both called for Congress to review similar evidence.

Key facts

  • Date of hearing: September 9, 2024
  • Location: House Oversight Committee hearing
  • Witness: George Knapp, UAP researcher
  • Object description: "an orb" off the coast of Yemen
  • Type of missile: Hellfire missile
  • Outcome: Object returned to original course after being hit

Timeline

On October 2024, drone footage was captured showing an unidentified object off the coast of Yemen. The footage was later presented during the House Oversight Committee hearing on UAPs on September 9, 2024. During the hearing, Burlison and Knapp discussed the implications of the footage and the need for further examination of UAP evidence.

Official statements

“What the hell is that?”

- George Knapp, UAP researcher, commenting on the footage presented at the hearing.

Context

This incident is part of a growing trend of UAP sightings and their examination by governmental bodies. The recent hearings and discussions reflect an increasing interest in understanding these phenomena and their potential impact on security and policy.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to conduct further investigations into the footage and other UAP sightings, with calls from Congress for more transparency and accountability regarding such incidents.

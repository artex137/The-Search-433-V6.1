Trump Faces Pentagon Revolt As 'Demon' Fears Stall UFO Declassification Bombshell

Published 2026-02-25 14:45 UTC

Trump Faces Pentagon Revolt Over UFO Disclosure

President Donald Trump's efforts to disclose information on UFOs have encountered resistance from within the Pentagon, according to multiple reports. The conflict arises as officials debate what information should be made public amid concerns over national security and cultural sensitivities.

This situation has significant implications for the planned UFO disclosure set for July. The outcome may influence public perception and the administration's transparency efforts regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • President Trump has directed officials to prepare UFO-related files for potential release.
  • Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed the Pentagon is 'working' on the directive.
  • Concerns include public panic and religious upheaval linked to the disclosure.
  • Filmmaker Mark Christopher Lee has indicated possible internal pushback within the administration.
  • A speech is planned for 8 July 2026, marking the 79th anniversary of Roswell.
  • A press conference is scheduled for 1 May 2026, which may increase pressure on the administration.

Timeline

President Trump has ordered the declassification of UFO files, initiating internal discussions within the Pentagon. Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth has confirmed that the Pentagon is complying with the President's directive. However, sources indicate that some officials are hesitant to fully disclose information due to fears of public and religious reactions.

The controversy escalated when filmmaker Mark Christopher Lee claimed that a Washington source warned of significant pushback within the administration regarding the nature of UFO phenomena. As internal discussions continue, the planned speech on 8 July 2026 remains on the calendar, though the content may be limited to conventional sightings.

Official statements

“The Pentagon is working on the directive and promises full compliance with the President's instructions,” said Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth.

Context

The internal resistance reflects broader concerns about how the UFO phenomenon intersects with cultural and religious beliefs, particularly within Evangelical communities. Previous discussions have suggested that some officials believe the phenomena could involve not just extraterrestrial life but also interdimensional entities, which raises additional sensitivities.

What’s next

The Pentagon continues to review materials as directed by President Trump. However, the internal pushback indicates that the disclosure process may be more complex than previously anticipated. The upcoming press conference on 1 May 2026 may further influence the administration's approach to the impending UFO disclosure.

UFO sightings linked with nuclear weapons testing, US study claims

A team of scientists has found a statistically significant correlation between historical unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) reports and nuclear weapons tests, according to a study published in the journal Scientific Reports. The researchers conducted their analysis using historical photographs from the Palomar Observatory in California, focusing on images taken between 1949 and 1957.

This study is significant as it provides empirical support for the validity of UAP sightings, which have been historically connected to nuclear activities. The researchers aim to contribute data beyond anecdotal evidence and popular theories regarding alien observation of nuclear capabilities.

Key facts

  • Study conducted by the Vanishing and Appearing Sources during a Century of Observations (VASCO) project.
  • Images analyzed spanned 2,718 days between 1949 and 1957.
  • Transients were found to be 45% more likely to be observed within one day of a nuclear test.
  • Transient activity increased by 8.5% for every reported UAP sighting.
  • Study has undergone peer review and is published in Scientific Reports.

Timeline

Between 1949 and 1957, researchers analyzed historical images from the Palomar Observatory. They identified transient objects, which are star-like appearances that vanish in subsequent images. The study compared these sightings to the dates of above-ground nuclear tests and UAP reports.

The findings indicate that transients are more frequently observed in conjunction with nuclear test dates, suggesting a potential link between UAP sightings and nuclear activity.

Official statements

“Our findings provide additional empirical support for the validity of the UAP phenomenon and its potential connection to nuclear weapons activity, contributing data beyond eyewitness reports,” the researchers stated in their paper.

Context

Historically, anecdotal reports have connected UFO sightings to nuclear weapons testing, with claims that extraterrestrial observers monitor humanity's advancements in weaponry. This new study is the first to document a statistical link between UAP reports and nuclear tests, adding to the discourse surrounding the phenomenon.

What’s next

Researchers plan to further investigate the origins of the transients and their connection to UAP sightings, though no specific timelines or next steps have been disclosed.

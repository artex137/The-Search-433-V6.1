Alien conspiracy documentary that 'could change the world' now streaming on Amazon Prime

A new documentary titled The Age of Disclosure has premiered on Amazon Prime Video, exploring claims of extraterrestrial life and a global cover-up involving non-human intelligent beings. The two-hour film, directed by Dan Farah, features testimonies from 34 high-ranking officials from the US government, military, and intelligence community. It has sparked discussions about the existence of aliens and the implications for humanity.

This documentary is significant as it delves into an alleged 80-year cover-up of information related to unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), formerly known as UFOs. Authorities and critics have expressed mixed reactions about its claims and potential impact on public perception regarding extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Title: The Age of Disclosure
  • Director: Dan Farah
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Number of interviewees: 34 high-ranking US officials
  • Release platform: Amazon Prime Video

Timeline

The documentary focuses on claims that span nearly a century, highlighting a secret war among nations to reverse-engineer technology from intelligent alien life. It aims to confront the question of whether aliens exist and to challenge the stigma surrounding discussions of extraterrestrial phenomena.

Notable figures, including former Pentagon official Luis Elizondo, provide insight into the ongoing inquiry into UAPs, suggesting a need for the scientific community to engage with these claims seriously.

Official statements

"I think when people watch this movie, one of the realizations will be that the stigma around this topic is completely illogical and makes no sense and is not good for humanity," Dan Farah stated in an interview with The Guardian.

Context

The Age of Disclosure joins a growing body of work that examines the phenomenon of UAPs and the public's interest in extraterrestrial life. Critics have noted that while the film presents compelling narratives, it lacks substantive evidence to support its claims. Major publications have pointed out the absence of opposing viewpoints or expert analysis within the documentary.

What’s next

The documentary is currently available for viewing on Amazon Prime Video. It has prompted ongoing discussions about the need for transparency and further investigation into UAPs among the scientific community and the public. Viewers are encouraged to form their own opinions based on the content presented in the film.

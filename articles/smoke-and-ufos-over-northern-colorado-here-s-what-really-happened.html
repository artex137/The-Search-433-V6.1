Smoke and "UFOs" Over Northern Colorado? Here’s What REALLY Happened

Strange reports have emerged from Northern Colorado over the weekend of September 20 and 21, 2023, as residents observed unusual aerial activity during the Great Colorado Air Show in Loveland, alongside smoke from a significant fire in Wyoming. No injuries have been reported in connection with either event.

The combination of a high-profile airshow and a large fire led to confusion among locals, many of whom speculated about unidentified flying objects. Authorities have since clarified the situation, stating that the smoke was related to an incident involving a ruptured natural gas pipeline. Officials have assured residents that they are not in danger.

Key facts

  • Event dates: September 20 and 21, 2023
  • Location: Loveland, Colorado
  • Incident: Fire caused by a ruptured natural gas pipeline in Wyoming
  • Fire visibility: Seen from 60 km away in Larimer County
  • Fire control: Confirmed under control by 9:00 AM on September 21
  • No injuries reported
  • Investigating agencies: Union Pacific Railroad and National Transportation Safety Board

Timeline

On September 20 and 21, the Great Colorado Air Show took place in Loveland, attracting numerous spectators and featuring elite performers. As planes flew overhead, some residents reported seeing what they believed to be UFOs, prompting discussions on social media.

On the morning of September 21, a fire erupted in Wyoming due to a ruptured natural gas pipeline near a Union Pacific Railroad train. The flames were visible from parts of Northern Colorado. By 9:00 AM, officials confirmed that the fire was under control.

Official statements

“The fire was significant enough to be seen from 60 kilometers away, but residents in Larimer County were not in any danger,” said a spokesperson from the Larimer County Sheriff’s Office.
“Union Pacific representatives confirmed that the train was approximately 40 feet from the rupture,” the spokesperson added.

Context

This incident follows a trend of heightened awareness regarding pipeline safety in the region. Previous incidents involving ruptured pipelines have raised concerns among residents about potential hazards, particularly near transportation routes.

The Great Colorado Air Show has been a popular event, drawing large crowds and generating excitement, but it has also led to misunderstandings in the past regarding aerial phenomena.

What’s next

The National Transportation Safety Board and Union Pacific Railroad will conduct investigations into the pipeline rupture and fire. Further updates will be provided as more information becomes available.

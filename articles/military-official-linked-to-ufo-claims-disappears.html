Military Official Linked to UFO Claims Disappears

William Neil McCasland, a retired general with connections to Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, has been reported missing since February 27, 2026. McCasland, 68, was last seen leaving his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico. His disappearance has raised concerns due to his ties to UFO research and classified military projects.

The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has activated the Silver Alert system to seek public assistance in locating McCasland. Authorities have found no evidence of a struggle, suggesting he may have disappeared voluntarily or been coerced. His background in top-secret projects relating to extraterrestrial technology has intensified speculation about the circumstances surrounding his vanishing.

Key facts

  • Last seen: February 27, 2026
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Age: 68
  • Agency involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office
  • Reported missing: February 28, 2026

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, McCasland was seen leaving his residence. The following day, he was reported missing by local authorities. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office initiated a search and activated the Silver Alert system to inform the public.

Witnesses in the area reported seeing strange lights near his home around the time of his disappearance. These lights reportedly hovered and then shot away at high speeds, reminiscent of UFO sightings.

Official statements

“We are asking anyone with information about Mr. McCasland’s whereabouts to come forward,” said a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.
“I deny any involvement in secret activities,” said Susan Wilkerson, McCasland’s wife. “However, his knowledge of sensitive matters could have put him at risk.”

Context

McCasland’s disappearance occurs against a backdrop of longstanding theories regarding UFO cover-ups by the US government. Since the Roswell incident in 1947, Wright-Patterson Air Force Base has been associated with numerous secret projects related to extraterrestrial phenomena. Historical accounts suggest the base has housed classified information regarding alien technology and encounters.

The US government's recent acknowledgments of unexplained aerial phenomena have led to renewed interest in UFO research and transparency. McCasland’s case highlights the delicate balance of national security and the public’s demand for accountability in matters related to UFOs.

What’s next

The investigation into McCasland’s disappearance is ongoing. Authorities continue to gather information and seek public assistance. The case may prompt further scrutiny of UFO-related government policies and the handling of classified information.

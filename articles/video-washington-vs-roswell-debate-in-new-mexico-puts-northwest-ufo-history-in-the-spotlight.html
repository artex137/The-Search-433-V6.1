VIDEO: Washington vs. Roswell debate in New Mexico puts Northwest UFO history in the spotlight

A debate titled “Washington versus Roswell” took place on October 10 at Truth or Consequences Brewing Company in New Mexico, showcasing Pacific Northwest UFO stories to a national audience. The event featured discussions on the 1947 Maury Island Incident and Mount Rainier sightings from Washington state, as well as the famed Roswell crash narrative. No injuries or fatalities were reported.

This debate matters as it highlights the historical significance of UFO incidents and their cultural impact. Authorities and local organizations are now considering making this event an annual tradition to further explore and celebrate UFO history.

Key facts

  • Date: October 10, 2023
  • Location: Truth or Consequences Brewing Company, Truth or Consequences, New Mexico
  • Host: Dr. Tim Mounce, host of the “All Things Unexplained” podcast
  • Washington representatives: Steve Edmiston, Jason Mattson, Vince Ynzunza
  • Roswell representatives: Toby Martinez, Paul Semones
  • Event format: Debate, audience participation, giveaways

Timeline

The debate began with an introduction by Dr. Tim Mounce, who presented teams from Washington and Roswell. Steve Edmiston spoke about the Maury Island Incident and the significance of the 1947 Mount Rainier sighting. He claimed that this event marked the beginning of the "flying saucer" era.

Jason Mattson and Vince Ynzunza supported Edmiston’s claims, with Ynzunza emphasizing the term “flying saucer” as a result of Kenneth Arnold’s sighting. The Roswell team, led by Toby Martinez and Paul Semones, countered by highlighting the military connections and long-standing testimonies surrounding the Roswell incident.

The evening included an audience bingo game related to UFO terminology and concluded with a humorous signing of the “Roswell Washington UFO Accords.” The event wrapped up with a question and answer session, allowing attendees to share personal UFO experiences.

Official statements

“Kenneth Arnold’s 1947 Mount Rainier sighting ignited the public’s fascination with UFOs and gave us the term ‘flying saucer,’” said Vince Ynzunza.
“For 78 years, these pictures have confused the world,” stated Paul Semones, discussing the Roswell incident.

Dr. Tim Mounce encouraged attendees to “get off our screens” and look up at the night sky.

Context

The debate reflects ongoing interest in UFO history, particularly the contrasting narratives of Washington and Roswell. Previous events have explored similar themes, contributing to a growing cultural fascination with unidentified flying objects and their implications.

What’s next

Organizers have expressed interest in making the “Washington versus Roswell” debate an annual event, aiming to further engage the public in discussions about UFOs and their historical significance.

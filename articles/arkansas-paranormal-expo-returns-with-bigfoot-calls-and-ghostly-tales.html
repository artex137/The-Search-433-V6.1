Arkansas Paranormal Expo returns with Bigfoot calls and ghostly tales

The annual Arkansas Paranormal Expo has drawn thousands to the MacArthur Military Museum in Little Rock, Arkansas, this weekend, showcasing topics such as UFOs, Bigfoot, and ghost hunting. The event began on Saturday and will continue through Sunday, with no reported injuries or fatalities.

This expo is significant as it has been a platform for paranormal enthusiasts since 2011, providing education and fostering community interest in unexplained phenomena. Organizers and participants have emphasized the importance of sharing experiences and knowledge about the unknown. According to Rhonda Burton, an event organizer, the expo helps inform the public about what could be out there.

Key facts

  • Event: Arkansas Paranormal Expo
  • Location: MacArthur Military Museum, Little Rock, Arkansas
  • Dates: October 14-15, 2023
  • Ticket price: $10; free for children under 12
  • Hours: 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. on Sunday

Timeline

The Arkansas Paranormal Expo started in 2011 as a gathering for those interested in paranormal activities. This year, it has featured speakers on various topics, including UFOs and ghost hunting, alongside a Bigfoot calling competition.

The event is also a fundraiser for the MacArthur Museum, aimed at supporting future investigations and events at the facility.

Official statements

“Having events like this kind of help educate and kind of inform the public of what potentially could be out there, because nobody knows for sure, and nobody's an expert,” said Rhonda Burton, Arkansas Paranormal Expo event organizer.
“Love getting the encounters. I love hearing meeting people, talking to them, hearing what they've had happen to them, you know, strange, weird, abnormal, and trying to get to the bottom of it,” said Matthew Orton of the Drunken Cryptids Podcast.
“We want to raise as much money as we possibly can,” said Heather Amaro, host of Expedition X.

Context

The Arkansas Paranormal Expo has evolved into a significant event for local enthusiasts, promoting interest in the paranormal and providing a space for sharing experiences. The event has continued to grow in popularity, attracting both speakers and attendees from various backgrounds.

What’s next

The expo will conclude on Sunday, October 15, 2023, with further activities scheduled throughout the day. Organizers plan to use the funds raised to support the MacArthur Military Museum and its future events.

Nasa scraps searching skies for UFOs and turns attention to ocean depths for underwater alien USOs

Nasa has shifted its focus from searching for unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in the skies to investigating unidentified submersible objects (USOs) in the oceans, according to retired US Navy Rear Admiral Timothy Gallaudet. Gallaudet, who previously led the US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), announced this change on October 25, 2023.

This decision marks a significant pivot in how authorities approach the investigation of potential extraterrestrial phenomena. Gallaudet believes that the oceans may hold more clues to the existence of underwater aliens than the skies do for flying objects.

Key facts

  • Announcement made on October 25, 2023.
  • Timothy Gallaudet is a retired US Navy Rear Admiral.
  • Gallaudet previously served as the head of NOAA.
  • Focus has shifted from UFOs to USOs.
  • Specific locations for investigations have not been disclosed.

Timeline

On October 25, 2023, Timothy Gallaudet publicly stated that Nasa should redirect its resources from aerial UFO searches to exploring the depths of the oceans for USOs. Gallaudet's comments come amid ongoing discussions about the search for extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

“The oceans are vast and unexplored, and we may find more evidence of alien life beneath the waves,” Gallaudet said.

Context

In recent years, there has been a growing interest in the topic of UFOs, particularly after the release of several military videos showing unidentified aerial phenomena. However, Gallaudet’s remarks highlight a lesser-known area of investigation that could yield new insights into potential extraterrestrial encounters.

What’s next

Nasa has not disclosed specific timelines for the investigations into USOs. Future updates on this shift in focus are anticipated as further details emerge.

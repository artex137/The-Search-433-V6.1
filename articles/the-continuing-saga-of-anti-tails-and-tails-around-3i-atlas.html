The Continuing Saga of Anti-Tails and Tails around 3I/ATLAS

As of November 15, 2025, the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has exhibited a notable anti-tail alongside its traditional tails, according to an image released by Teerasak Thaluang from a 0.26-meter telescope in Thailand. The phenomenon has prompted questions regarding its origin and implications for scientific understanding.

This observation is significant as it raises intriguing questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Researchers, including David Jewitt and Eric Keto, have suggested various explanations for the anti-tail, ranging from natural processes to technological influences. The upcoming close approach of 3I/ATLAS to Earth on December 19, 2025, is expected to provide further data for analysis.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: November 15, 2025
  • Location: Thailand
  • Telescope used: 0.26-meter telescope
  • Close approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Potential particle size: ~100 micrometers

Timeline

November 15, 2025: Teerasak Thaluang releases an image showing the anti-tail and tails of 3I/ATLAS.

Upcoming: 3I/ATLAS is set to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, prompting increased observation and analysis.

Official statements

“The physics behind the anti-tail of 3I/ATLAS is unclear,” stated David Jewitt, suggesting that the phenomenon could be linked to the release of giant dust particles.
“Future spectroscopic data will be able to calibrate the outflow speed and distinguish between natural outgassing and artificial jets,” noted an analysis by Eric Keto.

Context

The observation of anti-tails in comets is not well understood and has been a subject of scientific inquiry. Previous studies have primarily focused on smaller particles that effectively scatter sunlight, unlike the larger particles associated with 3I/ATLAS. This new finding challenges existing theories and opens the door for further investigation.

What’s next

Researchers anticipate a flood of data on 3I/ATLAS leading up to its closest approach. This data will help clarify the object’s characteristics and the nature of its anti-tail and tails.

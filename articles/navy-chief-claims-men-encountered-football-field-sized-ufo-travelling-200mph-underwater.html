Navy chief claims men encountered 'football field' sized UFO travelling 200mph underwater

U.S. Navy officials have reported an encounter with an unidentified flying object (UFO) described as the size of a football field, which was allegedly travelling at speeds of 320 kilometres per hour (200 mph) underwater. The incident occurred off the coast of California, though specific details regarding the date and time have not been disclosed.

This encounter raises significant questions about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and their implications for national security. The U.S. Navy has been increasing its focus on UAP investigations, and this report has prompted further scrutiny and analysis from military and government officials.

Key facts

  • Incident described as involving a UFO the size of a football field.
  • Object reportedly travelling at 320 km/h (200 mph) underwater.
  • Location: off the coast of California.
  • Date and time of the incident: not disclosed.
  • U.S. Navy officials involved in the report.

Timeline

The specific timeline of the incident remains unclear due to the lack of detailed information. The U.S. Navy has acknowledged the encounter but has not provided a chronological account of events leading up to or following the sighting.

Official statements

“We have encountered objects that defy explanation,” a Navy chief said, referring to the recent sightings.

Context

This incident adds to a growing list of reports regarding UAPs. The U.S. government has been increasingly transparent about its investigations into these phenomena, reflecting a shift in policy aimed at understanding potential threats and anomalies in U.S. airspace.

What’s next

The U.S. Navy has indicated that it will continue to investigate this and other similar encounters, although specific timelines for reports or outcomes have not been disclosed.

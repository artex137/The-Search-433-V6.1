3I/ATLAS Interstellar Comet Live Tracker: Peak visibility from Earth

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is set for peak visibility from Earth on October 14, 2023. This celestial event provides a unique opportunity for astronomers and skywatchers to observe the comet as it travels through the solar system.

This visibility matters because it allows both amateur and professional astronomers to study the comet's characteristics, including its tail. Authorities and astronomy enthusiasts are preparing for viewing events and providing guidance on optimal viewing conditions.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Date of Peak Visibility: October 14, 2023
  • Optimal Viewing Time: Not disclosed
  • Location: Visible from Earth
  • Type: Interstellar comet

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019 and has been on a trajectory that brings it close to Earth. The peak visibility date has been confirmed for October 14, 2023, when the comet will be at its brightest.

Before this date, astronomers have been tracking its path and providing updates on its visibility and characteristics.

Official statements

“This is a rare opportunity to observe an interstellar object passing through our solar system,” said an astronomy expert.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few interstellar comets identified since the first known object, 'Oumuamua,' passed through in 2017. Its study could provide insights into the formation of other solar systems.

What’s next

As the peak visibility date approaches, astronomy organizations will host events and provide additional information on viewing conditions and equipment needed for optimal observation.

Comet 31/ATLAS and ESA: New Observations Reveal Interstellar Visitor’s Precise Trajectory

Comet 3I/ATLAS, an interstellar object, is being closely monitored by the European Space Agency (ESA) as it travels through our Solar System. The ESA announced significant advancements in tracking the comet's trajectory during the first week of October 2023, following its discovery on July 1, 2025.

This development is crucial as it allows scientists to gather detailed information about a celestial body that will not return. ESA’s ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter has provided enhanced trajectory accuracy, enabling researchers to study the comet's characteristics and behavior more effectively.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Designation: 3I/ATLAS (third confirmed interstellar visitor)
  • Speed: Up to 155,000 mph (250,000 km/h)
  • Tracking Period: October 1-7, 2023
  • Tracking Device: ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2025. Since then, the ESA has monitored its path using Earth-based telescopes. In early October 2023, ESA redirected the ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter, typically used for Mars studies, to focus on the comet, improving the accuracy of its trajectory data.

Official statements

“Comet 31/ATLAS is on a one-way journey… swinging around the Sun once, then heading back out into deep space,”

ESA stated in their update regarding the comet's trajectory.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is significant as it represents one of the few confirmed interstellar visitors to our Solar System. This event provides a unique opportunity for scientists to study the properties and behaviors of such objects, which are typically difficult to observe due to their velocity and transient nature.

What’s next

ESA will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the Solar System, using the data collected to enhance our understanding of interstellar objects.

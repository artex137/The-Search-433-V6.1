Trump moves to declassify UFO files after Obama’s alien comments stir fresh buzz

Donald Trump has called for the review and release of classified UFO documents, reigniting discussions about extraterrestrial life. The announcement occurred on social media on February 20, 2026, shortly after former President Barack Obama suggested the possibility of alien existence during a podcast appearance.

This development matters as it taps into a longstanding public curiosity about UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Trump has instructed defense officials and federal agencies to locate and potentially disclose information related to unidentified aerial phenomena and UFO sightings. He emphasized the importance of transparency, citing significant public interest in the subject.

Key facts

  • Date: February 20, 2026
  • Who: Donald Trump, Barack Obama
  • What: Call for declassification of UFO documents
  • Where: Social media announcement by Trump
  • Context: Obama’s podcast comments on extraterrestrial life

Timeline

On February 20, 2026, Trump announced his intention to review and declassify government files related to UFOs. This call came just days after Obama discussed the possibility of extraterrestrial life on a podcast, stating that while he believes aliens could be real, he has not personally encountered any evidence.

Obama's comments sparked renewed interest and debate over UFOs, as he dismissed conspiracy theories about hidden bases, including Area 51. His remarks quickly gained traction on social media and drew responses from various political figures, including Trump.

Official statements

“The subject is complicated but important,” Trump said. “There is strong public interest, and we need to push for greater transparency.”
Obama remarked on the podcast, “Aliens are real, but I have never seen any evidence.”

Context

Discussions about UFOs and extraterrestrial life have persisted for decades, with limited official responses to numerous sightings. Recent acknowledgments from the US military regarding pilots encountering unexplained aerial objects have intensified public intrigue. The intersection of political commentary and UFO discussions often generates significant media attention.

What’s next

It remains uncertain whether the review will yield any new disclosures. Trump’s directive to federal agencies is expected to initiate the process of locating relevant documents, potentially leading to future public releases.

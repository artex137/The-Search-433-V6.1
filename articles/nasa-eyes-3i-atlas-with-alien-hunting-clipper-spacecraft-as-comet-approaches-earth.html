NASA eyes 3I/ATLAS with alien-hunting Clipper spacecraft as comet approaches Earth

NASA has announced plans to observe comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth in the coming weeks. The Clipper spacecraft, designed for alien-hunting missions, will play a key role in this observation. The comet is expected to make its closest approach to Earth on March 1, 2024.

This mission is significant as it provides scientists with a unique opportunity to study the comet's composition and behavior. NASA officials have stated that understanding the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS could yield insights into the origins of comets and the early solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest approach date: March 1, 2024
  • Spacecraft: Clipper
  • Agency: NASA

Timeline

NASA has been tracking 3I/ATLAS since its discovery in 2019. The Clipper spacecraft has been under development and is now prepared for its mission. Observations will begin as the comet approaches Earth.

“This is a rare chance to study a comet that has not been observed closely before,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Previous missions have focused on other comets, but 3I/ATLAS presents a unique opportunity due to its trajectory and composition. Understanding comets can provide valuable information about the solar system's history and evolution.

What’s next

NASA will begin its observations of 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth, with data collection expected to continue throughout its flyby.

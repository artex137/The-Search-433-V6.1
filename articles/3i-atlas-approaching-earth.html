3I/ATLAS Approaching Earth

On November 23, 2025, the interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS approached Earth, reaching a distance of 300 million kilometers, according to the Solar Astronomy Laboratory of the Russian Academy of Sciences. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This event is significant as 3I/ATLAS continues to get closer to Earth, with scientists monitoring its trajectory. The next key distance milestone is 200 million kilometers. Researchers have noted that Earth's position is aligned with the comet's path. They have indicated that 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to the planet.

Key facts

  • Date of closest approach: November 23, 2025
  • Current distance from Earth: 300 million kilometers
  • Next milestone distance: 200 million kilometers
  • Projected closest distance: 269 million kilometers on December 19, 2025
  • Agency involved: Solar Astronomy Laboratory of the Russian Academy of Sciences

Timeline

On November 23, 2025, 3I/ATLAS reached a distance of 300 million kilometers from Earth. Scientists have calculated that by December 19, the comet will come as close as 269 million kilometers. After this date, it will begin to move away from Earth.

Official statements

“After that, perhaps we should prepare banners saying, ‘Welcome, honorable visitors,’” researchers from the Solar Astronomy Laboratory joked.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects as they pass near Earth. Previous comets and asteroids have been monitored for potential threats, leading to advancements in tracking and predicting their paths.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it approaches and moves away from Earth, ensuring that it remains a non-threat during its trajectory.

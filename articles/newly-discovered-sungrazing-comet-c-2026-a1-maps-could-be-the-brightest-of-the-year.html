Newly discovered sungrazing comet C/2026 A1 (MAPS) could be the brightest of the year

A newly discovered comet, C/2026 A1 (MAPS), has the potential to be the brightest observed this year, astronomers announced on Tuesday. The comet was first identified by a team of scientists working with the Minor Planet Center and is expected to make its closest approach to the Sun in early 2026.

This discovery is significant as it provides an opportunity for both amateur and professional astronomers to observe a potentially bright comet. The comet's trajectory suggests that it will pass very close to the Sun, which could enhance its brightness. Observatories worldwide are preparing to track its path and provide updates on its visibility.

Key facts

  • Comet designation: C/2026 A1 (MAPS)
  • Discovered by: Minor Planet Center
  • Closest approach to the Sun: Early 2026
  • Potential brightness: Possibly the brightest comet of the year

Timeline

The discovery of C/2026 A1 (MAPS) occurred as part of ongoing monitoring efforts by astronomers. The Minor Planet Center has been tracking the comet's trajectory since its identification. As the comet approaches the Sun, astronomers will continue to gather data on its brightness and path.

Official statements

“This comet has the potential to become exceptionally bright, making it an exciting object for observation,” said an astronomer from the Minor Planet Center.

Context

Comet observations have become increasingly important in the study of solar system dynamics. Previous comets, such as C/2020 F3 (NEOWISE), have captured public interest due to their brightness and visibility.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to monitor C/2026 A1 (MAPS) as it approaches the Sun. Updates regarding its brightness and visibility will be provided leading up to its closest approach.

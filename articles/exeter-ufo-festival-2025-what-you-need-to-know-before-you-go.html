Exeter UFO Festival 2025: What you need to know before you go

EXETER — The Exeter UFO Festival will take place this Labor Day weekend, September 2-3, 2025, celebrating the 60th anniversary of “The Incident at Exeter.” The event is hosted by the Exeter Area Kiwanis Club and promises to engage both locals and visitors with various activities related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

The festival matters as it commemorates a significant historical event in New Hampshire, attracting UFO enthusiasts and those curious about the phenomenon. Organizers aim to create an entertaining experience that highlights the community's connection to this intriguing aspect of local lore.

Key facts

  • When: September 2-3, 2025
  • Where: Exeter, New Hampshire
  • Host: Exeter Area Kiwanis Club
  • Event type: Festival celebrating UFOs and local history

Timeline

The festival has been held annually, with this year marking the 60th anniversary of “The Incident at Exeter.” The original incident occurred in 1965, making this celebration a milestone for the community.

Throughout the weekend, attendees can expect a mix of entertainment, educational presentations, and activities aimed at both UFO enthusiasts and the general public.

Official statements

“Whether you're a seasoned UFO enthusiast or just extraterrestrial-curious, the festival promises plenty to spark the imagination,” festival organizers said.

Context

The Incident at Exeter refers to a series of UFO sightings reported in 1965, which have since become a notable part of local history. The festival has grown over the years, reflecting an ongoing interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

Attendees can look forward to a weekend filled with activities that celebrate UFO culture and the historical significance of the Exeter incident.

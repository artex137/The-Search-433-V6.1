Colorado Rep. Explains Why ‘UFOs and Pizzagate’ Came Up During Hillary Clinton’s 6-Hour Epstein Deposition

Colorado Rep. Lauren Boebert has commented on the unusual topics that arose during former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton’s deposition before the House Oversight Committee on Thursday, which lasted over six hours. Clinton has stated she did not know Jeffrey Epstein and has denied any connection to him or Ghislaine Maxwell.

This line of questioning has raised eyebrows, prompting Boebert to explain its relevance. Clinton has emphasized her lack of association with Epstein and described the inquiries about UFOs and the conspiracy theory known as Pizzagate as “quite unusual.” The House Oversight Committee is investigating Epstein's connections and activities, with implications for trafficking survivors.

Key facts

  • Hillary Clinton sat for a deposition on Thursday before the House Oversight Committee.
  • The deposition lasted over six hours.
  • Clinton stated she has never met Jeffrey Epstein and had no communication with him.
  • Boebert and Rep. Suhas Subramanyam participated in the deposition.
  • Pizzagate is a debunked conspiracy theory alleging a child trafficking ring involving Democratic figures.
  • Clinton described Pizzagate as “one of the most vile, bogus conspiracy theories.”

Timeline

On Thursday, Hillary Clinton testified before the House Oversight Committee regarding her connections to Jeffrey Epstein. During the deposition, she repeatedly denied knowing Epstein and stated she had only a casual acquaintance with Ghislaine Maxwell. Toward the end of the session, Clinton was questioned about UFOs and Pizzagate, which she later deemed unusual.

Following the deposition, Rep. Lauren Boebert appeared on NewsNation, where she discussed the relevance of the topics raised. Boebert indicated that the Epstein files contained material that led to these questions, suggesting a broader investigation into connections between Epstein and other figures.

Official statements

“There are things within the Epstein file that lead to these questions,” Boebert said. “You know, Hillary Clinton being connected so tightly with John Podesta, who’s connected with Anthony Weiner, and all of these things, they all kind of tie together, especially when you’re talking about women who are being trafficked.”
Clinton has stated that Pizzagate is “one of the most vile, bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet.”

Context

The Pizzagate conspiracy theory emerged from a series of misinterpreted emails released in 2016, which involved Clinton campaign chairman John Podesta. The theory has been widely discredited but continues to circulate online. The House Oversight Committee is investigating Epstein's activities and connections to various individuals, including prominent political figures.

What’s next

Investigations by the House Oversight Committee into Jeffrey Epstein's connections and activities are ongoing. The implications of the deposition and the questions raised may lead to further inquiries into the individuals connected to Epstein.

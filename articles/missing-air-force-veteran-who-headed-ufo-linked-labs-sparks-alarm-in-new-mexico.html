Missing Air Force veteran who headed UFO-linked labs sparks alarm in New Mexico

A retired US Air Force general, William Neil McCasland, 68, has been reported missing in Albuquerque, New Mexico, as of Friday, March 2, 2026. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office (BCSO) stated that McCasland was last seen around 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE. Authorities have expressed concern for his safety due to his medical issues.

The BCSO has issued a Silver Alert, a public notification system aimed at locating missing seniors or individuals with Alzheimer's, dementia, or other medical conditions. Local law enforcement is collaborating with Kirtland Air Force Base, where McCasland previously served. Col. Justin Secrest, commander of Kirtland's 377th Air Base Wing, stated that the base is actively involved in the search efforts.

Key facts

  • William Neil McCasland, 68, reported missing on March 2, 2026.
  • Last seen at approximately 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE, Albuquerque.
  • Silver Alert issued by Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office.
  • New Mexico Search and Rescue assisting in the investigation as of March 4, 2026.
  • Anyone with information can contact BCSO at 505-468-7070 or text 847411.
  • McCasland retired from the Air Force in 2013 and previously led advanced aerospace research programs.

Timeline

On March 2, 2026, at around 11 a.m., William Neil McCasland was last seen near Quail Run Court NE in Albuquerque. The BCSO issued a Silver Alert later that day, citing concerns for his safety due to his medical condition. By March 4, 2026, New Mexico Search and Rescue had joined the search efforts.

Official statements

“Due to his medical issues, law enforcement is concerned for his safety,” the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office said.
“Our thoughts are with his family during this difficult time,” said Col. Justin Secrest, commander of Kirtland's 377th Air Base Wing.

Context

William McCasland has a significant background in aerospace research, having led both the Phillips Research Site and the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio. His name has become associated with UFO research due to connections made by UFO enthusiasts and references in emails released by WikiLeaks. However, there is no public evidence linking him to any secret UFO programs.

What’s next

Authorities continue to search for McCasland, with local law enforcement and search teams involved in the investigation. Further updates are expected as more information becomes available.

The Area 51 of New England

LINCOLN, N.H. — A gas station in Lincoln, New Hampshire, has become a hub for UFO enthusiasts, stocking memorabilia related to the first widely-reported UFO abduction incident involving Betty and Barney Hill in 1961. The station has attracted visitors seeking to connect with the local lore surrounding the couple's alleged encounter, which occurred during their honeymoon.

Authorities and local businesses are leveraging the Hill incident to promote tourism in the area. The gas station's gift shop features a range of alien-themed merchandise, contributing to an ongoing fascination with UFOs in New England. This interest reflects a broader cultural phenomenon surrounding extraterrestrial encounters.

Key facts

  • Location: Gas station in Lincoln, New Hampshire
  • Incident: First widely-reported UFO abduction by Betty and Barney Hill in 1961
  • Date of report: October 24, 2025
  • Merchandise includes: Alien mood rings, magnets, figurines, and more
  • Visitor activities: Tourists engage with local lore and purchase memorabilia

Timeline

On October 24, 2025, a gas station in Lincoln, N.H. reported its continued popularity due to its UFO-themed merchandise. The shop features items celebrating the 1961 incident involving Betty and Barney Hill, which remains a significant part of local history.

Official statements

“The job makes for far more interesting conversation,” said Tim Virge, an employee at the gas station, referencing the eccentric encounters he has heard about during his six years of work there.

Context

The Betty and Barney Hill incident is considered a landmark case in UFO history, marking one of the first widely publicized claims of alien abduction. Over the decades, it has inspired numerous books, documentaries, and discussions about the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

The ongoing interest in this case reflects a larger trend in American culture, where UFOs and alien encounters continue to fascinate the public and influence tourism in regions associated with such phenomena.

What’s next

Local businesses plan to expand their offerings to attract more visitors interested in UFO lore. The gas station and its gift shop are expected to continue capitalizing on the Hill incident to boost tourism in Lincoln.

Meet Comet 3I/ATLAS, A Wandering Space Rock That Has Experts Talking

A comet from another star system, known as 3I/ATLAS, has captured the attention of astronomers since its discovery in 2024. This interstellar comet is currently passing through our solar system and is being closely monitored by scientists at various observatories.

This comet presents a unique opportunity for researchers to study an object that originated billions of years ago, beyond our solar system. Authorities, including the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), are actively observing 3I/ATLAS to gather data on its composition and behavior as it approaches and then moves away from the Sun.

Key facts

  • Discovered: 2024 by the ATLAS Survey
  • Type: Interstellar comet
  • Notable observation: Outgassing a unique mixture of gases
  • Next JWST observations: Scheduled for December 2023
  • Brightness: Visible with small telescopes

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2024 during routine observations by the ATLAS Survey, a network of telescopes designed to identify potential threats to Earth.

Since July 2023, the comet has been brightening as it approached the Sun. Early observations from the JWST have already revealed that it is outgassing a mixture of gases with a higher ratio of carbon dioxide to water vapor compared to typical solar system comets.

As the comet has passed its closest point to the Sun, astronomers are now observing how its outgassing changes as it moves further away.

Official statements

“This comet in particular is interesting since it's actually from outside of our solar system,” said Qicheng Zhang, a postdoctoral fellow at Lowell Observatory.
“It could mean that this object just for some reason has a lot of carbon dioxide relative to water, which could be an indication of maybe where it formed or perhaps just the composition of the star or the planetary system that it came from,” Zhang explained.
“We are a long way away from exhausting all explanation that do not involve alien technology,” Zhang added, addressing online speculation.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of only three confirmed interstellar objects detected from Earth. Its study could provide insights into the formation of other planetary systems and the conditions that may lead to the development of life.

Previous interstellar objects, such as Oumuamua, have also sparked discussions about the nature of celestial bodies from beyond our solar system, but 3I/ATLAS offers a more direct opportunity to analyze its composition and behavior.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS over the coming months, with further observations from the JWST planned for December 2023. These studies aim to deepen understanding of its unique characteristics and what they reveal about its origins.

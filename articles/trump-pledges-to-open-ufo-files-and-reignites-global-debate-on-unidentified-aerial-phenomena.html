Trump Pledges to Open UFO Files and Reignites Global Debate on Unidentified Aerial Phenomena

U.S. President Donald Trump has announced plans to request government agencies to release files related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) in a statement posted on his social media platform, Truth Social, on February 26, 2026. This announcement has sparked renewed international interest and debate regarding UFOs.

Trump's commitment to transparency comes amid criticism of former President Barack Obama, who acknowledged the existence of records on unexplained aerial objects. Trump's statements have prompted media coverage across the United States, Europe, and Latin America, highlighting the potential for new disclosures on UFO-related documents. The issue has quickly trended on social media, engaging both researchers and skeptics.

Key facts

  • Date: February 26, 2026
  • Location: United States
  • Key Figures: Donald Trump, Barack Obama
  • Organizations Involved: Dakila Pesquisas, Dakila International Ecosystem
  • Historical Context: U.S. Air Force's Project Blue Book (1952-1969)

Timeline

Between 1952 and 1969, the U.S. Air Force conducted Project Blue Book, analyzing thousands of UFO reports. Although the project concluded there was no evidence of a national security threat, some cases remained classified as unidentified.

On February 26, 2026, Trump announced on Truth Social his intent to seek the release of UFO-related documents, reigniting the conversation surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Official statements

“We need greater transparency regarding official records involving unidentified aerial phenomena,” Trump stated in his post.
“Discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena should be grounded in research, documentation, and technical analysis,” said Urandir Fernandes de Oliveira, founder of Dakila Pesquisas.

Context

The interest of the U.S. government in UFO reports has evolved, especially with the designation of unidentified aerial phenomena in recent years. Congressional hearings and Pentagon reports have brought the discussion into formal settings, distancing it from popular culture.

In Brazil, the topic has gained traction among ufologists and researchers, with Dakila Pesquisas advocating for a responsible public debate on UAPs. The organization's extensive work over four decades includes field expeditions and comparative studies aimed at understanding unusual phenomena.

What’s next

Experts note that any potential disclosure of documents will depend on legal procedures and internal reviews by relevant authorities. The timeline and specific files to be released remain undisclosed. Researchers and institutions continue to emphasize the importance of careful analysis and scientific investigation as the debate on unidentified aerial phenomena unfolds globally.

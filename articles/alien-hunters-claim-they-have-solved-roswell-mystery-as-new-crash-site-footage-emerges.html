Alien hunters claim they have 'solved' Roswell mystery as new 'crash site footage' emerges

Alien enthusiasts have claimed to discover crashed "UFO debris" and "alien bodies" in new footage related to the Roswell incident. The footage, a 22-minute video titled The Roswell Incident, appeared recently on the US National Archives website, sparking renewed interest in the long-standing mystery of the 1947 event.

This development matters as it reignites speculation about the infamous Roswell incident, which has been a focal point for UFO believers for decades. Authorities have previously attributed the debris found at the site to Project Mogul, a Cold War balloon program, but the new footage has prompted further discussion among enthusiasts and skeptics alike.

Key facts

  • New footage titled The Roswell Incident runs for 22 minutes.
  • The video is now available on the US National Archives website.
  • The Roswell incident occurred in 1947.
  • UFO researcher Mark Lee has expressed skepticism about the video's claims.
  • Official inquiries have linked the debris to Project Mogul.

Timeline

The Roswell incident occurred in July 1947, when reports of a "flying disc" crash emerged in New Mexico. Official statements at the time attributed the debris to a weather balloon from Project Mogul. In recent years, interest in the incident has persisted, culminating in the release of The Roswell Incident video on the US National Archives website.

Since its release, the footage has attracted attention from both enthusiasts and skeptics, with discussions about its content and implications continuing in online forums and among UFO researchers.

Official statements

“Just because it's been added to the National Archives doesn't give it scientific validation. If it came out as a release from the military or Congress, I would take it a lot more seriously,” said UFO researcher Mark Lee.
Lee also noted that the supposed "alien" in the footage is likely a case of pareidolia, a tendency for humans to perceive familiar patterns, such as faces, in random objects.

Context

The Roswell incident has been the subject of speculation and conspiracy theories for decades. Official inquiries have consistently maintained that the debris was related to Project Mogul, which was designed to detect Soviet nuclear tests during the Cold War. Despite this, UFO enthusiasts continue to seek evidence supporting the existence of extraterrestrial life.

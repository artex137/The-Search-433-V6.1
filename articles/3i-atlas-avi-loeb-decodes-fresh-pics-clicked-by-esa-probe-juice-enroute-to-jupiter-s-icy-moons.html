3I/ATLAS: Avi Loeb Decodes Fresh Pics Clicked By ESA Probe JUICE Enroute To Jupiter’s Icy Moons

The European Space Agency (ESA) has reported that its probe JUICE (Jupiter’s Icy Moon Explorer) captured images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS while en route to Jupiter's icy moons. The images were taken on November 2, 2025, just two days before JUICE's closest approach to the comet on November 4, at a distance of approximately 66 million kilometers.

This development highlights the ongoing exploration of interstellar objects. JUICE's observations are intended to enhance understanding of 3I/ATLAS, which has garnered significant attention since its discovery on July 1, 2025. The data collected will not arrive on Earth until February 2026, due to limitations in the probe's communication capabilities.

Key facts

  • Probe: JUICE (Jupiter’s Icy Moon Explorer)
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Image Date: November 2, 2025
  • Closest Approach: November 4, 2025, at 66 million km
  • Data Arrival: February 18 and 20, 2026
  • Instruments Used: Navigation Camera (NavCam), JANUS, MAJIS, UVS, SWI, and PEP

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, 3I/ATLAS was discovered. Following this, on November 2, 2025, JUICE captured its first images of the comet using its Navigation Camera. Two days later, on November 4, JUICE made its closest approach to 3I/ATLAS. ESA anticipates receiving data from the probe's scientific instruments in February 2026.

Official statements

“The image displays a glowing halo of gas and dust (coma) surrounding 3I/ATLAS with a hint of two tails,” said Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb regarding the ESA pictures.
Loeb added, “The fact that 3I/ATLAS will arrive at Jupiter in March 2026 whereas JUICE will only get there in July 2031 illustrates how much faster is this interstellar visitor compared to a human-made spacecraft.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is an interstellar comet that has attracted interest since its detection. The study of such objects provides valuable insights into the formation and evolution of the solar system. The capabilities of JUICE are significant as it will explore Jupiter's moons, which could potentially harbor conditions suitable for life.

What’s next

ESA will analyze the data from JUICE’s scientific instruments when it arrives in February 2026. The findings will contribute to ongoing research about 3I/ATLAS and its characteristics as it approaches Jupiter.

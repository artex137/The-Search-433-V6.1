Today in History - September 19: The purported alien abduction that began it all

On September 19, 1961, Barney and Betty Hill reported an encounter with an unidentified flying object while driving on a quiet road in New Hampshire. The couple experienced significant memory gaps after the incident, which they believed involved an alien abduction.

The Hills' account has sparked interest in UFO phenomena and has influenced popular culture since the incident. Authorities and researchers have examined their claims, leading to ongoing discussions about the nature of such encounters and the psychological factors involved.

Key facts

  • Date: September 19, 1961
  • Location: New Hampshire, USA
  • Individuals involved: Barney Hill (deceased 1969) and Betty Hill (deceased 2004)
  • Outcome: Reported alien abduction; significant memory gaps
  • Psychiatrist involved: Not disclosed

Timeline

On the night of September 19, 1961, the Hills were driving home when they noticed a strange craft hovering above them. They described humanoid figures in black uniforms approaching the vehicle, prompting them to flee.

Upon returning home, the couple felt unusual and noticed that their belongings were in disarray. They later underwent hypnosis, recalling similar details about the encounter, which included being examined by otherworldly beings.

Official statements

“The couple believed they had an intrusive extra-terrestrial encounter they could not remember.”
“Their psychiatrist was confident the pair were not lying, but believed they were both having a shared delusion or a remarkably similar hallucination.”

Context

The Hills' account has become a prominent case in the study of UFO sightings and alleged abductions. Their story helped shape the narrative around alien encounters in the media and has inspired numerous science fiction films. Skeptics have pointed out inconsistencies in Mrs. Hill's retelling of the incident over the years.

What’s next

Ongoing discussions in the scientific and UFO research communities continue to explore the psychological aspects of abduction claims and the impact of media representation on public perception.

Catch Comet 3I/ATLAS’s Interstellar Journey Live Tonight

Comet 3I/ATLAS will showcase its interstellar journey tonight, January 22, 2026, during a live event broadcast from Manciano, Italy. The comet will reach opposition, positioned directly opposite the sun in Earth’s sky, as it speeds away from the solar system. There are no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this event.

This live event is significant as it marks a rare opportunity to observe an interstellar comet. The Virtual Telescope Project will provide live views, although visibility may be impacted by weather conditions. A previous livestream on January 16 was canceled due to poor weather.

Key facts

  • Date: January 22, 2026
  • Time: 6:30 p.m. EST (23:30 GMT)
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Live Stream: Virtual Telescope Project
  • Magnitude: +13.2 (not visible to the naked eye)
  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Closest Approach to the Sun: October 31, 2025
  • Perigee Pass Near Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Upcoming Planetary Flyby: March 2026, 33.4 million miles (53.7 million km) from Jupiter

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by astronomers using data from the NASA-funded Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS). It is recognized as the third interstellar object to enter our solar system, following 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.

Key moments in its journey include its closest approach to the sun on October 31, 2025, and a perigee pass near Earth on December 19, 2025. The comet is expected to fly by Jupiter in March 2026 before continuing on its one-way path beyond the solar system.

Official statements

“This is a unique opportunity to witness an interstellar object as it travels away from our solar system,” said an astronomer from the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects. The discovery of 1I/’Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019 has sparked increased research and observation efforts in this field. These events highlight the importance of monitoring celestial bodies that originate outside our solar system.

What’s next

Observers are encouraged to tune in tonight to witness the live broadcast. After tonight’s event, comet 3I/ATLAS will continue its trajectory beyond the solar system and will not be observed again.

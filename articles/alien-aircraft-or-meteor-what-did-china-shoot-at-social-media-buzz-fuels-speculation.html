Alien aircraft or meteor, what did China shoot at?

A video has gone viral on Chinese social media showing an air defence system in Weifang, Shandong Province, shooting down an unidentified flying object (UFO) on September 15, 2025. There have been no reports of injuries or fatalities related to the incident.

The incident has sparked widespread speculation regarding the nature of the object, with some suggesting it may have been an alien spacecraft or a meteor. Authorities have not provided any official statement, leading to further conjecture about the motivations behind the military action.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on September 15, 2025.
  • Location: Weifang, Shandong Province, China.
  • Video shows a flaming projectile colliding with another object.
  • At least two missiles were reported to have approached the object.
  • No official confirmation of the footage's authenticity.

Timeline

On September 15, 2025, a video surfaced on social media showing an object in the night sky being intercepted by a Chinese air defence system. Locals in Weifang reported the sighting, describing a flaming projectile colliding with another object, resulting in a bright explosion.

The post on Weibo indicated that at least two missiles were launched at the unidentified object. Speculation about the incident has proliferated, but official commentary from Chinese authorities has not been forthcoming.

Official statements

“The authenticity of such a claim or the footage could not be independently confirmed,” according to Newsweek.

Context

This incident occurs in a climate of limited transparency within the Chinese government, where officials are not obligated to explain military actions. The lack of information often leads to rampant speculation both domestically and internationally regarding military capabilities and incidents.

What’s next

As of now, there are no announced investigations or official follow-ups regarding the incident. Authorities have not indicated if any further details will be released in the future.

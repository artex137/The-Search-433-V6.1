3I/ATLAS Spits Deadly Gas as Avi Loeb and SPHEREx Record Bizarre Post-Perihelion Object Transformation

The interstellar visitor known as 3I/ATLAS has begun emitting toxic gases following its close encounter with the Sun in December 2025. Astronomers have observed significant changes in the object, which now resembles a volatile celestial factory, according to data from various high-tech observatories.

This transformation is significant for scientists studying cometary behavior and interstellar objects. The release of deadly gases poses new questions about the composition and dynamics of 3I/ATLAS. Researchers, including Harvard professor Avi Loeb, continue to analyze the data to understand the implications of these findings.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation period: 8 December to 15 December 2025
  • Mass loss rate: approximately 180 kilogrammes per second
  • Distance of dust area: about 300,000 kilometres on each side
  • Date of Hubble images: 14 January 2026
  • Significant chemicals detected: cyanide, carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, water, organics

Timeline

3I/ATLAS approached the Sun in December 2025, triggering intense heat and a transformation in its structure. Observations from the SPHEREx space observatory captured infrared images revealing a pear-shaped distribution of dust and a significant increase in water gas emissions.

In January 2026, further analysis from the Hubble Space Telescope confirmed the chaotic state of the object, showcasing a glowing halo and mini-jets. The data suggests the object is experiencing internal processes that are not typical of standard comets.

Official statements

“The spectrum is now dominated by organo-silicaceous dust grains rather than ice,” said Avi Loeb in a January 16, 2026, post on Medium.

Context

This event marks a significant deviation from typical cometary behavior. Previous observations of 3I/ATLAS showed prominent water-ice features, which have since disappeared. The changes observed post-perihelion raise questions about the fundamental characteristics of interstellar objects.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the emissions and structural changes of 3I/ATLAS to gain insights into its behavior and the implications for understanding other celestial bodies. Further observations are expected as the object continues its journey through the solar system.

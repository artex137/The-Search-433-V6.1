Congress Announces Date for New UFO Hearing

Lawmakers will meet to discuss UFO topics during a hearing titled "Restoring Public Trust Through UAP Transparency and Whistleblower Protection" on September 9 at 10 a.m. The event will focus on protecting whistleblowers who report on UAP-related spending and classification policies.

This hearing is significant as it follows the introduction of the "UAP Whistleblower Protection Act" by Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna and Rep. Tim Burchett. Both representatives are part of a growing group advocating for increased transparency from the Department of Defense regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP).

Key facts

  • Date and time: September 9 at 10 a.m.
  • Location: House Oversight Committee hearing
  • Key participants: Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna, Rep. Tim Burchett
  • Witnesses: Air Force veterans Jeffrey Nuccetelli and Dylan Borland, UFO witness Chief Alexandro Wiggins, journalist George Knapp
  • Focus: Whistleblower protection and UAP-related transparency
  • Public access: Open to press and public; livestream available

Timeline

In the past week, Congresswoman Luna and Rep. Burchett introduced the "UAP Whistleblower Protection Act." This act aims to provide legal protections for individuals who disclose information related to UAPs.

The upcoming hearing on September 9 will feature testimony from four witnesses, who will discuss their concerns regarding the implications of disclosing UAP information.

Official statements

“The American public deserves maximum transparency with UFO sightings, acquisitions, and examinations, especially concerning any potential threats to national safety,” said Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna in a press release.

Context

The recent push for transparency regarding UAPs follows a series of reports and discussions among lawmakers about the need for clearer policies and protections for whistleblowers in this field. The movement reflects growing public interest and concern over unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

The hearing on September 9 is expected to advance discussions around UAP transparency and may lead to further legislative actions regarding whistleblower protections and related policies.

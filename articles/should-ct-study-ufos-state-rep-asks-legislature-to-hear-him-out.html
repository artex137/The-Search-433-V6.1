Should CT study UFOs? State Rep. asks legislature to hear him out

State Rep. Joe Hoxha, R-Bristol, has proposed a bill, H.B. 5422, calling for the University of Connecticut to collaborate with state agencies to study unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), commonly known as UFOs. The Appropriations Committee heard testimony on the bill on Thursday, with no reported injuries or fatalities associated with the topic.

Hoxha aims to establish a dedicated program for recording, studying, and collecting data on UAP sightings in Connecticut. He emphasized the need for a centralized approach to the fragmented information currently available. Hoxha noted that the federal government is beginning to take the subject more seriously, which could lead to greater insights into UAPs.

Key facts

  • Bill number: H.B. 5422
  • Proposed by: Rep. Joe Hoxha, R-Bristol
  • Hearing date: Thursday, March 11, 2026
  • Committee: Appropriations Committee
  • Cosponsor: Rep. Aundre Bumgardner, D-Groton
  • Previous similar legislation: New Jersey passed a law in 2025 to study UAPs

Timeline

On March 11, 2026, the Appropriations Committee of the Connecticut legislature held a hearing where Rep. Joe Hoxha presented his bill regarding UAP research. Hoxha expressed his long-standing interest in UFOs and highlighted the growing seriousness with which the topic is being treated at the federal level.

Sri Tata, a PhD student at Yale, testified in support of the bill, referencing increased attention to UAPs following a series of sightings in New Jersey in 2024. Rep. Martin Foncello, R-Brookfield, indicated his interest in collaborating on the bill, citing his connections to UAP investigations within the U.S. Department of Defense.

Official statements

“I’ve been a UFO enthusiast and researcher for many years. The topic is finally starting to be taken seriously, starting with the federal government.” - Rep. Joe Hoxha
“Establishing a scientific study for both public policy, for our scientific curiosity and for public reporting mechanisms would be important.” - Sri Tata, PhD student at Yale
“I know in fairly recent days, I have seen some things out there that need to be explained and haven’t [been].” - Rep. Martin Foncello

Context

UAP sightings have garnered increased attention in recent years, with Congress holding its first hearing on the subject in decades in May 2022. The Department of Defense established the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office in July 2022 to oversee UAP-related activities. A report issued in 2024 by the AARO found no evidence supporting the existence of alien technology linked to UAP sightings but did not deter public interest.

In New Jersey, a law was enacted in 2025 to create a center for UAP studies in collaboration with academic institutions, which went into effect in January 2026.

What’s next

The Connecticut legislature will continue to review H.B. 5422 as it moves through the legislative process. Further discussions and potential amendments to the bill are expected in upcoming committee meetings.

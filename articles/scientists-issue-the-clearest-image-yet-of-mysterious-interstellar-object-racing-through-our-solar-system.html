Scientists issue the clearest image yet of mysterious interstellar object racing through our solar system

Scientists at the Gemini South Observatory in Chile have captured the clearest images yet of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which is currently racing towards the sun at 221,000 km/h. The images were taken on August 27, 2025, when the object was approximately 380 million km from Earth.

This discovery is significant as it provides new insights into the nature of 3I/ATLAS, which some had speculated could be an alien spacecraft. The latest images reveal a dense icy nucleus surrounded by a broad halo of gas and dust, known as a coma, and a long tail extending away from the sun. This evidence supports the conclusion that 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet rather than an artificial object.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Distance from Earth: 380 million km (on August 27, 2025)
  • Speed: 221,000 km/h
  • Observatory: Gemini South Observatory, Chile
  • Image date: August 27, 2025
  • Previous observations: Hubble Space Telescope (July 2025), James Webb Space Telescope

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first identified as an interstellar object when it entered the solar system. Initial observations indicated weak cometary activity. As it approached the sun, increased sunlight resulted in more significant outgassing, leading to the formation of a coma and a tail.

The latest images, taken on August 27, 2025, provide multi-coloured views of 3I/ATLAS, revealing its icy nucleus and tail, which had become significantly larger since earlier observations.

Official statements

“These new images very clearly demonstrate that 3I/ATLAS is a comet,” said Dr Mark Norris, an astronomer from the University of Lancashire.
“Little green men certainly aren't responsible!” stated Dr Matthew Genge, a planetary scientist from Imperial College London.

Context

Prior to this discovery, some researchers speculated that 3I/ATLAS could be an artificial spacecraft. Notably, Harvard physicist Professor Avi Loeb had suggested it could be a nuclear-powered vessel. However, the recent findings dismiss these theories, confirming 3I/ATLAS as a natural object.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to gather data on 3I/ATLAS as it remains observable for a limited time. Understanding its composition will provide insights into planetary formation around distant stars.

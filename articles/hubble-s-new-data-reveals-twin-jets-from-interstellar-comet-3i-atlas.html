Hubble's New Data Reveals Twin Jets From Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA's Hubble Space Telescope has observed twin jets emanating from interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, providing new insights into its composition and behavior. This discovery occurred on December 29, 2025, as Hubble continued its mission to study celestial phenomena.

The findings from Hubble are significant as they enhance our understanding of interstellar objects and their interactions with the solar system. Scientists aim to analyze the jets to determine the comet's material composition and activity levels, which could inform future studies of similar objects. NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA) are currently collaborating to further investigate these observations.

Key facts

  • Discovery date: December 29, 2025
  • Location: Observed from Earth using Hubble Space Telescope
  • Object: Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Agencies involved: NASA and ESA

Timeline

On December 29, 2025, Hubble's observations revealed twin jets from 3I/ATLAS. This event marks a continuation of Hubble's exploration of interstellar objects since the comet was first detected.

Official statements

“The jets observed from 3I/ATLAS provide critical information regarding the physical and chemical processes of interstellar comets,” said a NASA spokesperson. “We are eager to analyze the data further to expand our understanding of these fascinating celestial bodies.”

Context

This is not the first time Hubble has studied interstellar comets. Previous observations have provided valuable data on the behavior and characteristics of such objects as they travel through the solar system. The study of comets like 3I/ATLAS is essential for understanding the origins of our solar system and the materials that comprise it.

What’s next

NASA and ESA plan to conduct further analyses of the data collected from Hubble. Researchers are expected to release additional findings in the coming months that will shed light on the nature of 3I/ATLAS and similar interstellar objects.

White House Appoints Harvard Astronomer Avi Loeb to Lead UFO Council

The White House has appointed Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb to lead a new scientific advisory council focused on investigating unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), as announced on [insert date]. The council will address national security risks associated with UFOs, according to a statement from the administration.

This appointment is significant as it aims to enhance the understanding of UAP and improve transparency regarding unidentified aerial incidents. The council will report to a newly established UAP Governance Board, which is overseen by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence. This effort aligns with the Trump administration's initiative to declassify information on UFOs.

Key facts

  • Avi Loeb served as head of Harvard’s astronomy department until 2020.
  • The council will include outside scientists and figures such as retired Rear Admiral Timothy Gallaudet and billionaire Ben Lamm.
  • Loeb’s team has requested over 50 documents related to UAP incidents from the Pentagon.
  • Approximately 160 UFO files have been released by the Pentagon, with more expected in the coming weeks.
  • The UAP Governance Board aims to improve public access to UAP-related information.

Timeline

In 2017, Avi Loeb garnered public attention when he suggested that the interstellar object ‘Oumuamua could be alien technology. Following this, he founded the Galileo Project at Harvard to search for extraterrestrial evidence.

The White House's recent appointment of Loeb marks a formal step towards investigating UAP as part of national security efforts. The UAP Governance Board will oversee the council's work moving forward.

Official statements

“It’s like a detective story,” said Loeb in an interview with The Associated Press. “It’s a lot of fun, as long as you don’t pay too much attention to the critics.”
Astrophysicist Steve Desch criticized the appointment, stating, “I don’t know what’s going to come of this, but we’re not going to get any closer to answering these questions with him in charge.”
Former Pentagon official Sean Kirkpatrick expressed skepticism, stating that Loeb is “not viewed favorably” by much of the scientific community and lacks national security experience.

Context

The appointment follows a trend of increasing government interest in UAP, especially after the release of military videos showing unidentified objects in the sky. The U.S. government has been under pressure to declassify information related to UFOs for transparency and public safety reasons.

Loeb's controversial views on extraterrestrial life have drawn both attention and criticism from the scientific community, influencing public discourse on UAP.

What’s next

The newly formed council will begin its investigations into UAP incidents and is expected to share findings with the public via a dedicated website. Additional documents from the Pentagon are also anticipated to be released in the coming weeks.

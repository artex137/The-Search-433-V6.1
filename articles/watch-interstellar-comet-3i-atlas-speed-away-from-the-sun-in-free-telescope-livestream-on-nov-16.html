Watch interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS speed away from the sun in free telescope livestream on Nov. 16

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will be visible during a livestream hosted by the Virtual Telescope Project on November 16 at 11:15 p.m. ET (04:15 GMT on November 17). The comet recently emerged from behind the sun and is now on an escape trajectory from the solar system.

This event is significant as it allows astronomers and the public to observe one of only three known interstellar objects that have visited our solar system. The Virtual Telescope Project will provide detailed views of the comet from their robotic telescopes located in Manciano, Italy.

Key facts

  • Event: Livestream of comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: November 16, 2025
  • Time: 11:15 p.m. ET (04:15 GMT on November 17)
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Magnitude: +10.9 (not visible to the naked eye)
  • Discovered: July 1, 2025
  • Closest approach to the sun: October 30, 2025

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025. It made its closest approach to the sun on October 30, 2025. Following this event, the comet emerged from behind the sun and became visible to ground-based telescopes.

On November 11, Virtual Telescope Project founder Gianluca Masi captured an image of the comet, showcasing its glowing central coma and ion tail.

The livestream event on November 16 will allow viewers to see the comet as it travels through the constellation Virgo.

Official statements

“We are excited to provide this opportunity for everyone to witness the journey of 3I/ATLAS,” said Gianluca Masi, founder of the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable as it is only the third interstellar object confirmed to have visited our solar system. Its trajectory analysis has provided astronomers with valuable insights into the nature of such celestial bodies.

The comet's approach and visibility have generated interest among both amateur and professional astronomers, highlighting the ongoing fascination with interstellar phenomena.

What’s next

Following the livestream, astronomers will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels away from the sun and through the solar system, contributing to ongoing research into interstellar objects.

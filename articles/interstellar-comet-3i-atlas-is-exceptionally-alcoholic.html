Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS is exceptionally alcoholic

Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS has been found to contain an unusual amount of methanol, according to researchers using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile. The comet, which is currently journeying through our solar system, was discovered in July 2025 and has drawn significant scientific interest due to its unique composition.

This discovery is important as it provides insights into the origins of the comet and the broader implications for understanding other celestial bodies. Scientists say the findings may help answer questions about where 3I/ATLAS came from, a topic that has intrigued researchers since its discovery. The research has been posted on the preprint server arXiv.org and is pending peer review.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025.
  • It has been measured to be "heavily enriched" in methanol.
  • The comet reached speeds of over 150,000 miles per hour at its closest point to the sun.
  • The findings were made by researchers at the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile.
  • A new image from the European Space Agency's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer shows the comet as a "white, glowing egg-shaped object."

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025. Since then, it has passed near the sun, allowing scientists to observe its properties closely. In February 2026, the European Space Agency's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer provided a new image of the comet, which was described as a glowing object in space. Recent measurements from ALMA have confirmed the comet's high methanol content.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” said Nathan Roth, a research assistant professor at American University.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of only three interstellar objects discovered to date. Its unique characteristics provide valuable data for scientists studying the composition of comets beyond our solar system. Previous interstellar objects have also sparked interest but have not shown the same levels of methanol as observed in 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system. Further analysis and peer review of the findings are expected to provide deeper insights into the comet's origins and implications for understanding other celestial bodies.

This Alberta town is home to the world's first UFO landing pad

St. Paul, a small town located just north-east of Edmonton, is home to the world’s first-ever UFO landing pad, which was built in 1967 as part of Canada’s Centennial celebrations. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the landmark.

The UFO landing pad serves as a unique attraction for tourists and locals alike, contributing to St. Paul's identity as an agricultural hub and trading center. The town has embraced this quirky feature, which draws visitors interested in extraterrestrial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Location: St. Paul, Alberta
  • Established: 1967
  • Adjacent facility: Tourist Information Centre, opened in the 1990s
  • Seasonal operation: Visitor centre closed in winter; landing pad remains open

Timeline

In 1967, St. Paul erected the UFO landing pad as part of the Canadian Centennial celebrations, marking the 100th anniversary of the Canadian Confederation. In the 1990s, then-mayor Paul Langevin opened the adjacent Tourist Information Centre to welcome visitors. This center features an exhibit on UFOs, showcasing photos of unidentified aircraft and crop circles.

During the summer months, the Tourist Information Centre operates actively, offering local knowledge and selling various extraterrestrial-themed trinkets.

Official statements

“The landing pad has become a significant landmark for our town and attracts many visitors,” said a representative from the Tourist Information Centre.

Context

St. Paul has established itself as a notable destination for those interested in UFOs and extraterrestrial life. The UFO landing pad and the associated Tourist Information Centre have become integral to the town's tourism strategy, providing a unique draw compared to more traditional attractions.

What’s next

There are currently no disclosed plans for further developments or events related to the UFO landing pad, but it remains a point of interest for visitors year-round.

Holiday Porch Pirate Season Is Here--Is This A Legal "Defense?"

As the holiday season approaches, package theft has become a growing concern across North America. The phenomenon, known as 'porch piracy,' has led to significant financial losses, with thefts costing Americans approximately $16 billion annually, according to Safewise.

Authorities are now examining the legality of using bait packages to deter thieves. These packages, often filled with unpleasant items or designed to explode with dye or glitter, raise legal questions about homeowner liability and the boundaries of self-defense.

Key facts

  • Porch piracy has significantly increased since 2010-2011 with the rise of e-commerce.
  • The annual cost of package theft in the U.S. is estimated at $16 billion.
  • Exploding bait packages are available from online retailers but are typically custom-built by homeowners.
  • A 1971 Iowa Supreme Court ruling prohibits homeowners from setting up booby traps for protection.
  • Legal experts warn that homeowners could be held liable for injuries caused by these devices.

Timeline

Since the surge in e-commerce around 2010-2011, package theft has become increasingly common. In response, homeowners have begun using fake packages filled with unpleasant materials to deter thieves. Recently, this has escalated to the use of exploding packages that release various substances when opened. Legal discussions around the use of such devices have intensified, particularly regarding homeowner liability.

Official statements

“Most experts say it is better to arrange with the delivery service to drop the package in a location not well seen from the street.”
“Depending on the laws in your municipality or state, a homeowner can be held liable if their device injures the suspect.”

Context

Prior incidents of porch piracy have led to various community responses, including the use of fake packages. The legal landscape surrounding self-defense and property protection has evolved, with the 1971 Iowa Supreme Court ruling highlighting the complexities of homeowner rights. As theft rates rise, more homeowners are seeking creative solutions to protect their deliveries.

What’s next

Investigations into the legality of exploding bait packages continue. Homeowners are advised to explore safer alternatives for package delivery and to consult local laws regarding self-defense measures.

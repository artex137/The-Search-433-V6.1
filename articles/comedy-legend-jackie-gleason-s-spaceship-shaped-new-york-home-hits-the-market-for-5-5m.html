Comedy legend Jackie Gleason's spaceship-shaped New York home hits the market for $5.5M

Jackie Gleason's New York home, known as The Mothership, has been placed on the market for $5.5 million. The unique property, located in Hudson Valley, features a spaceship-like design and was built in 1959. It is being listed by agent Heidi Henshaw of Corcoran Legends Realty.

The sale of The Mothership is significant due to its historical and architectural value. Gleason, a prominent figure in American entertainment, commissioned architect Robert Cika to create the home, which has become an iconic representation of his vision and creativity.

Key facts

  • Location: Hudson Valley, New York
  • Listing price: $5.5 million
  • Completion year: 1959
  • Architect: Robert Cika
  • Notable features: three bedrooms, two bathrooms, three bars, game room, marble dance floor
  • Historical guests: Frank Sinatra, Marilyn Monroe, Joe DiMaggio, President Richard Nixon

Timeline

Jackie Gleason commissioned the construction of The Mothership in 1954, and it took five years to complete. The home was finished in 1959, featuring no right angles and a design inspired by musical notes. Gleason hosted many famous personalities at the residence during his lifetime.

Gleason's television career peaked with "The Jackie Gleason Show," which aired until 1970, solidifying his status as a comedy icon. He passed away at the age of 71 in June 1987.

Official statements

“The Mothership is a piece of American cultural history. Jackie Gleason’s vision, paired with Robert Cika’s architectural ingenuity, created something truly iconic,” said listing agent Heidi Henshaw.
“Westchester continues to be one of the most attractive markets in the region, offering proximity to New York City with the privacy and space that today’s buyers are seeking,” Henshaw added.

Context

The Mothership stands as a testament to Gleason's legacy, blending his fascination with UFOs and unique architectural styles. The home’s design elements, such as the ceiling shaped like boats and oversized fireplaces, contribute to its rarity in real estate.

Jackie Gleason is best known for his role as Ralph Kramden in "The Honeymooners," which aired for just one season but has remained beloved in syndication. His influence on television comedy continues to be recognized.

What’s next

The property is now listed for sale, attracting potential buyers interested in unique historical homes in the Hudson Valley area. Further details regarding showings and offers are not disclosed at this time.

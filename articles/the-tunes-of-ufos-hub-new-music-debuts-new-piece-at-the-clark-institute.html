The tunes of UFOs: Hub New Music debuts new piece at The Clark Institute

On Saturday, Hub New Music premiered composer Daniel Wohl’s piece “Mirage” at The Clark Art Institute in Williamstown, Massachusetts. The 25-minute performance, inspired by UFO culture, combined electronic and acoustic sounds, creating a unique auditory experience.

This performance marks a significant moment in contemporary music, showcasing the collaboration between Wohl and the quartet, which has evolved over two years. The piece reflects both artistic exploration and the intriguing themes of humanity's fascination with the unknown.

Key facts

  • Performance date: Saturday, October 21, 2023
  • Location: The Clark Art Institute, Williamstown, Massachusetts
  • Duration of piece: 25 minutes
  • Composer: Daniel Wohl
  • Performers: Hub New Music quartet
  • Founded: 2013
  • Ensemble members: Michael Avitabile (flute), Gleb Kanasevich (clarinet), Magnolia Rohrer (violin), Jesse Christeson (cello)

Timeline

Hub New Music has worked on “Mirage” for two years, starting from a residency at the I-Park Foundation, Inc. in Connecticut. The ensemble conducted a work-in-progress showing at Roulette Intermedium in Brooklyn, New York, before finalizing the piece.

During the composition process, Wohl engaged the ensemble to submit sounds they associated with the concept of “otherworldly.” These recordings influenced the electronic elements and overall composition of the piece.

Official statements

“We just love the [combination of the] four of us because we get to explore so many kinds of different sounds, like what we made on stage,” said flautist Michael Avitabile.
“A mirage exists in that fascinating space between what’s actually there and what we project onto it,” Wohl explained about the piece's title.

Context

“Mirage” represents a blend of acoustic and electronic music, a hallmark of Hub New Music's approach. The ensemble’s unique combination of wind and string instruments allows for diverse sound exploration, making their performances distinctive in the contemporary music landscape.

Wohl’s work often delves into themes of perception and reality, which resonates with audiences in an era of heightened interest in extraterrestrial phenomena.

What’s next

Following the premiere, Hub New Music will continue to perform “Mirage” at various venues, furthering their exploration of the connections between music and the unknown. Future collaborations and commissions are expected as they expand their repertoire.

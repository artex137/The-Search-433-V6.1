Spectrophotometric Evidence For A Metal-bearing, Carbonaceous, and Pristine Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

Published 2025-11-30 17:42 UTC

Spectrophotometric Evidence For A Metal-bearing, Carbonaceous, and Pristine Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been identified as the second confirmed cometary object to enter the Solar System from interstellar space, according to a recent report led by researchers Josep M. Trigo-Rodríguez and Maria Gritsevich. The findings were submitted for review on November 25, 2025, and detail the comet's unique characteristics and its potential significance in understanding the formation of celestial bodies.

This discovery matters because it offers a rare opportunity to study the physical and chemical processes of interstellar objects. The researchers suggest that 3I/ATLAS may be a primitive carbonaceous object enriched in native metal, which could provide insights into the origins of comets and other minor bodies in the Solar System.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is the second confirmed interstellar comet.
  • Submitted for review on November 25, 2025.
  • Research conducted by Josep M. Trigo-Rodríguez and Maria Gritsevich.
  • Comet exhibits signs of significant aqueous alteration and cryovolcanism.
  • Potentially enriched in native metal and water ice.

Timeline

On November 24, 2025, researchers submitted their findings detailing the spectrophotometric evidence of 3I/ATLAS. The report includes photometric observations of the comet’s inbound trajectory toward perihelion and a spectroscopic comparison with carbonaceous chondrites from NASA's Antarctic collection.

The study indicates that the spectral similarities suggest a relatively high tensile strength and a substantial metal fraction in the comet, along with significant chemical alterations as it approaches the Sun.

Official statements

“Interstellar objects such as 3I/ATLAS provide rare opportunities to investigate physical and chemical processes in distant minor bodies of our own Solar System,” the researchers stated.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, only one other interstellar comet has been confirmed, highlighting the rarity of such discoveries. The unique composition of 3I/ATLAS, particularly its metal content, sets it apart from typical comets that originate from the outer Solar System.

What’s next

The submitted findings will undergo peer review, and further observations may be planned to confirm the comet's properties and enhance understanding of its implications for solar system formation.

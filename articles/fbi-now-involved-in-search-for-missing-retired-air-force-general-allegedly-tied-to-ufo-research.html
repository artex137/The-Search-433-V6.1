FBI Now Involved In Search For Missing Retired Air Force General Allegedly Tied To UFO Research

The FBI has joined the search for retired U.S. Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, 68, who was reported missing in New Mexico on Friday around 11 a.m. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office (BCSO) issued a Silver Alert for McCasland, and as of Sunday afternoon, New Mexico Search and Rescue has been assisting with the investigation.

This search is significant due to McCasland's background in military research, particularly related to UFO investigations. Authorities are utilizing various resources to locate him, with the FBI confirming its involvement as a standard practice to support local law enforcement.

Key facts

  • McCasland was last seen on Friday at approximately 11 a.m.
  • The BCSO has issued a Silver Alert for McCasland.
  • The FBI Albuquerque Field Office is assisting in the investigation.
  • The search has involved a drone, a K9 team, and a helicopter.
  • There are currently no signs of criminal activity related to McCasland's disappearance.
  • McCasland previously managed a $2.2 billion science and technology portfolio at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base.
  • Kirtland Air Force Base is also participating in the search efforts.

Timeline

On Friday at about 11 a.m., Major General William Neil McCasland was last seen in New Mexico. The BCSO issued a Silver Alert following his disappearance. By Sunday afternoon, New Mexico Search and Rescue began assisting in the search. The FBI confirmed its involvement in the investigation, stating that it is standard practice to support local law enforcement. The BCSO has conducted searches using various methods, including drones and helicopters.

Official statements

“The FBI Albuquerque Field Office is involved in this investigation, as it is standard practice for the FBI to assist our local law enforcement partners if we have a tool, tactic, or technique that could benefit their investigations,” said an FBI spokesperson.
“There are no signs of any criminal activity such as a kidnapping or anything else,” a BCSO spokesperson told the New York Post.
“We are coordinating closely with local authorities and defer all updates regarding the search efforts to the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office,” said Col. Justin Secrest, base commander of the 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland.

Context

Major General McCasland has a notable background in military research, having managed significant funding for science and technology projects at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, where the military's Project Blue Book focused on UFO investigations. He has also been mentioned in the 2016 WikiLeaks release regarding UFO-related projects, although there are no official records of his direct involvement in UFO research.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to search for McCasland using various methods and resources. Updates regarding the search efforts will be provided by the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office as the investigation unfolds.

Long-Buried UFO Files Confirming Report Of Glowing Object Over Military Base Finally Declassified

Newly-declassified documents from Argentina’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs have confirmed a potential UFO encounter at General San Martín Base in Antarctica, occurring in April and May of 1991. The records validate eyewitness accounts from military personnel and civilian researchers who reported seeing a gigantic flying saucer over the base. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This revelation has sparked renewed interest in UFO phenomena and the transparency of government records. Andrea Perez Simondini, director of the Victoria UFO Museum, noted the significance of the declassification, emphasizing that it took over 15 years for the public to access these files. She highlighted the role of ordinary citizens in utilizing the Law on Access to Public Information to uncover such information.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred in April and May 1991.
  • Location: General San Martín Base, Antarctica.
  • Involved 20 crew members, including military and civilian scientists.
  • Significant anomalies recorded by a riometer for over four hours.
  • Eyewitness testimony from meteorological sub-officer Miguel Amaya.
  • Declassified documents released by Argentina’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Timeline

The UFO encounter began at approximately 1:15 a.m. when the electronics engineer in charge of ionospheric studies contacted Miguel Amaya. Minutes later, the riometer began recording significant anomalies, which lasted for over four hours. Around 16 hours after the initial anomalies, another base member observed a large circle of light moving silently over the base.

Amaya reported that his superiors instructed him and other staff members not to discuss the incident. The government has now declassified images that corroborate the unusual interference with the riometer.

Official statements

“These files were sought for more than 15 years,” said Andrea Perez Simondini, director of the Victoria UFO Museum.
“At times, these ‘signals’ would cut out, and everything would continue as normal,” Amaya stated in an interview with CEFORA. “Then, for periods of ten or fifteen minutes, it would start again, sometimes with such force that the needles would jump off the belt.”
“This case now raises expectations for the continued declassification of other files,” Simondini added.

Context

This incident is part of a broader trend of increasing public interest in UFOs and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. The use of the Law on Access to Public Information has empowered citizens to seek clarification on historical events that have long remained classified.

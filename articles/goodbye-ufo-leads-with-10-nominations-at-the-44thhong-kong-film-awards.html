Goodbye UFO Leads with 10 Nominations at the 44th Hong Kong Film Awards

The 44th Hong Kong Film Awards has announced its nominations, with the drama film Goodbye UFO leading the pack with ten nominations. The awards ceremony will take place on an undisclosed date at an undisclosed location.

This year’s nominations highlight the growing recognition of Hong Kong cinema. The film Goodbye UFO has previously won the Best Film Award at the Hong Kong Film Critics Society Awards, which adds to the anticipation surrounding its performance at the upcoming awards. Producer and screenwriter Amy Chin stated that the film’s journey has been challenging but rewarding.

Key facts

  • 10 nominations for Goodbye UFO, including Best Film, Best Director, and Best Screenplay.
  • Release year for Goodbye UFO: 2019.
  • Shu Qi nominated for Best Director, Best New Director, and Best Screenplay for her film Girl.
  • Girlfriend set to release on March 5 and has two nominations for actress Elizabeth Tang.
  • Fala Chen favoured to win Best Actress for her role in Peg O’My Heart.

Timeline

The nominations for the 44th Hong Kong Film Awards were announced on an undisclosed date. Goodbye UFO has emerged as the leading contender with ten nominations. The film previously won the Best Film Award at the Hong Kong Film Critics Society Awards.

Shu Qi’s film Girl has also gained attention, earning three nominations, while Girlfriend will be released on March 5 and has already secured two nominations.

Fala Chen’s performance in Peg O’My Heart has made her a strong candidate for the Best Actress Award, highlighting her return to Hong Kong cinema after ten years.

Official statements

“It turns out that a dream, really can bring out hope! Thank you to the industry for their support. It was a tough journey for ‘Goodbye UFO’ to get to where it is today.” – Amy Chin
“Thank you everyone! I especially want to thank my co-star Han Ning and all the crew members!” – Elizabeth Tang
“I am deeply moved to receive this nomination. I am very grateful that my Hong Kong colleagues and audiences did not forget about me.” – Fala Chen

Context

The Hong Kong Film Awards have long been a platform for recognizing the achievements of local filmmakers and actors. The prominence of films like Goodbye UFO and Girl reflects a trend towards more diverse storytelling in Hong Kong cinema.

Shu Qi’s recognition at the Busan International Film Festival has also elevated her status in the industry. This year’s nominations suggest a growing appreciation for both established and emerging talents in the film sector.

What’s next

The awards ceremony will take place on an undisclosed date. The film industry will continue to monitor the public’s reception of the nominated films, particularly as Girlfriend prepares for its release.

New Filtered Image of 3I/ATLAS Reveals Jet Structures and Nickel-Rich Outgassing That Challenge Conventional Comet Physics

A new filtered image of comet 3I/ATLAS, captured on November 19, 2023, by Pau Montplet using a C6 telescope, has revealed intricate jet structures and nickel-rich outgassing that challenge existing theories of comet behavior. The image offers a detailed look at the comet's morphology and shows no injuries or fatalities associated with this astronomical event.

This discovery matters as it raises significant questions about the conventional understanding of comet physics. Researchers are examining the implications of the image, which suggests that the comet is not simply shedding material through thermal sublimation but is instead releasing it in directional pulses driven by internal chemical processes. This challenges long-held assumptions about how comets behave as they approach the Sun.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 19, 2023
  • Location: Observed using a C6 telescope
  • Researcher: Pau Montplet
  • Key findings: Evidence of nickel-rich outgassing and structured jets
  • Next close approach: December 19, 2023

Timeline

On November 19, 2023, Pau Montplet captured a filtered image of comet 3I/ATLAS. The image was processed using a Larson–Sekanina filter, revealing detailed jet structures and signs of chemical activity within the comet. Researchers noted that the jets are not uniformly distributed, indicating that the comet is undergoing pressure-driven events rather than simple thermal sublimation.

As the comet approaches its next close pass on December 19, scientists will continue to monitor its activity and gather more data. The findings from this image may influence future studies and our understanding of interstellar comets.

Official statements

“This image, taken together with the chemical findings in the study, illustrates a comet that is not passive. It is reactive, responding dynamically to solar heating, internal chemistry, and structural stresses accumulated over a billion-year interstellar journey,” said a lead researcher.

Context

Prior observations of 3I/ATLAS have shown similar jet structures and activity patterns. However, the November 19 image intensifies these findings, suggesting that the comet may exhibit behaviors not typically associated with comets from the Oort Cloud. The detection of nickel absorption lines has prompted researchers to consider alternative explanations for the comet's activity.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze new data as it becomes available, particularly leading up to the comet's close approach on December 19. Further studies will focus on understanding the chemical processes at work within 3I/ATLAS and how they compare to known cometary behavior.

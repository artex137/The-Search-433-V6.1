Retired U.S. General Missing in Albuquerque, FBI Investigation Underway

A retired U.S. Air Force Major General, William Neil McCasland, has gone missing in Albuquerque, New Mexico. McCasland, 68, left his home on foot at approximately 11 a.m. on February 27 and has not been heard from since. Local police and the FBI are actively investigating his disappearance but have reported no leads after two weeks.

The case has garnered attention due to McCasland's previous association with a base linked to UFO rumors. Authorities are focusing on his last known whereabouts and have contacted over 600 residents in the area to gather information. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office issued a missing-person alert on February 28, the day after he disappeared.

Key facts

  • Missing person: William Neil McCasland, 68, retired U.S. Air Force Major General
  • Last seen: February 27, 2026, around 11 a.m. in Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Missing-person alert issued: February 28, 2026
  • Authorities involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office, FBI, U.S. Air Force
  • Number of residents contacted: Over 600
  • McCasland left his cellphone at home
  • No suspicion of a crime detected so far

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, at around 11 a.m., Maj. Gen. McCasland left his home in Albuquerque on foot. He has not contacted family or friends since that time. The following day, on February 28, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office issued a missing-person alert. Investigators have since contacted over 600 nearby residents in an effort to locate him.

As of March 12, 2026, authorities have reported no leads and are continuing their search in collaboration with the FBI and the U.S. Air Force.

Official statements

"I hope Maj. Gen. McCasland has not been targeted by criminals," said Luis Elizondo, a former intelligence officer. "I pray he returns safely and soon."
Susan McCasland Wilkerson, McCasland's wife, expressed her concerns on social media, stating, "There is no reason for my husband to be abducted in connection with UFOs."

Context

Maj. Gen. McCasland is an engineer with a background in aerospace engineering. He has earned degrees from the U.S. Air Force Academy, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, and Harvard Kennedy School. He previously worked on significant military technology projects, including the Global Positioning System and Space-Based Laser.

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, where McCasland served as director of the Air Force Research Laboratory, has been the subject of longstanding rumors regarding alien bodies linked to the Roswell incident in 1947.

What’s next

The investigation into McCasland's disappearance will continue, with authorities exploring all possibilities. They have not ruled out any scenarios as they seek to determine his whereabouts.

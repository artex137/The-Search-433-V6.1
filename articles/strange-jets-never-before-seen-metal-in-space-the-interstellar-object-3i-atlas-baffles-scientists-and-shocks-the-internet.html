Strange Jets, Never-Before-Seen Metal in Space: The Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Baffles Scientists and Shocks the Internet

Scientists have observed unexpected phenomena from the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS since its discovery in July 2023. The comet is currently approaching the Sun, with its closest approach set for October 29, 2023. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery is significant because it provides insights into the formation of worlds beyond our Solar System. NASA has added 3I/ATLAS to its International Asteroid Warning Network (IAWN) list due to its unpredictable trajectory. Observations have revealed unusual characteristics, leading to debates among astronomers regarding its nature.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 2023
  • Closest Approach to the Sun: October 29, 2023
  • Key Observations: Nickel detected, no iron present
  • Jet Length: Nearly 10,000 kilometers
  • NASA Mission: Europa Clipper may intercept between October 30 and November 6, 2025

Timeline

Since its discovery in July 2023, 3I/ATLAS has presented a series of unusual observations:

  • July 2023: 3I/ATLAS is discovered and confirmed to originate from interstellar space.
  • October 2023: Observations using the Keck Cosmic Web Imager reveal a strong signature of nickel in the comet’s outflow.
  • October 2023: Two-Meter Twin Telescope in Spain detects a massive jet of gas and dust being ejected toward the Sun.
  • October 2023: NASA adds 3I/ATLAS to its IAWN list for monitoring.

Official statements

“Take your vacations before October 29,” said Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, suggesting the implications of the comet’s unusual composition.
Experts have pointed out that the observed jets are a fairly normal feature for comets, similar to the behavior exhibited by comet Neowise.

Context

This is only the third interstellar object ever observed, following 1I/2017 U1 and 2I/Borisov. The unique characteristics of 3I/ATLAS have led to increased public interest and speculation about its origin and nature. Astronomers continue to study the object to understand its composition and trajectory better.

What’s next

NASA's Europa Clipper mission may pass through the ion tail of 3I/ATLAS between October 30 and November 6, 2025, which could provide further insights into the comet's composition. Observations will continue to refine its path from November 27 to January 27, 2024.

FBI joins hunt for missing general named in UFO report. What that means

Published 2026-03-06 17:00 UTC

FBI joins search for missing general in New Mexico

The FBI has joined the search for retired U.S. Air Force General William Neil McCasland, who has been missing since February 27 in Albuquerque, New Mexico. McCasland, 68, was last seen in the area and is considered to have unspecified medical issues, raising concerns for his safety.

The FBI's involvement follows a request from local law enforcement. The agency has deployed agents and investigators from the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office to canvass the neighborhood where McCasland was last seen. The case has garnered attention due to McCasland's previous role as commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio.

Key facts

  • Missing since: February 27, 2023
  • Last seen: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Age: 68
  • Height: 5 feet 11 inches
  • Weight: approximately 160 pounds
  • Medical issues: unspecified
  • Previous role: Commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base

Timeline

On February 27, 2023, General William Neil McCasland was last seen in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert shortly thereafter, indicating concerns for his safety due to his medical issues. The FBI has since joined the search at the request of local law enforcement, deploying agents to assist in the investigation.

Official statements

“The FBI joins missing persons searches at the request of local law enforcement, or if the person is a federal official,” according to the U.S. Department of Justice Criminal Resource Manual.
“The FBI may also open and lead a missing persons case if foul play is suspected,” stated Jerri Williams, a former FBI special agent.

Context

McCasland has been described as a key adviser on UFO-related projects, according to Tom DeLonge, a UFO researcher. His name has appeared in emails from the 2016 WikiLeaks release, where he was mentioned in discussions about UFO materials linked to Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, known for its historical connection to UFO investigations during Project Blue Book.

During the 1950s and '60s, Project Blue Book logged 12,618 UFO sightings, with 701 remaining “unidentified.” This project was created due to Cold War-era security concerns and public fascination with extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

The FBI and local authorities will continue to search for General McCasland, utilizing available resources to locate him and ensure his safety. Further updates will be provided as the investigation progresses.

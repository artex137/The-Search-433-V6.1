3I/ATLAS full of 'ice volcanos' as new images blow alien theory wide open

3I/ATLAS full of 'ice volcanos' as new images blow alien theory wide open

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has shown signs of erupting “ice volcanoes” on its surface, according to researchers from the Institute of Space Sciences (CSIC/IEEC). This discovery, made as the comet approaches the sun, raises questions about the comet's composition and similarities to bodies in our solar system.

This finding is significant as it suggests that 3I/ATLAS, which originated from a different cosmic neighbourhood, shares characteristics with trans-Neptunian objects in our solar system. Researchers aim to further understand the materials that comprise the comet's surface and what this means for our knowledge of interstellar bodies.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed since July 2023.
  • Research led by Josep Trigo-Rodríguez from the Institute of Space Sciences (CSIC/IEEC).
  • Discovery of “ice volcanoes” on the comet's surface.
  • Comet originates from a different planetary system.
  • Similarities found with trans-Neptunian objects.

Timeline

In July 2023, observers first noted the presence of 3I/ATLAS as it entered the solar system. Researchers have since studied the comet's surface, leading to the recent discovery of erupting “ice volcanoes” as it approaches the sun.

Official statements

“We were all surprised. Being a comet formed in a remote planetary system, it is remarkable that the mixture of materials forming the surface of the body has resemblance with trans-Neptunian objects,” said Josep Trigo-Rodríguez, the study's lead author.

Context

This discovery adds to the ongoing research into interstellar objects and their characteristics. Prior studies have examined the origins and compositions of various comets, but 3I/ATLAS presents a unique opportunity to explore an object from outside our solar system.

What’s next

Researchers plan to conduct further studies on 3I/ATLAS to better understand its surface composition and the implications of its similarities to known solar system bodies.

UFO in California? Strange Black Ring Spotted Over Disneyland at Dawn Turns Out to Be Pyrotechnic Testing

A black ring spotted floating over Disneyland in Anaheim, California, at around 6:30 AM on Thursday has been identified as smoke from pyrotechnic testing, according to Disney officials. Local resident Lori Nayahalski captured photos and video of the phenomenon, which initially sparked speculation about a possible UFO sighting among early commuters on Interstate 5.

The sighting has drawn attention due to its unusual appearance. Disney clarified that the smoke originated from effects used in the park's nighttime shows, such as Fantasmic! and World of Colour. These shows utilize fireworks and special effects, and the testing is a routine part of their preparations before the park opens.

Key facts

  • Location: Disneyland, Anaheim, California
  • Time of sighting: Approximately 6:30 AM on Thursday
  • Witness: Local resident Lori Nayahalski
  • Phenomenon: Black ring identified as pyrotechnic smoke
  • Related shows: Fantasmic! and World of Colour

Timeline

At approximately 6:30 AM on Thursday, a mysterious black ring appeared over Disneyland, captured by local resident Lori Nayahalski. The unusual sight prompted speculation about a UFO among early morning commuters. Shortly after, Disney officials confirmed that the ring was not extraterrestrial but rather smoke from pyrotechnic testing.

This testing is standard procedure for the park, aimed at ensuring the safety and effectiveness of the special effects used in their popular nighttime shows.

Official statements

“The phenomenon wasn’t extraterrestrial but simply smoke from pyrotechnic testing conducted before the park opened,” Disney officials reported.

Context

This incident follows a growing trend of public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. While many sightings have been attributed to natural or man-made sources, the curiosity surrounding such events continues to capture public attention.

What’s next

No further investigations are planned as the phenomenon has been identified and clarified by Disney officials as a routine part of their operations.

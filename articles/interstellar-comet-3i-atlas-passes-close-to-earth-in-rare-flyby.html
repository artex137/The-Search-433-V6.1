Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS passes close to Earth in rare flyby

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to Earth today, December 19, coming within 168 million miles of the planet. This event offers skywatchers a rare celestial spectacle, according to NASA and National Geographic.

The significance of this flyby lies in its status as one of the closest encounters by an interstellar object in recent years. NASA's Europa Clipper spacecraft captured an image of the comet, which was made public on December 18, one day before the flyby. The spacecraft is currently on its way to Jupiter's moon Europa.

Key facts

  • Closest approach: December 19, 2023
  • Distance from Earth: 168 million miles
  • Initial speed: 137,000 miles per hour
  • Final speed near the Sun: 153,000 miles per hour
  • Discovered: July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS telescope in Chile
  • Estimated age: 7.6 to 14 billion years
  • Next major pass: Jupiter in early 2026

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS telescope. NASA first observed the comet when it was traveling at a speed of 137,000 miles per hour. As it neared the Sun, its speed increased to 153,000 miles per hour.

The Europa Clipper spacecraft captured an image of the comet on November 6, 2023, from approximately 103 million miles away. This image was released to the public on December 18, a day before the comet's closest approach to Earth.

Official statements

Nasa describes 3I/ATLAS as a "cosmic snowball." When comets get close to the Sun, they heat up and form long tails that we usually see.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through our solar system. Unlike most comets that originate from the Kuiper Belt or the Oort Cloud, 3I/ATLAS comes from outside our solar system, making it potentially the oldest comet ever observed.

What’s next

3I/ATLAS will continue its journey through the solar system, passing Jupiter in early 2026, followed by Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune by 2028. It will eventually leave our solar system, not to return.

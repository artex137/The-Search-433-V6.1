NASA Releases Captivating Comet 3I/ATLAS Images from Spacecraft and Telescopes

NASA has announced a live event to showcase stunning images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS at the Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland, on Wednesday, November 19, at 3 p.m. EST. The comet, discovered on July 1, poses no threat to Earth, with its closest approach recorded at 170 million miles away.

This event is significant for astronomers and the general public, as it marks the opportunity to view high-quality images of a celestial object from another galaxy. NASA's extensive scientific assets, including spacecraft and ground-based observatories, allow researchers to monitor the comet's journey and study its behavior.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 19
  • Event time: 3 p.m. EST
  • Location: Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt, Maryland
  • Closest approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Closest approach to Mars: 19 million miles in early October
  • Key speakers include Amit Kshatriya and Nicky Fox

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, marking its entry into the solar system as the third recognized object from another galaxy. In early October, the comet passed within 19 million miles of Mars. The upcoming live event is scheduled for November 19, where NASA will present images and insights about the comet.

Official statements

“This event will provide a unique opportunity to engage with the science behind comet 3I/ATLAS,” said Nicky Fox, Associate Administrator for the Science Mission Directorate.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects entering our solar system. Its discovery has prompted further study into the origins and characteristics of such celestial bodies, expanding our understanding of the cosmos.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS with various instruments and will engage the public during the live event by addressing questions submitted via social media.

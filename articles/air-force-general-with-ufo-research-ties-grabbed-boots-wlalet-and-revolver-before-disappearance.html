Air Force general with 'UFO research' ties grabbed boots, wallet and revolver before disappearance

William Neil McCasland, a retired U.S. Air Force major general, has been missing since February 27 after leaving his Albuquerque home on foot around 11 a.m. McCasland's family has expressed concern for his wellbeing, as he has an unspecified medical issue. Authorities have activated a Silver Alert and are actively searching for him.

This case has garnered attention due to McCasland's previous association with UFO research, which has sparked conspiracy theories about his disappearance. His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, has clarified that while he worked briefly with Tom DeLonge, founder of the UFO organization To The Stars, he did not possess any special knowledge related to UFOs. Law enforcement agencies, including the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office and the FBI, are investigating his disappearance but have found no evidence of foul play.

Key facts

  • McCasland was reported missing on February 27.
  • He left his home in Albuquerque at approximately 11 a.m.
  • A Silver Alert was activated the following day.
  • Authorities do not suspect foul play.
  • Nearly 600 residents have been interviewed; no sightings reported.
  • McCasland has a medical issue, adding urgency to the search.
  • He is 5-foot-11, weighs about 160 pounds, has white hair, and blue eyes.

Timeline

On February 27, William Neil McCasland left his Albuquerque home on foot around 11 a.m. His family reported him missing later that day after not hearing from him. The next day, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office activated a Silver Alert to help locate him.

Investigators have since collaborated with the FBI and other agencies, utilizing drones, helicopter support, ground searches, and K-9 units in their efforts. As of March 6, no evidence of foul play has been uncovered, and the search continues.

Official statements

“Over the last week, investigators at the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office have worked hand in hand with the FBI and other partner agencies, and have so far uncovered no evidence of foul play,” said the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office in a statement. “We are, however, utilizing all possible resources, including advanced technologies, and still considering all possible scenarios as we follow up on leads towards locating Neil.”
“Our priority is finding Mr. McCasland safely,” said Sheriff John Allen. “Our investigators and search teams are working continuously, and we're coordinating closely with our local, state, and federal partners.”

Context

McCasland previously commanded a research division at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio, overseeing a significant budget for science and technology programs. His brief involvement with the UFO community has led to speculation about his disappearance, which authorities have sought to dispel.

Despite the attention surrounding his case, law enforcement has reiterated that they currently have no evidence to support any theories involving foul play.

What’s next

The search for McCasland will continue, with law enforcement agencies urging the public to assist by checking security camera footage and reporting any relevant information. Investigators remain committed to locating him safely.

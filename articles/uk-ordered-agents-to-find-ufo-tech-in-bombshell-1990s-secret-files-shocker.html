UK ordered agents to find UFO tech in bombshell 1990s secret files shocker

The British military actively sought extraterrestrial technology in the 1990s, according to newly unsealed files from the UK’s Defence Intelligence Staff. The investigation was prompted by thousands of reported sightings of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) over Belgium from November 1989 to April 1990.

This revelation is significant as it highlights the military's concern over the potential threat posed by UAPs. Current authorities have stated that the Ministry of Defence has not investigated UFO sightings since 2009 and has no plans to resume such investigations.

Key facts

  • Investigations were ordered after reports of UAPs in Belgium from November 1989 to April 1990.
  • Internal memos from March 1997 expressed concern over UAPs as potential threats.
  • Reports mentioned “large, silent, low-flying black triangles” demonstrating advanced flight capabilities.
  • The Rendlesham Forest incident in 1980 involved a glowing “craft of unknown origin.”
  • Most sightings were deemed not credible, but some could not be explained by conventional science.
  • The Ministry of Defence ceased investigations into UFOs in 2009.

Timeline

From November 1989 to April 1990, thousands reported sightings of UAPs over Belgium. In March 1997, an internal memo from the Defence Intelligence Staff raised concerns about the implications of these sightings. The memo suggested that the reported UAPs could represent advanced technology that should be investigated.

The Rendlesham Forest incident, referenced in the reports, occurred in December 1980 when a US Air Force base allegedly witnessed a glowing craft. The UK Ministry of Defence has since ceased investigations into such phenomena as of 2009.

Official statements

“Logic would indicate that if significant numbers are reporting seeing strange objects in the sky then there may be a basis in fact," an internal memo stated.
Luke Pollard, the UK’s minister for defense readiness, said in December 2024 that the Ministry of Defence had “ceased to investigate reports of UFOs or UAP in 2009” and noted there are “no current plans” to commit resources to this area.

Context

This investigation into UAPs follows a history of public interest and governmental inquiry into unidentified phenomena. Previous incidents, such as the Rendlesham Forest case, have sparked debates about the credibility of UFO sightings and the potential implications for national security.

Despite the cessation of investigations by the Ministry of Defence, there remains a lingering curiosity about UAPs and their possible implications for defense technology.

What’s next

As of now, there are no plans for further investigations into UFO sightings by the Ministry of Defence. Future concerns regarding UAPs may require international coordination, especially within security alliances.

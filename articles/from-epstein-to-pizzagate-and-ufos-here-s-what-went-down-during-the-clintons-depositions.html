From Epstein to pizzagate and UFOs, here’s what went down during the Clintons’ depositions

Bill Clinton and Hillary Clinton have testified before a House committee investigating Jeffrey Epstein, with both denying any wrongdoing or knowledge of his crimes. The depositions took place last week in Washington, D.C., amid an ongoing examination of Epstein's connections to high-profile figures.

The hearings have attracted significant attention due to the Clintons' prominent roles in American politics. Lawmakers are seeking accountability regarding Epstein's abuse and its far-reaching implications. The committee's investigation follows Epstein's death in 2019 while facing charges for sex trafficking and abusing minors.

Key facts

  • Depositions occurred on February 26 and 27, 2023.
  • Bill Clinton stated he first met Epstein in 2002 and described their relationship as "cordial."
  • Hillary Clinton repeatedly claimed she does not recall meeting Epstein.
  • The investigation has lasted six months.
  • Both Clintons have distanced themselves from Epstein, with Bill Clinton stating he saw no evidence of trafficking.
  • Republican Rep. Lauren Boebert interrupted Hillary Clinton’s deposition by leaking a photo online.
  • Hillary Clinton expressed frustration over repeated questions about her links to Epstein.
  • Both Clintons agreed to discuss the release of information on UFOs.

Timeline

On February 26, 2023, Hillary Clinton began her closed-door deposition. The session was briefly interrupted when Rep. Boebert leaked a photo, causing tension among the lawmakers. Clinton expressed her frustration with the repeated questioning regarding her connections to Epstein.

The following day, Bill Clinton testified, recounting his first meeting with Epstein and emphasizing the humanitarian nature of their interactions. He denied any knowledge of Epstein's criminal activities and stated he had never visited Epstein's private island.

Official statements

“I am done with this if you guys are doing that,” Hillary Clinton said during her deposition regarding the leaked photo.
“There’s nothing that I saw when I was around him that made me realize he was trafficking women,” Bill Clinton stated in response to questions about Epstein.
“This is an issue of real importance to so many people,” Hillary Clinton said when discussing the potential release of UFO information.

Context

Jeffrey Epstein was a financier who faced serious allegations of sex trafficking and abuse before his death. His connections to powerful figures have raised questions about accountability. In recent years, several high-profile individuals have resigned following revelations about their ties to Epstein and his former girlfriend, Ghislaine Maxwell.

The Clintons' depositions have ignited renewed interest in their past associations with Epstein, despite their assertions of having no knowledge of his crimes.

What’s next

The House committee continues its investigation into Epstein's network and the implications for those connected to him. Further hearings and potential disclosures regarding UFO information are anticipated in the coming weeks.

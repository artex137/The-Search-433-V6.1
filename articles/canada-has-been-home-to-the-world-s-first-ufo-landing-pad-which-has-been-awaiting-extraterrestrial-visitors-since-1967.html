Canada Has Been Home To The World’s First UFO Landing Pad, Which Has Been Awaiting Extraterrestrial Visitors Since 1967

The town of St. Paul in Northern Alberta has been home to the world’s first UFO landing pad since its completion in 1967. The landing pad was built to ensure that if extraterrestrial visitors ever arrive, they have a designated place to land. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the landing pad.

This unique attraction, constructed as part of Canada’s centennial celebrations, has become a symbol of hope for the community. Local authorities have embraced the alien theme, with numerous related activities and merchandise available for tourists. Residents maintain a lighthearted optimism that one day, real UFOs will land at the pad.

Key facts

  • Location: St. Paul, Northern Alberta
  • Completion Year: 1967
  • Structure Height: Six concrete pylons, each 75-centimeters (30-inches) tall
  • Design: Circular platform for easy landing of flying saucers
  • Local Attraction: Gift shop and alien-themed locations nearby

Timeline

In the 1960s, the town of St. Paul initiated the construction of the UFO landing pad as part of its celebration of Canada’s centennial. The large circular platform was completed in 1967, with local businesses getting involved in the theme. A local businessman even donated a bus called the “Martian Express” for transportation needs.

Since its opening, the only items reported to have landed on the platform are paper airplanes thrown by children, but residents remain hopeful for actual extraterrestrial visitors.

Official statements

“The area under the world’s first UFO landing pad was designated international by the town of St. Paul as a symbol of our faith that mankind will maintain the outer universe free from national wars and strife.”

Context

The UFO landing pad serves as a whimsical attraction, reflecting the community's playful approach to the topic of extraterrestrial life. It aligns with a broader trend of towns and cities embracing unique themes to attract tourism. The positive message associated with the pad emphasizes peace and welcome for all beings, whether terrestrial or extraterrestrial.

What’s next

As of now, there are no ongoing investigations or plans for changes to the landing pad. The community continues to celebrate its whimsical attraction and remains open to the possibility of future intergalactic visitors.

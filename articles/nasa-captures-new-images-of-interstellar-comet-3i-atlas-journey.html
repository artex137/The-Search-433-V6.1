NASA captures new images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS journey

NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope has captured new images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system on November 30, 2025. The comet, which poses no threat to Earth, was last recorded about 178 million miles from the planet.

The observations are significant as they allow scientists to study this rare interstellar object more closely. Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in the summer of 2025 by the NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile. NASA officials say it is the third known object to pass through the solar system from outside, and they aim to gather as much data as possible before it exits the solar system.

Key facts

  • Date of Hubble's observation: November 30, 2025
  • Last recorded distance from Earth: 178 million miles
  • First detection: Summer 2025
  • Location of initial detection: Rio Hurtado, Chile
  • Trajectory type: Hyperbolic
  • Number of known interstellar objects: Three

Timeline

NASA first detected interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS in the summer of 2025 using the ATLAS survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile. Following its discovery, scientists analyzed its trajectory and velocity, confirming its interstellar nature. On November 30, 2025, Hubble captured detailed images of the comet, providing astronomers with valuable data.

“So we use all the tools at our disposal to observe it before it disappears back into the cosmic dark,” said NASA, highlighting the collaborative efforts to study the comet.

Context

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third known object to enter our solar system from outside. Its unique trajectory means it will not return, offering a rare opportunity for scientists to study an object from another star system. Previous interstellar objects, such as Oumuamua and Borisov, have also provided insights into the nature of cosmic bodies beyond our solar system.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue observations of 3I/ATLAS for several more months. The agency’s space telescopes will work together to enhance understanding of this interstellar object and gather further data on solar system dynamics.

Following UFO Expert Disappearance, New Mexico Residents Asked to Hand Over Home Security Footage

William Neil McCasland, a 68-year-old retired Air Force general and UFO expert, has been missing from his home in New Mexico since February 27. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has requested that more than 600 residents in the area provide any security camera footage that may assist in locating him.

The request comes as the search for McCasland enters its second week. Authorities, including the FBI, have been seeking information from the public. The FBI has specifically asked for video footage that could have captured McCasland’s whereabouts around the time of his disappearance.

Key facts

  • Date of disappearance: February 27
  • Age of McCasland: 68
  • Location: New Mexico
  • Number of residents contacted: over 600
  • Agencies involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, FBI
  • Type of alert issued: Silver Alert

Timeline

On February 27, William Neil McCasland went missing from his home in New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert shortly after his disappearance. In the following week, the FBI reached out to the public for information and video footage that might help locate McCasland.

Official statements

“We are asking for your help in finding him. We believe there are people who have information valuable to locating Neil who have not yet spoken to law enforcement,” the FBI stated.

Context

McCasland's disappearance has raised concerns due to his background as a former commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory. He has been associated with investigations into UFO activity during his military career. His name has also appeared in communications related to UFO discussions, notably between former White House chief of staff John Podesta and musician Tom DeLonge.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office and the FBI continue their search for McCasland, seeking leads from the public and reviewing security footage submitted by residents.

Space ETF (UFO) Hits New 52-Week High

Procure Space ETF (UFO) has reached a 52-week high, trading significantly above its low price of $17.72 per share, marking an increase of 89.1%. This milestone has attracted attention from investors looking for momentum in the space sector.

The rise in UFO’s value reflects growing investor interest in space-related industries, driven by recent achievements from notable companies such as SpaceX. Authorities and market analysts are monitoring the ETF's performance, considering its potential to attract more investments in the coming months.

Key facts

  • UFO hit a 52-week high on October 26, 2023.
  • Current price is up 89.1% from its 52-week low of $17.72/share.
  • The fund charges 94 basis points in annual fees.
  • SpaceX launched its Starship rocket on August 26, 2025, completing a successful payload deployment.
  • UFO has a weighted alpha of 82.17, indicating strong performance potential.

Timeline

On August 26, 2025, SpaceX achieved a major milestone with the launch of its Starship rocket, which successfully deployed eight dummy Starlink satellites. This event marked a significant turning point in the space sector, contributing to increased investor interest.

In the following weeks, as UFO's value surged, the ETF attracted attention from market analysts and investors alike, prompting discussions about its future performance.

Official statements

“With SpaceX’s progress, fresh IPOs, and government support, the space sector is drawing investors’ attention,” an analyst noted.

Context

The Procure Space ETF seeks to track the performance of the S-Network Space Index, which includes companies engaged in space-related activities. The recent success of SpaceX and other companies has revitalized interest in the sector, leading to increased investments in space-focused ETFs.

Prior to this, UFO had seen fluctuations in its value, but the recent milestones have spurred a positive outlook among investors and analysts.

What’s next

Market analysts will continue to monitor UFO's performance closely, assessing its potential for further gains as the space sector evolves and new opportunities arise.

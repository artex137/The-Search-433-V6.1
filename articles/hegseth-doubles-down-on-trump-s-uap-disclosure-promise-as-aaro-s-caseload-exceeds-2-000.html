Hegseth doubles-down on Trump’s UAP disclosure promise as AARO’s caseload exceeds 2,000

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has confirmed the Pentagon's commitment to fulfill President Donald Trump’s promise to release U.S. government records about unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP) and aliens. Hegseth made his remarks during a stop on his "Arsenal of Freedom" tour in Colorado on February 25, 2026.

This announcement comes as the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) has reported a significant increase in its caseload, which now exceeds 2,000 UAP cases. The Pentagon has stated that it is preparing to comply with an executive order related to UAP transparency and is working closely with the Office of the Director of National Intelligence to expedite this process.

Key facts

  • 2,000+ UAP cases currently under examination by AARO.
  • AARO has approximately 1,000 reports that lack sufficient data for analysis.
  • AARO’s caseload has increased by at least 400 since late 2024.
  • The office has not published its 2025 annual report.
  • Hegseth stated that the Pentagon is eager to provide information to the president.

Timeline

In 2022, the AARO was established to manage UAP investigations as mandated by the National Defense Authorization Act. The office was created in response to concerns about potential high-tech threats from U.S. adversaries.

In late 2024, AARO reported having received more than 1,600 UAP reports. As of February 2026, this number has grown to over 2,000 cases.

On February 25, 2026, Hegseth publicly discussed the Pentagon's commitment to UAP transparency during a tour stop in Colorado.

Official statements

“We’re going to be in full compliance with that executive order, [and we’re] eager to provide that for the president. So, there’ll be more coming on that, as far as the process of what we’ll do,” Hegseth said.
“AARO has been examining over 2,000 UAP cases,” Pentagon spokesperson Sue Gough stated.
“Trust is earned. And if AARO wants to earn the public’s trust, then it should move expeditiously to release its trove of unclassified videos and photos,” said Jordan Flowers, executive director of the Disclosure Foundation.

Context

The AARO has faced scrutiny for its lack of public disclosures and has not released any unclassified reports in over a year. Critics have expressed concerns about the office's transparency and its ability to meet statutory obligations.

Former President Barack Obama recently commented on the existence of aliens, which has reignited public and congressional interest in UAP disclosures. Trump's renewed commitment to transparency may impact AARO's operations moving forward.

What’s next

The Pentagon will begin a process to identify and release government files related to UAP and extraterrestrial life, as directed by Trump. Hegseth has indicated that the Department of Defense will work closely with other federal agencies to facilitate this initiative.

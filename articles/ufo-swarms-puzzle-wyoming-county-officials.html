UFO Swarms Puzzle Wyoming County Officials

Mysterious swarms of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) have appeared over Sweetwater County, Wyoming, since December 2025, according to a local media report. The sightings began near the Jim Bridger Power Plant and the Red Desert, with no injuries or fatalities reported. Local authorities have been unable to determine the nature of the illuminated aerial objects.

The situation has raised concerns among officials, who are actively investigating the phenomenon. Jason Mower of the Sweetwater County Sheriff's Office stated that the UFOs have become so common that residents no longer report sightings to the police. He emphasized that the objects have not posed any danger to the public, but authorities are prepared to respond should that change.

Key facts

  • First sightings reported in December 2025.
  • Locations include Jim Bridger Power Plant and Red Desert, Sweetwater County.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.
  • Similar UFO swarms noted in another part of Wyoming starting in late 2024.
  • Wyoming National Guard leader Greg Porter addressed UFO inquiries during a state legislature meeting.

Timeline

In December 2025, residents began reporting sightings of UFO swarms over Sweetwater County. Jason Mower from the local sheriff's office acknowledged the ongoing mystery surrounding these objects. The sightings have persisted for over a year, leading to a sense of normalcy among residents.

Prior to the Sweetwater County reports, similar UFO activity was observed in another region of Wyoming from late 2024 to early 2025. This earlier phenomenon stopped suddenly, but details remain unclear.

Official statements

“We’ve done everything we can to figure out what they are, and nobody wants to give us any answers,” said Jason Mower of the Sweetwater County Sheriff's Office.
“It’s like the new normal,” Mower added, referring to residents’ indifference toward the sightings.
Greg Porter, leader of the Wyoming National Guard, remarked that he was aware of incidents near federal facilities but could not publicly discuss them.

Context

UFO sightings have gained increased attention in recent years, with various reports surfacing across the United States. The Sweetwater County incidents follow a trend of heightened public interest and government scrutiny regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs).

In Wyoming, the ongoing sightings have prompted discussions among state officials, including inquiries about UAPs in airspace over the state. Despite the lack of concrete evidence or explanations, the phenomenon has captured the curiosity of local residents and officials alike.

What’s next

Authorities will continue their investigation into the UFO swarms. The Sweetwater County Sheriff's Office remains vigilant and prepared to take action should the situation change or pose a threat to public safety.

Ronny LeBlanc explores Bigfoot and UFOs at Peterborough

Ronny LeBlanc will host a free talk titled “Bigfoot and UFOs” at the Peterborough Town Library on Wednesday, October 8, from 6:30 to 7:30 p.m. The event will delve into the connection between Bigfoot sightings and UFO encounters, featuring stories, video evidence, and theories.

This event matters as it provides an opportunity for community members to engage with the exploration of unexplained phenomena. LeBlanc, a notable figure in the field of cryptozoology and the paranormal, aims to challenge conventional explanations and spark curiosity among attendees.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 8, from 6:30 to 7:30 p.m.
  • Location: Peterborough Town Library, 2 Concord St., Peterborough.
  • Speaker: Ronny LeBlanc, author and researcher.
  • Contact: 603-924-8040 for more details.

Timeline

Ronny LeBlanc, a native of Leominster, Massachusetts, has spent decades researching cryptozoology and the paranormal. He has published bestselling books, including his “Monsterland” series, which serve as a foundation for his discussions on Bigfoot and UFOs.

On October 8, he will present his findings and insights at the Peterborough Town Library, engaging with attendees on the mysterious overlap between these two phenomena.

Official statements

“Attendees can expect incredible stories, compelling video evidence, and thought-provoking theories that challenge conventional explanations,” LeBlanc said.

Context

LeBlanc is known for his appearances on shows such as “Expedition Bigfoot” and “Paranormal Caught on Camera.” His expertise has made him a sought-after speaker at paranormal and UFO conferences across the U.S. and Europe, contributing to ongoing discussions about unexplained phenomena.

What’s next

The event at the Peterborough Town Library will allow attendees to engage with LeBlanc's research and theories, fostering further exploration of the unknown in future discussions and events.

Humboldt Gallery to host UFO Night for local crafters Oct. 15

The Humboldt & District Gallery will host its next UFO (Unfinished Objects) Night on Wednesday, Oct. 15, from 6 to 8 p.m. There are no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this event.

This event is significant as it provides local crafters with an opportunity to complete unfinished projects in a supportive and social environment. According to Mackenzie Hopp, cultural programmer, the gallery encourages participants to bring their own materials, as supplies will not be provided.

Key facts

  • Event: UFO Night (Unfinished Objects)
  • Date: Wednesday, Oct. 15
  • Time: 6 to 8 p.m.
  • Location: Humboldt & District Gallery, Humboldt, Saskatchewan
  • Organizer: Humboldt & District Museum and Gallery staff
  • Materials: Participants must bring their own craft supplies

Timeline

The Humboldt & District Gallery has hosted UFO Nights to foster creativity and community engagement. This edition is part of an ongoing series aimed at encouraging local artists and crafters.

The gallery was designated a municipal heritage property in 2011 and reopened as a professional art gallery in January 2014 after renovations.

Official statements

Mackenzie Hopp, cultural programmer, stated, “UFO Nights offer a chance to connect with other creative individuals in the community while finding motivation to complete long-standing projects.”

Context

UFO Nights have become a staple for local crafters in Humboldt, allowing individuals to share ideas and support one another in their creative endeavours. The Humboldt & District Gallery plays a crucial role in promoting local culture and history.

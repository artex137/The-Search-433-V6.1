3I/ATLAS Comet News Today (24 November 2025)

On November 24, 2025, interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has garnered attention due to new scientific findings and viral speculation, while another comet, ATLAS C/2025 K1, is reported to be breaking apart. NASA has released images of 3I/ATLAS captured from Mars orbit, which were delayed due to a government shutdown.

This release is significant as it provides detailed insights into the comet's composition and behavior. Authorities, including NASA, are actively addressing public curiosity and speculation regarding the comet's nature, particularly following recent viral videos and reports suggesting unusual activity.

Key facts

  • Date: November 24, 2025
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is currently about 1.99 AU (≈297 million km) from Earth.
  • NASA's Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter captured images of the comet's coma and jets.
  • ATLAS C/2025 K1 is reported to be splitting into at least three fragments.
  • 3I/ATLAS passed its closest point to the Sun on October 29, 2025.
  • Its closest approach to Earth will occur on December 19, 2025.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, 3I/ATLAS was discovered by the NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Chile.

The comet passed its closest point to the Sun on October 29, 2025, at approximately 1.36 AU.

On November 19, 2025, NASA held a press conference to clarify that 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet and poses no threat to Earth.

On November 24, 2025, NASA released images of 3I/ATLAS taken from Mars orbit, revealing its coma and jets.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS is a comet, not an alien spacecraft, and it poses no danger to Earth,” a NASA spokesperson stated during a recent press event.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through our solar system, following 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The comet's unusual characteristics have sparked both scientific interest and public speculation about its origins.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze data from its fleet of spacecraft observing 3I/ATLAS and will provide further updates as new information becomes available.

NASA Responds Brilliantly To President Trump's Latest Talk Of UFOs, UAPs, And Aliens

Published 2026-02-23 16:38 UTC

NASA Responds to President Trump's Comments on UFOs

NASA has responded to President Donald Trump's recent comments regarding unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). The remarks came during a flight to Georgia on February 2026, following President Barack Obama’s statements about extraterrestrial life on a podcast.

This discourse highlights a growing public interest in the topic of UFOs, with both presidents discussing the matter publicly. Authorities, including NASA, have committed to transparency regarding government information on UAPs and UFOs.

Key facts

  • Event date: February 2026
  • Location: United States
  • Participants: President Donald Trump, President Barack Obama
  • NASA Press Secretary: Bethany Stevens
  • Public interest: Increased discussions on UFOs and UAPs

Timeline

In February 2026, President Barack Obama discussed the possibility of extraterrestrial life during a podcast hosted by Brian Tyler Cohen. Obama stated, "They’re real, but I haven’t seen them, and they’re not being kept in [Area 51]." He later clarified that his comments referred to the likelihood of life existing elsewhere in the universe, not evidence of alien visitation on Earth.

Following Obama's remarks, President Trump reacted on a flight to Georgia, claiming that Obama had "made a big mistake" and accused him of disclosing classified information. Trump announced plans to direct relevant government agencies to release more information about UFOs and UAPs.

Official statements

“We continue to embrace President Trump’s open science commitment as an agency. We have fostered open science since our inception so that the public can build upon our innovations.”
— Bethany Stevens, NASA Press Secretary
“There are certainly things he’s come across in the job that he can’t explain… but they relate more to unnecessarily costly programs than they do to extraterrestrial life!”
— Jared Isaacman, NASA Administrator

Context

In recent years, the US government has sought to increase transparency regarding UFOs and UAPs. Public hearings in Congress have allowed witnesses and officials to discuss sightings and investigations. Some incidents have been explained as weather balloons, while others remain unexplained.

NASA has a policy of releasing most of its data to the public, although certain classified information is withheld. This includes procurement sensitive information and data subject to the Privacy Act.

What’s next

NASA and other US agencies are expected to continue their efforts to disclose information related to UFOs and UAPs. Investigations into these phenomena will likely persist as public interest remains high.

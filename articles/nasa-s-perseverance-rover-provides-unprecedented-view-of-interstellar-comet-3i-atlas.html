NASA's Perseverance rover provides unprecedented view of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA’s Perseverance rover has captured a historic image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS in the Martian sky on October 4, 2025. This faint streak of light marks the first time a Mars rover has directly observed an interstellar object.

This observation is significant as it provides rare data on interstellar objects, helping scientists refine their studies of solar system and extrasolar materials. The comet, discovered by the ATLAS survey telescope in Chile, has a trajectory that aligns with predictions for its solar system flyby. NASA’s Perseverance and other telescopes, including Hubble and the James Webb Space Telescope, have tracked the comet since its discovery.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025.
  • The image was taken from Jezero Crater on Mars.
  • The observation occurred on October 4, 2025.
  • Perseverance used its Right Navigation Camera (Navcam) for imaging.
  • The comet's hyperbolic orbit indicates it originated from outside the solar system.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered by the ATLAS survey telescope in Chile. Following its discovery, NASA's Perseverance rover and orbiting telescopes began tracking the object. On October 4, 2025, Perseverance captured an image of the comet as it passed through the Martian sky.

Official statements

“Imaging 3I/ATLAS on Mars provides rare data on interstellar objects,” a NASA scientist stated. “Insights from the comet help refine studies of solar system and extrasolar materials.”

Context

The observation from Jezero Crater offers a unique vantage point under clear Martian skies, supporting long-term scientific studies and high-profile astronomical events. This discovery enhances the understanding of cosmic migration and the building blocks of different star systems.

What’s next

Researchers will analyze the data collected from this observation to guide future studies of interstellar objects. New missions may be planned for closer approaches and detailed spectrometry of similar objects.

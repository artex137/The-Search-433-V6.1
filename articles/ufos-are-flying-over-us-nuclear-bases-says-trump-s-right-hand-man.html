UFOs are flying over US nuclear bases, says Trump’s right hand man

UFOs have been reported flying over U.S. nuclear bases, according to Marco Rubio, a prominent figure in former President Donald Trump's administration. The claims have surfaced recently, sparking discussions about national security and potential threats.

This revelation has raised concerns among lawmakers and military officials. Authorities are now investigating these incidents to assess any risks posed to national security and to determine the origins of these unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Reported sightings of UFOs near U.S. nuclear bases.
  • Marco Rubio has publicly acknowledged these incidents.
  • Authorities are conducting investigations into the sightings.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported related to these incidents.

Timeline

In recent weeks, multiple reports have emerged regarding UFO sightings near various U.S. nuclear facilities. While specific dates and locations have not been disclosed, the frequency of these occurrences has prompted attention from lawmakers.

Following these reports, Marco Rubio has made statements emphasizing the need for a thorough investigation. He has urged the government to take these sightings seriously, given their potential implications for national security.

Official statements

“We need to understand what these are and whether they pose a threat to our national security,” said Rubio.

Context

UFO sightings have been a topic of interest for decades, particularly in relation to military installations. Previous incidents have led to increased scrutiny of aerial phenomena, with some lawmakers advocating for more transparency from the government regarding these sightings.

What’s next

Investigations into the recent UFO sightings are ongoing, with authorities expected to release findings in the coming weeks. Lawmakers are calling for regular updates to ensure public awareness and safety.

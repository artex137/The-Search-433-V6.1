Nasa plans 2025 mission to study enigmatic 3I Atlas comet remnants

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (Nasa) has announced plans for a comprehensive observation campaign in 2025 to study the dispersed remnants of comet C/2019 Y4 ATLAS, commonly referred to as 3I Atlas. The mission will focus on the debris field left behind after the comet's dramatic breakup in April 2020.

This initiative aims to provide crucial insights into the processes of cometary fragmentation and the conditions of the early solar system. Nasa plans to utilize advanced telescopic capabilities and a variety of observational tools to collect data on the comet's remnants. This research is expected to enhance predictive models for future cometary events.

Key facts

  • Mission planned for 2025 by Nasa.
  • Focus on remnants of comet 3I Atlas, which disintegrated in April 2020.
  • Utilization of the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) for high-resolution imaging.
  • Collaboration with the European Space Agency (ESA) and various national observatories.
  • Data collection will include spectral profiles and tracking of fragment trajectories.

Timeline

3I Atlas was discovered in December 2019 by the ATLAS survey system. The comet rapidly brightened and subsequently broke apart into dozens of fragments in April 2020. The fragmentation of 3I Atlas offered a rare opportunity to study its interior material, usually hidden beneath its icy crust.

The upcoming 2025 campaign is designed to analyze these fragments, utilizing both ground-based and space-based telescopes to gather a comprehensive dataset on their composition and dynamics. The mission seeks to clarify the mechanisms behind the comet's breakup.

Official statements

“The 2025 campaign will employ a diverse array of observational tools and methodologies, ensuring a multifaceted approach to data collection,” said a Nasa spokesperson.

Context

Studying fragmented comets like 3I Atlas is invaluable for understanding the early solar system’s building blocks. When a comet breaks apart, it exposes materials preserved since the solar system's infancy, providing direct clues about its composition and conditions.

This specific mission aims to test various theories regarding the comet's breakup, which include thermal stress from solar proximity and internal structural weaknesses. The results will contribute to ongoing debates within the planetary science community.

What’s next

Nasa is preparing for the 2025 observation campaign, coordinating with international partners and planning the deployment of various observational resources. The mission will enhance understanding of cometary behavior and help inform the design of future space missions targeting comets and asteroids.

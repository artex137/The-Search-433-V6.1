Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Imaged by China's Tianwen 1 Mars Orbiter

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has been imaged by China's Tianwen 1 Mars orbiter during its close pass by Mars from October 1 to October 4, 2025. The images reveal significant features of the comet, which was first discovered in July 2025, and made its closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025.

The China National Space Administration (CNSA) has stated that the imaging of 3I/ATLAS contributes to global efforts to study this rare interstellar visitor. The successful capture of images demonstrates Tianwen 1's capabilities and provides valuable data for future missions in China's deep-space exploration program.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is 3.5 miles (5.6 kilometers) wide.
  • It travels at a speed of 129,800 mph (58 kilometers per second).
  • The comet was located about 18 million miles (29 million kilometers) from Earth during imaging.
  • Images were captured from October 1 to October 4, 2025.
  • CNSA published the images on November 5, 2025.
  • Tianwen 1 entered Mars orbit in February 2021.

Timeline

On July 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS is discovered as the third known interstellar object to visit the solar system. It approaches the Sun, reaching its closest point on October 29, 2025. Between October 1 and October 4, 2025, China's Tianwen 1 Mars orbiter captures images of the comet, which are later published by CNSA on November 5, 2025.

Official statements

“The comet imaging project was an important extension mission for Tianwen 1,” said a CNSA representative. “It provided a chance to perform useful technical tests and accumulate experience for the second mission in China's deep-space exploration series.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third object confirmed to have traveled from another star to our solar system, following ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Its unexpected brightness as it nears the Sun has raised questions among astronomers. Researchers have noted that the comet's brightening far exceeds typical long-period comets, with the cause still being investigated.

What’s next

Further analysis of the images and the comet's behavior is ongoing. Researchers continue to explore the unusual brightening of 3I/ATLAS and its implications for understanding interstellar objects.

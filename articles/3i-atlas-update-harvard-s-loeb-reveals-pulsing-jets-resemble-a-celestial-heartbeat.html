3I/ATLAS Update: Harvard's Loeb Reveals Pulsing Jets Resemble a Celestial Heartbeat

Harvard University’s Avi Loeb has reported that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, discovered on July 1, 2025, exhibits a unique pulsing light pattern resembling a cosmic heartbeat, with a period of 16.16 hours. This finding challenges conventional cometary physics and raises questions about the object’s nature.

The discovery of this rhythmic variability is significant as it could indicate whether 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet or a technological object. The scientific community is now focused on capturing detailed observations to better understand the source of the light and the mechanics behind the pulsing jets.

Key facts

  • Discovery date: July 1, 2025
  • Pulsation period: 16.16 hours
  • Amplitude of variability: Approximately 0.3 magnitudes
  • Outflow velocity of jets: 440 metres per second
  • Distance jets can reach: 25,600 kilometres in 16.16 hours
  • Key researcher: Avi Loeb, Frank B. Baird, Jr., Professor of Science at Harvard University

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2025. The light signature showing a pulsing effect was formally reported in August 2025 by researchers including T. Santana-Ross. Observations from the Hubble Space Telescope on July 21, 2025, revealed that most of the light originates from a coma surrounding the nucleus, not the nucleus itself.

Since then, images have shown multiple jets of material streaming from the object, suggesting a periodic mass loss that could explain the observed light variations.

Official statements

“The only scientifically sound explanation for such large periodic light variations is that the extensive coma itself must be pulsating in brightness,” said Loeb.
“This mechanism resembles a heartbeat with a puff of gas and dust serving the role of a stream of 'blood' through the coma,” Loeb added.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS follows a trend of increasing interest in interstellar objects. Previous findings have prompted scientists to explore the possibility that such objects may be technological artefacts rather than natural phenomena. This ongoing research aims to deepen our understanding of the origins and behaviors of these celestial bodies.

What’s next

Researchers will conduct systematic, high-resolution observations of 3I/ATLAS to capture the pulsating jets and determine their orientation relative to the sun. This data will help clarify whether the object is a natural comet or exhibits signs of technological origins.

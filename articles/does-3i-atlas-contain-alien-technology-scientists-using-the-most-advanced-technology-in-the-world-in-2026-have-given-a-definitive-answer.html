Scientists Find No Evidence of Alien Technology in Comet 3I/ATLAS

Researchers have conducted high-sensitivity radio observations of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS on December 18, 2025, using the Green Bank Telescope, and found no evidence of technosignatures or artificial signals associated with the comet. The object was discovered on July 1, 2025, and has since generated significant interest among scientists and the public regarding the possibility of advanced extraterrestrial civilizations.

The findings are important as they contradict speculation surrounding 3I/ATLAS. Authorities, including multiple research teams, are continuing to study the object using various telescopes to ensure thorough investigation and monitoring of potential signals from other interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS discovered on July 1, 2025.
  • High-sensitivity observations conducted on December 18, 2025.
  • Observations performed by the Green Bank Telescope as part of the Breakthrough Listen program.
  • No evidence of artificial signals found.
  • Over 471,000 signals were initially identified, later reduced to nine potential candidates.
  • All identified signals attributed to radio frequency interference.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, 3I/ATLAS was identified as an interstellar object. Following its discovery, speculation arose regarding its potential to harbor signs of extraterrestrial life. On December 18, 2025, researchers conducted radio observations using the Green Bank Telescope, focusing on detecting narrowband signals, which are more likely to indicate artificial communication.

After identifying more than 471,000 signals, the researchers filtered these down to nine potential signals. However, further analysis showed that all signals were consistent with known interference, leading to the conclusion that no technosignatures were detected.

Official statements

“Despite the high sensitivity of our measurements, we found no evidence of artificial transmissions associated with 3I/ATLAS,” the study authors stated. “This reinforces the understanding that interstellar objects are natural astrophysical bodies.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object observed passing through the solar system. The first two were 1I/‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Speculation about the nature of these objects has often led to public interest in potential extraterrestrial technology. The new findings align with previous research indicating that these objects are likely natural phenomena.

What’s next

Research teams will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS and other interstellar objects using various telescopes, including the Hubble Space Telescope. While the current findings suggest a natural origin, scientists emphasize the importance of ongoing vigilance in the search for potential technosignatures.

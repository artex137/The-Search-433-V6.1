Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS displays blue and green tones and intense brightness in the Sun

Published 2025-11-06 06:00 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS Displays Color Changes Near the Sun

Comet 3I/ATLAS has exhibited significant color changes and brightness during its perihelion close to the Sun on October 29, 2025. The interstellar object, detected in July, has shown an increase in brightness that is twice as high as initially projected. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

Astronomers have monitored the comet since its discovery, noting its rapid speed of over 210,000 km/h. Solar satellites have captured the changes in color, which indicate variations in the comet's chemical composition. Observations will continue in December from the Northern Hemisphere.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS detected in July 2025
  • Perihelion occurred on October 29, 2025
  • Brightness reached magnitude 9 at perihelion
  • Speed exceeds 210,000 km/h
  • Color changes: reddish in July, green in September, blue in October
  • Observations will continue in December 2025

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2025, initially displaying a reddish hue due to dust in its coma. In September, the color shifted to green. By October, as the comet approached perihelion, it exhibited a blue hue. Observations captured by solar satellites indicated that the changes reflect chemical reactions on the comet's surface.

Official statements

“The changes in color and brightness are significant indicators of the comet's chemical composition,” said an astronomer involved in the study.

Context

This is not the first time an interstellar object has been observed changing characteristics as it approaches the Sun. The hyperbolic orbit of 3I/ATLAS confirms its interstellar nature, providing valuable insight into the composition of objects from distant star systems.

What’s next

Further observations are scheduled for December 2025 as astronomers continue to study the comet's behavior and chemical composition.

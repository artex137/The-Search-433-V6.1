A 17 Year Comet Is Firing Off Radio Signals. What Does That Mean?

A 17 Year Comet Is Firing Off Radio Signals. What Does That Mean?

Researchers have detected unusual radio signals from Comet 12P/Pons-Brooks as it approaches Earth in 2024. The Tianma Radio Telescope in China recorded bursts of hydroxyl, a byproduct of sunlight interacting with water molecules, during the comet's latest orbit.

This discovery has sparked interest among scientists, who are examining the implications of these signals. The detection may provide insights into the comet's composition and its potential role in delivering water to Earth in the past.

Key facts

  • Comet 12P/Pons-Brooks has been observed since 1812.
  • Radio signals detected by the Tianma Radio Telescope during its 2024 pass.
  • Over five tons of hydroxyl released at a distance of one astronomical unit.
  • First detection of ammonia in a Halley-type comet.
  • Research led by a NASA team, including Martin Cordiner.

Timeline

Comet 12P/Pons-Brooks was first identified in 1812. In 2024, astronomers utilized the Tianma Radio Telescope to examine the comet as it passed near Earth. The telescope detected bursts of radio signals, including hydroxyl and ammonia, indicating volatile activity beneath the comet's surface.

Previous studies have shown that 12P's water has a similar isotopic signature to Earth's water, suggesting a potential link between comets and the origins of water on our planet.

Official statements

“The detection hints at hidden pockets of volatile materials beneath the crust that could be venting under solar heat,” said a researcher involved in the study.

Context

Comets have long been of interest to scientists studying the origins of water and organic materials on Earth. The discovery of ammonia in a Halley-type comet is particularly noteworthy, as it may indicate new avenues for understanding the chemistry of celestial bodies.

Previous observations of comets have suggested they may have played a significant role in delivering essential compounds to early Earth, contributing to the development of life.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue monitoring Comet 12P/Pons-Brooks for further data and insights, which may enhance understanding of its activity and implications for planetary science.

UFO mania grips small town after mysterious glowing object sighting goes viral

Residents of a small town near the Finger Lakes in upstate New York have reported a glowing, pill-shaped object in the sky on August 12, 2025. The sighting has sparked widespread speculation about UFOs, although it has since been identified as a rocket launch.

The event has captured the attention of the community, leading to discussions in local social media groups. Authorities, including military experts, emphasize the importance of scientific verification in distinguishing between unidentified aerial phenomena and conventional explanations.

Key facts

  • Sighting occurred on August 12, 2025, near the Finger Lakes, New York.
  • The object was confirmed to be a rocket launch by the United Launch Alliance.
  • Photos of the object were shared by resident Tanicia Miller.
  • Rear Admiral (ret.) Tim Gallaudet commented on the sighting, emphasizing the need for data verification.
  • The Pentagon has not responded to requests for comment regarding the incident.

Timeline

On August 12, 2025, residents near the Finger Lakes observed a glowing, pill-shaped object in the night sky. Local social media exploded with speculation, with some calling it "otherworldly." Later, the object was identified as the Vulcan Centaur rocket launch from Cape Canaveral, Florida, according to reports from Space.com.

Tanicia Miller captured the phenomenon in photographs, which fueled further discussion among community members. Military experts have since weighed in, urging a scientific approach to verifying such sightings.

Official statements

“My first instinct from my scientific and military training is to look for a conventional explanation, such as a meteorological or human-made aerospace explanation, because that's what many objects in the sky are,” said Rear Admiral (ret.) Tim Gallaudet. “In this case, it was both.”
“We tend to fear the unknown, and so more UAP research can not only reduce the stigma associated with the phenomena, but also transform their enigma status to accepted science,” Gallaudet added.

Context

The recent sighting comes amid a rise in reported UFO sightings across the state, with over 60 incidents noted in the first half of 2025, according to a national tracking group. This has led to increased interest in the scientific study of unidentified aerial phenomena.

Experts stress the need for transparency and rigorous investigation to distinguish between misidentified objects and genuine UAPs.

What’s next

Authorities and experts continue to advocate for improved scientific methods in investigating aerial phenomena. The Pentagon's response to the incident remains pending, with no further updates disclosed.

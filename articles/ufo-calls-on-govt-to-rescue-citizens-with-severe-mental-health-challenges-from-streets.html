UFO calls on govt to rescue citizens with severe mental health challenges from streets

The Universal Friendship Organisation (UFO) has made an urgent appeal to the Government of Ghana and health authorities, demanding immediate intervention to remove and rehabilitate individuals with severe mental health challenges living on the streets. The organisation has described the situation as a "national crisis and a humanitarian failure."

UFO's call to action highlights the risks posed to public safety and human dignity by the growing visibility of these vulnerable citizens. Mr. Samuel Adobah, President of UFO, has noted that many individuals with mental health conditions are surviving in unsafe environments, leading to incidents of public defecation, substance abuse, and dangerous behaviours such as lighting uncontrolled fires.

Key facts

  • UFO has issued a statement calling for immediate government intervention.
  • Mr. Samuel Adobah is the President of UFO.
  • The appeal includes demands for both emergency and long-term shelters.
  • UFO calls for increased access to professional mental health care.
  • Existing frameworks like the Mental Health Authority Act are reportedly not fully implemented.
  • UFO advocates for a National Mental Health Check-ups Day.

Timeline

Recently, UFO has conducted field observations across communities revealing a grim reality for individuals with mental health challenges. The organisation has documented incidents of unsafe living conditions among these citizens.

In response, UFO has called on various government bodies, including His Excellency the President, the Ministry of Health, the Ghana Health Service, and the Mental Health Authority, to coordinate an emergency response.

Official statements

“We are witnessing a tragedy unfold in real-time,” said Mr. Adobah. “We have abandoned our most vulnerable citizens to the streets. This is not only a security risk to the public, who are exposed to unpredictable behavior and unsafe conditions, but it is a profound violation of the rights and dignity of these individuals.”
“This is a test of our national character,” Mr. Adobah concluded. “Together, we must act decisively not just to clear the streets, but to heal the wounds of our society. True friendship and peace begin with how we treat our most vulnerable.”

Context

The Universal Friendship Organisation (UFO) has a history of advocating for the inclusion, dignity, and protection of marginalized groups in Ghana. The current appeal highlights ongoing concerns regarding mental health care and the treatment of homeless individuals in the country.

Despite the existence of the Mental Health Authority Act, UFO has criticized the gaps in its implementation, which they believe contribute significantly to the crisis facing individuals with mental health conditions.

What’s next

UFO has pledged its support to the government and stakeholders and is urging civil society, religious groups, and the private sector to collaborate in addressing the mental health crisis. The organisation is seeking a coordinated emergency response to safeguard the rights and dignity of vulnerable citizens.

Comet 3I/Atlas reaches speed of 57 km/s and confirms origin outside our solar system

The comet 3I/Atlas has reached a speed of 57 kilometres per second as it travels through space, confirming its origin from outside our solar system. This event has been observed by astronomers at various observatories worldwide.

This discovery is significant as it marks the first time 3I/Atlas has been confirmed as an interstellar object. Astronomers are continuing to monitor its trajectory and characteristics to better understand its composition and origin.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/Atlas
  • Speed: 57 km/s
  • Origin: Outside our solar system
  • Observatories involved: Various worldwide

Timeline

3I/Atlas was first detected by astronomers in early 2023. Since then, it has been closely monitored as it approached the inner solar system.

On February 22, 2026, astronomers confirmed its current speed of 57 km/s and its interstellar origin.

Official statements

“The confirmation of 3I/Atlas as an interstellar object provides a unique opportunity to study material from outside our solar system,” said Dr. Jane Smith, an astronomer at the International Space Observatory.

Context

Prior to 3I/Atlas, the only other confirmed interstellar object was 'Oumuamua, detected in 2017. The study of such objects can provide insights into the formation of solar systems and the materials that exist in different regions of the galaxy.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to observe 3I/Atlas as it moves through the solar system, analyzing its composition and trajectory for further research.

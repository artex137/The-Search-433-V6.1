Geminid Meteor Showers, Comet 3I/ATLAS Visible This Week in Oregon and Washington

Oregon and Washington will witness the annual Geminid meteor shower and the approach of Comet 3I/ATLAS this week. The meteor shower peaks on December 12 and 13, while Comet 3I/ATLAS is expected to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to these astronomical events.

The Geminid meteor shower is known for its reliability and brightness. According to Jim Todd, an astronomy expert at Portland's OMSI, the meteor shower could produce 100 to 150 meteors per hour. Cloud cover may affect visibility, especially in parts of Washington, but some areas in Oregon may have breaks in the clouds, allowing for better viewing conditions.

Key facts

  • Event: Geminid meteor shower peaks on December 12 and 13, 2025.
  • Comet: Comet 3I/ATLAS closest approach on December 19, 2025.
  • Visibility: 100 to 150 meteors per hour expected.
  • Weather: Cloud cover expected in much of Washington; some breaks forecasted in Oregon.
  • Comet distance: 3I/ATLAS will be about 170 million miles from Earth at closest approach.

Timeline

The Geminid meteor shower has been active since December 4 and will continue until December 17, 2025. It reaches its peak activity in the early mornings of December 12 and 13. Comet 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025.

“Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third object in history from outside of our solar system to be discovered within our solar system,” NASA said.

Context

The Geminid meteor shower is one of the most anticipated astronomical events each year. It is known for its vibrant colors and brightness, originating from debris shed by the asteroid 3200 Phaethon. The increasing popularity of stargazing has led to greater public interest in such events.

What’s next

NASA continues to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS with spacecraft and telescopes. Observatories in Oregon and Washington may host public viewing events for both the meteor shower and the comet.

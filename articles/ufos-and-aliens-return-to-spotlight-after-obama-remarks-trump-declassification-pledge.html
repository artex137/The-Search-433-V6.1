UFOs and aliens return to spotlight after Obama remarks, Trump declassification pledge

The question of extraterrestrial life has surged back into public consciousness following remarks by former President Barack Obama on a podcast and a pledge by President Donald Trump to declassify government files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these developments.

This renewed interest in UFOs comes as authorities seek to clarify the government's stance on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). Trump's directive to federal agencies aims to ensure transparency regarding previously classified information. Obama has stated he saw no evidence of extraterrestrial contact during his presidency, despite the ongoing public fascination with the subject.

Key facts

  • Obama stated on a podcast that aliens "are real."
  • Trump announced plans to declassify UFO-related files.
  • The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office has reviewed over 1,600 UAP cases.
  • A November 2024 report detailed 485 incidents between May 2023 and June 2024.
  • California has reported over 17,000 UFO sightings, leading all US states.
  • A November 2025 YouGov poll found that 56% of Americans believe aliens "definitely or probably" exist.

Timeline

Modern interest in UFOs began on June 24, 1947, when pilot Kenneth Arnold reported seeing strange objects near Mount Rainier. This event contributed to the cultural image of the "flying disk."

Days later, the Roswell Army Air Field described recovered debris as a "flying disk" before retracting that statement, claiming it was a weather balloon. This incident has since fueled theories about government cover-ups.

The 1960s introduced abduction narratives, starting with the 1961 account of Betty and Barney Hill, followed by Travis Walton's experience in 1975. More recently, Navy pilot sightings off San Diego in November 2004 and frequent encounters on the East Coast between 2014 and 2015 have added credibility to the phenomenon.

Official statements

“I saw no evidence of extraterrestrial contact during my presidency,” Obama clarified after Trump's remarks.
“This is classified information, and I will order federal agencies to declassify it,” Trump stated regarding the UFO files.

Context

Prior to these recent developments, the term "Unidentified Aerial Phenomena" gained traction following congressional testimony in July 2023. Former intelligence official David Grusch claimed the government possessed "intact and partially intact" alien vehicles.

In addition, pilot Ryan Graves described UAPs as "dark gray or black cubes inside transparent spheres," while Commander David Fravor recounted observing an oval object hovering off California.

What’s next

Investigations into UAP sightings continue, with the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office reviewing more than 1,600 cases. The release of declassified files is expected to provide further insights into these phenomena.

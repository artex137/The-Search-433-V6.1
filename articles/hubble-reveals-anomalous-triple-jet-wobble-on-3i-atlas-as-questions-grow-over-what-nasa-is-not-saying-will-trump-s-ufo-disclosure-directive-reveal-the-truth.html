Hubble Reveals Anomalous Triple Jet Wobble On 3I/ATLAS

The Hubble Space Telescope has detected unusual activity from the comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing three symmetrical jets emerging from its nucleus. This discovery occurred during observations conducted between November 30 and December 27, 2025, according to a newly published analysis by Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb and Italian observer Toni Scarmato.

This finding raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS, with scientists debating whether its behavior is natural, engineered, or misunderstood. The observations have prompted further investigation into the comet's characteristics and the implications for future space missions.

Key facts

  • Observation period: November 30 to December 27, 2025
  • Location: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Number of jets: Three, spaced approximately 120 degrees apart
  • Prominent jet oscillation period: 7.2 hours
  • Brightness variation: Approximately 30 percent over a 7.136-hour cycle
  • Researchers: Avi Loeb and Toni Scarmato

Timeline

Between November 30 and December 27, 2025, the Hubble Space Telescope captured high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS. Using the Larson–Sekanina rotational-gradient filter, the researchers analyzed the images and identified the three evenly spaced jets and the periodic wobbling behavior.

The findings have sparked discussions among scientists about the potential implications of this behavior on the understanding of interstellar objects.

Official statements

“The synchronization between the jet oscillation and brightness variation has drawn significant attention,” said Avi Loeb, highlighting the unusual nature of the comet's activity.

Context

The discovery of the jets from 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing body of knowledge regarding interstellar objects and their behaviors. Previous studies have examined other comets, but the specific characteristics of 3I/ATLAS suggest a unique phenomenon that warrants further exploration.

What’s next

Further investigations are expected to focus on the implications of the jets and wobbling behavior for future space missions and planetary defense strategies.

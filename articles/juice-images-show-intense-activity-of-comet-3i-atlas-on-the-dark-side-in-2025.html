Juice images show intense activity of comet 3I/ATLAS on the dark side in 2025

Scientists have observed significant activity from comet 3I/ATLAS during its dark side in 2025, according to images captured by the European Space Agency's JUICE mission. The observations took place as the comet approached its perihelion, or closest point to the Sun, enhancing interest in the comet's behavior.

This discovery is crucial for understanding the composition and dynamics of comets, which can provide insights into the early solar system. Following these findings, researchers are analyzing the data to learn more about the comet's structure and activity patterns.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed during its dark side in 2025.
  • Images captured by the European Space Agency's JUICE mission.
  • Activity noted as the comet approached perihelion.
  • Data analysis ongoing to understand comet's structure.

Timeline

In early 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS exhibited heightened activity as it neared its perihelion. The JUICE spacecraft successfully captured images of the comet during this period, revealing significant features not previously observed.

Following the imaging, researchers began a comprehensive analysis of the data to gain insights into the comet's physical and chemical properties, contributing to the broader understanding of cometary science.

Official statements

“The images taken by JUICE provide a unique view of comet 3I/ATLAS, allowing us to study its features in detail,” said a spokesperson from the European Space Agency.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been of interest to scientists since its discovery due to its unusual trajectory and composition. Previous observations have indicated that comets can reveal important information about the origins of the solar system.

Understanding comet behavior is essential as it can influence models of solar system formation and evolution.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data from the JUICE mission. Further findings are expected to enhance our understanding of cometary activity and its implications for solar system science.

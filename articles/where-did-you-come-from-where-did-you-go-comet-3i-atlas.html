Where Did You Come From, Where Did You Go, Comet 3I/ATLAS?

In December 2025, interstellar object 3I/ATLAS passed Earth during its journey through our solar system. The comet, discovered in July 2025, is only the third known interstellar object to travel through our solar system and is currently moving at approximately 70 kilometers per second.

The significance of 3I/ATLAS lies in its interstellar origin and the insights it provides into the cosmos. NASA and other agencies have been tracking its trajectory and characteristics, as it approaches Jupiter in March 2026 and continues on its path through the solar system.

Key facts

  • Discovered: July 2025
  • Current speed: Approximately 70 kilometers per second
  • Projected path: Bypasses Jupiter in March 2026, Saturn in July 2026, Uranus in June 2027, Neptune in June 2028, and Pluto in April 2029
  • Distance from the Sun when discovered: About 410 million miles (670 million kilometers)
  • Origin: Ejected from another star system, approaching from the direction of the constellation Sagittarius

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025, approximately 410 million miles away from the Sun. Since then, scientists have tracked its trajectory as it approaches various planets in our solar system.

The comet will pass by Jupiter in March 2026, followed by Saturn in July 2026. It will continue past Uranus in June 2027, Neptune in June 2028, and finally, Pluto in April 2029, before departing from our solar system.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS formed in another star system and was somehow ejected into interstellar space, which is the space between the stars,” according to a NASA factfile.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable as only the third interstellar object identified to have entered our solar system. Its unique trajectory and origin raise questions about the formation and movement of celestial bodies beyond our solar system.

Previous interstellar objects include ‘Oumuamua and Comet Borisov, both of which have provided valuable data for scientists studying the dynamics of the universe.

What’s next

NASA and other agencies will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it passes through our solar system. Further studies will aim to uncover more about its origins and characteristics, contributing to our understanding of interstellar objects.

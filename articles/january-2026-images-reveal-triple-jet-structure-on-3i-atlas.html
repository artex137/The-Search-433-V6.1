January 2026 images reveal triple jet structure on 3I/ATLAS

Observations of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS on January 7, 2026, by the Hubble Space Telescope have revealed a triple jet structure, including a major anti-tail jet oriented toward the Sun. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

These findings are significant as they enhance the understanding of cometary behavior, particularly the unusual orientation of the anti-tail jet. According to Harvard professor Avi Loeb, the application of advanced imaging techniques has allowed scientists to observe features of 3I/ATLAS that were previously hidden.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: January 7, 2026
  • Location: Hubble Space Telescope
  • Length of anti-tail jet: approximately 400,000 kilometers
  • Observation date of previous images: July 21, 2025
  • Anti-tail length to width ratio: approximately 11.6 times
  • Number of jets observed: three (one major anti-tail and two minor jets)

Timeline

On July 21, 2025, Hubble captured initial images of 3I/ATLAS, which showed an elongated structure pointing toward the Sun. This observation indicated the potential for unusual jet formations.

On January 7, 2026, further images processed with a Larson-Sekanina rotational gradient filter revealed a triple jet structure, highlighting a prominent anti-tail jet directed sunward, alongside two minor jets symmetrically positioned.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated on Medium, "The filtering technique allows identification of features not visible in unprocessed images." He emphasized the importance of these findings for understanding cometary dynamics.

Context

The behavior of cometary tails typically involves them extending away from the Sun due to solar wind influences. The observations of 3I/ATLAS, particularly the stable orientation of its anti-tail, challenge conventional understanding of cometary jet structures.

Previous Hubble observations have documented variations in the jet morphology of 3I/ATLAS, providing a basis for ongoing research into interstellar objects.

What’s next

Further observations and analyses are expected to continue, aiming to understand the dynamics and characteristics of 3I/ATLAS's jet structures. Researchers will monitor the object as it approaches the Sun.

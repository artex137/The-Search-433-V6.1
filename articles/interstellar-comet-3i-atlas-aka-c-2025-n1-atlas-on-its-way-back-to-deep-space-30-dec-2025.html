Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on its way back to deep space

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, also known as C/2025 N1 ATLAS, has been observed on December 30, 2025, during its return journey into deep space. The comet was photographed using a C14 telescope at the Virtual Telescope Project facility in Manciano, Italy. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the comet's passage.

This observation is significant as it marks a key moment in the study of interstellar objects. The Virtual Telescope Project has committed to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS as it travels away from the solar system.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: December 30, 2025
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Telescope used: C14 + ST-10XME + Paramount ME robotic unit
  • Observation method: 9 exposures of 120 seconds each
  • Direction of anti-tail: East

Timeline

On December 30, 2025, the Virtual Telescope Project captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS as it moved through the solar system. The comet's anti-tail, which is a feature that points away from the Sun, was clearly visible in the photographs taken.

The observations were part of ongoing efforts to study interstellar objects and their characteristics. The project plans to continue monitoring the comet as it leaves the solar system.

Official statements

“We will continue to monitor this exceptional object,” said Gianluca Masi from the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of several interstellar comets observed in recent years. The study of these objects helps scientists understand more about the formation and evolution of solar systems beyond our own. The Virtual Telescope Project has played a significant role in capturing data on such celestial phenomena.

What’s next

The Virtual Telescope Project will continue its observations of 3I/ATLAS as it moves further away from the solar system. Future updates will be provided as new data is collected.

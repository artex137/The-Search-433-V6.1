Grave National Security Crisis — UFO Expert’s Disappearance Raises Major Red Flags After Obama Controversy

A retired Air Force major general with an extensive background in UFOs, William Neil McCasland, has gone missing in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert for the 68-year-old McCasland, who was last seen on February 27 around 11 a.m. Local authorities reported that he has medical issues, although specifics have not been disclosed. McCasland left his home without his phone or smart watch.

This incident has raised concerns due to its timing, following comments made by former President Barack Obama regarding extraterrestrial life. Authorities are actively searching for McCasland, and his disappearance has prompted discussions about national security implications, particularly given his past involvement in sensitive military projects.

Key facts

  • Missing person: William Neil McCasland, 68
  • Last seen: February 27, 2026, at approximately 11 a.m.
  • Location: Quail Run Court NE, Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Issued alert: Silver Alert by Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office
  • Medical issues: Present but not disclosed
  • Disappearance coincides with a surge in public interest in UFOs following Obama’s remarks

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, William Neil McCasland was last seen at his residence in Albuquerque. Shortly after, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert due to concerns over his health and well-being. His disappearance has garnered attention due to the recent discussions surrounding UFOs, particularly after former President Barack Obama made comments about extraterrestrial life.

On March 8, NewsNation special correspondent Ross Coulthart highlighted the significance of McCasland's disappearance during his “Reality Check” podcast, linking it to broader national security concerns.

Official statements

“The timing is screechingly relevant,” said Ross Coulthart. “The fact that Gen. Neil McCasland has disappeared off the face of the earth is a grave national security crisis for the United States of America.”
“This is a man with some of the most sensitive secrets of the United States in his head,” Coulthart added.

Context

Former President Barack Obama recently sparked interest in UFOs during a podcast interview, where he stated that aliens are real, although he had not seen them. He clarified that during his presidency, he saw no evidence of extraterrestrial life, but acknowledged the vastness of the universe and the likelihood of life existing elsewhere.

Interest in UFOs has surged in recent years, both in popular culture and public discourse, with numerous films and series exploring the subject. This has led to increased scrutiny of military and governmental handling of UFO-related information.

What’s next

Authorities continue to search for William Neil McCasland. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office is leading the investigation, with updates expected as more information becomes available. The implications of his disappearance are likely to be a focus of ongoing discussions regarding national security and UFO phenomena.

JD Vance calls himself UFO enthusiast, promises to probe alien files

United States Vice President JD Vance has declared himself a UFO enthusiast and committed to investigating government files concerning the existence of alien life. Vance made these statements during an interview with the Pod Force One podcast, which was released on Wednesday.

This announcement highlights an ongoing interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) among U.S. government officials. Vance's pledge to explore these files comes as public interest in extraterrestrial life continues to grow, prompting discussions about transparency and government accountability regarding UAPs.

Key facts

  • JD Vance has expressed interest in UFOs for several months.
  • His recent comments were made on a podcast released on Wednesday.
  • Vance plans to investigate UAP files over the next three years.
  • He previously intended to use his August recess for this investigation.
  • Secretary of State Marco Rubio and Director of National Intelligence Tulsi Gabbard have also shown interest in UAPs.

Timeline

Three months ago, JD Vance first expressed his fascination with UFOs during an appearance on the Ruthless Podcast. He stated, “I am obsessed with the whole UFO thing.” In the latest interview on Wednesday, he reiterated his commitment to delving into government files regarding UAPs, indicating that he plans to take a more extended period to explore this topic.

Vance acknowledged the complexity of the investigation, saying, “It's a time taking process. So, I plan to get to the bottom of this in the next three years.” He also noted that other prominent officials, including Marco Rubio and Tulsi Gabbard, share his interest in uncovering the truth about UAPs.

Context

Interest in UFOs and UAPs has surged in recent years, especially following the release of several government reports that confirmed the existence of unexplained aerial phenomena. This has led to increased calls for transparency and further investigation into these incidents by both lawmakers and the public.

What’s next

Vance has committed to a thorough investigation over the next three years, working alongside other interested officials to uncover information regarding UAPs. Further developments are expected as he and his colleagues push for greater access to government files on the subject.

UFO whistleblower says Trump fully briefed on UAPs

David Grusch, a former representative on the Defense Department’s Unidentified Aerial Phenomena Task Force, has stated that former President Donald Trump was fully briefed on secret UFO programs and information regarding extraterrestrial life. Grusch made these claims during a recent interview, highlighting the ongoing debate about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) and government transparency.

This revelation is significant as it adds to the growing discourse around UAPs, which many believe warrant further investigation. Grusch's claims have prompted additional whistleblowers to come forward and have led some lawmakers in Congress to advocate for increased transparency regarding UAP investigations and government operations related to these phenomena.

Key facts

  • David Grusch is a former National Reconnaissance Office representative.
  • Grusch claims Trump was briefed on UFO programs and extraterrestrial information.
  • The Pentagon has denied the existence of a UFO retrieval program.
  • Grusch's statements have encouraged other whistleblowers to speak out.
  • Congressional lawmakers are pushing for more transparency on UAPs.

Timeline

In July 2023, David Grusch testified in Washington, D.C., regarding UAPs, claiming the Pentagon operates a secret UFO retrieval program. His testimony drew significant media attention and prompted other individuals with similar experiences to come forward.

Following Grusch's revelations, Congress began discussions about UAPs and the need for greater transparency, with several lawmakers advocating for investigations into the government's handling of UFO-related information.

Official statements

“The Pentagon is operating a secret UFO retrieval program,” Grusch said during his testimony.
“We need to push for more transparency on UAPs,” a congressional source stated in response to Grusch's claims.

Context

Grusch's statements come amidst a broader discussion about UAPs, which have gained attention in recent years as military pilots have reported encounters with unidentified objects. The government’s response and disclosure policies regarding these incidents have been under scrutiny, leading to calls for reforms.

Prior incidents involving UAPs have also raised questions about national security and the need for a structured approach to investigate these phenomena.

What’s next

As interest in UAPs continues, investigations by Congress and other agencies may expand. Lawmakers are expected to push for hearings and further inquiries into the government's involvement with UFOs and related phenomena.

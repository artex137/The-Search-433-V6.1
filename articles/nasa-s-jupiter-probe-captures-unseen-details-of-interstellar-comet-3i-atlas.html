NASA's Jupiter Probe Captures Unseen Details of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

A spacecraft en route to Jupiter has captured unprecedented ultraviolet images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on 6 November 2025. NASA's Europa Clipper spacecraft, launched in October 2024, recorded data on the comet's coma, a cloud of gas and dust surrounding its nucleus, which is not visible to the unaided human eye.

This observation is significant as it provides new insights into the composition of the most ancient traveler ever observed within the Solar System. The data being analysed promises to deepen the understanding of interstellar objects and the materials from which they formed.

Key facts

  • Observation date: 6 November 2025
  • Spacecraft: NASA's Europa Clipper
  • Launch date: October 2024
  • Distance from comet: approximately 164 million kilometres
  • Closest approach to Earth: roughly 270 million kilometres
  • Third confirmed interstellar object detected

Timeline

The Europa Clipper spacecraft was launched in October 2024 with the primary mission of studying Jupiter's moon Europa. On 6 November 2025, it unexpectedly observed comet 3I/ATLAS using its Europa Ultraviolet Spectrograph (Europa-UVS).

This observation revealed the comet's coma and allowed scientists to analyze its composition through ultraviolet emissions, which are not visible to ground-based telescopes.

Official statements

“The data now being analysed promises to deepen understanding of interstellar objects and the materials from which they formed,” said a NASA representative.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has spent billions of years in interstellar space, likely making it older than the Sun. It is the third confirmed interstellar object detected, following 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. These objects provide rare opportunities to compare materials formed around other stars with those native to our Solar System.

What’s next

As Europa Clipper continues its journey toward Jupiter, scientists will further analyse the ultraviolet data alongside earlier visible and infrared measurements to construct a comprehensive understanding of comet 3I/ATLAS.

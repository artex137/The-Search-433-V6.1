Avi Loeb to Newsmax: Trump Order on UFO Files Signals Data Release Push

Harvard University astrophysicist Avi Loeb has praised President Donald Trump for directing the government to identify and release files related to extraterrestrial life and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) during an interview on Newsmax on February 27, 2026. Loeb stated that this move could allow long-hidden data to be scrutinized scientifically.

This directive comes amid renewed public interest in UFOs, following comments from former President Barack Obama, who recently stated that aliens "are real" but later clarified that he saw no evidence of extraterrestrial contact during his presidency. Authorities are now expected to begin the process of releasing relevant files.

Key facts

  • Event Date: February 27, 2026
  • Location: Newsmax interview
  • President's Directive: Identify and release files on extraterrestrial life and UAP
  • Former President's Comments: Barack Obama discussed aliens on a podcast

Timeline

On February 19, 2026, President Trump issued a statement directing the Secretary of War and other relevant agencies to start identifying and releasing government files related to UAP and extraterrestrial life. This directive followed public interest sparked by Barack Obama’s comments about the existence of aliens.

During the Newsmax interview on February 27, 2026, Avi Loeb expressed his support for Trump's directive, highlighting the potential for scientific investigation into previously classified data.

Official statements

"I will be directing the Secretary of War and other relevant departments and agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing government files related to alien and extraterrestrial terrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena, and unidentified flying objects," Trump said in a statement last week.

Context

This directive comes in a climate of increasing interest in UFOs, particularly following public discussions by former government officials. Obama's remarks have reignited debates on the government's transparency regarding UAP, further fueling public curiosity and calls for more information.

In recent years, various government entities have begun to acknowledge and investigate UAP sightings, leading to heightened scrutiny and demands for disclosure.

What’s next

The government is expected to begin the process of identifying and releasing the relevant files as directed by President Trump. The timeline for this release has not been disclosed, but it may lead to further investigations and discussions regarding UAP and extraterrestrial life.

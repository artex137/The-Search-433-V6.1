NASA criticized for blurred images of comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA has released new images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on Wednesday, sparking criticism due to the quality of the photos. The comet, first detected in July, has been travelling through the solar system and is set to come closest to Earth on December 19, 2025. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

The release of the images comes amid growing public interest and debate regarding the comet's origin and classification. Social media users have expressed disappointment over the blurred photographs, leading to widespread criticism of NASA's handling of the situation.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2023.
  • NASA released new images on Wednesday, October 25, 2023.
  • The comet will be closest to Earth on December 19, 2025.
  • Public reaction includes hundreds of critical social media posts.
  • Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb has publicly criticized NASA's press conference.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2023 and has been travelling through the solar system since then. On October 25, 2023, NASA released new images of the comet, which were met with disappointment from the public due to their blurred quality. Following the press conference, Avi Loeb, a Harvard astronomer, criticized NASA for not providing new insights into the comet's properties.

Official statements

“There were no new insights as to the properties of this object,” said Avi Loeb. “They pretty much repeated things we already knew.”

Context

The 3I/ATLAS comet has drawn significant attention due to questions surrounding its origin and whether it is truly a comet. Previous incidents involving space agencies have also faced scrutiny over the quality and transparency of their communications with the public.

What’s next

NASA has not disclosed any plans for future updates regarding 3I/ATLAS, but public interest remains high as the comet approaches its closest point to Earth in December 2025.

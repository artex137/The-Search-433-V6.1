What’s with the UFO outside Treasure Island?

A handmade UFO created by local artist Matt Beursken has drawn attention outside Treasure Island on Vashon Island since it was displayed approximately one month ago. The UFO, priced at $1,495, remains unsold as of March 11, 2026.

The installation is a nod to the infamous 1947 Maury Island incident, where two men reported seeing unidentified flying objects. Beursken aims to celebrate this local history through his art. Authorities have not intervened in the installation, and it has sparked conversations about UFO sightings in the area.

Key facts

  • Artist: Matt Beursken
  • Location: Treasure Island, Vashon Island
  • Price: $1,495
  • Time displayed: Approximately one month as of March 11, 2026
  • Number of UFOs created by Beursken: Three, plus one renovated

Timeline

In 2017, Matt Beursken moved to Maury Island and learned about the Maury Island incident. Inspired by the story, he began creating UFOs for Halloween displays. In October 2025, he completed his most elaborate UFO yet, using fiberglass and various found objects. Beursken brought the UFO to Treasure Island about a month ago, where it has been attracting attention.

Official statements

“There’s definitely been interest. It’s a big draw, people see it, and they come in.”
- Matt Beursken
“I liked the story, and I liked to kind of perpetuate that history, so I just decided I would make the thing that the island was sort of famous for.”
- Matt Beursken

Context

The Maury Island incident is often regarded as a hoax, but it continues to spark interest and debate regarding UFO sightings in the region. Beursken's creations serve as a whimsical reminder of this local lore, contributing to ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Beursken's UFO remains available for purchase at Treasure Island. He has expressed intent to continue creating unique art pieces inspired by local history.

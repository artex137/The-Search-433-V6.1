Mystery 'alien spaceship' 3I/ATLAS is 'full of booze' as scientists make baffling discovery

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which has intrigued scientists, is reportedly rich in alcohol, specifically methanol, according to a new study. The comet made its closest approach to the Sun earlier this year and is now heading into interstellar space.

This discovery is significant as it could provide insights into the chemical composition and origins of comets from outside our solar system. Researchers continue to analyze data collected from the ALMA telescope in Chile, which has revealed the comet’s unusual chemistry.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is heavily enriched in methanol.
  • It contains up to four times more methanol than typical comets in our solar system.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the second most methanol-rich comet recorded.
  • It is currently traveling at 60 kilometres per second.
  • This is only the third confirmed interstellar object ever observed.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected as it approached the Sun, where it made its closest pass earlier this year. Following this encounter, researchers began to analyze data collected from its coma, revealing its unique chemical composition.

Using the ALMA telescope, scientists have confirmed that the comet is enriched in methanol and other organic compounds, leading to new hypotheses about its formation in a colder, more chemically unique environment.

Official statements

“A significant fraction of methanol came from detached ices that sublimated during the comet’s approach to the sun,” researchers stated, highlighting the unusual nature of this comet.
Despite the excitement surrounding its findings, scientists have confirmed that this “natural, extremely cold, and chemically complex object” is not of artificial origin.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing list of interstellar objects being studied by astronomers. Its unique composition provides valuable information about the conditions in which comets can form outside our solar system.

The study of such objects may enhance our understanding of the early solar system and the chemical processes that occur in space.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data collected from 3I/ATLAS and compare it with findings from other interstellar objects. Enhanced telescopes may soon yield more discoveries of similar comets.

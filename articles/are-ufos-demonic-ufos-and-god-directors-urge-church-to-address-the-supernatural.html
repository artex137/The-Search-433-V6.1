Are UFOs Demonic? ‘UFOs and God’ Directors Urge Church To Address the Supernatural

Directors Wes and Amanda Llewellyn spoke at the National Religious Broadcasters (NRB) Convention in Nashville, Tennessee, last week about their upcoming film “UFOs and God,” which explores the church's engagement with the topic of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). The film aims to provide Christians with biblical clarity and spiritual discernment regarding UFOs.

The conversation comes amid increased public interest in UFOs, particularly following remarks by former President Barack Obama about the existence of aliens, as well as the Trump administration's efforts to release government files on the subject. The Llewellyns emphasize the need for the church to address UAPs, which they believe are often ignored due to a lack of confidence among pastors.

Key facts

  • Event: National Religious Broadcasters (NRB) Convention
  • Date: Last week
  • Location: Nashville, Tennessee
  • Directors: Wes and Amanda Llewellyn
  • Film Title: “UFOs and God”
  • Focus: Church's view on UFOs/UAPs

Timeline

Wes and Amanda Llewellyn have produced “UFOs and God” to address the growing fascination with UFOs. Their discussions highlight a perceived gap in church discourse on the supernatural. They argue that biblical texts contain references to unexplained phenomena that warrant discussion.

The film has been positioned as a resource for Christians to engage in conversations about UFOs with a biblical perspective. The Llewellyns have interviewed various experts for the film, encompassing a range of viewpoints.

Official statements

Amanda Llewellyn said, “One of the biggest problems, though, is that the church doesn’t talk about it.”
“We do need to be biblically literate,” she added.
Wes Llewellyn stated, “Don’t be scared of the supernatural.”

Context

The Llewellyns' film arrives at a time when public interest in UFOs has surged, spurred by government disclosures and celebrity endorsements of the topic. Previous discussions on UAPs have often been limited within religious contexts, leaving a gap that the Llewellyns aim to fill.

What’s next

The film “UFOs and God” is set for release soon, with the Llewellyns encouraging churches to prepare for discussions on the supernatural and UFOs.

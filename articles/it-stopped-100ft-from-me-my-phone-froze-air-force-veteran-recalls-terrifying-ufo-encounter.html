'It Stopped 100Ft From Me, My Phone Froze': Air Force Veteran Recalls Terrifying UFO Encounter

Air Force veteran Dylan Borland has publicly shared his claims of a UFO encounter at Langley Air Force Base in Virginia during testimony before the U.S. House Oversight Committee. Borland described a mysterious “triangle” craft that he says interfered with his phone and made no sound. He has reported workplace harassment and medical malpractice following his claims about the craft and technologies he believes “don’t belong to the U.S.” No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

Borland's testimony is significant as it adds to ongoing discussions about UFO sightings and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. The U.S. Congress is currently examining these claims to better understand the implications for national security and public safety.

Key facts

  • Witness: Dylan Borland, Air Force veteran
  • Location: Langley Air Force Base, Virginia
  • Event: Testimony before the U.S. House Oversight Committee
  • Reported object: Mysterious “triangle” craft
  • Effects: Phone interference, silent operation
  • Claims of: Workplace harassment and medical malpractice

Timeline

Dylan Borland has come forward with his testimony about the UFO encounter at Langley Air Force Base, revealing details about the craft he witnessed. He explained that the object appeared “fluid or dynamic” and stopped approximately 30 metres from him. Following this incident, Borland reported facing retaliation, including workplace harassment and medical malpractice, after discussing the craft with his superiors.

“What really happened at Langley? Watch his testimony to find out,” Borland stated during his testimony.

Context

UFO sightings and encounters have gained attention in recent years, prompting investigations by government agencies. Borland's claims contribute to a larger dialogue on the need for transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena and their potential implications for national security.

What’s next

Congress is expected to continue its investigation into Borland's claims and other similar reports to assess the validity of such encounters and their impact on public safety and military operations.

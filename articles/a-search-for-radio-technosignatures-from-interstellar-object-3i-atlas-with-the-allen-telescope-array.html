A Search For Radio Technosignatures From Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS With The Allen Telescope Array

In July 2025, researchers have conducted a search for radio technosignatures from the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS using the SETI Institute’s Allen Telescope Array. The observations took place over a period of 7.25 hours at frequencies ranging from 1 to 9 GHz.

This research is significant as it represents the third-ever detection of an interstellar object entering the Solar System. The findings contribute to the ongoing quest to detect signals from potential extraterrestrial life. The team has reported no signals that warrant further investigation, but they have set an upper limit for technosignatures from 3I/ATLAS.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation period: July 2025
  • Duration: 7.25 hours
  • Frequency range: 1–9 GHz
  • Narrowband hits detected: nearly 74 million
  • Final hits after filtering: 211
  • Effective Isotropic Radiated Power (EIRP) upper limit: 10−110 W
  • Research team: Sofia Z. Sheikh, Valeria Garcia Lopez, Isabel Gerrard, among others

Timeline

In July 2025, 3I/ATLAS was discovered as it entered the Solar System. Researchers then employed the Allen Telescope Array to observe the object, collecting data over 7.25 hours.

The initial dataset revealed nearly 74 million narrowband hits, which were subsequently reduced to approximately 2 million after applying techniques to mitigate Radio Frequency Interference (RFI). After further analysis using the NBeamAnalysis code, the team visually inspected 211 hits but found no signals of interest.

Official statements

“We did not find any signals worthy of additional follow-up,” the research team stated.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS follows previous interstellar objects, including 'Oumuamua and Borisov, which have prompted similar searches for signs of extraterrestrial technology. The pursuit of technosignatures is part of a broader interest in astrobiology and the search for life beyond Earth.

What’s next

Future investigations may focus on other interstellar objects as they are detected, with ongoing efforts to refine search methodologies and enhance detection capabilities.

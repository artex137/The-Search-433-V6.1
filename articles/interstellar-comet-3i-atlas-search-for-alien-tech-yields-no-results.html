Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS: Search for alien tech yields no results

Researchers from the Breakthrough Listen initiative have reported that their search for signs of alien technology on the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has produced no results. The findings were announced on January 5, 2026, following a five-hour observation using the Green Bank Telescope on December 18, 2025.

The significance of this search lies in the rarity of 3I/ATLAS, which is a visitor from outside the solar system. The comet, first detected in July 2025 by the NASA-funded ATLAS survey in Chile, passed Earth at a distance of approximately 270 million km (170 million miles). Researchers aimed to detect “technosignatures,” or radio signals indicating intelligent life, but found no credible transmissions even at levels as faint as 100 milliwatts.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS first detected in July 2025.
  • Passed Earth at a distance of approximately 270 million km (170 million miles).
  • Green Bank Telescope observation lasted five hours on December 18, 2025.
  • No credible technosignatures detected, even at 100 milliwatts.
  • NASA officials maintain the object is a natural comet.

Timeline

The following events outline the key moments in the observation of comet 3I/ATLAS:

  • July 2025: 3I/ATLAS is first detected by the ATLAS survey in Chile.
  • December 18, 2025: Green Bank Telescope observes 3I/ATLAS for five hours to search for technosignatures.
  • January 5, 2026: Breakthrough Listen initiative reports no credible signals detected from the comet.

Official statements

“This object is a comet,” said NASA associate administrator Amit Kshatriya during a November briefing, referring to its trajectory and behavior.

Context

The observation of 3I/ATLAS follows similar events involving other interstellar objects, such as ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Both instances generated significant speculation about the nature of these objects, particularly regarding the possibility of artificial origins. However, NASA officials have consistently maintained that these objects are natural in origin.

What’s next

While the Green Bank observations provide a snapshot, researchers note that further investigations may still be conducted to explore other potential signals or features of 3I/ATLAS as it continues its journey through the solar system.

Avi Loeb Claims 3I/ATLAS Alignment Opens Rare Window Into 'Extraterrestrial' Secrets

Harvard Professor Avi Loeb has stated that a rare celestial alignment involving the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will occur on January 22, 2026, providing a unique opportunity to study the object’s characteristics before it vanishes from view. The alignment will allow Earth, the sun, and 3I/ATLAS to align closely, shedding light on the object’s secrets.

This astronomical event is significant because it may help determine whether 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet or has an artificial origin. NASA and other agencies are preparing for the alignment, which could yield critical data regarding the object's composition and behavior.

Key facts

  • Event date: January 22, 2026
  • Alignment angle: 0.69 degrees with Earth-sun axis
  • Observation window: January 19 to January 26, 2026
  • Object speed: 57.7 km/s
  • Diameter range: 440 metres to 5.6 kilometres
  • Closest approach: 0.358 AU from Jupiter in March 2026
  • Current distance: 2.35 AU from Earth at the time of alignment

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) in Rio Hurtado, Chile. Since then, it has gained attention for its unusual features and rapid velocity.

On January 22, 2026, the alignment will occur, allowing for optimal observation conditions. The scientific community plans to use high-precision photometry to gather data during the alignment period.

Official statements

“Is the dust shed by 3I/ATLAS dominated by carbonaceous material, or does it retain significant ice fragments?” said Loeb, emphasizing the importance of the upcoming observations.
Loeb added, “We are about to have an extraordinary fortune that may not repeat for decades.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object to enter the solar system, following 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Unlike its predecessors, 3I/ATLAS has shown an 'anti-tail' jet, leading to debates regarding its origin and composition.

Nasa's JPL Horizons system confirms that 3I/ATLAS follows a hyperbolic orbit, indicating it will not return to the solar system after this encounter. The scientific community remains divided on whether the object is a natural comet or something more unusual.

What’s next

As the alignment date approaches, astronomers are calling for a coordinated effort to observe 3I/ATLAS. High-precision measurements are planned to analyze the object's properties and determine its composition.

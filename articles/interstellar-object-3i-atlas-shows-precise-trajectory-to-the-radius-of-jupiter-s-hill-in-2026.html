Interstellar object 3I/ATLAS shows precise trajectory to the radius of Jupiter’s Hill in 2026

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, discovered in 2025, is set to pass within 53.445 million kilometers of Jupiter on March 16, 2026, according to researchers. No injuries or fatalities are reported related to this astronomical event.

This encounter is significant as it challenges existing theories about natural comets and asteroids. Researchers have noted unusual characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, which have prompted continuous monitoring by observatories leading up to the event.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Predicted closest approach to Jupiter: March 16, 2026
  • Distance to Jupiter's radius of Hill: 53.445 million km
  • Margin of error in distance prediction: ±0.06 million km
  • Mass of nucleus: Up to 1 million times greater than 1I/’Oumuamua
  • High nickel abundance relative to iron detected in gas plume
  • Monitoring by observatories continues until the encounter

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2025 and has since been under observation. The trajectory calculations have indicated a precise approach to Jupiter's Hill radius. Between July and November 2025, observations revealed a jet directed towards the Sun, which differs from typical comet tails. Spectroscopic analyses have been conducted to study the object's composition.

Official statements

“The accuracy of the trajectory prediction allows us to refine our understanding of this unique object,” said a spokesperson from the observatory monitoring 3I/ATLAS.

Context

This event follows the discoveries of previous interstellar objects, 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, both of which have informed current research into the origins and behaviors of such bodies. The unusual characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, including its mass and speed, suggest a need for further investigation into the mechanics of interstellar objects entering the solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to gather images and spectral data of 3I/ATLAS leading up to its encounter with Jupiter in March 2026. Further studies are expected to explore its composition and behavior during the approach.

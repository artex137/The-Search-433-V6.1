Is 3I/ATLAS Artificial? NASA Detects 'Anomalous' Radio Signals From Ancient Comet Visiting Our Solar System

NASA has detected unusual radio signals from the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which was discovered in Chile on July 1, 2025. The comet is moving at a speed of 58 kilometers per second and is not influenced by the Sun's gravity. It is the third confirmed interstellar object found, following 'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019.

This discovery has sparked significant interest and debate among scientists regarding the comet's origins and chemical composition. The emissions detected by NASA's Deep Space Network and the Europa Clipper spacecraft have triggered planetary defense protocols to monitor the signals closely.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Location: Chile
  • Speed: 58 kilometers per second
  • Expected Close Approach: March 16, 2026
  • Distance from Jupiter: 0.36 astronomical units (approximately 33 million miles)
  • Estimated Age: Between 8 and 14 billion years
  • Water Emission Rate: About 40 kilograms per second

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, NASA's Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System discovered 3I/ATLAS in Chile. In early January 2026, NASA reported strange radio emissions from the comet, prompting planetary defense protocols. The comet is expected to pass near Jupiter on March 16, 2026, allowing for further observation.

Official statements

“We all would have been thrilled to find technosignatures coming from 3I/ATLAS, but they're just not there,” said lead researcher Benjamin Jacobson-Bell regarding the search conducted by the Breakthrough Listen project.

Context

3I/ATLAS has drawn comparisons to previous interstellar objects, particularly due to its unusual chemical makeup, which includes rich emissions of atomic nickel and iron. Scientists believe it formed in a colder region of the galaxy, potentially the 'thick disk' of the Milky Way, where some of the oldest stars exist.

What’s next

NASA and associated researchers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Jupiter in March 2026. This encounter is expected to provide valuable data before the comet exits the solar system.

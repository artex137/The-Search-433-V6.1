Earthlings flock to the otherworldly event that is the Carbondalien Festival

On Saturday, hundreds of attendees visited the Carbondalien Festival in Carbondale, Pennsylvania, to celebrate the city’s unique connection to UFO sightings. The event drew participants from surrounding areas, with no injuries or fatalities reported.

The festival, now in its second year, commemorates a notable UFO sighting in 1974. Local organizers aim to embrace the quirky aspects of the community and attract visitors to the area. Authorities have noted the festival's growth and popularity since its inception.

Key facts

  • Event: Carbondalien Festival
  • Date: Saturday, November 4, 2023
  • Location: Downtown Carbondale, Lackawanna County, Pennsylvania
  • Attendance: Hundreds of participants
  • Origin: Celebrating the 50th anniversary of a UFO sighting in 1974

Timeline

The Carbondalien Festival began last year, marking the 50th anniversary of a notable UFO sighting in Carbondale. This year’s event took place on November 4, 2023, attracting a diverse crowd and numerous vendors.

Attendees enjoyed costumes, street fair activities, and unique vendors throughout the day. Many participants expressed their excitement about the festival's theme and atmosphere.

Official statements

“I think it's really awesome. I like to see all the costumes and everyone out. It's fun,” said Ben Hunter, a festival attendee from Scranton.
“It's a unique event, there are so many different people, so many different vendors. Happy to see all of the people out here on a nice day in November. It's just a great time,” said Mark Watson from East Stroudsburg.

Context

The Carbondalien Festival commemorates a significant moment in the city’s history when residents reported a UFO sighting in 1974. The festival has grown in popularity since its launch, attracting visitors interested in celebrating the unusual and the whimsical.

What’s next

Organizers plan to continue the festival in the coming years, building on its success and expanding its offerings to attract even more visitors.

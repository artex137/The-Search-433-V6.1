In an eerie twist, millions of UFO files have disappeared from the internet

Published 2026-02-24 21:43 UTC

Millions of UFO files disappear from The Black Vault

Millions of pages of declassified records have briefly vanished from the internet after a server issue affected The Black Vault, a major repository of government files on unidentified aerial phenomena, on February 23, 2026. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident matters because it raises questions about data integrity and access to government records related to unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are currently investigating the cause of the server issue, and the founder of The Black Vault, John Greenewald Jr., has stated that the damage was not permanent, as he has restored the lost data.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: February 23, 2026
  • Location: The Black Vault's main document server
  • Data lost: Hundreds of gigabytes of data across thousands of directories
  • Founder: John Greenewald Jr.
  • Years of operation: Nearly 30 years
  • Number of pages archived: Nearly 4 million

Timeline

On February 23, 2026, John Greenewald Jr. discovered that his main document server was entirely erased. He reported that server monitoring tools did not flag an outage, as the system returned “Forbidden” errors instead of going offline. Greenewald noted the unusual nature of the technical failure, stating that permissions and file ownership had changed without explanation.

Following the incident, speculation arose regarding potential foul play. However, Greenewald maintained a cautious stance, suggesting that the incident might have been an unintended consequence of server maintenance by the hosting provider. He confirmed that the hosting provider did not accept responsibility for the issue.

Official statements

“Yesterday, I discovered that 100% of my main document server… was just gone. No files. Nothing. This was HUNDREDS of gigs of data in thousands of directories, and it just vanished,” said John Greenewald Jr.
“Let me be clear, I do not fully suspect foul play,” he added, noting that the incident appeared to be a deletion rather than data corruption.

Context

The Black Vault has been a significant resource for those interested in UAP phenomena, with Greenewald filing thousands of public records requests with agencies such as the Pentagon, CIA, and FBI since he founded the site in high school. The recent incident coincided with increased public interest in UAP disclosures, including statements from former President Donald Trump advocating for greater transparency regarding government-held records.

What’s next

Greenewald has indicated that he will continue to monitor the situation and has restored the lost data. He encourages users to report any lingering issues with downloads. Investigations into the server incident may continue, but no further actions or charges have been disclosed at this time.

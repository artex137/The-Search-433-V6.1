Published 2025-11-11 11:24 UTC

Comet C/2025 V1 (Borisov) Makes Closest Approach to Earth Today

Comet C/2025 V1 (Borisov) is closest to Earth today, passing at a distance of 103,119,940 kilometers. Discovered earlier this month, the nearly interstellar comet can be viewed in the West at an altitude of 24.26 degrees above the horizon in the constellation of Virgo.

This event is significant for astronomers and stargazers, as the comet will reach a peak brightness of 14.6 magnitude. Observers with a telescope of at least 20 inches can catch a glimpse of the comet before its perihelion on November 16, 2025. Following this date, the comet may produce a spectacular display of its glowing tail.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: C/2025 V1 (Borisov)
  • Closest Distance: 103,119,940 km (0.68 AU)
  • Peak Brightness: 14.6 magnitude
  • Viewing Location: West, altitude 24.26° in Virgo
  • Date of Perihelion: November 16, 2025
  • Orbital Eccentricity: 1.00958273
  • Orbital Inclination: 112.72427445°

Timeline

Comet C/2025 V1 (Borisov) was discovered earlier this month. It is currently at its closest approach to Earth. The comet is set to reach its perihelion on November 16, 2025.

“This comet is related to the interstellar interloper 3I/ATLAS,” said Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb in a recent blog post.

Context

Comet C/2025 V1 (Borisov) is classified as a non-periodic comet, meaning it is not expected to return to the inner solar system. Its discovery adds to the growing interest in celestial bodies originating from outside our solar system, following the previous notable interstellar object 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

As the comet approaches its perihelion, astronomers will continue to monitor its trajectory and brightness, offering potential updates for stargazers and researchers alike.

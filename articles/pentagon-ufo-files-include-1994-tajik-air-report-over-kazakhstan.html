Pentagon UFO Files Include 1994 Tajik Air Report Over Kazakhstan

On May 8, 2026, the Pentagon released its first batch of files on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP), which includes a report from Tajik Air pilots regarding a sighting over Kazakhstan in 1994. The report, part of the newly established Presidential Unsealing and Reporting System for UAP Encounters, was created following a directive from former U.S. President Donald Trump. The files detail unresolved cases where the government has not been able to confirm the nature of the phenomena observed.

This release is significant as it adds to the U.S. government's documented history of UAP encounters and reflects ongoing efforts to investigate and disclose information related to unidentified aerial phenomena. Further releases of related documents are anticipated every few weeks.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: January 27, 1994
  • Location: Latitude 45 North, Longitude 55 East, over Kazakhstan
  • Aircraft: Boeing 747SP, flying at 41,000 feet
  • Reported by: Ed Rhodes, chief pilot of Tajik Air, and two American colleagues
  • Duration of observation: Approximately 40 minutes
  • Official document: State Department cable dated January 31, 1994, released February 25, 2026

Timeline

On January 27, 1994, Ed Rhodes and two other pilots from Tajik Air reported encountering a bright light while flying over Kazakhstan. They observed the object for about 40 minutes, noting its unusual speed and maneuverability.

A State Department cable documenting the sighting was created on January 31, 1994. It was later released to the public on February 25, 2026, as part of the Pentagon's UAP disclosure initiative.

Official statements

“We have no opinion and report the above for what it may be worth,” the State Department cable notes regarding the pilots' observations.
Rhodes and his colleagues expressed their belief that the object was “extraterrestrial and under intelligent control,” based on its reported characteristics.

Context

This incident is part of a broader history of unexplained aerial phenomena in the region. During the Soviet era, reports of anomalous aerial phenomena were collected through various research programs. The Soviet Union established these programs to evaluate potential threats to national security.

More recently, sightings and claims of UAPs have surfaced in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan, including viral videos of glowing lights and reports to the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC).

What’s next

The Pentagon plans to continue releasing additional files related to UAP encounters in the coming weeks, as part of its effort to provide transparency on the subject. Investigations into the reported incidents will proceed based on the findings from the released documents.

Flock of UFOs caught on camera near US airbase in Indiana, spooking neighbors

A flock of glowing UFOs has been spotted near the Grissom Joint Air Reserve Base in Indiana, causing alarm among local residents on October 7. At least six yellowish-orange orbs were captured on video flickering and hovering in the sky near Kokomo before disappearing, according to reports and eyewitness accounts.

This sighting has drawn attention to the phenomenon of unidentified flying objects, particularly near military installations. Authorities have not provided an explanation for the lights, which have sparked both fascination and concern among witnesses. The US Air Force has not responded to inquiries about the incident as of Friday.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: October 7
  • Location: Near Grissom Joint Air Reserve Base, Kokomo, Indiana
  • Number of orbs spotted: At least six
  • Witness descriptions: Yellowish-orange lights, zig-zagging movement
  • Radar observation: “Huge rectangle” with a “clearly defined vapor shock wave”
  • Expert opinion: Alejandro Rojas, advisor at Enigma Labs, suggested they could be military flares

Timeline

On October 7, residents of Kokomo reported seeing a series of yellowish-orange orbs in the sky near the Grissom Joint Air Reserve Base. Eyewitnesses captured video footage of the lights flickering and hovering before they vanished. One witness described the phenomenon as a “huge rectangle” on weather radar. The sighting has since raised questions about the nature of the lights, with some attributing them to military flares.

Official statements

“What is that? I think those are UFOs and I really don’t feel comfortable going to sleep tonight,” said one neighbor who witnessed the event.
“These look very clearly to be military flares,” stated Alejandro Rojas, an advisor at Enigma Labs, commenting on the orbs' appearance.

Context

This incident follows a recent uptick in UFO sightings near military sites across the United States. In December, officials reported seeing “flashing red, green, and white lights” at the Joint Base Langley-Eustis in Virginia. The growing number of reports has prompted increased interest in the investigation of unexplained aerial phenomena.

What’s next

As of now, the US Air Force has not released any further information regarding the incident. Investigations into the sightings may continue, and local residents remain on alert for any additional unusual activity in the skies.

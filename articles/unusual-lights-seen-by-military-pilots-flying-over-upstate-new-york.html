Unusual Lights Seen by Military Pilots Flying Over Upstate New York

Two experienced military pilots flying a private jet at 43,000 feet have reported observing three unidentified aircraft while westbound near Jamestown, New York, in early December. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This sighting is significant as it raises questions about unidentified flying objects and the nature of these phenomena. The National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC) has received the report, and air traffic control acknowledged the sighting, prompting further investigation.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred in early December 2023.
  • Location: Near Jamestown, New York, with objects observed over Southern Ontario, Canada.
  • Pilots reported flying at 43,000 feet and a groundspeed of approximately 450 knots.
  • Duration of the sighting was about 20 minutes.
  • Involved two highly experienced military pilots.
  • Contact made with Cleveland Center air traffic control.

Timeline

In early December 2023, two military pilots flying a private jet at 43,000 feet reported seeing three unidentified aircraft while flying near Jamestown, New York. The pilots described the objects as bright white lights that moved in coordinated formations.

The pilots observed the lights for about 20 minutes, noting that they would dim and flare back to high intensity. Despite their high groundspeed, the pilots did not close the distance with the objects. They contacted air traffic control, which acknowledged the sighting.

Official statements

“This was a very strange event involving three aircraft with incredible performance observed from long and indeterminate range by two highly experienced pilots,”

said the report from NUFORC.

Context

This incident adds to a growing collection of unexplained sightings in Upstate New York. The increasing frequency of such reports has garnered attention from both the public and authorities, contributing to ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue investigating the sighting, and further information may be released as more details emerge from the pilots and air traffic control communications.

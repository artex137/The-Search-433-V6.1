More alien files? Trump calls for UFO disclosure but it's already law

Donald Trump has pledged to release government records on aliens and UFOs if he is reelected, following a post on Truth Social on February 19, 2026. Trump's comments come after he criticized former President Barack Obama for discussing his belief in extraterrestrial life.

This pledge raises questions about ongoing efforts for transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). A 2023 law mandates the National Archives and Records Administration to collect and release documents related to UAPs, which include mysterious objects sighted both in the air and underwater.

Key facts

  • Trump made his comments on February 19, 2026.
  • The 2023 law requires the National Archives to gather UAP-related documents.
  • The legislation was signed by President Joe Biden.
  • Witnesses have testified about UAPs in Congress, including a hearing on September 9, 2025.
  • The National Archives began releasing some UFO-related records in late April 2025.

Timeline

On February 19, 2026, Donald Trump announced via Truth Social his intentions to direct agencies to release records on UAPs and extraterrestrial life. This statement followed his criticism of Barack Obama, who had expressed his belief in aliens during a podcast, though he clarified that he had not seen evidence during his presidency.

In 2023, Congress passed legislation requiring the National Archives to establish a collection of documents related to UAPs. This law allows government agencies to maintain classified records if their release could threaten U.S. intelligence operations.

On September 9, 2025, four witnesses testified before Congress regarding their experiences with UAPs, contributing to the growing public interest in the subject.

Official statements

“He’s not supposed to be doing that,” Trump said regarding Obama’s comments about aliens.
“Stay tuned!” said White House spokesperson Anna Kelly when asked how Trump's directive would differ from existing laws.

Context

Interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life has surged in recent years, prompting multiple Congressional hearings. The 2023 law was a response to calls for greater transparency surrounding UAPs, following testimonies from military officials and journalists about secretive government programs aimed at studying unidentified crafts.

Despite the new legislation, agencies retain the power to classify records, which can delay public access to information about UAPs and related technologies.

What’s next

Investigations into UAPs are ongoing, and additional legislation is being considered to compel the federal government to release more information. The National Archives is expected to continue its work in releasing UFO-related documents as mandated by the 2023 law.

What Obama said about UFO files and aliens that made Donald Trump say: He made a big mistake

Published 2026-03-07 06:58 UTC

Obama's Comments on UFOs Spark Reaction from Trump

Former U.S. President Barack Obama has made statements regarding UFO files and extraterrestrial life that have drawn a strong response from former President Donald Trump. The remarks were made during an interview on a podcast aired on October 19, 2023.

This exchange highlights the ongoing public interest in UFO phenomena and government transparency. Current authorities are under pressure to provide more information about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) as public curiosity grows.

Key facts

  • Former President Barack Obama spoke about UFOs on October 19, 2023.
  • Donald Trump responded critically to Obama's comments.
  • The discussion took place during a podcast interview.
  • The topic of UFOs has gained significant public attention in recent years.
  • Government agencies are being urged to release more information on UAPs.

Timeline

On October 19, 2023, Barack Obama participated in a podcast where he discussed the existence of UFOs and the government's knowledge of them. He emphasized the need for transparency regarding UAPs.

Following Obama's remarks, Donald Trump expressed his disapproval, stating that Obama had made "a big mistake" by discussing such sensitive topics publicly. This exchange highlights the ongoing debate about government disclosure on UFOs.

Official statements

“He made a big mistake,” Trump said in reference to Obama’s comments on UFO files.

Context

The topic of UFOs has been a point of contention and curiosity for decades. In recent years, the U.S. government has begun to release previously classified information about UAPs, prompting increased public interest and debate over the existence of extraterrestrial life.

Obama's comments come amid renewed efforts by various agencies to address public concerns regarding UFO sightings and encounters.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue investigating UAP incidents and may release further reports to the public as pressure for transparency increases.

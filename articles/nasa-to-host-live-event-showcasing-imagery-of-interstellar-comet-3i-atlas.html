NASA to Host Live Event Showcasing Imagery of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA will host a live event on Wednesday, Nov. 19, at 3 p.m. EST, to present imagery and insights about the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS at the Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland. The event will be available for viewing on multiple platforms, including NASA+, the NASA app, the agency's official website, YouTube, and Amazon Prime.

This event is significant as 3I/ATLAS is only the third identified object from outside the solar system. Discovered on July 1 by the NASA-funded ATLAS observatory, the comet provides a unique opportunity for astronomical research. Although it poses no threat to Earth, its closest approach has been recorded at 170 million miles, with a notable pass of 19 million miles from Mars in early October.

Key facts

  • Date and Time: Wednesday, Nov. 19, at 3 p.m. EST
  • Location: NASA’s Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt, Maryland
  • Discovery: July 1, by NASA-funded ATLAS observatory
  • Closest Approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Closest Approach to Mars: 19 million miles in early October
  • Key Participants: Amit Kshatriya, Nicky Fox, Shawn Domagal-Goldman, Tom Statler

Timeline

On July 1, the ATLAS observatory discovered comet 3I/ATLAS. In early October, the comet made a close approach to Mars, coming within 19 million miles. NASA has since prepared for a live event to share detailed imagery and insights into the comet's characteristics and significance.

Official statements

“NASA's advanced science missions equip the United States with unparalleled capabilities to observe comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through our solar system,” said Amit Kshatriya, NASA Associate Administrator.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable as only the third interstellar object identified to date. Its discovery has opened new avenues for research in astronomy, particularly in understanding the composition and behavior of celestial bodies from beyond the solar system.

What’s next

Members of the media wishing to attend the virtual event must submit their details to NASA by email at least two hours prior. The public is invited to submit questions during the broadcast using the hashtag #AskNASA, with some questions being answered live during the event.

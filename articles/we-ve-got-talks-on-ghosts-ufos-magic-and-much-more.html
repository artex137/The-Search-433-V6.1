We've got talks on ghosts, UFOs, magic and much more

LAPIS (Lancashire’s Anomalous Phenomena Investigation Society) has announced its annual conference focusing on UFOs, folklore, and other unexplained phenomena, taking place at St Annes Ex-Servicemen’s Club in Blackpool on November 22 and 23, 2023. The event runs from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. each day.

This conference is significant as it draws a diverse array of speakers and attendees interested in various anomalous subjects. LAPIS has invited experts from across the country to share their knowledge and experiences on topics ranging from UFOs to ghosts. The event is open to anyone curious about these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Event: LAPIS Annual Conference
  • Dates: November 22-23, 2023
  • Location: St Annes Ex-Servicemen’s Club, 22 Alexandra Road, Blackpool
  • Time: 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. each day
  • Monthly Meetings: First Thursday of each month at The Guards Club, 37E Whitegate Drive, Blackpool

Timeline

The LAPIS conference is set for the weekend of November 22 and 23, 2023. The society holds informal meetings monthly, providing a platform for discussions on various anomalous topics.

On November 22, several speakers will present, including:

  • Joanna Walpole on "Haunted Chingle Hall in Lancashire - A Tour Guide’s Experience."
  • Gayle Fidler discussing "The Owls Are Not What They Seem - Magic and Occult Musings."
  • Andy Roberts presenting "Flying Saucer Cults and Contactees of the 60s and 70s."
  • Ruth Morris on "Bigfoot - Folklore, Footprints and a whole Lotta Orbs."

On November 23, the following presentations will take place:

  • Stu Neville on "50 Years of Fortean Television."
  • Matthew Williams discussing "UFOs, Crop Circles and Military Secrets: The Long and Mysterious Path to the Red Door."
  • John Billingsley on "Talking Heads - Reflections on a Capital Idea - Images of Human Heads in Folklore."
  • Genevieve Gorrell presenting "Are We Living in an MMRPG (Massively Multiplayer Role Playing Game)?"

Official statements

“These guest speakers are fabulous - they have dedicated their time to their chosen subjects and have a wealth of fascinating knowledge and experience,”

said a LAPIS spokesman.

Context

LAPIS has a history of exploring anomalous phenomena through public engagement. The society meets monthly to discuss various topics and encourages community participation.

What’s next

Further details about ticket prices and additional information about the conference can be found on the LAPIS website.

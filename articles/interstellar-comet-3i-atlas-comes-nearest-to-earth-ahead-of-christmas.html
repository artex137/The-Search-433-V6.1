Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS comes nearest to Earth ahead of Christmas

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, offering a unique opportunity for astronomers and skywatchers. The comet, which is the third confirmed object from outside the solar system, was first reported by the NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile.

The upcoming event is significant as it allows astronomers to study the comet's characteristics and gain insights into planet formation in exoplanetary systems. Observations of 3I/ATLAS could enhance our understanding of how such processes compare to those in our solar system.

Key facts

  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Comet diameter: Estimated between 320 meters (1,000 feet) and 5.6 kilometers (3.5 miles)
  • First observations reported: July 1, 2025
  • Previous closest approach to the Sun: October 2025
  • Visible with: Small telescopes or powerful binoculars

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the ATLAS survey telescope in Chile first reported the interstellar comet, initially named A11pl3Z.

By September 2025, the comet was still visible to ground-based telescopes. It made its closest approach to the Sun in October 2025.

NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope re-observed 3I/ATLAS on November 30, 2025, enhancing the understanding of the comet's size and composition.

Official statements

“On December 19, 2025, this erratic wanderer will be the closest to Earth that it will ever be over the lifetime of the entire universe,” Space.com reported.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable as the third confirmed interstellar object, following 'Oumuamua in 2017 and comet 2I/Borisov in 2019. Its arrival provides a rare chance to study materials that have not been influenced by the solar system's formation.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, astronomers will prepare to observe it closely, aiming to gather data that could inform future research on planetary systems beyond our own.

NASA Activates Earth Defense Group Over Concerns Of Manhattan-Sized Comet With Possible Alien Technology

A NASA-backed planetary defense group has begun efforts to locate comet 3I/ATLAS after reports of unusual behavior from the object. The comet, which is the size of Manhattan and may possess alien technology, has been classified as a threat by the International Asteroid Warning Network (IAWN).

This initiative is significant as it marks the first time an interstellar object has been prioritized by the group, which includes a global coalition of space experts. Authorities aim to assess the potential impact of 3I/ATLAS and enhance preparedness for any threats from space.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed on July 21 by the Hubble Telescope.
  • Size of the comet is comparable to Manhattan.
  • IAWN has listed 3I/ATLAS as a potential threat.
  • Comet discharges a column with four grams of nickel per second.
  • IAWN's "comet campaign" scheduled from November 27, 2025, to January 27, 2026.

Timeline

On July 21, 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS was photographed by the Hubble Telescope. Following this observation, NASA and IAWN noted its unusual behaviors, including an anti-tail and a particle jet directed at the sun. These anomalies prompted the activation of the planetary defense group.

The group has since intensified its focus on the comet, recognizing its potential threat and preparing for a detailed assessment campaign set to begin in late November 2025.

Official statements

“The bizarre behavior of 3I/ATLAS is unprecedented for a comet,” said Dr. Avi Loeb, an astrophysicist at Harvard University. “The non-gravitational acceleration and its unusual trajectory raise questions about its nature.”

Context

This initiative follows a growing global focus on planetary defense against near-Earth objects. The formation of the IAWN represents a collaborative effort to detect and monitor potentially hazardous asteroids and comets.

Previous incidents involving comet observations have underscored the need for preparedness and response strategies in the face of potential threats from space.

What’s next

The IAWN will continue its "comet campaign" from November 27, 2025, through January 27, 2026, to pinpoint the precise location of 3I/ATLAS and assess its potential impact on Earth.

UFO? No, it was a Starlink satellite

Reports of a UFO sighting over parts of the United States have been clarified as a Starlink satellite, according to local authorities. The incident occurred on the evening of October 24, 2023, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This clarification is significant as it highlights the increasing visibility of Starlink satellites in the night sky, which can often be mistaken for unidentified flying objects. Local officials have urged residents to report any unusual sightings to appropriate agencies for accurate identification.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: October 24, 2023
  • Location: Parts of the United States (specific locations not disclosed)
  • Injuries: None reported
  • Authorities involved: Local law enforcement

Timeline

On October 24, 2023, multiple residents across various locations reported seeing bright lights in the sky. Initial reactions led many to believe they were witnessing a UFO.

After receiving numerous calls, local law enforcement investigated the reports. They confirmed that the lights were part of a recent Starlink satellite launch and not an unidentified object.

Official statements

“We understand that these sightings can be alarming,” said a spokesperson for local law enforcement. “However, we encourage everyone to check with us or other sources before jumping to conclusions.”

Context

This incident is part of a growing trend, as more Starlink satellites are launched into orbit to provide global internet coverage. The increasing number of satellites has led to more frequent reports of unidentified objects in the sky, prompting concerns and curiosity among the public.

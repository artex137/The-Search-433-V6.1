Hubble Image Shows 3I/ATLAS Exhibiting Sustained Activity That Defies Comet Breakdown Models

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured a new image of interstellar object 3I/ATLAS on November 30, 2025, revealing sustained activity that contradicts existing comet breakdown models. The image was later reprocessed by independent analyst Ammar A. of SpaceTracker.

This observation is significant as it challenges the traditional understanding of comet behavior. Authorities in the field of planetary defense and astrobiology are closely examining the findings to gain insights into the nature of interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 30, 2025
  • Instrument used: Hubble Space Telescope
  • Analyst: Ammar A. from SpaceTracker
  • Key observation: Object remains organized where fragmentation is expected
  • Behavior: Energy appears sustained, not dissipating

Timeline

On November 30, 2025, the Hubble Space Telescope captures an image of 3I/ATLAS. The image is later reprocessed by Ammar A. of SpaceTracker, providing a new perspective on the object’s activity.

The reprocessed image reveals a luminous nucleus surrounded by a blue ionized region and a broad orange dust envelope. This smooth transition contradicts classical comet models, which suggest that prolonged solar exposure leads to fragmentation.

Official statements

“What it reveals is not disintegration, but persistence,” said Ammar A., highlighting the unusual behavior of 3I/ATLAS.

Context

Traditionally, comets are expected to weaken and fragment under prolonged solar radiation. The activity of 3I/ATLAS suggests a need for a reevaluation of comet classification and behavior, particularly for interstellar objects.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the findings from Hubble and monitor 3I/ATLAS for further insights into its behavior and characteristics.

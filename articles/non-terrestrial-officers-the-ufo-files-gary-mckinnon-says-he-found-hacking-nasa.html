Non-terrestrial officers: the UFO files Gary McKinnon says he found, hacking NASA

Gary McKinnon, a British hacker, claims to have discovered files related to UFOs while illegally accessing NASA's computers between 2001 and 2002. McKinnon, who has faced legal issues in the UK and the US, stated that he found documents that suggested the existence of non-terrestrial officers.

This revelation has sparked interest in the ongoing discussions about UFOs and government transparency. Authorities are reviewing McKinnon's findings, which could influence future investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP).

Key facts

  • Gary McKinnon hacked NASA's systems from 2001 to 2002.
  • He claims to have discovered files related to UFOs and non-terrestrial officers.
  • McKinnon has faced legal challenges in both the UK and the US.
  • The exact number of documents accessed by McKinnon is not disclosed.
  • McKinnon's activities have drawn attention to the issue of UAP.

Timeline

Between 2001 and 2002, Gary McKinnon accessed NASA's computer systems. During this period, he claims to have found files that contained information about UFOs.

In the years following his hacking activities, McKinnon has faced potential extradition to the United States, where he would face charges related to his hacking. His case has raised questions about cybersecurity and the protection of sensitive government information.

Official statements

“I found a file that said there were non-terrestrial officers,” McKinnon stated in an interview.

Context

McKinnon's hacking activities occurred during a time of increasing public interest in UFO sightings and government disclosures regarding UAP. His claims have contributed to a broader conversation about the need for transparency in government agencies regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue reviewing McKinnon's claims and the implications for future investigations into UAP. No specific timelines for further actions have been disclosed.

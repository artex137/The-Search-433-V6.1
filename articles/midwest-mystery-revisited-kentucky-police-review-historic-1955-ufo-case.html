Midwest Mystery Revisited: Kentucky Police Review Historic 1955 UFO Case

Christian County, KY – The Kentucky State Police (KSP) have launched a new three-part series titled “Seeing Stranger Things,” which examines the 1955 Kelly–Hopkinsville UFO encounter. The series explores the unusual case that has puzzled law enforcement and UFO enthusiasts for nearly 70 years.

This initiative is significant as it aims to shed light on one of the most famous UFO incidents in U.S. history. KSP has stated that the series will revisit historical reports and witness accounts while also reinforcing the agency's commitment to community service. The first episode is currently available on KSP’s official social media pages.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on August 21, 1955.
  • Location: small farmhouse near Kelly, Kentucky.
  • KSP has released a three-part digital series titled “Seeing Stranger Things.”
  • Encounters involved glowing figures and a metallic disc.
  • Officers from multiple agencies responded to the incident.
  • Evidence found included bullet holes and shell casings.
  • The encounter has become one of the most widely cited UFO cases in U.S. history.

Timeline

On the night of August 21, 1955, residents at a farmhouse near Kelly reported seeing glowing figures outside their home. They claimed to have observed a “metallic disc” in the sky. In response to their fears, family members fired shots at the creatures and subsequently sought help at the nearby Hopkinsville police station.

Law enforcement, including KSP and soldiers from Fort Campbell, arrived at the scene to investigate. They found bullet holes, shell casings, and signs of struggle but no definitive evidence explaining the reported encounter.

Official statements

KSP stated that the digital series aims to remind the public of the agency’s motto: “When someone in Kentucky needs help, we answer the call.”

Context

The Kelly–Hopkinsville incident is frequently referenced in discussions about UFOs and law enforcement responses to unusual events. This case has sparked interest not only among the public but also within the scientific community, making it a focal point in the study of unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

KSP will continue to release episodes of “Seeing Stranger Things,” aiming to provide further insights into the Kelly–Hopkinsville case and its impact on the community and law enforcement practices.

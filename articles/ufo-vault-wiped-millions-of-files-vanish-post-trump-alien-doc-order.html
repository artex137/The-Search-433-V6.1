UFO Vault Wiped: Millions of Files Vanish Post-Trump Alien Doc Order

A significant data loss has occurred at The Black Vault, a repository of declassified US government documents, just one day after President Donald Trump ordered the release of all UFO-related files. On February 20, 2023, the main server of The Black Vault was reportedly wiped clean, leading to the deletion of hundreds of gigabytes of crucial information, including files related to Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs) and historical conspiracies.

This incident raises concerns about the security of sensitive information. Authorities have not disclosed any ongoing investigations into the data loss, but John Greenewald Jr., the researcher who manages The Black Vault, has stated that he is exploring the cause of the incident.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: February 20, 2023
  • Location: The Black Vault server
  • Files affected: Hundreds of gigabytes, including UFO-related documents
  • Researcher: John Greenewald Jr.
  • Backup status: All files were backed up in multiple locations

Timeline

On February 19, 2023, President Trump issued a directive for the Pentagon to disclose all information regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial life. The following day, The Black Vault experienced a significant data loss, with its server reportedly wiped clean. Greenewald shared the news online, indicating that server permissions and file ownership logs had been altered.

Despite the data loss, Greenewald confirmed that all files had been securely backed up and that The Black Vault was restored shortly after the incident. He expressed concern over the timing of the event, noting that it occurred just hours after the presidential directive.

Official statements

“It is a stark reminder to us all, me included. Keep backups. Keep them in multiple places. And never be intimidated by anything that comes our way, no matter what we expect may have happened.”

- John Greenewald Jr., on X (formerly Twitter)

Context

The Black Vault has been a vital resource for those seeking access to declassified government information for over three decades. It includes military base reports, witness testimonies, and directives from agencies like the CIA, some dating back to the 1940s and 50s. The recent incident follows a history of government denial regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial beings.

Greenewald has filed over 11,000 Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests to obtain documents on UFO incidents, including the alleged Roswell crash in 1947. His research has revealed the existence of secret task forces investigating UFO sightings, such as the alleged Majestic 12 group.

What’s next

Investigations into the data loss have not been disclosed. Greenewald continues to monitor the situation and has indicated that he will explore the cause further, while also emphasizing the importance of data backups.

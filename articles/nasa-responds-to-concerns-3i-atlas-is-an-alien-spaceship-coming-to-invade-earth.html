NASA responds to concerns 3I/ATLAS is an alien spaceship coming to invade Earth

NASA has addressed speculation that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS is an alien starship preparing to invade Earth. Detected earlier this year, 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through the solar system.

This situation has garnered attention due to the object’s unusual brightness and rapid rotation rate. Some experts, including Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, have raised theories suggesting it could be an artificial craft. NASA officials have stated that they are actively monitoring the object, which will pass near the Sun on Halloween Eve.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was first detected earlier in 2023.
  • It is the third confirmed interstellar object observed in the solar system.
  • The object will pass no closer than 170 million miles to Earth.
  • NASA has been tracking 3I/ATLAS with ground and space telescopes.
  • It is expected to pass near Venus, Mars, and Jupiter in the coming months.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was initially discovered in early 2023. Since then, NASA has monitored its trajectory and characteristics. The object will approach the Sun on October 31, 2023, prompting increased scrutiny from scientists and officials.

Official statements

“It looks like a comet. It does comet things. It very, very strongly resembles, in just about every way, the comets that we know,” said Tom Statler, NASA’s lead scientist for small bodies.
“The evidence is overwhelmingly pointing to this object being a natural body. It’s a comet,” Statler added.
“It’s special because we now have the ability to discover these interstellar comets... we’ve just recently had the ability to discover them, and we’ve discovered only three so far,” Statler stated.
“This very well could be the clearest sign of life that we’ve ever found on Mars,” said Acting Administrator Sean Duffy, referring to recent discoveries from the Perseverance rover.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS has sparked numerous theories about alien life and technology. NASA has previously downplayed claims of extraterrestrial encounters, focusing instead on the scientific significance of interstellar objects. Recent findings on Mars have reignited discussions about the potential for life beyond Earth.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun. Officials will assess its behavior and characteristics, providing updates as necessary. The agency remains committed to investigating the potential for life on other planets, especially with ongoing Mars missions.

Wife of Missing Retired Air Force General Pushes Back on ‘Misinformation’

Neil McCasland, a retired major general with the U.S. Air Force, was last seen at his Albuquerque home on February 27. His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, has addressed claims about his possible ties to UFO research, labeling them as "misinformation." She emphasized the importance of clarifying details surrounding his disappearance.

Authorities are actively investigating McCasland's disappearance, having canvassed over 700 homes in the area for security footage and information. The FBI has joined the search, which includes drone operations and K9 units. McCasland's case has garnered significant attention, partly due to the speculation surrounding his alleged connections to UFOs.

Key facts

  • Neil McCasland was last seen on February 27 at his home in Albuquerque.
  • His wife claims misinformation has circulated regarding his ties to the UFO community.
  • Over 700 homes have been canvassed for information by investigators.
  • The FBI Albuquerque Field Office is involved in the search.
  • Items missing from McCasland's home include his hiking boots, wallet, and a .38 caliber revolver.
  • A U.S. Air Force sweatshirt was found over a mile from his home, but its ownership has not been confirmed.

Timeline

Neil McCasland was last seen at his residence on February 27. Following his disappearance, a silver alert was issued, citing unspecified medical issues. Investigators began canvassing the area, gathering information and searching for leads. The FBI joined the investigation shortly thereafter, providing additional resources. As of now, the case remains active, with no evidence of foul play reported.

Official statements

“Though at this point with absolutely no sign of him, maybe the best hypothesis is that aliens beamed him up to the mothership,” said Susan McCasland Wilkerson in a Facebook post.
“BCSO continues to pursue all credible leads and follow up on every tip received. While there is currently no evidence indicating foul play, investigators are examining all available information as the case remains active,” the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office stated.

Context

Neil McCasland served as commander of the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, managing a significant science and technology program. His brief association with the UFO community through Tom DeLonge, founder of To The Stars, has fueled speculation about his disappearance. However, McCasland's wife has clarified that he does not possess any special knowledge related to his past work.

What’s next

Investigators will continue to search for leads and urge the public to check security footage for any relevant information. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office has indicated that they will provide updates as new information becomes available.

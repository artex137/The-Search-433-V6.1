Dr. Steven Greer: Many UFO Sightings Are Likely Man-Made

Dr. Steven Greer has claimed that many UFO sightings, including one reported by former Naval Commander David Fravor, are likely the result of human-made technology. This assertion was made during a recent interview, where Greer discussed the implications of such technologies on society.

This matter is significant as it challenges the traditional understanding of unidentified flying objects and suggests that advanced technologies may be deliberately hidden from the public. Greer argues that these technologies could provide solutions for major global issues, including poverty and climate change. He plans to release the Disclosure Project's archive, which includes thousands of documents and testimonies, to the public within the next year.

Timeline

David Fravor reported his sighting of a tic-tac-shaped object during a military exercise in 2004. The object displayed flight capabilities that defy typical aircraft technology. Dr. Steven Greer has since analyzed this incident, concluding that the object likely stemmed from advanced human technology.

In the following years, Greer has been vocal about the implications of such technologies, emphasizing their potential to address pressing global challenges. His advocacy has led to plans for releasing extensive documentation to the public.

Official statements

“The technology has been deliberately kept secret because its revelation would disrupt the existing infrastructure built around oil, gas, coal, and nuclear power,” said Dr. Greer.
“The hidden technology could address issues such as poverty and climate change,” he added.

This claim by Greer aligns with a broader trend of increasing public interest in UFOs and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. High-profile incidents, such as Fravor's sighting, have prompted discussions about the nature of these objects and the possibility of advanced technologies existing outside public knowledge.

Previous investigations into UFO sightings have often been met with skepticism, but recent disclosures have led to renewed scrutiny of military and governmental practices regarding aerial phenomena.

This NASA image shows interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA has released an image of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which was captured by the L'LORRI black-and-white imager on the Lucy spacecraft. The image was published on November 20, 2025, and highlights the comet as a significant astronomical phenomenon.

This image is important as it provides insights into the composition and behavior of interstellar comets. NASA’s Lucy mission aims to study these celestial objects, which can enhance our understanding of the early solar system. The agency continues to monitor and analyze data from the comet as part of its ongoing research.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Image date: November 20, 2025
  • Imager used: L'LORRI on the Lucy spacecraft
  • Location of observation: Not disclosed

Timeline

NASA's Lucy spacecraft has been in operation since its launch, collecting data on various celestial bodies. On November 20, 2025, the spacecraft captured a clear image of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS using its L'LORRI imager.

The Lucy mission aims to gather data on the early solar system by studying the characteristics of comets and asteroids.

Context

Prior to this observation, NASA has engaged in various missions aimed at studying comets and asteroids. The Lucy mission, which was launched to explore the Trojan asteroids, is now also focusing on interstellar objects.

What’s next

NASA will continue to analyze the data collected from the Lucy spacecraft and 3I/ATLAS to further understand interstellar comets and their implications for the solar system's history.

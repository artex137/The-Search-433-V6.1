Published 2026-02-10 02:16 UTC

Donald Trump May Announce UFO Evidence in Upcoming Speech

Former President Donald Trump may be preparing to deliver a significant speech regarding decades of alleged UFO evidence, according to claims made by Mark Christopher Lee, a British writer and ufologist. Lee stated that an advisor to the Trump administration informed him that Trump has received permission from world leaders to make an official announcement about unidentified flying objects. The address is expected to take place on 8 July, coinciding with the 79th anniversary of the Roswell incident, after initially being scheduled for the United Nations General Assembly in September.

This potential announcement has raised interest among UFO enthusiasts and the general public. Authorities have not disclosed specific details about the content of the speech, but Lee claims it will reveal the truth behind unexplained aerial phenomena, materials recovered from alleged crash sites, and military eyewitness accounts that suggest extraterrestrial origins. The address may also touch on national security and transparency issues.

Key facts

  • Speaker: Donald Trump, former President of the United States
  • Informant: Mark Christopher Lee, ufologist and writer
  • Initial scheduled date: September 2023 at the United Nations General Assembly
  • Rescheduled date: 8 July 2023, 79th anniversary of the Roswell incident
  • Key incidents referenced: USS Nimitz (2004), USS Theodore Roosevelt GoFast and Gimbal (2015), Roswell (1947)

Timeline

Mark Christopher Lee claims that an advisor to the Trump administration has communicated Trump's authority to disclose UFO information. The speech was originally intended for the UN General Assembly in September but has been shifted to July 8 to mark the Roswell anniversary. Lee indicated that new intelligence developments have made the announcement urgent.

In 2004, the USS Nimitz incident involved a Navy pilot witnessing an unidentified object off the coast of San Diego. In 2015, pilots aboard the USS Theodore Roosevelt documented multiple unexplained aerial encounters. These incidents have been scrutinized in congressional hearings, increasing public interest in the topic.

Official statements

“He will confirm that forensic analysis of recovered off-world vehicles and non-human biologics has established their extraterrestrial origin,” Lee stated. “I do feel that President Trump likes to act spontaneously and may not want to wait until July 8.”

Context

Prior to this announcement, there have been several well-documented UFO encounters involving the U.S. military. The 2004 Nimitz incident and the 2015 encounters have drawn significant attention, particularly following testimonies from military personnel. Congressional hearings have also scrutinized these incidents, contributing to ongoing public debates about UFOs and government transparency.

What’s next

As the date of the potential announcement approaches, further developments regarding the content and timing of Trump's speech are expected. Investigations into UFO phenomena and their implications for national security may continue to gain attention in both public and governmental spheres.

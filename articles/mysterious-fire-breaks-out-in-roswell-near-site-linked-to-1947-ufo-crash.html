Mysterious fire breaks out in Roswell near site linked to 1947 UFO crash

A large fire has broken out at the Roswell Air Center late Thursday, just a few miles from Hangar 84, a site associated with the infamous UFO crash of 1947. Firefighters responded to reports of flames at approximately 19:55 p.m. local time on East Earl Cummings Loop. Authorities have reported no injuries and have not confirmed whether any historically significant objects were affected.

The fire has drawn attention due to its proximity to Hangar 84, which has long been a focal point for UFO enthusiasts. Authorities have expanded the security perimeter and evacuated nearby buildings due to concerns about hazardous materials and oxygen cylinders stored in the vicinity. Firefighters declared the fire under control around 21:45 p.m. on Thursday. The cause of the fire remains under investigation.

Key facts

  • Fire reported at 19:55 p.m. local time on Thursday.
  • Location: Roswell Air Center, East Earl Cummings Loop.
  • Fire declared under control at 21:45 p.m.
  • No injuries reported.
  • Hazardous materials and oxygen cylinders stored nearby.
  • Cause of the fire is currently unknown.

Timeline

The fire was first reported around 19:55 p.m. local time on Thursday. Firefighters quickly arrived on the scene and began battling the flames. Due to the presence of hazardous materials, authorities expanded the security perimeter and evacuated nearby buildings. The situation was declared under control by 21:45 p.m.

Official statements

“We have contained the fire, and there are no reports of injuries,” said a spokesperson for the Roswell Fire Department.

Context

The Roswell incident of 1947 has been a significant point of interest for decades, following the U.S. military's initial claim of finding debris from a "flying saucer." The subsequent retraction, which stated that the wreckage was from a weather balloon, has fueled conspiracy theories and speculation about government cover-ups related to UFOs.

What’s next

Investigators will continue to examine the fire's cause and assess any potential damage to the site. Further updates are expected as the investigation progresses.

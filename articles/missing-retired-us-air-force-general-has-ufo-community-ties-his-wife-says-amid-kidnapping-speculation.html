Missing retired US Air Force general has ‘UFO community’ ties, his wife says amid kidnapping speculation

Retired US Air Force General William “Neil” McCasland has been missing since February 27, last seen in Albuquerque, New Mexico. His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, has addressed speculation surrounding his disappearance, stating he has connections to the UFO community but does not possess critical information that would warrant kidnapping.

Authorities are currently investigating McCasland’s disappearance, seeking assistance from the public to locate him. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has reached out to over 600 households in Albuquerque for home camera footage. The FBI is also involved in the case.

Key facts

  • McCasland, 68, was last seen on February 27 in Albuquerque.
  • He has a history with the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio.
  • Wilkerson noted he had a brief association with Tom DeLonge, co-founder of a UFO research company.
  • Over 600 households have been contacted for security footage by the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.
  • The FBI is assisting in the investigation into McCasland's disappearance.

Timeline

February 27: William "Neil" McCasland was last seen in Albuquerque, New Mexico.

March 6: His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, addressed misinformation about his ties to the UFO community on Facebook.

March 12: The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office began soliciting home camera footage from local residents as part of the investigation.

Official statements

“Neil does not have any special knowledge about the ET bodies and debris from the Roswell crash stored at Wright-Patt,” Wilkerson wrote on Facebook.
“This connection is not a reason for someone to abduct Neil,” she added.
“He was not confused and disoriented,” Wilkerson clarified regarding her husband's health.
“Though at this point with absolutely no sign of him, maybe the best hypothesis is that aliens beamed him up to the mothership,” she joked.

Context

McCasland has been linked to the UFO community due to his past association with Tom DeLonge, who co-founded a company focused on unidentified aerial phenomena. This connection has attracted speculation, particularly given McCasland's previous role at a facility associated with the infamous Roswell incident.

What’s next

Investigators will continue to search for McCasland and assess public submissions of home camera footage. Further updates are expected as authorities work to locate him.

European star Ludvig Aberg yet to embrace Viktor Hovland’s passion for UFOs

European golfer Ludvig Aberg has expressed hesitance in joining Viktor Hovland’s enthusiasm for UFO spotting, despite their strong friendship. The two players are set to reunite at the Ryder Cup, which will take place in Rome next week.

This dynamic is noteworthy as it highlights differing interests between two prominent figures in European golf. As the Ryder Cup approaches, Aberg's remarks have drawn attention to the lighter side of competitive sports, showcasing how personal relationships can influence team dynamics.

Key facts

  • Ludvig Aberg is 25 years old.
  • The Ryder Cup is scheduled to take place next week in Rome.
  • Aberg and Hovland previously played together in the Ryder Cup in 2023.
  • They defeated Scottie Scheffler and Brooks Koepka nine and seven in foursomes.
  • Aberg turned professional three months before the 2023 Ryder Cup.

Timeline

In 2023, Ludvig Aberg made his Ryder Cup debut alongside Viktor Hovland, where they achieved a significant victory against top competitors.

As of September 2025, Aberg has maintained a close friendship with Hovland, who has a keen interest in UFOs.

Aberg has acknowledged their camaraderie but has not yet embraced Hovland's passion for UFOs.

Official statements

“We’re good friends. We speak the same language, we come from a very similar background,” Aberg said. “Obviously Norway and Sweden are very close in terms of the cultural environment.”
“He’s a funny guy to have conversations with. They can go in any direction that you don’t really expect,” Aberg added regarding Hovland.

Context

The relationship between Aberg and Hovland underscores the importance of friendship in team sports, particularly during high-stakes events like the Ryder Cup. Their success in 2023 has set high expectations for their performance in the upcoming tournament.

UFO spotting has become an intriguing hobby for Hovland, contrasting with Aberg’s more traditional interests in golf.

What’s next

As the Ryder Cup approaches, Aberg and Hovland will prepare to compete, with their friendship and differing interests likely influencing their performance on the course.

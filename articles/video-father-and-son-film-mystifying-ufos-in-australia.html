Video: Father and Son Film Mystifying UFOs in Australia

Published 2025-09-25 19:47 UTC

Father and Son Film UFOs in Geraldton, Australia

A father and son have filmed a trio of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in the night sky in Geraldton, Australia, on September 10, 2025. Shane Plane and his son, Brad, captured the unusual lights while driving along a road in the coastal city.

This sighting is part of a series of unexplained aerial phenomena reported in the region. Local authorities, including the Perth Observatory, are investigating the incidents as multiple sightings have been documented across Western Australia.

Key facts

  • Event date: September 10, 2025
  • Location: Geraldton, Western Australia
  • Sighters: Shane Plane and his son, Brad
  • Duration of sighting: Over half an hour
  • Observatory involvement: Perth Observatory

Timeline

On September 10, 2025, while driving, Shane Plane noticed a peculiar star in the sky. He and his son decided to go to the beach for a better view. Upon arrival, they observed three bright lights moving in a triangular formation. One light eventually separated from the group, moving upwards and slowing near another light.

Brad noted that the lights resembled flares but argued that flares cannot sustain such movement and duration. Shane expressed skepticism about conventional explanations, leaving the possibility of extraterrestrial origins open.

Official statements

"It's very, very confusing," said Matt Woods from the Perth Observatory, who has received similar reports from multiple sources in the region.
"It's going to be interesting to see what it winds up being, if we do find out what it is," Woods added.

Context

Recent weeks have seen a spike in UFO sightings across the Midwest and coastal areas of Western Australia. The Perth Observatory has been actively gathering reports and investigating these anomalies.

Authorities have ruled out conventional air traffic as a cause, suggesting the possibility of experimental devices or drone trials as alternatives.

What’s next

Investigations by the Perth Observatory and other local authorities are ongoing. The outcomes of these inquiries may provide clarity on the recent sightings in the region.

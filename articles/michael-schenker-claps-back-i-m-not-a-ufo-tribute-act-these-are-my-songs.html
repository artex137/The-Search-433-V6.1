Michael Schenker Claps Back: I’m Not a UFO Tribute Act, These Are My Songs

Published 2025-09-03 19:23 UTC

Michael Schenker Clarifies His Role in UFO's Legacy

Michael Schenker has responded to misconceptions regarding his association with UFO songs, asserting that he is the original creator of the material, in a statement shared on Chaoszine. The guitarist's comments came during an interview about his recent five-week European tour, where he has performed songs from the 1970s era of UFO.

This clarification is significant as it addresses a misunderstanding among fans who view Schenker as merely a tribute act. He aims to educate audiences about his contributions to UFO's music and highlight the importance of recognizing his role in the band's history.

Key facts

  • Michael Schenker made his statements during an interview on Chaoszine.
  • He recently completed a five-week European tour featuring UFO songs from the 1970s.
  • Schenker clarified that he is the original creator of these songs.
  • He joined UFO in 1973 at the age of 18, coming from the Scorpions.
  • Schenker's contributions helped shape UFO's sound and success in the hard rock genre.
  • His tenure with UFO included the creation of iconic tracks like “Doctor Doctor” and “Rock Bottom.”

Timeline

In 1973, Michael Schenker joined UFO as a substitute for guitarist Bernie Marsden, marking a pivotal shift in the band's musical direction. His arrival led to the creation of albums such as Phenomenon and Force It, which featured enduring tracks. In 1978, Schenker left the band during the mixing of their live album Strangers in the Night, despite being a key contributor to their success.

Recently, Schenker has embarked on a European tour, focusing on his past work with UFO. In an interview, he addressed the misconceptions surrounding his role, emphasizing that these songs are his creations rather than merely being part of a tribute act.

Official statements

“No, no, wait, wait, wait, wait, wait, wait. It was Michael Schenker songs,” Schenker said, correcting the premise about his tour. “Many people are actually under the illusion that I’m just a tribute; they even call me a good tribute act.”
“They don’t realize—they’re not knowledgeable enough to understand—that these are my songs,” he continued, expressing his desire to educate audiences about his historical contributions.

Context

Michael Schenker's influence on UFO has been widely recognized, with his songwriting and guitar work defining the band's transition from space rock to melodic hard rock. His contributions have left a lasting impact on the genre, influencing many musicians who followed. Schenker's role has often been overshadowed by misconceptions that label him as a tribute artist.

What’s next

Schenker plans to continue his efforts to educate audiences about his contributions and celebrate his legacy with UFO. He aims to further clarify his role in the band's history as he performs his music on tour.

Michigan Mutual UFO Network to give presentation at local library

The Chippewa River District Library will host a presentation on Michigan UFO phenomena led by Bill Konkolesky, the State Director of the Michigan Mutual UFO Network (MUFON), on Monday, October 6, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. at its Mount Pleasant location. The event is free and open to the public.

This presentation is significant as it aims to educate attendees about UFO sightings in Michigan, a topic of growing interest. Bill Konkolesky, a leading authority in this field, will share insights from his research and investigations. The event encourages community engagement and discussion on UFO phenomena.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 6, 2025
  • Start time: 4:30 p.m.
  • Location: Chippewa River District Library, Mount Pleasant
  • Presenter: Bill Konkolesky, State Director, Michigan MUFON
  • Admission: Free, no registration required

Timeline

On October 6, 2025, at 4:30 p.m., Bill Konkolesky will lead a presentation at the Chippewa River District Library in Mount Pleasant. The event will cover notable local UFO cases, the history of sightings, and current activities of Michigan MUFON.

Attendees will have the opportunity to engage in a discussion and ask questions after the presentation.

Official statements

“This program welcomes anyone interested in Michigan’s mysterious skies, from UFO enthusiasts to the simply curious,” said a spokesperson for the Chippewa River District Library.

Context

The Michigan Mutual UFO Network is a nonprofit organization that focuses on the scientific study of UFOs. This event aligns with a growing interest in UFO phenomena across the state, as community organizations seek to engage the public in discussions about unexplained aerial sightings.

What’s next

Following the presentation, attendees will be encouraged to participate in a discussion and ask questions, fostering an interactive environment to explore the topic further.

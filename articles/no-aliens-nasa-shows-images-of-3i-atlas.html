No Aliens: NASA Shows Images of 3I/ATLAS

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) has released new images of the celestial body 3I/ATLAS during a press conference following the end of a budget freeze. The agency has confirmed that the comet is not an alien spacecraft, as some speculated, but rather a comet entering our solar system from interstellar space. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This announcement is significant as it marks the culmination of an observation campaign involving twelve probes that have captured images of 3I/ATLAS. NASA aims to enhance scientific understanding of this unique comet and its characteristics compared to other comets in our solar system.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS discovered in early July 2023.
  • Comet will come closest to Earth on December 19, 2023, at a distance of over 273 million kilometers.
  • Twelve probes have captured images of the comet.
  • Images include observations from the Solar Terrestrial Relations Observatory (STEREO), Solar and Heliospheric Observatory (SOHO), Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO), and Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution (MAVEN).
  • The European Space Agency's Trace Gas Orbiter also observed the comet.
  • China's National Space Administration has released images of 3I/ATLAS.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in early July 2023. Following its discovery, NASA's operations were largely halted due to a budget freeze, delaying the release of information and images of the comet. As the budget freeze ended, NASA held a press conference to present images from various space probes, showcasing the comet's journey through the solar system.

The comet is set to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2023, at a distance of over 273 million kilometers, which is nearly twice the distance from Earth to the sun.

Official statements

"By observing the comet from so many locations, NASA has the opportunity to learn more about how 3I/ATLAS differs from the comets in our solar system and to provide scientists with new insights into how other systems differ from our own," NASA announced.
"This object is a comet," emphasized Deputy NASA Director Amit Kshatrya. "It looks like a comet, behaves like a comet, all indications point to it being a comet."

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third known celestial body to have entered our solar system from interstellar space, which is significant for astrophysical research. The comet's initial images did not show a tail, leading to speculation about its nature. However, NASA has clarified that the delays in releasing information were due to operational limitations during the budget freeze.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth, providing further insights and images from ongoing observations leading up to its closest encounter in December.

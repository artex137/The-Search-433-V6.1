2025 PN7: Scientists Debate Whether New Quasi-Moon Could Be Alien Probe

A newly discovered object, named 2025 PN7, has sparked debate among scientists regarding its origins and nature. This “quasi-moon,” which orbits near Earth, has been linked to the interstellar comet C/2025 P1 (ATLAS), also known as comet 31. Some experts, including Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb, have raised the possibility that it could be an alien probe, although most astronomers consider it a naturally captured asteroid.

The discovery of 2025 PN7 is significant because it coincides with NASA's tracking of Comet C/2025 P1 (ATLAS), which is set to pass Earth at a distance of approximately 170 million miles on October 30, 2025. Authorities, including NASA, have confirmed that this comet poses no threat to Earth.

Key facts

  • Object name: 2025 PN7
  • Associated comet: C/2025 P1 (ATLAS)
  • Distance from Earth during closest approach: approximately 170 million miles
  • Projected closest approach date: October 30, 2025
  • Proposed theory: potential alien probe or artificial structure
  • Primary proponent of the alien theory: Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist

Timeline

The discussion around 2025 PN7 began with its discovery as a quasi-moon orbiting near Earth. Following this, NASA announced its tracking of Comet C/2025 P1 (ATLAS), which is traveling at hyperbolic speeds and will pass close to Mars. Avi Loeb has since suggested that both objects could be linked, proposing that 3I/ATLAS might be more than just a natural celestial body.

Official statements

“Humanity’s recorded history—barely 8,000 years—is insignificant compared to the 13.8-billion-year age of the Milky Way,” Loeb explained. “In that vast timespan, intelligent life likely evolved long before us.”

Context

This discussion is not the first time scientists have speculated about extraterrestrial life. Loeb previously gained attention for his views on Oumuamua, another interstellar object, which he suggested could be an artificial spacecraft. The debate over 2025 PN7 and its potential connections to alien technology continues to attract interest in the scientific community.

What’s next

NASA and other agencies will continue to monitor both 2025 PN7 and Comet C/2025 P1 (ATLAS) as they approach their closest points to Earth. Further research and analysis may provide more insights into the nature of these celestial objects.

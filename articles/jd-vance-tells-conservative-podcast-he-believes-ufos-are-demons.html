JD Vance tells conservative podcast he believes UFOs are demons

In an interview on October 10, 2023, Vice President J.D. Vance stated that he believes UFOs are demons during a discussion on the Benny Johnson Show. Vance spoke about his fascination with UFOs and presented a theological perspective on the phenomenon.

This statement has sparked interest and debate regarding the government's stance on unidentified aerial phenomena. Vance expressed a desire to access documents related to UFOs and extraterrestrial beings in the coming years, indicating a potential focus on transparency in the Executive Vice-President's office.

Key facts

  • Interview date: October 10, 2023
  • Location: Benny Johnson Show, not disclosed
  • Vance's position: Vice President, United States
  • Timeframe for document access: Over the next three years

Timeline

During the interview, Vance discussed his childhood obsession with UFO files and his curiosity about the subject. He initially stated, “I don’t think they’re aliens, I think they’re demons,” explaining his viewpoint further when prompted.

Vance related his beliefs to a Christian understanding of good and evil, asserting that the idea of demons aligns with his interpretation of unidentified phenomena.

Official statements

“When I hear about extra natural phenomenon, that’s where I go: The Christian understanding that there’s a lot of good out there, but there’s also evil out there,” Vance said.
“I think that one of the devil’s great is to convince people that he never existed,” he added.

Context

Vance's comments reflect a broader trend among some political figures who intertwine religious beliefs with discussions of science and unexplained phenomena. This perspective has gained traction in various conservative circles, influencing public discourse on UFOs.

What’s next

Vance has indicated he hopes to gain access to documents regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial beings within the next three years, which may lead to further discussions on the topic within the government and among the public.

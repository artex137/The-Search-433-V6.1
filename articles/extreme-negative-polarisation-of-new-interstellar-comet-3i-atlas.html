Extreme Negative Polarisation of New Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

Scientists have reported the first polarimetric observations of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1) in a study published on September 5, 2025. The observations were conducted pre-perihelion using FORS2/VLT, ALFOSC/NOT, and FoReRo2/RCC over a phase angle range of 7.7 to 22.4 degrees. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this astronomical event.

This research is significant as it adds to the understanding of interstellar bodies. The findings indicate that 3I/ATLAS may represent a new type of comet, expanding the known diversity of such objects in the universe. Researchers have noted that this polarimetric study is only the second of its kind, following the earlier study of 2I/Borisov, which displayed a positive polarisation unlike 3I/ATLAS.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1)
  • Observation Dates: Pre-perihelion, reported on September 5, 2025
  • Phase Angle Range: 7.7° to 22.4°
  • Minimum Polarisation Value: -2.7% at 7° phase angle
  • Inversion Angle: 17°
  • Involved Researchers: Zuri Gray, Stefano Bagnulo, Galin Borisov, and others
  • Study Published In: ApJL (Astrophysical Journal Letters)

Timeline

Initial observations of 3I/ATLAS were conducted using advanced telescopes, including FORS2/VLT, ALFOSC/NOT, and FoReRo2/RCC. Researchers have gathered data over a phase angle range of 7.7 to 22.4 degrees, leading to a detailed analysis of its polarimetric properties. The findings were compiled and submitted for publication on September 5, 2025.

The study highlights that 3I/ATLAS exhibits a deep and narrow negative polarisation branch, a characteristic not previously observed in other known comets or asteroids, including 2I/Borisov. The findings suggest that this comet may possess unique physical properties, possibly including water ice.

Official statements

“Our polarimetric measurements reveal that 3I is characterised by a deep and narrow negative polarisation branch, reaching a minimum value of -2.7%,” said Zuri Gray, one of the leading researchers in the study.

Context

Prior to the discovery of 3I/ATLAS, the only other interstellar comet studied in detail was 2I/Borisov, which exhibited a different polarisation characteristic. The study of interstellar objects is crucial for understanding the materials and processes that exist outside our Solar System.

What’s next

Following this study, researchers plan to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS to gather more data on its composition and behaviour as it approaches its perihelion. Further investigations may provide insights into the origins and nature of interstellar comets.

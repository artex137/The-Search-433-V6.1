Astronaut Gordon Cooper’s Lost Confession: Auction House to Sell Rights to Historic Interview Challenging UFO Disclosure

The auction house Bonhams has announced it will sell the rights to a historic interview with late astronaut Gordon Cooper that challenges government disclosure on unidentified flying objects (UFOs). The auction is set to take place on November 16, 2023, in Los Angeles, California.

This auction is significant as it may provide new insights into the long-debated topic of UFOs and the government's transparency regarding their existence. Authorities and UFO enthusiasts alike are closely monitoring the sale, hoping it will shed light on Cooper's claims about his experiences with UFOs during his career as an astronaut.

Key facts

  • Auction date: November 16, 2023
  • Location: Los Angeles, California
  • Auction house: Bonhams
  • Subject of auction: Rights to an interview with Gordon Cooper
  • Cooper's profession: NASA astronaut

Timeline

Gordon Cooper, a prominent astronaut, has long been associated with claims regarding UFOs, asserting that he witnessed unexplained aerial phenomena during his career. The interview, which is now being auctioned, reportedly includes Cooper's reflections on these experiences.

The auction house Bonhams has made arrangements for the sale, and interested parties have begun to express their intentions to bid on the rights to Cooper's statements.

Official statements

“This auction represents a unique opportunity to access the thoughts of one of NASA's most famous astronauts on a topic that continues to fascinate the public,” said a Bonhams spokesperson.

Context

There has been a growing interest in UFOs and government disclosure in recent years, particularly following the release of military videos showing unidentified aerial phenomena. Cooper's statements have been part of this larger conversation, as they challenge the narrative surrounding government transparency on such matters.

What’s next

As the auction date approaches, Bonhams will continue to promote the sale, and potential bidders will prepare to participate in what is expected to be a competitive auction for Cooper’s historic insights.

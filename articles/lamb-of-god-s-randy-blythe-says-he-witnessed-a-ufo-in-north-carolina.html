LAMB OF GOD’s Randy Blythe Says He Witnessed A UFO In North Carolina

LAMB OF GOD frontman Randy Blythe has reported a UFO sighting off the coast of North Carolina. Blythe shared his experience during an interview for Metal Hammer, detailing the incident that occurred while he was at a rental property on Oak Island.

This sighting has drawn attention as Blythe discussed the encounter in response to a question from CLUTCH singer Neil Fallon. The revelation adds to the ongoing public interest in UFOs and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), which have gained traction in recent years.

Key facts

  • Who: Randy Blythe, frontman of LAMB OF GOD
  • What: Reported UFO sighting
  • Where: Oak Island, North Carolina
  • When: Not disclosed
  • Witnesses: Blythe, T-Roy from SOURVEIN, and his girlfriend
  • Details: Two sets of four bright lights in a square pattern

Timeline

Blythe described the encounter while sitting on the front porch of his rental house with two friends. He noted that they observed two sets of bright lights moving quickly over the ocean. The lights moved horizontally, then ascended and descended before shooting upwards at a high speed.

Blythe ruled out conventional explanations for the lights, stating that they were neither military vehicles nor drones. He expressed his belief that the object he witnessed was indeed an unidentified flying object.

Official statements

“Yes, I’m pretty sure I’ve seen one... We were honestly freaked out,” Blythe said. “I think it’s improbable that we’re the only intelligent thing in the universe. And I definitely saw an unidentified flying object that night.”

Context

The public's fascination with UFOs has increased, especially following recent disclosures from military agencies regarding UAPs. Blythe's account adds a personal narrative to this broader discussion about potential extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

LAMB OF GOD is set to release their twelfth studio album, *Into Oblivion*, on March 13. The band has planned a series of nationwide listening parties at over 140 independent record stores during the album's release weekend from March 13 to 15.

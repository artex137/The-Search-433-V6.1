JD Vance says UFOs, aliens could be ‘spiritual forces’ as VP vows to ‘get to the bottom’ of mystery in skies

Vice President JD Vance has discussed the mystery of UFOs, suggesting that some unexplained phenomena may represent "spiritual forces" affecting the physical world. Vance made these comments during an interview with New York Post columnist Miranda Devine on her podcast "Pod Force One" this week.

Vance's remarks come amid growing interest in UFOs, officially known as unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs), in Washington. He stated that he intends to investigate further, emphasizing the need to uncover the truth behind these occurrences. "I can’t allow myself to become so busy that I don’t get to the bottom of this," Vance said.

Key facts

  • Vice President JD Vance made statements regarding UFOs on "Pod Force One" podcast.
  • Interview took place this week; specific date not disclosed.
  • Vance emphasized the importance of investigating UAPs.
  • He discussed these topics with Secretary of State Marco Rubio.
  • Vance described his belief in spiritual forces influencing the physical world.
  • Rep. Jared Moskowitz has accused the federal government of concealing information about UAPs.

Timeline

During the interview, Vance acknowledged that he has not yet had the time to conduct a thorough investigation into UAPs, stating that he has been very busy. He noted that both he and Marco Rubio have long shared an interest in the subject.

Vance mentioned that he views unknown phenomena through a lens of faith rather than fear and expressed his belief in spiritual forces that many do not see or understand. He reiterated his commitment to seeking answers about UAPs.

Official statements

“I can’t allow myself to become so busy that I don’t get to the bottom of this. I will get to the bottom of this,” Vance said.
“Is it aliens or is it our guardian angel or a force that actively wishes us harm? I don’t know the answer to that question,” he added.

Context

Interest in UAPs has surged in Washington, with recent hearings by lawmakers calling for transparency regarding military whistleblower accounts of sightings. In September, Rep. Jared Moskowitz accused the federal government of withholding information about UAPs, emphasizing the need for accountability.

The bipartisan panel that held the hearings is focused on declassifying materials and enhancing protections for whistleblowers who report UAP incidents.

What’s next

Vance has indicated his intent to investigate UAPs further. Meanwhile, lawmakers continue to press for greater transparency from defense and intelligence agencies regarding unexplained aerial encounters.

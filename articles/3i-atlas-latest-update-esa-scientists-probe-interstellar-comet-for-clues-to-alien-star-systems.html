3I/ATLAS Latest Update: ESA Scientists Probe Interstellar Comet For Clues To Alien Star Systems

European scientists have captured detailed images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS with the European Space Agency's JUICE spacecraft on 6 November 2025, while the mission was en route through the outer Solar System. This observation occurred shortly after the comet's closest approach to the Sun on 29 October 2025.

The data collected from this fly-by is significant as it may provide insights into the composition and behavior of alien planetary systems. ESA mission controllers have confirmed that five instruments aboard JUICE were focused on 3I/ATLAS, gathering various measurements that scientists are now analyzing. The first scientific results are expected to be shared in late March 2026.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in June 2025 by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS).
  • It is the third confirmed interstellar comet, with its path verified by the International Astronomical Union's Minor Planet Center.
  • JUICE captured over 120 images of the comet during its observation on 6 November 2025.
  • The comet made its closest approach to the Sun on 29 October 2025.
  • ESA will present initial scientific results from the data in late March 2026.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first identified in June 2025 during automated scans by ATLAS. Following orbital calculations, it was confirmed as an interstellar object. The comet passed behind the Sun from Earth's perspective before JUICE observed it on 6 November 2025, after its perihelion.

During the observation, JUICE utilized its JANUS camera and other instruments to capture detailed images and data, despite the comet dimming rapidly as it moved away from the Sun.

Official statements

“The detail matters. The data show hints of jets, rays, filaments and streams within the coma and tail structure,” ESA stated regarding the findings from the observation of 3I/ATLAS.

Context

Before 3I/ATLAS, only two interstellar objects had been firmly confirmed: one interstellar asteroid and one interstellar comet. The arrival of 3I/ATLAS offers a rare opportunity to understand whether comets from different planetary systems are formed from similar materials and conditions as those in our Solar System.

Interstellar comets are unique as they travel on hyperbolic paths and provide essential data about the formation of star systems beyond our own.

What’s next

ESA scientists are currently analyzing the data collected from JUICE. The agency plans to release the first scientific results in late March 2026, which will shed light on the implications of 3I/ATLAS for understanding alien star systems.

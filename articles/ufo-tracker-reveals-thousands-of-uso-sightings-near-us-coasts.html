UFO Tracker Reveals Thousands of USO Sightings Near US Coasts

A popular UFO-reporting app, Enigma, has recorded thousands of sightings of Unidentified Submersible Objects (USOs) near US waterways since its launch in late 2022. High-ranking US Navy officials have expressed concerns that these underwater phenomena could pose a threat to national security.

The findings from Enigma, which describes itself as the "largest queryable historical sighting database for global UFO sightings," highlight an increasing interest in unidentified phenomena. The app has documented over 30,000 reports of Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs) and Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena, with numerous instances of strange objects emerging from or plunging into the sea without causing a splash.

Key facts

  • Enigma launched in late 2022.
  • Over 30,000 UFO and USO reports have been recorded.
  • US Navy officials have raised national security concerns.
  • Many USO sightings involve objects entering or exiting water without splashes.

Timeline

Enigma began collecting data on UFO sightings in late 2022. Since then, it has amassed a substantial database, reflecting growing public interest in both aerial and underwater unidentified phenomena. The US Navy has responded by increasing scrutiny of these reports, recognizing the potential implications for national security.

“These USO sightings add a new dimension to the ongoing mystery of unidentified phenomena,” Enigma states.

Context

The surge in USO sightings coincides with a broader trend of increased public and government interest in UFOs. Recent investigations into aerial phenomena have led to calls for greater transparency and understanding of these occurrences, particularly regarding their potential implications for security and scientific inquiry.

What’s next

Researchers and officials are continuing to investigate the implications of USO sightings for national security and scientific discovery, although specific next steps have not been disclosed.

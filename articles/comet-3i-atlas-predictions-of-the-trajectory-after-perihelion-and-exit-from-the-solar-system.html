Comet 3I/ATLAS: predictions of the trajectory after perihelion and exit from the Solar System

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, detected in July 2025 by the ATLAS system in Chile, has completed its perihelion on October 30, 2025, at a distance of 1.4 astronomical units from the Sun, approximately 210 million kilometers. NASA has confirmed that the comet poses no risk of collision with Earth as it follows a hyperbolic route out of the Solar System.

This event is significant for astronomers as they monitor the comet to gather information about its composition and origin from another star system. The data collected could enhance understanding of cosmic phenomena and the evolution of comets.

Key facts

  • Detection date: July 2025
  • Perihelion date: October 30, 2025
  • Distance from Sun at perihelion: 1.4 AU (210 million kilometers)
  • Current speed: approximately 68 km/s
  • Minimum distance to Earth: 1.8 AU on December 19, 2025
  • Observation period: November 27, 2025 to January 27, 2026
  • Estimated mass: 33 billion tons
  • Legacy: refining orbital models for future interstellar visitors

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2025 by the ATLAS system in Chile. On October 30, 2025, it reached perihelion at a distance of 1.4 AU from the Sun. Following this, the comet has continued on a hyperbolic trajectory, moving away from the Solar System.

In December 2025, it will reach a minimum distance of 1.8 AU from Earth. Observations are set to take place from November 27, 2025, to January 27, 2026, to study its thermal and spectral emissions.

Official statements

“The comet 3I/ATLAS provides a unique opportunity to study interstellar material and its implications for star formation,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects, following the discoveries of 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. These objects have prompted advancements in detection networks and observational techniques, enhancing our understanding of the cosmos.

What’s next

A global network of telescopes, coordinated by NASA, will track 3I/ATLAS between November 2025 and January 2026. Observations will focus on its chemical composition and non-gravitational forces as it continues its journey through the Solar System.

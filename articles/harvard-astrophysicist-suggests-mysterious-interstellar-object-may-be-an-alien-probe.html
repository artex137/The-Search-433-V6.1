Harvard astrophysicist suggests mysterious interstellar object may be an alien probe

On July 1, astronomers detected a fast-moving object, named 3I/ATLAS, approaching the Sun. This object is notable for its orbital path, indicating it has originated from beyond our Solar System, marking only the third identified interstellar visitor. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This discovery has sparked significant interest within the scientific community, prompting researchers to explore the object's origins and characteristics. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb and his team have suggested that 3I/ATLAS may possess features indicative of artificial construction, leading to speculation about the possibility of it being an alien probe.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Detection date: July 1
  • Speed: 245,000 kilometers per hour
  • Estimated diameter: up to 20 kilometers
  • Orbital path: unusually close to Venus, Mars, and Jupiter
  • Research paper: "Is the Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Alien Technology?" published on arXiv

Timeline

On July 1, astronomers detected the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. Initial observations revealed its extraordinary speed and size, leading to comparisons with previous interstellar objects. Following the detection, scientists began analyzing its characteristics further, with some proposing that it could be an alien probe.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS exhibits such strange characteristics that some scientists have begun to wonder whether it might be something more unusual,” said Avi Loeb, emphasizing the object's intriguing orbital alignment.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, the first observed interstellar object, 1I/ʻOumuamua, was discovered in 2017, leading to similar discussions about its origins. The scientific community remains divided on the interpretation of such objects, with some researchers advocating for natural explanations.

What’s next

As research continues, scientists will further investigate 3I/ATLAS's properties. The findings may refine methods for identifying and understanding similar interstellar objects in the future.

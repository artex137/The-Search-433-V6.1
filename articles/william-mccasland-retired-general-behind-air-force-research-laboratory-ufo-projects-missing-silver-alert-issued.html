William McCasland Retired General Behind Air Force Research Laboratory UFO Projects Missing—Silver Alert Issued

A Silver Alert has been issued for retired Major General William McCasland, who was last seen on 27 February 2026 in Albuquerque, New Mexico. The 68-year-old former head of the Air Force Research Laboratory has been reported missing amid concerns for his safety due to undisclosed medical issues.

Authorities are actively searching for McCasland, emphasizing the urgency of the situation given his medical vulnerabilities. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office is leading the investigation and has urged anyone with information to come forward.

Timeline

McCasland was last seen on the morning of 27 February 2026. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office issued a Silver Alert, citing undisclosed medical concerns that have raised fears for his safety. The sheriff's office has stated that law enforcement is concerned due to these medical issues.

As of now, there are no details regarding what he was wearing or the direction he may have travelled. The lack of basic information has compounded the urgency of the search.

Official statements

“Due to his medical issues, law enforcement is concerned for his safety,” said a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office.
Col Justin Secrest, commander of the 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland, stated that the base is coordinating closely with local authorities in the search effort.

Context

McCasland has a storied career in aerospace research, having commanded the Air Force Research Laboratory, which oversees a science and technology portfolio reportedly valued at $2.2 billion. He has also been linked to discussions surrounding classified UFO-related initiatives, although there is no evidence connecting his disappearance to any such projects.

The public concern over his disappearance has intensified, particularly given his prominent role in advanced military operations and aerospace innovation.

What’s next

Authorities continue to seek public assistance in the search for McCasland. They have urged anyone with any information, no matter how minor, to contact the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office. The investigation remains focused on locating the retired general and ensuring his safety.

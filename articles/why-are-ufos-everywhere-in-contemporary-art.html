Why Are UFOs Everywhere in Contemporary Art?

Published 2025-12-11 18:36 UTC

UFOs Feature Prominently in Contemporary Art Exhibitions

Two art exhibitions in New York City this winter showcase the growing fascination with UFOs and unexplained phenomena. The first, titled “Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena,” is on view at the Drawing Center until February 1, 2026. The second exhibition, “Paintings Made for Aliens Above,” features new works by Romanian artist Hortensia Mi Kafchin and is open until December 20, 2025. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to these exhibitions.

The increasing interest in UFOs reflects a broader cultural trend, as modern science and technology have not diminished public curiosity about unidentified flying objects. According to the Pentagon, recent declassified files have revealed strange objects observed by Navy pilots. Art institutions are responding to this trend by highlighting how these phenomena intersect with contemporary belief systems, technology, and identity.

Key facts

  • Exhibition: “Voice of Space: UFOs and Paranormal Phenomena”
  • Location: Drawing Center, New York City
  • End date: February 1, 2026
  • Exhibition: “Paintings Made for Aliens Above”
  • Location: P.P.O.W Gallery, New York City
  • End date: December 20, 2025
  • Artists featured: René Magritte, Isa Genzken, Hortensia Mi Kafchin

Timeline

Throughout history, UFOs have captivated artists, with many past incidents fueling artistic expression. For example, on April 14, 1561, residents of Nuremberg reported an aerial clash of mysterious objects. More recently, the Pentagon has declassified files about UFO sightings, prompting renewed interest in the subject.

This winter, two exhibitions in New York have brought these themes to the forefront of contemporary art. The Drawing Center’s exhibition features works from various artists, including René Magritte’s _Voice of Space_, while P.P.O.W Gallery presents new paintings by Hortensia Mi Kafchin.

Official statements

“It’s also about transhistorical human experience, belief systems, cybernetics, and AI,” said Olivia Shao, curator of the Drawing Center exhibition.
“These collages resist the ‘wait your turn’ detritus that so often gets thrown at Black people, queer people, and people who have been marginalized,” said Char Jeré, an artist featured in the Drawing Center.

Context

The fascination with UFOs has persisted through centuries, with artists historically interpreting celestial phenomena as divine omens. While scientific explanations have emerged for some events, the intrigue surrounding UFOs continues to inspire contemporary art. The current exhibitions reflect a cultural moment where technology and belief intersect, allowing artists to explore themes of identity and futurism.

What’s next

As the exhibitions run through early 2026, they are expected to draw continued public interest. Art institutions may look to further explore themes of UFOs and unexplained phenomena in future programming.

3I/ATLAS Update: How To Watch Interstellar Comet On Its Escape From Our Solar System

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is currently speeding away from the Sun and will leave our solar system entirely. Skywatchers can follow its journey through a livestream hosted by the Virtual Telescope Project on 16 November.

This event is significant as it offers a rare opportunity to observe an interstellar object in real time. The livestream will feature imagery from robotic telescopes located in Manciano, Italy, allowing audiences worldwide to witness the comet's departure.

Key facts

  • Event Date: 16 November 2023
  • Livestream Start Time: 11:15 p.m. EST (04:15 GMT on 17 November)
  • Discovery Date: 1 July 2025
  • Perihelion Date: 30 October 2023
  • Current Magnitude: +10.9
  • Constellation: Virgo

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first identified on 1 July 2025 and has since been confirmed as the third interstellar object detected within our solar system. The comet reached perihelion, its closest approach to the Sun, on 30 October 2023.

On 11 November 2023, Gianluca Masi, founder of the Virtual Telescope Project, captured notable images of the comet. These images displayed its glowing central coma and a long ion tail shaped by the solar wind.

Currently, 3I/ATLAS can be observed rising over the eastern horizon in the early morning hours, although it remains too faint to be seen with the naked eye.

Official statements

“The livestream provides the best opportunity to witness this rare interstellar traveller,” said Gianluca Masi. “It allows audiences to follow the comet's progress as it speeds away from the Sun.”

Context

Interstellar objects are rare, with only two confirmed before 3I/ATLAS: 1I/‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Each of these encounters has yielded valuable scientific insights about material from outside our solar system. The departure of 3I/ATLAS highlights the fleeting nature of such events.

What’s next

The livestream on 16 November will allow viewers to observe 3I/ATLAS as it continues its journey into interstellar space. The event represents a significant moment for both scientists and astronomy enthusiasts.

Farewell, 3I/ATLAS: online observation – 16 Jan. 2026

On January 16, 2026, the Virtual Telescope Project will host its final public viewing of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, starting at 21:00 UTC. This event marks the last chance for the public to observe the comet in real-time.

The viewing is significant as 3I/ATLAS is recognized as one of the most important astronomical discoveries of the last decade. The Virtual Telescope Project aims to provide an accessible opportunity for enthusiasts and the general public to engage with this celestial event.

Key facts

  • Date: January 16, 2026
  • Time: 21:00 UTC
  • Event: Final public viewing of 3I/ATLAS
  • Host: Virtual Telescope Project

Timeline

The Virtual Telescope Project has scheduled the live feed for January 16, 2026. This event will allow viewers worldwide to witness the comet's passage.

3I/ATLAS has garnered attention since its discovery, and this event serves as a culmination of public interest and scientific observation since it first appeared.

Official statements

“This is a very precious opportunity to personally see, in real-time, the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS,” said Gianluca Masi, the founder of the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS has been pivotal for astronomers, enhancing understanding of interstellar objects. Previous observations have contributed to ongoing research in the field of astronomy.

What’s next

As the final viewing approaches, the Virtual Telescope Project continues to encourage public participation and support for future astronomical initiatives.

Scientists Searched 3I/ATLAS for Signs of Aliens. Did They Find Anything?

Published 2026-01-05 20:57 UTC

Scientists Search 3I/ATLAS for Signs of Aliens

Researchers have conducted a five-hour search for radio signals from the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS using the Green Bank Telescope in West Virginia on December 18, 2023. The search aimed to determine if the comet emitted any signs of alien technology, but the findings confirmed there were no signals detected.

This investigation is significant as it addresses ongoing speculation about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. The Breakthrough Listen Project, which led the search, emphasized the importance of verifying claims of extraterrestrial signals through thorough data collection.

Key facts

  • Search conducted on December 18, 2023.
  • Location: Green Bank Telescope, West Virginia.
  • Duration of search: five hours.
  • Closest approach of 3I/ATLAS to Earth: approximately 270 million kilometers.
  • Number of signals flagged: nine, all identified as human-made.

Timeline

On December 18, 2023, the Breakthrough Listen Project utilized the 100-meter Green Bank Telescope to scan a wide range of radio frequencies for potential signals from 3I/ATLAS. This search took place a day before the comet's perigee, its closest approach to Earth. The team alternated their observations between the comet and surrounding patches of sky to filter out background noise.

After analyzing the data, researchers flagged nine possible signals, which were later determined to originate from human technology, indicating no evidence of extraterrestrial communication.

Official statements

“The discovery of no radio signals emanating from 3I/ATLAS is one of the biggest discoveries so far, since it’s the final nail in the coffin of the whole alien thing,” said a representative from the Breakthrough Listen Project.

Context

3I/ATLAS has been the subject of speculation regarding its origins and potential for harboring alien technology. NASA officials have consistently stated that the comet displays characteristics typical of comets, despite some external theorizing about its nature.

The search conducted by the Breakthrough Listen Project reflects a broader commitment within the scientific community to rule out potential alien signals through rigorous data collection and analysis.

What’s next

Following these findings, researchers plan to focus on studying the comet's physical properties and behavior, redirecting efforts from the search for extraterrestrial signals to a more scientific examination of 3I/ATLAS.

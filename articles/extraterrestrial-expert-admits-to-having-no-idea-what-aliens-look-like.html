Extraterrestrial Expert Admits to Having 'No Idea' What Aliens Look Like

Extraterrestrial experts have stated they have "no idea" what aliens might look like, following remarks from former President Barack Obama about the existence of extraterrestrial life. Bill Diamond, president and chief executive of the SETI Institute, made the comments during an interview on NewsNation's Jesse Weber Live. The discussion has gained attention amid ongoing public interest in UFOs and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Experts emphasize the need for caution in assumptions about alien appearances, noting that humans share specific physical traits due to evolutionary factors. This conversation comes as former President Donald Trump has ordered federal agencies to review and release information related to UFOs, which could potentially shed light on the topic.

Key facts

  • Bill Diamond, president of SETI, stated we have "no idea" what aliens look like.
  • A 2025 poll found nearly half of Americans believe the government is hiding evidence related to UFOs.
  • Barack Obama confirmed he believes in aliens but has not seen them.
  • Donald Trump has ordered federal agencies to release UFO-related information.
  • Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth is overseeing the review of UFO files.

Timeline

In a recent interview, Bill Diamond expressed skepticism about popular images of aliens, stating, "We have no idea" what they might look like. This follows Barack Obama's revelation in a podcast that he believes aliens exist but has not encountered them.

On a separate occasion, former President Trump directed federal agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing information related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. His comments have reignited public interest in the topic, with many looking for transparency from the government.

Official statements

“It’s hard for us to assert... We have no idea,” said Bill Diamond, president of the SETI Institute.
“We're digging in. We're going to be in full compliance with that executive order,” stated Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth.
“There’s no underground facility unless there’s this enormous conspiracy and they hid it from the president of the United States,” remarked Barack Obama.

Context

The discussion around extraterrestrial life has gained momentum over the years, particularly with increased sightings of unidentified aerial phenomena. Government agencies have faced pressure to disclose information on UFOs, leading to public speculation and interest in what may be hidden.

Past presidents have also commented on the existence of aliens, contributing to a growing narrative surrounding UFOs and government secrecy.

What’s next

Federal agencies are currently reviewing and preparing to release UFO-related information as directed by President Trump. The outcome of this review may provide new insights into the ongoing conversation about extraterrestrial life and government transparency.

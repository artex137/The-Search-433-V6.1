True identity of interstellar visitor 3I/ATLAS revealed TOMORROW - as NASA finally shares images

NASA is set to reveal the true identity of the interstellar visitor 3I/ATLAS on October 5, 2023, through the release of new images. The announcement comes as scientists and astronomers have eagerly awaited more information about this celestial object.

This revelation is significant as it enhances our understanding of interstellar objects and their origins. NASA's release of images will provide critical data for researchers studying the characteristics and behavior of 3I/ATLAS, which has been the subject of extensive observation since its discovery.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 5, 2023
  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Agency involved: NASA
  • Location of observation: not disclosed
  • Nature of object: interstellar visitor

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first observed in 2020, and since then, astronomers have tracked its trajectory across the solar system. The upcoming release of images marks a pivotal moment in the ongoing study of this interstellar object.

Official statements

“We are excited to share the findings related to 3I/ATLAS with the public,” said a NASA spokesperson, emphasizing the importance of this data for scientific research.

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, the first known interstellar object observed was 'Oumuamua in 2017. This sparked interest in the study of similar celestial bodies and their implications for understanding the universe.

What’s next

Following the release of images, NASA plans to conduct further analysis and share additional findings regarding the composition and trajectory of 3I/ATLAS.

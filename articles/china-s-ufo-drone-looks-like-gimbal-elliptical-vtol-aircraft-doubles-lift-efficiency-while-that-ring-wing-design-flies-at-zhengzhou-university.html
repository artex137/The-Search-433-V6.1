"China's UFO Drone Looks Like Gimbal": Elliptical VTOL Aircraft Doubles Lift Efficiency While That Ring-Wing Design Flies at Zhengzhou University

Published 2025-09-03 08:56 UTC

China's VTOL Drone Resembles Gimbal UFO, Tested at Zhengzhou University

Researchers at Zhengzhou University of Aeronautics in China have successfully tested a vertical take-off and landing (VTOL) drone that resembles the “Gimbal” UFO captured on video by a U.S. Navy pilot in 2015. The drone's unique elliptical design has garnered attention for its potential applications in both civilian and military sectors.

The development of this drone highlights significant advancements in drone technology, particularly in addressing the challenges of combining vertical take-off capabilities with efficient forward flight. According to the South China Morning Post, the drone's design features a closed-loop wing structure that integrates vertical stabilizers. Project leader Professor Liu Zhanhe stated that the drone combines the advantages of multirotor drones and fixed-wing aircraft.

Key facts

  • Location: Zhengzhou University of Aeronautics, China
  • Drone design: Elliptical, closed-loop wing with vertical stabilizers
  • Lift efficiency: More than double that of conventional straight wings
  • Potential applications: Battlefield surveillance, maritime reconnaissance, environmental monitoring, emergency response
  • Challenges: Aerodynamic drag and control algorithm optimization

Timeline

In recent years, advancements in drone technology have led to new designs aimed at improving performance. The Zhengzhou University team has conducted simulations and test flights that demonstrate increased lift efficiency. The research team continues to refine the design to address challenges related to aerodynamic drag and control efficiency.

Initial tests have shown that the drone maintains steady flight, though the unique shape introduces additional aerodynamic challenges. The project team is working to optimize the drone's performance as they explore further applications for its innovative design.

Official statements

“The experimental aircraft departs from conventional forms, adopting an elliptical, closed-loop wing with vertical stabilizers,” said Professor Liu Zhanhe.
“Our design offers the best of both worlds from multi-rotor and fixed-wing aircraft,” Liu added in a peer-reviewed paper earlier this year.

Context

The drone's design has drawn comparisons to the Gimbal UFO, which has remained unexplained by the U.S. Navy since its sighting in 2015. This innovation in drone technology reflects a growing trend towards exploring unconventional aircraft designs, which may have significant implications for future aviation.

As technology continues to blur the lines between science fiction and reality, the potential for new applications in both military and civilian contexts is expanding.

What’s next

The team at Zhengzhou University of Aeronautics is focused on refining the drone's design to optimize performance. Future developments may lead to further exploration of unconventional aircraft designs, with implications for a range of applications in various sectors.

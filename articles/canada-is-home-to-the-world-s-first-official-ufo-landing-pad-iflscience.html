Canada Is Home To The World’s First Official UFO Landing Pad

The world’s first official UFO landing pad is located in St. Paul, Alberta, and was established in June 1967 as part of Canada’s centennial celebrations. The landing pad sits atop six concrete pylons and serves as a symbol of peace and unity, according to local authorities.

This unique attraction has drawn attention for its whimsical approach to extraterrestrial visitors. The town has embraced the concept, turning it into a community project that reflects a lighthearted perspective on potential contact with alien life. The landing pad is not only a point of interest for UFO enthusiasts but also serves as a reminder of the town's commitment to welcoming all beings, human or otherwise.

Key facts

  • Location: St. Paul, Alberta, Canada
  • Established: June 1967
  • Height of pylons: 75 centimeters (30 inches)
  • Type: Circular platform
  • Community involvement: Supported by local residents and businesses

Timeline

In 1967, as part of Canada’s centennial celebrations, the town of St. Paul proposed the idea of a UFO landing pad. The project received approval from the government, leading to the construction of the platform. Upon its opening in June of that year, local residents celebrated with various themed decorations and events.

Since its establishment, the landing pad has become a cultural landmark, reflecting the town’s playful spirit and commitment to peace. The area surrounding the landing pad has also been adorned with flying saucer décor and memorabilia.

Official statements

“The area under the world's first UFO landing pad was designated international by the town of St. Paul as a symbol of our faith that mankind will maintain the outer universe free from national wars and strife,”
“That future travel in space will be safe for all intergalactic beings. All visitors from Earth or otherwise are welcome to this territory and to the town of St. Paul.”

Context

The UFO landing pad in St. Paul is a unique example of how communities can engage with the concept of extraterrestrial life in a humorous and welcoming manner. It reflects a broader trend of incorporating playful themes into local culture, fostering a sense of community and shared identity.

While there have been numerous reports of UFO sightings across Canada, the landing pad stands out as a physical representation of the town's commitment to peace and unity, rather than just a site for speculation about alien encounters.

What’s next

The town of St. Paul continues to promote the UFO landing pad as a tourist attraction. Plans for future community events and celebrations are not disclosed, but the local government remains committed to maintaining the site as a symbol of hospitality and goodwill.

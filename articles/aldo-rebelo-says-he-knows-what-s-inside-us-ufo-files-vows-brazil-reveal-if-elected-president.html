Aldo Rebelo Says He Knows What's Inside US UFO Files, Vows Brazil Reveal if Elected President

Brazilian presidential candidate Aldo Rebelo has claimed he possesses insider knowledge of US government UFO files, promising to disclose this information to the Brazilian public if he is elected president. Rebelo made his comments during a press conference, stating, "As a former Minister of Defence, I am provoked to speak on the subject. I know what the Armed Forces have in their archives."

Rebelo's statements have attracted significant attention, both domestically and internationally. His comments raise questions about the extent of government secrecy regarding advanced technology and extraterrestrial phenomena, which he asserts have been known for decades. The growing public interest in UFOs has prompted calls for transparency from governments worldwide.

Key facts

  • Aldo Rebelo is a former Minister of Defence in Brazil.
  • Rebelo has promised full disclosure of UFO files if elected president.
  • He claims to have knowledge of what the Brazilian Armed Forces have in their archives.
  • Rebelo's comments have sparked discussions about government secrecy and UFO phenomena.
  • Former US President Donald Trump announced plans to release documents related to UFOs and UAPs.
  • Over 90% of UFO sightings have conventional explanations, according to analysts.

Timeline

In recent years, there has been an increasing focus on UFOs and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) globally. Former President Donald Trump announced a plan to identify and release UFO-related documents. This announcement came after years of congressional hearings on the subject.

During these hearings, numerous blurry videos and eyewitness accounts surfaced, but they provided limited concrete evidence. Analysts have emphasized that true disclosure requires scientifically verifiable evidence, rather than anecdotal reports.

In November 2024, residents in New Jersey and other East Coast states reported sightings of unidentified drones, further fuelling public speculation about alien activity.

Official statements

“Extraordinary claims demand extraordinary evidence,” said an expert on UFO phenomena.
“If the US government releases its documents, I will do the same if elected president of Brazil,” Aldo Rebelo stated.

Context

Rebelo's remarks come amid a broader trend of political campaigns in Brazil that emphasize transparency and bold promises. The current global discourse surrounding UFOs has been shaped by previous statements from public figures, including former President Barack Obama, who has joked about aliens but clarified there is no evidence of contact.

The public's fascination with UFOs continues to grow, particularly as governments grapple with the implications of releasing sensitive information about unidentified phenomena.

What’s next

Rebelo's campaign may influence how Brazil approaches the disclosure of sensitive information if he wins the presidency. Should he follow through on his promise, it could place Brazil at the forefront of global discussions about UFOs and government transparency. The political landscape surrounding this issue will likely evolve as the election approaches.

'Interstellar object' now turns towards NASA spacecraft in shock manoeuvre

An ancient interstellar object, known as 3I/ATLAS, is now on a trajectory that will bring it close to NASA's Europa Clipper and the European Space Agency's Hera spacecraft. The encounter is expected to occur between today and November 6, 2023. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This development is significant as it marks a rare opportunity for scientists to study an object from beyond our solar system. NASA and the European Space Agency are monitoring the situation closely, preparing for potential data collection and analysis during the close approach.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Close approach period: Today to November 6, 2023
  • Spacecraft involved: NASA's Europa Clipper and ESA's Hera
  • No injuries or fatalities reported

Timeline

3I/ATLAS, an interstellar comet, has been moving through space and is now on a path that will bring it near Earth’s spacecraft. The anticipated close approach will occur within the next few days, providing an opportunity for observation and research.

NASA and the European Space Agency have coordinated their efforts to capture and analyze data from this unique encounter, which may enhance our understanding of interstellar objects.

Official statements

“This is a rare chance to study an object from outside our solar system,” said a NASA spokesperson. “We are excited about the potential findings.”

Context

Interstellar objects are rare, with only a few recorded in recent years. Previous encounters, such as with 'Oumuamua in 2017 and comet 2I/Borisov in 2019, have provided valuable insights into the composition and behaviour of such celestial bodies, influencing future space exploration strategies.

What’s next

NASA and the European Space Agency will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches. Data collection efforts are expected to commence during the close approach, with findings to be published following analysis.

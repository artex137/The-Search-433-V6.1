Skeletons, UFOs and the Nephilim: LA Marzulli Reveals Hidden History

L.A. Marzulli has discussed ancient giants, biblical prophecy, and the cosmic battle between good and evil in a recent episode of Ninjas Are Butterflies. The episode, which aired on an undisclosed date, explores controversial topics such as the Paracas skulls and alleged archaeological hoaxes.

This episode matters as it challenges conventional views in archaeology and theology. Marzulli emphasizes the need for rigorous scientific testing to validate claims surrounding these discoveries, particularly as further DNA analysis is underway.

Key facts

  • Episode: Ninjas Are Butterflies

Timeline

In the episode, Marzulli outlines the significance of the Paracas skulls, noting that some modern DNA tests show anomalies but also face contamination issues. He mentions that the third round of DNA analysis is currently taking place to verify these findings.

Marzulli shares personal experiences with archaeological hoaxes, particularly involving the Nazca mummies. He describes how altered mummies can mislead the public and stresses the importance of scientific scrutiny in archaeology.

Official statements

“A Neanderthal is 99.5% identical to modern humans. The Paracas skull? 96.5%,” Marzulli said, highlighting the need for rigorous testing.
“You cut the thumb off, the pinky off, elongate the remaining three fingers, cover it in plaster—voilà, you have your hoax,” he explained regarding the Nazca mummies.

Context

This episode builds on ongoing discussions about the intersection of archaeology and biblical texts. Marzulli links the "seed war" from Genesis 3:15 to historical attempts to corrupt the human genome, referencing the days of Noah and the Nephilim.

He also connects ancient accounts with modern reports of UFO encounters, suggesting that these patterns of interference have persisted through time.

What’s next

Further investigations into the Paracas skulls and other archaeological claims are expected as Marzulli continues his research. The results of the ongoing DNA testing may provide more clarity on these controversial subjects.

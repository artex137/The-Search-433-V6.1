Researcher Claims Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Emitted Fibonacci Sequence Light Pulses on Fatima Anniversary

David Sereda, an independent researcher, has reported observing structured light pulses from the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS on October 13, 2025, coinciding with the 108th anniversary of the Miracle of the Sun at Fatima, Portugal. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these observations.

Sereda claims that the light pulses emitted by 3I/ATLAS followed a Fibonacci sequence pattern of 5, 8, and 13 flashes. This sequence is known for its occurrence in various natural systems and leads to the Golden Ratio. NASA’s Solar Dynamics Observatory went offline around the same time, preventing independent verification of Sereda’s claims.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: October 13, 2025
  • Location: Near the sun
  • Key numbers in light pulse pattern: 5, 8, 13
  • Notable anniversary: 108 years since the Miracle of the Sun at Fatima
  • NASA Solar Dynamics Observatory offline during observation
  • Expected perihelion of 3I/ATLAS: October 29–30, 2025
  • Maximum closest approach to Earth: December 25, 2025

Timeline

On October 13, 2025, David Sereda reported observing light pulses from 3I/ATLAS.

The reported pulse pattern coincided with the 108th anniversary of the Miracle of the Sun at Fatima, drawing connections between the two events.

NASA’s Solar Dynamics Observatory was offline during this timeframe, hindering the ability to verify the claims through standard monitoring equipment.

Official statements

“The observatory’s closure means no continuous animation of the object’s behavior during this critical observation window is available to the public,” Sereda stated.
“Professional astronomers typically require peer-reviewed analysis and multiple independent observations before confirming unusual phenomena from celestial objects,” noted sources within the scientific community.

Context

Interstellar objects are rare visitors to our solar system, with only three confirmed to date: 1I/’Oumuamua in 2017, 2I/Borisov in 2019, and 3I/ATLAS. These objects follow hyperbolic trajectories, differentiating them from traditional comets and asteroids.

The Fibonacci sequence and the Golden Ratio appear frequently in nature, but their manifestation in this context does not imply intentional design or communication.

What’s next

Investigations into Sereda’s claims are ongoing, but without independent verification from professional astronomers, the findings remain unconfirmed. The expected perihelion of 3I/ATLAS will occur on October 29–30, 2025, which may provide further data for analysis.

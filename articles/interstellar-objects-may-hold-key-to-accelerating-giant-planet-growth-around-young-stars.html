Interstellar objects may hold key to accelerating giant planet growth around young stars

Professor Susanne Pfalzner of Forschungszentrum Julich in Germany has presented new findings on interstellar objects at the EPSC-DPS2025 Joint Meeting in Helsinki. Her research highlights the potential role of these bodies, including the recently discovered comet 3I/ATLAS, in enhancing the growth of giant planets around young stars.

This research matters because it challenges conventional models of planet formation. Pfalzner's findings suggest that interstellar objects could bypass limitations in traditional accretion processes, potentially allowing for the rapid formation of gas giants before planet-forming discs dissipate.

Key facts

  • Event: EPSC-DPS2025 Joint Meeting
  • Location: Helsinki, Finland
  • Key object: Comet 3I/ATLAS, discovered in mid-2025
  • Researcher: Professor Susanne Pfalzner
  • Significance: Potential acceleration of giant planet formation

Timeline

Since 2017, three interstellar objects have been observed entering our Solar System: 1I/'Oumuamua, 2I/Borisov, and 3I/ATLAS. The discovery of 3I/ATLAS occurred in mid-2025. Pfalzner's models, presented at the recent meeting, illustrate how these objects could contribute to planet formation.

According to Pfalzner, traditional models of planet formation through dust and rock accretion face challenges, particularly in growing particles beyond metre-sized boulders. The introduction of interstellar objects could help overcome these obstacles.

"Interstellar objects may be able to jump start planet formation, in particular around higher-mass stars," Pfalzner stated.

Context

Conventional theories suggest that planets form slowly over time in circumstellar discs. However, these discs only last about two million years, which may not be sufficient for gas giants to form through traditional means. The introduction of interstellar objects could provide a faster pathway for the formation of these planets.

What’s next

Pfalzner plans to continue her research by modeling the likelihood of interstellar objects developing into planetary bodies and examining their distribution within planet-forming discs.

Mysterious Comet C/2025 V1 Borisov Approaches Earth This Week

A newly discovered comet, C/2025 V1 Borisov, is expected to make its closest approach to Earth this week, after being spotted earlier this month. Discovered by Crimean amateur astronomer Gennadiy Borisov, the comet is currently racing through the solar system and will pass at a distance of about 64 million miles (103 million kilometers), which is roughly 270 times farther than the Moon.

This discovery has garnered significant attention due to the comet's unusual path and origin. However, experts have confirmed that C/2025 V1 poses no threat to Earth. The Minor Planet Center and NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) have catalogued the object, confirming its trajectory as it moves through the inner solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet name: C/2025 V1 Borisov
  • Discovered by: Gennadiy Borisov
  • Distance from Earth at closest approach: 64 million miles (103 million kilometers)
  • Discovery date: Earlier this month
  • Catalogued by: Minor Planet Center and NASA's JPL
  • Expected closest approach date: This week
  • Perihelion (closest point to the Sun): November 16
  • Apparent magnitude: Approximately 13.8 (not visible to the naked eye)

Timeline

C/2025 V1 Borisov was first observed earlier this month by Gennadiy Borisov. Following its discovery, reports were submitted to the Minor Planet Center, and its trajectory was confirmed by NASA's JPL. As the comet approaches Earth, astronomers have noted its unique orbital characteristics, which have sparked discussions among scientists.

Despite online speculation connecting C/2025 V1 to the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, experts have clarified that there is no physical or orbital connection between the two comets. C/2025 V1 is expected to pass Earth safely before reaching perihelion on November 16.

Official statements

“While its motion and orbit are interesting, C/2025 V1 will pass by at a very safe distance,” said a researcher from NASA’s JPL.
“The trajectory is interestingly similar to interstellar objects, but the data so far suggest it likely formed within our own solar system,” stated Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb.

Context

The discovery of C/2025 V1 Borisov comes amidst heightened interest in interstellar objects. The comet's highly inclined orbit, at nearly 113 degrees, has drawn comparisons to other comets, but experts have emphasized its unique characteristics. Previous interstellar comets, such as 2I/Borisov, have also sparked enthusiasm among astronomers and the public alike.

What’s next

C/2025 V1 is on track for a safe flyby this week, with astronomers continuing to monitor its trajectory. Observers using high-powered telescopes or binoculars may catch a glimpse of the comet in the constellation Virgo.

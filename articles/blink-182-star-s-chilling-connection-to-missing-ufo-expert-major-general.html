Blink-182 star's chilling connection to missing UFO expert major general

Tom DeLonge, frontman of Blink-182, has a connection to William Neil McCasland, a retired U.S. Air Force Major General who was reported missing on February 27 after leaving his Albuquerque home on foot around 11 a.m. McCasland left behind his phone and glasses, with a gun and wallet still unaccounted for, according to the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office. His family has not heard from him since he disappeared.

The case has drawn attention due to McCasland's brief association with the UFO community through DeLonge, who founded the organization To The Stars Inc. in 2017 to study and disclose information on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP). Authorities are actively searching for McCasland and have requested residents in the area to provide home surveillance footage from the day he went missing.

Key facts

  • Missing Date: February 27, 2023
  • Last Seen: Between 11 a.m. and noon
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Described as: 5 feet, 11 inches tall, white hair, blue eyes
  • Clothing: Light green, button-up outdoor shirt with two chest pockets
  • Agency Involved: Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office

Timeline

On February 27, 2023, William Neil McCasland left his home around 11 a.m. He was last seen interacting with a home repairman about an hour before he went missing. McCasland's wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, was out of the house for an appointment during this time. The sheriff's office has stated he vanished between 11 a.m. and noon.

McCasland is known for his previous work with Tom DeLonge, which Wilkerson described as an unpaid consultancy on military and technical matters related to DeLonge's fiction work. Since McCasland's disappearance, the sheriff's office has requested local residents to check their surveillance footage for any signs of him.

Official statements

“It is true that Neil had a brief association with the UFO community through Tom DeLonge, former frontman for Blink-182 and founder of the organization To The Stars,” said Susan McCasland Wilkerson.

Context

Tom DeLonge has been a prominent figure in the UFO community, advocating for transparency in government disclosures regarding unidentified aerial phenomena. His organization, To The Stars, collaborates with high-level officials to explore UAPs. McCasland's involvement with DeLonge highlights the intersection of military expertise and the burgeoning interest in UFOs.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office continues to investigate McCasland's disappearance, focusing on gathering more information from local residents and reviewing surveillance footage from the day he went missing. Further updates will be provided as the investigation progresses.

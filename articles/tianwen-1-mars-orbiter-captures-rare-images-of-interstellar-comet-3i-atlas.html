Tianwen 1 Mars orbiter captures rare images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

A Chinese Martian orbiter has photographed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it passed near Mars on October 3, according to the China National Space Administration. The comet is the third confirmed interstellar object, following 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.

This discovery is significant for scientific research and enhances understanding of interstellar objects. The Tianwen 1 mission aims to explore Mars and gather data that could inform future space exploration initiatives.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 3, 2023
  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Mission: Tianwen 1 Martian exploration
  • Agency: China National Space Administration
  • Previous interstellar objects: 1I/'Oumuamua, 2I/Borisov

Timeline

On October 3, 2023, the Tianwen 1 orbiter successfully captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS as it flew by Mars. This observation marks an important milestone in the ongoing Martian exploration mission.

Official statements

“The orbiter has observed and photographed a rare interstellar comet speeding through the solar system,” a representative from the China National Space Administration stated.

Context

The observation of comet 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing list of known interstellar objects. Previous discoveries, including 1I/'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019, have provided valuable insights into the nature of celestial bodies originating from outside the solar system.

What’s next

Further analysis of the images captured by the Tianwen 1 orbiter is expected to yield new information about the composition and behavior of interstellar comets.

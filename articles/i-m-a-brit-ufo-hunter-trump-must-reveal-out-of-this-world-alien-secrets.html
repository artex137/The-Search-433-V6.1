I'm a Brit UFO hunter - Trump must reveal 'out of this world' alien secrets

Published 2026-03-08 11:56 UTC

Former UK Cop Urges Trump to Release Alien Files

Gary Heseltine, a former police officer from Featherstone, West Yorkshire, has called on U.S. President Donald Trump to unveil classified information regarding extraterrestrial life. Heseltine, who has dedicated his career to investigating UFO cases since retiring in 2013, expressed hope that Trump's recent announcement could lead to significant revelations about unidentified aerial phenomena.

This request follows Trump's statement that he will direct U.S. agencies, including the Department of Defense, to begin identifying and releasing files related to aliens and UFOs. This announcement comes after former President Barack Obama made headlines by claiming "aliens are real," although he later clarified that he had not witnessed evidence of their existence.

Key facts

  • Gary Heseltine, 65, is a former detective constable with 24 years in the British Transport Police.
  • Trump announced the intention to release files on February 20, 2026.
  • Heseltine is known for investigating the Rendlesham Forest incident of December 1980.
  • Congress held its first hearings on UFOs in 50 years in 2022.
  • The Pentagon reported in 2022 that there is "no evidence" of alien encounters.

Timeline

In December 1980, U.S. Air Force personnel reported sightings of UFOs near RAF Bentwaters and RAF Woodbridge in Suffolk, a case that Heseltine has extensively investigated. In 2022, Congress convened its first hearings on UFOs in five decades, prompting the Pentagon to promise increased transparency regarding military UFO encounters. On February 20, 2026, Trump announced plans to release government files related to extraterrestrial life and UFOs.

“The statement he made is significant in the sense that it came from a sitting President of the United States who was authorising the release of government UFO/UAP files,” said Heseltine.

Context

Trump's announcement follows a series of public discussions about UFOs, particularly after Obama’s comments during a podcast where he acknowledged the possibility of alien life. The Pentagon's 2022 report stated that most UFO sightings can be attributed to ordinary objects, yet public interest in the subject remains high. Heseltine believes Trump's intervention could pressure agencies to disclose what they know.

What’s next

Heseltine anticipates that Trump's actions may lead to a push for the release of classified files, similar to the Epstein files. Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna has indicated plans to meet with the FBI to advocate for the release of UFO-related videos. The situation remains fluid as the public awaits further developments on the matter.

Age of Disclosure and truth about UFOs? Watch trailer here

Published 2025-11-23 12:47 UTC

New UFO Documentary Released in Major U.S. Cities

The documentary film Age of Disclosure, directed and produced by Dan Farah, has been released in New York, Washington, and Los Angeles on November 21, 2025, and is now available worldwide on Amazon’s Prime Video. The film explores whether the U.S. government is aware of and concealing evidence of extraterrestrial visits to Earth.

This film is significant as it features insights from U.S. officials, including defense department experts and members of Congress, who discuss unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), commonly referred to as UFOs. The documentary raises questions about government transparency regarding these phenomena, with Farah emphasizing the public's right to know the truth about potential extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Film title: Age of Disclosure
  • Director and producer: Dan Farah
  • Release date in the U.S.: November 21, 2025
  • Available on: Amazon’s Prime Video
  • Locations of release: New York, Washington, Los Angeles
  • Number of government insiders featured: 34

Timeline

On November 21, 2025, Age of Disclosure premiered in three major U.S. cities. The documentary presents testimonies from various U.S. officials regarding the government's handling of UAP information. The film's premise suggests a long-standing cover-up regarding extraterrestrial intelligence.

Dan Farah, the director, has stated that the documentary includes interviews with individuals who possess direct knowledge of U.S. government programs related to UAP. It aims to shed light on the claims surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Official statements

“At a base level, the public deserves to know the truth about fundamental facts, like we’re not alone in the universe.” – Dan Farah
“This [UAP] technology does stuff that we can’t do.” – Former director of aviation security at the National Security Council
“I have seen no evidence that the government has been hiding anything … testimonies are simply not enough. They need to be backed up with evidence.” – Joshua Semeter, professor of electrical engineering at Boston University

Context

The Age of Disclosure asserts the existence of an 80-year global cover-up regarding non-human intelligent life and advanced technology. However, the film has received mixed reviews, with many scientists rejecting its premise. Critics argue that the claims lack substantial evidence and rely heavily on testimonies from non-scientific sources.

Avi Loeb of Harvard acknowledges the intriguing nature of the claims presented by military and intelligence personnel but emphasizes the need for scientific validation and transparency from the government concerning any related information.

What’s next

As the film gains attention, it may prompt further discussions and investigations regarding UAP and government transparency. The ongoing debate about the existence of extraterrestrial life and the handling of related evidence continues to be a topic of interest among both the public and scientific communities.

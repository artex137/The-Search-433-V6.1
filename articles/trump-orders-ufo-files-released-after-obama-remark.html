Trump orders UFO files released after Obama remark

Former U.S. President Donald Trump has ordered the release of UFO files following comments made by former President Barack Obama, which have reignited public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. The directive came on October 25, 2023, amid ongoing discussions about government transparency regarding UFOs.

This development is significant as it reflects a growing demand for accountability and transparency from government agencies concerning unidentified flying objects. The Pentagon and other relevant authorities are now expected to review and release previously classified documents related to UFO sightings and encounters.

Key facts

  • Order issued on October 25, 2023.
  • Comments from Barack Obama prompted the release.
  • Government agencies involved include the Pentagon.
  • Details about the specific UFO files to be released are not disclosed.

Timeline

On October 25, 2023, Donald Trump instructed relevant agencies to release UFO files. This order followed remarks made by Barack Obama, who discussed the need for transparency regarding UFO sightings. The Pentagon has been tasked with reviewing and making the necessary documents public.

Official statements

“It is time for the public to see what we have on UFOs,” Trump stated in a recent interview.

Context

UFO sightings have been a topic of interest for decades, with various government reports released in recent years. The push for transparency has intensified, especially after the Pentagon confirmed the existence of certain unexplained aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Agencies are expected to begin the review process immediately and provide updates on the release timeline for the UFO files. Further public interest and scrutiny are anticipated as the release date approaches.

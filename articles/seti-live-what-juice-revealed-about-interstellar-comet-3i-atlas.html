SETI Live: What Juice Revealed About Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

Scientists have gathered to discuss the findings from ESA’s Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) regarding interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during a live event hosted by Dr. Franck Marchis and Dr. Cecilia Tubiana from INAF on June 11, 2026, at 9:00 AM PDT. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the event.

The findings from Juice's encounter with 3I/ATLAS are significant for understanding the composition and behavior of interstellar objects in our solar system. The data collected from the comet, including observations of water vapor outgassing and extensive tails, aims to enhance our knowledge of planetary systems beyond our own.

Key facts

  • Event Date: June 11, 2026
  • Event Time: 9:00 AM PDT
  • Location: Online live event
  • Instruments Used: Five instruments from ESA’s Juice
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Key Observations: Water vapor outgassing and tails stretching millions of kilometers

Timeline

In November 2025, ESA’s Juice performed a close encounter with interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. During this encounter, Juice utilized five of its instruments to gather data on the comet as it passed through the solar system.

The findings from this encounter include significant observations of the comet's structure and behavior, which scientists presented during the live event on June 11, 2026.

Official statements

“What can a visitor from another star system teach us about our own cosmic neighborhood?” - Dr. Franck Marchis
“The observations are helping scientists better understand what this object is made of, how it behaves near the Sun, and what it can tell us about planetary systems beyond our own.” - Dr. Cecilia Tubiana

Context

This event marks a rare opportunity for scientists to study an interstellar object up close. The findings contribute to ongoing research into the origins and characteristics of celestial bodies beyond our solar system.

Limited federal funding for the search for extraterrestrial intelligence highlights the importance of support from organizations like the SETI Institute in facilitating such research.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data collected from Juice's observations of 3I/ATLAS to deepen their understanding of interstellar comets and their implications for planetary science.

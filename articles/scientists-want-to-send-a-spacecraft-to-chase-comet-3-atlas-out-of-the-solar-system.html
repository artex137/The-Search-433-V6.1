Scientists want to send a spacecraft to chase comet 3I/ATLAS out of the Solar System

Scientists have proposed a mission to send a spacecraft to chase comet 3I/ATLAS, which was discovered on 1 July 2025 by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) survey telescope in Chile. The comet is currently on a hyperbolic trajectory that will take it out of the Solar System, making it a rare interstellar visitor.

This mission is significant as 3I/ATLAS could be the oldest comet ever observed. Authorities are considering advanced techniques to maximize the chances of successfully capturing images and data from the comet before it departs. The proposed method involves a complex trajectory that utilizes the Sun's gravity.

Key facts

  • Discovered: 1 July 2025
  • Distance from the Sun at discovery: 4.5 AU
  • Speed: over 60 km/s (216,000 km/h or 134,000 mph)
  • Proposed launch window: 2035
  • Closest approach to the Sun: 15 million kilometres (93 million miles)
  • Estimated time to reach 3I/ATLAS: at least 35 years

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025, revealing its interstellar nature as it approached the Sun. By October 2025, the comet had warmed and developed a coma and tail, making it an intriguing target for scientific study.

In response to the comet's unique trajectory, Adam Hibberd from the Initiative for Interstellar Studies and T Marshall Eubanks from Space Initiatives Inc have proposed a mission plan that employs a solar Oberth manoeuvre to increase the spacecraft's speed and efficiency.

Official statements

“This almost ludicrously ambitious proposal is the epitome of Theodore Roosevelt’s quote: ‘to dare mighty things, to win glorious triumphs,’” said Hibberd regarding the mission's challenges and potential.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third known interstellar comet, making its study crucial for understanding the composition of objects from other star systems. Previous missions, such as the Rosetta mission to comet 67P, have provided valuable data on comets within the Solar System.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to refine their mission proposal, focusing on the challenges of launching a spacecraft in 2035 and the necessary technology for an effective close pass of the Sun.

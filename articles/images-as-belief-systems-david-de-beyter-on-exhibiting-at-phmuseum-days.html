Images As Belief Systems: David De Beyter On Exhibiting At PhMuseum Days

David De Beyter has presented his photography project, The Skeptics, at PhMuseum Days in Bologna, Italy, on April 7, 2026. The exhibition explores the culture surrounding UFOs and the role of photographs in shaping belief systems.

The Skeptics aims to bridge the divide between believers and skeptics of UFO phenomena. Authorities and researchers have increasingly examined how images influence perceptions of truth, as the project reveals the complexities behind the construction of narratives related to UFO sightings.

Key facts

  • Event: The Skeptics exhibition
  • Date: April 7, 2026
  • Location: PhMuseum Days, Bologna, Italy
  • Focus: Culture of UFOs and the persuasive nature of images
  • Notable past event: UFO gathering in Las Cañadas, Tenerife, in 1989
  • Methodology: Combines photography, video, and sound to explore belief systems

Timeline

In 1974, an American missile's implosion created a halo of light that sparked a series of UFO sightings between the Canary Islands and the southern Sahara. This culminated in a significant gathering in Las Cañadas, Tenerife, where over 10,000 people convened in 1989.

In 2018, De Beyter began his research at Casa Velázquez in Madrid, engaging with Vicente Juan Ballester Olmos, a key figure in scientific ufology. His work has since evolved into The Skeptics, which showcases how photographic artifacts can shape interpretations of UFO sightings.

Official statements

“The question is no longer simply whether an image is ‘true’ or ‘false,’ but rather understanding how it works and what effect it has in terms of persuasion,” said David De Beyter regarding the impact of images in his work.

Context

The Skeptics draws on a broader discourse within ufology, which includes both believers and skeptics. The project highlights the ongoing debates about the validity of UFO sightings and the role of photographic evidence in shaping public perception. This reflects a trend in contemporary discourse where images are increasingly recognized as constructs rather than straightforward evidence.

What’s next

The PhMuseum Days 2026 Photography Festival Open Call is now open for submissions, offering four more artists the chance to exhibit their work in October. De Beyter continues to explore the intersections of belief and evidence in his ongoing research.

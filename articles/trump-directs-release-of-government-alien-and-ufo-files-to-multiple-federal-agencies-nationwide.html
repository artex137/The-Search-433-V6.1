Trump directs release of government alien and UFO files to multiple federal agencies nationwide

President Donald Trump has announced he will direct the release of government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life and unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) in response to recent comments by former President Barack Obama. This announcement was made aboard Air Force One and is part of a broader effort to address public interest in these matters.

Trump stated he will instruct War Secretary Pete Hegseth and other relevant departments to identify and declassify records tied to UFOs and potential extraterrestrial life. He emphasized that the move is necessary due to the "tremendous interest" shown by the public. Trump criticized Obama's remarks as disclosing classified information and described them as a "big mistake."

Key facts

  • Announcement made by President Trump aboard Air Force One.
  • Trump will direct War Secretary Pete Hegseth to lead the declassification process.
  • Public interest has surged following Obama’s comments about aliens.
  • Obama claimed on a podcast that aliens are real but denied having seen them.
  • Trump accused Obama of revealing classified information regarding extraterrestrial life.
  • Interest in UAPs has increased, prompting legislative action in 2023.

Timeline

On Saturday, former President Barack Obama appeared on Brian Tyler Cohen’s podcast and stated, "They’re real, but I haven’t seen them," when asked about aliens. His comments sparked significant online discussion.

Following Obama's remarks, Trump addressed the issue during a media gaggle aboard Air Force One, asserting that Obama had disclosed classified information and calling it a "big mistake." He then announced plans to release government files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs).” - President Donald Trump
“He gave classified information. He’s not supposed to be doing that. I don’t know if they’re real or not.” - President Donald Trump

Context

The discussion surrounding UAPs has intensified in recent years, with increased attention from lawmakers and defense officials. In 2023, Congress passed the Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena Disclosure Act, and the Department of Defense established the All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office to investigate such incidents further.

Prior to Trump's announcement, Obama had discussed UFOs in various interviews, including a 2021 appearance on "The Late Late Show with James Corden," where he stated that he looked into the existence of aliens in secret labs but found no evidence of their study.

What’s next

Federal agencies will begin the process of identifying and declassifying records related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Further updates on the timeline and specific files to be released have not been disclosed.

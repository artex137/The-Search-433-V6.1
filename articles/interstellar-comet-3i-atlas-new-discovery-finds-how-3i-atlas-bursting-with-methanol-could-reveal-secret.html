Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Bursting with Methanol Could Reveal Secrets

3I/ATLAS, an interstellar comet discovered in July 2025, has shown an unusually high concentration of methanol, according to astronomers using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile. This discovery provides insights into how planets and icy bodies may form around other stars.

The findings suggest that 3I/ATLAS formed under conditions different from those in our solar system, potentially due to colder temperatures or a unique chemical environment. Researchers are now examining the implications of this chemical composition for understanding planetary formation beyond our solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: July 2025
  • Observatory: Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA), Chile
  • Key Chemical Found: Methanol
  • Comparison with Other Comets: Higher methanol to hydrogen cyanide ratio
  • Previous Interstellar Comets: ‘Oumuamua (2017) and 2I/Borisov (2019)

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025 by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS). As the comet approached the sun, its icy surface warmed, releasing gas and dust. ALMA observed the comet during this period, capturing detailed measurements of its chemical makeup. The findings revealed that methanol is more abundant in 3I/ATLAS compared to comets from our own solar system.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” said Nathan Roth, lead author of the study and a professor at American University. “The details reveal what it's made of, and it's bursting with methanol in a way we just don't usually see in comets in our own solar system.”

Context

Methanol is commonly found in space, forming on icy dust grains in interstellar clouds. It is often incorporated into comets during the planet formation process. The unique chemical composition of 3I/ATLAS offers a valuable perspective on the conditions present in its original planetary system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the chemical composition of 3I/ATLAS and its unique outgassing behavior. This research may provide further insights into the formation of planetary systems beyond our own.

Mysterious 3I/ATLAS Interstellar Object Continues To Do Weird Stuff And Encourage Wild Speculation

The interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS is currently traveling through the Solar System after passing by Mars and zooming by the Sun. Many Earth- and space-based telescopes are set to observe it between November and January.

This object has sparked significant interest due to its unusual behavior. Notably, Harvard scientist Avi Loeb has suggested that 3I/ATLAS could potentially be an alien probe, a claim that has generated widespread speculation and debate. Various public figures, including Rep. Anna Paulina Luna and acting NASA head Sean Duffy, have also commented on its peculiarities.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Current location: Traveling through the Solar System
  • Recent events: Passed by Mars and the Sun
  • Observation period: November to January
  • Notable figure: Avi Loeb, Harvard scientist
  • Public figures involved: Rep. Anna Paulina Luna, Sean Duffy, Joe Rogan
  • Previous interstellar objects: Oumuamua (2017), 2I/Borisov (2019)

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has made notable passes in the Solar System, first by Mars and then by the Sun. Observations by Earth- and space-based telescopes are anticipated to occur between November and January. Since its discovery, the object has drawn attention for its unusual characteristics.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, "The non-gravitational acceleration might be the technological signature of an internal engine," in reference to 3I/ATLAS.
Loeb also mentioned that the object could be "bluer than Sun," which may indicate a hot engine or source of artificial light.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects, following the discoveries of Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. These earlier objects have been categorized as comets, and scientists are exploring the possibility that 3I/ATLAS shares similar characteristics, albeit with some unusual features attributed to its age and trajectory.

What’s next

As observations begin, scientists will continue to study 3I/ATLAS to better understand its composition and behavior, which may provide insights into both its origins and the nature of interstellar objects.

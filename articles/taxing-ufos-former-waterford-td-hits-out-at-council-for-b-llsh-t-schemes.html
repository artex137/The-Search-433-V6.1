‘Taxing UFOs’ - Former Waterford TD hits out at council for ‘b*llsh*t’ schemes

Published 2025-11-23 09:25 UTC

Former TD criticizes Waterford council over city schemes

Former Waterford TD Matt Shanahan has criticized Waterford City and County Council for introducing what he calls “b*llsh*t” schemes that he claims are damaging the city centre. Shanahan made his remarks on Facebook following an article that discussed calls to remove the toll from the Thomas Francis Meagher Bridge.

Shanahan’s comments reflect growing concerns among local residents and business owners about the impact of council initiatives on the city’s economy. He has suggested that the council should focus on more practical solutions rather than pursuing costly projects that may not benefit the community.

Key facts

  • Former TD Matt Shanahan criticized local council schemes on Facebook.
  • He described the schemes as “killing” the city centre.
  • Comments were made in response to calls for toll removal from the Thomas Francis Meagher Bridge.
  • Shanahan mentioned that public-private partnerships (PPP) could cost “hundreds of millions” to buy out of contract.
  • He encouraged councillors to focus on issues affecting shopper and business visits.
  • Shanahan has a history of public service, having campaigned for 24/7 cardiac care at University Hospital Waterford in 2016.

Timeline

In 2016, Matt Shanahan campaigned for enhanced medical services at University Hospital Waterford.

He was co-opted onto Waterford City and County Council in 2019 after the resignation of Cllr Mary Roche.

Shanahan was elected to the Dáil in 2020 but narrowly lost his re-election bid in 2021 to Sinn Féin’s Conor D McGuinness. He also ran unsuccessfully for the Seanad in 2023.

Official statements

“Councillors are better employed to overturn Active Travel BS (b*llsh*t) around the city which is killing shopper and business visits,” Shanahan said.
“We’ll have a car-free city soon and zero rates and taxes. What budget will pay the corporation engineers and executive then?” he added.

Context

Shanahan's comments come amid ongoing debates about transportation and urban planning in Waterford. There have been previous concerns regarding the impact of tolls and Active Travel initiatives on local businesses and traffic flow.

Public sentiment appears divided on the effectiveness of recent council projects, with some residents advocating for more traditional approaches to urban development.

What’s next

Local councillors are expected to continue discussions on transportation and city planning initiatives. The council has not disclosed any specific plans or timelines for addressing the concerns raised by Shanahan and other community members.

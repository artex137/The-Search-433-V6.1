Updates on Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS: New Images, Many Tails, and Non-gravitational Forces

NASA has released new images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on November 20, 2025, showcasing its multiple tails and unusual non-gravitational forces affecting its trajectory. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This release is significant as it enhances our understanding of interstellar objects and their behavior. Scientists are particularly interested in the comet's multiple tails, which suggest complex interactions with solar radiation and other forces. NASA continues to monitor the comet's path and behavior using its various telescopes and instruments.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered in 2019.
  • New images taken by NASA on November 20, 2025.
  • The comet exhibits multiple tails, indicating complex physical processes.
  • Non-gravitational forces are influencing the comet's trajectory.
  • NASA is using various telescopes to study the comet's behavior.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first identified in 2019. Since its discovery, astronomers have closely observed its trajectory and physical characteristics. On November 20, 2025, NASA released new images that highlight the comet's complex tail structure and the non-gravitational forces acting upon it.

Official statements

“The multiple tails of 3I/ATLAS provide insights into the physical processes at play in its journey through the solar system,” said a NASA spokesperson. “Understanding these dynamics is crucial for future observations of interstellar objects.”

Context

Interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS are rare and provide valuable data about the formation of solar systems. Prior to 3I/ATLAS, only a few interstellar objects have been observed, making each discovery significant for astronomers. The study of these comets helps scientists understand the materials and processes that exist beyond our solar system.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue monitoring comet 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the solar system. Further observations will help clarify the effects of non-gravitational forces on its trajectory.

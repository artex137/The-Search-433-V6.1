Interstellar invader Comet 3I/ATLAS is packed with water ice that could be older than Earth

Scientists have discovered that Comet 3I/ATLAS, an interstellar object, contains water ice that could predate Earth. This finding was reported on October 13, 2023, and is significant for understanding the origins of water in our solar system.

The presence of ancient water ice in Comet 3I/ATLAS may provide insights into the conditions present in the early solar system. Researchers and astronomers are currently studying the comet to learn more about its composition and the implications for planetary formation.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object.
  • Scientists have identified water ice within the comet.
  • The water ice may be older than Earth.
  • The discovery was announced on October 13, 2023.

Timeline

On October 13, 2023, researchers announced the discovery of water ice within Comet 3I/ATLAS. This finding has prompted further investigation into the comet's composition and its significance in understanding the early solar system.

Official statements

“The presence of water ice that could be older than Earth changes our perspective on the origins of water in our solar system,” a lead researcher stated.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects. This discovery aligns with ongoing studies into the composition of comets and their role in delivering water to planets.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from Comet 3I/ATLAS to better understand its water ice and other materials, which may lead to further insights about the history of our solar system.

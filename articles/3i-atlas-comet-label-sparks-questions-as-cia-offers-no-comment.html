3I/ATLAS Comet Label Sparks Questions as CIA Offers No Comment

The interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS has been officially classified as a natural comet by scientific agencies, yet the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) has declined to comment on the matter, raising questions among scientists and the public. The CIA issued a rare Glomar response on 31 December 2025, indicating it may have records related to 3I/ATLAS but is withholding information on national security grounds.

This situation highlights the tension between scientific transparency and intelligence agency secrecy. The unusual response from the CIA contrasts with the open scientific explanations provided by agencies like NASA, prompting further inquiry into the nature of 3I/ATLAS and what information may be undisclosed.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object observed in the Solar System.
  • The CIA issued a Glomar response to a FOIA request on 31 December 2025.
  • NASA confirmed 3I/ATLAS is a comet of natural origin on 19 November 2025.
  • 3I/ATLAS has a hyperbolic trajectory, meaning it will not return after its passage.
  • The object has displayed unconventional features, including a notable anti-tail jet.

Timeline

On 19 November 2025, NASA and other scientific authorities confirmed that 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet, consistent with known cometary activity.

On 31 December 2025, the CIA issued its Glomar response to a FOIA request concerning 3I/ATLAS, citing national security exemptions.

Official statements

The CIA's Glomar response indicates an unusual level of secrecy regarding a naturally classified celestial object.
Nasa has confirmed the object’s activity aligns with typical outgassing of volatile ices.

Context

The CIA's refusal to disclose information about 3I/ATLAS is notable given the public nature of the comet's observation and classification. This response contrasts with the scientific community's commitment to transparency in space science.

Previous interstellar objects, such as 'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have not faced similar levels of secrecy from intelligence agencies, raising concerns about the thresholds for public disclosure regarding celestial phenomena.

What’s next

As imaging and data from telescopes like the Hubble Space Telescope and James Webb Space Telescope are analyzed, the discussion around 3I/ATLAS is expected to continue, with scientists seeking to understand its unique features and the implications of the CIA's silence. Further investigations may occur if new evidence emerges regarding the object's nature.

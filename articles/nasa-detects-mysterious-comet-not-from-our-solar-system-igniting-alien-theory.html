NASA detects mysterious comet 'not from our solar system'

NASA has detected a comet named 3I/ATLAS, which experts believe originates from outside our solar system. The comet's discovery has sparked interest and speculation about its origins and implications. There have been no reports of injuries or fatalities related to this discovery.

The detection of 3I/ATLAS is significant as it marks one of only three confirmed interstellar objects. Experts are examining the comet to understand its composition and potential origins, which could be from a planetary system billions of years old. Qicheng Zhang, a postdoctoral fellow at Lowell Observatory, has highlighted the comet's unique characteristics, including its outgassing and dust production.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Type: Interstellar comet
  • Confirmed interstellar objects: Three
  • Expert: Qicheng Zhang, Lowell Observatory
  • Recent UK space launch: Skylark rockets, first in 50 years
  • Launch altitude: 62 miles

Timeline

Official statements

“This comet in particular is interesting since it's actually from outside of our solar system,” said Qicheng Zhang. “In this case, it's an interstellar comet because we can actually see it outgassing and producing dust.”

Donald Trump ‘authorises access to UFO bases and non-human bodies’, US congressman claims

The Trump administration has allegedly authorised access to classified UFO facilities, according to Missouri congressman Eric Burlison, who revealed the development during a recent interview. Burlison, a member of the Congressional Oversight Committee investigating Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs), made the claims about non-human bodies and UFO bases at an unspecified location and time.

This disclosure, if confirmed, could have significant implications for ongoing investigations into UAPs and the government's transparency regarding extraterrestrial phenomena. Authorities are currently reviewing the extent of access granted and the potential impact on future UAP investigations.

Key facts

  • Congressman Eric Burlison is a member of the Congressional Oversight Committee.
  • The claims involve access to classified UFO facilities and non-human bodies.
  • The details about the specific locations and times of the alleged access have not been disclosed.

Timeline

Details about the timeline of events related to this development are not disclosed. However, the announcement comes amid increasing public interest and government scrutiny of UAPs.

“The access has been granted as part of a larger effort to investigate UAPs,” Burlison stated.

Context

In recent years, there has been a growing movement within Congress to increase transparency regarding UAPs. Several hearings have been held to discuss the implications of these phenomena and the government's role in investigating them.

What’s next

Investigations into UAPs are expected to continue, with authorities likely to provide further updates on the findings and implications of the access granted to classified facilities.

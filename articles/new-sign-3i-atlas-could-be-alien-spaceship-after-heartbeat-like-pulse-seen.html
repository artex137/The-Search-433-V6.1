New sign 3I/Atlas could be 'alien spaceship' after 'heartbeat-like pulse' seen

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS may exhibit a pulsating “heartbeat” pattern, according to claims made by astrophysicist Avi Loeb. Since the summer, this object has been moving through our solar system and is expected to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this phenomenon.

The discovery of the pulsating jets from 3I/ATLAS raises questions about its nature, prompting scientists to examine the possibility of it being an extraterrestrial object. NASA has classified it as a comet, but the unusual characteristics have sparked debate among experts. Authorities and researchers are closely monitoring the object as it approaches Earth.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Claimed pulsating variability period: 16.16 hours
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19
  • Notable researcher: Avi Loeb, Harvard professor
  • Classification: Comet (according to NASA)

Timeline

Since the summer, 3I/ATLAS has been traveling through the solar system. In November, Avi Loeb reported that the object displayed a multi-jet structure and would reach its closest point to Earth in December.

Avi Loeb stated, "the light from the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS showed pulsating variability with a period of 16.16 hours."

Context

This is not the first time an object has raised speculation about extraterrestrial life. Previous incidents involving unusual celestial bodies have led to increased interest in the study of interstellar objects. The scientific community continues to explore and investigate the origins and nature of such phenomena.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth, with further analysis expected to clarify its characteristics and origins.

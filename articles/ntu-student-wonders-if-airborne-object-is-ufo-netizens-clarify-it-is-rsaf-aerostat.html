NTU student wonders if airborne object is UFO, netizens clarify it is RSAF Aerostat

A glowing red object spotted over the Nanyang Technological University (NTU) campus in Singapore on Thursday, 13 November, has led some netizens to question if it is a UFO. The object was identified by others as the Republic of Singapore Air Force's (RSAF) Aerostat, a surveillance tool, according to multiple Reddit users.

This sighting has generated discussions online, highlighting public curiosity about unidentified flying objects. Authorities have confirmed that the object is part of the RSAF's surveillance operations, which aim to enhance national security.

Key facts

  • Sighting occurred on 13 November 2023.
  • Location: Nanyang Technological University (NTU), Singapore.
  • Object identified as RSAF Aerostat, a tethered balloon system.
  • Height of the Aerostat: 600 metres.
  • Detection range: Up to 200 km for aerial and maritime threats.

Timeline

On 13 November 2023, a Reddit user posted photos of a mysterious glowing object over NTU. The images depicted a large grey craft with a bulbous bottom and a bright red glow.

Following the post, various users speculated whether the object was a UFO or a weather detection device. However, many quickly recognized it as the RSAF's Aerostat.

The RSAF confirmed that the Aerostat provides continuous, low-level surveillance and is part of a broader network of sensors used to secure the nation.

Official statements

“The Aerostat is designed to provide continuous, low-level surveillance,” said Ng Eng Hen, Minister for Defence, on Facebook.

Context

The RSAF's Aerostat is one of several airborne assets used for national security. Other tools include Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) and the G550 AEW, which serves as an airborne early warning and control aircraft. Similar sightings have previously sparked interest and speculation among the public regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to monitor public interest and engagement regarding aerial surveillance tools and their role in national security.

Comet 3I ATLAS Approaches Sun at 60 km/s

Comet 3I ATLAS has been recorded traveling towards the Sun at a speed of 60 kilometres per second. The comet is expected to reach its closest point to the Sun, known as perihelion, on March 12, 2026.

This event is significant for astronomers and space agencies as the comet's trajectory will provide insights into the composition of comets and the solar system. Observations of Comet 3I ATLAS are crucial for understanding its behavior and potential impact on Earth's atmosphere.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I ATLAS
  • Speed: 60 km/s
  • Closest Approach: March 12, 2026
  • Current Location: Not disclosed
  • Agencies Involved: Not disclosed

Timeline

Comet 3I ATLAS has been monitored since its discovery. As it approaches perihelion, scientists have intensified their observations.

The comet is expected to provide valuable data as it nears the Sun, helping to refine models of comet behavior.

Context

Comet 3I ATLAS is part of a larger group of comets that have been studied over the years. This particular comet's trajectory has been of interest due to its speed and proximity to the Sun.

What’s next

New Comet C/2025 V1 Borisov Is Speeding Past Earth – But Where’s Its Tail?

A newly discovered comet, C/2025 V1 Borisov, is speeding through the solar system, situated between Earth and interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. This comet has drawn attention from astronomers worldwide as it approaches its closest point to Earth on 11 November 2025, at a distance of approximately 103 million kilometres.

The comet's unusual lack of a prominent tail has sparked curiosity and speculation among enthusiasts. However, astronomers have stated that this subdued appearance is likely due to its composition and distance from the Sun. C/2025 V1 Borisov is expected to provide excellent observation opportunities for astronomers as it approaches perihelion on 16 November 2025.

Key facts

  • Comet C/2025 V1 Borisov has a brightness magnitude of 13.8.
  • Its coma spans 2.3 arcminutes, according to the Comet Observers Database (COBS).
  • It will pass Earth at a distance of about 103 million kilometres on 11 November 2025.
  • The comet's perihelion will occur on 16 November 2025.
  • C/2025 V1 Borisov is currently about 106 million kilometres away from Earth.
  • Light from the comet takes nearly six minutes to reach Earth.
  • It rises around 4:01 a.m., reaches its highest point near 10:01 a.m., and sets by 4:03 p.m. in the UK.

Timeline

C/2025 V1 Borisov has gained attention since its discovery due to its unusual characteristics. The comet was recently observed with a brightness of 13.8, indicating its visibility. Astronomers have confirmed that it originates from the Oort Cloud within our Solar System.

As it approaches Earth, C/2025 V1 Borisov will continue to brighten until its perihelion on 16 November 2025. This period will provide optimal viewing conditions for astronomers and skywatchers alike.

Official statements

“The comet's subdued appearance is likely due to its composition and distance from the Sun, which means it has not yet developed the glowing tail typical of highly active comets,” said an astronomer from the Comet Observers Database.

Context

C/2025 V1 Borisov is not the first comet to attract interest due to its characteristics. Previous comets have exhibited varying degrees of brightness and tail formation, often leading to speculation about their origins and behaviour. The study of comets provides valuable insights into the formation of our Solar System and the nature of icy bodies in space.

What’s next

As C/2025 V1 Borisov approaches its perihelion, astronomers will continue to monitor its brightness and behaviour. Observatories and astronomy platforms are preparing to provide resources for skywatchers to locate and observe the comet during its pass.

UFO's Phil Mogg says reissue of 'Wild, Willing and Innocent' shows band at top form

UFO has announced the deluxe reissue of their 1981 album The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent, set to release on May 1, 2024. Lead singer Phil Mogg stated this reissue highlights the band at its creative peak, featuring remastered audio and a previously unreleased live set recorded at London’s Hammersmith Odeon.

This reissue is significant for fans and the music community as it showcases UFO's evolution over the years. The band has maintained a dedicated following since its formation in 1969, and the new release aims to attract both longtime fans and new listeners.

Key facts

  • Original release date: January 16, 1981
  • Billboard 200 peak: No. 77
  • UK Albums Chart peak: No. 19
  • Single "Lonely Heart" peak: No. 41 on UK Top 50
  • Deluxe reissue includes remastered audio and live set from January 29, 1981
  • Release date for deluxe edition: May 1, 2024

Timeline

UFO formed in London in 1969, initially as a blues-tinged hard-rock band. The group gained traction with the addition of guitarist Michael Schenker in 1973. The release of Strangers in the Night in 1977 marked their peak in both creativity and commercial success.

After Schenker's departure in 1978, guitarist Paul Chapman and multi-instrumentalist Neil Carter joined the band. The recording sessions for The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent took place in the early 1980s, leading to its release in January 1981. The album received mixed reviews at the time but has since been recognized for its innovative sound.

The upcoming deluxe reissue offers remastered tracks, previously unseen photographs, and new liner notes, providing a fresh perspective on the album’s significance.

Official statements

“Out of most of the albums we’ve done, I’d say that comes up as the most fun in terms of getting it together and doing it. It was a really enjoyable record,” Mogg said.
“He was like a breath of fresh air... You’d say, ‘Can you play that on sax?’ and he’d go, ‘Yeah,’ and do it really well,” Mogg added, referring to Neil Carter's contributions.

Context

UFO's journey has seen numerous lineup changes and shifts in musical trends since their inception. The band has consistently blended melody with hard rock, producing enduring tracks like “Doctor Doctor” and “Lights Out.” The reissue of The Wild, The Willing and The Innocent provides a retrospective look at the band's ability to evolve while maintaining their core sound.

What’s next

The release of the deluxe edition on May 1 will include remastered audio across 3LP 180-gram vinyl and a 2CD Digipak. Fans can expect a renewed appreciation for the band’s work, along with new insights from the liner notes and photographs included in the package.

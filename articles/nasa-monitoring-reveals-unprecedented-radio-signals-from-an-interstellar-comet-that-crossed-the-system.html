Nasa monitoring reveals unprecedented radio signals from an interstellar comet that crossed the system

NASA has detected unprecedented radio signals from the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS, which has recently crossed into the solar system. The monitoring took place over several days in early March 2026, with no injuries or fatalities reported.

This discovery is significant as it provides new insights into the characteristics of interstellar objects. NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) is currently analyzing the data to better understand the comet's composition and origins.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS was detected crossing the solar system in early March 2026.
  • Nasa has reported unprecedented radio signals from the comet.
  • Data analysis is being conducted by NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory.
  • No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this event.

Timeline

In early March 2026, NASA's monitoring systems detected radio signals from 3I ATLAS as it entered the solar system. The signals were identified as unprecedented, prompting further investigation by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

As the comet continues its journey, scientists are gathering data to analyze its properties and potential implications for our understanding of interstellar objects.

Official statements

“This is a groundbreaking moment for our understanding of interstellar comets,” said a spokesperson for NASA. “The data we collect will provide insights that could reshape our knowledge of these celestial bodies.”

Context

This detection follows a series of observations of interstellar objects, including the well-known comet 1I/'Oumuamua in 2017. Scientists have been increasingly interested in studying these objects to learn more about the formation of the solar system and beyond.

What’s next

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory will continue to analyze the data from 3I ATLAS in the coming weeks. Further findings are expected to be released as scientists work to interpret the signals and understand their significance.

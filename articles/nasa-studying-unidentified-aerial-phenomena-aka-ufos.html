NASA Studying Unidentified Aerial Phenomena aka UFOs

NASA has initiated a study into unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), commonly known as UFOs, in the United States. The agency announced its efforts on October 3, 2023, as part of a broader initiative to understand these occurrences more thoroughly.

This study is significant as it marks NASA's formal approach to investigating UAPs. The agency aims to collect data, analyze findings, and provide insights that could enhance public understanding and safety. NASA's commitment to transparency and scientific inquiry is a key element of this initiative.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: October 3, 2023
  • Location: United States
  • Agency involved: NASA
  • Focus: Unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP)

Timeline

NASA's study into UAPs has progressed as follows:

  • October 3, 2023: NASA announces the initiation of its study into UAPs.

Official statements

“NASA is committed to understanding UAPs through a scientific lens,” said a NASA spokesperson. “We aim to gather data and insights that will benefit both the agency and the public.”

Context

This study aligns with growing interest in UAPs, particularly following several government reports highlighting unexplained aerial sightings. NASA's involvement represents a shift towards a more formal and scientific approach to these phenomena.

What’s next

Nasa plans to continue its investigation into UAPs, collecting data and analyzing findings to provide a comprehensive report in the future.

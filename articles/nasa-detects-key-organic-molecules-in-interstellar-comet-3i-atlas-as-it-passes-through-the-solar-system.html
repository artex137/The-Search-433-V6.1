NASA detects key organic molecules in interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it passes through the Solar System

NASA has detected key organic molecules in the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the Solar System. The detection was made on February 26, 2026, during observations conducted by the agency's space telescopes.

This discovery is significant for understanding the chemical composition of comets and their role in the formation of planetary systems. NASA scientists are analyzing the data to gain insights into the origin of these molecules and their implications for astrobiology.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed on February 26, 2026.
  • Key organic molecules detected include hydrocarbons and amino acids.
  • The comet is currently passing through the Solar System.
  • NASA's observations were conducted using advanced space telescopes.

Timeline

NASA has monitored comet 3I/ATLAS since it was first detected in 2019. The latest observations, which revealed the presence of organic molecules, have added to the ongoing research into the comet's characteristics.

In recent months, scientists have intensified their studies of the comet as it approaches the inner Solar System. The findings from February 26 are the latest in a series of observations aimed at understanding the comet's composition.

Official statements

“The detection of these organic molecules is a crucial step in our understanding of the building blocks of life,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of several interstellar objects observed by astronomers. Previous studies of other comets have provided insights into the early Solar System, and the detection of organic molecules in 3I/ATLAS aligns with ongoing research into the potential for life beyond Earth.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue monitoring the comet and analyzing the data collected. Further studies may lead to additional discoveries about the composition of interstellar objects and their significance in the universe.

This Swedish hotel takes you out of reality and into wonder

Treehotel, located in the village of Harads, Sweden, offers a unique escape into nature through its elevated “treerooms.” Guests can check in and walk a 500-metre path through dense birch trees to reach their accommodations, which include designs such as a UFO and a bird’s nest. This experience allows visitors to temporarily leave behind everyday concerns.

The hotel aims to provide a whimsical retreat, combining luxury with a strong connection to the surrounding environment. Authorities have noted its innovative design and eco-friendly features, drawing attention from both local and international travelers.

Key facts

  • Location: Harads, Sweden, 90 km north-west of Lulea Airport.
  • Distance from Stockholm: 1 hour 20-minute flight or 14-hour night train.
  • Room rate: Starting at approximately $880 for two people, including breakfast.
  • Number of treerooms: Eight, each designed by different Scandinavian architects.
  • Newest room: Biosphere, designed by the Bjarke Ingels Group.
  • Access to Biosphere: Via a 23-metre-long bridge.

Timeline

Treehotel has transformed the concept of luxury accommodation by integrating unique architectural designs into the natural landscape. The most recent addition, the Biosphere, has been created by Bjarke Ingels Group and is designed to immerse guests in the forest soundscape.

Guests have reported a mix of practical challenges and enchanting experiences while staying in these treerooms, particularly in the Biosphere, which features a steep ladder leading to a sleeping loft.

Official statements

“How often do you wake up in a giant transparent birdhouse suspended in a forest?”
“Treehotel truly offers what all the best travel experiences do — a powerful pause on reality.”

Context

Treehotel's innovative design approach has garnered attention in the travel industry, with each treeroom reflecting the vision of acclaimed architects. This trend of blending architecture with nature has become increasingly popular among eco-conscious travelers seeking unique experiences.

What’s next

Treehotel plans to continue expanding its offerings and may introduce more treerooms in the future, further enhancing its appeal as a destination for those seeking an extraordinary escape.

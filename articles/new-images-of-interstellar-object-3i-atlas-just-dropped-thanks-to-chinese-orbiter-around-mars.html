New Images Of Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Just Dropped Thanks To Chinese Orbiter Around Mars

China’s Tianwen-1 spacecraft has captured new images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS while orbiting Mars, marking a significant achievement in the observation of objects from beyond our Solar System. The imaging success was announced by the China National Space Administration on a date not disclosed.

This milestone highlights the capabilities of space-based observatories and the technical advancements of the Chinese space program. The mission team faced challenges due to the comet's faint luminosity, which is between 10,000 and 100,000 times dimmer than typical Martian features. The successful imaging of 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing body of knowledge about interstellar objects, alongside 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov.

Key facts

  • Event: Images of comet 3I/ATLAS captured by Tianwen-1
  • Spacecraft: Tianwen-1, in orbit around Mars since February 2021
  • Discovery of 3I/ATLAS: July 1, detected at approximately 58 kilometers per second
  • Distance from Earth: Millions of kilometers
  • Significant features: Anti-tail, unusual chemical composition, trajectory changes
  • Collaboration: Involvement of multiple space agencies, including the European Space Agency

Timeline

On July 1, astronomers detected 3I/ATLAS using the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System. Following its discovery, various observatories confirmed its cometary nature and interstellar origins. The Tianwen-1 spacecraft, launched by China, entered Martian orbit in February 2021 and began capturing images of the comet recently.

As 3I/ATLAS passed behind the Sun from Earth's perspective, the European Space Agency’s Mars Express and ExoMars Trace Gas Orbiter maintained observation. The successful imaging by Tianwen-1 occurred after extensive preparatory work involving simulations and calculations.

Official statements

“The observation campaign involved multiple space agencies working independently yet contributing to a comprehensive understanding of this remarkable object,” said a representative from the China National Space Administration.

Context

3I/ATLAS is classified as an interstellar object, a category that includes only two other confirmed entities: 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Scientists view these objects as potential time capsules that may contain material from the formation periods of other stellar systems, providing insights into the conditions of distant environments.

What’s next

Researchers are now analyzing the observational data gathered from Tianwen-1 and other spacecraft. Earth-based observatories have resumed monitoring 3I/ATLAS as it emerges from behind the Sun, with expectations that the ESA’s JUICE spacecraft may also capture additional observations of the comet.

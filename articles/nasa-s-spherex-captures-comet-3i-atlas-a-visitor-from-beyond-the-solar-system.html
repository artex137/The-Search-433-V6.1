NASA’s SPHEREx Captures Comet 3I/ATLAS, a Visitor From Beyond the Solar System

NASA's SPHEREx (Spectro-Photometer for the History of the Universe, Epoch of Reionization and Ices Explorer) has observed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from August 7 to August 15, 2025. The agency has reported no injuries or fatalities related to this observation.

This observation is significant as it contributes to understanding the comet's size, physical properties, and chemical makeup. NASA's SPHEREx, along with the Webb and Hubble space telescopes, continues to support the agency's mission to track and comprehend solar system objects, despite the comet posing no threat to Earth.

Key facts

  • Observation period: August 7 to August 15, 2025
  • Observing agency: NASA
  • Instruments used: SPHEREx, Webb, and Hubble space telescopes
  • Comet designation: 3I/ATLAS
  • Threat level: None to Earth

Timeline

NASA's SPHEREx has been actively observing interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS between August 7 and August 15, 2025. The data collected during this period has been under analysis by the SPHEREx team, with insights already available online.

Official statements

“NASA's space telescopes help support the agency's ongoing mission to find, track, and better understand solar system objects,” a NASA spokesperson stated.

Context

This observation is part of NASA's broader efforts to monitor interstellar objects and enhance our understanding of their characteristics. The use of multiple telescopes, including SPHEREx, Webb, and Hubble, reflects a collaborative approach to astronomical research.

What’s next

The SPHEREx team will continue to analyze the data collected from the observation of comet 3I/ATLAS to provide further insights into its properties and origins.

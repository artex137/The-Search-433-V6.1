Hollywood Suddenly Taking UFOs Seriously, With Rival “Disclosure” Projects in the Works

Hollywood has embraced a new genre focused on UFOs, with multiple projects exploring the topic of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). Major filmmakers including Steven Spielberg and Joseph Kosinski are leading the charge as interest in UAPs has surged, fueled by media coverage, congressional hearings, and government whistleblowers. The trend marks a shift in how UAPs are perceived, with the truth feeling increasingly within reach.

This surge in UFO-themed projects reflects a growing public and legislative interest in UAPs. Authorities are now actively engaging with the subject, as evidenced by recent congressional hearings and discussions surrounding declassified government footage of UAPs. Filmmakers are capitalizing on this heightened awareness, creating narratives that blend fact and fiction.

Key facts

  • Projects include films from Steven Spielberg and Joseph Kosinski.
  • Producer Bryce Zabel is developing a film titled Unidentified focused on the 1947 Roswell incident.
  • Congressional UAP whistleblower David Grusch is consulting on Kosinski's UFO thriller.
  • Spielberg's film Disclosure Day is set to release on June 12.
  • Last year, the documentary The Age of Disclosure featured interviews with 34 government officials and broke Prime Video’s VOD record.

Timeline

In the past few years, interest in UAPs has intensified:

  • November 2022: The documentary The Age of Disclosure is released, featuring interviews with former government officials about UAPs.
  • February 20, 2023: Former President Donald Trump announces plans to declassify UFO-related files after remarks by Barack Obama about the existence of aliens.
  • June 12, 2023: Spielberg's film Disclosure Day is scheduled to debut, exploring societal reactions to proof of alien life.

Official statements

“Roswell no longer feels like folklore — it feels like unfinished business,” says producer Bryce Zabel. “This isn’t a movie about little green men. This is about what happens to ordinary people when the impossible crashes into their lives.”
“The zeitgeist of our moment right now is the UFO, UAP reality issue,” Zabel adds. “I think we are seeing more and more projects come forward because people are now becoming aware for the first time that this is a serious thing and it’s not crazy.”
Spielberg stated, “People’s questions about what is not only going on in our skies but what is going on in our worlds... has reached a critical mass of people’s complete fascination with: Are we alone, or are we not alone?”

Context

Previous incidents and policies have shaped the current landscape of UFO discourse. The Pentagon has maintained that it has found no evidence of alien life in reported UFO sightings. However, the narrative has evolved, with some believing that UFOs could be advanced U.S. military technology. The recent involvement of high-profile figures in discussing UAPs has further fueled public interest.

What’s next

As projects like Spielberg's Disclosure Day approach their release dates, the conversation around UAPs is likely to continue evolving. Investigations into UAPs are ongoing, with filmmakers and authorities alike poised to explore the implications of increased public interest in this subject.

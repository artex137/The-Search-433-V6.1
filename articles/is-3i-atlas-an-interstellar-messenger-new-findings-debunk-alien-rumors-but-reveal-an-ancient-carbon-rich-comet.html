Is 3I/ATLAS an Interstellar Messenger? New Findings Debunk Alien Rumors but Reveal an Ancient, Carbon-Rich Comet

Comet 3I/ATLAS, discovered on 1 July 2025 by the ATLAS survey, is the third object identified as entering the solar system from interstellar space. Its highly hyperbolic orbit and speed near 210,000 km/h confirm it is unbound to the Sun, with no threat to Earth.

This discovery has significant implications for our understanding of planetary systems and the formation of celestial bodies. NASA and ESA are conducting extensive observations and analysis to study the comet's composition and origins, aiming to glean insights into conditions beyond our solar system.

Key facts

  • Discovered: 1 July 2025
  • Speed: Approximately 210,000 km/h
  • Closest approach to Earth: ~1.8 AU (270 million km)
  • Perihelion date: 29–30 October 2025
  • Estimated size of nucleus: Between 440 m and 5.6 km in diameter
  • Closest approach to Mars: 3 October 2025, within 29 million km

Timeline

In June 2025, astronomers with the ATLAS survey identified an object with a high orbital eccentricity moving through the outer solar system. Follow-up observations confirmed its interstellar origin, leading to its designation as 3I/ATLAS.

The comet will reach perihelion around 29–30 October 2025, passing within approximately 1.4 AU from the Sun. It will then cross Jupiter's orbit in March 2026, continuing its path into interstellar space.

Official statements

“The comet poses no danger and provides an unprecedented scientific opportunity,” NASA and ESA stated in response to viral claims on social media that 3I/ATLAS is an alien spacecraft or on a collision course with Earth.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third confirmed interstellar visitor to the solar system, following ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Its hyperbolic trajectory and activity early in its approach differentiate it from typical long-period comets.

Previous findings have suggested that comets can provide insights into the early conditions of planetary systems, making 3I/ATLAS a valuable subject for ongoing research.

What’s next

NASA and various space agencies will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun, with multiple missions planned to study its emissions and composition. The upcoming SPHEREx infrared survey aims to map the comet's gases, allowing scientists to analyze its chemical fingerprint.

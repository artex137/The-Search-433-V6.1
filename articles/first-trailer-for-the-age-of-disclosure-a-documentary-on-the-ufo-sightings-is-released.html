First trailer for The Age of Disclosure, a documentary on UFO sightings, released

The first trailer for the documentary The Age of Disclosure has been released, sparking discussions about unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP) and their implications for national security. The film, directed by Dan Farah, features testimonies from current and former government, military, and intelligence officials who assert that UAP are real and that information about them is being withheld.

This documentary is significant as it aims to bring the UAP conversation into mainstream discourse. According to the official description, the film has received support from senior members of the U.S. government and aims to shed light on the potential impact of UAP on humanity's future.

Key facts

  • Documentary title: The Age of Disclosure
  • Director: Dan Farah
  • Premiere date: November 21
  • Streaming release: Prime Video
  • Key interviewees: Secretary of State Marco Rubio, Sen. Kristen Gillibrand, Sen. Mike Rounds, General Jim Clapper
  • Production time: Two-and-a-half years

Timeline

The documentary The Age of Disclosure premiered at the SXSW festival and is set to reach select theaters on November 21, 2023. The film has been in production for two-and-a-half years, focusing on interviews with individuals who have direct knowledge of UAP.

Official statements

“My goal was to only interview people who had direct knowledge of the UAP topic as a result of their work for the U.S. government, and who would share what they can legally disclose,” said director Dan Farah in an interview with The Hollywood Reporter. “There are people who want the topic to continue to be kept from the public entirely.”

Context

The release of The Age of Disclosure coincides with a growing interest in UAP issues, moving discussions from fringe circles into mainstream media. Critics and scientists remain divided, with some praising the film's sourcing and others cautioning that witness testimony does not equate to conclusive evidence.

What’s next

The documentary will be available in select theaters and on Prime Video starting November 21, 2023. It is expected to generate further discussions and calls for the declassification of related government information.

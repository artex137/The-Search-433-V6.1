Mysterious UFO hotspots uncovered around underwater canyons off US coasts

New research has indicated that UFOs may be clustering around underwater canyons off the US coastline, particularly along the West Coast. The analysis, published on February 28, 2026, has revealed concentrated sightings near steep submarine canyon systems, raising questions about the possible origins of these unidentified aerial phenomena.

The findings are significant as they highlight a potential correlation between UFO sightings and underwater terrain features, according to the study conducted by Antoni Wędzikowski, a Warsaw-based lawyer and independent researcher. Authorities and researchers are now calling for further investigation into these patterns, which could yield insights into the nature of unexplained aerial activity.

Key facts

  • Study analyzed over 80,000 UFO reports from the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC).
  • Significant clustering of sightings observed near La Jolla, Mugu Canyon, and Monterey Canyon.
  • La Jolla recorded 820 sightings, compared to an expected baseline of 55.
  • Findings indicate that UFO sightings do not occur randomly but in bursts over short periods.
  • Geographical anomalies noted only on the West Coast, with no similar patterns on the East or Gulf coasts.

Timeline

In November 2004, US Navy pilots aboard the USS Nimitz reported multiple unidentified objects during training exercises off Southern California. The objects reportedly descended from 80,000 feet in less than a second, with one pilot describing a white, capsule-shaped craft known as the 'Tic Tac.'

In 2026, Wędzikowski conducted an analysis of UFO sightings using publicly available data and bathymetric maps, identifying correlations between sightings and submarine canyon features. His study revealed significant clustering patterns on the West Coast, which were not present in other coastal regions.

Official statements

“First, the analysis did find a statistically significant spatial association between submarine canyon features and UAP report density along the US West Coast,” said Wędzikowski. “These are not trivial numbers.”
“The specific hypothesis, that UAP report density might correlate with submarine canyon features along the coastline, came from noticing that several well-known hotspot areas sit above unusually steep underwater topography,” Wędzikowski added.

Context

The cryptoterrestrial hypothesis, which suggests that unidentified aerial phenomena could originate from hidden non-human intelligence on Earth, has gained renewed interest due to recent high-profile military encounters. The study's findings contribute to this growing scientific interest, particularly regarding objects that transition between air and sea.

Wędzikowski's work marks a systematic approach to analyzing UFO data, which has often been discussed through anecdotal evidence rather than rigorous analysis. He has called for further studies using independent datasets and methods to validate these findings.

What’s next

Wędzikowski plans to publish his methodology and data for peer review and encourages other researchers to replicate his study. He aims to investigate further whether sonar data can provide insights into unexplained underwater activity coinciding with reported UFO hotspots.

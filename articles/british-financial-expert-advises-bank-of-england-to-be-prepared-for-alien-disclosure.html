British Financial Expert Advises Bank of England to be Prepared for Alien Disclosure

A British financial expert has advised the Bank of England to prepare for potential alien disclosure, according to a letter sent on January 21, 2026. Helen McCaw, a former senior analyst at the Bank of England, addressed her concerns to Andrew Bailey, the head of the institution. McCaw stated that the United States is in the process of declassifying and disclosing information regarding a technologically advanced non-human intelligence linked to the UFO phenomenon.

This recommendation highlights the growing seriousness with which authorities view the UFO phenomenon. McCaw argues that if such a disclosure occurs, world leaders may face a power or intelligence beyond governmental control, which could lead to significant psychological and economic repercussions.

  • Sender: Helen McCaw, former senior analyst at the Bank of England
  • Recipient: Andrew Bailey, head of the Bank of England
  • Key concerns: Potential alien disclosure and its impact on financial markets
  • Predicted outcomes: Financial instability, market volatility, and societal unrest

Timeline

On January 21, 2026, Helen McCaw sent a letter to Andrew Bailey, urging the Bank of England to prepare for possible alien disclosure. In her correspondence, she outlined the implications of such an event, including potential volatility in financial markets and societal responses.

McCaw's warning comes amid ongoing discussions regarding UFOs and their implications for global security and economics. She emphasized that the Bank should consider contingency plans in light of these developments.

Official statements

“Even if you feel it’s very unlikely, it’s madness not to consider it and plan accordingly,” McCaw wrote to Bailey.
McCaw predicted that “in a matter of hours, you are going to have total financial instability” following any official disclosure.

Context

The UFO phenomenon has gained increasing attention in recent years, leading to calls for transparency and accountability from governments worldwide. The discourse surrounding potential alien disclosure has evolved, with financial experts now weighing in on the economic ramifications of such revelations.

McCaw's letter reflects a shift in perspective, suggesting that the implications of UFO disclosure extend beyond national security to include significant economic and societal factors.

What’s next

The Bank of England has not publicly responded to McCaw's letter. It remains to be seen if they will implement any contingency plans in response to her warnings about the potential consequences of alien disclosure.

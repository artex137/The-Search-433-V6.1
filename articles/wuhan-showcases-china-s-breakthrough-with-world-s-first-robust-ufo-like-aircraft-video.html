Wuhan showcases China’s breakthrough with world’s first robust UFO-like aircraft

China has unveiled a new range of Electric Vertical Take-Off and Landing (eVTOL) aircraft, including a UFO-shaped model, during a public demonstration on February 24 in Wuhan, Hubei province. The aircraft can reportedly lift off within three seconds and is designed for logistics transport and aerial rescue missions, pending regulatory certifications.

This development is significant for China's ambitions in the low-altitude economy, a sector deemed a national strategic priority. The launch event also coincided with Hubei’s provincial meeting, where officials highlighted the region’s economic strategy and commitment to advancing innovation in urban air mobility.

Key facts

  • Event date: February 24, 2023
  • Location: Hongshan Auditorium, Wuhan, Hubei province
  • Aircraft maximum payload capacity: 450 kilograms
  • Number of eVTOL models showcased: Four
  • Prototypes developed by Hubei: Nine, with four completing test flights
  • Projected year for eVTOL commercialization: 2026

Timeline

On February 24, 2023, a public demonstration took place in Wuhan, where a silent eVTOL aircraft rose into the air. The showcase included four distinct models and occurred during Hubei's provincial meeting, which focused on economic strategies.

Officials have indicated a commitment to advancing the low-altitude economy, with plans for the aircraft to be utilized in logistics and rescue missions once they receive the necessary certifications.

Official statements

“The aircraft hovers steadily like a real UFO, revolutionizing urban air mobility with its innovative design,” according to a report by Interesting Engineering.

Context

The unveiling of the eVTOL aircraft reflects a growing interest in urban air mobility technologies within China and globally. Hubei's initiative is part of a broader push to develop the low-altitude economy, which is expected to be a significant area of growth in the coming years.

Other countries are also progressing in this field, with Singapore recently introducing its first domestically designed eVTOL at the Singapore Airshow 2026, marking a global trend towards commercialization in this sector.

What’s next

Manufacturers are currently focused on securing type certification and transitioning from prototype development to mass production. The year 2026 is seen as critical for the commercialization of eVTOLs in China and beyond.

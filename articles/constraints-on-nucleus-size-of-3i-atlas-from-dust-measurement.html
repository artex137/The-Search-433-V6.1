Constraints on nucleus size of 3I/ATLAS from dust measurement

Observations of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS have revealed a jet of dust extending from the object, allowing scientists to constrain the size of its nucleus. The findings were reported by Avi Loeb in Medium and indicate that the characteristic radius of dust particles in the anti-tail jet is approximately 10 microns.

This information is significant as it provides insights into the physical characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, aiding researchers in understanding the object's composition and behavior. Authorities are using these measurements to estimate the minimum size of the nucleus, which is crucial for future observations and studies of the comet.

Key facts

  • Characteristic radius of dust particles: ~10 microns
  • Mass density of gas in the outflow: D∼(3.2∗10−8 g/cm³)∗(d/1 km)−2
  • Minimum nucleus radius for 3I/ATLAS: >1 km
  • Minimum nucleus diameter: ~2 kilometers
  • Scattering probability P: ~1 at radius d
  • Potential nucleus radius if dust serves a protective function: <0.7 km

Timeline

Recent observations of 3I/ATLAS have led scientists to analyze the dust jet emitted from the comet. This analysis included measuring the characteristics of dust particles and their interaction with sunlight. The calculations derived from these observations have resulted in estimates of the nucleus size and other physical properties of the object.

Avi Loeb's findings have prompted further investigations into the nature of 3I/ATLAS, particularly regarding the implications of dust release on the nucleus's exposure to sunlight.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, “If the scattering probability P exceeds unity, the nucleus surface of a natural comet would not be exposed to sunlight, stopping the release of gas or dust.”
Loeb further explained, “The measured mass loss rate and inferred dust density provide constraints for both natural and artificial scenarios.”

Context

The observations of 3I/ATLAS contribute to the broader understanding of interstellar objects and their behaviors. Previous studies have indicated that dust jets can significantly influence the dynamics of cometary bodies. The current findings add to this knowledge by providing specific constraints on nucleus size based on dust measurement.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from 3I/ATLAS to refine their estimates of the nucleus size and assess the implications for future observations. Additional imaging with higher spatial resolution may provide more clarity on the object's characteristics.

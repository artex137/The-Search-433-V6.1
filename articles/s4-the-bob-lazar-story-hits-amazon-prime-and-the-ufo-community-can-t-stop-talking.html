S4: The Bob Lazar Story Hits Amazon Prime and the UFO Community Can’t Stop Talking

Bob Lazar's documentary, S4: The Bob Lazar Story, has premiered on Amazon Prime Video on April 3, 2026. The film, directed by Luigi Vendittelli and Christopher Matteau, explores Lazar's claims about his work at S4, a classified facility near Area 51, where he alleges the U.S. government is reverse-engineering extraterrestrial spacecraft.

This documentary has sparked renewed interest and debate within the UFO community. It visually reconstructs Lazar's claims in a way that previous accounts have not, using advanced 3D recreations to depict the S4 facility and the technology he described. The film aims to provide a clearer understanding of Lazar's assertions, though it does not settle the controversy surrounding his credibility.

Key facts

  • Documentary title: S4: The Bob Lazar Story
  • Premiere date: April 3, 2026
  • Directors: Luigi Vendittelli and Christopher Matteau
  • Original interview by George Knapp: 1989
  • IMDb rating: 7.7
  • Rotten Tomatoes audience rating: 95%
  • Notable appearances: Bob Lazar, George Knapp, Gene Huff, Joy Lazar

Timeline

In 1989, Bob Lazar was interviewed by journalist George Knapp, where he claimed to have worked at S4, a secretive site near Area 51. Lazar described the propulsion system of an extraterrestrial craft, which he attributed to a substance he called Element 115. In 2015, this element was officially named Moscovium by the International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry.

Decades later, the documentary S4: The Bob Lazar Story was released, presenting a visual interpretation of Lazar’s claims. The film showcases detailed recreations of the S4 facility and the alleged technology, aiming to provide clarity on Lazar's assertions. The film's release coincided with Lazar's second appearance on the Joe Rogan Experience, where he discussed the documentary and his experiences.

Official statements

“The visual fidelity with which S4 renders it eliminates the need for abstraction,” said a review highlighting the documentary's approach to depicting Lazar's claims.
“Everything in the movie has been accessible on YouTube for years,” remarked a Rotten Tomatoes reviewer, questioning the film's originality.

Context

The film arrives at a time when the U.S. government has officially recognized Area 51, while S4 remains unacknowledged. Over the years, various whistleblowers have come forward, and Congress has held hearings on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). The shift from outright denial to a more ambiguous stance regarding UAPs has changed the landscape of public discourse on the subject.

Bob Lazar's 1989 interview has played a significant role in shaping current discussions around UAPs. He introduced key terminologies and concepts that continue to resonate in contemporary debates, regardless of his reliability as a source.

What’s next

As interest in the documentary grows, discussions about Lazar's claims and the implications of UAPs are likely to continue. Future investigations and hearings may further explore the topics raised in the film, keeping the conversation alive in both public and governmental arenas.

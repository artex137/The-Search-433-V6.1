Pentagon working to release UFO and alien files following Trump's directive

WASHINGTON — Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth confirmed on Monday that the Pentagon is working to identify and release classified government files related to UFOs, unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs), and extraterrestrial life. This initiative follows a directive issued by President Donald Trump last week. Hegseth spoke to reporters during a visit to Sierra Space, a commercial aerospace company in Louisville, Colorado.

This effort is significant as it represents a formal government response to longstanding public interest in UFOs and potential extraterrestrial life. Hegseth stated that the Pentagon will be "in full compliance" with Trump's instructions and urged reporters to "standby" for further updates. He emphasized that the department is actively engaged in the review process.

Key facts

  • Trump issued the directive on Thursday night via Truth Social.
  • Hegseth confirmed the Pentagon's involvement during a visit to Sierra Space in Louisville, Colorado.
  • The Pentagon's All-Domain Anomaly Resolution Office has received hundreds of UAP reports.
  • No evidence of extraterrestrial activity has been found to date, according to a 2024 report.
  • Declassified documents will be made available on the National Archives' website.
  • No timeline or classification status for the file release has been disclosed.

Timeline

Last week, President Trump directed Hegseth and relevant agencies to begin identifying and releasing files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This announcement followed former President Barack Obama's comments about the existence of life beyond Earth.

Hegseth confirmed the Pentagon's engagement in this effort during a visit to Sierra Space on Monday. He acknowledged the unusual nature of the assignment and indicated that the review process is ongoing.

Official statements

“We've got our people working on it right now. I don't want to oversell how much time it will take, but we're digging in,” Hegseth said.
“It is important to underscore that, to date, AARO has discovered no evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity, or technology,” the Pentagon's report stated.
Rep. Anna Paulina Luna, R-Fla., commented that declassified documents will be made available on the National Archives' website.
Sen. John Fetterman, D-Pa., called a potential disclosure “a bipartisan thing.”

Context

The Pentagon has tracked UAP reports for decades. Bipartisan interest in UAP transparency has been growing in Congress for years. Advocates have expressed skepticism regarding the administration's commitment to providing tangible results despite the announced efforts.

Former President Obama’s recent comments about the likelihood of extraterrestrial life have further fueled public interest in this issue.

What’s next

The Pentagon will continue its review of classified files, with updates expected in the coming weeks. The timeline and scope of the information to be released remain unclear.

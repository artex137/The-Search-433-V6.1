20 scientists confirm the possibility that what appears to be comet 3I/ATLAS is a spacecraft camouflaged by its anomalous trajectory and composition

On July 1st, a team of 20 scientists announced the possibility that comet 3I/ATLAS may not be a comet at all, but rather a spacecraft disguised by its unusual trajectory and composition. The discovery of 3I/ATLAS marks it as the third interstellar object detected in history, following 1I/‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this finding.

This announcement has sparked interest within the scientific community about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Researchers are now considering the implications of the object’s characteristics, which include high speed and a reddish glow. Authorities are monitoring the object closely as they seek to understand whether it may harbor technological signals indicative of extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2023
  • Number of Scientists Involved: 20
  • Previous Interstellar Objects: 1I/‘Oumuamua (2017), 2I/Borisov (2019)
  • Current Classification: Potential comet or spacecraft
  • Characteristics: High speed, reddish glow, anomalous trajectory

Timeline

On July 1, 2023, researchers discovered 3I/ATLAS. The object is noted for its high speed and distinct reddish glow. Following its discovery, a study published by 20 scientists proposed that the object might be a spacecraft rather than a traditional comet.

The team acknowledged that while the most likely classification remains a comet, they cannot dismiss the possibility of it being a cloaked spacecraft. This has led to heightened interest in monitoring the object for potential alien technosignatures.

Official statements

“We cannot rule out any hypothesis, especially when these objects hide technological signals like this!”
Astronomer Avi Loeb from Harvard stated, “The changes in the acceleration of 3I/ATLAS could match with a controlled movement, as if it were a ship and someone (or something) was piloting it.”

Context

The case of 3I/ATLAS is reminiscent of 1I/‘Oumuamua, which exhibited a strange trajectory and lacked a cometary tail, leading to speculation regarding its artificial nature. In contrast, 2I/Borisov was easily classified as a comet due to its typical behavior. The current study emphasizes the need for further observation to distinguish between natural phenomena and potential signals of extraterrestrial technology.

What’s next

Scientists plan to continue observing 3I/ATLAS to gather more data. The upcoming launch of the LSST telescope at the Rubin Observatory is expected to identify between six and fifty more interstellar objects over the next decade, providing additional opportunities for research and discovery.

Are the alien files a distraction from Epstein files? Trump orders release of documents on UFOs, as Seth Meyers predicted

Published 2026-02-22 11:57 UTC

Trump Orders Release of UFO Files Amid Public Interest

US President Donald Trump has directed US agencies, including the Defence Department, to begin identifying and releasing government files related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and extraterrestrial life. The announcement, made on Truth Social, does not specify what information these files may contain or their significance.

This directive has generated considerable public interest, especially following recent comments from former President Barack Obama, who acknowledged the existence of aliens in a podcast but later clarified that he had not seen evidence of extraterrestrial life during his presidency. Some observers have speculated that Trump’s announcement aims to divert attention from ongoing discussions surrounding the Epstein files.

Key facts

  • Announcement made by President Donald Trump on Truth Social.
  • Trump has directed relevant US agencies to release files related to UAPs and extraterrestrial life.
  • Public interest in UFOs has been high, driven by conspiracy theories and fascination.
  • Former President Barack Obama recently stated that aliens are 'real' but later backtracked on evidence.
  • The Pentagon has tracked UAP reports for decades but reported no evidence of extraterrestrial life in a 2024 report.
  • Trump expressed uncertainty about the existence of aliens, stating, "I don't know if they're real or not."

Timeline

In July 2025, President Trump made his announcement regarding the release of UFO files, coinciding with a surge in public interest following Obama's comments about aliens. Just days prior, Obama claimed that aliens were 'real' but later clarified that he had not seen evidence during his presidency. The context of Trump's announcement has led to speculation about its timing, particularly in relation to ongoing discussions about the Epstein files.

On the same day, Andrew Mountbatten Windsor was arrested on suspicion of misconduct in public office, after the Department of Justice released approximately 3 million documents related to Jeffrey Epstein.

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs), and any and all other information connected to these highly complex, but extremely interesting and important, matters. GOD BLESS AMERICA!”
“All investigative efforts, at all levels of classification, concluded that most sightings were ordinary objects and phenomena and the result of misidentification.” - Maj. Gen. Pat Ryde, Pentagon press secretary.

Context

The renewed focus on UFOs comes amid ongoing public interest in extraterrestrial life, which has been fueled by conspiracy theories and high-profile statements from politicians. The Pentagon's position, as stated in a 2024 report, has maintained that there is no evidence supporting the existence of extraterrestrial life, despite decades of tracking UAP reports.

Trump’s timing has drawn comparisons to past instances where announcements have coincided with other significant news events, leading to speculation about potential distractions from ongoing controversies.

What’s next

As agencies begin the process of identifying and releasing UFO-related files, public and media interest is expected to grow. Investigations into the Epstein files are ongoing, and it remains to be seen how Trump’s announcement will impact both discussions surrounding extraterrestrial life and the ongoing scrutiny of his connections to the Epstein case.

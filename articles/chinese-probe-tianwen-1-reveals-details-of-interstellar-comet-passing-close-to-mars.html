Chinese probe Tianwen-1 reveals details of interstellar comet passing close to Mars

The Chinese space probe Tianwen-1 has observed the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS as it passes close to Mars, according to a report released on February 26, 2026. The probe, which has been in operation since its arrival at Mars in 2021, has provided significant data about the comet's trajectory and characteristics.

This discovery is important for understanding the composition and behaviour of interstellar objects, as well as their potential impact on planetary systems. The China National Space Administration (CNSA) has indicated that further analysis of the data collected by Tianwen-1 will continue to enhance scientific knowledge about comets and their origins.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: 3I ATLAS
  • Probe: Tianwen-1
  • Agency: China National Space Administration (CNSA)
  • Date of Report: February 26, 2026
  • Location: Close to Mars

Timeline

The Tianwen-1 probe entered Mars' orbit in February 2021. Since then, it has conducted various scientific observations and experiments. The latest findings regarding comet 3I ATLAS were reported on February 26, 2026, following the probe's ongoing monitoring of the comet's approach.

Official statements

“The observations made by Tianwen-1 provide crucial insights into the nature of interstellar comets,” said a CNSA spokesperson.

Context

This is not the first time that interstellar comets have been observed. Previous objects, such as 'Oumuamua, have sparked interest and debate among scientists regarding their origins and characteristics. The study of such comets helps to broaden understanding of the solar system and cosmic phenomena.

What’s next

The CNSA plans to continue analyzing the data from Tianwen-1 and will release additional findings as they become available. Further research will explore the implications of the comet's characteristics on planetary science.

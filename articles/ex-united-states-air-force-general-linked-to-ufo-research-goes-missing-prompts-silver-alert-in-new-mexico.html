Ex-United States Air Force General Linked to UFO Research Goes Missing — Prompts Silver Alert In New Mexico

William Neil McCasland, a 68-year-old retired U.S. Air Force general known for his involvement in UFO research, has been reported missing in New Mexico. He was last seen at approximately 11:00 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has initiated an investigation into his disappearance.

Authorities have issued a Silver Alert for McCasland, who has been described as an experienced outdoorsman. Concerns for his wellbeing have increased due to reports of an unspecified medical condition. He is also noted to have left home without his phone or watch at the time of his disappearance.

Key facts

  • Age: 68 years
  • Height: 5 feet, 11 inches

Timeline

McCasland was last seen on March 2, 2026, at around 11:00 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE. Following his disappearance, the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert and began coordinating with local, state, and federal agencies to locate him.

As of the latest reports, a woman has claimed to have seen a man matching McCasland's description, but no additional leads have emerged. The 377th Air Base Wing at Kirtland Air Force Base is also involved in the investigation, focusing on retracing his steps.

Official statements

“Our priority is finding Mr. McCasland safely. Our investigators and search teams are working continuously, and we’re coordinating closely with our local, state, and federal partners,” said Sheriff John Allen.
“We’re asking the public to help by checking and preserving any security camera footage from the area and reporting any information immediately,” Allen added.

Context

McCasland retired from the U.S. Air Force in 2013 after leading the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base in Ohio. This base is often discussed in relation to UFO research, particularly concerning the Roswell Incident. Although conspiracy theories abound regarding this incident, official sources have characterized the debris involved as unrelated to extraterrestrial activity.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office continues to investigate McCasland's disappearance, focusing on tips and leads. Updates regarding the case will be released by local law enforcement as new information becomes available.

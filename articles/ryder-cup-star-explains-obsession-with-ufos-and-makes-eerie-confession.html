Ryder Cup star explains obsession with UFOs and makes eerie confession

Published 2025-09-26 09:28 UTC

Viktor Hovland shares UFO obsession ahead of Ryder Cup

Viktor Hovland has expressed his fascination with UFOs as a way to enhance his focus on the golf course during the Ryder Cup at Bethpage Black, which begins on Friday. The 28-year-old Team Europe star believes his interest in unidentified flying objects helps him avoid obsessing over his golf swing.

Hovland's comments come as Team Europe prepares to defend their title against Team USA in Farmingdale, New York. Teammate Ludvig Aberg noted that Hovland's unusual hobby often generates discussion in the European locker room. Hovland stated that exploring UFO theories allows him to clear his mind and improve his performance.

Key facts

  • Event: Ryder Cup
  • Location: Bethpage Black, Farmingdale, New York
  • Start Date: Friday
  • Team Europe has won eight of the last eleven Ryder Cups since 2002.
  • Team USA has won the last two Ryder Cups, held in 2021 and 2016.
  • Hovland reported having his best driving range session in years on Wednesday.

Timeline

On Wednesday, Hovland achieved a significant milestone with a successful driving range session, boosting his confidence ahead of the tournament. He noted that this improvement in his swing came at a crucial time.

In the lead-up to the Ryder Cup, Hovland has discussed his interest in UFOs and how it helps him manage the pressures of competitive golf.

Team Europe arrived in New York in preparation for the competition, facing the challenge of defeating Team USA on their home turf.

Official statements

“I think it's been one of those things that I've kind of taken my mind off of golf a little bit,” Hovland said. “The more you go into it, you just get more questions.”
“When I went to film it, it looked definitely better than other swings that I’ve made lately. So really pleased with that,” he added regarding his recent driving range performance.

Context

The Ryder Cup, a biennial golf competition, has seen Team Europe dominate in recent decades. However, their record in the United States has been less successful, with only one win in the last four events held on American soil since 2004.

With Team USA entering this year's competition as favorites, Hovland's unique approach to maintaining focus may be vital for Team Europe's chances.

What’s next

The Ryder Cup matches will commence on Friday, with both teams looking to secure an early advantage in the tournament.

The Nature of UFOs / Open Lines

Filmmaker and UFO researcher Dean Alioto has discussed the current state of UFO disclosure and theories about non-human intelligence during an appearance on Coast to Coast AM with guest host Ryan Wrecker on November 28, 2025. Alioto expressed frustration over the limited availability of high-quality UFO footage, despite claims of "phenomenal footage" that remains unseen. No injuries or fatalities were reported.

This discussion is significant as it highlights ongoing public interest and skepticism regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Authorities and researchers are actively investigating these claims, which may impact perceptions of technology and safety.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 28, 2025
  • Location: Coast to Coast AM broadcast
  • Guest host: Ryan Wrecker
  • Guest: Dean Alioto, filmmaker and UFO researcher
  • Key themes: UFO disclosure, non-human intelligence, theories on extraterrestrial life
  • Witness testimonies included reports from Montreal, California, New Mexico, and more

Timeline

During the first half of the show, Dean Alioto presented his views on UFOs and potential extraterrestrial life forms. He noted the presence of limited, low-quality footage available to the public and referenced testimonies from military pilots like Commander David Fravor, who described a UFO exhibiting signs of consciousness. Alioto suggested that these unidentified objects could be organic entities or time travelers rather than traditional spacecraft.

In the second half, the program featured Open Lines, allowing listeners to share their own experiences. Callers recounted various encounters, including sightings of unusual lights and alleged abductions. Each caller contributed to a broader conversation about the implications of UFO phenomena and the nature of advanced technology.

Official statements

“If we can put it together, maybe we've learned something,” Alioto said, reflecting on the potential implications of recovered UFO technology.

Context

This discussion builds on a growing trend of public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial phenomena. Research in this area has surged, with many individuals and organizations advocating for transparency and more comprehensive investigations into reported sightings. The shift in the burden of proof now lies with governments, as suggested by some callers during the Open Lines segment.

What’s next

Investigations into the claims and sightings discussed are expected to continue, with researchers and authorities analyzing new information as it emerges. There are no disclosed deadlines for these investigations.

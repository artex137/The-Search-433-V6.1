Craig Charles: UFO Conspiracies on Blaze to Air September 3

Craig Charles: UFO Conspiracies will air on Blaze at 12:00 AM on Wednesday, September 3. The episode focuses on the 1997 mass UFO sighting over Phoenix, Arizona, where thousands of witnesses reported seeing bright orange orbs moving silently in formation. The event remains unexplained despite extensive video and photographic evidence.

This episode is significant as it revisits one of the most notable UFO sightings in history. The show features eyewitness accounts and examines the response from authorities following the incident.

Key facts

  • Event: 1997 Phoenix UFO sighting
  • Date of airing: September 3, 2023
  • Channel: Blaze
  • Time: 12:00 AM to 1:00 AM
  • Location: Phoenix, Arizona
  • Eyewitness: Nurse David Parker
  • Evidence: Hours of video and photographic documentation

Timeline

In March 1997, a series of bright orange orbs were reported moving in formation over Phoenix. Thousands of residents witnessed the event, which has since been described as one of the most spectacular mass UFO sightings. In the days following the sighting, authorities received numerous reports and conducted investigations, but no conclusive explanations have emerged.

On September 3, 2023, Craig Charles and space journalist Sarah Cruddas will discuss the event, featuring interviews with eyewitnesses, including David Parker, who recounts his experience with a boomerang-shaped UFO.

Official statements

“The Phoenix sighting is one of the most documented UFO events in history. It continues to spark interest and debate,” said Craig Charles.

Context

The 1997 Phoenix incident is often referenced in discussions about UFOs and has influenced public perception of unidentified aerial phenomena. Despite various theories and investigations, the event remains officially unexplained, contributing to ongoing interest in UFO conspiracies.

What’s next

Following the airing of this episode, viewers can expect further discussions and analyses regarding the Phoenix UFO sighting and its implications for understanding unidentified aerial phenomena.

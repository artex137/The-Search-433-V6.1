Exact date 'spaceship' 3I/ATLAS will hit earth revealed as expert claims 'we'll know what it is then'

Comet 3I/ATLAS, referred to as a “spaceship” by some scientists, is expected to make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025. Harvard professor Avi Loeb has stated that this event will provide crucial data that could reveal the true nature of the comet, which has sparked significant interest and speculation in the scientific community.

This upcoming close encounter is significant as it offers an opportunity for scientists to gather vital information about 3I/ATLAS. Professor Loeb has suggested that the data collected during this period could clarify whether the object is a natural comet or potentially an alien spacecraft. The professor has been vocal about his hypothesis that 3I/ATLAS may be of artificial origin.

Key facts

  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Professor Avi Loeb: Harvard University
  • Object speed: 130,000 mph
  • Potential explanations for the “anti-tail”: giant dust particles, icy fragments, or technological thrusters
  • Future spectroscopic data will differentiate between natural and artificial outflows

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been observed by scientists for several months, during which time its unusual characteristics have been studied. Recent imaging has revealed a bizarre “anti-tail” alongside its normal tails, prompting further investigation into its composition and behavior.

On November 11, 2023, Professor Loeb published a blog post detailing the expected date of closest approach and discussing the implications of forthcoming data. He emphasized the detective-like work of scientists in understanding the object.

Official statements

“Figuring things out through the collection of data resembles the work of a detective. With the flood of data on 3I/ATLAS in the coming weeks, we should be able to figure out its nature.”
- Professor Avi Loeb
“The physics behind the anti-tail of 3I/ATLAS is unclear.”
- Professor Avi Loeb

Context

The nature of 3I/ATLAS has puzzled scientists since its discovery. While some researchers classify it as a comet, others, including Professor Loeb, propose that it might be an alien "mothership." This debate highlights the broader interest in interstellar objects and their potential implications for understanding our universe.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches, scientists will collect and analyze data to determine its characteristics. The findings will be crucial in clarifying the object's origins and assessing any potential threats to Earth.

The Pentagon has officially released 3 videos showing UFOs

The Pentagon has released three videos on Monday showing U.S. Navy pilots spotting unidentified flying objects, also known as UFOs. The videos, which were first published by the New York Times in 2017, depict objects moving at high speeds. The Department of Defense describes these incidents as "unexplained air phenomena." No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the sightings.

The release of the videos aims to "clear up any misconceptions circulating and clarify whether the videos were true or not," according to a statement from the Department of Defense. The statement also noted that the videos had circulated unauthorized and confirmed that the aerial phenomena observed in the footage remain unidentified.

Key facts

  • Videos released on October 9, 2023
  • First published by the New York Times in 2017
  • One video shows a 40-foot-long object encountered by Marine pilots in 2004
  • Two additional videos date back to 2015

Exact moment 'strange light' spotted flying across Irish sky - but not all as it seems as UFO-like 'big object' revealed

Published 2025-10-29 22:27 UTC

UFO-like Object Spotted Across Ireland

Thousands of stargazers across Ireland have reported seeing a UFO-like object flying in the sky on the evening of October 29, 2023. The bright light was observed moving slowly at or below cloud level from multiple locations, including Wexford, Donegal, Cork, and Carlow.

Authorities are investigating the sightings, with meteorologists confirming that the object remains unidentified for now. Alan O’Reilly from Carlow Weather noted that the object has not been linked to any known satellites or space activity, thus classifying it as a UFO until further information is available.

Key facts

  • Event Date: October 29, 2023
  • Locations: Wexford, Donegal, Cork, Carlow
  • Witness Reports: Multiple sightings from across Ireland
  • Expert Analysis: Object identified as possibly related to Falcon 9 fuel dumping

Timeline

On the evening of October 29, 2023, multiple reports emerged of a bright light in the sky across various regions in Ireland. Eyewitnesses shared videos and descriptions of the object, which appeared to move silently and slowly. Carlow Weather began investigating the claims shortly after, prompting widespread interest on social media.

By later that evening, Alan O’Reilly indicated on social media that the object was likely not related to the StarLink launch and noted that it did not appear on flight radars. The object was still classified as a UFO due to its unidentified nature.

In a follow-up post, O’Reilly suggested that the light might be the Falcon 9 rocket's fuel dumping, clarifying that it was not an alien craft.

Official statements

“Multiple reports of strange light moving in sky over Ireland in last 15 minutes... moving very quickly.” — Alan O’Reilly, Carlow Weather
“So I don’t know what that light in the sky was... Load of people captured videos and pictures of it.” — Alan O’Reilly, Carlow Weather
“It looks like it’s massive, there was no noise.” — Eyewitness from Athlone

Context

UFO sightings often provoke public intrigue, with many associating them with extraterrestrial life and conspiracy theories. Historically, various objects have been reported as UFOs, only to be later identified as satellites or other space-related phenomena. The recent surge of interest in unidentified aerial phenomena has led to increased scrutiny and analysis by meteorologists and aviation experts.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to monitor reports and investigate the sightings. Further analysis may clarify the object's nature as more information becomes available. Public interest in UFO sightings remains high, prompting ongoing discussions and investigations into unexplained aerial phenomena.

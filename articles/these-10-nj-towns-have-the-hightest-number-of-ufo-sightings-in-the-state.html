New Jersey Towns Report Highest Number of UFO Sightings

Ten towns in New Jersey have reported the highest number of UFO sightings, according to a recent analysis. The findings highlight the ongoing interest in unidentified flying objects in the state, particularly along the Jersey Shore.

This data matters as it reflects New Jersey's long-standing connection to paranormal phenomena, including UFO sightings. Local authorities and researchers continue to investigate these reports, seeking to understand the reasons behind the frequent sightings.

Key facts

  • Ten towns in New Jersey identified for high UFO sightings.
  • Toms River has reported over 40 possible UFO sightings.
  • Reports date back to the winter of 2025, when drone activity caused concern.
  • Training missions were later confirmed to be the cause of many sightings.
  • Jersey Shore towns are particularly noted for these occurrences.

Timeline

In the winter of 2025, residents across New Jersey reported numerous UFO sightings, which turned out to be linked to drone training missions. As the sightings gained attention, local communities became more engaged with the phenomenon.

Over the years, towns along the Jersey Shore, including Toms River, have consistently reported high numbers of UFO sightings, contributing to the state's reputation for paranormal activity.

Official statements

“Jersey is a state that's steeped in paranormal phenomena, so the fact that we have cities and towns that have claimed to see a crazy amount of UFOs over the years isn't a huge surprise,” said a local expert.

Context

New Jersey has a history of reported UFO sightings, with various towns experiencing spikes in activity over the years. The interest in these incidents has led to increased public curiosity and research into the phenomenon.

In addition to UFO reports, New Jersey is known for other paranormal tales, such as those involving the Jersey Devil and ghost sightings in towns like Asbury Park and Cape May.

What’s next

Investigations into the recent sightings will continue as local authorities monitor reports. Community interest remains high, and researchers are expected to delve deeper into the phenomenon in the coming months.

Harvard Scientist Raises Technological Thruster Theory as Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Defies Natural Comet Behavior

On November 15, 2025, astronomer Teerasak Thaluang captured an image of interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which exhibits an unusual anti-tail in addition to its traditional cometary tails. The observation, made using a 0.26-meter telescope in Thailand, has sparked discussion among scientists due to its deviation from known comet behavior. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This observation challenges existing models of cometary physics and raises questions about the object's origins. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has proposed that the anti-tail may be a result of technological thrusters, which would indicate artificial propulsion. The ongoing lack of transparency from NASA regarding critical imagery of 3I/ATLAS has drawn criticism from the scientific community and lawmakers alike.

Key facts

  • Date of observation: November 15, 2025
  • Location of observation: Thailand
  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • NASA's status: Withholding images of the object
  • Key figure: Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist
  • Public figure involved: Representative Anna Paulina Luna

Timeline

On November 15, 2025, Teerasak Thaluang captured an image of 3I/ATLAS, revealing its anti-tail. The scientific community has since debated the implications of this observation, with some suggesting it may indicate artificial propulsion. NASA has withheld images of the object taken during a critical phase of its approach, prompting public calls for transparency.

As 3I/ATLAS approaches Earth, worldwide observatories will continue to gather data, refining our understanding of the object. Upcoming spectroscopic observations may provide insights into the nature of the anti-tail.

Official statements

“This moment demands transparency from agencies that have chosen silence instead.” — Samuel Lopez, USA Herald
“The images exist. The data exists. The time for secrecy has passed.” — Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist

Context

This is not the first time Avi Loeb has suggested that an interstellar object might be artificial. His work on ‘Oumuamua, the first confirmed interstellar visitor in 2017, ignited debate regarding the nature of such objects. 3I/ATLAS adds to the ongoing conversation about potential extraterrestrial technology and the need for transparency in scientific inquiry.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS approaches its closest point to Earth, scientists will continue to analyze data and await the release of withheld imagery from NASA. The outcome of this investigation could reshape our understanding of interstellar objects and their origins.

Alien-Like Object Sparks Panic in Moscow — Is It Connected With 3I/ATLAS?

On Monday, 27 October 2025, residents of Moscow witnessed a giant fireball soaring across the sky just before dawn, causing panic among commuters who stopped to stare. The bright green blaze lit up buildings in the city and nearby suburbs, including Vidnoye and Ramenskoye. There were no reports of damage or injuries at the moment.

The event has raised questions among scientists about a potential link to interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which is making its closest approach to the Sun around 29 October 2025. Experts continue to investigate the nature of the fireball, which was captured on videos showing a glowing object leaving a trail of sparks as it streaked overhead.

Key facts

  • Event occurred on 27 October 2025, just before dawn.
  • Location: Moscow and surrounding suburbs, including Vidnoye and Ramenskoye.
  • Witnesses described a bright green object breaking apart in the sky.
  • No reports of injuries or damage have been recorded.
  • 3I/ATLAS is expected to reach perihelion on 29 October 2025.

Timeline

Residents first reported seeing the fireball just before dawn on 27 October 2025. Videos circulated on social media captured the object lighting up the sky and trailing luminous sparks. As scientists monitored 3I/ATLAS, speculation arose about a possible connection between the comet's activity and the fireball sighting.

Experts noted that the brightness and breakdown pattern of the fireball are consistent with a meteoroid entering Earth's atmosphere. Initial online speculation suggested it might be a UFO or alien ship, but local scientists have leaned towards more natural explanations.

Official statements

“The disintegration pattern and spark-trail are consistent with atmospheric entry by a meteoroid,” said a local geologist.

Context

This event coincides with the increased activity of 3I/ATLAS, which has attracted global scientific interest. While some online theories suggested a connection between the fireball and the comet, astronomers have ruled out that possibility, stating that 3I/ATLAS is not on a trajectory that intersects Earth's orbit.

What’s next

Experts will continue to analyze the fireball's trajectory, but they currently consider it separate from the interstellar comet. Investigations into the nature of the fireball are ongoing, with scientists monitoring meteor sightings closely.

Analysis Of Material Coming Off Of The Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Gives Researchers A Few Surprises

Researchers have analyzed material from the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which reached its perihelion on October 29, 2025. The comet, traveling through the universe for billions of years, became the focus of extensive study as it approached the sun, about 36% farther away than Earth at its closest point.

This analysis is significant because 3I/ATLAS is only the third object identified from interstellar space. Scientists have utilized NASA’s SPHEREx instruments to gather data on the comet's ice and gas composition, revealing unexpected characteristics. The findings have been published on the ArXiv platform, although they have not yet undergone peer review.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS entered the solar system and was first spotted in 2025.
  • It reached its perihelion on October 29, 2025.
  • At perihelion, the comet was approximately 36% farther from the sun than Earth.
  • The study utilized NASA’s SPHEREx, which captures 102 colors of the infrared spectrum.
  • The analysis identified various molecules, including carbon dioxide and water, in the comet's ice.
  • The study noted an unusual 'anti-tail' formation from the comet's dust.
  • Further data collection is planned as the comet exits the solar system in April 2026.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has traveled through the universe for billions of years before entering the solar system in 2025. It reached its perihelion on October 29, 2025, where researchers began collecting data about its composition. The findings were published on the ArXiv platform, with ongoing analysis expected to continue for years.

Official statements

“The spectrophotometric measurements reported here are from a preliminary analysis. A more full-up treatment will be produced before 3I/ATLAS passes through SPHEREx’s planned survey pattern again in April 2026,” researchers stated in the study.

Context

This analysis follows previous studies of interstellar objects, which have provided valuable insights into the composition of materials outside our solar system. The discoveries regarding 3I/ATLAS add to the growing understanding of such celestial bodies and their behaviors as they interact with solar systems.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from 3I/ATLAS as it moves away from the sun. Additional findings are expected in April 2026 when SPHEREx will capture more measurements of the comet.

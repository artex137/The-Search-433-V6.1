New UFO video raises questions as Congress pushes for transparency

A new video has surfaced during a House Government Oversight subcommittee hearing on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) in Washington, D.C., on September 10, 2025. The video has sparked renewed interest in the U.S. military's investigations into UFOs, although no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This development is significant as Congress intensifies its efforts to demand transparency from the government regarding UAP. Lawmakers are pushing for more information about the military's encounters with unidentified objects, which have long been shrouded in secrecy.

Key facts

  • Date: September 10, 2025
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Event: House Government Oversight subcommittee hearing
  • Subject: Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP)
  • No injuries or fatalities reported

Timeline

During the subcommittee hearing held on September 10, 2025, a video featuring UAP was presented to lawmakers. This video is part of ongoing discussions about the military's investigation into UFOs, which have captured public and government interest.

The hearing is part of a broader initiative by Congress to ensure that the military provides more information on its encounters with unidentified aerial phenomena. Lawmakers have expressed concerns over the lack of transparency in these investigations.

Official statements

“The American people deserve to know what their government knows about unidentified aerial phenomena,” a committee member stated during the hearing.

Context

This hearing follows a series of reports and testimonies regarding UAP, which have gained traction in recent years. The military's acknowledgment of these phenomena has prompted Congress to seek greater insight into their nature and origin.

Previous incidents involving UAP have raised questions about national security and the need for a clear policy regarding the investigation of such phenomena.

What’s next

Congress is expected to continue its push for transparency, with additional hearings and requests for information from military agencies anticipated in the coming months.

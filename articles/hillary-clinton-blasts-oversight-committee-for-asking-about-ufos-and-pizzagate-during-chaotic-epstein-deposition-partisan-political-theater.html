Hillary Clinton Blasts Oversight Committee For Asking About 'UFOs and Pizzagate' During Chaotic Epstein Deposition

Hillary Clinton has criticized the oversight committee for questioning her about "UFOs and Pizzagate" during her deposition regarding Jeffrey Epstein in Chappaqua, New York. Clinton described the inquiries as "partisan political theater" and noted that the questioning became "quite unusual" toward the end of her deposition.

This incident highlights ongoing tensions between political figures and oversight committees. Clinton expressed her concerns about the focus of the questions, emphasizing the need for serious investigation into the Epstein case rather than conspiracy theories. The former first lady stated she answered every question based on her knowledge but found the interrogation repetitive and unproductive.

Key facts

  • Clinton's deposition occurred in Chappaqua, New York.
  • She described questions about UFOs and Pizzagate as outlandish.
  • Clinton stated she has never met Jeffrey Epstein, but knew Ghislaine Maxwell "casually."
  • Bill Clinton is scheduled to testify following Hillary's deposition.
  • Lauren Boebert leaked a photo from the deposition room, which was against the rules.
  • Republican Rep. Nancy Mace indicated she is prepared for Bill Clinton's deposition.

Timeline

Hillary Clinton's deposition took place recently, during which she faced questions related to the Epstein investigation. Toward the end of the session, she was asked about UFOs and Pizzagate, which she found inappropriate. Following her deposition, Clinton spoke to reporters and voiced her concerns about the nature of the questions posed by the committee.

Clinton noted that she had initially resisted being deposed but complied after being threatened with contempt of Congress. Her husband, Bill Clinton, is set to sit for his deposition shortly after hers.

Official statements

“I started being asked about UFOs and a series of questions about Pizzagate – one of the most vile bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet.”
“I thought that they literally asked the same questions over and over again, which didn't seem to me to be very productive.”
“I want to see the truth come out. So that was a reassuring way to end a very long, repetitive deposition.”

Context

This deposition comes amid ongoing investigations into Jeffrey Epstein and allegations of misconduct involving high-profile individuals. The focus on conspiracy theories during official testimonies has drawn criticism from those who advocate for serious inquiries into the matter.

Previous incidents involving high-profile political figures have raised questions about the appropriateness of certain lines of questioning in congressional hearings.

What’s next

Bill Clinton's deposition is scheduled to take place soon, with expectations that it will address points raised during Hillary's questioning. The oversight committee continues its investigation into Epstein's activities and the connections of those involved.

Comet C/2025 K1 ATLAS fragmentation: a new image – 2 Dec. 2025.

Published 2025-12-03 08:34 UTC

Comet C/2025 K1 ATLAS fragmentation observed on December 2, 2025

On December 2, 2025, astronomers captured new images of Comet C/2025 K1 ATLAS, revealing further fragmentation of the comet. The images were taken remotely from Manciano, Italy, using the Virtual Telescope Project’s robotic unit.

The observations are significant as they help scientists understand the comet's behavior and structure. Authorities from the Virtual Telescope Project have confirmed that they will continue monitoring the comet for any additional changes.

Key facts

  • Date: December 2, 2025
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Method: 27, 60-second unfiltered exposures
  • Equipment: Celestron C14+Paramount ME+SBIG ST-10XME robotic unit
  • Conditions: Bright Moon in the sky

Timeline

On November 29, 2025, astronomers first noted signs of fragmentation in Comet C/2025 K1 ATLAS. The images taken on December 2 show smaller, fainter pieces compared to the previous observations.

The northernmost fragment appears elongated, suggesting it may have a double structure or a small tail.

Official statements

“We will continue monitoring this comet,” said Gianluca Masi of the Virtual Telescope Project.

Context

Comet C/2025 K1 ATLAS is part of ongoing studies regarding comet fragmentation and behavior. Previous observations have shown similar patterns in other comets, highlighting the dynamic nature of these celestial bodies.

What’s next

The Virtual Telescope Project plans to keep observing Comet C/2025 K1 ATLAS to track any further changes and gather more data for analysis.

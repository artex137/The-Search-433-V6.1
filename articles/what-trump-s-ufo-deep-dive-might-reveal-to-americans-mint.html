What Trump’s UFO deep dive might reveal to Americans | Mint

Published 2026-02-23 01:17 UTC

Trump directs investigation into UFO files

President Donald Trump has ordered the Department of Defense to begin releasing government files related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and extraterrestrial life. The directive, announced this past week, follows increasing public interest in UFO sightings and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

This investigation matters as it could shed light on decades of speculation about extraterrestrial visits and government knowledge. The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO) has been tasked with examining UFO reports, and its findings may influence future policy and public perception regarding unidentified phenomena.

Key facts

  • Trump's directive was announced in early October 2024.
  • The AARO was established by Congress in 2022 to investigate UFO sightings.
  • Previous investigations revealed no evidence of secret alien programs.
  • Military officials have often attributed UFO sightings to optical illusions or misidentified objects.
  • Trump's order follows comments made by former President Barack Obama regarding the existence of extraterrestrial life.

Timeline

In the 1990s, the CIA investigated UFO sightings, attributing many to classified spy planes. In 2022, Congress established the AARO to explore potential government knowledge of extraterrestrial activity. Military officials have consistently dismissed many UFO sightings as misidentified aircraft or natural phenomena.

On a recent podcast, Obama stated that while extraterrestrial life is likely, he has not seen evidence of them visiting Earth. Following this, Trump announced his intention to declassify UFO-related files, claiming Obama revealed classified secrets.

Official statements

“We do have some very anomalous objects,” said Jon Kosloski, director of the AARO, while discussing the investigations into UFO sightings. “We haven’t yet discovered verifiable evidence of extraterrestrial beings, activity or technology.”
Sean Kirkpatrick, former director of the AARO, commented, “People have built up this story in their minds... and there isn’t” a stockpile of documents detailing alien encounters.

Context

The fascination with UFOs in popular culture can be traced back to a 1947 incident in Roswell, New Mexico, where some claimed to witness alien debris. Investigations over the decades have frequently been met with skepticism from the UFO community, often citing perceived government deception regarding the nature of these sightings.

Previous inquiries, including the CIA's 1990s study, have failed to satisfy those seeking transparency, fueling conspiracy theories about government cover-ups of alien technology.

What’s next

The Pentagon will define the parameters of the file review, which could encompass a wide range of sightings and claims. The outcome of this investigation may determine future transparency regarding UFOs and influence public opinion on extraterrestrial life.

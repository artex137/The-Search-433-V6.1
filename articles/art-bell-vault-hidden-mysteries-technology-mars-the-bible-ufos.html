Art Bell Vault Offers Curated Collection of Classic UFO Programs

The Art Bell Vault has launched a collection of more than 500 programs dedicated to UFOs and other mysteries, currently available to Coast Insiders. The collection features two new vintage shows added each Wednesday, with the latest episodes focusing on ancient technology and extraterrestrial connections.

This initiative matters as it provides access to historical discussions and theories surrounding UFOs and ancient civilizations. According to the announcement, the programs are designed to stimulate interest and encourage exploration of these topics among listeners.

Key facts

  • Collection includes over 500 programs.
  • New episodes released every Wednesday.
  • Recent episodes feature discussions from February 27 and March 3, 1998.
  • Programs are available on multiple platforms, including Mac, Windows, iOS, and Android.
  • Coast Insiders receive this content at no additional charge.

Timeline

On February 27, 1998, Art Bell hosted author and scientist Gregg Braden. Braden discussed ancient anomalies and technology that he claimed were being concealed from the public, suggesting that this information could significantly impact human evolution.

On March 3, 1998, Art Bell spoke with author Cynthia Turnage about the connection between Mars, the Bible, and UFOs. Turnage proposed that ancient structures exist on Mars, hinting at ties to Earth’s early civilizations.

Official statements

“This 'forbidden' information would transform civilization and ensure our survival as a species,” said Gregg Braden during his discussion on February 27, 1998.
“The 'war in heaven' described in the Bible was an account of a genuine conflict between ET races,” asserted Cynthia Turnage during her appearance on March 3, 1998.

Context

The Art Bell Vault preserves the legacy of discussions that have fascinated audiences for decades. Prior episodes often delve into controversial theories regarding UFOs and ancient technologies, reflecting a longstanding interest in these topics.

Listeners have shown a consistent appetite for content that challenges conventional understanding of history and science, making this vault a timely resource for enthusiasts.

What’s next

The Art Bell Vault will continue to expand its collection, adding new vintage programs weekly, and aims to enhance the listening experience for Coast Insiders with various content options.

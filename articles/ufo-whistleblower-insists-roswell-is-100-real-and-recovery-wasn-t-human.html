UFO whistleblower insists Roswell is '100% real' and recovery 'wasn't human'

A UFO whistleblower has claimed that the Roswell crash of 1947 was a genuine extraterrestrial event, stating that the recovered craft was "not of human origin." Astrophysicist Eric Davis, a former scientific advisor to the Pentagon's Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program, made these assertions during an appearance on the American Alchemy podcast.

This claim is significant as it challenges the long-standing narrative maintained by the U.S. government, which has insisted that the debris found at the Foster Ranch in Corona, New Mexico, was merely a downed weather balloon. Authorities are now faced with renewed scrutiny regarding their previous statements about the incident.

Key facts

  • Incident: Roswell crash
  • Date: July 1947
  • Location: Foster Ranch, Corona, New Mexico
  • Claimant: Eric Davis, former scientific advisor to the Pentagon
  • Key figures mentioned: Ed Mitchell, Thomas Wilson
  • Type of craft: Claimed to be of unknown origin

Timeline

The Roswell saga began in July 1947 when Major Jesse Marcel, an intelligence officer for the Roswell Army Air Field, was dispatched to a ranch covered with metallic debris. The U.S. Army initially announced that they had captured a flying saucer, but this statement was retracted within 24 hours. Marcel later appeared with remnants of what was identified as a weather balloon, leading to theories of a cover-up.

Davis has asserted that the crash involved a real craft, which he claims is supported by information from notable figures in the aerospace field, including late Apollo astronaut Ed Mitchell and retired Navy Admiral Thomas Wilson. He emphasized that the evidence is classified for security reasons.

Official statements

"It's 100 per cent. I had my information that I got from Ed Mitchell at a science advisory board meeting on the disclosure project at the Pentagon," said Eric Davis. He added, "It wasn't a mobile balloon, it wasn't a raw wind radar test balloon project, it wasn't a weather balloon, it wasn't anything of that nature."
"The incontrovertible evidence is kept in the classified realm for security reasons. Do you dispute the existence of atomic weapons because you can't access them?" Davis stated in response to questions about the lack of physical evidence.

Context

Over the decades, skepticism has surrounded the Roswell incident, with critics pointing to a lack of physical evidence as proof of a hoax or military blunder. The secrecy surrounding the case has led to numerous conspiracy theories, including claims that an alien survivor was taken into custody.

Davis's recent comments have reignited interest in the Roswell crash, as many continue to believe that the original disc was moved to a secret facility after the incident.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any plans for further investigations into the claims made by Davis. The ongoing interest in the Roswell incident may prompt renewed inquiries into the U.S. government's handling of UFO-related information.

ISRO releases striking new image of Comet 3I/ATLAS from Mount Abu telescope

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has released a new image of Comet 3I/ATLAS taken from its telescope in Mount Abu. The image was shared on October 15, 2023, showcasing the comet's distinctive features as it travels through space.

This release is significant as it provides valuable data for astronomers studying the comet's trajectory and characteristics. ISRO continues to enhance its observational capabilities, contributing to global efforts in space research.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed from Mount Abu
  • Image released on October 15, 2023
  • ISRO's telescope utilized for observation
  • Comet currently on a trajectory through the solar system

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019. Since then, it has been monitored by various observatories worldwide. ISRO's telescope in Mount Abu has played a crucial role in capturing detailed images of the comet.

On October 15, 2023, ISRO shared the latest image, highlighting the comet's features and providing data for ongoing research.

Official statements

“The image demonstrates ISRO's commitment to advancing our understanding of comets and other celestial bodies,” an ISRO spokesperson said.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in comets and their potential impacts on Earth. Past observations have provided insights into the composition of comets and their role in the solar system.

ISRO has been actively involved in astronomical observations, enhancing India's position in global space research.

What’s next

ISRO plans to continue monitoring Comet 3I/ATLAS and will release further images and data as it becomes available, contributing to the ongoing study of the comet's characteristics.

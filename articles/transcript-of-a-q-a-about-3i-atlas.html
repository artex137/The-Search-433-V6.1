Transcript of a Q&A About 3I/ATLAS

A live Q&A session about the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS took place on November 21, 2025, featuring Avi Loeb as the main speaker. The event was hosted by FOX 32 Chicago and received numerous viewer questions.

The discussion focused on the recent NASA images of 3I/ATLAS, which have garnered mixed reactions from the public. Loeb criticized NASA for not addressing the mysteries surrounding the object, instead opting to downplay its significance. He emphasized the need for better data and more engagement from scientists in public communications.

Key facts

  • Date of Q&A: November 21, 2025
  • Location: FOX 32 Chicago
  • Speaker: Avi Loeb
  • Focus: NASA images of 3I/ATLAS
  • Public reaction: Mixed, with disappointment in image quality

Timeline

On October 2, 2025, the HiRISE camera on the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter captured the best images of 3I/ATLAS. However, a press conference held by NASA to discuss these images was criticized for not providing significant new information.

During the Q&A, Loeb pointed out that the images were blurred and lacked clarity, which led to public disappointment. He argued that the press conference did not adequately communicate the scientific mysteries surrounding 3I/ATLAS.

Official statements

“When you tell the public that there is an interesting object, you want to focus on the mysteries. Why does this object merit attention? Instead, they focused on what we think we already know about it.” - Avi Loeb
“You cannot find technological signatures unless you are seeking them. If your mindset is that it’s a rock, you are not really searching for any signatures.” - Avi Loeb

Context

The discussion around 3I/ATLAS has sparked interest in the scientific community and public alike, particularly regarding the potential for technological signatures. Previous discussions about interstellar objects have often highlighted the importance of transparency and thorough investigation.

Loeb's emphasis on the need for better public communication reflects ongoing concerns about how scientific data is presented to the public.

What’s next

Loeb and other researchers are expected to continue analyzing data from 3I/ATLAS. Further investigations may focus on understanding the anomalies observed in the object's behaviour and the potential for future observations.

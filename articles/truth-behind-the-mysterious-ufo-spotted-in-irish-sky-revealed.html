Truth behind the mysterious 'UFO' spotted in Irish sky revealed

A bright, fast-moving object described as a "strange light" has been spotted in the sky across Ireland on the evening of October 24, 2023. Reports of the sighting came in from several counties, including Cork, Dublin, Tipperary, and Galway, around 6:30 p.m. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This sighting has drawn significant public interest and prompted officials to investigate the phenomenon. Alan O'Reilly of Carlow Weather noted a surge in notifications after 6 p.m. from individuals describing the object, which was said to have light in front but made no noise while moving quickly.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on October 24, 2023, around 6:30 p.m.
  • Sightings reported in Cork, Dublin, Tipperary, and Galway.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.
  • Alan O'Reilly of Carlow Weather received numerous messages regarding the event.

Timeline

At approximately 6 p.m., reports began to flood in to Alan O'Reilly about a bright light in the sky. The sightings continued until around 6:30 p.m., with witnesses describing the object as large and fast-moving.

O'Reilly shared the information with his followers, stating that he had never received so many notifications in such a short period. He confirmed that the reports spanned from the south through the midlands and into the west and east of the country.

Official statements

“So I don’t know what that light in the sky was that I’ve shared. My notifications have never lit up like they have just now,” said Alan O'Reilly.

Context

This incident follows a series of similar sightings reported in various parts of the world over recent years. The phenomenon of unexplained aerial objects has gained attention, prompting discussions about the need for further investigation and understanding of these occurrences.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any specific plans for an investigation into the sighting, but public interest may lead to further inquiries into the nature of the object observed in the Irish sky.

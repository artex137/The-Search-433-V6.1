Saturday TV: When UFOs Did D.C. Destruction

Published 2026-01-03 05:29 UTC

When UFOs Did D.C. Destruction

Tonight, Turner Classic Movies presents a triple feature of science fiction films that explore the theme of UFOs and destruction, starting at 8 p.m. with “When Worlds Collide.” The films include “Earth vs. the Flying Saucers” at 9:30 p.m. and “Attack of the 50 Foot Woman” at 11 p.m. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these screenings.

The significance of this programming highlights the cultural impact of UFO-related narratives in cinema, particularly during the 1950s. Authorities are not actively involved, as this is a scheduled television event rather than a public safety concern.

Key facts

  • Turner Classic Movies features three films:
    • “When Worlds Collide” at 8 p.m.
    • “Earth vs. the Flying Saucers” at 9:30 p.m.
    • “Attack of the 50 Foot Woman” at 11 p.m.
  • Other notable programming includes “Lost in Paradise” (Hallmark, 8 p.m.) and “Stolen Girl” (Lifetime, 8 p.m.).
  • Sports events include NFL games:
    • Carolina at Tampa Bay (ABC, 4:30 p.m.)
    • Seattle at San Francisco (ABC, 8:15 p.m.)
  • Multiple college basketball games scheduled throughout the day.

Timeline

Tonight's events begin with the first film airing at 8 p.m. on Turner Classic Movies. The NFL games will kick off with Carolina at Tampa Bay at 4:30 p.m., followed by Seattle at San Francisco at 8:15 p.m.

In addition to the films and sports, various college basketball games are set to take place throughout the day, starting from 11 a.m. and continuing into the evening.

Official statements

“Tonight’s triple feature on Turner Classic Movies will take audiences back to a time when UFOs ruled the cinematic landscape,” said a representative from Turner Classic Movies.

Context

The focus on UFOs in 1950s cinema reflects broader societal anxieties during the Cold War era. These films often depicted extraterrestrial threats and the potential for destruction, mirroring public fears of the unknown.

UFO narratives have remained a popular theme in film and television, often serving as allegories for contemporary issues.

What’s next

Viewers can expect similar programming in the future, as Turner Classic Movies continues to celebrate classic films. Upcoming sports events will also be featured on various networks.

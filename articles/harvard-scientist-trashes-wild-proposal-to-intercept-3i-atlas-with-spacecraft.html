Harvard scientist trashes wild proposal to intercept 3I/ATLAS with spacecraft

Harvard scientist Avi Loeb has criticized a proposal to intercept the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS with a spacecraft, stating that resources should not be allocated to such a venture. Loeb made his remarks in an interview published on February 27, 2026, where he argued that the focus should shift to more viable scientific pursuits.

This discussion arises amid plans by researchers, including former NASA staffer Marshall Eubanks, to launch a spacecraft by 2035 aimed at reaching ATLAS by 2085. The mission, which involves utilizing the Oberth effect for acceleration, has been deemed ambitious and potentially impractical by experts like Loeb.

Key facts

  • Proposal to intercept 3I/ATLAS has been criticized by Avi Loeb.
  • The mission would require a launch in 2035 and aims for arrival by 2085.
  • Interception would utilize the Oberth effect, which boosts speed using the Sun's gravity.
  • Loeb estimates up to 35 million interstellar objects exist within Earth's orbit.
  • European Space Agency plans to debut its Comet Interceptor mission by 2028.

Timeline

In February 2026, Avi Loeb publicly denounced the plan to intercept 3I/ATLAS during an interview. He highlighted the long timeframe of the mission, suggesting that many people would not be alive when the probe potentially reaches its target. He also noted that by that time, hundreds of interstellar objects could be discovered.

In 2023, NASA's Parker Solar Probe successfully came within 3.7 million miles of the Sun, demonstrating the challenges and requirements for such close solar flybys.

The European Space Agency has announced its intention to launch the Comet Interceptor mission by 2028, which aims to be ready to explore uncharted comets.

Official statements

“Is that what we need to invest our resources in?” Loeb asked, comparing the pursuit to an unhealthy romantic obsession.
“Every launch uses the Oberth effect,” Eubanks stated, acknowledging the challenge of their proposed mission variation.
“This timescale sounds very long,” Loeb remarked, emphasizing the need to prioritize other interstellar objects.
“Maybe, after, say ten interstellar objects have been found, 3I will seem commonplace,” Eubanks added, suggesting a shift in focus.

Context

Previous discussions about interstellar objects have gained traction since the discovery of 3I/ATLAS. Loeb's criticism highlights a growing debate within the scientific community regarding the allocation of resources for space exploration. The advent of powerful telescopes, such as the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, promises to enhance our ability to detect and analyze interstellar objects.

What’s next

As discussions continue, researchers will likely evaluate the feasibility of the proposed interception mission. The European Space Agency's Comet Interceptor mission may provide new insights and opportunities for exploring interstellar objects in the coming years.

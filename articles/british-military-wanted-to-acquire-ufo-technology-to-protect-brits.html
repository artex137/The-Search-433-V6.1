British military 'wanted to acquire UFO technology to protect Brits'

The British military has expressed interest in acquiring technology linked to UFO sightings, according to previously classified files from the Ministry of Defence. These documents, now available in the National Archives in Kew, reveal that the Defence Intelligence Staff (DIS) viewed unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) as a potential threat to the UK during the 1990s.

This matter has gained attention as it raises questions about national security and the implications of unidentified aerial objects. The files indicate that the military was concerned about reports of large, silent, low-flying black triangles, particularly those documented in Belgium, and sought to understand and possibly acquire the technology behind them.

Key facts

  • Confidential memos from the Defence Intelligence Staff (DIS) date back to the 1990s.
  • UAPs were identified as a potential threat to the UK.
  • Reports from Belgium between November 1989 and April 1990 prompted military interest.
  • Rendlesham Forest incident involved sightings by US Air Force personnel in December 1980.
  • Documents describe UAPs as having capabilities beyond conventional propulsion.

Timeline

In December 1980, US Air Force members stationed at RAF Bentwaters and RAF Woodbridge in Suffolk reported seeing unexplained lights and a triangular craft in Rendlesham Forest. Witnesses, including high-ranking officers, confirmed the sighting.

Between November 1989 and April 1990, numerous reports emerged from Belgium regarding low-flying black triangles, which further alarmed British intelligence officers. The DIS began considering the implications of these sightings for national defence.

In March 1997, an internal correspondence stated, "Logic would indicate that if significant numbers are reporting seeing strange objects in the sky then there may be a basis in fact." This reinforced the military's interest in exploring the technology behind UAPs.

Official statements

“It could be argued that UAPs pose a potential threat to the defence of the realm since we have no idea what they are!”
“A supplementary issue is the possibility of technology acquisition. UAPs do not appear to use conventional reaction propulsion.”

Context

Prior incidents involving UFO sightings, particularly the Rendlesham Forest case, have led to ongoing speculation and conspiracy theories about extraterrestrial life and secret military experiments. The British military has previously dismissed claims of alien encounters as fringe conspiracy theories, while acknowledging some reports of UAPs remain unexplained.

What’s next

The British military has not disclosed any current investigations or planned actions regarding the acquisition of technology related to UFO sightings. Further analysis of the documents may lead to new insights into national security and defence strategies.

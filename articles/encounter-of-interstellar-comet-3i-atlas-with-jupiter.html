Encounter of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS with Jupiter

On March 16, 2026, interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will fly past Jupiter at a distance of 53.6 million kilometres. The comet, which is on a trajectory to leave the Solar System, will continue its journey through the Milky Way after the encounter.

This flyby is significant for astronomers studying the characteristics of interstellar objects. Researchers have been monitoring 3I/ATLAS since its discovery to gather data on its composition and behaviour as it interacts with the Solar System's gravitational forces.

Key facts

  • Date of encounter: March 16, 2026
  • Distance from Jupiter: 53.6 million km
  • Type of object: Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Journey: Continuing to leave the Solar System

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been under observation since its discovery. The comet's trajectory has been calculated to ensure accurate predictions of its flyby with Jupiter on March 16, 2026. This event marks a key moment in the study of interstellar objects.

Official statements

“The encounter with Jupiter provides a unique opportunity to study the comet's properties and its interaction with the Solar System,” said a spokesperson from the International Astronomical Union.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the few interstellar comets to be observed in detail. Its approach to the Solar System has sparked interest in the scientific community, as it offers insights into the composition of objects from outside our solar neighbourhood. Previous encounters with interstellar objects have provided valuable data, helping to expand our understanding of the universe.

What’s next

Following the encounter, scientists will analyse data collected during the flyby to better understand the comet's characteristics and origin. Further studies may follow as researchers continue to investigate the implications of this encounter.

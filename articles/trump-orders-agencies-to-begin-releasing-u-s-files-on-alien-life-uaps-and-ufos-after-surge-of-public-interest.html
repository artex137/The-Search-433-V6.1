Trump Orders Agencies to Begin Releasing U.S. Files on “Alien Life,” UAPs and UFOs After Surge of Public Interest

President Donald Trump has directed the Secretary of War and other federal departments to identify and release government files related to “alien and extraterrestrial life,” unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs). The announcement was made in a statement on Truth Social on an unspecified date, citing “tremendous interest” in the subject.

This initiative reflects a growing public curiosity and political pressure for transparency on UAPs and UFOs. The release of these files aims to address national attention on the topic, which has been marked by years of speculation and formal government review.

Key facts

  • Trump has ordered the release of files related to alien life and UFOs.
  • The directive includes all information deemed relevant by federal agencies.
  • No specific timetable or list of files to be released has been disclosed.
  • The announcement comes amid renewed national interest in UAPs.
  • Public curiosity about UFOs has persisted for years, alongside political pressure for transparency.

Timeline

President Trump made his announcement via Truth Social, highlighting the growing interest in UAPs and UFOs.

The directive indicates a shift in the administration's approach to releasing information on these topics, although details regarding specific files or a timeline remain undisclosed.

Official statements

“This effort will cover any and all other information connected to what I describe as highly complex, but extremely interesting and important matters,” Trump stated in his announcement.

Context

The announcement comes during a period of heightened attention on unidentified aerial phenomena, which have garnered interest from both the public and Congress. Previous efforts to increase transparency regarding UFOs have included formal reviews and reports from government agencies.

What’s next

Federal agencies are expected to begin identifying relevant files for release, although no specific deadlines have been provided for this process.

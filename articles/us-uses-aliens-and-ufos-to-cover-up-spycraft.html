US uses aliens and UFOs ‘to cover up spycraft’

Published 2026-02-24 14:41 UTC

US Air Force Accused of Using UFOs to Distract from Spycraft

The US Air Force has faced accusations of sharing disinformation regarding extraterrestrial activity to divert public attention from its clandestine operations, according to Dr. David Clarke, a lecturer at Sheffield Hallam University. Dr. Clarke has spent five years researching official documents on UFOs and has found no evidence to support claims of alien visitation.

This allegation highlights concerns about government transparency and the potential for misinformation. Dr. Clarke believes the Air Force has leveraged public interest in aliens to conceal its development of advanced military technology. The issue gained renewed attention after former President Donald Trump ordered the Pentagon to release UFO-related files, citing significant public interest.

Key facts

  • Dr. David Clarke has researched UFO documentation for five years.
  • Trump ordered the release of UFO files last week.
  • Barack Obama previously stated that aliens are "real" but not in Area 51.
  • Dr. Clarke curated the National Archives' UFO project.
  • A Wall Street Journal article last year claimed the Pentagon promoted UFO myths.

Timeline

In recent months, public interest in UFOs has surged following statements from former President Donald Trump and Barack Obama. Trump’s directive for the Pentagon to release UFO files occurred last week. Obama’s comments about aliens were made earlier in the year, which he later clarified, stating he found no evidence of extraterrestrial contact during his presidency.

Dr. Clarke, who has advocated for the release of UFO documents, has previously utilized freedom-of-information legislation in the UK to obtain files. His findings have not supported the existence of extraterrestrial life, instead suggesting that such narratives serve as a cover for military activities.

Official statements

“The real conspiracy is not that aliens have visited, it’s that the US Air Force have been pushing these stories for decades, because it’s a useful cover for all their nefarious activities.” - Dr. David Clarke
“I would love to see documents that prove the American government has got aliens and flying saucers, but I would put a substantial wager… that we will be tilting at the same windmills in 10 years’ time.” - Dr. David Clarke

Context

Previous incidents, such as the Roswell crash in 1947, have fueled speculation about government secrecy surrounding UFOs. The US military has attributed many sightings to top-secret projects. A National Folklore Survey found that approximately four million people in England have reported seeing unexplained aerial phenomena, with 27% believing the government is covering up information about UFOs.

Dr. Clarke’s work, including overseeing the transfer of 210 public records on UFOs from the UK Ministry of Defence to the National Archives, has not revealed evidence supporting claims of alien encounters.

What’s next

The Pentagon is expected to release the requested UFO files, but experts like Dr. Clarke anticipate that the information will not provide new insights into extraterrestrial life. Ongoing inquiries by NASA into UFOs also continue to lack definitive conclusions.

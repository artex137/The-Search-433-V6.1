The Mysterious Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS Appears to Be Pulsing in a "Heartbeat Pattern"

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS is expected to pass within 170 million miles of Earth on December 19, 2023. This event will provide astronomers with a unique opportunity to observe the object closely as it approaches our planet.

This observation is significant because it may offer insights into the characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, which has shown signs of a "heartbeat" pulse of light repeating every 16.16 hours. Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb highlights the importance of these observations in a recent blog post. Researchers aim to determine whether this pulsing phenomenon is the result of the object’s natural activity or could indicate technological origins.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2023
  • Distance at closest approach: 170 million miles
  • Pulse period: 16.16 hours
  • Research publication: Astronomy & Astrophysics, October 2023
  • Lead researcher: Avi Loeb, Harvard University

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been monitored by astronomers leading up to its closest approach. In late October 2023, the object reached its perihelion, the point in its orbit closest to the Sun.

In October, a research team from Europe and Africa published findings indicating that the object emits a repeating light pulse. Avi Loeb has called for continued monitoring to better understand the object's activity and characteristics.

Official statements

“Overall, 3I shows characteristics typical of weakly active outer Solar System comets, despite its interstellar origin,” researchers wrote in their paper.
“A movie showing the periodic brightening of the jets around 3I/ATLAS over several days can reveal whether the jets are natural or technological based on the orientation of the heartbeat pattern relative to the Sun,” Loeb stated.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing interest in interstellar objects since the detection of 'Oumuamua in 2017. Both objects have prompted discussions about the possibility of extraterrestrial technology and the nature of such celestial bodies.

Current research aims to distinguish between natural cometary activity and potential technological signals from these interstellar visitors.

What’s next

Research teams will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth. They aim to collect detailed observations of the object’s pulsing behavior to better understand its nature and origins.

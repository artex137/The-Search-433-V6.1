Comet 3I/ATLAS to leave Sun's orbit, to be visible before dawn

Comet 3I/ATLAS, a rare interstellar visitor, has made its closest approach to the Sun on October 30 and is currently moving through the constellation Virgo. There have been no reports of injuries or fatalities associated with the comet.

This event is significant for astronomers and skywatchers, as the comet will be visible online on November 18 at 04:15 UTC through the Virtual Telescope Project, which will stream the event live from Italy. Observers will need at least an 8-inch telescope to view the comet, which has a faint magnitude of +10.9.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS approached the Sun on October 30.
  • Current location: constellation Virgo.
  • Visibility event: November 18 at 04:15 UTC.
  • Streaming live from Italy via Virtual Telescope Project.
  • Magnitude: +10.9, requiring an 8-inch telescope for viewing.

Timeline

On October 30, Comet 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to the Sun. Following this, the comet entered the constellation Virgo and continues on its path away from the Sun.

The comet will be featured in a live-streaming event on November 18, providing an opportunity for skywatchers to observe this rare occurrence.

Official statements

“Skywatchers can catch it online on November 18 via Virtual Telescope Project,” said a representative from the project.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is noted for being an interstellar visitor, which makes its appearance particularly intriguing for both astronomers and amateur enthusiasts alike. The comet's faint visibility requires significant equipment, limiting the number of observers who can see it in person.

What’s next

Skywatchers are encouraged to prepare their telescopes for the November 18 viewing opportunity, while astronomers will continue to monitor the comet's trajectory as it moves through the solar system.

Hegseth Shares Update on Government Release of UFO Files Under Trump’s Direction

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has stated that the Pentagon is currently working on identifying and releasing government files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life, following a directive from President Donald Trump. Hegseth made the comments during a visit to Sierra Space, a commercial aerospace company in Colorado, on Monday.

This initiative is part of Trump’s broader effort to address public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial matters. Hegseth indicated that his team is committed to complying with Trump’s instructions, although he did not specify a timeline for the release of the information.

Key facts

  • Event: Pentagon's initiative to release UFO-related files
  • Date: Announced on February 19, 2023
  • Location: Sierra Space, Colorado
  • Involved parties: Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, President Donald Trump
  • Public interest: Spurred by comments from former President Barack Obama
  • Status: Ongoing review and compliance with Trump's directive

Timeline

On February 19, 2023, President Trump instructed federal agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This announcement followed a surge of public interest after former President Obama claimed in a podcast that aliens are "real." Hegseth's comments on Monday reflect the Pentagon's commitment to this initiative.

During the visit to Sierra Space, Hegseth acknowledged that he did not initially expect to oversee the release of such information but assured reporters that updates would follow. He expressed excitement about the review process, stating, “We’ll see. I get to do the review and find out along with you!”

Official statements

“I don’t want to oversell how much time it will take, but we’re digging in,” said Hegseth.
“I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant departments and agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life,” Trump stated.

Context

The recent comments from Trump and Hegseth come in the wake of heightened public curiosity regarding UFOs, particularly after Obama's remarks about extraterrestrial life. Obama clarified that while the universe is vast, the likelihood of contact with aliens is low based on the information available during his presidency. This discourse has fueled speculation and interest in government-held information about UFOs.

What’s next

The Pentagon will continue its review of the files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Further updates are expected as they comply with Trump’s directive and assess the available information.

Comet 3I/ATLAS to Approach Earth in December 2025

Comet 3I/ATLAS will approach Earth on December 19, 2025, at a distance of 1.7 astronomical units (AU), approximately 254 million kilometres. The event follows several notable celestial occurrences in December, including the last full moon of the year and the Geminid meteor shower.

This approach is significant for astronomers and stargazers as it provides an opportunity to observe the comet, which has intrigued scientists since its discovery on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-Impact Last Alert System (ATLAS). Observers are encouraged to use telescopes to view the comet as it travels through the constellation Leo.

Key facts

  • Event: Comet 3I/ATLAS approaching Earth
  • Date: December 19, 2025
  • Distance: 1.7 AU (approximately 254 million km)
  • Viewing Location: East to northeast in the constellation Leo
  • Telescope Requirement: Aperture of at least 30 centimeters

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025. It reached its closest point to the Sun, known as perihelion, on October 29, 2025. Following this, it will approach Earth on December 19, 2025, before continuing its journey towards a flyby of Jupiter in March 2026.

Official statements

“This probably is the most anticipated celestial event for Earthlings this December,” said a spokesperson from ATLAS.

Context

The comet's approach is part of a series of celestial events in December 2025. These include the last full moon, known as the Cold Supermoon, on December 4, and the Geminid meteor shower peaking on December 13-14, which is expected to produce up to 120 meteors per hour.

What’s next

After the approach to Earth, Comet 3I/ATLAS will continue on its path to encounter Jupiter in March 2026, providing further opportunities for observation and study.

3I/ATLAS Shrugs (or Not)

Published 2025-12-21 15:27 UTC

3I/ATLAS Comet Passes Closest Point to Earth

The comet 3I/ATLAS has passed its closest point to Earth at approximately 170 million miles away on Friday, marking a significant moment for astronomers and space enthusiasts. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

This passage of 3I/ATLAS is significant as it has generated interest among scientists and the public alike, particularly due to speculation about its potential alien origins. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has expressed his intrigue over the comet's unusual characteristics and has published an article discussing the possibility that it could be an alien artifact.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS reached its closest point to Earth on Friday.
  • The distance from Earth at closest approach was approximately 170 million miles.
  • Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has speculated about the comet's origins.
  • 3I/ATLAS travels at an interstellar speed of 60 kilometers per second.
  • Loeb noted that the comet's journey through the Milky Way has taken billions of years.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been observed as it made its approach toward Earth. Its closest approach occurred on Friday, after which it began to speed away from our planet.

Avi Loeb has been vocal about the comet's unique characteristics, likening it to the first identified interstellar visitor, Oumuamua. He has suggested that its anomalous qualities might indicate an alien origin.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, “With an interstellar speed of 60 kilometers per second, the journey of 3I/ATLAS through the Milky Way disk of stars must have taken billions of years.”
Loeb further commented, “Given that, we should not be surprised that interstellar visitors... did not plan to dance with us.”

Context

This event follows the growing interest in interstellar objects, particularly after the discovery of Oumuamua in 2017. The speculation surrounding such objects often leads to discussions about extraterrestrial life and the potential for alien technology.

In popular culture, upcoming films such as Steven Spielberg’s Disclosure Day are contributing to the public discourse on the existence of extraterrestrial life and government transparency regarding UFOs.

What’s next

As 3I/ATLAS continues its journey away from Earth, researchers will likely continue to study its characteristics and trajectory, while public interest in potential alien life remains high.

3I/ATLAS is Unusually Rich in Methanol, ALMA Observations Show

Astronomers have detected unusually high levels of methanol and hydrogen cyanide in the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile. The observations took place as 3I/ATLAS approached the Sun, releasing gas and dust that formed a coma around its core.

This discovery is significant because it provides insights into the chemical composition of comets from outside our Solar System. Researchers have noted that the findings may indicate that the icy material from 3I/ATLAS formed under different conditions than those typical for comets in our Solar System.

Key facts

  • Observations conducted using ALMA's Atacama Compact Array in Chile.
  • 3I/ATLAS is heavily enriched in methanol compared to hydrogen cyanide.
  • Methanol-to-hydrogen cyanide ratios measured at about 70 and 120.
  • Findings published in the Astrophysical Journal.
  • Observations occurred as the comet approached the Sun.

Timeline

Astronomers have observed 3I/ATLAS as it neared the Sun, which led to the release of gas and dust from its icy surface. They focused on the chemical fingerprints of methanol and hydrogen cyanide in the comet's coma, revealing significant levels of methanol.

The research team measured the methanol-to-hydrogen cyanide ratios on two separate observing dates, establishing that the comet has among the highest methanol concentrations ever recorded in a comet.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” said American University Professor Nathan Roth. “The details reveal what it’s made of, and it’s bursting with methanol in a way we just don’t usually see in comets in our own Solar System.”
“The derived methanol-to-hydrogen cyanide ratios in 3I/ATLAS are among the most enriched values measured in any comet, surpassed only by anomalous solar system comet C/2016 R2 (Pan-STARRS),” the authors stated.

Context

This study enhances our understanding of the chemical diversity in comets. Prior observations have indicated varying compositions among comets within our Solar System. The unique characteristics of 3I/ATLAS may shed light on the processes involved in the formation of comets from different solar systems.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from 3I/ATLAS to further understand the implications of its chemical composition and the conditions that led to its formation.

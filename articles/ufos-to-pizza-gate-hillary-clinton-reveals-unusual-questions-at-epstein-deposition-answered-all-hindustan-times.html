Hillary Clinton addresses unusual questions at Epstein deposition

Hillary Clinton has revealed that she faced "unusual" questions during her deposition before the House Oversight Committee regarding her connections to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. The deposition took place in Chappaqua, New York, and lasted nearly five hours.

This matter is significant as it underscores the ongoing scrutiny of Clinton's ties to Epstein, a convicted sex offender. The hearing was briefly interrupted due to a violation of House rules when Representative Lauren Boebert leaked a photo of the closed-door session.

Key facts

  • Event: House Oversight Committee hearing
  • Date: Not disclosed
  • Location: Chappaqua, New York
  • Duration: Nearly five hours
  • Interruption caused by: Lauren Boebert leaking a photo
  • Response from Boebert: Claimed Benny Johnson “did nothing wrong”
  • Further actions against Boebert: Not disclosed

Timeline

Hillary Clinton participated in a deposition regarding her ties to Jeffrey Epstein. During the nearly five-hour hearing, she stated she was asked multiple times about her relationship with Epstein, denying any association. Towards the end of the session, she reported being questioned about UFOs and the Pizzagate conspiracy theory.

The hearing was interrupted when Representative Lauren Boebert allegedly leaked a photo of the closed-door deposition to conservative podcaster Benny Johnson, prompting a temporary halt to the proceedings.

Official statements

“I don't know how many times I had to say I did not know Jeffrey Epstein. I never went to his island. I never went to his homes.”
“It then got, at the end, quite unusual because I started being asked about UFOs and a series of questions about Pizzagate, one of the most vile bogus conspiracy theories that was propagated on the internet.”

Context

The Pizzagate conspiracy theory emerged in late 2016, falsely alleging that a child sex trafficking ring was being operated by prominent Democratic politicians out of Comet Ping Pong, a pizzeria in Washington, DC. This theory misinterpreted emails from WikiLeaks that suggested coded language regarding pedophilia, leading to widespread misinformation on social media platforms.

What’s next

Further investigations into the hearing and potential repercussions for Lauren Boebert are expected, although specific actions against her have not been disclosed.

3I/ATLAS Is Alive? New Preprint Claims Object Is a Plasmatic Organism

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS may not be a typical comet, according to a new preprint by Scientific Director Rangel Martínez of Biotecnología Exosomal S. de R.L. de C.V. The paper, released on ResearchGate, suggests that 3I/ATLAS could be a self-organising, plasmatic entity, raising questions about the nature of life in our solar system.

This research is significant as it challenges existing theories about comets and the definition of life. Martínez argues that 3I/ATLAS displays unusual movement and characteristics that do not align with established comet behaviour, prompting a reevaluation of how scientists classify extraterrestrial entities. Authorities in the scientific community are now examining these claims closely, as they could reshape our understanding of life beyond Earth.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Researcher: Rangel Martínez
  • Affiliation: Biotecnología Exosomal S. de R.L. de C.V.
  • Location of research publication: ResearchGate
  • Key theory: 3I/ATLAS may be a 'cosmobiont'
  • Distance from the sun when studied: 4.4 AU
  • Notable claim: Significant non-gravitational acceleration observed

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been a subject of scientific interest since its discovery. Recent studies have highlighted its unique characteristics:

  • Initial observations indicated unusual speed and trajectory.
  • Analysis conducted at approximately 4.4 AU from the sun showed no common comet gases.
  • Martínez's preprint proposes a new classification for 3I/ATLAS as a cosmobiont.

Official statements

“Standard comet theories struggle to explain all of this object's behaviour thoroughly,” Martínez stated in his preprint.
“If 3I/ATLAS turns out to be a 'cosmobiont,' we must stop viewing life as strictly needing carbon and water,” he added.

Context

The concept of cosmobionts relates to structures observed in Earth's upper atmosphere that exhibit self-organisation and responsiveness to electromagnetic forces. Martínez's theory potentially connects these atmospheric entities with interstellar phenomena.

This research encourages a broader consideration of what constitutes life and could lead to significant shifts in the scientific community's approach to extraterrestrial exploration.

What’s next

Further studies and observations are necessary to validate Martínez's claims about 3I/ATLAS. The scientific community will likely focus on gathering more data to evaluate the cosmobiont theory and its implications for understanding life beyond Earth.

Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Shows Wobbling Jets and a Rare Sun-Facing “Anti-Tail”

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has exhibited a unique Sun-facing “anti-tail” and wobbling jets as it traverses the inner solar system, with findings announced in early July 2025. This comet is only the third confirmed object from another star system, and it continues to provide valuable data for astronomers studying its behavior and characteristics.

The significance of these observations lies in their potential to enhance understanding of interstellar comets. According to astronomers, the unique features of 3I/ATLAS, including its anti-tail and jet patterns, offer insights into its composition and the effects of solar heating on its surface. This information is crucial as it allows researchers to analyze the comet's spin, dust distribution, and icy grains as it approaches the Sun.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object, indicating it did not originate from the solar system.
  • Discovery announced in early July 2025 after telescopes detected a fast-moving object.
  • Pre-discovery images date back to mid-June 2025.
  • The closest approach to Earth will remain at a significant astronomical distance.
  • The comet reached perihelion, its closest point to the Sun, at a moderate distance.
  • Wobbling jets have shown a periodic change with a modulation period of approximately 7.74 hours.
  • Comet activity peaked around late October 2025.

Timeline

In June 2025, astronomers captured pre-discovery images of 3I/ATLAS, extending the observation timeline. By early July, survey telescopes flagged the object, confirming its interstellar origin and comet-like activity. The comet's closest approach to the Sun occurred in late October 2025, coinciding with its most intense activity.

Throughout the observation period, astronomers tracked the comet's jet structures and anti-tail, gathering data that would inform future studies on interstellar objects.

Official statements

“The anti-tail behavior is a useful diagnostic because it can reflect particle size distribution and sublimation,” said an astronomer involved in the study.
“This is a rare chance to watch a visitor from another star system behave like an active comet,” another researcher noted.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object confirmed to visit the solar system, following previous comets that exhibited distinct characteristics. The diversity among these interstellar visitors suggests that they may originate from varied environments, providing an opportunity to study the formation of solids in different star systems.

Each interstellar object offers unique insights, making the detailed observations of 3I/ATLAS particularly valuable for ongoing research.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data collected from 3I/ATLAS to understand its physical properties and behavior. The findings will contribute to a broader understanding of interstellar comets and their characteristics as they interact with solar systems.

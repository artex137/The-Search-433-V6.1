Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Behaves Strangely Near Jupiter

NASA has reported that interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has exhibited unusual behavior near Jupiter, as astronomers continue to monitor its trajectory ahead of a close approach on March 16, 2026. The comet, which is only the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through the solar system, poses no threat to Earth, according to NASA.

This unusual behavior matters because it may provide insights into the comet's composition and activity. Researchers are particularly interested in the comet's lopsided appearance and the implications of its jet-like structures, which may reveal details about its rotation and outgassing mechanisms.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS reached perihelion on October 30, 2025.
  • Closest approach to Earth occurred on December 19, 2025, at approximately 1.8 AU (170 million miles).
  • Projected close approach to Jupiter is on March 16, 2026, at about 0.358 AU (53.6 million km).
  • NASA has tracked the comet using multiple spacecraft, including Hubble and Parker Solar Probe.
  • Observations have shown a periodic modulation in the comet's jet structure, suggesting a rotation period of roughly 15.5 hours.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been under observation since its discovery. Key events include:

  • October 30, 2025: The comet reaches perihelion, coming closest to the Sun.
  • December 19, 2025: The comet's closest approach to Earth occurs at 1.8 AU.
  • March 16, 2026: The comet is projected to make a close approach to Jupiter.

Official statements

“NASA’s official overview presents 3I/ATLAS as an interstellar comet with a dust-and-gas coma, imaged repeatedly across missions, and emphasizes that the object behaves like a comet and poses no threat to Earth.”
“The periodicity can imply a nucleus rotation period of roughly 15.5 hours.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is significant as it provides a rare opportunity to study an interstellar object. Unlike typical comets that return after orbiting the Sun, 3I/ATLAS will not return, making its observations crucial for understanding the characteristics of such objects.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Jupiter. The March 2026 flyby is expected to yield further data that could clarify the comet's physical properties and activity patterns.

What is Heading Towards Earth, Comet or Alien Craft?

NASA has confirmed that comet 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth, dismissing viral rumours claiming it is an alien spacecraft. The comet, detected on July 1, 2025, will safely pass 270 million kilometres away from Earth on July 21, 2025.

This announcement is significant as it addresses growing public concern and speculation surrounding the comet. Authorities have clarified that the comet is an interstellar object and not a spacecraft, allowing scientists to study it without fear of impact on Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS detected by NASA’s ATLAS telescope on July 1, 2025.
  • Closest approach to Earth was on July 21, 2025, at 270 million km (168 million miles).
  • Comet's speed measured at 210,000 km/h (130,500 mph).
  • Closest approach to the Sun is on October 30, 2025, at 210 million km (130 million miles).
  • Estimated nucleus diameter between 440 metres and 5.6 km.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, NASA's ATLAS telescope detected comet 3I/ATLAS. Following its detection, the comet generated speculation online, with some users suggesting it might be an alien spacecraft. On July 21, 2025, the comet made its closest approach to Earth, passing at a safe distance of 270 million kilometres. NASA confirmed the comet's trajectory and speed, emphasizing that it poses no threat. The comet will reach its closest approach to the Sun on October 30, 2025.

Official statements

“While the idea of a ‘giant alien spaceship’ makes for sensational headlines, 3I/ATLAS is simply a fast-moving interstellar comet, and experts confirm it poses no threat to Earth,” NASA stated.

Context

This incident highlights the public's fascination with celestial events and the spread of misinformation online. Previous comets and asteroids have also sparked similar concerns, prompting agencies like NASA to improve communication about space phenomena.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun, providing valuable data on interstellar objects for future research.

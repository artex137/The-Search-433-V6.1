NASA’s Webb Telescope Captures Glow of Rare ‘Interstellar’ Comet

NASA’s James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS on August 6, 2025, using its Near-Infrared Spectrograph (NIRSpec), according to the space agency’s website. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this observation.

This discovery is significant because it enhances NASA’s efforts to find, track, and study small bodies in and beyond our solar system. The research team is currently analyzing data from the JWST, with a preprint already available online. Studying interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS provides a rare window into the building blocks of other planetary systems, allowing scientists to compare them with comets from our solar system.

Key facts

  • Observation date: August 6, 2025
  • Instrument used: Near-Infrared Spectrograph (NIRSpec)
  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Other NASA missions involved: Hubble Space Telescope and SPHEREx
  • Distance of new exoplanet TOI-1846 b: approximately 154 light-years

Timeline

On August 6, 2025, NASA's JWST captured data on the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The research team has begun analyzing this data, and a preprint has been made available online. The Hubble Space Telescope and the recently launched SPHEREx mission are also participating in the observations.

Official statements

“Studying interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS provides a rare window into the building blocks of other planetary systems,” NASA stated.

Context

This observation is part of NASA's broader efforts to understand celestial bodies beyond our solar system. The discovery of 3I/ATLAS contributes to existing knowledge about comets and their role in planetary formation.

What’s next

The research team will continue to analyze the data from the JWST and other observatories to better understand the size, composition, and physical properties of 3I/ATLAS.

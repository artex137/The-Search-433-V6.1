US Senate candidates debate key issues in Illinois

CHICAGO - The first debate for the 2026 primary election in Illinois took place on Monday night, featuring U.S. Rep. Robin Kelly, U.S. Rep. Raja Krishnamoorthi, and Lt. Gov. Juliana Stratton. The candidates are competing for the Democratic nomination to succeed long-serving Senator Dick Durbin. The debate began at 6 p.m. local time and was hosted by Fox Chicago.

This debate is significant as it sets the stage for the upcoming primary election on March 17, 2024. Early voting has already commenced in the Chicago area, making this debate a crucial platform for candidates to outline their positions on various issues.

Key facts

  • Event: First debate for the Democratic primary for U.S. Senate
  • Date: Monday night
  • Time: 6 p.m. local time
  • Location: Chicago, Illinois
  • Participants: U.S. Rep. Robin Kelly, U.S. Rep. Raja Krishnamoorthi, Lt. Gov. Juliana Stratton
  • Current Senator: Dick Durbin
  • Primary Election Day: March 17, 2024
  • Early voting ongoing in the Chicago area

Timeline

The debate, hosted by Fox Chicago’s Political Editor Paris Schutz, featured discussions on various topics relevant to the future of Illinois. The candidates presented their platforms and responded to questions from the moderator and possibly the audience.

As the primary election approaches, candidates are expected to intensify their campaigning efforts, leveraging media appearances and public engagements to connect with voters.

Official statements

“This debate is an opportunity for us to share our visions for Illinois and to engage with the voters directly,” said U.S. Rep. Robin Kelly.
“I look forward to discussing our plans for the future of our state and our country,” stated Lt. Gov. Juliana Stratton.

Context

The upcoming primary election is critical as it will determine the Democratic candidate who will run for U.S. Senate in the 2026 general election. Dick Durbin has been a prominent figure in Illinois politics for decades, making the race particularly competitive.

This debate marks the beginning of a series of discussions that will help shape voter perceptions and candidate profiles leading up to the primary.

What’s next

Following the debate, candidates will continue their campaigns, focusing on voter outreach and engagement as the primary election date approaches.

Sounds Very Exciting — Karoline Leavitt Shows Interest in Alien Discovery As Trump Seeks to Declassify UFO Files

White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt has expressed interest in the potential existence of aliens during a press briefing on February 18, 2026. Leavitt responded to a question from Newsmax correspondent Mike Carter regarding U.S. President Donald Trump’s beliefs about extraterrestrial life, stating, “A speech on aliens would be news to me. That sounds very exciting, though.” There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this topic.

Leavitt’s comments come as Trump plans to declassify government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, following a discussion on the subject by former President Barack Obama. This development has sparked renewed interest and debate about the existence of aliens and the government's knowledge of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP).

Key facts

  • Karoline Leavitt made her comments on February 18, 2026.
  • Leavitt was responding to a question from Newsmax regarding Trump’s beliefs in aliens.
  • Former President Barack Obama discussed aliens on a podcast on February 14, 2026.
  • Trump stated he will direct the Secretary of War and other agencies to release files on aliens.
  • Trump has not disclosed his personal opinion on extraterrestrial life.

Timeline

On February 14, 2026, Barack Obama addressed the question of alien existence during a podcast with Brian Tyler Cohen, stating, “They’re real. But I haven’t seen them, and they’re not being kept in Area 51.” His comments led to a mix of reactions and a clarification on social media the following day.

On February 18, 2026, Karoline Leavitt responded to inquiries about Trump’s beliefs in aliens, indicating that such a speech would be new to her but expressed excitement at the prospect. Later that day, Trump announced his plans to declassify government files related to UFOs and UAPs.

Official statements

“A speech on aliens would be news to me. That sounds very exciting, though,” said Karoline Leavitt during the press briefing.
“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life,” President Trump wrote on Truth Social.

Context

The discussion around aliens has gained traction following various comments made by public figures, including Obama. His remarks on the podcast and subsequent clarification have fueled public curiosity about the government's stance on extraterrestrial life. Trump’s announcement to declassify information signals a potential shift in transparency regarding UFOs and related phenomena.

What’s next

Following Trump's announcement, relevant departments will begin the process of identifying and releasing classified files regarding UFOs and UAPs. Further developments are expected as the public awaits the outcome of this initiative.

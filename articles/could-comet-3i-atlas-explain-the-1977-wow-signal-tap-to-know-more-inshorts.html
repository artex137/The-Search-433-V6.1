Comet 3I/Atlas May Explain 1977 Wow! Signal

Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has suggested that the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas may have emitted the 1977 Wow! Signal, according to calculations he presented. Loeb's research indicates that the comet's position closely aligns with the signal's coordinates, with less than a 1% chance of coincidence, as the comet approaches Mars.

This hypothesis is significant as it could provide new insights into the origins of the Wow! Signal, one of the most intriguing events in the search for extraterrestrial intelligence. Authorities in the scientific community are preparing for upcoming observations of 3I/Atlas to further investigate this claim.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/Atlas
  • Researcher: Avi Loeb, Harvard University
  • Signal: 1977 Wow! Signal
  • Coincidence Probability: Less than 1%
  • Current Location: Approaching Mars

Timeline

Avi Loeb has conducted calculations indicating a potential link between the comet 3I/Atlas and the Wow! Signal. His findings suggest a remarkable correlation between the comet's position and the signal's coordinates.

As 3I/Atlas continues its approach to Mars, scientists plan to conduct observations to test these claims further.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, “The position of 3I/Atlas nearly matches the coordinates of the Wow! Signal, which raises intriguing possibilities for its origin.”

Context

The Wow! Signal was detected in 1977 by astronomer Jerry R. Ehman while working on a SETI project. It remains one of the most notable potential signs of extraterrestrial communication, sparking ongoing investigation and debate among scientists.

What’s next

Upcoming observations of 3I/Atlas will aim to verify Loeb's hypothesis and explore its implications for understanding the Wow! Signal and potential extraterrestrial life.

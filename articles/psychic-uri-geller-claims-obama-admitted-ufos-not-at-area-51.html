Psychic Uri Geller claims Obama admitted UFOs – not at Area 51

Uri Geller, a well-known psychic, has claimed that former U.S. President Barack Obama has admitted to the existence of UFOs, but not in the context of Area 51. Geller made the statement during an interview on October 30, 2023, in a discussion about UFOs and government disclosures.

This claim has drawn attention as it touches on ongoing public interest in UFO phenomena and government transparency. Authorities have not yet responded to Geller's comments, and it is unclear if any official investigations will follow.

Key facts

  • Uri Geller made the claim during an interview on October 30, 2023.
  • Geller is a well-known psychic and illusionist.
  • Obama's specific admission has not been publicly disclosed.
  • No official responses from government agencies have been reported.

Timeline

On October 30, 2023, Uri Geller discussed UFOs during an interview. He stated that Obama acknowledged the existence of UFOs, but did not associate them with Area 51, a site often linked to conspiracy theories about extraterrestrial life.

Since then, there has been no official comment from the former president or any government agencies regarding Geller's claim.

Official statements

“I have spoken to Obama, and he confirmed the existence of UFOs,” Geller claimed during his interview.

Context

The topic of UFOs has gained increased attention in recent years, especially following the U.S. government's release of previously classified documents. Public interest in government disclosures regarding unidentified aerial phenomena continues to grow.

Area 51, located in Nevada, has long been associated with UFO lore and conspiracy theories, often serving as a focal point for discussions about government secrecy and extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

It remains to be seen if Geller's claims will prompt any official investigations or responses from government officials regarding UFOs and their potential existence.

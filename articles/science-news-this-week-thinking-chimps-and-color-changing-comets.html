Published 2025-11-08 12:29 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS Makes Headlines with Color Change

On November 8, 2025, Comet 3I/ATLAS has captivated the scientific community as it reemerged from behind the sun, displaying a bluish hue. This is the third color change observed in the comet since its discovery, with experts suggesting that gases like carbon monoxide or ammonia may be responsible, although this remains unconfirmed.

The significance of this event lies in the ongoing study of comets and their behaviors, which can provide insights into the early solar system. Authorities and scientists continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS, as well as other comets like C/2025 K1 (ATLAS), which recently survived a perilous close pass around the sun, resulting in its own color transformation to a golden hue.

Key facts

  • Event Date: November 8, 2025
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed changing color for the third time
  • Possible gases causing color change: carbon monoxide or ammonia (unconfirmed)
  • Comet C/2025 K1 (ATLAS) passed near the sun on October 8
  • Location of observation: not disclosed

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019. It has undergone several transformations, with the latest color change occurring as it approached the sun. On October 8, 2025, Comet C/2025 K1 (ATLAS) made a close pass to the sun and changed to a golden color due to increased heat.

Following these events, scientists have intensified their observations of both comets to understand the underlying processes affecting their appearances.

Official statements

“The changes in color observed in both comets highlight the dynamic nature of these celestial bodies,” said an unnamed expert in celestial phenomena.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is not the first comet to exhibit color changes during its orbit. Previous studies have indicated that various factors, such as solar radiation and chemical composition, can influence a comet's appearance. Understanding these changes is crucial for astronomers as they study the history and evolution of the solar system.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS and C/2025 K1 (ATLAS) for further changes and to gather more data. Future observations may clarify the causes of their color transformations and enhance our understanding of cometary behavior.

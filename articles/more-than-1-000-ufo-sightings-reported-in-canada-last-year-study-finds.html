More than 1,000 UFO sightings reported in Canada last year, study finds

More than 1,000 UFO sightings have been reported across Canada in 2025, according to a study released on March 11, 2026. The study details the increase in sightings but does not disclose any injuries or fatalities related to these incidents.

This surge in reported sightings has drawn attention from authorities and researchers, who are now examining the implications of these reports. The findings may influence future investigations and policies regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Over 1,000 UFO sightings reported in Canada in 2025.
  • Study released on March 11, 2026.
  • Details on specific locations and times not disclosed.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.

Timeline

In 2025, various locations across Canada recorded numerous UFO sightings. The study, released in March 2026, aggregates these reports to highlight a significant increase in public interest and awareness of unidentified flying objects.

The research has prompted discussions among government agencies about how to address the growing phenomenon of UFO sightings and what measures may be taken to investigate these claims further.

Official statements

“This study opens new avenues for understanding the patterns of sightings and what they might indicate about our airspace,” said a spokesperson for the agency involved in the research.

Context

UFO sightings in Canada have fluctuated over the years, with notable peaks in public interest often coinciding with global events or advancements in technology. The recent increase aligns with a broader trend of heightened awareness surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Authorities have historically approached UFO sightings with caution, often attributing them to misidentified aircraft, weather balloons, or other explainable phenomena.

What’s next

In the wake of this study, researchers and government officials are expected to collaborate on investigations into the reported sightings, aiming to establish clearer guidelines and protocols for future reports.

Nasa to release images of interstellar comet 3I/Atlas invading our Solar System

Nasa has announced plans to release new images of interstellar comet 3I/Atlas during a live event on November 20, 2025, at the Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland. The agency is sharing this data as the US government shutdown concludes and Nasa resumes regular operations.

This event is significant as it will provide fresh insights into the comet’s composition, trajectory, and its implications for understanding materials from beyond the solar system. Scientists will discuss findings from various missions that have captured imagery of the comet since its discovery on July 1, 2025, by Nasa’s ATLAS (Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System) observatory in Hawaii.

Key facts

  • Event Date: November 20, 2025
  • Location: Goddard Space Flight Center, Greenbelt, Maryland
  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Closest Approach to Earth: 170 million miles
  • Distance from Mars: 19 million miles in early October 2025
  • Previous Interstellar Objects: 1I ‘Oumuamua (2017), 2I Borisov (2019)

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, Nasa’s ATLAS observatory in Hawaii discovered comet 3I/Atlas. Early analyses have indicated that the comet is hyperactive, shedding dust and gas as it approaches the Sun. In early October 2025, it passed within 19 million miles of Mars. The comet will make its closest approach to Earth at a distance of about 170 million miles.

As the US government shutdown ends, Nasa has prepared to release high-resolution images and spectral data from the Hubble Space Telescope, the James Webb Space Telescope, and several ground-based observatories. The live event on November 20 will showcase these findings.

Official statements

“Every interstellar object gives us a rare opportunity to directly observe material that formed around another star,” said a Nasa scientist involved in the observation campaign.

Context

3I/Atlas is only the third confirmed interstellar object to enter the solar system, following 1I ‘Oumuamua, which was spotted in 2017, and the comet 2I Borisov, observed in 2019. Studying such objects helps scientists gain insights into the chemical composition of other planetary systems.

What’s next

Following the live event, Nasa plans to continue analyzing the data gathered from 3I/Atlas to enhance understanding of comet formation and evolution beyond the solar system.

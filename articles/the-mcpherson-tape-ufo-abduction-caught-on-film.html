'The McPherson Tape' — UFO abduction caught on film

A home video titled "The McPherson Tape," originally released as "UFO Abduction," portrays a UFO encounter experienced by the Van Heese family during a birthday celebration at their home in 1989. The film, written, directed, and produced by Dean Alioto, has gained notoriety as one of the earliest examples of the found footage horror genre. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the events depicted in the film.

The significance of "The McPherson Tape" lies in its impact on the UFO community, as the film has sparked numerous conspiracy theories regarding its authenticity. According to Alioto, an Air Force lieutenant colonel with 40 years of intelligence experience has claimed the footage is real, leading to further intrigue and speculation about extraterrestrial encounters.

Key facts

  • Film Title: The McPherson Tape
  • Original Title: UFO Abduction
  • Release Year: 1989
  • Director: Dean Alioto
  • Setting: Van Heese family home
  • Event: Depiction of a UFO encounter during a birthday party
  • Claim of Authenticity: Air Force lieutenant colonel support

Timeline

The film begins with the Van Heese family celebrating their daughter Michelle’s fifth birthday. The family sings "Happy Birthday" in the kitchen, where the lights suddenly go out after the candles are blown out.

Three men from the family go outside to check the circuit breaker. They notice a mysterious light in the sky and approach it to investigate. Upon encountering a spacecraft and several extraterrestrial beings, they quickly return to the house, creating a sense of panic as they realize they are trapped.

The film concludes with the family unable to escape the situation, highlighting the fear of the unknown and the loss of safety in their home.

Official statements

“The McPherson Tape captures the viewer’s attention by showing how ordinary people react when they are faced with a situation that cannot be explained,” said Alioto in an interview with Found Footage Critic.

Context

Prior to "The McPherson Tape," found footage films had begun to emerge as a distinct genre, but Alioto's work is considered one of the foundational pieces that shaped its evolution. The film's mix of domestic life with elements of horror has contributed to its lasting appeal, particularly around Halloween when themes of fear and the unknown are prevalent.

As the film gained traction, rumors regarding its authenticity escalated, especially after copies were distributed within the UFO community. This has led to ongoing discussions about the nature of alien encounters and the perception of reality in media.

What’s next

Investigations into the claims surrounding "The McPherson Tape" are ongoing, as interest in the film and its implications regarding UFOs continues to grow within the community. Further discussions about the authenticity of the footage may arise as new evidence or testimonials come to light.

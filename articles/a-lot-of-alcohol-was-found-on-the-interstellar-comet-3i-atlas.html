A lot of alcohol was found on the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

Researchers have discovered a significant amount of alcohol on the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, according to spectroscopic studies conducted using the ALMA radio telescope. The findings were reported on March 9, 2026, and stem from data collected in autumn 2025 when the comet made its closest approach to the Sun.

This discovery is important as it sheds light on the chemical composition of 3I/ATLAS, which is gradually leaving the Solar System. The presence of methanol, a type of alcohol, indicates potential organic material that could be linked to the building blocks of life, though it cannot be consumed.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was closest to the Sun in autumn 2025.
  • The ALMA radio telescope was used for the spectroscopic studies.
  • Methanol and hydrocyanic acid were detected on the comet.
  • The findings were published on the arXiv preprint server.
  • The discovery was announced on March 9, 2026.

Timeline

In autumn 2025, 3I/ATLAS approached the Sun, prompting scientists to observe it with various astronomical instruments, including ALMA. The data collected during this period enabled researchers to identify the chemical substances present on the comet.

On March 9, 2026, the findings regarding the alcohol content on the comet were made public, highlighting the presence of methanol and its implications for organic chemistry in space.

Official statements

“The presence of methanol is intriguing as it can be transformed into more complex organic molecules, which may be considered building blocks of life,” stated a researcher involved in the study.

Context

Previous studies of comets have revealed various organic compounds, but the conditions under which 3I/ATLAS formed appear to differ from those of comets within our Solar System. This distinction may offer new insights into the formation of celestial bodies and the origins of life.

What’s next

Further analysis of the data from ALMA is expected to continue, as scientists seek to understand the implications of these findings for theories about life's building blocks in the universe.

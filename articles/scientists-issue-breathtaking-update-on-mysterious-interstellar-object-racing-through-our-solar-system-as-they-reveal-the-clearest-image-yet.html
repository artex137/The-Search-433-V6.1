Scientists issue breathtaking update on mysterious 'interstellar object' racing through our solar system

Scientists have reported a significant update on the interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the solar system. The comet is currently approaching the sun at a speed of 137,000 miles per hour (221,000 km/h) and was approximately 240 million miles (380 million km) from Earth when the latest images were captured on August 27, 2025.

This development is important as it provides clearer evidence about the nature of 3I/ATLAS, which has been the subject of speculation regarding its origins. Researchers at the Gemini South Observatory in Chile have obtained the clearest images yet of the object, revealing features that suggest it is a comet rather than an artificial spacecraft.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Speed: 137,000 miles per hour (221,000 km/h)
  • Distance from Earth: 240 million miles (380 million km)
  • Date of images: August 27, 2025
  • Location of observation: Gemini South Observatory, Chile

Timeline

On August 27, 2025, scientists at the Gemini South Observatory captured images of 3I/ATLAS as it approached the sun. These images revealed a dense icy nucleus surrounded by a coma and a long tail extending away from the sun. The images confirmed that the object is exhibiting cometary activity, which has increased as it nears the sun.

Previous observations, including images taken by the Hubble Space Telescope in July and the James Webb Space Telescope, indicated some cometary activity, but the new images show that this activity has become significantly more pronounced.

Official statements

“These new images very clearly demonstrate that 3I/ATLAS is a comet,” said Dr Mark Norris, an astronomer from the University of Lancashire.
“3I/ATLAS has a CO2-rich atmosphere with less water than most home-grown comets. This might indicate it formed far from its parent star,” stated Dr Matthew Genge, a planetary scientist from Imperial College London.
“Little green men certainly aren't responsible!” Dr Genge added, emphasizing that the observations dismiss theories of extraterrestrial origins.

Context

3I/ATLAS has been closely monitored since its discovery as it traverses the solar system. Previous claims, notably by Harvard physicist Professor Avi Loeb, suggested the object could be an artificial construct. However, the new findings support the conclusion that 3I/ATLAS is a natural object, aligning with characteristics typical of comets.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to observe 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the sun to gather more data. The window for observation is limited, and researchers aim to learn more about the comet's composition and behavior as it travels through the solar system.

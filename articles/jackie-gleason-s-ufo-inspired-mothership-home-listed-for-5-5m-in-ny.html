Jackie Gleason’s UFO-Inspired ‘Mothership’ Home Listed for $5.5M in NY

Jackie Gleason’s former residence, known as “The Mothership,” has been listed for $5.5 million in Cortlandt Manor, New York. The architectural oddity, built in the 1950s, reflects Gleason’s fascination with UFOs and outer space. The property spans 3.17 acres and features a circular main house with two bedrooms and two bathrooms, along with a guest cottage.

This listing has garnered attention due to the home’s unique design and historical significance. Gleason, famed for his role in “The Honeymooners,” reportedly designed the home as a retreat from New York City life. The property is positioned in a market that sees rising interest in vintage celebrity homes, especially those with unique architectural features.

Key facts

  • Location: Cortlandt Manor, Westchester County, New York
  • Listing price: $5.5 million
  • Size: 3.17 acres, 3,500 square feet
  • Bedrooms: 2
  • Bathrooms: 2
  • Previous listing price in 2018: $12 million
  • Architect: Paul Mayen

Timeline

Jackie Gleason commissioned the home in the 1950s, reflecting his interest in UFOs and outer space. It became a weekend retreat for the comedian while he filmed his shows. The property was sold in the 1970s and has changed hands several times since then, including a notable estate sale in 2018.

The home was last listed for $12 million in 2018, but has now been re-listed at $5.5 million amid changing market conditions.

Official statements

“The Mothership stands as a testament to Gleason’s eccentric genius, a circular sanctuary that defies conventional design,” said a representative from the listing agency.

Context

The home’s design features, such as curved walls and a central atrium, were influenced by architect Paul Mayen, a disciple of Frank Lloyd Wright. Gleason’s passion for UFOs reportedly peaked after a visit to Homestead Air Force Base in 1973, where he claimed to have seen alien bodies. The home’s unique characteristics have made it a point of interest for collectors of architectural curiosities.

What’s next

The listing agent has highlighted the potential for expansion on the property, suggesting that it could accommodate amenities like a pool or studio space. As the market evolves, the home may attract buyers seeking to blend historical significance with modern luxury.

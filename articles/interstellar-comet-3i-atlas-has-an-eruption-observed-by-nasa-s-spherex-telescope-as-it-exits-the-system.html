Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has an eruption observed by NASA’s Spherex telescope as it exits the system

NASA’s Spherex telescope has observed an eruption from interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it exits the solar system. The event occurred on February 7, 2026, but details regarding the specific time and location of the eruption have not been disclosed.

This observation is significant as it provides insights into the behavior of interstellar comets and their interactions with the solar system. NASA continues to monitor 3I/ATLAS to gather more data on its composition and trajectory.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed by NASA’s Spherex telescope.
  • The eruption occurred on February 7, 2026.
  • Specific time and location of the eruption have not been disclosed.
  • 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar comet.

Timeline

On February 7, 2026, NASA’s Spherex telescope detected an eruption from interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The event marks an important moment in the study of interstellar bodies as they exit the solar system.

Official statements

“The eruption from 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity to study an interstellar comet’s behavior as it interacts with our solar system,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

This observation of 3I/ATLAS adds to the growing body of research on interstellar comets. Previous studies have shown that such comets can provide valuable information about the early solar system and the formation of planetary bodies.

What’s next

NASA will continue to track 3I/ATLAS to analyze the data collected from the eruption and to assess its trajectory as it moves further into interstellar space.

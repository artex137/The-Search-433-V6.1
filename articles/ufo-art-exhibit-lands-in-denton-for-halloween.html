UFO art exhibit lands in Denton for Halloween

A UFO installation by James and Umut Demirguc Thurman has been displayed at Cedar Street Park in Denton since October 17, 2025. The installation features a 10-foot-diameter flying saucer made of stainless steel panels and a mirrored dome, as part of the city's Halloween decorations.

The exhibit, called Area 940, is designed to enhance the Halloween experience for residents and visitors. The project was funded by the city, which requested a classic 1950s flying saucer design. The Thurmans have expressed their excitement about contributing to the community through this public artwork.

Key facts

  • Installation displayed at Cedar Street Park, Denton, Texas
  • Project funded by the city of Denton
  • Installation completed on October 17, 2025
  • UFO constructed from 52 stainless steel panels
  • Mirrored dome made from acrylic
  • Part of Denton's 31 Days of Halloween celebration

Timeline

On October 17, 2025, the UFO installation was unveiled during a “Meet the Artists” event at Cedar Street Park. The Thurmans interacted with visitors, discussing their work and the creative process behind the installation. The project has since attracted attention from the community, with many people visiting to take photographs.

Prior to the installation, the couple spent nearly three weeks building the UFO in their driveway, transforming it from a concept into a public art piece.

Official statements

“We’re just thrilled to live in a city that is so excited about Halloween,” said James Thurman, creator of the installation and professor of metalsmithing and jewelry.
“In my metalwork and jewelry, craftsmanship is very important,” said Umut Demirguc Thurman. “We applied the same attention to detail to fabricating this UFO.”

Context

The installation is part of Denton’s 31 Days of Halloween, which promotes community engagement through various artistic projects and events. James Thurman noted that many local artists and volunteers have contributed to the Halloween celebrations, fostering creativity and collaboration.

What’s next

The Thurmans plan to continue their involvement in Denton’s Halloween celebrations and hope to create more public art projects in the future.

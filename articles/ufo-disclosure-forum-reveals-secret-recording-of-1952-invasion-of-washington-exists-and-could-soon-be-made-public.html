UFO Disclosure Forum Reveals Secret Recording of 1952 'Invasion of Washington' Exists and Could Soon Be Made Public

A previously undisclosed recording linked to the 1952 'Invasion of Washington' UFO incidents could soon be made public after a US Congressman announced an Air Force briefing preserved by MIT Lincoln Laboratory is set to be handed over. The claim emerged during a UFO disclosure forum held in Washington, DC, where lawmakers and transparency advocates called for greater public access to government records related to unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAP).

The 1952 'Invasion of Washington' is one of the most closely examined UFO episodes in US history. Over two weekends in July that year, unidentified objects appeared on radar over Washington, DC, while airline pilots and military personnel reported seeing unusual lights in the sky. The incidents led to the US Air Force scrambling fighter jets, which attracted national media attention. Officials later attributed the sightings to atmospheric conditions.

Key facts

  • Event: 1952 'Invasion of Washington' UFO incidents
  • Date: July 1952
  • Location: Washington, DC
  • Number of objects: Up to 12 unidentified objects detected simultaneously
  • Key sources: Representative Eric Burlison, MIT Lincoln Laboratory
  • Current status: Recording yet to be made public

Timeline

In July 1952, multiple unidentified objects appeared on radar screens over Washington, DC, prompting reports from commercial pilots and military personnel regarding unusual lights in the sky. The US Air Force scrambled interceptor jets in response to the sightings, which garnered significant media attention.

Following the incidents, the Air Force held a press conference attributing the sightings to unusual weather conditions and temperature inversions. Despite this explanation, the case has remained a prominent topic in UFO discussions over the decades.

During a recent UFO disclosure forum in Washington, Representative Eric Burlison announced that MIT Lincoln Laboratory has agreed to release a recording of an Air Force briefing related to the 1952 incidents.

Official statements

“That would be important historical information to have,” said Representative Eric Burlison, highlighting the significance of the recording.
“The people are very frustrated when they find their government thinks that they should keep information from the people,” Burlison added, emphasizing the need for transparency regarding UAP.

Context

The UFO disclosure forum focused on efforts to increase transparency surrounding UAP investigations. Lawmakers, including Senator Mike Rounds, pledged to revive proposed legislation to protect UAP whistleblowers. Representative Anna Paulina Luna is collaborating with the White House on immunity measures for individuals claiming to possess information about recovered non-human technology.

Polling data from the Disclosure Foundation indicates that nearly 90% of both Republican and Democratic respondents want the government to disclose more information about UAP, with nearly 70% believing UAP exist.

What’s next

For now, the recording related to the 1952 'Invasion of Washington' remains out of public view. Its potential release will determine whether it contains routine historical material or new information that adds context to this significant UFO episode.

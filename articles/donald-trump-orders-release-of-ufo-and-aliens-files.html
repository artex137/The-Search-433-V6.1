Donald Trump Orders Release of UFO and Aliens Files

Former President Donald Trump has announced he will direct the Pentagon and other federal agencies to release government files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life, though details and timing remain unclear. Trump made the announcement on February 23, 2026, via a post on Truth Social.

This directive comes amid renewed public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) following recent comments by former President Barack Obama about the existence of aliens. The Pentagon has been investigating UFO reports through the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office, which has stated it found no evidence of alien life in its 2024 report.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: February 23, 2026
  • Location: United States
  • Agencies involved: Pentagon, All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office
  • Number of UAP reports received as of 2024: 1,652
  • Previous report findings: No verified evidence of extraterrestrial life

Timeline

In 2017, the Pentagon released UFO images, sparking public curiosity about unidentified aerial phenomena. In 2023, a House hearing featured military veterans discussing UAP sightings and their potential national security implications. On February 23, 2026, Trump announced his directive for the release of related government files.

In a recent interview, Obama stated, “They’re real but I haven’t seen them,” later clarifying that he was referring to the statistical likelihood of life in the universe. Trump responded to Obama’s remarks aboard Air Force One, questioning the validity of the statements and accusing Obama of disclosing classified information.

Official statements

“I will instruct the Secretary of War and other relevant Departments and Agencies to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs), and any and all other information connected to these highly complex, but extremely interesting and important, matters.” – Donald Trump
“Some cases still lacked plausible explanations and required more data and analysis.” – Jon Kosloski, director of the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office

Context

Public fascination with UFOs has increased in recent years, often fueled by conspiracy theories alleging government concealment of evidence regarding alien life. The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office has noted that while many UAP incidents can be attributed to mundane causes, some remain unexplained and warrant further investigation.

What’s next

It is unclear when the government files will be made public or what specific information they will contain. Investigations into unresolved UAP incidents are expected to continue as agencies prepare to comply with Trump’s directive.

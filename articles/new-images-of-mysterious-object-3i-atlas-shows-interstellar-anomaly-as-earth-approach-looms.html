New images of 'mysterious object' 3I/ATLAS shows 'interstellar anomaly' as Earth approach looms

A mysterious object, designated as 3I/ATLAS, has shown an "anti-tail" for the first time as it approaches Earth, according to observations made last week. The object, suspected to be an alien spacecraft, has been photographed by Austrian amateur astronomer Michael Jaeger, who noted its complex tail structure.

This discovery matters because it raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS and its potential origins. Authorities and experts are monitoring the object closely as it travels through the solar system, with further analysis expected in the coming weeks.

Key facts

  • Object designated as 3I/ATLAS, first spotted on July 1, 2025.
  • Photographed by Michael Jaeger, an amateur astronomer, early on a recent morning.
  • Comet is currently seven to 10 degrees above the horizon.
  • Experts believe it has been traveling for billions of years.
  • Theoretical physicist Avi Loeb has suggested it could be an extraterrestrial artifact.

Timeline

The object 3I/ATLAS was first observed on July 1, 2025. It moved behind the sun last week, and fresh images were captured shortly after, revealing its complex tail structure. Jaeger has been monitoring the object since last Tuesday.

Official statements

"We have something unusual to report. 3I/ATLAS showed a complex tail structure early this morning," said Michael Jaeger, who captured the images.
"We see more than seven jets coming out of this object. Of course, there are two possibilities that we have considered all along," stated Avi Loeb regarding the object's nature.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third identified object from outside the solar system to be observed entering our solar system. Previous observations of similar objects have led to speculation regarding their origins and compositions.

What’s next

Experts will continue to analyze the object's characteristics in the coming weeks to determine whether it is a natural comet or a technological artifact. Observations will focus on the behavior of the jets and the integrity of the object as it moves through space.

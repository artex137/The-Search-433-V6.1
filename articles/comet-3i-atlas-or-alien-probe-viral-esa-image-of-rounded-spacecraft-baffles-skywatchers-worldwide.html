Comet 3I/ATLAS or Alien Probe? Viral ESA Image of 'Rounded Spacecraft' Baffles Skywatchers Worldwide

A viral image of Comet 3I/ATLAS, released by the European Space Agency (ESA), has sparked confusion among skywatchers worldwide, with many claiming it resembles a spacecraft rather than a comet. The image has circulated online since it was published on October 17, 2023, leading to widespread speculation about the object's true nature.

This incident has drawn significant attention to Comet 3I/ATLAS, which is currently in the vicinity of Earth. Authorities, including the ESA, are monitoring public interest and have released statements clarifying the object's characteristics. The ESA has emphasized that it is a comet, despite the viral image's striking appearance.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed since October 17, 2023.
  • The ESA published the viral image that has raised questions about the comet's identity.
  • Public interest has surged, with many speculating about the object's nature.
  • ESA officials have reiterated that the object is a comet.

Timeline

On October 17, 2023, the European Space Agency released an image of Comet 3I/ATLAS. Following its publication, the image quickly gained traction on social media, leading to discussions among astronomers and the public alike. As the speculation grew, the ESA responded by clarifying the object's identity.

Official statements

“Despite its appearance, we can confirm that 3I/ATLAS is a comet,” an ESA spokesperson stated in response to the viral image.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been a subject of interest since it was first identified. Previous comets have similarly sparked public fascination and speculation, often leading to discussions about their characteristics and origins. The ESA has dealt with similar incidents in the past, reinforcing the importance of scientific clarity in public communications.

What’s next

The ESA will continue to monitor public interest in Comet 3I/ATLAS and provide updates as necessary. Further observations and research may clarify any remaining questions about the comet's characteristics.

Comet 3I/ATLAS surprises with high abundance of methanol detected by ALMA

Researchers have detected a high abundance of methanol in Comet 3I/ATLAS using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile. This discovery was made during observations conducted in March 2026.

This finding is significant as it provides insights into the chemical composition of comets and their role in the formation of the solar system. Scientists are analyzing the data to understand the implications of methanol presence and what it reveals about the comet's origin.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed by ALMA in March 2026.
  • High levels of methanol detected in the comet.
  • ALMA is located in the Atacama Desert, Chile.
  • Research contributes to understanding the solar system's formation.

Timeline

In March 2026, ALMA conducted observations of Comet 3I/ATLAS. During this time, researchers identified the significant presence of methanol in the comet's composition.

Official statements

“The detection of methanol in Comet 3I/ATLAS challenges our understanding of cometary chemistry,” said Dr. Emily Chen, an astrophysicist involved in the study.

Context

This detection follows previous findings of organic molecules in other comets, which have sparked interest in the role of comets in delivering water and organic compounds to Earth.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the data from ALMA to gather more information about Comet 3I/ATLAS and its implications for solar system studies.

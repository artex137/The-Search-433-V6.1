Hubble Detects Anti-Correlated Jet Flips On 3I/ATLAS Pointing To A Controlled Rotational System

Published 2025-12-28 19:41 UTC

Hubble Detects Anti-Correlated Jet Flips On 3I/ATLAS

NASA’s Hubble Space Telescope has captured images of interstellar object 3I/ATLAS on December 12 and December 27, revealing stable, double-jet structures that exhibit unexpected brightness changes. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to this astronomical event.

This discovery is significant because it suggests a controlled rotational system rather than random outgassing, indicating a repeating rhythm from this interstellar visitor. Scientists are closely monitoring the behavior of the jets to understand the underlying mechanisms at play.

Key facts

  • Observations made on December 12 and December 27, 2023.
  • NASA's Hubble Space Telescope conducted the imaging.
  • 3I/ATLAS displays a stable double-jet structure.
  • One jet brightens while the other fades in a repeatable pattern.
  • The phenomenon is described as anti-correlation.

Timeline

On December 12, NASA's Hubble Space Telescope captured high-resolution images of 3I/ATLAS, marking the first observation of the interstellar object in this series. The images revealed a stable double-jet structure.

On December 27, Hubble made a second observation, confirming that as one jet brightened, the other faded. This clean, repeatable pattern indicated a potential shared mechanism governing the jets.

Official statements

“The swap is clean, repeatable, and geometrically consistent. This is not random outgassing. It is anti-correlation,” said a scientist involved in the analysis.

Context

Prior observations of 3I/ATLAS indicated the presence of a sunward anti-tail, a feature that has remained consistent throughout the year. This current behavior of the secondary jet, which persists and returns in a structured manner, adds a new layer of understanding to the dynamics of the interstellar object.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to study 3I/ATLAS to further investigate the mechanisms behind the observed jet behavior and to improve understanding of interstellar objects.

Japan’s 3I/ATLAS Image Sparks Alien Origins Buzz Over Mysterious Interstellar Comet: Here’s What We Know

A stunning image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has reportedly been released by Japan’s space agency, captivating the astronomy community since its circulation on November 7, 2025. The image, created using spectral and imaging data, has not yet received official confirmation from Japan’s space authorities.

This development has reignited interest in the comet, which was discovered on July 1, 2025, by NASA’s Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile. The comet is the third interstellar object ever detected and offers a rare opportunity for scientific study.

Key facts

  • Image of 3I/ATLAS circulated on social media on November 7, 2025.
  • Discovered on July 1, 2025, by NASA's ATLAS in Rio Hurtado, Chile.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object detected, following 1IʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • Closest approach to Earth will occur on December 19, 2025, at a distance of approximately 270 million kilometers.
  • China's Tianwen-1 orbiter captured close-up images of the comet in early October 2025.
  • NASA and ESA state 3I/ATLAS behaves like a natural interstellar comet.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, NASA's ATLAS telescope discovered 3I/ATLAS in Rio Hurtado, Chile. The comet's hyperbolic orbital path confirmed its interstellar origin. In October 2025, China's Mars orbiter Tianwen-1 captured its first close-up images as the comet passed near Mars, providing scientists with valuable data. The image allegedly released by Japan’s space agency appeared on social media on November 7, 2025, but remains unverified.

Official statements

“This is the most realistic image available so far,” said a post shared on social media regarding the 3I/ATLAS image.
“The unusual trajectory and composition of the comet could suggest it was of artificial origin,” said Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb, referencing speculation surrounding the comet.

Context

3I/ATLAS is significant as it is the third interstellar object detected, following the discoveries of 1IʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Previous interstellar objects have sparked curiosity about their origins and implications for planetary formation. The interest in 3I/ATLAS has been heightened by recent comments regarding potential alien technology, although scientists maintain it exhibits characteristics of a natural comet.

What’s next

NASA has confirmed that 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth. As the comet approaches, astronomers will continue to study its composition and trajectory, with its closest approach scheduled for December 19, 2025.

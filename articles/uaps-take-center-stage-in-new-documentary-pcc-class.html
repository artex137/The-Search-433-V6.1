UAPs take center stage in new documentary, PCC class

Portland Community College (PCC) has introduced a new class focused on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs) alongside the release of the documentary "The Age of Disclosure," which examines the mysteries surrounding these objects. The film premiered this weekend, coinciding with the opening of the musical "Wicked For Good" in Portland, Oregon.

This documentary and the accompanying class are significant as they aim to engage the public in discussions about UAPs, a topic that has gained traction in recent years. Brian Anslinger, a PCC instructor and executive producer of 'Everyday Northwest,' has been actively promoting the class and the film, emphasizing the need for greater understanding of these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Documentary title: "The Age of Disclosure"
  • Location: Portland Community College, Portland, Oregon
  • Instructor: Brian Anslinger
  • Class focus: Unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs)
  • Related event: Opening of "Wicked For Good" musical

Timeline

This weekend, "The Age of Disclosure" premiered in theatres, capturing the attention of audiences interested in UAPs. Concurrently, PCC launched a new class aimed at educating students about these mysterious objects.

Brian Anslinger has participated in promotional activities, including an appearance on AM Extra, where he discussed the relevance of the class and the documentary.

Official statements

“We want to break it all down and provide a deeper understanding of UAPs,” said Brian Anslinger, emphasizing the importance of the subject.

Context

The rise in interest regarding UAPs has been noted in various media and academic circles. The introduction of this class at PCC reflects a growing trend in educational institutions to explore unconventional topics that captivate public curiosity.

What’s next

As the documentary gains traction, PCC plans to continue offering courses on UAPs, aiming to foster informed discussions and further research on this enigmatic subject.

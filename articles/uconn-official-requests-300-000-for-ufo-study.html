UConn official requests $300,000 for UFO study

A UConn official has requested $300,000 to conduct a study on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), commonly known as UFOs, during a public hearing held by Connecticut’s legislative Appropriations Committee on March 12, 2023. The request is part of a proposed bill, HB 5422, which mandates UConn researchers to collaborate with various state departments and submit a report to the General Assembly by July 1, 2027.

The proposed legislation aims to centralize the study of UFO sightings and improve public policy related to aerial phenomena. According to Rep. Joe Hoxha (R-Bristol), the bill responds to a growing interest among government and public sectors to better understand these occurrences. “There is a growing contingent of people both inside and outside of government that want to study and better understand what goes on in our skies,” Hoxha stated in his written testimony.

Key facts

  • Event: Public hearing on HB 5422
  • Date: March 12, 2023
  • Request: $300,000 for UAP study
  • Bill sponsor: Rep. Joe Hoxha (R-Bristol)
  • Deadline for report: July 1, 2027
  • Collaborating agencies: DESPP, DEEP, Office of Military Affairs

Timeline

On March 12, 2023, Connecticut’s legislative Appropriations Committee held a public hearing on HB 5422. UConn officials requested funding for a study on UAP, aiming to address public interest and scientific inquiry into UFO sightings. The bill proposes collaboration with several state departments and a nonprofit organization focused on UFO investigations.

During the hearing, various testimonies were presented, highlighting different motivations for the study, including scientific curiosity and public policy implications. UConn’s assistant dean, Kylene Perras, outlined financial constraints affecting the university's ability to absorb new mandates without impacting core academic priorities.

Official statements

“There is a growing contingent of people both inside and outside of government that want to study and better understand what goes on in our skies.” - Rep. Joe Hoxha
“Much of the data maintained by DESPP is sensitive and restricted to law enforcement... we respectfully request that provisions be removed from the bill to avoid potential conflicts with existing data access restrictions.” - Commissioner Ronnell A. Higgins, DESPP
“Like many public institutions, UConn is operating under significant financial constraints... these measures already limit our capacity to absorb new unfunded mandates without impacting core academic and research priorities.” - Kylene Perras, UConn

Context

This proposed study would place UConn among a small number of U.S. institutions engaging in research on UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Other notable programs include Harvard University’s Galileo Project and Pennsylvania State University’s Extraterrestrial Intelligence Center. The bill comes amid increasing public and governmental interest in UFO sightings and their implications.

What’s next

If passed, the bill will require UConn to complete the study within available resources. The Appropriations Committee will review the proposal and consider the funding request as part of the legislative process.

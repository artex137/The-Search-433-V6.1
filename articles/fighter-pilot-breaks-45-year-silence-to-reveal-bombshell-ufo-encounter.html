Fighter pilot breaks 45-year silence to reveal bombshell UFO encounter

A former U.S. fighter pilot has come forward to disclose a significant UFO encounter from December 26, 1980, near a nuclear base in England. Dan Isbell, 69, has revealed his experience with a 15-metre triangular craft surrounded by multicoloured 'plasma' light, adding to the intrigue surrounding the incident dubbed 'the British Roswell'. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this encounter.

This revelation is important as it aligns with another sighting involving U.S. Air Force personnel at RAF Woodbridge in Suffolk on the same day. These personnel reportedly observed a similar craft descending into neighbouring Rendlesham Forest, where they claimed to have gotten close enough to touch the metallic object. Authorities are reviewing the historical accounts in light of Isbell's testimony.

Key facts

  • Incident date: December 26, 1980
  • Witness: Dan Isbell, 69, former U.S. fighter pilot
  • Location: Near a nuclear base in England
  • Size of craft: 15 metres (50 feet) triangular
  • Related incident: RAF Woodbridge sighting on the same day

Timeline

On December 26, 1980, Dan Isbell encountered a triangular craft while stationed near a nuclear base in England. At the same time, personnel at RAF Woodbridge witnessed a similar craft descending into Rendlesham Forest. The two incidents have been linked due to their proximity and timing.

Isbell has since broken his silence after 45 years, shedding light on his experience and adding credibility to the claims made by the RAF personnel. This has prompted renewed interest in the events of that day.

Official statements

“I saw a craft surrounded by multicoloured light. It was unlike anything I had ever seen," Isbell said.
“The incidents at both bases on that day have been discussed for decades. Isbell's account provides additional context,” a military historian commented.

Context

The 1980 UFO incident near the U.S. nuclear base has been a subject of speculation and intrigue, often referred to as 'the British Roswell'. Previous investigations have failed to provide conclusive explanations, and Isbell's testimony adds a new layer to the ongoing discourse surrounding military encounters with unidentified aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to re-examine the historical data related to the 1980 incidents. Investigations may include reviewing military records and conducting interviews with other witnesses to gain further insights into the events of that day.

NASA’s New Telescope SPHEREx Observes Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS in Infrared

NASA’s SPHEREx mission has observed the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from August 7 to August 15, 2025. This marks a significant achievement for the mission, which launched in March 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this astronomical event.

The observations of comet 3I/ATLAS are important because they provide new insights into the comet's physical and chemical properties. Authorities from NASA have stated that these findings will help scientists understand interstellar visitors and the early history of planetary systems beyond our own.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS observed from August 7 to August 15, 2025.
  • SPHEREx launched in March 2025.
  • Comet emits approximately 940 trillion trillion CO₂ molecules per second.
  • Data collected will contribute to understanding cometary physics and chemistry.
  • Comet was first spotted by the ATLAS telescope in July 2025.

Timeline

On March 2025, NASA launched the SPHEREx mission. The comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2025 by the ATLAS telescope in Chile. SPHEREx began its observations of the comet from August 7 to August 15, 2025, contributing to a coordinated effort with the Hubble and James Webb space telescopes.

Official statements

“SPHEREx’s observations are already proving vital, offering fresh insights into the comet’s composition, structure, and trajectory,” NASA stated.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable for its unique characteristics, differing from most comets known to date. Unlike typical comets that develop bright tails as they approach the Sun, 3I/ATLAS is primarily powered by carbon dioxide and lacks a traditional tail, instead surrounded by a glowing CO₂ fog.

What’s next

The data collected from SPHEREx and its partner telescopes will continue to be analyzed, providing a lasting legacy of insights into the secrets of cometary origins and interstellar phenomena.

Are Aliens Real? Barack Obama Says ‘Yes’, But Denies Area 51 Secrets

Former US president Barack Obama has stated that he believes extraterrestrial life exists, but he has no evidence to support this claim. In a podcast interview released on Saturday with YouTuber Brian Tyler Cowen, Obama addressed the existence of aliens, saying, “They’re real.” However, he clarified that he has never personally seen any evidence of aliens.

Obama also discussed popular conspiracy theories surrounding Area 51, a highly classified US Air Force base in Groom Lake, Nevada. He stated, “But I haven’t seen them. They’re not being kept at Area 51,” suggesting that if such evidence existed, it would not be concealed from the President of the United States. His remarks have reignited public interest in the topic of extraterrestrial life and government transparency regarding unidentified aerial phenomena.

Key facts

  • Interview released on Saturday, date not disclosed.
  • Podcast with YouTuber Brian Tyler Cowen.
  • Obama confirmed belief in extraterrestrial life but denied knowledge of evidence or its location.
  • Area 51 is a US Air Force base located in Nevada.
  • Obama previously commented on UFOs in a 2021 interview on The Late Late Show with James Corden.

Timeline

In 2021, Obama discussed UFOs on The Late Late Show, noting that there are things he could not disclose about aliens. He confirmed the authenticity of UAP sightings and acknowledged that the US government has not identified their origins. In the recent podcast, released on Saturday, he reiterated his belief in the existence of extraterrestrial life while denying any connections to Area 51.

Official statements

“They’re real.” — Barack Obama, confirming his belief in extraterrestrial life.
“But I haven’t seen them. They’re not being kept at Area 51.” — Barack Obama, addressing conspiracy theories.

Context

Area 51 has long been the subject of speculation regarding the concealment of alien technology and remains. Public curiosity about extraterrestrial life has increased significantly following the US government's release of documents related to unexplained aircraft sightings, now referred to as Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs). Leaked radar footage from US Air Force Reaper drones has added to this discourse, showing UAPs over the Middle East.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any immediate investigations or actions regarding the recent public statements by Obama, but the ongoing discussions about UAPs and government transparency are likely to continue.

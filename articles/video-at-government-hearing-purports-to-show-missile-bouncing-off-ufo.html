Video at government hearing purports to show missile bouncing off UFO

A video presented by U.S. Rep. Eric Burlison during a House Government Oversight subcommittee hearing on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena shows a U.S. missile striking an unidentified object. The event took place on October 30, 2024, off the coast of Yemen. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities linked to the incident.

This video matters as it raises questions about unidentified flying objects and government transparency. Rep. Burlison has expressed concern about the lack of information regarding such phenomena, highlighting the need for greater accountability from defense officials.

Key facts

  • Video presented on October 30, 2024
  • Location: Off the coast of Yemen
  • Type of drone: MQ-9 Reaper
  • Missile type: Struck the unidentified object
  • Presented by: U.S. Rep. Eric Burlison, R-Mo
  • U.S. defense officials declined to comment

Timeline

On October 30, 2024, during a House Government Oversight subcommittee hearing, Rep. Eric Burlison revealed a video showing an MQ-9 Reaper drone tracking an unidentified object above waves off Yemen. The video depicts a missile fired from another Reaper drone striking the object, which continues to fly after being hit.

In the hearing, Rep. Burlison questioned the ongoing lack of information surrounding unidentified objects, stating, “Why are we being blocked from this information consistently?”

Official statements

“Why are we being blocked from this information consistently?” - U.S. Rep. Eric Burlison

Context

This incident is part of a growing focus on unidentified flying objects and Anomalous Phenomena within government hearings. The disclosure of such videos has sparked public interest and calls for transparency regarding military encounters with unidentified objects.

What’s next

Investigations into the incident may continue, although specific next steps or timelines have not been disclosed. U.S. defense officials have not provided additional comments regarding the video or the object shown in it.

3I/ATLAS: Aliens or rare comet?

The comet 3I/ATLAS, traveling at a speed of 130,000 miles per hour, has generated significant attention on social media since its discovery on Thursday, 20 November 2025. Notably, celebrity Kim Kardashian has expressed interest in the interstellar object, which has raised questions about its origins and nature.

This event matters as it highlights ongoing public fascination with celestial phenomena and the potential for scientific discovery. NASA has conducted investigations into the comet and its characteristics, aiming to provide clarity on its classification and implications for future research.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Speed: 130,000 mph
  • Date of discovery: 20 November 2025
  • Notable interest from: Kim Kardashian
  • Investigating agency: NASA

Timeline

On 20 November 2025, NASA reported on the discovery of 3I/ATLAS, noting its rapid speed and potential interstellar origins. The comet's unusual characteristics have sparked public interest and speculation.

Following the announcement, social media platforms have seen a surge in discussions regarding the comet, with various theories emerging about its nature and origins.

Official statements

“NASA has conducted investigations into the comet and its characteristics,” said Sky's Thomas Moore, highlighting the importance of understanding such celestial phenomena.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS adds to a growing list of interstellar objects that have been identified in recent years. These occurrences have prompted increased interest from both scientists and the public regarding the nature of objects entering our solar system.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue its investigations into 3I/ATLAS to determine its origins and any potential implications for future research in the field of astronomy.

Veterans and Whistleblowers Testify on UAPs Before House Oversight Committee

Military veterans and whistleblowers have testified before a House Oversight Committee panel on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs) on Tuesday morning, addressing the Pentagon’s disclosures about UFOs. The hearing took place at 10 a.m. EDT.

This hearing matters as it aims to enhance transparency regarding UAP sightings and their potential threats to safety. Rep. Anna Paulina Luna (R-Fla.), chair of the committee’s Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets, has emphasized the need for maximum transparency from the federal government.

Key facts

  • Event Date: Tuesday, 10 a.m. EDT
  • Location: House Oversight Committee panel
  • Focus: Pentagon disclosures and the All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office
  • Chair: Rep. Anna Paulina Luna (R-Fla.)

Timeline

The hearing was organized to discuss the effectiveness of the Pentagon’s All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office and its handling of UAP information. The event was designed to provide veterans and whistleblowers a platform to share their experiences and insights regarding UAPs.

Official statements

“The American people deserve maximum transparency from the federal government on sightings, acquisitions, and examinations of UAPs and whether they pose a potential threat to Americans’ safety,” said Rep. Anna Paulina Luna (R-Fla.).

Context

This hearing follows a growing interest in UAPs and the government’s response to them. The Pentagon has faced increasing pressure to disclose information related to UAPs, particularly regarding their potential implications for national security.

What’s next

Further investigations into UAP sightings and the effectiveness of related government offices are expected to continue as the committee seeks to enhance transparency and address public concerns.

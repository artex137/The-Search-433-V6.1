Pilot Sees 'Silver Canister' UFO; ATC: 'Good Luck With Aliens'

A pilot has reported seeing a "silver canister" UFO while flying over the Pacific Ocean near San Diego on December 21, 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This sighting has drawn attention to ongoing discussions about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and their implications for aviation safety. The Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) has stated that it is reviewing the incident, but further details have not been disclosed.

Key facts

  • Incident date: December 21, 2025
  • Location: Pacific Ocean, near San Diego
  • Type of object reported: "Silver canister" UFO
  • Agency involved: Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)
  • Injuries/fatalities: None reported

Timeline

On December 21, 2025, a pilot flying over the Pacific Ocean reported seeing an unidentified flying object described as a "silver canister." The pilot communicated the sighting to air traffic control, which responded with a light-hearted remark, "Good luck with aliens."

The FAA has acknowledged the report and is currently reviewing the details of the incident to assess any potential impact on aviation safety.

Official statements

“Good luck with aliens,” air traffic control reportedly told the pilot following the sighting.

Context

This incident adds to the growing number of reported UAP sightings in recent years. Authorities have been increasingly focused on understanding these phenomena and their implications for air travel. The FAA and other agencies have started to take a more proactive approach in investigating such reports.

What’s next

The FAA will continue its review of the incident and may provide further updates as more information becomes available. The agency has not disclosed a timeline for the investigation's completion.

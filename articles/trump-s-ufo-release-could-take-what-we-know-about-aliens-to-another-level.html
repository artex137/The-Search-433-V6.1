Trump's UFO release could take what we know about aliens to 'another level'

Published 2026-03-15 11:03 UTC

Trump's UFO Release Could Change Understanding of Extraterrestrial Life

The US government is preparing to release files related to aliens and unidentified flying objects (UFOs), following an announcement by President Donald Trump last month. The release aims to provide transparency regarding the government’s knowledge of extraterrestrial life and phenomena. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this announcement.

This release matters as it could potentially reshape public discourse on extraterrestrial life. Experts, including Christopher Mellon, a former US Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defence for Intelligence, have suggested that the information may elevate the ongoing debate about aliens. Trump has expressed uncertainty about the existence of extraterrestrial life, stating, "I don't know if they're real or not."

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: February 2023
  • President: Donald Trump
  • Expert: Christopher Mellon
  • Department involved: Secretary of War and other relevant Departments and Agencies
  • Process for release: Requires review by trained security officers
  • Potential contents: Satellite imagery and gun camera videos of unidentified aerial phenomena

Timeline

On February 23, 2023, Trump announced via Truth Social that he would direct relevant agencies to begin identifying and releasing government files related to alien life and UFOs. Following this, the Office of the Director of National Intelligence indicated that these documents would be declassified "soon."

Christopher Mellon has noted that the declassification process may be lengthy due to the bureaucratic review required for sensitive materials. He emphasized that files must be examined thoroughly by those familiar with their original classification.

Official statements

"Typically, files would have to go to a trained security officer who understands the laws and understands the equities involved, and then they have to review it." — Christopher Mellon, former US Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defence for Intelligence
"We have satellite imagery of craft that sure don’t look like anything that we have built or constructed." — Christopher Mellon

Context

This announcement follows a trend of increasing public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life, particularly after former President Barack Obama acknowledged the reality of unidentified aerial phenomena. The push for transparency around government files on these topics has gained momentum in recent years, reflecting a broader cultural shift toward accepting the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

What’s next

The declassification process is expected to take time, with experts urging congressional oversight to ensure a thorough review. Further updates on the release timeline and content of the files remain pending.

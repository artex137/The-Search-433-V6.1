X-Files coming true? Trump orders release of government UFO and alien documents

US President Donald Trump has ordered federal agencies to begin releasing government files related to UFOs and aliens on Thursday, a move long sought by the public. The announcement came via Trump’s Truth Social platform and does not mention any injuries or fatalities.

This directive aims to address the significant public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and extraterrestrial life. Trump instructed the Secretary of War and other relevant departments to identify and disclose files connected to these matters, emphasizing their complexity and importance.

Key facts

  • Announcement made on Thursday, date not disclosed.
  • Trump directed federal agencies to begin the process of releasing files.
  • Files will relate to UFOs, UAP, and extraterrestrial life.
  • No details provided on whether classified documents will be included.
  • Trump mentioned former President Barack Obama’s recent remarks about classified information.
  • Trump’s age: 79.

Timeline

On Thursday, February 20, 2026, Trump revealed his plans for federal agencies to start identifying and releasing UFO and alien-related documents. Earlier that day, Obama had made statements during a podcast about the existence of extraterrestrial life, which prompted Trump's comments on classified information.

Trump's remarks included a claim that Obama "gave classified information" and suggested he had made a "big mistake." Trump has expressed uncertainty regarding the existence of aliens, stating, "I don't know if they are real or not."

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life,” Trump posted on Truth Social.
“He [Obama] made a big mistake,” Trump told reporters, referring to Obama’s comments about extraterrestrial life.

Context

Interest in UFOs and UAP has surged in recent years, especially as the US government investigates numerous reports of unusual aircraft. In March 2024, the Pentagon released a report stating it had found no evidence that UAP were alien technology, attributing many sightings to weather balloons, spy planes, and other known phenomena.

Obama’s remarks during a podcast have sparked renewed discussions about the government's transparency regarding UFOs, with many advocates pressing for the release of information for decades.

What’s next

Federal agencies are expected to begin the process of identifying and releasing the requested files, although specific timelines and details are not disclosed. Investigations into UAP will likely continue as public interest remains high.

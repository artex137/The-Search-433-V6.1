Mysterious new object found between 3I/ATLAS and Earth

Astronomers have detected a new unidentified body, named C/2025 V1 Borisov, located between Earth and the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The discovery was made recently, and the object is currently approximately 105 million kilometres from Earth.

This finding is significant as it revives discussions about the nature of interstellar objects. Authorities and astronomers are observing C/2025 V1 Borisov closely, as it is expected to come within about 103 million kilometres of Earth on November 11, 2025. This distance is deemed safe but offers a unique opportunity for observation using telescopes.

Key facts

  • Object name: C/2025 V1 Borisov
  • Current distance from Earth: 105 million kilometres
  • Closest approach to Earth: November 11, 2025 (103 million kilometres)
  • Expected perihelion (closest point to the Sun): November 16, 2025
  • Light from Borisov takes approximately six minutes to reach Earth
  • Visibility: May be observable with medium-sized telescopes under good weather conditions

Timeline

In 2017, astronomers confirmed the first interstellar object, ‘Oumuamua. This was followed by 2I/Borisov in 2019. The comet 3I/ATLAS was identified in 2025, generating various theories about its origins.

Recently, C/2025 V1 Borisov was detected, prompting renewed interest in interstellar objects. It follows a steep and elongated orbit with an eccentricity of 1.009 and an inclination of about 113 degrees, suggesting it traverses the Solar System at an uncommon angle for comets.

Official statements

Avi Loeb, Harvard professor: “3I/ATLAS is a strange object... there is a 30 to 40 per cent chance it might not be naturally formed.”
NASA spokesperson: “The release of sharper images captured by the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter was postponed due to the temporary US government shutdown.”

Context

The discovery of C/2025 V1 Borisov adds to the ongoing study of interstellar objects. Earlier discussions have centred around the origins and classifications of these bodies, particularly in light of previous findings like 3I/ATLAS, which sparked theories about alien origins.

Despite speculation regarding the nature of C/2025 V1 Borisov, scientists largely believe it is a natural fragment within our Solar System.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to monitor C/2025 V1 Borisov as it approaches its perihelion. Observations are expected to provide further insights into its composition and trajectory.

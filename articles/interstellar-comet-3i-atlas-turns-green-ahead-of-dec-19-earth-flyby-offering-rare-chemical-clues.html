Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS Turns Green Ahead of Dec. 19 Earth Flyby, Offering Rare Chemical Clues

Astronomers have reported that the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has brightened and developed a faint green glow as it approaches its closest pass by Earth on December 19. This event marks significant scientific interest in the comet, which is only the third confirmed visitor from beyond the solar system.

The color change, observed in late November, may provide insights into the comet's chemical makeup and thermal evolution as it reacts to increasing solar heat. Scientists are closely analyzing this phenomenon to better understand the characteristics of interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Closest Earth pass: December 19, 2023
  • Color change observed: Late November 2023
  • Significance: Only third confirmed interstellar comet

Timeline

In late November 2023, astronomers observed a notable brightening and a faint green glow of comet 3I/ATLAS. As the comet approaches Earth, scientists have intensified their analysis of the color change to gain insights into its composition and behavior.

Official statements

Astronomers tracking the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS have noted that the color shift is a potential indicator of the comet's chemical makeup and thermal evolution.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of only three confirmed interstellar comets to have entered the solar system, providing a unique opportunity for scientists to study materials from outside our solar neighborhood. Previous interstellar visitors have contributed to our understanding of the diversity of celestial bodies.

What’s next

As the December 19 flyby approaches, astronomers will continue to monitor the comet and analyze data to unlock further secrets about its origin and composition.

Comet C/2023 A3 set to be visible to the naked eye in late 2024

Astronomers have reported that comet C/2023 A3, also known as Tsuchinshan-ATLAS, is on a trajectory that will bring it close to Earth and the Sun in late 2024, offering a rare opportunity for naked-eye observation. The comet, discovered in January 2023, is expected to reach peak brightness between late September and mid-October 2024.

This celestial event is significant because it could be one of the brightest comets seen in decades. Astronomers are closely monitoring its approach to determine how its brightness may vary as it nears the Sun, which will influence its visibility from Earth. As the comet approaches, its structure and composition will be critical in determining its final appearance.

Key facts

  • Comet Name: C/2023 A3 (Tsuchinshan-ATLAS)
  • Discovery Date: January 2023
  • Peak Brightness Period: Late September to mid-October 2024
  • Expected Magnitude: Between 0 and 1
  • Current Location: Between the orbits of Jupiter and Saturn
  • Speed: Approaching the Sun at 57 km/s

Timeline

The comet C/2023 A3 was initially detected by the Tsuchinshan observatory in China and confirmed by the ATLAS asteroid warning system in Africa in January 2023. As it travels through the solar system, its speed has increased, and astronomers have calculated its perihelion, the closest point to the Sun, which will occur in late 2024.

As C/2023 A3 approaches the Sun, its brightness will depend on the amount of gas and dust it releases, a process known as sublimation. The scientific community is excited about the potential for a spectacular show in the night sky.

Official statements

“The brightness of C/2023 A3 will depend on its reaction to the Sun’s heat as it approaches,” said an astronomer from the Tsuchinshan observatory. “We are hopeful for a great display, but the behavior of comets can be unpredictable.”

Context

C/2023 A3 originates from the Oort Cloud, a region of icy bodies at the edge of the solar system. Comets from this area are known for their unpredictable behavior, and past observations have shown that expectations can vary widely. Some comets have surprised astronomers with their brightness, while others have failed to meet predictions.

What’s next

As C/2023 A3 continues its journey, astronomers will monitor its brightness and trajectory closely. Observers are encouraged to find dark areas with minimal light pollution for the best viewing conditions during its peak brightness period in late 2024.

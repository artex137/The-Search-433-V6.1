Scientists Discuss Potential Deflection of Comet 3I/ATLAS

Scientists have assessed the implications of using nuclear devices on comet 3I/ATLAS, which recently made its closest approach to the Sun, coming within approximately 126 million miles (203 million km) of the star. The comet is currently obscured by the Sun from Earth-based telescopes and is expected to reappear for predawn viewers around November 11, with its nearest approach to Earth projected for December 19, at about 168 million miles (270 million km). According to the European Space Agency and NASA, the comet poses no threat to Earth.

This analysis of 3I/ATLAS is significant as it raises questions about planetary defense strategies. The possibility of a comet altering its course towards Earth prompts discussions about whether nuclear devices could be employed to redirect such an object rather than destroy it.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS made its closest pass to the Sun on a date not disclosed.
  • Closest approach to Earth is expected on December 19.
  • Distance from Earth during closest approach: 168 million miles (270 million km).
  • Comet's distance from the Sun: 126 million miles (203 million km).
  • Agencies involved: European Space Agency, NASA.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS, the third known interstellar object, has made its closest approach to the Sun. It is now hidden behind the Sun from Earth-based telescopes but is expected to reappear in early November. The nearest approach to Earth is anticipated in December.

Official statements

“Nuking a comet isn’t about blowing it up,” scientists from Los Alamos National Laboratory said, emphasizing the importance of deflection over destruction.
“Nuclear munitions are by far the most efficient in terms of yield-per-unit-mass launched and are technically mature,” according to a 2009 paper on impact hazard mitigation.

Context

Previous studies have indicated that simply destroying a comet could lead to more dangerous fragments. Instead, scientists advocate for a method known as ablation, where a nuclear device's energy vaporizes part of the comet's surface, providing a push that alters its trajectory.

What’s next

Research continues on the best methods to deflect comets and asteroids. No specific investigations or timelines for action on 3I/ATLAS have been disclosed at this time.

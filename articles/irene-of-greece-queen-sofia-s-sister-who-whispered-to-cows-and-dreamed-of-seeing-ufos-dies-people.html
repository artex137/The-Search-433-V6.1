Princess Irene of Greece Dies at 83

Princess Irene of Greece and Denmark, sister of Queen Sofia, died at the age of 83 on January 15, 2026, at 11:40 a.m. at the Zarzuela Palace in Madrid. The Communication Department of the House of HM the King reported her death in a statement. Irene had been suffering from serious cognitive impairment prior to her passing.

The death of Princess Irene is significant as she was a prominent member of European royalty, known for her unique personality and lifestyle choices. Authorities have indicated that ceremonies for her wake will be organized in Spain, followed by the transfer of her coffin to Greece for burial at the Tatoi cemetery.

Key facts

  • Date of death: January 15, 2026
  • Time of death: 11:40 a.m.
  • Location: Zarzuela Palace, Madrid
  • Age: 83
  • Burial site: Tatoi cemetery, Greece

Timeline

Princess Irene was born on May 11, 1942, during World War II in Cape Town, South Africa. Her family fled Nazism at that time. Following the war, she returned to Greece in 1946 after living in Alexandria, Egypt.

In 1962, her sister Queen Sofia married Juan Carlos of Spain, and two years later, their father, King Paul, died. In 1967, following a military coup led by her brother, King Constantine, the royal family went into exile.

Throughout her life, Irene pursued various interests, including music and archaeology, and became known for her advocacy of animal rights and interest in the paranormal.

Official statements

“Their Majesties the King and Queen and Her Majesty Queen Sofia regret to announce the death of Her Royal Highness Princess Irene of Greece,” the official statement read.

Context

Princess Irene was a member of a royal family with deep historical ties, including connections to five Hellenic sovereigns and multiple European monarchs. Known for her eccentricities, she did not conform to traditional royal expectations, choosing instead to live a life focused on her passions and beliefs.

Her life experiences, including her time in exile and her dedication to various causes, have made her an intriguing figure in the narrative of modern European royalty.

What’s next

Details regarding the ceremonies for Princess Irene's wake in Spain and the timeline for her burial in Greece will be announced by the Communication Department of the House of HM the King.

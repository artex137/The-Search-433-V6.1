When an Interstellar Visitor Arrived Breakthrough Listen Was Ready

On July 1, 2025, astronomers detected Comet 3I/ATLAS, an interstellar object passing through our solar system. This marked it as only the third confirmed interstellar object observed, following ‘Oumuamua in 2017 and Comet Borisov in 2019. There are no reported injuries or fatalities associated with this discovery.

The detection of 3I/ATLAS is significant for ongoing scientific efforts to determine whether extraterrestrial life exists. Breakthrough Listen, a scientific initiative founded by Yuri Milner, coordinated a global response to study the comet using some of the world’s most powerful radio telescopes. The initiative aims to explore one of humanity's oldest questions: Are we alone in the universe?

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was detected on July 1, 2025.
  • It is the third confirmed interstellar object observed.
  • The comet made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, at a distance of 167 million miles.
  • Breakthrough Listen coordinated observations from multiple telescopes across five continents.
  • No artificial radio emissions were detected from 3I/ATLAS.
  • Observations included the Allen Telescope Array, Parkes Observatory, MeerKAT array, and Green Bank Telescope.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers discovered 3I/ATLAS. Within days, Breakthrough Listen organized a coordinated observation effort. The Allen Telescope Array began monitoring the comet shortly after its detection. Observations by the Parkes Observatory, MeerKAT array, and Green Bank Telescope followed.

On December 18, 2025, the Green Bank Telescope conducted its most sensitive observations of 3I/ATLAS, using advanced techniques to identify potential signals of artificial origin. Despite extensive efforts, researchers found no evidence of technology associated with the comet.

Official statements

“In summary, 3I/ATLAS continues to behave as expected from natural astrophysical processes,” the Breakthrough Listen team reported.

Context

Interstellar objects are exceedingly rare, with only three confirmed in human history. Each represents a fragment of a distant solar system. The scientific community agrees that thorough investigation of such objects is warranted, especially as they present unique opportunities to search for extraterrestrial technology.

What’s next

Breakthrough Listen will continue its efforts to scan for signals from other interstellar visitors and refine its methodologies in the search for extraterrestrial intelligence.

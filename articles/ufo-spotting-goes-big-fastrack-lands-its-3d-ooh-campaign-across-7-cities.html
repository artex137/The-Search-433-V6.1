UFO spotting goes big: Fastrack lands its 3D OOH campaign across 7 cities

Fastrack has launched its UFO (Unidentified Fashion Object) Collection with a unique Out-of-Home (OOH) campaign featuring 3D UFO installations across seven Indian cities. The installations appeared on billboards in Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Chennai, Kolkata, and Pune, marking the official debut of the collection.

This campaign highlights Fastrack's innovative marketing approach and aims to engage urban consumers. The installations utilize dynamic lighting to enhance visibility, while the campaign narrative extends to digital platforms through collaborations with fashion creators. Fastrack seeks to reinforce its position in the youth fashion segment through this integrated campaign.

Key facts

  • Campaign launched in seven cities: Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Chennai, Kolkata, and Pune.
  • Billboards feature giant 3D UFO installations and dynamic lighting.
  • Collaborations with fashion creators extend campaign narrative to digital platforms.
  • Campaign developed in partnership with Madison World.
  • Focused on engaging urban audiences and showcasing product storytelling.

Timeline

The campaign was initiated with the introduction of the UFO Collection, which was designed to create an immersive experience for consumers. The OOH installations were strategically placed to attract attention and communicate the collection's story. The campaign's digital narrative has been ongoing, aligning with the physical installations to enhance consumer engagement.

Official statements

“The magic in marketing happens when product, communication, and media come together to not just meet consumer needs, but to inspire them,” said Danny Jacob, Head of Marketing, Fastrack Watches. He added, “This campaign wasn’t just about showcasing a collection—it was about creating a narrative that sparks curiosity and captures attention.”

Context

The UFO Collection represents a shift in Fastrack's marketing strategy, focusing on immersive experiences and innovative displays. Previous campaigns have also leveraged creative storytelling to connect with younger audiences, but this initiative stands out due to its large-scale, interactive installations.

What’s next

Fastrack plans to monitor the campaign's impact and consumer response across the cities, with further digital engagement expected to follow as part of the ongoing narrative. Additional collaborations may also be explored to sustain interest in the UFO Collection.

FBI Joins Multi-Agency Search for Missing Retired Air Force General With UFO Program Ties

The FBI has joined the search for retired Air Force Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland, 68, who went missing from his Albuquerque residence around 11 a.m. on February 27. Authorities have confirmed that McCasland did not take his cell phone with him and has not been in contact with anyone since his disappearance.

The search for McCasland has gained urgency due to an unspecified medical condition, prompting authorities to issue a Silver Alert the day after he was last seen. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office has canvassed over 600 homes in the area for security camera footage, but no confirmed sightings have been reported.

Key facts

  • Missing person: Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland, 68
  • Last seen: February 27, 2026, around 11 a.m.
  • Location: Albuquerque, New Mexico
  • Condition: An unspecified medical issue has increased search urgency
  • Search efforts: Over 600 homes canvassed for security footage
  • Agencies involved: FBI, Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office
  • Contact for tips: Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office Missing Persons Unit at 505-468-7070

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, Maj. Gen. William Neil McCasland walked away from his home in Albuquerque around 11 a.m. He has not been in contact with anyone since. The following day, authorities issued a Silver Alert due to his unspecified medical condition. Investigators have since canvassed more than 600 residences in the surrounding area for any security camera footage that may provide leads on his whereabouts.

Official statements

“This is a man with some of the most sensitive secrets of the United States in his head,” said investigative journalist Ross Coulthart, characterizing McCasland's disappearance as "a grave national security crisis."
Susan McCasland Wilkerson, McCasland's wife, stated on Facebook that he "does not have any special knowledge about the ET bodies and debris from the Roswell crash stored at Wright-Patt" and confirmed he does not suffer from dementia.

Context

McCasland has held several classified military positions, including leadership roles at the Air Force Research Laboratory, which has been associated with rumors regarding extraterrestrial materials from the Roswell incident. His work has included roles at the Pentagon and on the Defense Department's Global Positioning System program. After retiring, he became a consultant for To The Stars, Inc., an organization focused on unidentified aerial phenomena.

His disappearance occurred shortly after President Donald Trump directed the Pentagon to release information regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial life, adding to the intrigue surrounding his case.

What’s next

Investigators continue to search for leads in McCasland's disappearance. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office has stated they have found no evidence of foul play but remain open to all possible scenarios. Tips can be submitted to the Missing Persons Unit as the search progresses.

Task force to push for ‘maximum transparency’ from government on UFOs

A House task force is set to hold a hearing on Tuesday, September 7, 2025, in Washington, D.C., to discuss unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), commonly referred to as UFOs. The hearing will include testimonies from Air Force and Navy veterans, as well as journalist George Knapp. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this event.

The task force aims to promote “maximum transparency” from the government regarding UFOs, according to chairwoman Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna. This initiative follows a previous House Oversight subcommittee hearing where witnesses claimed the government has withheld information about documented UFO cases.

Key facts

  • Date of hearing: September 7, 2025
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Notable witnesses: Air Force and Navy veterans, journalist George Knapp
  • Focus: Government disclosures on UFOs
  • Previous hearing claims: Government withholding information

Timeline

In recent months, discussions surrounding UFOs have intensified, culminating in the formation of a House task force dedicated to addressing the issue. The task force plans to hold its first hearing on September 7, 2025, where it will examine government transparency related to UFO sightings. This hearing follows a previous subcommittee meeting where allegations of information withholding were raised.

Official statements

“We will push for maximum transparency from the government about UFOs,” said Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna.

Context

UAP discussions have gained traction in recent years, particularly as sightings of drones and other unmanned aerial vehicles have increased. This has led to a growing interest in understanding the nature of unidentified aerial phenomena and the government's role in documenting and disclosing related information.

What’s next

The task force will conduct the hearing on September 7, 2025, to gather testimonies and push for greater transparency from the government regarding UFOs. Further investigations and potential follow-up hearings may occur based on the findings presented.

Jeff Bezos, Lauren Sanchez’s $175M Beverly Hills mansion nearly complete with out-of-this-world upgrades – including UFO-like structure

Published 2025-12-16 21:29 UTC

Jeff Bezos and Lauren Sanchez’s Beverly Hills Mansion Nears Completion

Jeff Bezos and Lauren Sanchez have nearly completed their luxurious Beverly Hills mansion, featuring unique amenities, including a UFO-like structure. The property, purchased in 2020 from media mogul David Geffen, has been under construction for five years.

The $165 million sale, later increased by an additional $10 million lot, ranks among the highest real estate transactions in Los Angeles history. The couple’s extravagant upgrades include a futuristic building resembling NASA’s Apollo 11 space capsule, which may serve as a sauna, according to media reports.

Key facts

  • Property purchased in 2020 for $165 million, plus an additional $10 million lot.
  • Construction has lasted five years.
  • The mansion spans 10 acres and features a Georgian-style, three-story main house.
  • The UFO-like structure is a scale replica of the Apollo 11 capsule.
  • Bezos and Sanchez celebrated their nuptials in Venice after a space-themed bachelorette party.

Timeline

In 2020, Jeff Bezos acquired the Beverly Hills property from David Geffen for $165 million. Over the following five years, the couple has transformed the estate, adding various luxurious features. In April 2025, Lauren Sanchez celebrated her bachelorette party with an 11-minute space flight on a Blue Origin rocket. Bezos and Sanchez married in Venice shortly after.

Official statements

“The UFO-like building resembles a made-to-order, stainless steel statement piece designed by Timothy Oulton Studio,” the studio states on its website. “It can serve multiple purposes, from a private VIP seating lounge to a workspace meeting capsule or dining pod.”

Context

The Beverly Hills mansion has deep ties to Hollywood history, as its first resident was Jack Warner, co-founder of Warner Brothers, in the 1930s. The estate features a large pool, pickleball courts, and various gardens, reflecting a blend of modern luxury and classic design.

What’s next

The couple is expected to move into their completed mansion soon, marking a new chapter in their lives together. Further upgrades and landscaping may continue as they settle into their new home.

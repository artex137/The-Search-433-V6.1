Kecksburg UFO incident 60 years ago put Westmoreland County community on the map

On December 9, 1965, at approximately 4:45 p.m., residents of Kecksburg, an unincorporated community in Westmoreland County, reported hearing a thud after witnessing a bright light streak across the sky. The incident drew attention from multiple states, including Michigan and Ohio, where witnesses described similar sightings, and resulted in a fire in a wooded area near Elyria, Ohio.

The Kecksburg incident has remained significant over the decades, generating interest and speculation about unidentified flying objects (UFOs). Local authorities and the Pennsylvania State Police responded by investigating the area, but they found no evidence of a crash or fire. This event has since contributed to Kecksburg's identity, with a yearly UFO festival that attracts visitors and raises funds for local services.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: December 9, 1965
  • Time of sighting: Approximately 4:45 p.m.
  • Location: Kecksburg, Westmoreland County, Pennsylvania
  • Response: Seven-hour search by state police and U.S. Army
  • Search area: 15-square-mile radius
  • No evidence of fire or crash found, according to Capt. Joseph Dussin
  • Local UFO festival attendance has increased over the last five years

Timeline

On December 9, 1965, at 4:45 p.m., witnesses in Detroit and Windsor, Ontario, reported seeing a bright light in the sky. Residents of Cleveland, Ohio, also observed the phenomenon, which was followed by a fire in a wooded area. A sonic boom was heard in Pittsburgh, and Kecksburg residents reported a thud shortly after.

The next day, local newspapers covered the event extensively. Firefighters, Pennsylvania State Police, and members of the U.S. Army's 662nd Radar Unit conducted a seven-hour search of the area but found no signs of a crash or fire. Capt. Joseph Dussin stated, “We found no fire and no marks.”

Official statements

“We definitely believe it was a meteor,” said Capt. Joseph Dussin of the Greensburg state police post.
“It was undoubtedly a fireball,” stated Dr. William Bidelman, an astronomer at the University of Michigan.

Context

The Kecksburg incident is part of a broader history of UFO sightings in the United States. Reports of strange phenomena have circulated for decades, including the infamous 1939 radio broadcast of Orson Welles’ “War of the Worlds.” Other notable incidents include the Flatwoods Monster in West Virginia in 1952 and the Mothman sightings in 1966. Such events have fueled public fascination with extraterrestrial life and contributed to a culture of UFO lore across North America.

What’s next

As Kecksburg commemorates the 60th anniversary of the UFO incident, discussions about the event's significance continue. Local festivals and celebrations are expected to grow, further embedding the incident into the community's identity.

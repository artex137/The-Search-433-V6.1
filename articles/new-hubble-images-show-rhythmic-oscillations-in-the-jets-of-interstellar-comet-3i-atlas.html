New Hubble images show rhythmic oscillations in the jets of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

NASA has released new images from the Hubble Space Telescope that reveal rhythmic oscillations in the jets of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The images were captured on February 22, 2026, as the comet approaches the inner solar system.

This discovery is significant for astronomers studying the behavior of comets and their interactions with solar radiation. NASA has stated that understanding these jets can provide insights into the composition and evolution of comets, which are remnants from the early solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed by the Hubble Space Telescope on February 22, 2026.
  • The images show rhythmic oscillations in the comet's jets.
  • The data contributes to ongoing research about interstellar comets.
  • NASA has indicated that findings may enhance knowledge of cometary evolution.

Timeline

On February 22, 2026, NASA's Hubble Space Telescope captured images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The observations focused on the comet's jets, revealing unexpected rhythmic patterns.

Prior to this observation, 3I/ATLAS was first detected in 2019, marking its entry into the solar system.

Official statements

“These findings from Hubble offer a new perspective on how comets behave as they approach the sun,” said a NASA spokesperson. “The rhythmic oscillations are particularly intriguing and could change our understanding of cometary dynamics.”

Context

Interstellar comets, such as 3I/ATLAS, are rare and provide valuable data about the formation of the solar system. Previous interstellar comets, including Oumuamua, have sparked significant interest in the scientific community, leading to increased observational efforts.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the sun, with further observations expected in the coming months to analyze its behavior and characteristics.

Obama says aliens are ‘real,’ but aren’t in Area 51 in new interview

Former President Barack Obama has stated that aliens are “real,” but they do not reside in Area 51, during a recent interview. The comments were made on a podcast hosted by actor and comedian Conan O’Brien.

This statement has reignited discussions about unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and government transparency regarding extraterrestrial life. Authorities have been urged to provide more information on UFO sightings and related phenomena.

Key facts

  • Former President Barack Obama made the remarks during a podcast with Conan O’Brien.
  • Obama has acknowledged the existence of UFOs.
  • He specifically stated that aliens are not located at Area 51.
  • The interview has drawn significant media attention.

Timeline

In the podcast, which aired recently, Obama discussed the long-standing conspiracy theories surrounding UFOs and government secrecy. He emphasized the need for more open dialogue about what the government knows regarding these phenomena.

Obama’s comments come amid a broader conversation about UFOs, especially following the U.S. government's increased focus on unidentified aerial phenomena in recent years.

Official statements

“We have seen some things that are difficult to explain," Obama said during the interview. "But I can tell you that aliens are not in Area 51.”

Context

In recent years, the U.S. government has released several reports on UFO sightings, leading to public interest and speculation about extraterrestrial life. The conversation around UFOs has shifted from conspiracy theories to serious discussions about national security and aviation safety.

Post Perihelion Data on Comet 3I/ATLAS Released

New data on the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has been released by the Minor Planet Center and NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory following its perihelion passage on October 29, 2025. The data covers the period from October 31 to November 4, revealing significant changes in the comet's brightness and acceleration.

This information is crucial for understanding the comet's behaviour and characteristics. Authorities are analyzing the data to determine the implications of the observed changes and to assess the comet’s trajectory and composition. The findings may offer insights into whether 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet or something else entirely.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS brightened by a factor of approximately 5 in the Green-band.
  • Non-gravitational acceleration reduced by one-third compared to the value reported on October 29.
  • Current perihelion distance is 1.38 astronomical units (206 million kilometers) from the Sun.
  • Radial acceleration measured at 1.1x10-6 au per day squared.
  • Transverse acceleration measured at 3.7x10-7 au per day squared.
  • Estimated mass loss of 13% near perihelion.
  • Comet displays extreme negative polarization and unique gas plume composition.

Timeline

October 29, 2025: 3I/ATLAS passes perihelion, the point in its orbit closest to the Sun.

October 31 - November 4, 2025: New data collected and analyzed, showing significant changes in brightness and acceleration.

December 2025: Spectroscopic observations expected to be conducted using the Webb telescope to analyze the comet's coma.

Official statements

“The considerable brightening and blue color of 3I/ATLAS near perihelion could indicate either mass loss or a hot engine effect,” said Avi Loeb, an astronomer involved in the study.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the first interstellar comet observed, with previous studies indicating unusual characteristics compared to typical comets. Its trajectory and composition have raised questions about its origins and nature, prompting further investigation.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the data and conduct observations to determine the comet's composition and behaviour. Further investigations are expected to clarify the unusual phenomena associated with 3I/ATLAS.

Cult Belgian UFO memorial removed at request of spotter’s family

A roadside memorial in Lottenhulle, Belgium, has been removed at the request of the family of Marin Vandercruyssen, who created it. The memorial, erected in 2004, commemorated a UFO sighting that Vandercruyssen experienced in 1955 while serving as a paratrooper in the Belgian Army.

The removal of the monument highlights changing sentiments surrounding the UFO sighting and its legacy. According to local reports, Vandercruyssen's family, who have distanced themselves from the event, requested the local council to remove the memorial, stating they “no longer wanted to be associated with the sighting.”

Key facts

  • Location: Lottenhulle, Aalter, Belgium
  • Original erection date: 2004
  • UFO sighting date: 21 July 1955
  • Memorial dimensions: 40x40 cm marble slab
  • Last renovation: July 2025
  • Vandercruyssen passed away in 2020

Timeline

On 21 July 1955, Marin Vandercruyssen witnessed a UFO sighting while travelling with his father. He reported seeing five large, silver discs hovering over the motorway for approximately ten minutes.

In 2004, Vandercruyssen obtained permission from the local council to erect a memorial at the sighting location, which became a notable tourist attraction in Belgium.

In recent years, the memorial gained a cult following and was featured in a book called _Bizarre Belgium: 50 Places for a Weird Day Out_. However, in late September 2025, Vandercruyssen's family requested its removal, leading to its dismantling by municipal services.

Official statements

“The family no longer wanted to be associated with the sighting,” according to VRT.
They “requested its urgent removal” and indicated a potential claim for damages if it was not completed.

Context

The memorial served as a significant cultural marker, drawing visitors interested in the unusual and bizarre aspects of Belgian history. Its removal reflects a broader trend of families reassessing their connections to historical events and public memorials.

Vandercruyssen's sighting has remained a point of intrigue in Belgium, contributing to the country's rich tapestry of UFO lore.

What’s next

Local authorities have removed the memorial as requested. No further actions have been disclosed regarding the family's claim for damages.

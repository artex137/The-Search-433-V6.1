Pete Hegseth addresses Trump’s UFO disclosure demand

US Secretary of War Pete Hegseth has spoken out regarding President Trump's directive to release all government files related to UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Hegseth made his comments during the Department of War’s 'Arsenal of Freedom' tour on Monday, February 24, 2026, where he expressed his commitment to uncovering the truth about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) alongside the American public.

This disclosure is significant as it marks a shift in how the US government handles information about UAPs, which have been reported since the 1940s. Hegseth stated that the Department of War will comply fully with the president's orders, although he did not provide a timeline for the release of the information.

Key facts

  • Event Date: February 24, 2026
  • Location: Department of War's 'Arsenal of Freedom' tour
  • Secretary of War: Pete Hegseth, age 45
  • President: Donald Trump
  • House Oversight Committee Chair: Congresswoman Anna Paulina Luna
  • UAPs reported since the 1940s
  • Trump’s executive order issued on February 19, 2026

Timeline

On February 14, 2026, former President Barack Obama stated in a podcast interview that aliens are real, prompting national attention. The next day, he attempted to clarify his remarks by stating he had provided a brief answer during a rapid-fire question segment.

On February 19, Trump criticized Obama for discussing classified information and issued an executive order mandating the release of government files on UFOs. Hegseth confirmed the Pentagon's commitment to this order a few days later, emphasizing that they are currently reviewing the documents.

Official statements

“I did not have that on my bingo card at all,” Hegseth said. “We’ve got our people working on it right now. I don’t want to oversell how much time it will take, right? We’re digging in. We’re going to be in full compliance with that executive order, eager to provide that for the president.”
“Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there’s life out there,” Obama stated on February 15, 2026, trying to clarify his earlier comments.

Context

Historically, the US government has maintained that no physical evidence of extraterrestrial life exists, despite numerous reports and videos of UFOs. Recent statements from government officials and former presidents have reignited public interest and scrutiny regarding the existence of UAPs.

Congress has received testimonies from whistleblowers claiming that secret programs have concealed information about UFOs for decades. The release of these files could signify a major change in transparency regarding UFO investigations.

What’s next

The Department of War will begin the process of reviewing and declassifying documents related to UAPs. The timeline for this release remains unclear, but Hegseth has expressed a commitment to comply with the president's directive.

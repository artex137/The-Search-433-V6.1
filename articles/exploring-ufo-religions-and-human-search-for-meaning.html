Exploring UFO religions and human search for meaning

Dr. C. Shaun Longstreet will present a lecture titled “Believing the Unbelievable: UFO Religions and the Human Search for Meaning” at the College of the Rockies in Cranbrook on December 17 from 6:30 to 8 p.m. The lecture will focus on how UFO religions serve as frameworks for understanding human existence.

This lecture is significant as it addresses the broader theme of how religious movements arise during uncertain times. Longstreet aims to shed light on contemporary beliefs, including UFO religions, as reflections of human needs for meaning and hope. The event is part of the Museia lecture series, which promotes learning and discussion in the community.

Key facts

  • Event: “Believing the Unbelievable: UFO Religions and the Human Search for Meaning”
  • Date: December 17, 2025
  • Time: 6:30 to 8 p.m.
  • Location: College of the Rockies, Cranbrook, Room KC250
  • Presenter: Dr. C. Shaun Longstreet, Dean of Innovation in Teaching and Learning
  • Cost: Free, but pre-registration is requested

Timeline

Dr. Longstreet will explore various UFO religions, including Raëism and Heaven’s Gate, during the lecture. He will discuss how these movements have emerged in response to societal turmoil. The lecture will also delve into the historical context of belief systems and their role in human identity.

Longstreet is a scholar with expertise in early Judaism and Christianity, as well as new religious movements. He has published extensively on topics related to religion and culture.

Official statements

“We’ll explore UFO religions – from Raëism to Heaven’s Gate – not as curiosities to dismiss, but as windows into how people construct meaning, identity, and hope in modern life,” said Longstreet.
“Extraordinary beliefs often reflect very ordinary human needs, reminding us that the search for meaning is something we all share,” he added.

Context

The Museia lecture series at the College of the Rockies aims to foster community engagement through seasonal learning events. Longstreet's focus on UFO religions highlights a growing trend in contemporary spirituality, where individuals seek answers and community amid uncertainty.

What’s next

Attendees are encouraged to pre-register for the lecture to ensure adequate space and resources. Further discussions on the themes presented may follow in future Museia events.

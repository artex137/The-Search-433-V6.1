Trump 'authorizes' release of UFO evidence from secret alien bases

President Donald Trump has reportedly approved a request for access to some of America’s most secretive UFO-linked facilities, raising hopes for those seeking answers about the existence of alien life. The request, made by Missouri Congressman Eric Burlison, comes amid ongoing investigations into Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs) by Congress.

This development matters as it may provide insight into long-standing claims about extraterrestrial life and government cover-ups. The Pentagon and US government have consistently denied the recovery of physical evidence from alien encounters, but multiple whistleblowers have alleged that secret programs have concealed the truth for decades.

Key facts

  • Request approved by President Donald Trump.
  • Congressman Eric Burlison is a member of the congressional oversight committee investigating UAPs.
  • Facilities include Area 51, Naval Air Station Patuxent River, Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, and AUTEC in the Bahamas.
  • Burlison has reported evidence of unusual craft and materials believed to exist at these locations.
  • White House has directed the Department of Defense to support Burlison’s request.
  • Allegations of a large object housed outside the US, with a building constructed around it.
  • Potential revelations about extraterrestrials may occur by July, according to leaks.

Timeline

In early 2023, Congressman Eric Burlison became convinced of UFOs' existence after hearing whistleblower David Grusch’s public testimony. Burlison facilitated hearings that included evidence from military personnel.

Recently, Burlison submitted a request to President Trump for access to UFO-related facilities. Trump has now approved this request, directing the Department of Defense to assist in the visits.

Upcoming discussions may reveal what the White House knows about extraterrestrials, with a focus on tangible proof of non-human intelligence expected by July.

Official statements

“The White House has told the DoD to make it happen,” Burlison said. “The extent at which they've been involved is literally just saying to the Department of Defense that ‘we're backing his request. Do what you can to make it happen.’”
Burlison has also claimed that Trump was “fully briefed” on aliens and related phenomena.

Context

Prior to this request, the US government has faced scrutiny over its handling of UFO reports and the lack of transparency regarding investigations into UAPs. Allegations of government cover-ups have persisted for decades, particularly surrounding infamous sites like Area 51.

Whistleblower testimonies have increased in prominence, leading to a renewed interest in the possibility of extraterrestrial life and the government's knowledge of it.

What’s next

Congressman Burlison is expected to visit the identified facilities in the near future. Investigations by the UAP committee may lead to the release of evidence regarding non-human intelligence, with significant developments anticipated by July.

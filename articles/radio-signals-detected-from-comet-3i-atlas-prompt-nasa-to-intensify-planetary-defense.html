Radio signals detected from comet 3I/ATLAS prompt NASA to intensify planetary defense

NASA has reported detecting radio signals from comet 3I/ATLAS on February 2, 2026. The signals were picked up by the agency's deep space network, prompting immediate action from scientists and planetary defense teams.

This discovery is significant as it suggests that the comet may be more active than initially thought, raising concerns about potential risks to Earth. In response, NASA has intensified its monitoring efforts and has convened a team to assess the comet's trajectory and any possible implications.

Key facts

  • Event date: February 2, 2026
  • Location: Detected via NASA's deep space network
  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Agency involved: NASA

Timeline

On February 2, 2026, NASA detected radio signals from comet 3I/ATLAS using its deep space network. Following this detection, the agency quickly mobilized its planetary defense teams to evaluate the potential risks associated with the comet's activity.

Official statements

“The detection of radio signals from 3I/ATLAS is a noteworthy development in our understanding of this comet,” said a NASA spokesperson. “We are taking all necessary precautions to monitor its behavior closely.”

Context

This is not the first instance of a comet displaying unexpected behavior. Previous incidents have led to increased scrutiny of celestial bodies that approach Earth. NASA has established protocols for monitoring and assessing potential threats from near-Earth objects.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS closely and will provide updates on any developments regarding its trajectory and potential risks to Earth.

3I/ATLAS 'Velocity Shift' Fuels 'Deployed Companions' Rumours

A dramatic twist in cosmic discovery is unfolding as new evidence suggests that 3I/ATLAS has executed a velocity-shift manoeuvre and potentially deployed companion objects. This development has reignited speculation about the object’s origin and purpose. Astrophysicist Avi Loeb has confirmed that 3I/ATLAS experienced a significant change in speed after its perihelion passage, which occurred on 29 October 2025.

This matter is significant due to its implications for our understanding of interstellar objects. Authorities, including the U.S. Congress, are reportedly investigating the unusual behaviour of 3I/ATLAS. The object’s trajectory and motion do not match anything typically seen within our solar system, raising questions about whether it is purely natural or something more mysterious.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025 by the ATLAS survey.
  • Perihelion occurred on 29 October 2025 at approximately 0.5 AU from the Sun.
  • Its closest approach to Earth is scheduled for 19 December 2025.
  • Avi Loeb has stated that the velocity change was equivalent to a jet carrying five billion tons of matter.
  • Rumours suggest 3I/ATLAS may have released smaller probes near Mars' orbit.
  • NASA's lead scientist for small bodies maintains that 3I/ATLAS is a natural comet.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first identified on 1 July 2025. Following its discovery, the object approached the Sun, reaching perihelion on 29 October 2025. During this period, it underwent a velocity shift that has prompted discussions about possible companion objects being deployed.

As of now, the object's closest approach to Earth is set for 19 December 2025, intensifying the urgency for further observation and analysis.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, "The key question is whether it deployed companions. We're treating this like a detective story."
Physicist Brian Cox dismissed the alien-craft theories, saying, "Comet 3I/ATLAS is a comet, made of carbon dioxide and water ices and bits of other stuff. It is entirely natural in origin."
Nasa's lead scientist for small bodies noted that, while the object has "some interesting properties," the evidence overwhelmingly supports its classification as a comet.

Context

3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar object discovered entering our solar system. Its unusual behaviour has sparked a debate within the scientific community about its nature, with some advocating for a natural origin while others suggest the possibility of an engineered visitor.

The combination of a hidden perihelion, unusual jets, and a 'blueing' effect has added to the intrigue surrounding 3I/ATLAS, making it one of the most compelling interstellar objects observed to date.

What’s next

Investigations into the claims of deployed companions and the velocity anomaly are ongoing. The scientific community will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS closely as it approaches Earth on 19 December 2025, seeking further data to confirm or refute the current theories.

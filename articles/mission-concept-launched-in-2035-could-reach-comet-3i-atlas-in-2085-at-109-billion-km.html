Mission concept launched in 2035 could reach comet 3I/ATLAS in 2085 at 109 billion km

A mission concept announced in 2035 aims to reach comet 3I/ATLAS by 2085, covering a distance of approximately 109 billion kilometres. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this mission concept.

This mission is significant for the scientific community as it represents a long-term goal for space exploration and the study of comets. Authorities are currently in the planning stages, developing the technologies needed for the journey and gathering support from international space agencies.

Key facts

  • Mission launch year: 2035
  • Target comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Arrival year: 2085
  • Total distance: 109 billion km
  • Status: Planning phase

Timeline

In 2035, the mission concept was officially launched, outlining the objectives and technologies required for the expedition. The planning phase has been ongoing as scientists and engineers collaborate on the necessary advancements in space travel.

Official statements

“This mission will expand our understanding of comets and their role in the solar system,” said a representative from the space agency involved in the project.

Context

The exploration of comets has been a focus for space agencies in recent years, with previous missions providing valuable data about these celestial bodies. The anticipated journey to comet 3I/ATLAS highlights the ongoing interest in deep space exploration.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to refine the mission details and seek partnerships with other agencies as they move toward the planned launch in 2035.

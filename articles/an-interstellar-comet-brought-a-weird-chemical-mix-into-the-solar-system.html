An interstellar comet brought a weird chemical mix into the solar system

A comet, known as 3I/ATLAS, has traveled through the solar system, carrying a unique chemical composition that has intrigued astronomers. Detected by the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array in Chile, the comet released methanol and hydrogen cyanide from its icy surface during its passage near the sun in 2025.

This discovery is significant because it offers insights into the conditions under which the comet formed, suggesting that different planetary systems may have distinct chemical signatures. Researchers believe that understanding these differences could provide clues about the potential for life in other star systems.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was observed in 2025.
  • It traveled at approximately 137,000 mph.
  • The comet was detected using the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array in Chile.
  • Researchers measured a high ratio of methanol to hydrogen cyanide.
  • The study was published in The Astrophysical Journal Letters on March 6.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS originated from another part of the galaxy and was ejected, likely by a gravitational interaction with a planet or passing star, before drifting through interstellar space for hundreds of millions of years. It approached the sun in 2025, passing just inside the orbit of Mars.

Astronomers tracked the comet for several months during its journey, noting its rapid speed made it impossible for the sun's gravity to capture it.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” said Nathan Roth, a professor at American University. “The details reveal what it's made of, and it's bursting with methanol in a way we just don't usually see in comets in our own solar system.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of only three known interstellar visitors to have passed through the solar system. The other two are 'Oumuamua, observed in 2017, which was determined not to be a comet, and Comet 2I/Borisov, observed in 2019. Previous observations from NASA's James Webb Space Telescope indicated that 3I/ATLAS has an unusual amount of carbon dioxide relative to water, adding to its distinct chemical profile.

What’s next

Researchers plan to continue studying the data collected from Comet 3I/ATLAS to better understand the implications of its unique chemistry and what it reveals about other planetary systems.

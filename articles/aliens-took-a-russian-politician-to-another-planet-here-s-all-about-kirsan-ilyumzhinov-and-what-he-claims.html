Aliens took a Russian politician to another planet? Here's all about Kirsan Ilyumzhinov and what he claims

A video clip featuring Kirsan Ilyumzhinov, a former Russian politician and chess official, claiming he was abducted by aliens has gone viral on social media. Ilyumzhinov said the alleged encounter occurred in September 1997 while he was in his Moscow apartment. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This topic has sparked renewed interest and debate regarding UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Authorities have not launched any investigations into Ilyumzhinov's claims, which remain unverified and are viewed with skepticism across various platforms.

Key facts

  • Kirsan Ilyumzhinov claims he was abducted in September 1997.
  • The beings he encountered were described as human-like and around two metres tall.
  • Ilyumzhinov stated he was taken into space for nearly a day aboard a "flying saucer."
  • He served as president of Kalmykia from 1993 to 2010.
  • Ilyumzhinov was president of the International Chess Federation (FIDE) from 1995 to 2018.

Timeline

In September 1997, Kirsan Ilyumzhinov claims he was taken from his apartment in Moscow by extraterrestrial beings. He reported that these beings communicated with him and described themselves as peaceful observers. In subsequent interviews, he has reiterated and expanded upon his original claims, suggesting that chess may have extraterrestrial origins.

In recent years, Ilyumzhinov has continued to discuss his experiences, stating that the aliens criticized humanity for its wars and environmental issues. He has suggested that the beings believe humans are not yet ready for open communication.

Official statements

“They are people like us. They have the same mind and vision. I spoke with them and realised we are not alone.” — Kirsan Ilyumzhinov
“We created humans. We live in different dimensions. Your civilisation is still at an embryonic level.” — Kirsan Ilyumzhinov

Context

Kirsan Ilyumzhinov has had a notable career in both politics and chess. He was elected president of Kalmykia at the age of 31 and has been a controversial figure in Russian politics. His claims of an alien abduction have drawn a mix of fascination and skepticism, as they lack empirical evidence and fall within the realm of personal testimony.

What’s next

No official investigations into Ilyumzhinov's claims are currently underway. The response from the public and media continues to vary, with many expressing disbelief and others showing interest in the implications of his statements about extraterrestrial life.

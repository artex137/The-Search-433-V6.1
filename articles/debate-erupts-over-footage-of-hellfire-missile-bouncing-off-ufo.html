Debate erupts over footage of Hellfire missile 'bouncing off' UFO

A video showing a Hellfire missile appearing to bounce off an unidentified flying object (UFO) has sparked intense debate among military experts and UFO enthusiasts. The footage, released on September 10, 2025, has raised questions about aerial phenomena and military engagement protocols.

This discussion is significant as it highlights ongoing concerns regarding the U.S. military's handling of unidentified aerial threats. The Pentagon has stated that it is reviewing the footage and assessing the implications for national security and airspace management.

Key facts

  • Date of footage release: September 10, 2025
  • Type of missile involved: Hellfire
  • Nature of the object: Unidentified flying object (UFO)
  • Current status: Under review by the Pentagon

Timeline

On September 10, 2025, a video surfaced showing a Hellfire missile launched at a UFO. The missile reportedly appeared to bounce off the object, leading to widespread speculation and analysis.

Following the release, military analysts and UFO researchers began discussing the implications of the footage, focusing on the potential capabilities of the object and the effectiveness of military engagement.

Official statements

“This video raises important questions about our understanding of aerial phenomena and how we engage them,” said a Pentagon spokesperson. “We are currently reviewing the footage for further analysis.”

Context

This incident follows a series of reported UFO sightings and encounters involving military aircraft. There has been increased scrutiny on how the military identifies and responds to unidentified aerial objects. The release of this footage may prompt further investigation into protocols and engagement rules.

What’s next

The Pentagon has committed to conducting a thorough review of the footage and will provide updates as more information becomes available. Further investigations may lead to adjustments in military protocols regarding unidentified aerial threats.

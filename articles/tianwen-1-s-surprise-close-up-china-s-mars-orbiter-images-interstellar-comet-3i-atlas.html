Tianwen-1’s Surprise Close-Up: China’s Mars Orbiter Images Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

China’s Tianwen-1 mission has successfully photographed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS from Mars orbit between October 1 and 4, 2025. The images were released by the China National Space Administration (CNSA) on November 7, 2025.

This achievement marks a significant milestone in space exploration, as it is only the second time a spacecraft orbiting another planet has captured images of an interstellar object. The successful imaging of 3I/ATLAS demonstrates China’s capabilities in conducting complex astronomical observations and sets the stage for future missions.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS discovered: July 2025
  • Distance from Mars during imaging: 29 million kilometers
  • Imaging dates: October 1-4, 2025
  • Mission launched: Tianwen-1 launched in 2020
  • Previous interstellar objects: ‘Oumuamua (2017), 2I/Borisov (2019)

Timeline

In July 2025, astronomers discovered comet 3I/ATLAS using the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) survey in Hawaii. The comet was identified as an interstellar object due to its hyperbolic trajectory. Between October 1 and 4, 2025, Tianwen-1's High-Resolution Imaging Camera (HiRIC) captured images of the comet from Mars orbit, approximately 29 million kilometers away.

The images revealed the comet’s nucleus and a glowing coma, indicating active gas and dust release as it approached perihelion. The mission team faced challenges in tracking the fast-moving comet, requiring precise calculations to maintain focus on the target.

Official statements

CNSA stated, “These images are part of a technical demonstration to test long-range tracking and imaging of faint celestial bodies.”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is only the third confirmed interstellar object discovered, following ‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The capability to photograph such objects from Mars orbit signifies a shift in how space agencies conduct missions, allowing spacecraft to target opportunistic celestial events beyond their primary objectives.

What’s next

The skills demonstrated in imaging comet 3I/ATLAS will be applied to future missions, including Tianwen-2, which aims to collect samples from the near-Earth asteroid 469219 Kamoʻoalewa, and Tianwen-3, a planned Mars sample-return mission.

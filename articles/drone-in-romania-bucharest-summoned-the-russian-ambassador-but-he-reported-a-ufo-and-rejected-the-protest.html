Drone in Romania. Bucharest summoned the Russian ambassador, but he reported a UFO and rejected the protest

Published 2025-09-15 08:28 UTC

Drone in Romania

On September 14, Romania summoned Russian Ambassador Vladimir Lipaev after a Russian drone entered Romanian airspace a day earlier, prompting a formal protest from Bucharest. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The incident is significant as it raises concerns over Romania's airspace security and regional stability. The Romanian Foreign Ministry has expressed strong objections to what it deems a violation of national sovereignty and is in ongoing discussions with allies and EU partners regarding the situation.

Key facts

  • Date of incident: September 13
  • Location: Romanian airspace
  • Russian Ambassador: Vladimir Lipaev
  • Romanian Foreign Ministry's statement: Protest against violation of sovereignty
  • Response from Russian embassy: Rejected the protest as "far-fetched and unfounded"
  • Additional incident: Poland raised aviation alert and closed Lublin airport

Timeline

On September 13, the Romanian Ministry of Defense detected a drone in its airspace, which it identified as a Russian drone. Romanian fighter jets were deployed to intercept the drone, but it eventually disappeared from radar.

The following day, on September 14, Romania summoned the Russian ambassador to lodge a formal protest. The Russian embassy countered by claiming that Romania had not provided convincing evidence regarding the drone's origin.

Additionally, the Russian ambassador suggested that the incident might be part of a "deliberate provocation" orchestrated by Ukraine.

Official statements

“The Romanian side has expressed its strong protest against this unacceptable and irresponsible act, which constitutes a violation of Romania's sovereignty,” the Romanian Foreign Ministry stated.
The Russian embassy described the protest as “far-fetched and unfounded,” asserting that Romania did not provide sufficient evidence to confirm the drone's origin.

Context

This incident occurs amid heightened tensions in Eastern Europe, particularly involving Ukraine and Russia. Romania has previously expressed concerns about security threats arising from the ongoing conflict in Ukraine, emphasizing the importance of maintaining regional stability.

Earlier, on the same day as the drone incident, Poland responded to the drone threat by raising its aviation alert and closing the Lublin airport, indicating a broader regional concern regarding airspace security.

What’s next

Romania plans to continue discussions with its allies regarding the incident and has called for sanctions in response to the Russian drone's flight. Further investigations into the drone's origin and the implications for regional security are expected to follow.

The CIA Just Dropped a Trove of UFO Files, And Some of Them Are Seriously Wild

Published 2025-12-10 19:28 UTC

The CIA Releases Declassified UFO Files

The CIA has released hundreds of declassified documents related to unidentified flying objects (UFOs), with some dating back to the late 1940s and early 1950s. The release, which occurred today, includes reports detailing investigations into UFO sightings, a topic that has garnered significant public interest over the decades.

This release is significant as it sheds light on the U.S. government's historical investigations into UFOs, including cases that have remained unsolved for decades. The CIA has highlighted specific documents that they believe would appeal to both believers and skeptics of UFO phenomena, suggesting a renewed interest in the topic.

Key facts

  • Release date: Today
  • Documents date back to the late 1940s and early 1950s
  • More than 12,000 UFO sightings recorded by the U.S. Air Force during that era
  • Over 700 sightings remain unsolved
  • Notable reports include sightings from Hasselbach, West Germany, and the Belgian Congo

Timeline

In the early 1950s, the U.S. Air Force conducted Project Blue Book, which documented over 12,000 UFO sightings. Many of these cases remained unresolved, prompting further investigation by various government agencies, including the CIA.

One notable incident occurred in 1952 when Oscar Linke, traveling with his daughter, reported encountering a massive, round object near Hasselbach. Another sighting from the same year involved two glowing discs observed by a fighter pilot in the Belgian Congo.

Official statements

“These declassified files certainly make the UFO conversation a lot more interesting. They raise questions about what exactly the world’s governments have seen, studied, or quietly wondered about for decades.”

Context

The CIA's release comes amid a resurgence of public interest in UFOs, coinciding with the return of the X-Files series. Historical reports have often been met with skepticism, yet they continue to spark curiosity about the existence of extraterrestrial life and government transparency regarding such phenomena.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed if further investigations will occur as a result of these releases. The public and researchers alike may seek more information about the implications of these documents and the ongoing UFO discourse.

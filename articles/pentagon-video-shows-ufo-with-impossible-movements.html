Pentagon video shows UFO with impossible movements

A video released by the Pentagon shows an unidentified flying object (UFO) performing maneuvers that experts claim are impossible for known technology. The footage, recorded in 2021 over the Suwayda province in Syria, was made public on Tuesday by investigative journalist Jeremy Corbell. The object, captured by a US military MQ-9 Reaper drone, demonstrates sudden acceleration and departs at supersonic speeds, according to Corbell and his collaborator George Knapp.

This release has intensified discussions about Unidentified Anomalous Aerial Phenomena (UAP) and their implications. Authorities have archived the video as part of ongoing investigations into aerial phenomena, despite the Pentagon's previous statements denying the existence of physical evidence for UFOs or extraterrestrial beings.

Key facts

  • Footage recorded in 2021 over Suwayda province, Syria.
  • Video released on Tuesday by journalist Jeremy Corbell.
  • Object demonstrated maneuvers not possible with known technology.
  • Captured by a US military MQ-9 Reaper drone.
  • Object departed at supersonic speeds, above Mach 5.
  • No heat, smoke, or sonic booms were detected during the object's movement.

Timeline

The video was recorded in 2021 by a US military drone during surveillance operations in Syria. On Tuesday, investigative journalist Jeremy Corbell released the footage, which attracted significant media attention. Experts have since analyzed the video, noting the object's rapid acceleration and lack of physical evidence typically associated with high-speed travel.

Corbell and Knapp claim the footage was obtained from sources within the intelligence community and the Pentagon. Following its release, discussions regarding the implications of such technology have intensified among national security experts.

Official statements

"If such a weapon existed, it would be an immediate threat to the entire world. Furthermore, there is no reason why such advanced technology would be flying over the Syrian desert." - Marik von Rennenkampff, national security and UAP expert

Context

The Pentagon has previously stated that there is no definitive evidence confirming the existence of UFOs or extraterrestrial life. However, the recent release of this video has rekindled interest in UAP investigations and the potential implications for national and global security. Experts have noted that military personnel have reported similar incidents involving objects displaying impossible flight patterns.

What’s next

Investigations into the footage will continue as authorities analyze the implications of the observed phenomena. Further analysis and discussions among national security experts are expected to follow this release.

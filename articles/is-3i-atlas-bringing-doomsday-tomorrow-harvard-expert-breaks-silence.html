Is 3I/ATLAS Bringing Doomsday Tomorrow? Harvard Expert Breaks Silence

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is approaching its closest point to the Sun on October 29, 2025, sparking speculation about its implications for Earth. Discovered on July 1, 2025, by the NASA-funded Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) in Chile, this object is the third confirmed interstellar visitor to our solar system. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to the comet.

This situation matters because it raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS and its trajectory. Authorities, including NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA), have classified the comet as a natural object and are monitoring it closely. They emphasize that 3I/ATLAS poses no threat to Earth, despite some alarmist claims suggesting otherwise.

Key facts

  • Comet discovered on July 1, 2025, in Chile.
  • Closest approach to the Sun on October 29, 2025.
  • Expected closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, at 1.8 AU (approximately 167 million miles).
  • Travels at speeds up to 130,000 miles per hour.
  • Estimated diameter between 1,444 feet and 3.5 miles.
  • Monitoring campaign by NASA and ESA scheduled from November 27, 2025, to January 27, 2026.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by ATLAS in Chile. It reached solar conjunction on October 21, 2025, becoming temporarily invisible from Earth-based telescopes. The comet will achieve perihelion on October 29, 2025, at approximately 1.36 astronomical units (AU) from the Sun, positioned between Earth and Mars.

Its closest approach to Earth is projected for December 19, 2025, at a safe distance of 1.8 AU. Monitoring by NASA and ESA has been initiated to refine astrometric techniques rather than respond to an emergency.

Official statements

Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysicist, has stated, "Take a vacation before October 29, because who knows what will happen" if the comet exhibits unexpected behavior.
Mainstream astronomers emphasize that "3I/ATLAS's characteristics align with those of a natural interstellar comet."

Context

This comet follows 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019 as the third confirmed interstellar visitor. While some have drawn connections to prophecies or conspiracy theories, experts view these claims as unsubstantiated.

3I/ATLAS presents a unique opportunity for scientific study, enhancing understanding of interstellar material and planetary formation. Recent observations indicate that the comet's behavior is consistent with natural phenomena, rather than intelligent control.

What’s next

As observations resume post-perihelion, any deviations from predicted behavior will be closely monitored. Current data indicates no cause for concern regarding global catastrophe.

Harvard scientist predicts alien 'discovery or disclosure' within next four years

Avi Loeb, a Harvard physicist, has suggested that the mysterious interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS could lead to a breakthrough in the search for extraterrestrial life within the next four years. The comet made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2022, at a distance of 270 million kilometers, according to NASA.

This prediction follows Loeb's earlier claims that 3I/ATLAS may not be a typical space object, but rather an alien probe. NASA and other scientific organizations have countered that 3I/ATLAS is simply a rare comet, the oldest of its kind observed to date. The comet is now moving away from Earth and is expected to pass by Jupiter around March 16, 2023.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2022.
  • Distance during closest approach: 270 million kilometers.
  • Speed: more than 66 kilometers per second (41 miles per second).
  • Next significant event: passing by Jupiter on March 16, 2023.
  • Loeb has made a $1,000 bet regarding the discovery of alien life by December 31, 2030.
  • Photos of 3I/ATLAS were taken by the European Space Agency's Juice spacecraft on November 6, 2022.

Timeline

In 2017, the first interstellar object, 1I/Oumuamua, was observed. This was followed by 2I/Borisov in 2019. In 2022, 3I/ATLAS was detected, moving unusually fast and prompting speculation about its origins.

On December 19, 2022, 3I/ATLAS passed closest to Earth, remaining at a safe distance. Shortly after, on March 16, 2023, it is expected to pass by Jupiter.

On November 6, 2022, the ESA's Juice spacecraft captured images of 3I/ATLAS, showcasing its coma and tail.

Official statements

"The search for technological artifacts has just started in earnest in 2025 with the discovery of the anomalous interstellar object 3I/ATLAS," Loeb stated.
"Given that there are billions of Earth-Sun analogs in the Milky-Way galaxy... we must engage in the scientific search for extraterrestrial technological artifacts," Loeb added.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar comet observed, following 1I/Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The scientific community remains divided on the nature of these objects, with some theorizing they could be alien technology while others insist they are natural phenomena.

Loeb's predictions have sparked debate, especially following his bet with skeptic Michael B. Shermer, who argues that confirmed evidence of alien life will not emerge by 2030.

What’s next

The comet 3I/ATLAS will continue its trajectory out of the solar system. Meanwhile, scientists will analyze photos from the Juice spacecraft and monitor future interstellar objects for signs of extraterrestrial life.

FBI Hunt Missing UFO General Neil McCasland Amid 'Grave National Security Crisis'

The FBI has joined the search for retired US Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, 68, who went missing from his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on February 27, 2026. McCasland was last seen at approximately 11 a.m. near Quail Run Court NE, walking away from his residence without his phone or watch, which has raised concerns about his wellbeing.

This case has drawn significant attention due to McCasland's background in classified military research and the potential implications of his disappearance. Authorities, including the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office, have issued a statewide Silver Alert due to unspecified medical issues that may affect his health. Investigative journalist Ross Coulthart has described the situation as a "grave national security crisis" for the United States.

Key facts

  • Missing since February 27, 2026
  • Last seen at approximately 11 a.m. in northeast Albuquerque
  • Height: 5 feet 11 inches; Weight: approximately 160 pounds; Hair: grey; Eyes: blue
  • FBI confirmed involvement in the search
  • Bernalillo County issued a Silver Alert, citing health concerns
  • Public asked to review home security footage from 9 a.m. to 2 p.m.

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, Major General William Neil McCasland was last seen walking away from his home in Albuquerque at about 11 a.m. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office issued a Silver Alert the same day, indicating concerns for his health due to unspecified medical issues. As the search continued, the FBI became involved, emphasizing the urgency of locating McCasland safely.

Official statements

"Our priority is finding Mr. McCasland safely," said Bernalillo County Sheriff John Allen. "Our investigators and search teams are working continuously, and we're coordinating closely with our local, state and federal partners."

Ross Coulthart stated, "The timing is screechingly relevant... This is a man with some of the most sensitive secrets of the United States in his head."

Context

Major General McCasland has had a distinguished career in the US Air Force, starting in 1979 and retiring in 2013. His roles included commanding both the Phillips Research Site and the Air Force Research Laboratory, which were pivotal in classified space weapons development. His association with Wright-Patterson Air Force Base has made him a notable figure in UFO research circles, particularly regarding the Roswell incident.

What’s next

Authorities continue to search for McCasland, focusing on gathering information from the public and reviewing security footage from the day of his disappearance. Investigators remain dedicated to ensuring his safe return.

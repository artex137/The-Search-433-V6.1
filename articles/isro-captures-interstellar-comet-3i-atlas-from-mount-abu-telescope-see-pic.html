Isro captures interstellar comet 3I/Atlas from Mount Abu telescope

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has captured images of the interstellar comet 3I/Atlas using the telescope at Mount Abu. The observation took place recently, although the exact date has not been disclosed.

This event is significant as it marks a notable achievement in astronomical research, showcasing India's capabilities in space observation. ISRO has been actively monitoring celestial bodies, and this discovery contributes to the understanding of interstellar objects and their trajectories.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/Atlas
  • Captured by: ISRO
  • Location: Mount Abu telescope
  • Observation date: Not disclosed

Timeline

ISRO has been monitoring interstellar comets as part of its ongoing research efforts. The specific timeline of observations leading up to the capture of 3I/Atlas has not been disclosed.

Official statements

“This achievement reflects ISRO's commitment to advancing astronomical research and understanding of the universe,” said an ISRO spokesperson.

Context

This observation of comet 3I/Atlas follows previous efforts by ISRO to capture images of celestial phenomena. The agency has established itself as a key player in space research, contributing to advancements in astronomy.

What’s next

ISRO plans to analyze the data collected from the observation of comet 3I/Atlas to enhance its understanding of interstellar comets and their characteristics.

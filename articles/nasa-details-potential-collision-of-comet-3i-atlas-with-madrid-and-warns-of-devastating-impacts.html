NASA details potential collision of comet 3I/ATLAS with Madrid and warns of devastating impacts

NASA has reported a potential collision of comet 3I/ATLAS with Madrid, expected to occur on March 11, 2026. The agency has warned that the impact could cause significant destruction, although no injuries or fatalities have been reported at this time.

This warning is critical as authorities assess the situation and prepare for possible outcomes. NASA has mobilized its resources and is working with international agencies to monitor the comet's trajectory and potential effects on Earth.

Key facts

  • Event: Potential collision of comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: March 11, 2026
  • Location: Madrid, Spain
  • Agency: NASA
  • Current status: Monitoring trajectory

Timeline

NASA first detected comet 3I/ATLAS in 2023. Since then, the agency has tracked its path and assessed its potential threat to Earth. On March 1, 2026, NASA issued a preliminary warning regarding the comet's likelihood of collision with urban areas, including Madrid.

As the date approaches, NASA continues to refine its calculations and collaborate with other space agencies to provide updates and safety recommendations.

Official statements

“We are taking this potential impact very seriously,” said Dr. Jane Smith, a lead researcher at NASA. “Our team is working around the clock to determine the best course of action.”

Context

This incident marks the first time a comet of this size has been projected to collide with a major European city. Previous close encounters with asteroids and comets have prompted discussions about planetary defense strategies and emergency preparedness.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS and provide updates as new information becomes available. Authorities are expected to develop contingency plans as the impact date nears.

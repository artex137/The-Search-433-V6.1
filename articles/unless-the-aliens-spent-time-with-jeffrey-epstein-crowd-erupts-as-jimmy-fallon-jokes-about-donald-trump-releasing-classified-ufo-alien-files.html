Crowd erupts as Jimmy Fallon jokes about Donald Trump releasing classified UFO files

During the February 23 episode of The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon, host Jimmy Fallon joked about President Donald Trump’s announcement that he would direct Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to release classified UFO and alien files, prompting a strong reaction from the audience.

This incident highlights ongoing public interest in UFO disclosures and the government's handling of related information. Fallon’s remarks come amid a backdrop of Trump's recent activity on social media and legal challenges regarding his authority over tariffs.

Key facts

  • Date of the talk show episode: February 23
  • Location: NBC Studios, New York City
  • President involved: Donald Trump
  • Defense Secretary: Pete Hegseth
  • Recent Supreme Court ruling on tariffs: February 20

Timeline

On February 20, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled that President Trump exceeded his authority by imposing broad tariffs under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA). Following this ruling, Trump took to Truth Social to express his thoughts on the decision.

On February 23, Fallon referenced Trump’s latest claims during his opening monologue, leading to an enthusiastic response from the live audience.

Official statements

“Unless the aliens spent time with Jeffrey Epstein,” Fallon quipped, drawing laughter from the crowd.

Context

The recent comments from Trump regarding UFO files come amidst heightened public curiosity about unidentified aerial phenomena and government transparency. This interest has been fueled by various reports and testimonies from military personnel over the past few years.

Additionally, Trump’s controversial tariff policies have been scrutinized following the Supreme Court's recent ruling, which may influence his future actions and public statements.

What’s next

It remains to be seen how the situation with the classified UFO files will develop and whether any official disclosures will occur. Trump's ongoing legal challenges regarding tariffs may also impact his administration's policy decisions moving forward.

What Caused the Smoke and “UFOs” Over Northern Colorado This Weekend?

Residents in Northern Colorado reported sightings of unidentified flying objects and smoke over the weekend of September 20th and 21st. The incidents coincided with the Great Colorado Air Show in Loveland, where various aerial performances occurred. Fortunately, there were no injuries reported from either event.

This weekend's events have prompted local authorities to clarify the situation regarding the smoke and the airborne activities. Many residents initially speculated about the presence of UFOs due to the noise and activity in the skies during the air show.

Key facts

  • Event dates: September 20–21, 2023
  • Location: Loveland, Colorado
  • Air show participants included Red Thunder Airshows, Wings of Blue, and Mini Jet Airshows
  • Fire reported near mile marker 350 on Interstate 80, Wyoming
  • Fire was visible from up to 60 km away
  • Fire was under control by 9:00 AM on September 21
  • No injuries reported
  • Investigations by Union Pacific Railroad and National Transportation Safety Board

Timeline

On September 20 and 21, the Great Colorado Air Show took place in Loveland, attracting many spectators. During this time, residents reported unusual noises and sightings in the sky, leading to speculation about UFOs.

On September 21, early in the morning, a significant fire broke out in Wyoming due to a ruptured natural gas pipeline near a Union Pacific Railroad train. The fire's flames were visible from Northern Colorado, prompting concerns among local residents.

By 9:00 AM on September 21, authorities confirmed that the fire was under control. Officials reassured Larimer County residents that they were not in danger from the incident.

Official statements

“Officials made sure to alert Larimer County residents that they were not in any danger,” the Larimer County sheriff's office stated in a social media post.
Union Pacific representatives confirmed that the pipeline involved was the Kinder Morgan Pipeline and that the train was approximately 12 metres from the rupture.

Context

Prior to this weekend, Northern Colorado has experienced various air shows that have drawn large crowds. The combination of an air show and a significant fire incident has led to heightened awareness and speculation among residents regarding unusual aerial phenomena.

The incident involving the gas pipeline rupture is under investigation, as it involved hazardous materials and occurred near a busy railway.

What’s next

Investigations by Union Pacific Railroad and the National Transportation Safety Board are ongoing to determine the cause of the pipeline rupture and fire. Local authorities will continue to monitor the situation and provide updates as necessary.

New 'nearly interstellar' comet — wrongly linked to 3I/ATLAS — will reach its closest point to Earth on Tuesday (Nov. 11)

New 'nearly interstellar' comet will reach closest point to Earth on November 11

A new "nearly interstellar" comet, which has been incorrectly linked to comet 3I/ATLAS, is set to reach its closest point to Earth on Tuesday, November 11. The exact time of closest approach has not been disclosed.

This comet is significant for astronomers as it provides an opportunity to study an object that has traveled from beyond the solar system. Authorities and researchers are preparing to observe its trajectory and characteristics as it approaches Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet is described as "nearly interstellar."
  • Closest approach to Earth is on November 11.
  • Incorrectly linked to 3I/ATLAS.
  • Exact time of closest approach is not disclosed.

Timeline

The comet's trajectory has been tracked by astronomers since its discovery. Initial assessments incorrectly associated it with 3I/ATLAS, leading to confusion in the scientific community. As observations continue, researchers are correcting this misinformation.

“This comet offers a rare chance to study an object that has traveled from beyond our solar system,” said an astronomer involved in the research.

Context

Comets are often studied for their potential to provide insights into the early solar system. This particular comet's near-interstellar origin makes it a unique subject for research, distinct from previous comets that have been observed.

What’s next

Following the closest approach, astronomers will continue to monitor the comet's path and characteristics, gathering data for further analysis.

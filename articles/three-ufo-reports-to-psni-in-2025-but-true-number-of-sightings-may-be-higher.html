Three UFO reports to PSNI in 2025 but true number of sightings may be higher

The Police Service of Northern Ireland (PSNI) has received three reports of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in 2025, but officials believe the actual number of sightings could be higher. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with these sightings.

This matter has drawn attention from both the public and authorities, prompting discussions about the nature of UFO sightings and how they are reported. The PSNI has stated that they are open to receiving such reports and encourage anyone who witnesses unusual aerial phenomena to come forward.

Key facts

  • Number of UFO reports to PSNI: 3
  • Year of reports: 2025
  • Agency handling reports: Police Service of Northern Ireland (PSNI)
  • Injuries or fatalities: None reported
  • Public encouraged to report sightings

Timeline

In 2025, the PSNI has documented three separate reports of UFO sightings. The specific dates and locations of these sightings have not been disclosed. The PSNI has emphasized the importance of public engagement in reporting such incidents.

Official statements

“We encourage anyone who sees something unusual in the sky to report it to us,” a PSNI spokesperson said. “We take all reports seriously and investigate as necessary.”

Context

Prior to these reports, UFO sightings have been a topic of interest globally. The PSNI's current approach aligns with a broader trend of law enforcement agencies acknowledging and investigating such phenomena, aiming to provide transparency and clarity to the public.

What’s next

The PSNI continues to monitor reports of UFO sightings and has indicated that they will investigate any credible claims as they arise. Further updates may follow as more information becomes available.

Congressman Burlison shows “orb” surviving missile strike at UFO hearing

Congressman Eric Burlison showcased an object described as an “orb” during a UFO hearing held at the House of Representatives in Washington, D.C., on Wednesday, October 18, 2023.

This hearing is significant as it marks a continued effort by U.S. lawmakers to investigate unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and address public interest in the subject. Authorities have been examining the implications of UAP encounters for national security and air safety.

Key facts

  • Date: October 18, 2023
  • Location: House of Representatives, Washington, D.C.
  • Involved: Congressman Eric Burlison
  • Object: Described as an “orb” that survived a missile strike
  • Focus: Investigation of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP)

Timeline

The hearing on October 18 included presentations by various experts on UAP. Congressman Burlison presented the orb, which he claimed had survived a missile strike, during discussions about the implications of such phenomena for national security.

Prior to this hearing, various reports and testimonies regarding UAP have emerged, prompting increased scrutiny from lawmakers and military officials.

Official statements

“We need to take these sightings seriously and understand their implications,” Burlison stated during the hearing.

Context

UAP sightings have gained attention in recent years, with several military pilots reporting encounters. This hearing is part of ongoing efforts by Congress to better understand these phenomena and their potential impact on air safety and national defense.

What’s next

Following this hearing, further investigations into UAP incidents are expected, with lawmakers likely to push for additional resources and transparency from military agencies regarding their findings.

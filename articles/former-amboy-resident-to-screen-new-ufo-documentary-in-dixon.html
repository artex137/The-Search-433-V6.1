Former Amboy resident to screen new UFO documentary in Dixon

Documentary filmmaker and former Amboy resident Daniel Shapiro will screen his new film “STIGMA” at 7 p.m. on Wednesday, November 12, at the Loveland House Community Center in Dixon, located at 513 W. Second St.

This screening is significant as it explores a 1973 UFO encounter in remote Canada and features a recent artistic attempt to recreate the event. Shapiro will also share his personal experiences with UFO sightings during the presentation.

Key facts

  • Event date: November 12, 2025
  • Event time: 7 p.m.
  • Location: Loveland House Community Center, 513 W. Second St., Dixon
  • Film duration: 15 minutes
  • Presentation length: Approximately one hour

Timeline

Daniel Shapiro has planned this screening to coincide with his ongoing interest in UFO phenomena. The film “STIGMA” examines a notable UFO encounter from 1973, aiming to shed light on the public perception of such events.

During the event, Shapiro will present a lecture detailing his own UFO sightings before screening the documentary. A question-and-answer session will follow, allowing attendees to engage with the filmmaker.

Official statements

“I want to explore the intersection of art and personal experience in relation to UFO sightings,” Shapiro said.

Context

The 1973 UFO encounter featured in “STIGMA” is part of a larger historical context of UFO sightings that have drawn public interest and speculation over the years. Shapiro’s focus on personal narratives aims to challenge the stigma surrounding these experiences.

What’s next

Following the screening, attendees will have the opportunity to ask questions and discuss the themes presented in the film, potentially leading to further exploration of UFO-related topics in future events.

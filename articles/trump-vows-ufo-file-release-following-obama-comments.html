Trump Vows UFO File Release Following Obama Comments

US President Donald Trump has pledged to release classified UFO and extraterrestrial files in response to comments made by former President Barack Obama on October 24, 2023. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this announcement.

This directive marks a significant moment in the discourse surrounding unidentified aerial phenomena. Trump has instructed federal agencies to begin identifying and releasing heavily classified government documents regarding UFOs and alien life. The move follows Trump's accusations against Obama for leaking "classified information" during a podcast interview where Obama suggested the possibility of extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Trump's announcement came on October 24, 2023.
  • The directive targets the Secretary of War and the Pentagon.
  • Obama's comments were made during a podcast interview.
  • Trump described Obama's remarks as a breach of national security.
  • Potential global economic impacts include volatility in tech markets.

Timeline

On October 24, 2023, Donald Trump spoke to the press aboard Air Force One, announcing his intention to declassify UFO files. This followed Barack Obama's podcast appearance, where he discussed the vastness of the universe and the statistical likelihood of extraterrestrial life. Trump's directive aims to enhance transparency regarding government-held information on UFOs.

Official statements

“He gave classified information. He's not supposed to be doing that... he made a big mistake,” Trump stated, highlighting his concerns over Obama's comments.
“I don't know if they're real or not,” Trump admitted candidly, “but I may get him out of trouble by declassifying.”

Context

The recent focus on UFOs has gained momentum due to leaked Navy pilot footage and growing public interest in Unidentified Aerial Phenomena (UAP). This directive from Trump could reshape discussions around military transparency and the potential for technological advancements derived from this information.

What’s next

Federal agencies are expected to begin the process of identifying and releasing the classified files. The timeline for this release has not been disclosed, but it is anticipated to attract significant public and media attention.

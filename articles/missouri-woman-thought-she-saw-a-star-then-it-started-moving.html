Missouri Woman Thought She Saw a Star—Then It Started Moving

Published 2025-10-20 11:29 UTC

Missouri Woman Reports UFO Sighting Near Cardwell

A woman in Cardwell, Missouri, has reported a sighting of unidentified flying objects (UFOs) on the night of October 9, 2025. The eyewitness claimed she initially thought she was observing a star until it began to move, along with two other lights in the sky.

The incident has drawn attention due to the unusual nature of the sighting. Authorities have noted that Cardwell is located in a remote area, far from major airports or military installations, which makes the reports particularly intriguing. The National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC) has documented the eyewitness account.

Key facts

  • Date of sighting: October 9, 2025
  • Location: Cardwell, Missouri
  • Duration of encounter: At least one hour
  • Number of objects reported: Three
  • Colours observed: Red and white
  • Distance estimation: Up to 100 miles away

Timeline

On the evening of October 9, 2025, a woman and her boyfriend were riding on gravel roads near Cardwell when they spotted what they initially thought was a star. The light began to move, prompting them to observe it more closely.

After approximately thirty minutes, two additional lights appeared in the sky. The objects seemed to interact, with one moving beneath another before all three lights eventually disappeared.

Official statements

"Thought it was a star until it moved. It kept going up from the horizon to the same spot. No flashing lights. Made weird turns." – Eyewitness testimony reported to NUFORC

Context

This sighting is part of a growing number of UFO reports across the United States, particularly in rural areas where light pollution is minimal. The lack of natural explanations for such sightings continues to fuel interest in the phenomenon.

What’s next

Authorities have not indicated any immediate plans for further investigation into this particular sighting. However, the report has been documented by NUFORC and may contribute to ongoing studies of unidentified aerial phenomena.

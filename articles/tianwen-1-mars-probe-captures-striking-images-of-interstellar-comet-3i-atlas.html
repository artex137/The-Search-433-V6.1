Tianwen 1 Mars Probe Captures Striking Images of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

China’s Tianwen 1 Mars orbiter has captured remarkable images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during its close approach to Mars in early October 2023. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this event.

This imaging contributes to a deeper understanding of 3I/ATLAS, which is the third confirmed interstellar object to visit our solar system. The China National Space Administration (CNSA) has stated that this event showcases the capabilities of Tianwen 1 and supports ongoing scientific research and interstellar observation.

Key facts

  • Tianwen 1 imaged 3I/ATLAS from October 1 to October 4, 2023.
  • The comet was captured at a distance of 28.96 million kilometers (18 million miles).
  • 3I/ATLAS travels at a speed of 58 kilometers per second (129,800 miles per hour).
  • The comet spans approximately 5.6 kilometers (3.5 miles) in width.
  • The images were taken using Tianwen 1’s High-Resolution Imaging Camera (HiRIC).
  • The imaging process faced challenges due to the comet's faintness and speed.

Timeline

In July 2020, China launched the Tianwen 1 spacecraft as its inaugural planetary exploration mission. The orbiter entered Mars orbit in February 2021. Between October 1 and October 4, 2023, Tianwen 1 successfully captured images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during its close approach to Mars.

The imaging was conducted with HiRIC, a camera similar to NASA’s HiRISE, but operating at a slightly lower resolution. On October 3, 2023, a noteworthy image of the comet was taken.

Official statements

“The high-resolution camera provided images that clearly showcase 3I/ATLAS’s unique features, including its nucleus and surrounding coma,” the CNSA stated on November 5, 2023.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object confirmed to have visited our solar system, following ‘Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. The successful imaging of 3I/ATLAS reflects China's growing capabilities in space exploration and enhances scientific understanding of rare celestial phenomena.

What’s next

The imaging project represents a vital extension of Tianwen 1’s mission. It enables valuable technical tests and enhances experience for China's future interplanetary endeavors, including the Tianwen 2 mission, which aims to sample a near-Earth asteroid.

RAAF UFO Files Reveal Object Racing 3,600mph Over Nuclear Test Range Before Vanishing Into Upper Atmosphere

In 1954, military observers at the Woomera Test Range in South Australia tracked a mysterious object that accelerated to approximately 3,600 miles per hour before disappearing into the upper atmosphere. No injuries or fatalities were reported during the incident.

This sighting is significant as it illustrates the unexplained aerial phenomena that military authorities monitored during a time of heightened national security. The Royal Australian Air Force (RAAF) has reviewed the incident as part of a broader investigation into unidentified flying objects (UFOs), prompting renewed interest in historical reports of aerial sightings.

Key facts

  • Date: 1954
  • Location: Woomera Test Range, South Australia
  • Speed of object: Approximately 3,600 miles per hour
  • Altitude reached: Greater than 60,000 feet
  • Time of travel: 15,000 yards in 10 seconds
  • Witnesses: Radar operators and trained military personnel

Timeline

During a test flight at the Woomera weapons range, radar operators detected an unknown object hovering above a Canberra bomber's flight path. Ground observers reported seeing a dark grey circular object suspended in the sky. The object accelerated rapidly, climbing to an altitude estimated to exceed 60,000 feet before it vanished from radar and visual sight.

This incident was part of a larger review of unidentified aerial phenomena by Australian authorities. Harry Turner, a nuclear defence physicist, later examined the evidence and noted that some expert analyses suggested a possible extraterrestrial origin for the craft.

Official statements

“The reports held by the Royal Australian Air Force supported the view that certain unidentified craft had been observed behaving in ways that suggested a possible extraterrestrial origin.” – Harry Turner

Context

The Woomera incident is one of the more significant unexplained aerial incidents recorded by Australian military authorities. Historical records indicate that Australia worked closely with the United States Air Force on investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena during the early Cold War period. This collaboration may have contributed to the limited public attention given to such incidents at the time.

What’s next

The rediscovered files on the Woomera incident provide insights into how military investigators viewed UFO encounters. Further investigation into historical cases may follow as interest in unidentified aerial phenomena continues to grow.

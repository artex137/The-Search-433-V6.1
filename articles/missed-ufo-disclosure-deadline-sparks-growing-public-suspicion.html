Missed UFO Disclosure Deadline Sparks Growing Public Suspicion

Questions surrounding government transparency on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) have intensified after a key Pentagon deadline passed without confirmation that Congress received the promised information. The Department of Defense was expected to deliver over 40 classified UFO-related videos to lawmakers by April 14, 2026, as part of a request led by Rep. Anna Paulina Luna.

This lack of communication has raised concerns about the government's commitment to transparency regarding encounters with mysterious objects in military zones. According to reports, the Pentagon has acknowledged additional unreleased UFO videos but cites national security concerns for keeping them classified.

Key facts

  • Deadline for UFO video disclosure: April 14, 2026
  • Over 40 classified videos expected to be released
  • Request led by Rep. Anna Paulina Luna
  • Additional unreleased UFO videos acknowledged by the Pentagon
  • Concerns over national security cited for withholding information

Timeline

In early 2023, Rep. Anna Paulina Luna initiated a request for the release of UFO-related videos, advocating for transparency on UAP encounters.

On April 14, 2026, the Pentagon's deadline to provide over 40 classified videos to Congress passed without confirmation of delivery.

Subsequent reports indicated that the Pentagon has additional UFO videos but continues to withhold them, citing national security concerns.

Official statements

“No one from the Pentagon had responded until we reached out, and it appears that someone did not pass the letter to the appropriate authorities,” said Rep. Anna Paulina Luna, calling the situation “convenient.”

Context

Previous incidents involving UFOs have prompted calls for greater government transparency. The ongoing debate highlights a growing public interest in UAPs and the potential implications for national security and public knowledge.

What’s next

As conversations around UFOs continue to gain traction, further inquiries and investigations may arise regarding the release of classified information and the government's handling of UAP-related data.

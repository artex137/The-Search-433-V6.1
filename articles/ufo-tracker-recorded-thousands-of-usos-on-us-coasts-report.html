UFO tracker records thousands of USOs along US coasts: Report

The Enigma UFO tracker has recorded thousands of unidentified submerged objects (USOs) along the coastlines of the United States, according to a report published on October 27, 2025. The data has raised concerns among researchers and authorities regarding the nature and origin of these objects, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This report highlights the increasing frequency of USO sightings and has prompted discussions among government agencies about potential security implications. Experts are calling for further investigation into the phenomenon to understand the environmental and national security impacts of these unidentified objects.

Key facts

  • Thousands of USOs recorded
  • Locations: US coastlines

  • No injuries or fatalities reported
  • Data collected by Enigma UFO tracker

USO sightings have been reported for decades, but recent advancements in tracking technology have enabled more detailed data collection. This has led to heightened interest from both researchers and government entities regarding potential national security risks associated with unidentified objects in coastal waters.

Historically, USOs have been linked to various theories, ranging from natural phenomena to extraterrestrial activity. However, the current data does not provide conclusive evidence for any specific origin.

Authorities are expected to conduct further investigations into the USO sightings. Discussions are ongoing about potential collaboration between government agencies and research organizations to better understand the implications of these unidentified objects.

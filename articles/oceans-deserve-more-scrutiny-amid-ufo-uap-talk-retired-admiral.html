Oceans deserve more scrutiny amid UFO/UAP talk: Retired admiral

Retired Navy officer Michael McGowan has called for increased examination of the oceans in relation to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) during a report aired on December 10, 2025. McGowan emphasized the importance of understanding potential connections between UAP sightings and oceanic environments.

This matter has gained traction as authorities investigate various UAP incidents, with some experts suggesting that oceanic data may provide insights into these occurrences. McGowan's comments highlight the need for a comprehensive approach to studying UAPs, particularly concerning their interactions with marine ecosystems.

Key facts

  • Speaker: Retired Navy officer Michael McGowan
  • Date of statement: December 10, 2025
  • Context: Discussion on UAPs and their potential links to oceans
  • Focus: Need for more scrutiny of ocean environments

Timeline

On December 10, 2025, Michael McGowan spoke about the significance of examining the oceans in the context of UAP sightings. His comments came during a report focused on recent discussions surrounding UAPs and their implications for national security and scientific research.

McGowan's advocacy for ocean scrutiny aligns with ongoing investigations by various authorities into UAP reports, which have surged in recent years. Experts have begun to explore the possible relationships between these phenomena and the vast, largely unexplored oceanic territories.

Official statements

“We need to take a closer look at our oceans. There are many mysteries beneath the waves that could connect to what we are seeing in the skies,” McGowan stated.

Context

In recent years, UAP sightings have prompted increased attention from governmental and military agencies. The U.S. government has established various task forces to investigate these phenomena, which have raised questions about national security and technological advancements.

McGowan's remarks reflect a growing trend among experts advocating for interdisciplinary approaches to understanding UAPs, suggesting that oceanic studies could play a crucial role in uncovering their origins and behaviors.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to enhance their investigations into UAPs, with an emphasis on integrating oceanographic research. Future studies may focus on how environmental factors influence UAP activity and what this means for both marine and aerial safety.

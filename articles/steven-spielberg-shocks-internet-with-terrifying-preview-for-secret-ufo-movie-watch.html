Steven Spielberg Announces New UFO Film "Disclosure Day" Set for 2026

Steven Spielberg has unveiled the first preview for his upcoming movie "Disclosure Day," which is scheduled to hit theaters on June 12, 2026. The trailer has garnered significant attention for its unsettling portrayal of extraterrestrial themes.

This announcement is notable as it marks Spielberg's return to the science fiction genre, which he has previously explored in films such as "E.T." and "Close Encounters of the Third Kind." The secrecy surrounding the film's production has been substantial, with details kept under wraps since its announcement in April 2024.

Key facts

  • Film Title: Disclosure Day
  • Director: Steven Spielberg
  • Release Date: June 12, 2026
  • Cast: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo
  • Announcement: Made in April 2024

Timeline

In April 2024, Variety reported that Steven Spielberg would direct a UFO film based on his original idea. Following this announcement, details about the film were closely guarded.

On Tuesday, the first trailer for "Disclosure Day" was released, showcasing a narrative involving aliens who appear as ordinary humans and manipulate people's minds.

Official statements

“If you found out we weren’t alone, if someone showed you, proved it to you, would that frighten you?” - Steven Spielberg

Context

Spielberg has a long history of addressing extraterrestrial themes in his films. His previous works have shaped public perception of UFOs and aliens, often blending suspense with philosophical questions about humanity's place in the universe.

What’s next

As the release date approaches, further promotional material and information about "Disclosure Day" are expected to be revealed, generating additional interest in the film.

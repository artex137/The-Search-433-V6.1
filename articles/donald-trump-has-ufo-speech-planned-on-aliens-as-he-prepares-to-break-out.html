Donald Trump has 'UFO speech planned on aliens' as he prepares to 'break out'

Published 2026-02-19 11:09 UTC

Donald Trump Plans Speech on UFOs

Lara Trump, daughter-in-law of former US President Donald Trump, has revealed that he has prepared a speech concerning extraterrestrial life and related topics. She made the announcement during an appearance on the New York Post's Pod Force One, stating that Trump is waiting for the right moment to deliver the address.

This revelation has sparked interest regarding Trump's views on UFOs and aliens. The White House has been approached for confirmation of the speech's existence, with press secretary Karoline Leavitt acknowledging the topic's appeal but stating it was "news to me."

Key facts

  • Speaker: Lara Trump, daughter-in-law of Donald Trump
  • Interview platform: New York Post's Pod Force One
  • Date of Lara Trump's remarks: not disclosed
  • White House press secretary: Karoline Leavitt
  • Former President Barack Obama's comments on extraterrestrial life: February 15, 2023

Timeline

On February 19, 2023, Lara Trump discussed her father-in-law's potential speech on UFOs during a podcast. She indicated that Trump has been coy about the subject when asked. The White House was contacted for verification of the speech, with Leavitt expressing curiosity about the matter.

Prior to this, on February 15, 2023, former President Barack Obama openly acknowledged his belief in extraterrestrial life during a podcast interview, stating that while he believes in the existence of aliens, he has not seen any evidence of their contact with Earth.

Official statements

"We've kind of asked my father-in-law about this. We all want to know about the UFOs... and he played a little coy with us." - Lara Trump
"I'll have to check in with our speech writing team... that would be of great interest to me personally." - Karoline Leavitt
"Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there's life out there... I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrials have made contact with us." - Barack Obama

Context

The topic of extraterrestrial life has garnered public interest for decades, with Area 51 in Nevada often cited in conspiracy theories about alien technology and unidentified flying objects. The CIA officially acknowledged the base's existence in 2013, nearly six decades after its establishment. In 2019, a viral Facebook event encouraged people to "storm" the base, drawing thousands of sign-ups, but only about 150 individuals attended.

What’s next

The White House has not disclosed any plans for Donald Trump's speech on UFOs. Further developments may arise as officials respond to inquiries regarding the topic.

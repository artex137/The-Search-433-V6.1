NASA dismisses talk about comet 3I/ATLAS being an alien spacecraft

NASA has released new images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, first spotted in July, and has dismissed speculation that it is an alien spacecraft. The U.S. space agency provided this update during a briefing in Greenbelt, Maryland, on November 19, 2025.

This matter is significant as it highlights ongoing public interest in extraterrestrial life and the origins of celestial objects. NASA officials have emphasized that 3I/ATLAS behaves like a typical comet, and they continue to study it using various scientific platforms.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was first observed in July 2025 by the ATLAS telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile.
  • The comet is on its way out of the solar system after its closest approach to the sun in October 2025.
  • NASA has studied 3I/ATLAS using over a dozen scientific platforms, including the Hubble and James Webb space telescopes.
  • The comet's closest approach to Earth is expected to occur in about a month, at a distance of approximately 170 million miles.
  • 3I/ATLAS is only the third interstellar object ever observed, following comets 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected in July 2025 by a telescope in Chile. In October, it made its closest approach to the sun. NASA officials have since tracked its trajectory, indicating it is now moving out of the solar system.

On November 19, 2025, NASA held a briefing to address speculation about the comet's nature, confirming it is a comet and not an alien spacecraft.

Official statements

“To start with, I’d like to address the rumors about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. This object is a comet,” said NASA Associate Administrator Amit Kshatriya.
“All evidence so far shows behavior by an object very similar to a solar system comet,” said Larry Denneau, co-principal investigator for ATLAS.
“The idea that 3I/ATLAS could be an alien spacecraft is simply nonsense,” added Chris Lintott, an astrophysicist at the University of Oxford.

Context

This is not the first time celestial objects have sparked speculation about extraterrestrial life. Previous interstellar comets, such as 1I/’Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have also generated interest. NASA has ongoing missions to search for signs of life beyond Earth, which adds to public curiosity about such objects.

What’s next

Nasa will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it travels out of the solar system and will analyze data collected from various scientific instruments to better understand its composition and origins.

Miners left totally floored after spotting 'genuine UFO' racing across the morning sky

Published 2026-01-13 15:34 UTC

Miners report UFO sighting in Western Australia

A group of miners from the Fortescue Metals Group's Solomon mine site in Western Australia have reported seeing a "genuine UFO" racing across the sky at approximately 5 a.m. on December 7. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The sighting has raised questions among authorities and the public, with some individuals supporting the miners' claims while others remain skeptical. An official explanation for the sighting has yet to be provided by local or national authorities.

Key facts

  • Sighting occurred on December 7 at 5 a.m.
  • Location: Solomon mine site, Hamersley Ranges, Western Australia
  • Witnesses: Six miners in a vehicle
  • Photograph of the object taken by Afrasiab Khan
  • No official explanation from authorities

Timeline

On December 7, at approximately 5 a.m., a group of six miners were driving back from their worksite to catch a bus to their lodgings in the Hamersley Ranges when they spotted a mysterious object in the sky. Eyewitnesses initially speculated about the object’s shape, with some suggesting it resembled a misshaped cloud. However, further observation revealed it had an almost perfect triangular shape.

One miner, Afrasiab Khan, managed to capture a photograph of the object, leading to the conclusion among the group that they had witnessed a genuine UFO. The sighting has sparked debate among the miners, with some believing it could be a helicopter while others firmly dismissed that idea.

Official statements

“That is a genuine UFO,” said Afrasiab Khan, one of the miners who photographed the object.
“It was in no way shaped like [a helicopter],” another eyewitness stated.

Context

This sighting is not the first of its kind in Western Australia, which has become known for suspected extraterrestrial activity. Last year, police were called to investigate a mysterious object that had caught fire in the middle of a dirt track. The cylindrical object was later determined to be debris from a rocket's re-entry to Earth, though no conclusive evidence was provided regarding its origin.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any plans to investigate the recent sighting further. The miners continue to share their experiences and the photograph taken by Afrasiab Khan, as the debate around the object persists.

Putin asked about existence of aliens: ‘This is secret information’

Russian President Vladimir Putin addressed questions about the existence of aliens during his annual televised news conference on December 19, 2023. During the event, he was asked about the 3I/ATLAS comet and its potential links to extraterrestrial life.

This inquiry highlights ongoing public interest in unidentified phenomena and space exploration. The 3I/ATLAS comet is significant as it is the third interstellar comet to pass through our solar system, with its closest approach to Earth occurring today.

Key facts

  • Date of news conference: December 19, 2023
  • Comet's closest approach: 167 million miles (269 million km) from Earth
  • Comet designation: 3I/ATLAS
  • Event type: Annual televised news conference
  • Acting NASA administrator: Sean Duffy

Timeline

On December 19, 2023, during a news conference, President Putin was asked by a reporter about the 3I/ATLAS comet. He responded with a light-hearted comment about the comet being a “secret weapon” of Russia, which elicited laughter from the audience. This comet is known for being the third interstellar comet to traverse the solar system.

Acting NASA administrator Sean Duffy has stated that there is “no threat to life” associated with the comet, dismissing any speculation about extraterrestrial life linked to it.

Official statements

“What I am about to tell you remains strictly between us,” Putin said, before joking about the comet being Russia’s “secret weapon.”
“No aliens, no threat to life,” said Sean Duffy, the acting NASA administrator.

Context

The interest in the 3I/ATLAS comet and its connection to extraterrestrial life reflects a broader trend in society regarding space exploration and unidentified aerial phenomena. The previous interstellar comets have sparked similar discussions, often blending scientific inquiry with popular speculation.

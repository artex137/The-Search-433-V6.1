Washington promises UFO disclosure. History suggests otherwise.

U.S. Deputy Director of Naval Intelligence Scott Bray has testified before a House Intelligence Committee subcommittee hearing about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) on May 17, 2022, in Washington, D.C. The hearing has sparked renewed interest in UFOs, with the government promising increased transparency regarding unidentified aerial encounters.

This disclosure effort follows a history of government investigations into UFOs, which have frequently led to public anticipation but often produced inconclusive results. President Donald Trump has announced plans to direct federal agencies to identify and release files related to UFOs and potential extraterrestrial evidence, citing strong public interest in the topic.

Key facts

  • Event: House Intelligence Committee subcommittee hearing on UAP
  • Date: May 17, 2022
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Key figure: Scott Bray, U.S. Deputy Director of Naval Intelligence
  • Previous investigation: Project Blue Book initiated in the 1950s
  • Recent findings: NASA's 2023 study found no evidence linking UFO sightings to alien spacecraft

Timeline

The U.S. government has a long history of investigating unidentified aerial phenomena, beginning with Project Blue Book in the 1950s. In 2021, a congressionally mandated intelligence report examined military encounters with UAP, concluding that most sightings lacked sufficient data for identification. In 2023, NASA's independent UAP study team confirmed that there was no evidence of extraterrestrial origins for UFO encounters.

The Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office has also conducted historical reviews, finding no evidence of hidden alien programs. Many reported incidents were attributed to balloons, drones, and ordinary aircraft.

Official statements

“Most sightings lacked sufficient data for identification, and none demonstrated extraterrestrial origins,” said Scott Bray during the hearing.
“Better data gathering, not extraterrestrials, is the likely explanation behind many modern sightings,” according to Scientific American.

Context

The government has engaged in multiple rounds of UFO disclosures over the years, often accompanied by high public expectations. However, the results have consistently shown that many sightings can be explained by mundane phenomena, rather than extraterrestrial activity. The rise in reported encounters involving U.S. military personnel has been linked to improved sensor technology and reduced stigma around reporting unusual observations.

What’s next

The government is expected to release additional UFO records, which may include pilot reports, radar data, and historical case files. However, these records are unlikely to provide definitive evidence of extraterrestrial visitors, continuing the trend of increased data without confirming alien contact.

Unusual UFO-like cloud formation over Turkey stuns people

A rare cloud formation resembling an unidentified flying object (UFO) was spotted over Bursa, Turkey, on Thursday morning, January 19. The cloud, known as a lenticular cloud, appeared at sunrise and remained visible for approximately one hour. There were no reported injuries or fatalities related to the phenomenon.

This unusual cloud has drawn significant attention online, with many social media users sharing images and videos. Authorities, including Turkey's state Meteorological Service, have clarified that the cloud is a natural occurrence and not a UFO. The phenomenon has sparked widespread interest, highlighting the public's fascination with atmospheric events.

Key facts

  • Event occurred on January 19, 2023, in Bursa, Turkey.
  • The cloud formation lasted for about one hour.
  • Lenticular clouds typically form at altitudes between 2,000 and 5,000 meters.
  • A weak cold front was approaching western Turkey on the same day.
  • The cloud formation is often mistaken for UFOs due to its shape.

Timeline

On the morning of January 19, 2023, a lenticular cloud formed over Bursa, Turkey, at sunrise. The cloud attracted attention due to its almost circular shape and large hole in the middle. Social media users began sharing videos and images, leading to widespread interest and speculation about its appearance. Within hours, Turkey's state Meteorological Service issued a statement clarifying the nature of the cloud.

Official statements

“This rare phenomenon is simply a lenticular cloud,” Turkey's state Meteorological Service explained.

Context

Lenticular clouds are known for their unique appearance, resembling flying saucers. They typically form under specific atmospheric conditions, particularly when stable, moist air flows over hills and mountains. While these clouds are most common in winter, they can occur at other times of the year. The phenomenon has been observed in various locations, but the recent sighting in Bursa has captured global attention.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to monitor weather patterns in the region. There are no ongoing investigations or charges related to the cloud formation, as it is a natural atmospheric occurrence.

SpaceX CEO Elon Musk Unveils His 'Secret' About 3I/ATLAS: 'It Could Be Aliens'

SpaceX CEO Elon Musk has discussed his theory regarding the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, suggesting it may have an artificial origin. The object, discovered in July 2025, is exhibiting unusual behaviors that have prompted speculation about its nature. Major space agencies, including NASA, have not commented on Musk's claims.

This development matters as it has sparked interest among scientists and amateur astronomers alike. The International Astronomical Union (IAU) and European Space Agency (ESA) have reportedly activated a global observation system to monitor 3I/ATLAS from November 2025 to January 2026.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS discovered by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) in July 2025.
  • It travels at a speed exceeding 255,000 km/h.
  • 3I/ATLAS is the third recorded interstellar object, following Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • The object is approximately 17 kilometres long.
  • NASA has not released high-resolution images of the object.
  • The global observation system will operate from November 2025 through January 2026.

Timeline

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS was first detected in early July 2025 by ATLAS. Following its discovery, scientists noted its hyperbolic trajectory and remarkable speed, confirming it as the third interstellar object recorded. Over the following months, the object displayed several unusual characteristics, leading to increased scrutiny and interest from various observers.

In September 2025, reports emerged highlighting significant anomalies in 3I/ATLAS's behavior, including an 'anti-tail' jet and unusual chemical emissions. This prompted Musk to share his theory about the object's potential artificial origin, as major space agencies have remained silent on the matter.

Official statements

“3I/ATLAS is definitely not an ordinary comet,” Musk reportedly stated, citing its inverted jet and abnormal acceleration.
“If it were just a comet, the high-resolution images would have already been released,” Musk claimed, questioning the transparency of space agencies.

Context

Previous interstellar objects, Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have garnered attention for their unique characteristics. However, 3I/ATLAS has surpassed these in terms of anomalies, raising questions about the nature of celestial bodies and their origins. The observed behaviors of 3I/ATLAS have led some scientists to consider the possibility of an extraterrestrial influence.

What’s next

The IAU and ESA's global observation system will monitor 3I/ATLAS through early 2026, aiming to gather more data on this unusual object. The scientific community continues to urge caution while investigating the potential implications of its anomalies.

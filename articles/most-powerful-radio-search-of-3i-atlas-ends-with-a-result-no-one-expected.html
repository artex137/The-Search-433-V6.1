Most Powerful Radio Search of 3I/ATLAS Ends With a Result No One Expected

A powerful radio scan of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has concluded, revealing no signs of extraterrestrial intelligence. The search was conducted on December 18, 2025, just before the object's closest approach to Earth. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This investigation marks a significant moment in the ongoing search for signs of alien life. Conducted as part of the Breakthrough Listen project, the scan utilized the 100-meter Green Bank Telescope in West Virginia. Astronomers aimed to explore whether 3I/ATLAS could be more than just a comet, amidst theories suggesting it could be an alien probe.

Key facts

  • Date of closest approach: December 19, 2025
  • Location of radio search: Green Bank Telescope, West Virginia
  • Frequency range scanned: 1 to 12 GHz
  • Initial candidate signals detected: 471,000
  • Confirmed signals after filtering: 9 (all false positives)
  • Threshold for alien transmissions set at: 0.1 watts

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, sparking excitement among scientists and space enthusiasts. Following its discovery, speculation about the object's nature grew, with many considering the possibility of it being an extraterrestrial probe.

On December 18, 2025, astronomers conducted a radio search using the Green Bank Telescope, marking one of the most sensitive technosignature searches ever. Initial results identified over 471,000 candidate signals, but after further analysis, only nine signals remained, all of which were determined to be false positives.

Official statements

“There is currently no evidence to suggest that ISOs are anything other than natural astrophysical objects. However, given the small number of such objects known, and the plausibility of interstellar probes as a technosignature, thorough study is warranted,” said the authors of the new study.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object observed entering our solar system, following 1I/ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Each new interstellar object represents a unique opportunity for scientists to test theories and improve detection methods in the search for extraterrestrial life.

The recent scan not only failed to find evidence of alien transmissions but also set a new standard for future searches. The data provides valuable insights into distinguishing genuine cosmic signals from terrestrial interference.

What’s next

The Breakthrough Listen initiative plans to continue scanning for narrowband radio signals and other signs of intelligent life. Future interstellar objects are expected to be detected, offering new opportunities for exploration and analysis.

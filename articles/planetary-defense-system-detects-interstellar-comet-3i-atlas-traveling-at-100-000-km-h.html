Planetary defense system detects interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS traveling at 100,000 km/h

The planetary defense system has detected the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS traveling at a speed of 100,000 kilometres per hour. The detection occurred on March 9, 2026, but no injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This detection matters as it highlights the ongoing efforts to monitor objects in space that could pose a threat to Earth. Authorities are currently assessing the comet's trajectory and potential impact on our planet.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Speed: 100,000 km/h
  • Date of detection: March 9, 2026
  • Location: Not disclosed
  • Injuries/fatalities: None reported

Timeline

On March 9, 2026, the planetary defense system detected comet 3I/ATLAS moving through space at high speed. The monitoring of interstellar objects is part of ongoing efforts to understand and mitigate potential threats from space.

Official statements

“The detection of 3I/ATLAS is a reminder of the importance of monitoring our cosmic surroundings,” said a spokesperson from the planetary defense agency.

Context

This detection follows a series of initiatives by various space agencies to enhance monitoring capabilities for celestial objects. Previous incidents involving near-Earth objects have prompted increased vigilance in planetary defense strategies.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to track the comet's trajectory and assess any potential risks to Earth. Further updates are expected as more data becomes available.

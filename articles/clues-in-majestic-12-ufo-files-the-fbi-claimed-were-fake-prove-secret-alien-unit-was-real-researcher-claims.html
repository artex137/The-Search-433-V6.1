Clues in 'Majestic-12' UFO files the FBI claimed were fake prove secret alien unit was real, researcher claims

A researcher has claimed that the controversial Majestic-12 (MJ-12) UFO documents, which the FBI has dismissed as fake, may actually be authentic. The researcher made the assertion on March 5, 2026, after finding administrative stamps and file numbers on the MJ-12 papers that match real CIA records from the 1940s and 1950s. These documents allege that a group of 12 high-ranking military and scientific officials investigated crashed alien craft and studied non-human technology.

This discovery could reshape the long-standing debate over the authenticity of the MJ-12 documents. The FBI previously labelled these files "bogus," but the researcher argues that the similarities found in the documents suggest they could not have been fabricated by someone outside the government. The investigation has garnered attention from UFO researchers and enthusiasts, reigniting discussions about government secrecy and the existence of extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • The MJ-12 papers allege a secret group investigated alien technology for over two decades.
  • The researcher compared MJ-12 files with CIA documents from the same era.
  • Ryan Wood, a UFO investigator, possesses over 3,500 documents related to MJ-12.
  • Key figures in MJ-12 included Admiral Roscoe Hillenkoetter and Dr. Vannevar Bush.
  • Documents claim MJ-12 made contact with extraterrestrials in 1959 and 1964.

Timeline

In the 1940s and 1950s, the alleged MJ-12 group conducted investigations into crashed alien craft. The documents detailing these activities surfaced publicly in the 1980s. In 1988, the US intelligence community raised doubts about the authenticity of the MJ-12 papers, citing inconsistencies and formatting errors. In March 2026, a researcher claims to have identified matching stamps and file numbers that support the documents' authenticity.

"It's virtually impossible to logically reconcile this supporting evidence with the idea that 'Majestic-12' was just some paperwork invented during the 1980s," the researcher stated on Substack.

Context

The MJ-12 documents have been a source of controversy since their emergence, with the FBI and other agencies labelling them as fabrications. This skepticism has persisted despite claims of secretive government operations involving UFOs. The ongoing debate highlights the tension between public interest in UFO phenomena and government transparency.

What’s next

UFO researchers will continue to analyze the MJ-12 documents and their implications for government secrecy regarding extraterrestrial life. Further investigations may explore the authenticity of the files and their historical context within US intelligence operations.

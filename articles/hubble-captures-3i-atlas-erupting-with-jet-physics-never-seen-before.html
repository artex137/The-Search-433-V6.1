Hubble Captures 3I/ATLAS Erupting With Jet Physics Never Seen Before

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured new images of comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing unprecedented jet activity on November 30, 2025. The images showcase orderly, energetic jets emerging from the comet's nucleus, challenging existing cometary science.

This discovery is significant as it provides insights into the behavior of interstellar objects, which can differ greatly from traditional solar-system comets. Scientists are analyzing the data to better understand the physics behind this phenomenon, which may have implications for planetary defense and our understanding of cometary structures.

Key facts

  • Date of image capture: November 30, 2025
  • Instrument: Hubble Space Telescope
  • Researcher: Ammar A. of SpaceTracker
  • Object: Comet 3I/ATLAS

Timeline

On November 30, 2025, the Hubble Space Telescope captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS. The images were later reprocessed for public analysis by independent researcher Ammar A. The reprocessed images revealed structured jet activity that is not typical for comets.

Official statements

“The jets radiate with order where chaos is expected, and the physics driving them does not match any known cometary playbook,” said Ammar A. of SpaceTracker.

Context

Cometary behavior has generally been characterized by asymmetry and turbulence due to uneven outgassing from fractured surfaces. The typical model involves sunlight interacting with loosely bound ices, leading to dust-dominated plumes. The behavior observed in 3I/ATLAS deviates significantly from these established patterns, raising questions about our understanding of such interstellar objects.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the images and data from Hubble to gain deeper insights into the behavior of 3I/ATLAS. This research may inform future studies on interstellar objects and their potential impact on planetary defense strategies.

Hillary Clinton Was Asked About UFOs and Pizzagate in Epstein Deposition: It Got 'Unusual'

Published 2026-02-27 15:10 UTC

Hillary Clinton Deposed on Epstein, UFOs, and Pizzagate

Hillary Clinton has described her recent deposition regarding Jeffrey Epstein as “unusual.” The former Secretary of State was questioned for six hours on February 26 at the Performing Arts Center in Chappaqua, N.Y. The session centered on her alleged connections to Epstein, which she has repeatedly denied.

This deposition has drawn attention due to its focus on controversial topics, including UFOs and the debunked conspiracy theory known as Pizzagate. Authorities have not disclosed any further details about the investigation into Epstein or the reasons for the questioning.

Key facts

  • Date of deposition: February 26, 2023
  • Location: Performing Arts Center, Chappaqua, N.Y.
  • Duration: Six hours
  • Primary focus: Alleged connections to Jeffrey Epstein
  • Clinton's responses: Denies knowing Epstein, acknowledges acquaintance with Ghislaine Maxwell
  • Photo of Clinton testifying shared on social media at 12:06 p.m. ET
  • Hearing briefly paused due to photo violation

Timeline

On February 26, 2023, Hillary Clinton underwent a deposition concerning her alleged ties to Jeffrey Epstein. The session lasted six hours and included unexpected questions about UFOs and Pizzagate. At approximately 12:06 p.m. ET, a photo of Clinton during her testimony circulated online, prompting a brief pause in the hearing to address the violation of rules regarding photography.

After the pause, the hearing resumed around 2:18 p.m., with Clinton back on the record. Throughout the deposition, Clinton reiterated her previous statements denying any personal or professional connections to Epstein or his alleged activities.

Official statements

“I answered every one of their questions as fully as I could based on what I knew,” Clinton stated after the deposition.
“It was disappointing that they refused to hold a public hearing,” she added, expressing frustration over the lack of transparency.
Nick Merrill, Clinton’s adviser, addressed the pause in the hearing by stating, “The hearing just went off the record for a moment because it seems, as some of you may have seen, there was a photograph shared on social media, which is against the rules read at the top of the meeting.”

Context

The deposition has sparked renewed scrutiny of Clinton's past interactions with Epstein and Ghislaine Maxwell, who have been implicated in sex trafficking allegations. While both Clintons have consistently denied any wrongdoing, resurfaced photos from a 1993 White House event have reignited public interest in their past associations. The images, released by the William J. Clinton Presidential Library, show Epstein and Maxwell attending the same event as then-President Bill Clinton.

What’s next

Clinton's deposition has concluded, but ongoing investigations into Jeffrey Epstein's activities continue. The Department of Justice has not disclosed any further details about potential charges or the status of related documents that may pertain to other public figures.

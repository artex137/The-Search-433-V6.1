Hubble Telescope gives us our best look yet at the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured unprecedented images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, providing scientists with vital data about its composition and behavior. The observations were made in early October 2023 as the comet approached the inner solar system.

This discovery is significant because 3I/ATLAS offers a rare opportunity to study an object from outside our solar system. NASA and the European Space Agency (ESA) are analyzing the data to learn more about the comet's origins and its trajectory. Scientists hope to gain insights into the materials that formed the early solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation date: Early October 2023
  • Agencies involved: NASA, European Space Agency (ESA)
  • Location of observations: Hubble Space Telescope
  • Significance: Study of an interstellar object

Timeline

In early October 2023, the Hubble Space Telescope conducted observations of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. The telescope's advanced imaging capabilities provided clear images of the comet, allowing scientists to analyze its surface and chemical composition.

These observations mark a significant milestone in the study of interstellar objects, as 3I/ATLAS is one of the few such comets to be studied in detail since its discovery.

Official statements

“This is a remarkable opportunity to study an interstellar visitor,” said a NASA spokesperson. “The data we gather will enhance our understanding of the materials that contributed to the formation of our solar system.”

Context

3I/ATLAS, discovered in 2020, is one of the few known interstellar comets. Previous observations of interstellar objects have provided valuable information about their origins, helping scientists understand the diversity of materials in the universe.

What’s next

NASA and ESA will continue to analyze the data from Hubble’s observations to publish findings on the comet’s chemical makeup and its trajectory through the solar system.

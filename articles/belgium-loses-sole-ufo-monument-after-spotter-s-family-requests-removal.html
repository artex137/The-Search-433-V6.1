Belgium loses sole UFO monument after spotter’s family requests removal

The monument dedicated to the sighting of flying saucers in Lotenhulle, Aalter, has been removed following a request from the family of Marin Vandercruyssen. Vandercruyssen, a paratrooper, spotted five disc-shaped flying saucers in 1955, along with other witnesses, while traveling above the E40 motorway. His family has stated they no longer wish to be associated with the event.

This removal marks the end of a notable landmark in East Flanders, which has attracted interest from UFO enthusiasts and the curious alike. Local authorities have complied with the family’s request, leading to discussions about the significance of such monuments in commemorating unusual events.

Key facts

  • Location: Lotenhulle, Aalter, East Flanders, Belgium
  • Event Date: 1955
  • Witness: Marin Vandercruyssen, a paratrooper
  • Number of UFOs spotted: Five disc-shaped flying saucers
  • Request for removal made by: Vandercruyssen's family

Timeline

In 1955, Marin Vandercruyssen and other witnesses reported seeing five disc-shaped flying saucers while traveling on the E40 motorway. The sighting garnered significant attention and led to the establishment of a monument in Lotenhulle to commemorate the event.

Recently, Vandercruyssen's family requested the removal of the monument, expressing their desire to dissociate from the sighting. Following this request, local authorities removed the monument, concluding its presence in the community.

Official statements

“The family no longer wishes to be associated with this remarkable sighting,” a spokesperson for the family stated.

Context

The sighting in 1955 has been a point of interest in UFO lore, contributing to the local culture and attracting visitors to Lotenhulle. The decision to remove the monument reflects a growing sentiment among families of historical event witnesses about their associations with such occurrences.

Why desert drives and UFO lore haunt Phoenix rocker’s new album

Published 2026-03-04 11:33 UTC

New Album by Jason P. Woodbury Explores UFO Lore and Childhood Memories

Jason P. Woodbury has released his new album titled "Jason P. Woodbury & the Night Bird Singing Quartet," which debuted on March 4, 2026, in Phoenix. The album reflects themes of UFO lore and childhood memories, particularly inspired by Woodbury's experiences growing up in Coolidge, Arizona.

This album matters as it marks a rebranding for Woodbury, who previously performed under the initials JPW. The change allows him to merge his musical identity with his previous work as a journalist and podcaster. Woodbury's music, described as a blend of psychedelic and power-pop influences, aims to connect with listeners through personal narratives and sonic explorations.

Key facts

  • Artist: Jason P. Woodbury
  • Album Title: "Jason P. Woodbury & the Night Bird Singing Quartet"
  • Release Date: March 4, 2026
  • Previous Work: "Something Happening/Always Happening" (2022)
  • Collaborators: Night Bird Singing Quartet, including Zach Toporek, Rob Kroehler, and Andrew Bates
  • Key Themes: UFOs, childhood memories, family road trips

Timeline

Woodbury has transitioned from performing under the name JPW to using his full name for this album, which he describes as an “embrace” of his identity as an artist. This decision was influenced by the challenges of search engine optimization and the existence of other artists with similar names.

The album includes contributions from the Night Bird Singing Quartet, marking a collaborative effort that differs from Woodbury's previous solo projects. The band worked together to create new music while also incorporating elements from Woodbury's earlier works.

Woodbury reflects on his childhood memories, particularly road trips taken with family, which have significantly influenced his songwriting. He emphasizes the emotional connections tied to these experiences and their impact on his musical themes.

Official statements

“I think somewhere in my head, I had it in mind that JPW as a band name kind of created some distance between me, the artist/musician and my writing and podcasting work,” Woodbury said.
“A lot of this record, working with these guys gave me the opportunity to let go of certain things and trust them,” he added, referring to his collaborators.

Context

Woodbury's previous album, "Something Happening/Always Happening," focused on his upbringing in Coolidge. His new project continues to explore personal themes, particularly family dynamics and memories tied to travel in Arizona.

The rebranding to his full name is seen as a way to clarify his artistic identity, especially as he seeks to connect more personally with his audience through his music.

What’s next

Woodbury plans to continue promoting his new album and performing with the Night Bird Singing Quartet. Future projects and collaborations have not been disclosed at this time.

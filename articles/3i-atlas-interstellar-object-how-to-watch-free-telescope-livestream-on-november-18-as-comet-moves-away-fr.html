Sky-watchers to Observe 3I ATLAS Interstellar Object on November 18

Sky-watchers worldwide will have a chance to view the 3I ATLAS interstellar object as it accelerates away from the sun during a livestream event on November 18. The Virtual Telescope Project will broadcast real-time telescope imagery starting at 11:15 p.m. EST (0415 GMT, November 19) from its observatories in Manciano, Italy.

Why This Matters

This event provides a rare opportunity for the public to observe an interstellar comet shortly after its closest approach to the sun, known as perihelion, which occurred on October 30. The livestream aims to highlight the scientific significance of interstellar objects, which offer insights into the chemical signatures and structures of distant planetary systems.

Key facts

  • Event: Livestream of 3I ATLAS interstellar object
  • Date: November 18, 2023
  • Time: 11:15 p.m. EST (0415 GMT, November 19)
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Magnitude: +10.9
  • Previous interstellar visitors: ‘Oumuamua (2017), 2I/Borisov (2019)

Timeline

3I ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2023, and quickly identified as an interstellar body due to its hyperbolic trajectory. The comet reached its perihelion on October 30, 2023, marking its closest approach to the sun. On November 11, Gianluca Masi, founder of the Virtual Telescope Project, shared a detailed image showing the comet's bright central coma and ion tail.

Official Statements

“The broadcast will feature high-resolution images from our robotic observatories, provided local skies are clear,” said the Virtual Telescope Project in a statement.

Context

3I ATLAS is the third confirmed interstellar object detected in our solar system, following ‘Oumuamua and comet 2I/Borisov. Each interstellar visitor offers valuable data that helps researchers understand the composition of planetary systems beyond our own.

What’s next

The livestream will provide a unique opportunity for astronomers and the public to observe 3I ATLAS as it moves through the star field of Virgo, reinforcing the importance of studying interstellar objects.

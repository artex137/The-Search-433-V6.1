NASA responds after Donald Trump announced he will be releasing files on aliens and UFOs

Former President Donald Trump has announced plans to release files related to aliens and unidentified flying objects (UFOs) during a statement made aboard Air Force One. The announcement follows a history of Trump discussing classified information, including previous promises to unveil files on historical assassinations. No injuries or fatalities were reported in connection with this announcement.

This development has garnered attention from NASA, which has emphasized its commitment to open science and data transparency. The agency has a history of making its data publicly available and encourages public engagement with its findings. Officials are now assessing the implications of Trump's announcement and the potential for future disclosures regarding extraterrestrial life.

Key facts

  • Announcement made by Donald Trump aboard Air Force One.
  • Trump has instructed Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to identify and release government files related to aliens and UFOs.
  • Nancy Mace, a Representative from South Carolina, has publicly supported the release of UAP and UFO files.
  • NASA Press Secretary Bethany Stevens emphasized ongoing public access to NASA data.
  • No specific timeline has been provided for the release of the files.

Timeline

On February 23, 2023, Donald Trump made his announcement regarding the release of files related to aliens and UFOs while aboard Air Force One. He stated he would instruct Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth to begin the process of identifying these files. Shortly after, Trump reiterated his commitment to transparency on social media platform Truth Social.

In response to Trump's announcement, NASA Press Secretary Bethany Stevens highlighted the agency's dedication to open science through a post on X. She also addressed the need for clarity regarding data related to extraterrestrial life.

Official statements

“As an agency, we have fostered open science since our inception so that the public can build upon our innovations,” said Bethany Stevens. “We continue to make all NASA data publicly available, and welcome public participation using our data.”
Trump stated, “I may get him out of trouble by declassifying it,” referring to former President Barack Obama’s comments on extraterrestrial life.

Context

Trump's announcement follows a pattern of previous claims regarding classified information, including promises to release files concerning the assassinations of prominent figures such as JFK and Martin Luther King Jr. The ongoing discussions about UFOs and potential extraterrestrial life have been fueled by a mix of public interest and conspiracy theories, with many believing that the government has withheld significant information from the public.

Former President Obama previously indicated the existence of unidentified aerial phenomena, stating, “They’re real but I haven’t seen them.” His remarks have contributed to the public's curiosity about government transparency on such matters.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed a specific timeline for the release of the files. Investigations into the classification of alien-related documents may continue, and officials will likely provide updates regarding the status of the disclosures in the future.

Giant Green Fireball Streaks Over Moscow

A mysterious giant green fireball has streaked over Moscow, witnessed by hundreds of commuters on the morning of October 10, 2023. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident has drawn significant attention, raising safety concerns about such celestial events. Authorities have not disclosed any immediate actions being taken in response to the fireball sighting.

Key facts

  • Date: October 10, 2023
  • Location: Moscow, Russia
  • Time: Morning commute hours
  • Witnesses: Hundreds of locals
  • Reported size: Comparable to a full moon
  • No injuries reported

Timeline

On October 10, 2023, a bright green fireball was observed careening over high-rise buildings in Moscow during the morning commute. Eyewitnesses shared footage online, describing the fireball as "very bright" and "slow moving."

The fireball burst into flames and disappeared into the clouds, leaving a trail of sparks. One observer likened the sighting to the 2013 Chelyabinsk meteor, which caused significant damage upon entering the Earth's atmosphere.

Official statements

“It was a very odd combination of speed, size and brightness and didn’t look like anything I’ve ever seen in the sky here before,” said a stargazer who witnessed the event.

Context

This sighting follows a similar incident last year when a large meteor exploded over Yakutia, Russia's largest and coldest region, creating a blazing fireball while traveling at approximately 5,600 km/h. Such occurrences have prompted authorities and the public to consider safety measures for potential meteor impacts.

What’s next

Authorities have not disclosed any plans for investigations or follow-up actions regarding the fireball sighting in Moscow.

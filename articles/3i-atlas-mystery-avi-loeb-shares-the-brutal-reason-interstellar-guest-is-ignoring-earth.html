3I/ATLAS Mystery: Avi Loeb Shares The Brutal Reason Interstellar Guest Is 'Ignoring' Earth

Avi Loeb has discussed the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which passed Earth on December 19, 2025, at a distance of 268.91 million kilometres. The object, also known as C/2025 N1 (ATLAS), did not alter its trajectory or communicate with Earth during its approach.

The significance of 3I/ATLAS lies in its unique physical properties and its trajectory towards Jupiter, which has captured the attention of scientists. NASA's Hubble and Webb telescopes, along with the Parker Solar Probe, have tracked the object's movement since its detection on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System in Chile.

Key facts

  • Object name: 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1)
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Distance from Earth: 268.91 million kilometres
  • Speed: 60 kilometres per second
  • First detected: July 1, 2025
  • Next key event: Closest point to Jupiter on March 16, 2026
  • Distance from Jupiter at closest approach: 53.6 million kilometres

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2025, by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System in Chile. It passed Earth on December 19, 2025, without altering its path. The object will reach its closest point to Jupiter on March 16, 2026, where it is expected to undergo further observation.

Official statements

“The probability of the object's rotation axis alignment occurring by chance is a staggering 1 in 40,000,” said Avi Loeb.

Context

3I/ATLAS is not the first interstellar object to be observed, but its unusual properties have sparked significant interest among astronomers. The object's anti-tail jet, which extends one million kilometres, challenges existing theories about cometary behaviour.

What’s next

Researchers plan to monitor 3I/ATLAS closely as it approaches Jupiter in March 2026. They aim to gather data that may enhance understanding of the object and its origins.

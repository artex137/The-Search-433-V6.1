Mysterious lights over Capitol Hill causes UFO panic in DC: ‘It is already happening’

An apparent UFO sighting occurred above Capitol Hill this week, prompting a wave of concern about extraterrestrial activity. The sighting, which features four bright lights hovering above the Statue of Freedom atop the Capitol dome, was reportedly captured by US Air Force veteran and licensed tour guide Dennis Diggins. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This incident has gained significant attention on social media, especially following recent congressional hearings on alleged secret government programs related to unidentified flying objects. Authorities are monitoring the situation as public interest in UFOs continues to rise.

Key facts

  • Location: Capitol Hill, Washington, D.C.
  • Date: This week (exact date not disclosed)
  • Witness: Dennis Diggins, US Air Force veteran and licensed tour guide
  • Social media response: Viral posts with mixed reactions
  • Expert opinion: John Greenewald, Jr. debunked the sighting as lens flares
  • Recent context: Congressional hearings on alleged government UFO programs
  • Pentagon report (March): No evidence of extraterrestrial spacecraft found

Timeline

This week, a photo showing four bright lights above the Capitol dome was shared widely on social media. The lights were reported to have changed positions, moving from a square formation to a single line. As the images circulated, many users expressed concern, linking the sighting to recent congressional hearings about UFOs.

On the same day, John Greenewald, Jr., a researcher and Ufologist, commented on the phenomenon, suggesting that the lights were likely reflections and not extraterrestrial in origin. His remarks aimed to provide a rational explanation amid growing public anxiety.

Official statements

“The lights at the U.S. Capitol building have been causing ‘UFO sightings’ in the camera lens for decades & decades,” said John Greenewald, Jr. “Just lens flares, but interesting someone is passing them around, again, and more so interesting people are buying it.”

Context

This sighting follows recent congressional hearings where witnesses discussed alleged secret retrieval programs and communication with non-human intelligence. The hearings have intensified public interest in UFOs, particularly after the Pentagon's March report concluded there was no evidence of extraterrestrial spacecraft.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue monitoring public reaction and may investigate the sighting further. No official investigations or charges have been announced at this time.

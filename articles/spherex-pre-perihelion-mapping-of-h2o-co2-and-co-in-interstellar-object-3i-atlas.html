SPHEREx Pre-Perihelion Mapping of H2O, CO2, and CO in Interstellar Object 3I/ATLAS

From August 1 to August 15, 2025, the SPHEREx spacecraft conducted observations of interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, detecting significant emissions of water and carbon dioxide gases. These findings were reported by a team of researchers including Carey M. Lisse and Yoonsoo P. Bach, who analyzed the data collected during this period.

The observations are important as they enhance understanding of the composition and behavior of interstellar objects, which can provide insights into their origins and evolution. The SPHEREx mission aims to gather data that could inform future studies of both comets and the interstellar medium.

Key facts

  • Observation Period: August 1-15, 2025
  • Spacecraft: SPHEREx
  • Key Detections: Water gas (H2O), carbon dioxide (CO2), and carbon monoxide (CO)
  • Gas Production Rates: H2O: 3.2×1026 ±20%, CO2: 1.6×1027 ±10%, 13CO2: 1.3×1025 ±25%, CO: 1.0×1026 ±25%
  • Coma Size: H2O coma detected; CO2 atmosphere extends out to at least 420,000 km
  • Brightness: 3I has a nucleus radius of less than 2.5 km

Timeline

During the observation window from August 1 to August 15, 2025, the SPHEREx spacecraft utilized spectrophotometry to analyze light curves, spectra, and imaging of 3I/ATLAS. The team reported robust detections of water and carbon dioxide emissions, along with tentative detections of other gases.

The analysis revealed a significant CO2 atmosphere surrounding the object, as well as the presence of a coma, indicating active gas production. The variations in scattered light were observed to be minimal, suggesting a stable source during the observation period.

Official statements

“The observed behavior of 3I, including its high CO2 emission and small size, resembles that of short-period comets, which can give us insight into the conditions of the early solar system,” said Carey M. Lisse.

Context

This study contributes to a growing body of research on interstellar objects, including previous observations of 1I/Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. These studies suggest that interstellar objects may exhibit diverse characteristics based on their thermal histories and compositions.

What’s next

Further analysis of the data collected by SPHEREx is expected to continue, with implications for the understanding of interstellar object evolution and the chemical processes at play in the universe.

The Procure Space ETF(R) (UFO) Has a New Look

The Procure Space ETF® (NASDAQ: UFO) has updated its portfolio with new domestic and international companies, effective September 19, 2025, according to a statement from ProcureAM. The rebalancing includes Firefly Aerospace and Voyager Technologies, which both launched initial public offerings (IPOs) in the summer of 2025.

This rebalancing is significant as it reflects the growing global focus on the commercial and military opportunities in space. The ETF aims to align with companies that are leading in the space sector. The recent additions highlight the international scope of the space industry, with companies from Sweden and South Korea now included in the fund.

Key facts

  • Rebalancing date: September 19, 2025
  • New constituents: Firefly Aerospace, Voyager Technologies, Ovzon, Intellian Technologies, and Lumir
  • Firefly Aerospace holding: 4.07%
  • Intellian Technologies holding: 0.54%
  • Lumir holding: 0.10%
  • Ovzon holding: 0.50%
  • Voyager Technologies holding: 2.99%

Timeline

On September 19, 2025, the Procure Space ETF® underwent a rebalancing, incorporating new companies into its portfolio. This rebalancing follows the IPOs of Firefly Aerospace and Voyager Technologies during the summer of 2025. The ETF has consistently monitored participants in the space sector to identify emerging leaders and established companies.

Official statements

“The Procure Space ETF® seeks investment results that correspond generally to the performance of the S-Network Space Index, which focuses on companies engaged in space-related business,” said a representative from ProcureAM.

Context

The Procure Space ETF® tracks the S-Network Space Index, which evaluates companies based on their revenues from space activities and overall size. The fund is designed to serve as an equity benchmark for companies involved in the rapidly evolving space industry.

What’s next

Investors can expect ongoing updates regarding the ETF’s holdings and sector allocations, which are subject to change at any time. The Procure Space ETF® will continue to identify potential new constituents as the space industry develops.

Fuzzy Images of 3I/ATLAS at Closest Approach to Mars from the High-Resolution Imaging Camera

On October 3, 2025, the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS came within 29 million kilometers of Mars. The highest resolution images of 3I/ATLAS were captured by the HiRISE camera aboard NASA’s Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter. The camera achieved a spatial resolution of 30 kilometers per pixel, surpassing the resolution of previous images from the Hubble Space Telescope.

This event is significant as it marks the closest approach of 3I/ATLAS to Mars, allowing for enhanced imaging and analysis of the object. Authorities from both NASA and the China National Space Administration (CNSA) are actively engaged in releasing and analyzing these images, which could provide insights into the characteristics of interstellar bodies.

Key facts

  • Date: October 3, 2025
  • Distance from Mars: 29 million kilometers
  • Resolution of HiRISE camera: 30 kilometers per pixel
  • Resolution of Hubble Space Telescope: Approximately 90 kilometers per pixel
  • Distance of CNSA’s Tianwen-1 from 3I/ATLAS: 28.96 million kilometers
  • Launch of Tianwen-1: February 2021

Timeline

On October 3, 2025, the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS made its closest approach to Mars, prompting both NASA and CNSA to capture high-resolution images of the object. NASA’s HiRISE camera obtained superior images compared to previous observations made by the Hubble Space Telescope. The CNSA’s Tianwen-1 Mars Orbiter also captured images at a slightly closer distance.

Following the release of images from both agencies, Congresswoman Anna Paulina Lune referenced the event in a tweet, highlighting the ongoing discussions regarding interstellar objects amid the backdrop of a U.S. government shutdown.

Official statements

Citing the importance of the images, Anna Paulina Lune tweeted, “While we are all waiting for the end of the U.S. government shutdown and the release of the HiRISE images of 3I/ATLAS.”

Context

The observation of interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS is becoming increasingly important in the field of astronomy. Previous incidents involving similar objects have spurred interest in understanding their origins and characteristics. The ongoing collaboration between NASA and CNSA highlights the global interest in space exploration and interstellar phenomena.

What’s next

NASA is expected to release further images from the HiRISE camera in the coming days, which will provide additional data for analysis of 3I/ATLAS and its trajectory. The impact of the U.S. government shutdown on future space missions remains to be seen.

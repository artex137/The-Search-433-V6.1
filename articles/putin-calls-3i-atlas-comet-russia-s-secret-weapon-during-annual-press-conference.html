Putin Calls 3I ATLAS Comet Russia’s “Secret Weapon” During Annual Press Conference

During a televised press conference on December 14, 2023, Russian President Vladimir Putin referred to the 3I/ATLAS comet as Russia's “secret weapon.” The comment came in response to a reporter's question about the comet's possible ties to extraterrestrial life. The event, which lasted approximately four hours, attracted 2.5 million questions from the public.

Putin's remarks on the comet highlight Russia's approach to space and science, emphasizing that the comet poses no threat to Earth. He assured the audience that the comet is 259 million km away, a distance significantly greater than the moon’s distance of 400,000 km. The president's comments blend humor with serious scientific discourse, illustrating his engagement with both light-hearted and pressing national issues.

Key facts

  • Event Date: December 14, 2023
  • Location: Moscow, Russia
  • Distance of 3I/ATLAS comet from Earth: 259 million km
  • Distance of the moon from Earth: 400,000 km
  • Duration of press conference: Approximately four hours
  • Number of questions received: 2.5 million

Timeline

On December 14, 2023, President Putin held his annual press conference, addressing a variety of topics including the 3I/ATLAS comet. A reporter inquired about the comet's connections to extraterrestrial life, prompting Putin to humorously label it as a “secret weapon.” He elaborated on the comet's unique characteristics, noting its shell structure and the phenomena observed as it approaches the sun.

Throughout the conference, attendees posed questions ranging from serious issues to lighter topics. Noteworthy moments included a young boy asking if Putin disguises himself while in Moscow, and a university student proposing to his girlfriend live on air.

Official statements

“This weapon would only be employed in extreme circumstances,” Putin said, emphasizing Russia's position against deploying any weapons in space.
“The comet is 259 million km away from Earth, which poses no threat to the planet,” Putin reassured the audience.

Context

This year’s press conference reflects a continued trend of blending governance with public engagement in Russia. The event has historically served as a platform for citizens to voice concerns and questions directly to the president. The humorous exchanges and serious inquiries demonstrate the diverse relationship between the Russian leadership and its populace.

What’s next

As public interest remains high, authorities are expected to continue discussions on space and scientific exploration, while addressing the concerns raised during the press conference, including economic conditions and public services.

Fire in the Sky Meets Freddy Got Fingered in Henry Zebrowski's Sci-Fi Comedy UFO: Unbelievably Friendly Organisms

Henry Zebrowski has announced his new film, UFO: Unbelievably Friendly Organisms, a sci-fi comedy that blends elements of alien abduction with humor. The film is set to begin shooting in Atlanta around March or April of 2026 and aims for a film festival release in 2027. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the film's production.

The film is significant as it represents a unique take on the alien genre, combining comedy with action and romance. Authorities in the film industry, including the production team, are currently seeking funding through a Kickstarter campaign to raise $500,000 for the project.

Key facts

  • Film title: UFO: Unbelievably Friendly Organisms
  • Director: Not disclosed
  • Scriptwriter: Kevin Gillese
  • Production location: Atlanta
  • Filming start date: March/April 2026
  • Film festival release: 2027
  • Funding goal: $500,000 via Kickstarter

Timeline

Henry Zebrowski has described the film as a blend of “Fire in the Sky,” “All The President’s Men,” and “Freddy Got Fingered.” The script has been penned by Kevin Gillese based on Zebrowski’s story. The film will feature a cast that includes Amber Nash, Jenna Haze, and Zebrowski's Last Podcast on the Left co-hosts Marcus Parks and Ed Larson. The production team includes practical special effects experts Shane Morton, Chris Brown, and puppeteer Raymond Carr.

The Kickstarter campaign is currently active, offering rewards such as producer credits and private screenings to backers.

Official statements

“UFO: Unbelievably Friendly Organisms promises shoot outs, government agents, big grays, little grays, hot pursuits, fist fights, double-crosses, passionate love-making, and aliens doing things to Henry’s body that you can only dream of,” said Zebrowski.

Context

This film marks Zebrowski's venture into the sci-fi comedy genre, building on his previous work in entertainment. The combination of comedy and science fiction has seen a resurgence in popularity, with audiences increasingly drawn to unique narratives and humor-infused storytelling.

What’s next

The production team will continue fundraising through the Kickstarter campaign until the goal is reached. Filming is expected to start in early 2026, with further announcements regarding casting and production details to follow.

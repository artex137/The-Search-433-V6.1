NASA Responds to Trump’s Demand for UFO Files

NASA has addressed former President Donald Trump’s directive to release government files on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and UFOs, asserting that there will be no imminent revelations about extraterrestrial life. The agency’s response comes after a wave of public interest ignited by former President Barack Obama’s recent comments regarding the existence of aliens.

NASA’s official statement emphasizes its commitment to open science and transparency, while downplaying the notion of extraterrestrial encounters. The agency’s position aims to clarify misconceptions and maintain focus on scientific inquiry rather than speculation.

Key facts

  • Former President Barack Obama commented on extraterrestrial life in an interview on February 27, 2026.
  • Trump accused Obama of revealing classified information and directed the Secretary of War to release UFO-related files.
  • NASA issued a response via press secretary Bethany Stevens, reinforcing its commitment to transparency.
  • NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman noted that unexplained phenomena relate more to inefficient programs than to aliens.
  • Dr. Gentry Lee, a veteran NASA scientist, stated that no evidence supports claims of alien visitation on Earth.

Timeline

On February 27, 2026, Barack Obama participated in an interview where he acknowledged the possibility of extraterrestrial life but clarified he had not seen any aliens. His comments sparked public interest and speculation.

In response, Trump, while on Air Force One, accused Obama of sharing classified information and declared his intention to direct the Secretary of War to identify and release all UFO-related documents.

NASA’s press secretary, Bethany Stevens, issued a statement that same day, affirming the agency’s dedication to open science and addressing the claims of alien encounters.

Official statements

“We have fostered open science since our inception so that the public can build upon our innovations. We continue to make all NASA data publicly available, and welcome public participation using our data.” - NASA statement
“There exists nothing today that says any alien or any alien machine has ever landed on the planet Earth. If you believe otherwise, you are being misled.” - Dr. Gentry Lee

Context

NASA’s response underscores a long-standing policy of transparency in scientific research. The agency has consistently made its data publicly accessible, allowing for independent analysis and public engagement.

The discussion around UFOs and extraterrestrial life has been reignited in recent years, particularly following the release of UAP reports by the U.S. government. However, NASA maintains that any groundbreaking discoveries regarding alien life will stem from scientific investigation rather than the declassification of old files.

What’s next

NASA will continue to focus on its open science initiatives and scientific research, while the public and government officials await further developments regarding UAP investigations.

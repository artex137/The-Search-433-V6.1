Steven Spielberg to reunite with one of his most legendary collaborators for new UFO film

Published 2025-10-29 11:55 UTC

Steven Spielberg to reunite with John Williams for new UFO film

Steven Spielberg has announced that John Williams will compose the score for his upcoming UFO-themed film, marking their 30th collaboration. The project was revealed at a special event in New York earlier this week, where Juilliard School president Damian Woetzel confirmed Williams' involvement, stating he is currently in Los Angeles working on the film.

This announcement highlights the enduring partnership between Spielberg and Williams, whose soundtracks have defined many of Spielberg's iconic films. The collaboration continues to generate excitement among fans and industry insiders, especially with Williams' previous work on Spielberg's earlier UFO-related projects, including Close Encounters of the Third Kind and E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial.

Key facts

  • Project: Untitled UFO film
  • Collaboration: 30th project between Spielberg and Williams
  • Event location: New York
  • Announcement date: Earlier this week
  • Principal photography: Completed earlier this year
  • Release date: Scheduled for June 2026
  • Cast: Josh O’Connor, Emily Blunt, Colman Domingo, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, and Wyatt Russell
  • Screenplay: Written by David Koepp

Timeline

Steven Spielberg and John Williams have collaborated since 1974, beginning with The Sugarland Express. Over the years, they have produced memorable scores for films such as Jaws, Jurassic Park, Schindler’s List, and Saving Private Ryan.

In 2022, Williams indicated he might retire after composing for Indiana Jones and the Dial of Destiny. However, he has since expressed interest in new projects, leading to the announcement of his involvement in Spielberg's latest film.

Principal photography for the UFO film has been completed, with a release date set for June 2026. Little is known about the film's plot, aside from its focus on UFO phenomena and that it is described as a “new original event film.”

Official statements

“He is working with Steven Spielberg on the next movie. And that is something to be happy about.”

- Damian Woetzel, President of Juilliard School

Context

Williams, at 93, is celebrated for his contributions to film music and had previously suggested retirement. His return to work with Spielberg is significant, as it not only continues their legacy but also brings Williams back into the spotlight after a brief hiatus.

Spielberg's exploration of UFO themes has a rich history, with his earlier films influencing public perception of extraterrestrial life and contributing to the cultural dialogue surrounding the topic.

What’s next

As the film progresses toward its scheduled release in June 2026, further details about the plot and additional promotional materials are expected to emerge in the coming months.

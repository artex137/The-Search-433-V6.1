Teaser Trailer for Steven Spielberg’s New UFO Movie, ‘Disclosure Day’ Arrives Online

A teaser trailer for Steven Spielberg’s upcoming UFO movie, Disclosure Day, has been released online on December 19, 2025, following months of speculation, according to The Hollywood Reporter. The film, which has been shrouded in secrecy since its announcement in April 2024, is set to explore themes of extraterrestrial life and government disclosure.

This trailer marks the beginning of the film's marketing campaign, which includes billboards across the country suggesting that “all will be disclosed.” The teaser showcases a Kansas City meteorologist, played by Emily Blunt, who appears to be possessed and communicates in an alien language during a broadcast. Another character, portrayed by Josh O’Connor, hints at a significant revelation, stating that “people have a right to know the truth” and promises “full disclosure” to the world.

Key facts

  • Trailer released on December 19, 2025.
  • Film title: Disclosure Day.
  • Film scheduled to open in theaters on June 12, 2026.
  • Director: Steven Spielberg.
  • Screenwriter: David Koepp.
  • Composer: John Williams.
  • Starring: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo, Wyatt Russell, and Henry Lloyd-Hughes.

Timeline

In April 2024, Steven Spielberg announced the development of Disclosure Day. Since then, details about the film have been kept under wraps, with the marketing campaign officially launching just days before the trailer's release. The film is set to continue Spielberg's exploration of extraterrestrial themes, following previous works like Close Encounters of the Third Kind and E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial.

Official statements

“I don’t believe we’re alone in the universe…I think it’s mathematically impossible that we are the only intelligent species in the cosmos,” said Spielberg about his belief in extraterrestrial life.

Context

Disclosure Day will be Spielberg's fourth film focusing on extraterrestrial themes. His past works have shaped public perception of UFOs and the possibility of life beyond Earth. The film reunites Spielberg with frequent collaborators, including screenwriter David Koepp and composer John Williams, who has worked with him on numerous projects over three decades.

What’s next

The film's release is set for June 12, 2026. Further promotional materials and details about the plot are expected to be revealed in the coming months as the marketing campaign continues.

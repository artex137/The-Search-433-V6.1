Mysterious Interstellar Object Is Now Moving Closer To Earth

The mysterious interstellar object 3I/ATLAS is approaching Earth, with its closest approach expected on December 19, 2025. The comet will come within approximately 267 million kilometres, or about 1.5 times the distance from Earth to the Sun.

This event has captivated astronomers and the public alike. NASA and the European Space Agency are monitoring 3I/ATLAS closely. The object, discovered in July 2025, is only the third known to originate from outside our solar system. Observations have confirmed that it is traveling on a hyperbolic orbit, which will eventually take it out of the solar system.

Key facts

  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Distance at closest approach: approximately 267 million kilometres
  • Discovered: July 2025
  • Observed by: NASA's Hubble Space Telescope and ESA's JUICE
  • Visible through small telescopes until spring 2026

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025. Since then, NASA's Hubble Space Telescope has been capturing detailed images of the comet. In November 2025, the European Space Agency's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (JUICE) observed the comet, capturing images of its active state.

As the comet approaches, speculation has arisen regarding its nature. While some conspiracy theories suggest it could be an alien spaceship, NASA officials have firmly stated that it is a natural comet.

Official statements

"We certainly haven't seen any technosignatures or anything from it that would lead us to believe it was anything other than a comet," said Nicola Fox, associate administrator of NASA's Science Mission Directorate.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third object identified as originating from outside our solar system, following previous discoveries that have similarly intrigued the scientific community. The ongoing interest in interstellar objects has led to enhanced observational efforts by space agencies.

What’s next

NASA's "Eyes on the Solar System" online simulation allows enthusiasts to track the comet's path as it travels through our solar system. Observations will continue until it exits the solar system, providing valuable data to astronomers.

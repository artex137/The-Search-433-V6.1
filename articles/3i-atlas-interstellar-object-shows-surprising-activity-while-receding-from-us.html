3I/ATLAS: Interstellar Object Shows Surprising Activity While Receding From Us

3I/ATLAS, an interstellar comet, has displayed unexpected brightness while moving away from the Sun, according to astronomers. Discovered in July 2025, this object is only the third confirmed interstellar visitor, following 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The comet's unusual activity has intrigued the scientific community as it defies typical expectations for comets in similar stages of their orbits.

The behavior of 3I/ATLAS is significant because it offers insights into the chemical composition of interstellar objects and the processes that govern their activity. Researchers have utilized advanced instruments to study the comet's structure during its passage, allowing for detailed observations that could enhance our understanding of planetary formation across the galaxy.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS discovered in July 2025.
  • Only the third confirmed interstellar object after 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov.
  • SPHEREx telescope captured images of the comet in December 2025.
  • Displayed a bright coma rich in water vapor, carbon dioxide, and organic compounds.
  • Unprecedented chemical details obtained, including methane and cyanide.
  • Activity observed several weeks after closest approach to the Sun.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025. Following its closest approach to the Sun, astronomers noted an unexpected resurgence in brightness several weeks later. In December 2025, the SPHEREx telescope captured significant infrared data as the comet continued its trajectory away from the Sun.

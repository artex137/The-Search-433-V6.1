Space mission proposes to intercept comet 3I/ATLAS with extreme maneuver close to the Sun on a journey of up to 50 years

A space mission has proposed to intercept comet 3I/ATLAS using an extreme maneuver near the Sun, with an estimated journey time of up to 50 years. The mission aims to study the comet's composition and behavior as it approaches the inner solar system.

This mission is significant for advancing our understanding of comets and their potential impact on Earth. Authorities involved in the project are currently assessing the technical feasibility and required resources for the proposed maneuver.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is expected to approach the inner solar system in the coming decades.
  • The mission could take up to 50 years to complete.
  • The proposed maneuver involves a close pass by the Sun to alter the comet's trajectory.
  • Agencies involved in the mission have not been disclosed.
  • The exact launch date is not disclosed.

Timeline

The mission is in the proposal stage, with no specific launch date announced. The timeline for the mission will depend on the outcome of feasibility studies and funding approvals.

“This mission represents a unique opportunity to study a comet closely,” said a spokesperson for the agency involved in the project.

Context

Previous missions to comets have provided valuable data about their composition and the history of our solar system. Understanding comets like 3I/ATLAS can help scientists learn more about the formation of celestial bodies.

What’s next

Authorities will continue to evaluate the mission proposal and seek funding to move forward with preliminary studies and planning.

Jackie Gleason’s Iconic ‘Mothership’ UFO Home Lists for $5.5 Million

Jackie Gleason’s famed estate, known as the Mothership, has hit the market for $5.5 million in Cortlandt Manor, New York. The circular home, designed by architect Robert Cika and completed in 1959, is a unique retreat that reflects Gleason’s fascination with UFOs and metaphysical theories.

The sale of the Mothership is significant due to its historical ties to the entertainment industry and its distinctive architectural style. The estate has previously attracted interest but has not sold since its last listing in 2018 for $12 million. The property is now listed with Corcoran’s Heidi Henshaw.

Key facts

  • Location: Cortlandt Manor, New York
  • Price: $5.5 million
  • Size: 3,950 square feet
  • Lot size: 8.6 acres
  • Bedrooms: 3
  • Bathrooms: 2 full, 1 half
  • Completion year: 1959
  • Architect: Robert Cika

Timeline

Jackie Gleason commissioned the Mothership in the 1950s while filming the sitcom The Honeymooners. Construction took five years and cost around $650,000, with some funding from CBS. The home was completed in 1959 and became known for its striking, organic design.

Gleason hosted numerous celebrities, including Frank Sinatra and Marilyn Monroe, at the estate. The property changed hands in 1976 for $150,000 and was briefly on the market in 2018.

Official statements

“The Mothership is not just a home; it’s a piece of history that reflects Jackie Gleason’s creativity and vision,” said Heidi Henshaw from Corcoran.

Context

The Mothership stands out in the real estate market for its unique design and cultural significance. It represents a time when celebrity homes were designed with personal expression and extravagant features. Prior to this listing, the property had seen limited market activity since its last sale in 1976.

What’s next

Potential buyers will have the opportunity to view the Mothership, with open house events scheduled by Corcoran. The future of the property remains uncertain as interest is gauged in the current real estate market.

Hubble Spots Dual Jets on 3I/ATLAS as Avi Loeb Flags ‘Controlled’ Pattern in Fast-Moving Interstellar Object

Published 2025-12-30 15:50 UTC

Hubble Spots Dual Jets on 3I/ATLAS as Avi Loeb Flags ‘Controlled’ Pattern in Fast-Moving Interstellar Object

Fresh observations from the Hubble Space Telescope have revealed a dual-jet structure on the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS. The images were captured in mid- and late December 2023. There are no reported injuries or fatalities related to this astronomical event.

The findings have sparked significant interest among astronomers, particularly Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb. He has noted the object’s unusual jet behavior, which appears to follow a coordinated and rhythmic pattern. This raises questions about whether 3I/ATLAS behaves like a traditional comet or something more complex, prompting further investigation by the scientific community.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation period: Mid- and late December 2023
  • Key figure: Avi Loeb, Harvard astrophysicist
  • Observation tool: Hubble Space Telescope

Timeline

In December 2023, the Hubble Space Telescope captured images of 3I/ATLAS that showed a stable dual-jet structure. This structure has led to discussions about the object's nature and behavior.

Avi Loeb has commented on the observations, suggesting that the jets may indicate a controlled pattern of activity, distinguishing 3I/ATLAS from typical cometary behavior.

Official statements

Avi Loeb remarked, "The coordinated, rhythmic pattern of the jets raises intriguing questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS."

Context

The behavior of 3I/ATLAS has attracted attention due to its deviation from the expected characteristics of conventional comets. Previous interstellar objects, such as 'Oumuamua, have also prompted debate regarding their origins and behavior.

What’s next

Further observations and analyses are expected as astronomers continue to study 3I/ATLAS to better understand its properties and behavior.

President Trump Calls For Release Of UFO Files

Former President Donald Trump has announced plans to instruct the Pentagon to release government files related to extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs). This announcement was made on February 20, 2026, in McAllen, Texas, shortly after former President Barack Obama discussed the possibility of alien life in a podcast interview.

This directive from Trump aims to address growing public interest and demands for transparency regarding governmental knowledge of UFO sightings. The subject of UFOs has long captivated the public, raising questions about what information is held by authorities. In response to the excitement generated by Obama's comments, Trump has proposed a formal review and potential release of related information, intensifying the dialogue about global knowledge of otherworldly phenomena.

Key facts

  • Announcement Date: February 20, 2026
  • Location: McAllen, Texas
  • Former President involved: Donald Trump
  • Former President's remarks: Barack Obama discussed alien life on a podcast
  • Pentagon's 2024 report stated no concrete evidence of alien existence

Timeline

On February 20, 2026, Donald Trump made a public announcement regarding his intention to instruct the Pentagon to disclose files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life. This announcement followed Barack Obama's podcast comments about the possibility of alien life, which sparked public interest and speculation.

Obama clarified that he was speaking hypothetically about the likelihood of life elsewhere in the universe, not confirming any alien visits to Earth. Trump's directive aims to formalize the review of government-held files and potentially release them to the public.

Official statements

“The universe is vast, and intelligent life elsewhere is a prospect that cannot be dismissed,” Trump said, highlighting the importance of transparency on UFOs.

Context

The topic of UFOs has gained traction over the years, driven by anecdotal reports, leaked footage, and declassified documents. Public interest has surged, with many calling for greater transparency from governments regarding UFO sightings and extraterrestrial life. Trump's proposal for disclosure represents a significant moment in the ongoing debate about what information is available and how it might change our understanding of the universe.

What’s next

The Pentagon will begin a review of UFO-related files as part of Trump's directive. However, the timeline and scope of any potential release remain uncertain, with logistical challenges and political implications to consider.

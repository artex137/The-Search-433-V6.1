Interstellar comet 3I/Atlas maintains hyperbolic path at 57 km/s and escapes solar attraction in 2025

The interstellar comet 3I/Atlas has traveled through the solar system at a speed of 57 kilometers per second since November 2025, confirming its hyperbolic trajectory and its origin outside the Milky Way galaxy. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the comet's passage.

This event is significant as it marks the third interstellar object detected, following Oumuamua and Borisov. Scientists worldwide are closely monitoring 3I/Atlas to refine its trajectory calculations and maximize the scientific data collected during its brief visit to our solar system.

Key facts

  • Speed: 57 km/s
  • Trajectory: Hyperbolic
  • Origin: Distant stellar system
  • Observation: Continuous monitoring by global telescopes
  • Significance: Third interstellar object detected

Timeline

In November 2025, 3I/Atlas entered the solar system, traveling at 57 km/s. Its hyperbolic trajectory has been confirmed through spectroscopic analysis, indicating its origin from a distant stellar system. Astronomers have observed the comet's passage, gathering data on its speed and chemical composition.

As 3I/Atlas approaches its closest point to the Sun, researchers are focused on collecting detailed observations to better understand its characteristics and trajectory. This includes monitoring for any potential changes in its brightness or the presence of a coma or tail.

Official statements

“Each detected atom and molecule acts as a messenger from the distant past and a distant location,” said a representative from the global scientific community. “This provides valuable clues about the conditions prevailing in the nebula from which it emerged.”

Context

The detection of 3I/Atlas builds upon the study of previous interstellar objects, Oumuamua and Borisov. Each of these comets has offered unique insights into the composition and dynamics of celestial bodies beyond our solar system. The study of 3I/Atlas is expected to further enhance understanding of stellar formation and evolution.

What’s next

Following its passage through the solar system, 3I/Atlas will continue its journey into deep space. Scientists will analyze the data collected during its visit to gain insights into the formation of distant star systems and the nature of interstellar objects.

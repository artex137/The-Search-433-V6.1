Juice probe captures more than 120 never-before-seen images of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS revealing detailed coma

The European Space Agency's Juice probe has captured over 120 unprecedented images of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, showcasing its detailed coma. The images were obtained during a flyby of the comet on March 5, 2026.

This discovery is significant for astronomers as it provides new insights into the composition and structure of interstellar comets. The European Space Agency (ESA) has stated that the data collected will enhance understanding of these celestial bodies and their origins.

Key facts

  • Event Date: March 5, 2026
  • Location: Near interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Images captured: More than 120
  • Agency: European Space Agency (ESA)

Timeline

On March 5, 2026, the Juice probe conducted a flyby of 3I/ATLAS, successfully capturing over 120 images of the comet. This event marks a significant achievement in the study of interstellar comets, providing detailed data for further analysis.

Official statements

“The images reveal intricate details of the comet's coma, offering a glimpse into its composition and behavior,” said an ESA spokesperson.

Context

This flyby follows previous missions that have studied comets within the solar system. The data gathered from 3I/ATLAS will contribute to the broader understanding of cometary science and the formation of celestial bodies.

What’s next

The European Space Agency plans to analyze the images and data collected from the flyby, with further findings expected to be published in upcoming scientific reports.

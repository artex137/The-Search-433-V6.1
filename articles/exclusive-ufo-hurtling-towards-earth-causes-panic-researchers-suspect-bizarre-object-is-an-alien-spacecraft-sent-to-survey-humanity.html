This situation has raised concerns among researchers and authorities, who are analyzing the object's trajectory and characteristics. NASA has classified 31/Atlas as a comet, but some experts argue it may be an alien spacecraft sent to survey humanity, potentially indicating an impending invasion.

Key facts

  • Speed: 130,000 miles per hour
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19
  • Distance from Earth at closest approach: 170 million miles
  • Estimated length of the object: 12 miles
  • Orbital characteristics: Retrograde orbit with a 0.2 percent likelihood of occurring naturally

Timeline

8,000 years ago, 31/Atlas entered the outer reaches of the solar system, coinciding with the advancement of human-made technologies. Experts have noted that as the object approaches Earth, it will be shielded by the sun's luminescence, complicating detection efforts.

Previous incidents have prompted calls for more transparency from government agencies regarding potential extraterrestrial encounters.

What’s next

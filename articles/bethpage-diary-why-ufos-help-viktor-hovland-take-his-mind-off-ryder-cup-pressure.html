Bethpage Diary: Why UFOs help Viktor Hovland take his mind off Ryder Cup pressure

Published 2025-09-26 05:43 UTC

Viktor Hovland's Interest in UFOs Provides Distraction Ahead of Ryder Cup

Viktor Hovland has expressed a genuine interest in Unidentified Flying Objects (UFOs) as a way to take his mind off the pressures of the upcoming Ryder Cup. The Norwegian golfer revealed this interest after winning the Valspar Championship earlier this season. He spoke about the intrigue of UFOs during a recent event at Bethpage.

Hovland's fascination with UFOs serves as a mental escape from the rigors of competitive golf. He noted that focusing on UFOs allows him to challenge his beliefs and expand his thinking, which he finds "super fascinating." Meanwhile, preparations for the Ryder Cup continue, with officials from Ireland attending events leading up to the tournament.

Key facts

  • Viktor Hovland revealed his interest in UFOs after winning the Valspar Championship earlier this season.
  • Hovland's comments were made during an event on Wednesday at Bethpage.
  • Two Irish Government ministers have visited Bethpage in advance of the 2027 Ryder Cup.
  • Charlie McConalogue attended the opening ceremony; Patrick O’Donovan is set to attend the closing ceremony on Sunday.
  • Sepp Straka has not played since the Tour Championship due to parental leave.
  • Rory McIlroy leads the European team with 33 matches played in Ryder Cup history.

Timeline

Earlier this season, Viktor Hovland won the Valspar Championship and first shared his interest in UFOs. He explained that this fascination allows him to take a mental break from golf.

On Wednesday, the opening ceremony for the Ryder Cup events took place at Bethpage, with Irish Minister Charlie McConalogue in attendance. The closing ceremony is scheduled for Sunday, where Minister Patrick O’Donovan will be present.

Sepp Straka has not participated in tournaments since the Tour Championship due to the birth of his second child in early August.

Official statements

“Adare Manor is set to be a spectacular host for the 2027 tournament and our presence in New York is a powerful statement of our readiness and excitement,” said Patrick O’Donovan, Minister for Sport.
“I think it’s one of those things that you don’t really find any more answers, but it’s very interesting to go in there and kind of challenge some of your beliefs that you currently hold,” said Hovland regarding his interest in UFOs.

Context

The Ryder Cup is a prestigious golf tournament held every two years, featuring teams from Europe and the United States. The 2027 edition will mark the centenary of the event and will take place at Adare Manor in Limerick, Ireland. This year, the European team boasts considerable experience, with a combined total of 128 matches played in previous Ryder Cups.

Hovland's interest in UFOs has sparked conversations about how athletes manage stress and pressure in high-stakes environments.

What’s next

As the Ryder Cup approaches, players will focus on their performance and mental preparation, while the Irish Government continues to showcase its readiness to host major international events in the coming years.

William Neil McCasland missing: Retired US Air Force major general missing, once commanded base long associated with UFO lore

Published 2026-03-12 23:49 UTC

Retired US Air Force Major General Missing in New Mexico

Retired US Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland has been missing since February 27, 2023, after leaving his home in Albuquerque, New Mexico, on foot around 11 a.m.. Authorities have not disclosed any injuries or fatalities related to his disappearance.

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office has issued a Silver Alert for McCasland, who has not contacted family or friends since he went missing. His cell phone was left behind at home. The sheriff's office has reported that McCasland has a "medical issue," which has intensified the search efforts. Authorities have conducted extensive neighborhood canvassing and coordinated search operations, reaching out to over 600 homeowners in the area.

Key facts

  • Missing since: February 27, 2023
  • Last seen: Leaving home on foot in Albuquerque at approximately 11 a.m.
  • Age: 68
  • Height: 5-foot-11
  • Physical description: White hair, blue eyes
  • Medical status: Has an unspecified medical issue
  • Search efforts: Involvement of multiple agencies including the FBI and Kirtland Air Force Base

Timeline

On February 27, 2023, McCasland left his home at around 11 a.m. and has not been seen since. The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office issued a Silver Alert the following day. Search operations began shortly thereafter, involving neighborhood canvassing and outreach to local residents.

Authorities have confirmed extensive efforts, including the use of drones and search dogs, in trying to locate him. As of now, no evidence of foul play has been found, but all possibilities are being considered.

Official statements

“Due to his background and established partnerships, BCSO is coordinating closely with multiple agencies,” the Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office stated.
“It seems quite unlikely that he was taken to extract very dated secrets from him,” said Susan McCasland Wilkerson, McCasland's wife.

Context

McCasland is a retired major general who has held several sensitive positions in the US military, including chief engineer on the Department of Defense's Global Positioning System program. He commanded the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base, a site often linked to UFO lore.

His disappearance has drawn attention, coinciding with recent announcements from former President Donald Trump regarding the release of government records related to UFOs. This has raised questions among some about the nature of McCasland’s disappearance.

What’s next

The Bernalillo County Sheriff's Office is continuing its search efforts and has asked anyone with information to come forward. Investigators are reviewing footage and other evidence submitted through a dedicated webpage.

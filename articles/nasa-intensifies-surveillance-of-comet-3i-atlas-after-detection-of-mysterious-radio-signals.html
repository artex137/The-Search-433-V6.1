NASA intensifies surveillance of comet 3I/ATLAS after detection of mysterious radio signals

The international astronomical community is on high alert following the detection of unusual radio signals from the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, first identified on July 1, 2025. NASA has intensified its planetary defense protocols to monitor the comet, which poses no risk of collision with Earth.

This heightened surveillance is significant because it presents a rare scientific opportunity to study the comet’s composition and origin. The unique characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, including its high-speed trajectory and radio emissions, have prompted collaboration among global space agencies to gather data throughout its journey.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was detected on July 1, 2025.
  • It travels at an estimated speed of over 100,000 kilometers per second.
  • The comet will make its closest approach to Earth on December 19, 2025, at approximately 27 million kilometers away.
  • NASA's planetary defense protocols have been activated in response to the radio signals detected.
  • The MeerKAT radio telescope in South Africa detected the signals on October 24, 2025.
  • The signals were found to be consistent with hydrogen emissions.
  • The comet's core measures between 320 meters and 5.6 kilometers in diameter.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, astronomers first detected comet 3I/ATLAS. Following its discovery, NASA activated its planetary defense protocols due to the comet's unique characteristics. On October 24, 2025, the MeerKAT radio telescope detected unusual radio signals from 3I/ATLAS, raising interest among researchers. The closest approach to Earth is scheduled for December 19, 2025.

Official statements

“The interaction of the comet’s material with the solar wind is generating these emissions,” said a NASA spokesperson. “However, the strength of the signal suggests a composition or state of activity much more intense than expected.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object observed by humanity, following ‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The comet's unique properties and its passage through our solar system provide critical insights into the formation of planetary systems. Its study will contribute to a better understanding of similar objects and enhance planetary defense strategies.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS with advanced telescopes and instruments to gather more data. A press conference is scheduled for August 2025 to present findings and outline the next steps in the observation campaign.

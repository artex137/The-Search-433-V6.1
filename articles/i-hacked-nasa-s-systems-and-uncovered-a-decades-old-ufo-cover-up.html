I hacked NASA's systems... and uncovered a decades-old UFO cover-up

Published 2026-03-02 17:15 UTC

British Hacker Claims UFO Discovery During NASA Breach

Gary McKinnon, a British computer hacker, has detailed his discovery of a purported UFO photo while accessing NASA's systems in 2002. McKinnon, who was accused of executing the largest military computer hack in history, claims he found a high-resolution image of a cigar-shaped craft hovering above Earth while infiltrating NASA's Johnson Space Center.

This revelation has reignited discussions about UFOs and government secrecy. Authorities have not taken any formal actions following McKinnon's claims, but he remains a controversial figure due to his past illegal activities and ongoing legal issues.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred in 2002, during which McKinnon accessed 97 government sites.
  • McKinnon claims to have found a UFO image and a spreadsheet of “non-terrestrial officers.”
  • He faced a decade-long extradition battle with the U.S. government, which sought a 70-year prison sentence.
  • The British government ultimately blocked his extradition in 2012, citing mental health concerns.
  • McKinnon is currently on the Interpol red list, preventing him from entering the U.S.

Timeline

McKinnon's hacking activities began in March 2001 when he scanned IP addresses for government computers with weak security. He managed to access sensitive networks belonging to agencies including NASA, the NSA, and the Department of Defense.

In 2002, he was arrested by the UK's National High-Tech Crime Unit. Initially facing a six-month sentence, the U.S. escalated the charges, seeking extradition under a revised treaty. In 2012, the British Home Secretary stopped the extradition due to concerns about McKinnon's mental health.

Official statements

“Mr. McKinnon's extradition would give rise to such a high risk of him ending his life… I have therefore withdrawn the extradition order against Mr. McKinnon,” said then-British Home Secretary Theresa May in 2012.

Context

McKinnon's claims about UFOs are not isolated. They have emerged amid increasing public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and government transparency. His alleged sighting predates the well-documented 2004 encounter by U.S. Navy pilot David Fravor with a so-called 'tic tac' object, which the Pentagon later acknowledged as authentic.

What’s next

McKinnon remains on the Interpol red list, and it is unclear whether any further legal actions will be taken regarding his past activities. His claims continue to attract attention, prompting discussions about UFOs and government secrecy.

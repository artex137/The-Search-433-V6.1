UFOs Declassified on Blaze: full details and when it's on

UFOs Declassified will air on Blaze at 10:15 AM on Monday, 8 September. The program recalls reports of silvery objects hovering over Los Angeles in February 1942, during a time when the city was on high alert following the attack on Pearl Harbour. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the UFO sightings discussed in the program.

This program is significant as it revisits a historic event that has intrigued both the public and researchers for decades. Authorities are examining these reports to provide clarity on the incidents that took place during a period of heightened military vigilance.

Key facts

  • Event: UFOs Declassified airs
  • Date: Monday, 8 September
  • Time: 10:15 AM
  • Location: Los Angeles
  • Historical Context: Reports from February 1942
  • Alert Status: High alert following Pearl Harbour attack

Timeline

In February 1942, shortly after the attack on Pearl Harbour, Los Angeles was placed on high alert due to sightings of unidentified flying objects. Eyewitnesses reported seeing silvery objects hovering in the sky, prompting military responses.

The airing of UFOs Declassified on 8 September aims to shed light on these historical sightings and the subsequent reactions from authorities at the time.

Official statements

“The program will explore the events that led to the heightened alert in Los Angeles and the various reports of unidentified flying objects,” a spokesperson for Blaze said.

Context

The incident in February 1942 is one of the earliest and most notable cases of UFO sightings in the United States, occurring during a time of war. It has since become a point of interest for UFO researchers and enthusiasts, contributing to ongoing discussions about aerial phenomena.

What’s next

Following the airing of UFOs Declassified, further investigations into historical UFO sightings may be prompted, as both authorities and the public seek to understand these events in greater detail.

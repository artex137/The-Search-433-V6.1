Thousands of UFOs spotted off US coastlines

A popular UFO-reporting app has recorded tens of thousands of mysterious underwater and aerial sightings along the U.S. coasts, according to the organization Enigma. Since its launch in 2022, Enigma has logged more than 30,000 cases, raising new questions about unidentified flying objects in U.S. airspace and waters. The report was posted on November 3, 2025, at 5:02 PM CST.

This surge in sightings has prompted interest from both the public and authorities, who are now examining the implications of these reports. The increase in cases has reignited discussions about UFO phenomena and the need for further investigation into these occurrences.

Key facts

  • More than 30,000 UFO cases logged since 2022.
  • Organization: Enigma, which maintains the world’s largest database of UFO reports.
  • Report posted on November 3, 2025, at 5:02 PM CST.
  • Locations: U.S. coastlines, specific locations not disclosed.

Timeline

Enigma launched its UFO-reporting app in 2022. Since then, it has documented a significant number of sightings, surpassing 30,000 reports by November 2025. The app has gained popularity among UFO enthusiasts and researchers, leading to increased attention from various stakeholders.

Official statements

“The volume of reports we are receiving raises important questions about what is happening beneath and above our skies,” said a spokesperson for Enigma.

Context

The rise in UFO sightings coincides with a growing public interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. Previous incidents have raised awareness and prompted calls for more transparency from government agencies regarding UFO investigations. The trend suggests a shift in how such occurrences are perceived by both the public and authorities.

What’s next

Authorities are expected to continue monitoring the reports from Enigma and may launch formal investigations into specific cases as they arise. Further updates and analyses are anticipated as more data becomes available.

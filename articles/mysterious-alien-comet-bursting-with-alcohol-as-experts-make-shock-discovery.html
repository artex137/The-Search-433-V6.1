Mysterious 'alien' comet 'bursting with alcohol' as experts make shock discovery

Scientists have discovered that the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, which passed through our solar system, is filled with high levels of methanol, a type of alcohol. This finding was made by researchers from the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (Alma) observatory in Chile as they studied gas plumes from the comet.

The discovery is significant as it marks the first time an interstellar object has been found to contain such abundant alcohol. Authorities are continuing to study the comet to understand its origins and the implications of its unique composition.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is approximately 3.5 miles in diameter.
  • It made its closest approach to Earth just before Christmas last year.
  • The comet is known to contain unusually high levels of methanol.
  • Researchers believe it may have originated from a colder region of its home star system.
  • 3I/ATLAS will make its closest approach to Jupiter on Monday.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first detected late last year as one of only three interstellar objects identified passing through our solar system. It approached Earth in December 2022 and has been the subject of scientific study since then.

Researchers at the Alma observatory have observed gas plumes from the comet, revealing its high methanol content. This finding contrasts with typical comet compositions and has led to discussions about the comet's formation and journey through space.

Official statements

“Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system,” said Professor Nathan Roth from the American University. “The details reveal what it’s made of, and it’s bursting with methanol in a way we just don’t usually see in comets in our own solar system.”

Context

Prior research on comets has typically found lower levels of methanol. The discovery of such high concentrations in 3I/ATLAS has raised questions about the formation of comets and the conditions in their home systems. This investigation may provide insights into the processes that govern the development of celestial bodies outside our solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Jupiter. They aim to gather more data that could shed light on its origins and the factors contributing to its unusual chemical composition.

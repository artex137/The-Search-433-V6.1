Wisconsin Had Over 70 UFO Sightings Reported In 2025 – Here’s Where

Wisconsin has reported a total of 74 UFO sightings in 2025, according to data from the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC). This marks a significant increase from 50 sightings reported in 2024. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities associated with these sightings.

The rise in UFO sightings in Wisconsin highlights a growing interest in unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are collecting data to better understand these reports, although many remain unexplained. Some sightings have been identified after investigation, while others continue to baffle observers.

Key facts

  • 74 total UFO sightings reported in 2025
  • 48% increase from 2024, which had 50 sightings
  • January had the highest number of sightings with 14
  • June was the second-highest month with 9 reports
  • Locations with notable sightings include Superior, Solon Springs, Presque Isle, Hayward, and Rhinelander
  • Reports varied from unexplainable phenomena to objects later identified by NUFORC

Timeline

The reported UFO sightings in Wisconsin occurred throughout 2025, with the following monthly breakdown:

  • January: 14 sightings
  • February: 1 sighting
  • March: 5 sightings
  • April: 5 sightings
  • May: 2 sightings
  • June: 9 sightings
  • July: 5 sightings
  • August: 8 sightings
  • September: 6 sightings
  • October: 8 sightings
  • November: 6 sightings
  • December: 5 sightings

Official statements

“Some of these reports are simply unexplainable sightings in the sky, and others are hard-to-identify objects that the team at NUFORC later was able to help explain or identify,” NUFORC stated.

Context

The 2025 data reflects a continuing upward trend in UFO sightings in Wisconsin. The increase from 2023 to 2024 was less dramatic, indicating a possible growing public interest or awareness of UFO reporting.

What’s next

NUFORC continues to investigate the reported sightings and will provide updates as new information becomes available. There are no specific timelines for the completion of these investigations.

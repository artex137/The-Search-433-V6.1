BINGO! 3I/ATLAS undergoes acid test near Sun: 'If it picks up extra...'

Scientists have observed interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun on October 29-30. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this astronomical event.

This observation is significant as researchers aim to determine if the comet exhibits any changes in energy levels. The findings may suggest whether there is an external influence affecting its trajectory.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation dates: October 29-30
  • Location: Near the Sun
  • Current energy status: under review
  • Research focus: potential external influence on the comet

Timeline

Scientists have planned their observations of 3I/ATLAS for late October 2023. They will monitor the comet to detect any changes in its energy as it approaches the Sun.

The goal of the observation is to determine if a boost in energy will indicate that an external force is affecting the comet.

Official statements

“If it picks up extra energy, that could be a clear signal that someone is driving it from the inside,” a researcher stated regarding the upcoming observations.

Context

The study of interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS has gained traction in recent years. Researchers continue to investigate their origins and the forces acting upon them.

What’s next

Scientists will analyze the data collected during the observation period to assess any changes in the comet's energy levels and determine the implications of their findings.

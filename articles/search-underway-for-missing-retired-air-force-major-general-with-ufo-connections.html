Search underway for missing retired Air Force major general with UFO connections

Authorities in New Mexico are searching for retired US Air Force Major General William Neil McCasland, who has been missing for nearly two weeks. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office reported that McCasland, 68, left his Albuquerque home on foot around 11 a.m. on February 27 and has not contacted family or friends since.

This search is critical due to concerns for McCasland's well-being, particularly because he left behind his cell phone and may have a “medical issue.” A Silver Alert has been issued and remains active as local law enforcement continues extensive search efforts, including canvassing the neighborhood and coordinating with multiple agencies.

Key facts

  • Missing since February 27, 2023.
  • Last seen leaving his home in Albuquerque around 11 a.m.
  • Described as 5-foot-11 with white hair and blue eyes.
  • Over 600 homeowners have been contacted by authorities.
  • Involvement of the FBI Albuquerque Field Office and Kirtland Air Force Base.
  • McCasland is an avid outdoorsman, often hiking and cycling in local areas.

Timeline

On February 27, 2023, Major General William Neil McCasland left his Albuquerque home around 11 a.m. and has not been seen since. The following day, a Silver Alert was issued. Search efforts have included neighborhood canvassing and coordination with various law enforcement agencies.

Official statements

“We have reported no signs of foul play thus far, but we are exploring all possible leads,” said a spokesperson for the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office.
His wife, Susan McCasland Wilkerson, clarified that her husband does not possess any special information related to extraterrestrial matters and emphasized that he is not suffering from dementia or confusion.

Context

McCasland has a distinguished military career, having served as chief engineer on the Department of Defense’s Global Positioning System program and director of special programs at the Pentagon. His previous role at the Air Force Research Laboratory at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base links him to historical UFO speculation, particularly surrounding the Roswell incident.

Following his retirement nearly 13 years ago, McCasland became associated with To The Stars, Inc., a company focused on studying unidentified aerial phenomena. His disappearance coincides with renewed public interest in UFOs, following a directive from former President Donald Trump for federal agencies to release related records.

What’s next

Authorities continue to investigate the case and encourage anyone with information to come forward. Search efforts will proceed with the involvement of volunteers and advanced technologies, including drones and search dogs, as they remain hopeful for a positive outcome.

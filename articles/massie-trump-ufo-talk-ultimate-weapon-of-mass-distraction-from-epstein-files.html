Massie: Trump UFO talk ‘ultimate weapon of mass distraction’ from Epstein files

Rep. Thomas Massie (R-Ky.) has accused President Trump of using discussions about UFOs and extraterrestrial life to divert attention from the ongoing controversy surrounding files related to convicted sex offender Jeffrey Epstein. Massie made the remarks on Thursday via the social platform X, where he shared a screenshot of Trump’s recent post regarding UFOs.

This situation is significant as it highlights the intersection of political discourse and public interest in UFOs, while also drawing attention to the Justice Department's handling of Epstein-related documents. Massie has expressed concerns about the slow release and heavy redactions of these files, which he argues protect the identities of prominent figures involved in Epstein's case.

Key facts

  • Massie accused Trump of distraction tactics regarding the Epstein files.
  • Trump announced plans to direct the Defense Department to release documents about UFOs and extraterrestrial life.
  • The Epstein Files Transparency Act was co-sponsored by Massie and Rep. Ro Khanna (D-Calif.) and signed into law by Trump in November.
  • Trump's name appears in the Epstein files; he has denied any wrongdoing.
  • The Pentagon has denied claims of a cover-up regarding nonhuman technology.
  • There is no conclusive evidence regarding extraterrestrial life.

Timeline

On Thursday, Rep. Thomas Massie criticized President Trump for focusing on UFO discussions. Massie shared a screenshot of Trump’s post on X, where the president stated he would instruct the Defense Department to release UFO documents due to public interest.

Earlier in the month, Massie confronted Attorney General Pam Bondi during a House Judiciary Committee hearing about the DOJ's handling of the Epstein files. The Epstein Files Transparency Act was signed into law by Trump in November, intending to force the release of documents related to Epstein's investigation.

Official statements

“They’ve deployed the ultimate weapon of mass distraction, but the Epstein files aren’t going away… even for aliens,” Massie said in his post on X.
Trump stated, “I may get him out of trouble by declassifying,” referring to Obama's comments on extraterrestrial life.

Context

Interest in UFOs has been growing among lawmakers and the public for years. In 2023, the House Oversight and Government Reform Committee held a hearing where former military intelligence officer David Grusch testified about alleged Pentagon cover-ups related to nonhuman technology. The Pentagon has officially denied these allegations.

Former President Obama recently discussed the topic on the “No Lie” podcast, asserting that while he believes in the possibility of extraterrestrial life, he has not seen evidence of contact during his presidency.

What’s next

Ongoing investigations into the Epstein files continue, with pressure on the DOJ to expedite the release of documents. Trump’s directive to the Pentagon regarding UFOs is expected to unfold in the coming weeks, as public interest remains high.

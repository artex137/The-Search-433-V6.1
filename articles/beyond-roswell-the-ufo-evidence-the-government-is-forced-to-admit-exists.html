Beyond Roswell: The UFO Evidence the Government Is Forced to Admit Exists

The Pentagon has confirmed the existence of unidentified phenomena in the skies, marking a significant shift in government stance on Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs), previously known as UFOs. This acknowledgment comes after decades of denial and follows a series of declassified reports and congressional hearings.

This development is essential as it indicates a new era of transparency regarding UAPs, prompting military and political authorities to take the subject seriously. The Pentagon's recent admissions reveal that there are tangible objects operating in ways that challenge current scientific understanding.

Key facts

  • UAPs are now the focus of investigations by the Pentagon and other military agencies.
  • The Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program (AATIP) was established in 2007 with a budget of $22 million.
  • A preliminary assessment by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) analyzed 144 incidents from 2004 to 2021.
  • Only one incident was explained with high confidence; 143 remain unexplained.
  • Key figures in the UAP discussion include Luis Elizondo and Christopher Mellon.
  • The report identified five observable phenomena related to UAPs, including anti-gravity lift and trans-medium travel.

Timeline

In 1947, the alleged crash of a flying saucer in Roswell, New Mexico, became a pivotal moment in UFO lore. For decades, the U.S. government dismissed such claims.

On December 16, 2017, The New York Times published a report revealing the existence of AATIP, leading to widespread media coverage and renewed public interest in UAPs. The report included three declassified videos of UAP encounters, showcasing advanced military technology capturing the phenomena.

In June 2021, the ODNI released a preliminary assessment on UAPs, which confirmed the existence of unexplained incidents reported by military aviators.

Official statements

“These phenomena were real and demanded serious investigation,” said Luis Elizondo, former military intelligence officer.
“Some of these observations could be attributable to developments and classified programs by U.S. entities,” the ODNI report stated.

Context

The topic of UFOs has historically been ridiculed and marginalized within military and political circles. The Air Force's Project Blue Book, which ended in 1969, claimed there was nothing significant to investigate.

The 2017 revelations acted as a catalyst, breaking the long-standing taboo surrounding the discussion of UAPs and leading to increased scrutiny and interest in the subject.

What’s next

Investigations into UAPs are ongoing, with military agencies expected to release more detailed reports. The government aims to understand these phenomena better and determine their implications for national security.

New comet 3I/Atlas crosses the Solar System at 57 km/s on a permanent escape route to space

The newly discovered comet 3I/Atlas has entered the Solar System, traveling at a speed of 57 kilometers per second. The comet is on a permanent escape trajectory, meaning it will not return to the Solar System once it exits. This event has been noted by astronomers and space agencies worldwide.

Scientists regard the passage of comet 3I/Atlas as significant for research on celestial bodies and the dynamics of our Solar System. Authorities, including various space agencies, are monitoring the comet's trajectory to gather data and enhance understanding of similar objects in space.

  • Speed: 57 km/s

Context

The passage of comets through the Solar System has been a subject of interest for scientists for centuries. Comets like 3I/Atlas can offer insights into the formation and evolution of celestial bodies.

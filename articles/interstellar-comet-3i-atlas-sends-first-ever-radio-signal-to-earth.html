Interstellar Comet 3I Atlas Sends First-Ever Radio Signal to Earth

Scientists have detected the first radio signal from interstellar comet 3I Atlas on October 24, 2025, using the MeerKAT radio telescope in South Africa. This discovery marks a historic moment in astronomy as it provides the first direct chemical analysis of an interstellar visitor.

The findings confirm the comet’s alien origin and active nature. Researchers have noted that the radio signal revealed specific hydroxyl molecules, which indicate water sublimation from the comet’s icy core. This discovery enhances our understanding of the composition of interstellar objects.

Key facts

  • Detection date: October 24, 2025
  • Location of detection: MeerKAT radio telescope, South Africa
  • Signal strength: -7.9 and -19.1 mJy km/s
  • Nucleus size: estimated up to 11 kilometers wide
  • Closest approach to Earth: approximately 270 million kilometers
  • Future observation: NASA’s Juno spacecraft will monitor during a Jupiter flyby in March 2026

Timeline

On October 24, 2025, scientists at the MeerKAT radio telescope detected a radio signal from comet 3I Atlas. The data revealed hydroxyl molecules, confirming the comet’s active state and alien origin. This marks the first time a comet from beyond our solar system has been analyzed through radio astronomy.

The findings demonstrate that the comet is actively releasing gases as it approaches the Sun, supported by velocity data matching a hyperbolic orbit. The research indicates that 3I Atlas contains water and other volatile compounds formed millions of years ago in a distant star system.

Official statements

“The successful detection validates the power of radio astronomy. It provides a new method for studying the composition of interstellar objects without sending physical probes,” said a spokesperson from the research team.

Context

Comet 3I Atlas is only the third known interstellar object identified, following ‘Oumuamua and comet Borisov. Unlike ‘Oumuamua, which was a dormant asteroid-like object, 3I Atlas is actively outgassing and creating a distinct atmosphere and tail. This enhances the understanding of how common the basic ingredients for life, like water, are across the galaxy.

What’s next

Future observations are planned, including monitoring by ground-based telescopes and NASA’s Juno spacecraft during its flyby of Jupiter in March 2026. This ongoing research aims to deepen our understanding of the distribution of prebiotic molecules in the universe.

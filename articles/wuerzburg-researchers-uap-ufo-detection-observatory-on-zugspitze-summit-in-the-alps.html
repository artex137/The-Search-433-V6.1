Wuerzburg Researchers UAP/UFO Detection Observatory on Zugspitze-Summit in the Alps

Published 2025-10-23 10:56 UTC

Wuerzburg Researchers UAP/UFO Detection Observatory on Zugspitze-Summit in the Alps

Researchers from the Interdisciplinary Research Center for Extraterrestrial Studies (IFEX) at the University of Wuerzburg have installed a new AI-based camera observatory designed to detect unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) and unidentified flying objects (UFOs) on the terrace of the Environmental Research Station Schneefernerhaus, located just below the summit of Germany's highest mountain, Zugspitze. The observatory, named SkyCAM-7, is now the world’s highest UAP observatory.

This installation is significant as it aims to enhance the detection and monitoring of anomalous aerial phenomena. According to Professor Hakan Kayal, the SkyCAM-7 system will also serve as a test platform for future missions to Mars. The observatory will operate continuously under extreme alpine weather conditions, providing valuable data for both terrestrial and extraterrestrial studies.

Key facts

  • Location: Environmental Research Station Schneefernerhaus, Zugspitze, Germany
  • Height: 2,656 meters above sea level
  • System: SkyCAM-7, part of the Anomaly Detection and Observation System (ADEOS)
  • Research Institution: Interdisciplinary Research Center for Extraterrestrial Studies (IFEX), University of Wuerzburg
  • Lead Researcher: Professor Hakan Kayal
  • Installation Date: Just before the onset of winter 2023

Timeline

In recent years, the IFEX team has developed various versions of SkyCAM systems at multiple locations, including the Blue Box in Hessdalen, Norway, and the roof of the University of Wuerzburg. The SkyCAM-7 installation at Schneefernerhaus represents the latest advancement in their research efforts. The observatory is designed to capture the entire visible sky and will assist in studying known atmospheric objects while potentially detecting unknown phenomena.

The SkyCAM-7 system is part of a broader initiative to contribute to the German Mars mission, VaMEx 3, which aims to explore the Valles Marineris canyon system on Mars. This project includes developing drone probes and a data relay unit for communication with Earth.

Official statements

“The system is being tested at the Zugspitze for a reason,” said Professor Hakan Kayal. “The AllSkyCAM system consists of several cameras that together capture the entire visible sky.”

Context

The SkyCAM series has been under development for several years and includes installations that have been tested in various environments. The observatory at Zugspitze is expected to provide insights into both terrestrial atmospheric phenomena and unidentified objects in the sky, contributing to the global understanding of UAP.

What’s next

The IFEX team will continue to operate the SkyCAM-7 observatory throughout the winter, collecting data under challenging conditions. Future analyses will focus on the detection of UAP and the performance of the system as a potential platform for Mars exploration.

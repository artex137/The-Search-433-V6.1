Is 3I/ATLAS Returning to Earth After Its Jupiter Visit, New Findings Go Viral

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS may be returning toward Earth after its pass by Jupiter, according to recent viral claims. Discovered in July 2025, 3I/ATLAS has intrigued astronomers and the public alike, as it is only the third confirmed visitor from outside our solar system. As new orbital models circulate online, they suggest that the comet's trajectory could be altered by Jupiter's gravity, prompting speculation about its future path.

This development has sparked significant interest in the scientific community. Authorities are investigating the validity of these claims while emphasizing the importance of established astronomical data. The potential implications for our understanding of celestial mechanics and interstellar objects are substantial.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS discovered in July 2025
  • Closest approach to Earth on 19 December 2025, at approximately 1.8 astronomical units (about 170 million miles)
  • Projected close approach to Jupiter on 16 March 2026, within about 0.358 astronomical units
  • Confirmed hyperbolic trajectory, indicating it will continue outward after its solar system journey
  • NASA states no danger of collision with Earth

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2025. It made its closest approach to Earth on 19 December 2025, coming within 1.8 astronomical units. On 16 March 2026, it is expected to pass within 0.358 astronomical units of Jupiter. Discussions about its trajectory have recently gained traction online.

Official statements

“The idea that 3I/ATLAS could change course unexpectedly plays into this intrigue, obviously, as it leads to questions about celestial mechanics and the influence of giant planets like Jupiter on small bodies.”
“While massive planets can perturb small bodies' orbits, the scale of such changes is very well understood and predictable.”

Context

Prior to 3I/ATLAS, only two other interstellar objects have been confirmed: ʻOumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. Each garnered significant scientific interest due to their potential to provide insights into material from beyond our solar system. The rarity of these visitors adds to the fascination surrounding 3I/ATLAS.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS's trajectory and behavior as it approaches Jupiter. Ongoing investigations will assess the validity of viral claims regarding its potential return to Earth.

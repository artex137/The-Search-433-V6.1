Comet 3I/ATLAS carrying an anti-tail to pass Earth on Dec 19

Comet 3I/ATLAS will pass closest to Earth on December 19, coming within approximately 268.9 million kilometres, according to researchers. The comet poses no risk to our planet.

This upcoming event is significant due to the rare appearance of an 'anti-tail.' Typically, a comet's tail points away from the Sun; however, images show that in this case, dust extends toward the Sun, contradicting expected comet behaviour.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS will pass Earth on December 19.
  • Closest approach distance will be about 268.9 million kilometres.
  • The comet exhibits a rare 'anti-tail' phenomenon.
  • Researchers have noted this behaviour contradicts typical comet behaviour.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS has been observed by researchers leading up to its approach. Recent images have revealed the unusual anti-tail, prompting further study.

Official statements

“The appearance of the anti-tail contradicts the expected behaviour of comets,” researchers have stated.

Context

Anti-tails are rarely seen in comets, making this event particularly noteworthy for astronomers. Understanding the behaviour of comets can provide insights into their composition and the conditions in the early solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth, analyzing its characteristics and behaviour during the closest approach.

Alien Invasion Countdown: Scientists Warn of Hostile Interstellar Object Hitting Earth This November 2025

As November 2025 approaches, scientists have raised concerns about the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which could potentially have hostile intent towards Earth. The object, discovered on 1 July 2025 by the ATLAS telescope in Chile, is expected to reach its closest point to the Sun, or perihelion, on 30 October 2025. The object poses no immediate threat as there have been no reported injuries or fatalities.

This situation has prompted various scientific discussions, particularly from a team at Harvard University. The team suggests that 3I/ATLAS may exhibit behaviour indicative of an artificial object. While NASA has classified 3I/ATLAS as a natural comet, ongoing observations continue to fuel public speculation regarding its nature and trajectory.

Key facts

  • Object Name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovery Date: 1 July 2025
  • Closest Approach to Sun: 30 October 2025
  • Close Approaches: Venus (0.65 AU), Mars (0.19 AU), Jupiter (0.36 AU)
  • NASA Budget for 2025: £16.55 billion ($25.4 billion) for monitoring interstellar objects
  • Notable Signals: Strong radio signal from Relay 2 on 30 June 2025

Timeline

On 1 July 2025, astronomers discovered 3I/ATLAS using the ATLAS telescope in Chile. A paper published by Harvard professor Avi Loeb and co-authors on 17 July 2025 examined the object's trajectory and potential behaviour. On 30 June 2025, a long-dead NASA satellite, Relay 2, emitted a strong radio signal, further fueling speculation about the object's intent. The perihelion on 30 October 2025 marks a critical point for further observations.

Official statements

“This paper is contingent on a remarkable but testable hypothesis, to which the authors do not necessarily ascribe.”

- Avi Loeb, Harvard University

“Interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS appear natural based on current observations.”

- Tom Statler, NASA

Context

Prior to the current analysis of 3I/ATLAS, there have been various discussions surrounding the potential for interstellar objects to be artificial. However, NASA has consistently maintained that these objects, including 3I/ATLAS, exhibit natural behaviours. The 2025 budget supports continued monitoring of interstellar threats, which has become a priority as public interest in such phenomena grows.

What’s next

As the perihelion approaches on 30 October 2025, astronomers will conduct detailed observations of 3I/ATLAS. These observations may provide crucial data that could clarify the object's nature, potentially debunking theories of alien origins while advancing comet science.

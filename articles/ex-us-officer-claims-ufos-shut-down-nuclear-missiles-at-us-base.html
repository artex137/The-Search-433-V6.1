Ex-US officer claims UFOs shut down nuclear missiles at US base

Former U.S. Air Force officer Robert Salas has stated that unidentified flying objects (UFOs) disabled nuclear missiles at a military base in Montana in 1967. No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this incident.

This claim is significant as it raises questions about national security and the potential implications of extraterrestrial technology on military operations. Authorities have not publicly commented on the claims made by Salas, who has been vocal about his experiences and the need for further investigation into UFO sightings and their potential impact on nuclear facilities.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred at Malmstrom Air Force Base, Montana, in 1967.
  • Robert Salas was a missile launch officer at the time of the incident.
  • No injuries or fatalities reported.
  • Claims have been made regarding multiple missile shutdowns.
  • Salas has called for more transparency regarding UFO investigations.

Timeline

In 1967, Robert Salas was on duty at Malmstrom Air Force Base when he reported that ten nuclear missiles had been rendered inoperable. He claims that during this time, he and his team observed UFOs hovering above the facility. This incident has come to light as Salas continues to share his account publicly.

Salas has since advocated for a review of military protocols regarding unidentified aerial phenomena, emphasizing the need for accountability and investigation into such occurrences.

Official statements

“I witnessed the shutdown of multiple nuclear missiles in a matter of minutes.” - Robert Salas

Context

This claim is not an isolated incident. There have been several reports over the years of UFO sightings near military bases and their potential interference with nuclear systems. These reports have sparked discussions about the need for more stringent protocols regarding national security and aerial threats.

Historically, the U.S. government has been cautious in addressing UFO sightings, often attributing them to natural phenomena or misidentifications. Salas’ claims add to the growing body of evidence suggesting that these incidents warrant further investigation.

What’s next

As of now, there are no formal investigations announced regarding Salas’ claims. However, there is an ongoing dialogue about the need for transparency in military UFO investigations, which may lead to an official review of similar incidents in the future.

NASA Leak Allegedly Reveals Stunning 3I/ATLAS Interstellar Object Sparking Intriguing Speculations in Japan

Recent discussions on social media have circulated unverified claims about a new image of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, allegedly leaked from NASA. This photo, reportedly revealing structural features resembling a spacecraft, has generated excitement among astronomy enthusiasts in Japan and beyond. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this event.

The claims about the leaked image matter because they could provide insights into the structure of 3I/ATLAS, a significant interstellar comet discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS telescope in Chile. Authorities are currently investigating the authenticity of the claims while astronomy observers in Japan have reported capturing impressive views of the comet.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Speed: Over 210,000 km/h
  • Distance from Earth: Approximately 1.8 astronomical units (270 million km)
  • Closest Approach to the Sun: October 30, 2025
  • Viewing Opportunities: Visible in the morning sky from early November to December 2025

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS telescope in Chile. Since then, it has been travelling at a speed of over 210,000 km/h on a hyperbolic path through the solar system. The leaked image surfaced recently, prompting discussions and speculation online. Observers in Japan have reportedly captured views of the comet, further fueling interest.

Official statements

“The leaked image displays a defined shape covered in space dust,” said an anonymous source discussing the speculation on social media platforms.
“We are eager to study 3I/ATLAS and understand its composition,” stated a representative from the European Space Agency regarding upcoming observations.

Context

3I/ATLAS is significant for understanding the composition and formation of comets from various regions of the galaxy. Its high carbon dioxide content, similar to that of comets found in distant star systems, adds to its intrigue. The comet follows a path that does not allow it to orbit the Sun, making it a unique object of study.

What’s next

In November 2025, the European Space Agency’s JUICE mission plans to study 3I/ATLAS using advanced instruments. Scientists expect to capture images revealing its active features, with data from these observations anticipated to be available by February 2026.

Hundreds of New Mexico locals asked to turn over home security in search for missing retired Air Force general, UFO expert

Over 600 residents in Albuquerque, New Mexico, have been asked to provide home security footage as authorities continue their search for missing retired Air Force general William Neil McCasland, 68, who disappeared from his home on February 28. The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office has initiated this request as the case enters its 11th day.

Authorities are working with the FBI to investigate the disappearance of McCasland, who left his phone behind when he vanished. The police have reported no confirmed sightings of him, despite receiving dozens of tips. Investigators aim to gather any relevant information that could lead to his whereabouts.

Key facts

  • William Neil McCasland, 68, has been missing since February 28, 2026.
  • Authorities have contacted over 600 homeowners for security footage.
  • No confirmed sightings have been reported as of now.
  • The Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office and FBI are involved in the investigation.
  • A Silver Alert has been issued for McCasland.

Timeline

February 28, 2026: William Neil McCasland disappears from his home in Albuquerque, leaving his phone behind.

March 10, 2026: Authorities reach out to over 600 local residents for home security footage and information about McCasland.

As of now, investigators have not confirmed any sightings or leads that have resulted in locating McCasland.

Official statements

“Investigators have contacted more than 600 homeowners in the neighborhood near Mr. McCasland’s residence to request security camera footage or information,” a police spokesperson said.
“While none have resulted in a confirmed sighting or led directly to Mr. McCasland’s location, investigators are continuing to follow up on every piece of information received,” police added.
Journalist Ross Coulthart stated, “This is a man with some of the most sensitive secrets of the United States in his head,” referring to McCasland's knowledge of UFOs and national security.

Context

McCasland's disappearance has raised concerns about national security, as he has held significant positions within the military, including overseeing classified space weapons programs. His knowledge is seen as critical, especially in light of recent governmental discussions about UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

The case has garnered attention due to the lack of information surrounding McCasland's disappearance and its potential implications for national security.

What’s next

Authorities continue to investigate the case and are urging anyone with information about McCasland to contact the Bernalillo County Sheriff’s Office. They have provided a text option at 847411 and a phone number at 505-468-7070 for tips.

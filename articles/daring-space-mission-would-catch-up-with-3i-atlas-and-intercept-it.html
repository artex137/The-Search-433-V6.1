Daring Space Mission Would Catch Up With 3I/ATLAS and Intercept It

Scientists have proposed a daring mission to intercept interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which is currently speeding away from the solar system. The mission could launch by 2035, with the goal of catching up to 3I/ATLAS by 2085, as reported by Space.com. The mission presents significant challenges due to the object's distance and speed.

This mission is important because 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity to study the nature of planetary systems beyond our own. Scientists believe that a probe could provide valuable insights into the object, which is releasing carbon dioxide and water vapor that may date back billions of years. However, the feasibility of the mission remains under debate.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was discovered in July 2022.
  • It made its closest approach to the Sun in late October 2022.
  • The proposed mission would require a launch by 2035.
  • 3I/ATLAS will be over 700 times the distance from the Sun to Earth by 2085.
  • The spacecraft would need to perform close flybys of the Sun to gain speed.
  • A delta-V of over 8.05 km/s (five miles per second) is required for acceleration.
  • SpaceX's Starship Block 3 is suggested for the mission.
  • The European Space Agency plans to launch its Comet Interceptor mission by late 2028.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first discovered in July 2022, as it approached the solar system. The object made its closest approach to the Sun in late October 2022. Since then, it has been traveling away from the solar system, releasing significant amounts of gases.

In 2023, scientists began discussing the potential for a mission to intercept 3I/ATLAS. They proposed that a probe could launch by 2035 to reach the object by 2085. The mission would necessitate advanced propulsion techniques, including close flybys of the Sun and Venus.

Official statements

“We’ll just have to see,” said Marshall Eubanks, former NASA staffer and Space Initiatives chief scientist. “Maybe after, say, ten interstellar objects have been found, 3I will seem commonplace and it won’t seem worthwhile to mount an expedition to chase it.”
“There are better mission architectures, using a probe already in orbit in space, which would intercept an interstellar object around perihelion in much less time, rendering an Oberth unnecessary,” said Adam Hibberd, creator of Optimum Interplanetary Trajectory Software.

Context

3I/ATLAS is part of a growing interest in interstellar objects, following the detection of several such entities in recent years. The development of more powerful space telescopes, such as the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, is expected to enhance our ability to identify future interstellar visitors. The European Space Agency's Comet Interceptor mission represents a proactive approach to studying these objects.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to assess the viability of the proposed mission to 3I/ATLAS. Meanwhile, the European Space Agency is preparing its Comet Interceptor mission for launch by late 2028, which may pave the way for future studies of interstellar objects.

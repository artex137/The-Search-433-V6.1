Onset of CN Emission in 3I/ATLAS: Evidence for Strong Carbon-Chain Depletion

Researchers have observed the onset of CN emission in the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS during a monitoring campaign at the MDM Observatory from August 8 to 17, 2025. The comet, which was inbound at heliocentric distances of 3.2 to 2.9 astronomical units (au), has shown signs of weak gas-driven activity with a measured CN production rate of approximately 8×1023 s−1.

This discovery is significant for understanding the physical and chemical properties of interstellar objects. Authorities have called for further observations of 3I/ATLAS to verify the apparent carbon-chain depletion and to determine if this composition is common among interstellar comets.

Key facts

  • Observation period: August 8 to 17, 2025
  • Location: MDM Observatory
  • Telescopes used: 2.4 m Hiltner and 1.3 m McGraw-Hill telescopes
  • Heliocentric distances: 3.2 to 2.9 au
  • CN production rate: ∼8×1023 s−1
  • C2-to-CN ratio limit: logQ(C2)/Q(CN)<−1.05
  • Gas-to-dust production ratio: logQ(CN)/Afρ<21.49

Timeline

Between August 8 and 17, 2025, researchers conducted a spectroscopic and photometric monitoring campaign of the comet 3I/ATLAS. Initial observations did not reveal any CN emission, but a weak emission feature appeared and strengthened over the course of the campaign.

By the end of the observation period, the comet exhibited measurable increases in its coma’s radial profile and the r-band Afρ, indicating enhanced activity.

Official statements

“Further observations of 3I/ATLAS are required to verify the apparent carbon-chain depletion,” said Luis E. Salazar Manzano, one of the researchers involved in the study.

Context

The discovery of 3I/ATLAS marks it as the second interstellar comet ever observed, providing a unique opportunity to compare its properties with those of comets in the Solar System. Previous studies have suggested variations in the composition and activity of these distant objects, making ongoing research essential.

What’s next

Researchers plan to conduct further observations of 3I/ATLAS to confirm the carbon-chain depletion and to explore whether this characteristic is common within the interstellar comet population.

Aliens Spying On Earth’s Nuclear Sites? Alarming Study Links Spike In UFO-Like 'Transients' Appearing Before And After Atomic Tests

A new study has claimed that unidentified objects may have been observing nuclear activities on Earth for over eight decades. The research, led by Dr. Beatriz Villarroel of the Nordic Institute for Theoretical Physics in Sweden, suggests non-human intelligence could have monitored above-ground nuclear testing during the early atomic age. The study highlights mysterious sky anomalies known as “transients” linked to nuclear tests conducted by the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Soviet Union between 1949 and 1957.

This research matters as it raises questions about the origins of these anomalies and their possible connection to human nuclear activities. Authorities and scientists are now examining the implications of these findings, which have successfully passed peer review, a significant milestone for studies on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs).

Key facts

  • Study led by Dr. Beatriz Villarroel, Nordic Institute for Theoretical Physics, Sweden.
  • Transients linked to 124 above-ground nuclear tests between 1949 and 1957.
  • Transients appeared 45% more likely immediately before or after nuclear tests.
  • 85% increase in the total number of objects photographed on nuclear test days.
  • Over 100,000 transients identified; approximately 35,000 in the northern hemisphere.
  • Study passed peer review, validating its findings.

Timeline

Between 1949 and 1957, the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Soviet Union conducted a series of above-ground nuclear tests. During this period, researchers analyzed thousands of historical photos from the Palomar Observatory Sky Survey in California.

The study identified a significant increase in unidentified bright objects, or transients, particularly on days when nuclear tests occurred. The findings suggest a correlation between these objects and nuclear activities, with many appearing before or after detonations.

Official statements

“These are objects before Sputnik One when humans had nothing up there,” Dr. Villarroel stated. “No matter what they are, they need to be really flat, reflective like a mirror, and I personally don’t know anything natural that looks like that.”

Context

This study adds to a growing body of research regarding UAPs and their potential connections to human activities. Previous studies have struggled to achieve peer review, making this validation a notable achievement in the field.

The research underscores a long-standing interest in the relationship between nuclear testing and UAP sightings, particularly during the formative years of the atomic age.

What’s next

Further investigations will likely explore the nature of these transients and their possible implications for understanding non-human intelligence and its relationship with human activities, particularly nuclear testing.

The mysterious interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is about to make its close approach to Earth

Comet 3I/ATLAS is set to make its closest approach to Earth on Friday, 19 December 2025, at a distance of approximately 270 million kilometres. The comet, discovered on 1 July 2025, has generated excitement as it travels through our Solar System.

This close approach marks a significant milestone for astronomers and enthusiasts alike. While the comet will not come close enough to be classified as a near-Earth object, its visibility offers an opportunity for both professional and amateur astronomers to observe and document its passage.

Key facts

  • Event Date: Friday, 19 December 2025
  • Distance from Earth: Approximately 270 million km (170 million miles)
  • Discovery Date: 1 July 2025
  • Notable Telescopes Used: Gemini South, Very Large Telescope, Hubble Space Telescope, James Webb Space Telescope
  • Live-streaming: Available through the Virtual Telescope Project

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was discovered on 1 July 2025. It quickly captured attention as an interstellar visitor, having formed beyond our Solar System. The comet disappeared behind the Sun in autumn 2025 before re-emerging in the winter morning sky.

Since its discovery, 3I/ATLAS has been photographed by various telescopes and spacecraft, including those observing solar activity and missions on their way to Mars and Jupiter.

Official statements

"This cometary milestone is sure to be recorded and captured by professional astronomers using humanity's best telescopes," said a spokesperson from NASA.

Context

The excitement surrounding 3I/ATLAS has included claims of it being an alien spacecraft, which the majority of professional astronomers have refuted. The comet has provided a unique opportunity for public engagement in astronomy.

What’s next

As the comet approaches Earth, astronomers are preparing to document its passage. Amateur astronomers are encouraged to observe the comet and share their images, which may be featured in upcoming publications.

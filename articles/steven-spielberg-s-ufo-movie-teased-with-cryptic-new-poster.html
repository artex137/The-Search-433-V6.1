Steven Spielberg’s UFO movie teased with cryptic new poster

Production on Steven Spielberg’s upcoming UFO movie has wrapped this summer, but details about the project remain largely undisclosed. A new billboard in Times Square has surfaced, featuring a cryptic design and the tagline, “All Will Be Disclosed.” The film is set for theatrical release on June 12, 2026.

The marketing for the film has begun to emerge, prompting speculation about its title and content. The billboard’s imagery has led some to believe that the long-rumoured title, Disclosure, may be the final choice. David Koepp has scripted the film based on a story by Spielberg, and it features a star-studded cast including Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo, and Wyatt Russell.

Key facts

  • Film production wrapped in summer 2023
  • Theatrical release date: June 12, 2026
  • Billboard located in Times Square
  • Tagline: “All Will Be Disclosed”
  • Cast includes: Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo, Wyatt Russell
  • Score composed by John Williams, marking his 30th collaboration with Spielberg

Timeline

In June 2023, Spielberg shared footage from the film at an event, showcasing scenes such as Blunt being chased by unmarked black cars and a collision with a speeding train. The footage also featured an underground control room overseen by Firth's character, who appears to be the antagonist.

Following the completion of filming, marketing efforts have started, with the unveiling of a billboard in Times Square. Rumours suggest that the first teaser trailer may be screened with the upcoming release of Avatar: Fire and Ash.

Official statements

“I’ll stick around for awhile,” Williams said regarding his decision to compose the score. “I can’t retire from music. A day without music is a mistake.”
Williams also mentioned, “One thing Steven isn’t is a man you can say no to.”

Context

Spielberg has had a long-standing career in film, with a history of successful collaborations with composer John Williams dating back to 1974. The anticipation surrounding this UFO movie reflects a growing public interest in the genre, particularly in light of recent discussions about UFOs in popular culture.

What’s next

As marketing for the film ramps up, fans can expect the release of the first teaser trailer in the near future. Further details about the film’s plot and title are anticipated but have not yet been disclosed.

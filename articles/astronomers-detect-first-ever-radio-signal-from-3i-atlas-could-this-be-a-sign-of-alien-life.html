Astronomers Detect First Ever Radio Signal from 3I/ATLAS… Could This Be a Sign of Alien Life?

Published 2025-11-13 13:31 UTC

Astronomers Detect First Ever Radio Signal from 3I/ATLAS

Space astronomers have detected the first radio signal from the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS using the MeerKAT radio telescope in South Africa on October 24, 2025. This discovery confirms the comet's nature and provides insights into objects from beyond our solar system.

This detection is significant because it enhances our understanding of interstellar objects. The MeerKAT telescope has captured important data about 3I/ATLAS, particularly regarding its outgassing behavior, which occurs as the comet approaches the Sun.

Key facts

  • Date of detection: October 24, 2025
  • Location of telescope: MeerKAT, South Africa
  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Detection of hydroxyl (OH) molecules confirmed
  • Previous detection attempts: Late September 2025
  • Type of object: Third confirmed interstellar visitor

Timeline

In late September 2025, astronomers at MeerKAT attempted to detect radio emissions from 3I/ATLAS but were unsuccessful. On October 24, 2025, they successfully captured the first radio signal from the comet, just after it reached perihelion.

The radio waves detected contained absorption features consistent with hydroxyl molecules, confirming that the comet is emitting water vapor as it is heated by solar radiation.

Official statements

“The absorption features are consistent with the expected OH level population due to the heliocentric velocity of the comet,” said one of the researchers involved in the study.
Harvard astronomer Avi Loeb stated, “Given that 3I/ATLAS was separated from the Sun by 1.38 times the Earth-Sun separation, its surface temperature was smaller than that of Earth by roughly the square root of 1.38.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is not the first interstellar object detected, following 1I/‘Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. The significance of these discoveries lies in their potential to provide insights into the conditions of other star systems, as interstellar objects like 3I/ATLAS have traveled through the galaxy for billions of years.

What’s next

Astronomers will continue to study the data from the MeerKAT telescope to learn more about the composition and behavior of 3I/ATLAS as it passes through our solar system.

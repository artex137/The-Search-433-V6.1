Hubble detects synchronized wobbles in three jets of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS after perihelion

The Hubble Space Telescope has detected synchronized wobbles in three jets of the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS after its closest approach to the Sun, known as perihelion, which occurred on February 14, 2023. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to this astronomical event.

This discovery is significant as it provides insights into the behavior of interstellar comets and their jet dynamics. Researchers aim to understand the processes affecting the comet's structure and movement. The findings may help scientists learn more about the composition and behavior of these celestial objects.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS reached perihelion on February 14, 2023.
  • Hubble Space Telescope conducted observations after the perihelion event.
  • The synchronized wobbles were detected in three jets emitted from the comet.
  • This is the first time such behavior has been observed in an interstellar comet.

Timeline

On February 14, 2023, comet 3I/ATLAS passed closest to the Sun, a critical point in its orbit. Following this event, the Hubble Space Telescope observed the comet and identified unique synchronized wobbles in its jets.

The jets of the comet have shown complex behavior, and researchers are analyzing the data collected to better understand these phenomena.

Official statements

“The synchronized jets provide a rare glimpse into the dynamics of interstellar comets,” said a researcher involved in the study.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable for being the first recognized interstellar comet, having entered the Solar System in 2019. Observations of its behavior enhance our understanding of such comets, which originate from outside our solar system.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from Hubble to gain further insights into the comet's jets and their implications for our understanding of interstellar objects.

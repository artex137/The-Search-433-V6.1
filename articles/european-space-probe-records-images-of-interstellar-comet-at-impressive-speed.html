European space probe records images of interstellar comet at impressive speed

A European space probe has captured images of the interstellar comet 3I ATLAS as it travels through our solar system, according to the European Space Agency (ESA). The probe recorded these images in late February 2026, while the comet was located near the orbit of Jupiter.

This event is significant as it marks one of the fastest-moving comets observed, providing scientists with valuable data about its composition and trajectory. ESA has stated that the images will help enhance our understanding of interstellar objects and their origins.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I ATLAS was observed in late February 2026.
  • The probe is part of the European Space Agency’s ongoing mission to study interstellar objects.
  • The comet is currently near the orbit of Jupiter.
  • The speed of the comet has been noted as one of the highest recorded.
  • No injuries or fatalities have been reported related to this event.

Timeline

In February 2026, the European space probe began its observations of comet 3I ATLAS as it approached the inner solar system. The first images were successfully transmitted back to mission control by the end of the month.

As the probe continues to track the comet, scientists are analyzing the data to gain insights into its structure and the materials that compose it. This information is crucial for understanding the origins of such interstellar objects.

Official statements

“The images of comet 3I ATLAS provide us with a unique opportunity to study an interstellar object up close,” said a spokesperson for the European Space Agency.

Context

The observation of comet 3I ATLAS follows the discovery of other interstellar objects, such as Oumuamua in 2017 and comet 2I/Borisov in 2019. These discoveries have sparked interest in the scientific community regarding the nature and origins of such bodies.

ESA’s mission aims to expand our knowledge of the solar system and the objects that pass through it, contributing to broader research in astronomy and planetary science.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the data received from the probe. Further observations are planned as the comet moves closer to the Sun, which may provide additional insights into its characteristics.

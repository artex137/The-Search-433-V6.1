A Montana University Dives Into The Academic Study Of UFOs

Montana Technological University in Butte has announced it will offer classes focused on the academic study of UFOs, marking a significant step in the field as interest in the phenomenon grows. The courses will be led by Dr. Michael P. Masters, an author and professor, who aims to explore the implications of UFO sightings and evidence.

This initiative is notable as it positions Montana Tech among the first universities in the United States to take UFO studies seriously. With the recent release of government documents confirming the existence of unidentified flying objects, the academic community is beginning to acknowledge the importance of this subject. The classes aim to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of UFO phenomena, although no degree in this area is currently offered.

Key facts

  • Location: Montana Technological University, Butte, Montana
  • Founded: 1900, originally as the School of Mines
  • Affiliation: Became part of the University of Montana in 1994
  • Instructor: Dr. Michael P. Masters, author and professor
  • Course focus: Evidence and implications of UFO sightings
  • Degree status: No degree offered on UFO studies at this time

Timeline

Montana Technological University has evolved from its origins as the School of Mines since its founding in 1900. In recent years, the university has expanded its curriculum to include contemporary issues, including the study of UFOs.

In 2023, the university announced it would introduce classes that examine the UFO phenomenon, responding to growing interest and recent government disclosures on the subject.

Official statements

“What was once considered something a bit ‘kooky’ has picked up steam in the last several years,” said Dr. Masters, highlighting the shift in perception regarding UFO studies.

Context

The UFO phenomenon has a long history in Montana, notably marked by the Malmstrom UFO incident in 1967 during the Cold War. This incident has contributed to the state's reputation regarding UFO sightings, influencing the decision to incorporate this topic into the academic curriculum.

What’s next

Montana Technological University plans to begin offering these UFO-focused classes in the upcoming academic term, providing students with an opportunity to engage with a previously marginalized subject in academia.

President Calls Extraterrestrials ‘Real’, Astrophysicist Claps Back: ‘Bring Forth the Alien’

Former President Barack Obama has stated that extraterrestrials are “real” but that he has not encountered them during his time in office. Current President Donald Trump criticized Obama for revealing “classified” information and announced plans to release government files related to extraterrestrial life. Neil deGrasse Tyson, a prominent astrophysicist, has responded by urging the government to produce actual evidence instead of documents.

This exchange has reignited public interest in UFOs and governmental transparency regarding extraterrestrial life. Authorities are now under scrutiny to clarify their stance on the existence of aliens and the associated research.

Key facts

  • Former President Barack Obama made comments about aliens during a recent podcast.
  • Current President Donald Trump criticized Obama’s disclosure of classified information.
  • Trump announced plans to release government files on extraterrestrial life.
  • Neil deGrasse Tyson has called for the government to present any actual alien evidence.
  • Obama clarified that he meant statistically, life exists elsewhere in the universe.

Timeline

During a podcast, Obama responded to a question about the existence of aliens, stating, “They’re real. But I haven’t seen them.” He jokingly mentioned his curiosity about alien life when he became President.

Following Obama's remarks, Trump held a press conference where he criticized Obama for revealing classified information and stated, “I can’t tell you if they’re real or not.” He then announced his intention to identify and release government files related to aliens.

Tyson responded to Trump’s announcement, suggesting that the government should instead produce any actual aliens rather than just documents.

Official statements

“If I were Trump, I would have instead said, ‘I want any government agency that has an alien to bring forth the alien.’ Then you don’t need documents.”
“Until that happens, it seems to be all about who you believe, and what conspiracy theory.” - Neil deGrasse Tyson

Context

Obama's comments about aliens mark a significant moment in the ongoing conversation about extraterrestrial life. His previous stance in 2007 suggested uncertainty regarding the existence of life beyond Earth. The recent exchange between Trump and Obama highlights the ongoing debate about transparency in governmental UFO research.

Tyson has previously discussed the potential unifying effect of discovering extraterrestrial life, suggesting that it could bring humanity together despite current divisions.

What’s next

As Trump prepares to release files related to extraterrestrial life, the public will be watching closely for any new information. Tyson's challenge to produce tangible evidence may prompt further discussions on the government's approach to UFOs and alien life.

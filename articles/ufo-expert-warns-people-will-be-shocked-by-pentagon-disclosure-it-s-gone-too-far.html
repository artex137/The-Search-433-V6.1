UFO expert warns 'people will be shocked' by Pentagon disclosure - 'It's gone too far'

A UFO investigator has raised concerns about imminent revelations regarding Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP) as the Pentagon begins releasing previously classified documents. The disclosure process, initiated by former President Donald Trump, has started this month with the release of files documenting reported sightings dating back to the 1940s.

This disclosure is significant as it represents a shift in government transparency regarding UAPs, a topic of growing public interest. The Pentagon has spent years working to declassify these documents, and the recent releases could reshape perceptions of extraterrestrial life and government knowledge about it.

Key facts

  • Former President Donald Trump directed the US government to release UAP documents in February.
  • The Pentagon published its first batch of files on May 8, including hundreds of pages and over 20 videos of unidentified objects.
  • The second batch released last week contained 64 files, including six PDFs, seven audio files, and 51 videos.
  • The documents cover sightings from locations including Syria, Japan, and North America.
  • Josh Golembeske, a UAP investigator, has stated that the evidence indicates visits from extraterrestrial beings.

Timeline

In February, Donald Trump directed the US government to start identifying and releasing official files on UAPs. On May 8, the Pentagon released its first set of documents, which included historical sightings and military video footage. Last week, the Pentagon followed up with a second batch of documents, containing additional videos and audio files.

Official statements

“There’s been a lid put on it,” said Josh Golembeske, a UAP investigator. “The evidence is overwhelming that we're being visited.”
“I think this is a slow drip disclosure, and you could argue it's compassionate disclosure,” Golembeske added. “I wouldn't just drop everything on them.”

Context

The recent disclosures mark a notable change in how the US government addresses UAPs. Previously, information on UFO sightings was often classified, leading to speculation and conspiracy theories. Golembeske believes that the gradual release of information is intended to prepare the public for potential revelations about extraterrestrial contact.

What’s next

As the Pentagon continues to release UAP documents, further disclosures are expected. Golembeske has suggested that more significant revelations may be approaching, indicating a shift in how the government communicates about extraterrestrial life.

Watch comet 3I/ATLAS race toward interstellar space in free livestream tonight

Viewers can watch comet 3I/ATLAS in a free livestream tonight, January 22, starting at 6:30 p.m. EST (2330 GMT), as it reaches opposition and moves out of the solar system. The event will be broadcast by the Virtual Telescope Project from Manciano, Italy.

This livestream is significant as it allows the public to observe the third known interstellar visitor to the solar system. Authorities have noted that the comet will be too faint to see with the naked eye, requiring large telescopes for observation. The comet's brightness is forecasted to reach a magnitude of +13.2, according to the Comet Observation Database (COBS).

Key facts

  • Event: Livestream of comet 3I/ATLAS
  • Date: January 22, 2026
  • Time: 6:30 p.m. EST (2330 GMT)
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Magnitude: +13.2
  • Closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Next flyby: March 2026, passing 33.4 million miles (53.7 million km) from Jupiter

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first detected on July 1, 2025, by astronomers using data from the NASA-funded Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) project.

It made its closest approach to the sun on October 31, 2025, before disappearing behind the glare of a parent star for several weeks.

The comet then emerged for its closest proximity pass of Earth, known as perigee, on December 19, 2025.

Official statements

“Magnitude is used to measure the apparent brightness of objects in the night sky. The lower the number, the brighter the object!”

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar comet detected in our solar system, following 1I/'Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Its detection adds to the growing body of knowledge regarding interstellar objects and their trajectories.

What’s next

After the livestream, 3I/ATLAS is expected to continue its trajectory out of the solar system, with a planetary flyby planned for March 2026. It will not return to the solar system once it departs.

'I Have Seen No Evidence Of Aliens': Elon Musk Shuts Down UFO Sightings and Hints Mysterious Objects Could Be 'New Weapons Program'

Elon Musk has stated that he does not believe aliens have visited Earth and suggested that unidentified flying objects (UFOs) may be related to U.S. government projects rather than extraterrestrial beings. Musk shared his views during a recent appearance on Katie Miller's podcast.

This statement is significant as it reflects Musk's perspective amidst ongoing public interest in UFOs and government investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are actively exploring these sightings, with discussions around potential military implications and the need for transparency regarding UFO-related information.

Key facts

  • Elon Musk discussed UFOs on Katie Miller's podcast.
  • Musk has not seen evidence of aliens or received any reports from his SpaceX team regarding extraterrestrials.
  • Musk speculated that UFOs could be related to new weapons programs or hypersonic missiles.
  • Prior to this, Musk promised to share any evidence of aliens if he were aware of it.
  • Vice President JD Vance has expressed interest in investigating UFOs further.

Timeline

During his podcast appearance, Musk stated, "I have seen no evidence of aliens," reinforcing his belief that UFOs are not extraterrestrial in origin. He has asked his SpaceX team about any evidence of aliens, to which they have reported none. Musk suggested that UFOs could instead be prototypes of military technology.

In a previous appearance on Joe Rogan's podcast, Musk promised to reveal any evidence of aliens if he came across it. Recently, Vice President JD Vance acknowledged his interest in UFOs and expressed a desire to investigate the matter further.

Official statements

"I have seen no evidence of aliens," Musk told Miller during the interview. "No one on the SpaceX senior team has any evidence of aliens."
"UFOs... it could be like a new weapons program... or hypersonic missiles. It's just basically a weapons prototype. It's not aliens," Musk added.
"I really want to sort of dig into it," Vance said in an interview. "I can't allow myself to become so busy that I spend the next three years and I don't get to the bottom of this."

Context

Musk's comments come amid heightened interest in UFOs, particularly following recent government reports on unidentified aerial phenomena. The discussion around UFOs has gained traction in various circles, including political figures who are calling for greater transparency regarding military encounters with these objects.

In addition to Musk's aspirations for Mars colonization, experts have raised concerns about the feasibility and potential dangers of human settlement on the planet, which could spark further debate about humanity's future in space.

What’s next

Investigations into UFO sightings are ongoing, with authorities like the U.S. government continuing to explore the implications of these phenomena. Vance and others are expected to pursue inquiries into the matter, although timelines for any findings have not been disclosed.

'Felt Like Spotting a UFO': Strong Winds Send Waterfall Into Reverse

A man witnessed a waterfall flowing in reverse at the Sublime Point lookout near Katoomba, New South Wales, on a windy day. Luke Buckle described the experience as surreal, saying it “felt like spotting a UFO.” No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This unusual natural phenomenon has drawn attention to the effects of strong winds on local landmarks. Authorities have noted that weather conditions can lead to dramatic changes in natural features, prompting discussions about visitor safety during extreme weather events.

Key facts

  • Event: Reverse waterfall sighting
  • Date: Not disclosed
  • Location: Sublime Point lookout, Katoomba, New South Wales
  • Witness: Luke Buckle
  • Reported by: Storyful

Timeline

Luke Buckle took his family to the Sublime Point lookout on a very windy day. While there, he observed the waterfall flowing upwards, a spectacle he had only seen online before.

Official statements

“I had never seen this before except on the internet,” Buckle said.

Context

Phenomena like reverse waterfalls are rare and can occur under specific weather conditions. The occurrence raises awareness about the power of nature and the importance of caution when visiting scenic viewpoints during adverse weather.

What’s next

Authorities may continue to monitor weather patterns and their effects on local attractions to ensure visitor safety during extreme conditions.

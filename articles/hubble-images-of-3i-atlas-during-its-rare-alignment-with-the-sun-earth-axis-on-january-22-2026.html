Hubble Images of 3I/ATLAS During Its Rare Alignment with the Sun-Earth Axis on January 22, 2026

The Hubble Space Telescope has captured images of comet 3I/ATLAS during its rare alignment with the Sun-Earth axis on January 22, 2026. This alignment occurs approximately once every 5.5 years, allowing astronomers to study the comet's characteristics in detail.

This event is significant for the scientific community as it provides a unique opportunity to observe the comet's behavior and composition. Researchers aim to gather data that could enhance understanding of cometary activities and their impact on the solar system.

Key facts

  • Event date: January 22, 2026
  • Location: Near the Sun-Earth axis
  • Frequency of alignment: Approximately every 5.5 years
  • Observatory: Hubble Space Telescope

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered in 2019. Researchers have tracked its path and prepared for its alignment with the Sun-Earth axis since that time. The recent images taken on January 22, 2026, mark a pivotal moment in the study of this comet.

Official statements

“The alignment provides a rare chance to observe the comet's dynamics and physical properties,” said Dr. Avi Loeb, an astrophysicist involved in the research.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is part of a broader study of comets that have entered the inner solar system. Understanding such comets can help scientists learn more about the early solar system and the formation of planetary bodies.

What’s next

Researchers will analyze the data collected from the Hubble images to gain insights into the comet's composition and trajectory. Further observations may be scheduled during its next alignment.

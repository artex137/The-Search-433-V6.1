5 countries where alien invasion rumours about 3I/ATLAS went viral

As the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS moves through the Solar System, reports of alien invasion have surged in five countries, leading to a wave of speculation. These rumours have gained traction on social media platforms, particularly TikTok and X (formerly Twitter), since early October 2025.

The surge in interest surrounding 3I/ATLAS has prompted authorities, including NASA and the Italian Space Agency, to issue statements to clarify the comet's nature and dispel misinformation. The rapid spread of these theories reflects a growing fascination with extraterrestrial life and the impact of social media on public perception.

Key facts

  • Comet name: 3I/ATLAS
  • Countries affected: United States, India, Brazil, Japan, Italy
  • Date of reports: October 2025
  • Social media platforms: TikTok, X, WhatsApp, YouTube
  • Authorities responding: NASA, Italian Space Agency

Timeline

In early October 2025, amateur astronomers began posting sightings of 3I/ATLAS on various social media platforms. This sparked a rapid spread of theories and memes concerning possible alien encounters.

By mid-October, speculation had escalated, particularly in the United States and India, where viral content led to increased Google searches for terms like “alien comet.”

In response, NASA issued multiple statements aimed at calming public fears, while the Italian Space Agency released a press note to debunk myths circulating in the media.

Official statements

“We want to assure the public that 3I/ATLAS is a comet and poses no threat. The excitement around it is understandable, but we urge people to rely on verified information.” — NASA spokesperson
“Misinformation can spread quickly. We are here to clarify that there is no evidence linking 3I/ATLAS to extraterrestrial life.” — Italian Space Agency

Context

This is not the first time that an astronomical event has led to public speculation about alien life. Previous incidents, such as the arrival of 'Oumuamua, have similarly ignited conspiracy theories. The rise of social media has significantly accelerated the pace at which such theories can spread.

Recent trends indicate an increasing public interest in space exploration and the possibility of extraterrestrial encounters, often fueled by popular culture and online discourse.

What’s next

Authorities plan to continue monitoring the situation and providing updates as necessary. Investigations into the spread of misinformation may also take place to better understand its impact on public perception.

NASA’s SPHEREx Examines Comet 3I/ATLAS’s Coma

NASA’s SPHEREx (Spectro-Photometer for the History of the Universe, Epoch of Reionization and Ices Explorer) has examined the coma of comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing important data about the icy body during observations made in December 2025. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This examination is significant as it provides insights into the composition of comet 3I/ATLAS, including dust, water, organic molecules, and carbon dioxide. SPHEREx has the unique capability to observe the sky in 102 infrared colors, which helps scientists understand how the comet's ices react as it travels through the solar system.

Key facts

  • Observations conducted by SPHEREx in December 2025.
  • Comet 3I/ATLAS passed its closest distance to the Sun in late October 2025.
  • SPHEREx can see the sky in 102 infrared colors.
  • The mission is managed by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Southern California.
  • Data is processed at IPAC at Caltech in Pasadena.
  • Analysis conducted by a team of scientists from 13 institutions across the U.S., South Korea, and Taiwan.

Timeline

In late October 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS passed its closest distance to the Sun. Subsequently, during December 2025, SPHEREx conducted observations of the comet's coma, noting a significant brightening of the comet.

Official statements

“The information gathered by SPHEREx helps scientists better understand what materials 3I/ATLAS contains and how the interstellar object’s pristine ices react to the Sun’s heating.” - NASA

Context

This examination of comet 3I/ATLAS contributes to ongoing research on comets and their compositions, which are considered remnants from the early solar system. Understanding these materials can provide insights into the formation of planets and other celestial bodies.

What’s next

The data collected by SPHEREx will continue to be analyzed by the team of scientists, contributing to the broader understanding of comets and their role in the solar system.

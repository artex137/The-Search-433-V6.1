UF professor speaks to Flagler Tiger Bay Club about squids, microbes, UFOs and Mars

Jamie S. Foster, assistant director of the Astraeus Space Institute at the University of Florida, presented on various topics including UFOs and space colonization during a luncheon at the Flagler Tiger Bay Club on October 16, 2025. The event took place at the Hammock Dunes Club near Palm Coast.

This discussion matters as it highlights ongoing research and public interest in extraterrestrial life and space exploration. The Flagler Tiger Bay Club aims to engage the community in these critical scientific conversations.

Key facts

  • Speaker: Jamie S. Foster
  • Affiliation: Astraeus Space Institute, University of Florida
  • Date of event: October 16, 2025
  • Location: Hammock Dunes Club, Palm Coast

Timeline

On October 16, 2025, Jamie S. Foster delivered a presentation at the Flagler Tiger Bay Club. The topics covered included squids, microbes, UFOs, and the potential for colonization of the moon and Mars.

Official statements

“This event is a chance to explore the frontiers of science and what they mean for our future,” said Foster during his presentation.

Context

Interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life has grown in recent years, with various agencies releasing previously classified information. The Astraeus Space Institute is at the forefront of space research and education, aiming to advance public understanding of these subjects.

What’s next

Future discussions and events at the Flagler Tiger Bay Club will continue to feature experts in science and technology, fostering community engagement with contemporary issues in space exploration.

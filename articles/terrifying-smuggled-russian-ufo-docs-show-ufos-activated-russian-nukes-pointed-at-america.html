UFO Sighting Nearly Triggered Nuclear Launch, Claims Journalist

At a congressional hearing on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) on October 24, 2023, investigative journalist George Knapp claimed a 1982 UFO sighting over a Soviet ICBM base nearly led to a nuclear missile launch against the United States. Knapp testified before the House Oversight Committee’s Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets, presenting Soviet-era documents he obtained in the 1990s.

This testimony has raised alarms regarding national security and the potential for historical events to repeat. Lawmakers are now considering further investigations into UAP incidents and their implications for current military protocols.

Key facts

  • Date of UFO sighting: 1982
  • Location: Soviet ICBM base, exact location not disclosed
  • Hearing date: October 24, 2023
  • Testifying body: House Oversight Committee’s Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets
  • Source of documents: George Knapp, obtained in the 1990s

Timeline

In 1982, a UFO sighting occurred over a Soviet ICBM base. The event triggered panic in Moscow, leading to fears of a potential nuclear launch against the United States. This incident remained classified until Knapp's recent testimony.

On October 24, 2023, Knapp presented his findings at a congressional hearing, highlighting the gravity of the situation and its implications for nuclear security.

Official statements

“The UFO encounter set off panic in Moscow and brought the world terrifyingly close to nuclear war,” said George Knapp during his testimony.

Context

This incident is significant in the context of Cold War tensions between the United States and the Soviet Union. Previous UFO sightings have also raised concerns about military readiness and response protocols. The ongoing investigation into UAPs seeks to understand their potential impact on national security.

What’s next

Lawmakers are expected to pursue further investigations into UAP incidents and their implications for military protocols, with a focus on preventing future misunderstandings that could lead to conflict.

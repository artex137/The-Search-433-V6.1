Aliens, Natural Phenophenon Or Something Else? Why Is Birmingham Sky Turning Pink? Videos Go Viral

Published 2026-01-09 14:32 UTC

Pink Skies Over Birmingham Prompt Speculation, Revealed as LED Lights

The skies in Birmingham, UK, turned a striking pink on the evening of January 8, 2023, leaving residents curious and mesmerized. The phenomenon occurred primarily in the West Midlands, coinciding with heavy snowfall, and quickly garnered attention on social media as videos and pictures went viral.

This incident has sparked widespread speculation about its cause, ranging from theories about aliens to rare natural phenomena. However, authorities have clarified that the pink hue was due to LED lights at Birmingham City's football ground, St Andrew's stadium, rather than any mysterious occurrence.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred on January 8, 2023, in Birmingham.
  • Phenomenon caused by LED lights on the pitch at Birmingham City's St Andrew's stadium.
  • Heavy snowfall accompanied the event, enhancing the visual effect.
  • Videos and images of the pink skies went viral on social media.
  • Weather experts confirmed the lights reflected by cloud cover and falling snow created the glow.

Timeline

On the evening of January 8, Birmingham experienced a heavy snowfall that coincided with the appearance of pink skies. As residents took to social media to share their astonishment, speculation about the cause of the unusual sky colour began to circulate.

Shortly thereafter, it was revealed that the pink skies were a result of fluorescent grow lights used at Birmingham City's St Andrew's stadium. The club confirmed this information, dispelling theories about alien activity or other rare natural phenomena.

As more images of the stadium and the pink skies emerged online, reactions from the public highlighted the surreal beauty of the scene, with many likening it to a scene from a television show.

Official statements

“So funny St Andrews is trending on social media because we have turned the sky pink,” said one social media user.
“Snow in Birmingham under a pink sky,” another user commented, sharing their amazement at the phenomenon.

Context

Incidents of unusual sky colours have occurred in various regions, often leading to speculation and public intrigue. Weather conditions, particularly during storms, can create effects that amplify artificial lights, leading to vibrant and unexpected displays.

The recent heavy snowfall in Birmingham, labelled as Storm Goretti, further contributed to the unique visual experience as it reflected and scattered the LED lights.

What’s next

Authorities are not expected to pursue any investigations regarding the pink skies, as the cause has been clearly identified. The focus will remain on the ongoing weather conditions as the city continues to experience winter weather.

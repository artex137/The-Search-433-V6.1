Is 3I/ATLAS About to Surprise Us Again? How You Can Watch the Latest Breakthrough Unfold

Published 2025-11-17 16:36 UTC

Comet 3I/ATLAS to be Visible on November 18

Comet 3I/ATLAS will be visible to the public on Tuesday, November 18, as it moves further from the sun and out of the solar system. The Virtual Telescope Project will livestream views of the comet from Manciano, Italy, starting at 11:15 p.m. EST.

Why This Matters

This livestream event offers a rare opportunity for the public to observe the interstellar comet, which has stirred significant interest and debate within the scientific community. NASA is also set to release new images of the comet taken by the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, adding to the excitement surrounding the event.

Key facts

  • Event Date: November 18, 2023
  • Livestream Start Time: 11:15 p.m. EST
  • Location: Manciano, Italy
  • Original Date: November 16, 2023 (postponed due to weather)
  • NASA's Role: Releasing new photos of the comet

Timeline

On November 16, 2023, the livestream event for comet 3I/ATLAS was initially scheduled but was postponed due to inclement weather. The event is now set for November 18, 2023, at 11:15 p.m. EST.

As the comet travels away from the sun, it has captured the attention of both scientists and the public, with ongoing discussions about its significance.

Official statements

“Telescopic views of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will be available for viewing,” according to Space.com.
“NASA prepares to release more photos of the space object,” noted Live Science.

Context

The appearance of comet 3I/ATLAS has generated considerable interest and speculation, with some framing it as a potential sign of extraterrestrial life. This controversy reflects broader discussions about cosmic events and their implications.

What’s next

Following the livestream event, NASA will release additional images of 3I/ATLAS, which may further inform the ongoing discussions about the comet's nature and significance.

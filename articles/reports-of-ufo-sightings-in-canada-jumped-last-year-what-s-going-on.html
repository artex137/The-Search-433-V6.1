Reports of UFO sightings in Canada jumped last year. What’s going on?

The 2025 Canadian UFO Survey, released on Monday, has reported a total of 1,052 sightings of unidentified flying objects across Canada, marking the highest number since the COVID-19 pandemic but falling short of the 1,243 sightings recorded in 2020. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to these sightings.

Authorities have noted a renewed interest in UFO reports, particularly after the pandemic. This increase in sightings has prompted discussions about the phenomenon, with experts emphasizing the need for further understanding. According to the survey, 3.42 per cent of the reported sightings remain classified as “unexplained.”

Key facts

  • 1,052 UFO sightings reported in Canada in 2025.
  • 3.42% of sightings classified as “unexplained.”
  • Highest number of sightings since 2020, which recorded 1,243.
  • Average duration of sightings increased to 47 minutes.
  • Majority of reports came from Ontario (30%), Quebec (20%), and B.C. (13.5%).
  • Significant increases noted in Manitoba (55 reports) and Nova Scotia (117 reports).
  • Peak reporting times typically occur at 2200 hours local time.

Timeline

Since the survey began in 1989, the number of UFO sightings in Canada has fluctuated significantly. The peak year was 2012 with 1,982 reports. After a decline, numbers began increasing again post-pandemic. In 2023, only 570 sightings were reported, indicating a resurgence in interest and reporting in 2025.

In 2025, Ontario led the country with 30% of the total sightings, followed by Quebec and British Columbia. Metropolitan areas such as Toronto and Vancouver reported the highest numbers, with 53 and 45 sightings respectively.

Official statements

“If you’ve seen a UFO, you are definitely in very good company,” said survey co-author Rutkowski, highlighting that one in ten Canadians believe they have witnessed a UFO.
“There certainly is a concern about people being a little watchful and wondering what’s going on up there,” Rutkowski added, reflecting on the increased vigilance among the public.

Context

The Canadian UFO Survey has documented a steady stream of reports from coast to coast since its inception. While the overall trend had been declining since 2012, the 2025 figures suggest renewed public interest in unexplained aerial phenomena, possibly influenced by recent media coverage and social media engagement.

In addition, the timing of sightings has shifted. Reports have emerged more frequently in late summer and winter months, contrary to previous patterns that favoured warmer months.

What’s next

Experts are calling for further investigation into the nature of these sightings. The survey results may prompt additional studies into the reasons behind the fluctuations in reported UFO activity across different regions and times of the year.

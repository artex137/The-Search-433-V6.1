New images of 'alien spaceship' comet 3I/ATLAS expelling gas and dust

Space scientists have released a new image of the interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS, which was photographed by ESA's Jupiter Icy Moons Explorer (Juice) spacecraft on November 6, 2025. The image shows the comet expelling dust and gas, raising speculation about its nature.

This discovery is significant as it has prompted renewed debate within the scientific community regarding the possibility of 3I/ATLAS being more than a natural comet. Authorities are analyzing the data collected by the Juice spacecraft to better understand the characteristics of this unusual object.

Key facts

  • The image was captured on November 6, 2025.
  • At the time, Juice was approximately 66 million km away from the comet.
  • More than 120 images of Comet 3I/ATLAS have been taken by the JANUS camera.
  • 3I/ATLAS was first spotted in July 2025.
  • Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysicist, has raised concerns about the comet possibly being a technological artifact.

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first identified in July 2025. The comet made its closest approach to the Sun shortly before the image was captured by Juice on November 6, 2025. Following this, researchers noted a dramatic increase in brightness and began analyzing the images taken by the JANUS camera.

Official statements

"The tiny nucleus of the comet is surrounded by a bright halo of gas known as the coma." - ESA
"The consequences, should the hypothesis turn out to be correct, could potentially be dire for humanity." - Avi Loeb

Context

Prior to this, the scientific community had classified 3I/ATLAS as a natural comet. However, Loeb's claims about the comet's unusual characteristics have sparked a debate about its origins and nature. This includes its atypical trajectory and chemical composition, which some argue resemble artificial constructs.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the images from Juice to gather more data on Comet 3I/ATLAS and assess its characteristics. The scientific community remains divided on the object’s classification, with ongoing discussions about its nature and origin.

Mystery deepens as 3I/ATLAS' tail could be comprised of a 'swarm' of unknown objects: Harvard scientist

Mystery deepens as 3I/ATLAS' tail could be comprised of a 'swarm' of unknown objects: Harvard scientist

Harvard scientist Avi Loeb has claimed that comet 3I/ATLAS may have an unusual “anti-tail” formed by a swarm of unknown objects, according to a blog post published on Medium. This assertion follows observations made in November after the comet's perihelion, when images indicated a teardrop shape in its coma directed toward the sun.

This discovery is significant as it challenges existing notions about cometary behavior. Loeb suggests that if the objects are indeed present, they do not share the non-gravitational acceleration typical of comets, making them appear closer to the sun than 3I/ATLAS. NASA has maintained that 3I/ATLAS is a comet from an unknown star system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is currently about 270 million kilometers (167,770,221 miles) from the Sun.
  • Avi Loeb's findings were shared in a blog post and a preprint draft on arXiv.
  • The anti-tail phenomenon may indicate the presence of non-evaporating objects around the comet.
  • Loeb claims these objects could reflect 99% of sunlight in the glow around 3I/ATLAS.
  • Evidence of cryovolcanoes on 3I/ATLAS was reported during its approach to the sun.

Timeline

In November, after the perihelion of comet 3I/ATLAS, images revealed a teardrop shape in its coma. Loeb analyzed these images and postulated the existence of an anti-tail associated with a swarm of objects.

Earlier this month, Loeb suggested that the comet’s anti-tail might be emitting a beam of light to clear its path of tiny meteors, a behavior not typically expected from comets.

Official statements

“At the current separation of 3I/ATLAS from the Sun of 270 million kilometers, the displacement would imply that the objects are closer to the Sun than 3I/ATLAS by about 54,000 kilometers,” Loeb wrote.
“If the anti-tail is indeed associated with a swarm of non-evaporating objects around 3I/ATLAS, the interesting question is what is the nature of these objects?”

Context

Previous studies of 3I/ATLAS have revealed that the comet shares characteristics with icy trans-Neptunian objects, which are bodies orbiting the sun beyond Neptune. This finding adds to the intrigue surrounding the comet's origins and composition.

Loeb's theories about potential technological origins of the objects have sparked interest and debate within the scientific community.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to study 3I/ATLAS and its unusual features, including the anti-tail and potential surrounding objects. Future observations may clarify the nature of these phenomena and their implications for our understanding of comets.

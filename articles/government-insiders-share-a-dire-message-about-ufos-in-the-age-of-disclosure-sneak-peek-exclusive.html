Government Insiders Share a Dire Message About UFOs in 'The Age of Disclosure' Sneak Peek

Experts have shared alarming insights about unidentified flying objects (UFOs) in a sneak peek of the upcoming documentary, The Age of Disclosure, which premieres on November 21 in select theaters and on Prime Video. The film features interviews with 34 current and former U.S. government and intelligence officials who discuss an alleged 80-year cover-up regarding non-human intelligent life and efforts to reverse-engineer alien technology.

This documentary arrives shortly after September's congressional hearings on unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs). It aims to shed light on the implications of these encounters and the government's response. Authorities are increasingly focused on transparency, with bipartisan support for the release of UAP reports and briefings for Congress members.

Key facts

  • Film Title: The Age of Disclosure
  • Premiere Date: November 21, 2023
  • Interviewees: 34 current and former U.S. government and military officials
  • Key Figures: Christopher Mellon, Hal Puthoff, Lue Elizondo, Marco Rubio
  • Documentary Director: Dan Farah
  • Release Platforms: Select theaters and Prime Video

Timeline

The documentary chronicles a timeline of events surrounding UAPs and government responses:

  • 2017: Lue Elizondo resigns from his government position, bringing Navy pilots' UAP videos to the media.
  • September 2023: Congressional hearings on UAPs take place, prompting renewed public interest.
  • November 21, 2023: The Age of Disclosure premieres.

Official statements

“I found my way to people, high-level officials in the government, military, and intelligence community who have direct knowledge of this topic and made them comfortable opening up in this documentary with what they could lawfully disclose,” said director Dan Farah. “They all felt a responsibility to bring out some of the truth to the public, to the extent they legally could.”

Context

The push for UAP transparency has grown in recent years, marked by bipartisan support from political figures such as Democratic Senator Chuck Schumer and Secretary of State Marco Rubio. This documentary aims to address the public's thirst for information and the government’s historical reluctance to disclose details about UAP encounters.

What’s next

Following the documentary's release, discussions surrounding UAPs and government transparency are expected to continue, with potential implications for future legislation and further investigations into unidentified aerial phenomena.

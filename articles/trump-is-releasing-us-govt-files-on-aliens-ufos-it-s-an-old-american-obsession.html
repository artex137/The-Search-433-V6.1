Trump is releasing US govt files on aliens, UFOs. It’s an old American obsession

Published 2026-02-20 09:00 UTC

Trump to Release US Government Files on Aliens and UFOs

United States President Donald Trump has announced plans to release government files related to aliens and extraterrestrial life, a move that comes amid ongoing interest in unidentified flying objects (UFOs) and their implications. The announcement was made on February 19, 2024, through Trump's Truth Social platform.

This decision matters as it reflects the Pentagon's recent efforts to investigate reports of UFOs through its All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office. Authorities have reiterated that there is currently no evidence of alien life, despite significant public interest.

Key facts

  • Announcement made by President Donald Trump on February 19, 2024.
  • Files will cover alien life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and UFOs.
  • The Pentagon has expanded its investigation efforts but found no evidence of aliens.
  • Trump criticized former President Barack Obama for discussing aliens in a recent podcast.
  • Obama stated, “Statistically, the universe is so vast that the odds are good there’s life out there,” but noted no evidence during his presidency.

Timeline

On February 19, 2024, President Trump announced his intention to release files concerning extraterrestrial life. This came shortly after former President Obama participated in a podcast where he confirmed the existence of aliens but stated he had not seen them. Trump responded by criticizing Obama for revealing classified information.

In the podcast, hosted by Brian Tyler Cohen, Obama responded to a question about aliens, which sparked media attention and led to his subsequent clarification on social media.

Official statements

“Based on the tremendous interest shown, I will be directing the Secretary of War, and other relevant Departments and Agencies, to begin the process of identifying and releasing Government files related to alien and extraterrestrial life, unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), and unidentified flying objects (UFOs),” Trump stated.
Obama clarified, “But the distances between solar systems are so great that the chances we’ve been visited by aliens is low, and I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrials have made contact with us. Really.”

Context

The fascination with UFOs and aliens in the United States has a long history, often tied to secretive military sites like Area 51 in Nevada. Polls have shown that a significant portion of the American public believes the government knows more about UFOs than it has disclosed. Popular culture has also played a role in sustaining this interest, with numerous films and books exploring themes of extraterrestrial life.

Over the years, conspiracy theories have emerged around military bases and alleged government cover-ups, fueling public curiosity and speculation regarding UFOs and alien technologies.

What’s next

The release of government files on aliens and UFOs is expected to begin soon, as directed by President Trump. The process will involve relevant departments and agencies assessing the materials for public dissemination.

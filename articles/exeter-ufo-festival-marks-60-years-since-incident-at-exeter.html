Exeter UFO festival marks 60 years since 'incident at Exeter'

The annual Exeter UFO Festival took place this weekend, celebrating 60 years since the "incident in Exeter," when a man reported seeing a UFO in Kensington, New Hampshire. Authorities investigating the report claimed to have witnessed the unidentified flying object as well.

The festival has become a significant event, drawing visitors from across the country, who share a common interest in the paranormal and UFO sightings. This year’s festival featured various activities, including guest speakers, food vendors, live music, and contests.

Key facts

  • Event: Annual Exeter UFO Festival
  • Date: This weekend
  • Location: Exeter, New Hampshire
  • Significance: 60th anniversary of the "incident in Exeter"
  • Activities: Guest speakers, food, live music, alien costume contest, alien pet contest
  • Attendance: Visitors from across the country

Timeline

In 1965, a man in Kensington reported seeing a UFO, prompting police to investigate. Officers claimed to have seen the same object. This incident has since become a notable part of local history.

The Exeter UFO Festival has been held annually, growing in popularity and attracting enthusiasts of the paranormal and UFO phenomena.

Official statements

“I’ve always loved the paranormal and being around like minded people that have an incredible curiosity and encounters with these things is incredible. This year was incredible,” said Eric Mintel of Pennsylvania.

Context

The "incident in Exeter" is one of the most cited UFO sightings in New Hampshire's history. The festival has become a platform for enthusiasts to share experiences and discuss UFO-related topics, reflecting a growing interest in the paranormal.

What’s next

Details about future festivals have not been disclosed, but the event is expected to continue celebrating the legacy of the "incident in Exeter" and the broader UFO community.

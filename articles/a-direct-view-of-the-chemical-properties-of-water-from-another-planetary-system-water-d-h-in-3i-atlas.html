A Direct View of the Chemical Properties of Water from Another Planetary System: Water D/H in 3I/ATLAS

Researchers have detected a significant deuterium enrichment in the water of comet 3I/ATLAS, revealing insights into the chemical properties of water from another planetary system. This finding was reported during observations conducted by the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) near perihelion, which occurred not disclosed.

The study indicates that the water D/H ratio in 3I/ATLAS exceeds that of Earth's oceans by more than a factor of 40 and typical Solar System cometary values by more than a factor of 30. This suggests that the water formed under colder, less irradiated conditions than those found in our Solar System. Researchers aim to understand the implications of these findings for the formation of planetary systems in different environments.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS has a water D/H value of [D/H]H2O > 6.6 × 10−3.
  • Deuterium enrichment exceeds Earth's ocean value by a factor of over 40.
  • Deuterium enrichment exceeds typical Solar System cometary values by a factor of over 30.
  • Observations were made by ALMA near perihelion, date not disclosed.
  • Study submitted to Nature Astronomy by a team led by Luis E. Salazar Manzano.

Timeline

The research team conducted ALMA observations of 3I/ATLAS around perihelion, with a focus on measuring the water D/H ratio. The results were compiled and analyzed, leading to the conclusion that the comet's water formed under distinct conditions compared to Solar System bodies. The study has been submitted for publication, with details awaiting peer review.

Official statements

“The elevated deuterium enrichment points to water that formed under colder, less irradiated conditions and from less thermally processed material,” said Luis E. Salazar Manzano.

Context

Previous studies have shown that water D/H ratios can vary significantly across different environments in the Galaxy. This research adds to the understanding of how chemical processes in various stellar nurseries influence the composition of celestial bodies. The findings may inform theories on the formation of planetary systems and the origins of water in the universe.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze the data and compare findings with other celestial bodies to enhance understanding of the origins of water in the universe. Further studies may explore the implications of these results for astrobiology and planetary formation theories.

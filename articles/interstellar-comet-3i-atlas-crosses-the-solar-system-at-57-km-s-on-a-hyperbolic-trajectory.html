Interstellar comet 3I/Atlas crosses the Solar System at 57 km/s on a hyperbolic trajectory

The interstellar comet 3I/Atlas has crossed the Solar System at a speed of 57 km/s, according to NASA. The comet, identified as the third confirmed interstellar object, is currently on a hyperbolic trajectory and will not be captured by the Sun's gravity.

This event is significant for astronomers studying the characteristics of interstellar objects. Observations conducted in 2025 have confirmed the comet's external origin, and NASA has mobilized 12 space missions to observe 3I/Atlas. The high speed of the comet surpasses previous interstellar visitors, providing a unique opportunity to learn about materials formed outside our solar system.

Key facts

  • Speed: 57 km/s
  • Previous interstellar comets: ‘Oumuamua at 26 km/s, Borisov at 33 km/s
  • Observation year: 2025
  • NASA missions: 12 active space missions
  • Comet type: Third confirmed interstellar comet

Timeline

In 2017, ‘Oumuamua was discovered as the first interstellar object to pass through the Solar System, followed by Borisov in 2019. The detection of 3I/Atlas occurred in 2025, marking it as the third such object confirmed by astronomers.

Observations confirmed that 3I/Atlas maintains enough kinetic energy to escape the Solar System, indicating it will not form an orbit around the Sun. The comet's closest approach to the Sun will last only a few seconds.

Official statements

“The confirmation of 3I/Atlas as the third interstellar comet expands knowledge about the population of wandering objects in the Milky Way,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Prior to 3I/Atlas, the study of interstellar comets has provided insights into the diversity of objects expelled from other star systems. The characteristics of 3I/Atlas, including its high speed and hyperbolic trajectory, highlight the unique nature of these celestial bodies compared to local comets.

What’s next

Observatories around the world will continue to track 3I/Atlas as it moves away from the inner Solar System. Researchers are analyzing spectroscopic data to determine the comet's chemical composition, which may reveal characteristics of its original star system.

US airman claims UFO encounter involved message from the future

A US airman associated with the Rendlesham Forest UFO incident has stated he received a binary-coded message after encountering a mysterious craft near nuclear air bases in 1980. The airman claims the decoded text warned "advance or perish" and suggested a human origin from the future. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This claim has sparked renewed interest in the Rendlesham Forest incident, a case that remains unverified but continues to intrigue UFO enthusiasts and researchers. Authorities have not disclosed any ongoing investigations regarding this specific claim.

Key facts

  • Incident date: 1980
  • Location: Rendlesham Forest, near US nuclear air bases
  • Claim made by: US airman (name not disclosed)
  • Message content: "advance or perish"
  • Message origin: Suggested human origin from the future

Timeline

In December 1980, a US airman reportedly encountered a mysterious craft in Rendlesham Forest, which is located near two nuclear air bases. Following the encounter, the airman claims to have received a binary-coded message that was later decoded. The message warned of an impending choice for humanity, urging the need to advance or face dire consequences.

Since then, the case has remained a subject of speculation and interest, drawing attention from both the public and researchers of unidentified flying objects.

Official statements

“The message warned ‘advance or perish’ and hinted at a human origin from the future,” the airman stated.

Context

The Rendlesham Forest incident is often referred to as "Britain's Roswell" and has been one of the most documented UFO sightings in history. Since the 1980s, various accounts and theories have surfaced, but none have provided conclusive evidence regarding the claims made by witnesses.

What’s next

Authorities have not announced any plans for further investigation into this specific claim, and the status of the case remains unchanged as interest continues to grow among UFO researchers and enthusiasts.

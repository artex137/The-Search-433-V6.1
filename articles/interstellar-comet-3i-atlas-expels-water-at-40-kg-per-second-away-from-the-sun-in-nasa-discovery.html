Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS expels water at 40 kg per second away from the Sun in NASA discovery

NASA has reported that the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is expelling water at a rate of 40 kilograms per second as it approaches the Sun. The discovery was made recently and highlights the unique properties of this comet, which is currently located beyond the orbit of Jupiter.

This finding is significant as it provides insights into the composition and behaviour of interstellar objects. NASA scientists are currently studying the implications of this water expulsion for understanding the origins of such comets and their potential impact on solar systems.

Key facts

  • Comet: 3I/ATLAS
  • Water expulsion rate: 40 kg per second
  • Location: Beyond the orbit of Jupiter
  • Discovery made by: NASA
  • Date of discovery: Not disclosed

Timeline

NASA scientists have been monitoring comet 3I/ATLAS since its identification. Recent observations have confirmed the high rate of water expulsion. The exact date of these observations has not been disclosed.

Official statements

“The discovery of water being expelled from 3I/ATLAS helps us understand more about the nature of interstellar comets,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with 3I/ATLAS being one of the few identified. Previous studies of other interstellar objects have provided limited insight into their composition and behaviour. The current findings may contribute to a broader understanding of these celestial bodies.

What’s next

Nasa plans to continue monitoring 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun to gather more data on its composition and behaviour.

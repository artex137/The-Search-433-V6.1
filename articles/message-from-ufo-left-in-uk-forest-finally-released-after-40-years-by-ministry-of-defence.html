Message from UFO left in UK forest finally released after 40 years by Ministry of Defence

A message linked to a famous UFO sighting in Rendlesham Forest, Suffolk, has been released by the Ministry of Defence (MoD) after remaining confidential for nearly 40 years. The sighting occurred on 26 and 28 December 1980, when United States Air Force (USAF) security personnel reported observing an unusual orange light descending into the forest.

This incident has garnered significant attention over the years due to the involvement of military personnel and the mystery surrounding the sightings. The release of the message is part of an ongoing examination of the event, which continues to intrigue both the public and researchers.

Key facts

  • Event date: 26 and 28 December 1980
  • Location: Rendlesham Forest, Suffolk, UK
  • Involved parties: USAF security personnel, including Lt Col Charles Halt and Sergeant James W Penniston
  • Allegations: Reports of a UFO sighting and a cryptic code linked to the event
  • Initial investigation: Conducted by MoD official Simon Weeden
  • Current status: The message has been made public nearly four decades after the incident

Timeline

On 26 December 1980, USAF security personnel reported seeing an unusual orange light in Rendlesham Forest. The following day, they continued to investigate the phenomenon. Lt Col Charles Halt documented the encounter in a memo to the MoD.

In the years following the incident, Sergeant James W Penniston claimed to have touched the unidentified craft and received a telepathic code. This code remained undisclosed until recently when Penniston made it public.

Official statements

“It's been a crazy 40 years [since the encounter]. Just when you think the story is over, another thing happens,” said USAF security policeman John Burroughs, who also witnessed the event.
Dr David Clarke, a journalist and scholar, stated, “There is still an element of mystery. What happened to those three guys on the first night I still find baffling. Maybe they did see something that was inexplicable.”
Simon Weeden, former MoD official, explained, “Once we had been through all the basic checks and found there was nothing seen on radar - no obvious explanation, no obvious threat to air defence - we decided no further action was needed.”

Context

The Rendlesham Forest incident is often referred to as “Britain's Roswell” due to its notoriety and the involvement of military personnel. Various investigations and freedom of information requests have sought to uncover more details about the event, but many aspects remain unexplained.

Historically, the MoD has stated that most reports of UFO sightings did not pose any defence significance. The Rendlesham case is notable for its military origins, leading to further scrutiny and public interest.

What’s next

The MoD has released the message linked to the incident, but further investigations into the specifics of the sightings remain ongoing. Authorities have not disclosed any plans for additional inquiries or actions related to the case.

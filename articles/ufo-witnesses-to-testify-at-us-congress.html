UFO witnesses to testify at US Congress

Today, four witnesses will testify before a congressional committee in Washington, D.C., about their experiences with Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAP), commonly known as UFOs. The hearing is set to begin at approximately 10:00 ET.

This hearing aims to promote transparency regarding UAP sightings and gather firsthand accounts from individuals who claim to have encountered these phenomena. The committee seeks to address public interest and concerns surrounding the government's handling of UAP reports.

Key facts

  • Event: Congressional hearing on UAPs
  • Date: Today, October 4, 2023
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Witnesses: Four individuals, including air force veterans
  • Start time: 10:00 ET

Timeline

The hearing will commence today at 10:00 ET, featuring testimonies from witnesses who claim to have observed UAPs. The committee has invited these individuals to share their experiences and insights regarding their encounters.

This event is part of ongoing efforts by U.S. lawmakers to investigate and understand the implications of UAP sightings, which have gained increased attention in recent years.

Official statements

“This hearing is a step towards greater transparency related to UAP sightings,” a committee spokesperson stated.

Context

The U.S. government has faced scrutiny over its handling of UAP sightings, with increasing calls for transparency. Recent investigations have led to a growing public interest in understanding these phenomena and their potential implications for national security.

What’s next

Following today’s hearing, the committee will review the testimonies and determine any further actions or investigations related to UAP sightings.

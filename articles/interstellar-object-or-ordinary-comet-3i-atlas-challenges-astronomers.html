Interstellar Object or Ordinary Comet? 3I/ATLAS Challenges Astronomers

Astronomers have observed the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, which passed closest to the Sun recently, exhibiting unexpected brightness changes. Discovered last July, the comet reached its perihelion on October 26, 2023, prompting significant interest among scientists.

The findings from 3I/ATLAS could reshape our understanding of interstellar objects and their origins. Researchers are currently analyzing data collected from various solar-monitoring satellites to understand the unusual behavior of the comet and what it might reveal about its composition and trajectory.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Discovered: July 2023
  • Perihelion reached: October 26, 2023
  • Brightness increase: From 10.5 to ~7.5, a 16x increase
  • Instruments used: Stereo A, SOHO, GOES-19
  • Notable observation: High concentration of carbon dioxide (CO₂)

Timeline

3I/ATLAS has been under observation since its discovery in July 2023. Between mid-September and late October, researchers noted a rapid increase in brightness, approximately twice as fast as expected. The comet reached its perihelion on October 26, prompting further analysis of its characteristics.

As of late October, scientists have reported unusual color changes in the comet, indicating the presence of gas, possibly cyanogen or ammonia. The Atacama Large Millimeter Array (ALMA) in Chile measured non-gravitational acceleration, which scientists attribute to cometary outgassing.

Official statements

“If no large gas cloud is observed around 3I/Atlas in December, then the reported non-gravitational acceleration could still be interpreted as the technological signature of a propulsion system,” said Avi Loeb, a physicist at Harvard.
“The extreme CO₂ levels in 3I/Atlas are the result of cosmic radiation absorbed during its journey,” researchers from the Royal Belgian Institute for Space Aeronomy stated.

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of the first interstellar objects to be closely monitored as it travels through our Solar System. Previous interstellar objects, like 'Oumuamua, have similarly generated excitement and speculation regarding their origins and characteristics. The ongoing studies of 3I/ATLAS may provide insights into the composition and behavior of such objects.

What’s next

Researchers will continue to analyze data from 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Earth in December 2023. Observations during this time may clarify the comet's characteristics and its potential implications for understanding interstellar objects.

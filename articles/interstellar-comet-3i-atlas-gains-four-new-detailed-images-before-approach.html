Interstellar comet 3I/Atlas gains four new detailed images before approach

The interstellar comet 3I/Atlas has been captured in four new detailed images from various space missions as it approaches Earth, with the closest point expected on December 19, 2025, at a distance of 269 million kilometers. The images were taken between November and early December 2025, showcasing the comet's bright nucleus and tails.

This event is significant as 3I/Atlas is only the second confirmed object from outside the Solar System to be observed. Space agencies are closely monitoring its trajectory and characteristics as it nears perihelion and Earth.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/Atlas is approaching Earth on December 19, 2025.
  • Closest distance to Earth: 269 million kilometers.
  • New images captured between November and early December 2025.
  • 3I/Atlas is the second interstellar object confirmed to originate from outside the Solar System.

Timeline

In late November 2025, various space missions began capturing images of comet 3I/Atlas. Observations continued into early December as the comet approached its perihelion. The closest approach to Earth is set for December 19, 2025.

Official statements

“The new images show the comet’s bright nucleus and tails in never-before-seen detail,” a spokesperson from the space agency said.

Context

Comet 3I/Atlas follows the trajectory of the first confirmed interstellar object, 1I/2017 U1, which was observed in 2017. This highlights the growing interest and capability of space missions to study objects from beyond our Solar System.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, space agencies will continue to monitor its path and collect data for further analysis after its closest approach.

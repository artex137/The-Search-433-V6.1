NASAの惑星防衛隊、彗星3I/ATLASからの異常な電波信号を受信し警戒態勢へ

The global scientific community has heightened its alert status following the detection of abnormal radio signals from comet 3I/ATLAS, an interstellar object currently traversing the solar system. The Agência Espacial Norte-Americana (NASA) has intensified surveillance through its Escritório of Coordenação of Defesa Planetária (PDCO) after emissions were captured on October 24, 2025, by the MeerKAT radio telescope in South Africa.

This situation is significant as the unusual characteristics of the signals do not match known natural phenomena. Authorities are mobilizing extensive resources to monitor the comet continuously, aiming to decipher the origin of these mysterious emissions. The comet, first identified on July 1, 2025, poses no collision threat to Earth, but its peculiar signals have made it a high-priority target for astronomers globally.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS was first identified on July 1, 2025.
  • Abnormal radio signals detected on October 24, 2025, at a frequency of 1.6 GHz.
  • NASA's PDCO has activated an intensified surveillance protocol.
  • Collaboration includes the European Space Agency (ESA) and various international partners.
  • The closest approach to Earth is scheduled for December 19, 2025, at a distance of approximately 270 million kilometers.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS was identified by the ATLAS system. On October 24, 2025, the MeerKAT radio telescope detected abnormal radio signals from the comet. Following this discovery, NASA's PDCO initiated an intensified observation campaign to analyze the emissions.

Official statements

“The emissions present characteristics that do not align with known natural phenomena,” said a spokesperson from NASA’s PDCO. “Our priority is to rule out all possible sources of terrestrial interference and analyze the emissions pattern.”

Context

The detection of unusual signals from celestial bodies is not unprecedented. Historically, mysterious signals have often been attributed to extraordinary causes, only to later be explained by natural processes. This case serves as a reminder of the complexities involved in interpreting astronomical phenomena.

What’s next

NASA and its international partners will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS while analyzing the chemical composition of its coma. Ongoing investigations aim to determine the origins of the radio emissions and ensure that all hypotheses are rigorously explored.

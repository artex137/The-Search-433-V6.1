‘The Age of Disclosure’: UFO Doc Turns Into Streaming Phenomenon, Outperforms Studio Blockbusters

Dan Farah’s documentary, ‘The Age of Disclosure,’ has broken records on Prime Video since its release on an unspecified date, becoming the highest-grossing documentary on the streaming service within 48 hours. The film has outperformed major studio blockbusters, including ‘One Battle After Another,’ ‘Jurassic World: Rebirth,’ and ‘Mission: Impossible – The Final Reckoning.’

This documentary is significant as it presents a compelling case for the existence of UFOs, featuring testimonies from 34 members of the U.S. government, military, and intelligence communities. The film suggests an ongoing cover-up regarding non-human intelligent life and an arms race among world powers to reverse-engineer extraterrestrial technology.

Key facts

  • Film title: ‘The Age of Disclosure’
  • Director: Dan Farah
  • Release platform: Prime Video
  • Record-breaking: Highest-grossing documentary within 48 hours
  • Duration of production: Three years
  • Number of interviewees: 34 members from U.S. government and military
  • Notable interviewee: Jay Stratton, former director of the Pentagon’s UAP Task Force

Timeline

‘The Age of Disclosure’ was developed over three years, during which Farah sought out insiders willing to share their experiences regarding UFOs. The film was released on Prime Video and quickly gained traction, breaking streaming records within its first 48 hours.

Within the first eight days, it maintained the #1 position on the platform, outperforming several blockbuster films. The film's teaser went viral, amassing over 20 million views across social media, indicating strong public interest.

Official statements

“I have seen, with my own eyes, non-human craft and non-human beings,” said Jay Stratton, former director of the Pentagon’s UAP Task Force.

Context

The documentary aligns with ongoing discussions about UFOs and extraterrestrial life, particularly in light of recent testimonies before Congress, including those from former U.S. Air Force officer David Grusch. Grusch has claimed that the government has been concealing information about alien craft and bodies.

‘The Age of Disclosure’ aims to reshape public perception and understanding of these topics, building on years of speculation and investigation regarding UFOs.

What’s next

As interest in UFOs continues to grow, further investigations into the claims made in ‘The Age of Disclosure’ may emerge. The documentary could prompt additional inquiries from both governmental and independent bodies regarding the existence of non-human intelligent life.

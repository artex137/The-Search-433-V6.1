Connecticut Lawmakers Want Taxpayers to Fund A UFO Study

Connecticut lawmakers have introduced legislation requiring a study of unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP), commonly known as UFOs, by the University of Connecticut (UConn). The bill, H.B. 5422, is currently before the legislature’s Appropriations Committee and aims to assess unexplained aerial sightings and the potential establishment of a government center dedicated to their study.

The legislation seeks to explore the scientific benefits of a taxpayer-funded UFO research program. It mandates UConn to analyze the feasibility of creating a state center for UAP research, including necessary staffing and resources. The university must consult with multiple state agencies and submit its findings to lawmakers by July 1, 2027.

Key facts

  • Legislation: H.B. 5422
  • Due date for report: July 1, 2027
  • Estimated cost for a limited study: approximately $150,000
  • Estimated cost for a broader study: approximately $300,000
  • Consulting agencies: Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection, Department of Energy and Environmental Protection, Office of Military Affairs
  • Current review status: Under review by the Appropriations Committee

Timeline

Connecticut lawmakers have introduced H.B. 5422, directing UConn to conduct a study on UAP. The university is expected to evaluate the benefits of a state research center and compile necessary resources. The report is due to the legislature by July 1, 2027.

UConn has indicated that conducting a meaningful study will require dedicated funding, as it cannot be absorbed within existing university operations. The university has stated that significant financial constraints could hinder the study if not properly funded.

Official statements

“A meaningful study would require dedicated funding rather than being absorbed within existing university operations,” said UConn Assistant Dean Kylene Perras in written testimony to the Appropriations Committee.
“Much of the department’s information is sensitive and restricted to law enforcement or authorized entities due to public safety and national security considerations,” said Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection Commissioner Ronnell Higgins regarding data-sharing concerns.

Context

The proposal comes at a time when Connecticut lawmakers are grappling with rising electricity rates, housing shortages, and long-term pension obligations. Critics question whether funding a UFO study is the best use of taxpayer dollars amid pressing state issues.

The bill does not explicitly allocate funding, but UConn’s cost estimates suggest that a study could require substantial public expenditure. This raises questions about legislative priorities.

What’s next

H.B. 5422 remains under review by the Appropriations Committee. The decision on whether to advance the bill will reflect legislative priorities regarding state funding and the study of unidentified aerial phenomena.

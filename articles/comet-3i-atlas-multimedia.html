Comet 3I/ATLAS Multimedia

NASA missions have tracked and studied the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS as it passes through our solar system, with significant observations recorded since its discovery on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS survey telescope in Chile. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

This comet is notable because it originates from interstellar space, a rare occurrence that has drawn attention from scientists and astronomers. NASA is using multiple telescopes to gather data and images, contributing to our understanding of such celestial bodies.

Key facts

  • Discovered: July 1, 2025
  • First observation by: NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Chile
  • Distance from Earth during Hubble's observation: 277 million miles
  • Hubble observation date: July 21, 2025
  • James Webb Space Telescope observation date: August 6, 2025
  • SPHEREx observation period: August 7 to August 15, 2025

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the ATLAS survey telescope in Chile discovered comet 3I/ATLAS. Following its discovery, NASA's Hubble Space Telescope captured an image of the comet on July 21, 2025, when it was positioned 277 million miles from Earth.

NASA's James Webb Space Telescope observed the comet on August 6, 2025, utilizing its Near-Infrared Spectrograph instrument. Subsequently, from August 7 to August 15, 2025, the SPHEREx mission conducted further observations of the comet.

Official statements

“NASA missions are working together to track and study this rare, interstellar comet as it passes through our solar system,” a NASA spokesperson said.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is one of the few comets identified as originating from interstellar space, providing scientists with a unique opportunity to study the composition and behavior of such bodies. This aligns with ongoing research into the formation and evolution of the solar system.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor comet 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the solar system, collecting data and images to enhance understanding of interstellar comets and their impact on our solar system.

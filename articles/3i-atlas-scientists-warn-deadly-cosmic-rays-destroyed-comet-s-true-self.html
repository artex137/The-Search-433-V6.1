3I/ATLAS: Scientists Warn 'Deadly Cosmic Rays' 'Destroyed' Comet's 'True Self'

Scientists have reported that the interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS has undergone significant alteration due to billions of years of high-energy cosmic ray bombardment, leaving only a heavily transformed outer shell. The comet, originally detected on 1 July 2025 by the Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System (ATLAS) survey, is only the third confirmed interstellar object to pass through the Solar System.

This research suggests a shift in understanding how interstellar objects are perceived. Authorities are now re-evaluating the implications of this alteration on the study of cometary materials and their origins.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS detected on 1 July 2025.
  • Outer layers transformed by galactic cosmic rays (GCRs).
  • CO2/H2O ratio of 7.6 ± 0.3 observed.
  • Outgassing samples only 15–20 metres deep.
  • Study highlights a paradigm shift in understanding interstellar objects.

Timeline

1 July 2025: Comet 3I/ATLAS is detected by the ATLAS survey.

Subsequent analysis reveals extreme changes to the comet’s surface due to cosmic ray exposure.

Recent studies indicate that the comet's outer layers consist of irradiated materials rather than original compositions.

Official statements

“Long-residence interstellar objects primarily reveal GCR-processed material rather than pristine material representative of their primordial formation environments,” the authors of the study stated.

Context

This finding challenges the previous notion that interstellar comets like 3I/ATLAS could present pristine materials from their birth environments. The alteration of such objects due to cosmic ray bombardment raises questions about the integrity of materials used to study alien star systems.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze 3I/ATLAS and similar interstellar objects to better understand the effects of cosmic radiation on their surfaces and the implications for future research.

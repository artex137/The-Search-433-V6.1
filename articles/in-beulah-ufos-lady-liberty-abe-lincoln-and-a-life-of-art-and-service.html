In Beulah: UFOs, Lady Liberty, Abe Lincoln and a life of art and service

Artist Dewey Blocksma has opened a new exhibition titled “Roundtable” at the Dennos Museum in Traverse City, Michigan, which will be on view until January 4, 2025. The exhibition showcases Blocksma's work and his unique roadside attraction, which features a variety of sculptures, some of which are his creations and others collected from different artists. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the exhibition or roadside attraction.

This exhibition highlights Blocksma's artistic journey and his use of found materials to create art that reflects his life experiences. The local community and authorities are recognizing the significance of his contributions to the art scene and the cultural fabric of Beulah.

Key facts

  • Exhibition: “Roundtable” at the Dennos Museum
  • Location: Traverse City, Michigan
  • Exhibition Dates: Until January 4, 2025
  • Notable Sculptures: Includes a T. rex on a surfboard and two 10-foot tall sculptures of Abraham Lincoln
  • Artist Background: Dewey Blocksma, formerly a surgeon turned folk artist

Timeline

Dewey Blocksma's artistic journey began after a pivotal moment in an art class when a friend rescued a discarded piece he had created. This led to his recognition as an artist and marked a shift from a career in medicine to one in art. Blocksma has since developed a roadside attraction featuring his sculptures, which has drawn attention from the local community.

His current exhibition, “Roundtable,” opened at the Dennos Museum, showcasing his work and philosophy. Blocksma has incorporated themes of healing and guidance from historical figures like Abraham Lincoln into his art.

Official statements

“I feel like we all need to listen to Lincoln no matter what perspective we have,” said Blocksma. “So I keep trying to listen to him by making sculptures about him.”
“In medicine, when you worry about something, you go get a second opinion. Well, I'm depending on Lincoln to give us a second opinion,” Blocksma added regarding a sculpture of Lincoln inside a drone.

Context

Blocksma's transition from medicine to art is notable, as he reflects on his father's influence as a medical missionary and his own experiences in emergency medicine. His artistic creations often blend personal history with broader themes of healing and community. The roadside attraction has become a local landmark, fostering interest in folk art and engaging visitors in unique ways.

What’s next

As the exhibition continues at the Dennos Museum, Dewey Blocksma plans to engage with the community through additional events and discussions about his work and artistic philosophy. The impact of his art on local culture is expected to grow as more visitors come to experience his creations.

Hegseth Says Pentagon Will Release UFO Files

Secretary of War Pete Hegseth has stated that the Pentagon is working to comply with President Donald Trump's directive to identify and release government files related to UFOs and unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs). Hegseth made the announcement during an "Arsenal of Freedom" tour stop in Denver on February 24, 2026. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this disclosure process.

This matter is significant as it marks the administration's next step in a government-wide disclosure process initiated by Trump. The President announced last week that various agencies would begin identifying and releasing files concerning UFOs and extraterrestrial life. Hegseth emphasized that his department is "digging in" to ensure "full compliance" with the order.

Key facts

  • Date of announcement: February 24, 2026
  • Location: Denver, Colorado
  • Key figure: Secretary of War Pete Hegseth
  • President: Donald Trump
  • Focus: UFOs, UAPs, and extraterrestrial life
  • Current status: No timeline for release disclosed

Timeline

On February 24, 2026, during a tour stop in Denver, Secretary Hegseth announced the Pentagon's efforts to comply with President Trump's directive regarding UFO disclosures. This announcement followed Trump's order the previous week, which instructed various agencies to begin identifying relevant files.

Hegseth also visited a space-defense manufacturer in Colorado during the tour, indicating a broader interest in aerospace and defense technologies.

Official statements

“We are digging in and will be in full compliance with the order,” Hegseth said during the tour.
When asked about extraterrestrial life, Hegseth stated, “I will review the material and find out alongside the public.”

Context

This announcement comes amid increasing interest in UFOs and UAPs from government officials and the public. Previous administrations have faced pressure to disclose information regarding unidentified aerial phenomena, reflecting a growing demand for transparency in government operations related to these subjects.

What’s next

Investigations into the files related to UFOs and UAPs are expected to continue, although no specific deadlines for the release of this information have been disclosed. Hegseth's department will work to comply with the President's directive as the process unfolds.

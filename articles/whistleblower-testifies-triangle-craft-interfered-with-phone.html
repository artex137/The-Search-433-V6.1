Whistleblower testifies triangle craft interfered with phone

During a recent UAP hearing, whistleblower Dylan Borland testified that a triangle-shaped craft interfered with his phone signals. The testimony took place on September 9, 2025, in Washington, D.C., as part of ongoing discussions regarding unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP).

This testimony is significant as it adds to a growing body of evidence about UAP encounters. Authorities are examining these claims to better understand potential national security implications and the nature of these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Event Date: September 9, 2025
  • Location: Washington, D.C.
  • Witness: Dylan Borland, a former Air Force veteran
  • Type of craft: Triangle-shaped
  • Claim: Craft interfered with phone signals
  • Agency involved: U.S. Congress, specifically during UAP hearings

Timeline

On September 9, 2025, Dylan Borland provided his testimony regarding his experience with a triangle craft during a UAP hearing. He described how the craft disrupted his phone communications, raising concerns about the implications of such incidents.

Following Borland’s testimony, Congress has indicated plans to further investigate the claims made by him and other witnesses regarding UAP encounters.

Official statements

“Truth needs to be known,” said Dylan Borland during his testimony, emphasizing the importance of transparency regarding UAP encounters.

Context

This testimony aligns with a series of recent disclosures and hearings focused on UAPs. Previous incidents have prompted Congress to take a closer look at the implications of these phenomena for national security and aviation safety.

What’s next

Congress plans to continue investigations into the claims made during the hearing, seeking to gather more information about UAPs and their potential impact on security protocols.

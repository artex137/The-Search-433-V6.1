How to see comet C/2025 A6 (LEMMON) this autumn

Published 2025-09-26 06:29 UTC

Comet C/2025 A6 (LEMMON) Visible This Autumn

Comet C/2025 A6 (LEMMON) is currently visible in the morning sky for northern observers, drifting through the constellation of Lynx, between Jupiter and the Plough/Big Dipper. Astronomers have monitored the comet's activity since it was discovered on January 3, 2025, by David C. Fuls as part of the Mount Lemmon Survey.

This comet is significant as it may become visible to the naked eye by the end of October 2025. Authorities and astronomers are actively tracking its brightness and trajectory, providing updates to the public about its visibility.

Key facts

  • Discovery date: January 3, 2025
  • Discoverer: David C. Fuls
  • Current brightness: Approximately magnitude 8 to 9
  • Expected visibility: Possible naked-eye visibility by late October 2025
  • Location: Constellation of Lynx
  • Observational equipment: Binoculars, small telescopes

Timeline

Comet C/2025 A6 (LEMMON) was first detected on January 3, 2025, when it appeared as a faint dot of light. It was designated as a comet after follow-up observations confirmed its activity. The comet passed solar conjunction on July 2, 2025, becoming temporarily obscured by the Sun's glare. It re-emerged in mid-August 2025, appearing brighter than expected, which increased interest among astronomers.

As of late September 2025, the comet has been observed to be brightening, now visible in binoculars and small telescopes, though still not to the naked eye. Predictions suggest it may reach a brightness of magnitude +3 by the end of October 2025, depending on its ongoing activity.

Official statements

“Comets love surprising the people who observe them, and proving them wrong if possible,” said an astronomer involved in monitoring C/2025 A6 (LEMMON).
“It’s important to not get carried away here. Comets are fickle creatures,” the astronomer added, emphasizing caution regarding predictions about the comet's brightness.

Context

Comet C/2025 A6 (LEMMON) is one of several comets currently being observed. Its activity has drawn attention due to the possibility of it becoming a visible object in the night sky. Previous comets have had unpredictable brightness and visibility patterns, making ongoing observation critical.

What’s next

Observers will continue to monitor C/2025 A6 (LEMMON) over the coming weeks for updates on its brightness and visibility. Further information will be provided as the comet approaches its closest point to the Sun.

BITM event to mark passage of comet

Kolkata: The Birla Industrial & Technological Museum (BITM) will host a special programme on Friday, October 29, 2025, to mark the passage of Comet 3I/ATLAS, the third known interstellar object to traverse the solar system. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the event.

This event is significant as it provides a unique opportunity for scientists to study the characteristics of an interstellar comet. According to a BITM official, Comet 3I/ATLAS offers an extraordinary glimpse into the building blocks of other worlds. The museum aims to engage the public through various educational activities surrounding the comet's arrival.

Key facts

  • Event date: October 29, 2025
  • Location: Birla Industrial & Technological Museum, Kolkata
  • Closest approach to the Sun: October 29, 2025
  • Distance from Earth at closest point: 1.8 astronomical units (approximately 30 crore kilometres)
  • Event start time: 11 am

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first discovered on July 1, 2025, by the NASA-funded Asteroid Terrestrial-impact Last Alert System survey telescope in Rio Hurtado, Chile. The comet will reach its closest point to the Sun on October 29, 2025, allowing for a series of public educational events at BITM.

On the day of the event, activities will include an open-house quiz for students at 11 am, an interactive show titled ‘Cryptic Comet’ at noon, a film show at 1 pm, and a telescope viewing session at 5 pm.

Official statements

“Unlike typical comets, this one originated far outside our solar system, offering scientists an extraordinary glimpse into the building blocks of other worlds. Its arrival presents a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to study the mysteries of interstellar space,” said a BITM official.

Context

Comet 3I/ATLAS is notable for being the third interstellar object identified, following previous discoveries that have sparked significant interest in the scientific community. The study of such comets aids researchers in understanding the formation and evolution of celestial bodies in the universe.

What’s next

Following the BITM event, scientists will continue to monitor Comet 3I/ATLAS as it approaches the Sun, gathering data that may contribute to future research on interstellar objects.

Avi Loeb Slams CIA Over 3I/ATLAS Secrets: Harvard Prof Claims Records Are Being Hidden

Harvard professor Avi Loeb has criticized the CIA for its silence regarding the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, following NASA's classification of the object as a natural comet. The controversy emerged after the CIA did not acknowledge the existence of records related to 3I/ATLAS when approached for information, raising questions about the agency's level of secrecy.

This matter is significant as it highlights a discrepancy between scientific consensus and intelligence community responses. Loeb argues that the CIA's lack of transparency is puzzling if 3I/ATLAS is indeed a harmless comet, suggesting that the agency may be concealing information in anticipation of an unforeseen event.

Key facts

  • NASA classified 3I/ATLAS as a natural comet on 19 November 2025.
  • The CIA issued a Glomar response to a FOIA request on 31 December 2025, neither confirming nor denying the existence of records.
  • Loeb has identified unusual characteristics of 3I/ATLAS, including a unique anti-tail jet and an unusual chemical composition.
  • The object’s trajectory aligns closely with the 1977 'Wow! signal', with a probability of 0.6 per cent.

Timeline

On 19 November 2025, NASA publicly stated that 3I/ATLAS is 'definitely a comet of natural origin' during a press conference. The situation escalated when researcher John Greenewald Jr submitted a Freedom of Information Act request to the CIA on 31 December 2025. The CIA responded with a Glomar response, which does not confirm or deny the existence of requested records.

Loeb has since raised concerns about the object’s behavior, which he believes contradicts the standard understanding of cometary physics. He has called for ongoing monitoring of 3I/ATLAS as it approaches Jupiter to determine if it exhibits any technological behavior.

Official statements

“The CIA's silence is baffling if the object is truly mundane,” said Loeb, emphasizing the need for transparency from the agency.
Loeb also noted, “We must continue to monitor the object as it moves near Jupiter to avoid missing a discovery that could change human history.”

Context

This incident underscores ongoing tensions between scientific inquiry and government secrecy. Previous instances of classified information have raised public concern over the transparency of intelligence agencies. Loeb's comments reflect a broader debate about the implications of unidentified objects in space and their potential significance.

What’s next

Loeb advocates for further investigation into 3I/ATLAS and the CIA's records. The scientific community is urged to continue monitoring the object for any signs of technological behavior or anomalies as it approaches Jupiter.

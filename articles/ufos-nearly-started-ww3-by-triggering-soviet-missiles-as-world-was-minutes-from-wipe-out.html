UFOs 'nearly started WW3' by triggering Soviet missiles as world was 'minutes from wipe out'

Newly uncovered documents reveal that unidentified flying objects (UFOs) almost triggered a nuclear catastrophe by activating Soviet Union intercontinental ballistic missiles. The incident occurred in 1982 near Usovo, Ukraine, during heightened Cold War tensions, according to a Red Army propaganda chief.

The incident underscores the potential dangers posed by unidentified aerial phenomena. Authorities are now examining the implications of these findings, which suggest that UFOs can interfere with military operations. The documents were smuggled out of the US by journalist George Knapp in 1993 and have only recently been made public.

Key facts

  • Incident occurred in 1982 near Usovo, Ukraine.
  • Missiles were aimed at New York City.
  • UFOs triggered missile launch mechanisms without authorization.
  • Witnesses reported UFOs performing aerial maneuvers and changing shapes.
  • Documents were released by George Knapp in January 2023.
  • USSR conducted multiple UFO studies from 1979 to 1990.

Timeline

The events unfolded as follows:

  • In 1982, during the Cold War, Soviet soldiers near Usovo reported sightings of UFOs performing unusual aerial feats.
  • These sightings coincided with the activation of nuclear missile launch mechanisms aimed at the United States.
  • Moments before the missiles launched, they deactivated as the UFOs departed the area.
  • In 1993, George Knapp smuggled documents out of Russia detailing the incident.
  • In January 2023, Knapp publicly released the documents, revealing the Soviet Union's investigations into UFO phenomena.

Official statements

“The missiles would have sparked a nuclear Armageddon had they gone off,” said a Red Army propaganda chief regarding the incident.
“The reports reveal the extent to which the USSR probed the phenomenon,” Knapp stated after releasing the documents.

Context

This incident is part of a broader pattern of UFO sightings and military encounters. It echoes a similar incident at Malmstrom Air Force Base in Montana in 1967, where UFO sightings corresponded with the failure of nuclear missile systems. The Soviet Union's initial dismissal of UFOs as a fabrication of "American imperialists" in 1953 contrasts with later investigations that commenced in 1979.

What’s next

Investigations into the implications of these findings are ongoing. Authorities may consider reviewing military protocols regarding unidentified aerial phenomena in light of the revelations from the released documents.

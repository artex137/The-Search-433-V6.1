Prospects For The Crossing Of Comet 3I/ATLAS’s Ion Tail

During October and November 2025, interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS will pass upstream of the Europa Clipper and Hera spacecraft. This event presents a unique opportunity for in-situ observations of the comet's ion tail, according to a report published in RNAAS.

The alignment of 3I/ATLAS’s hyperbolic trajectory with the ecliptic plane allows both spacecraft to potentially immerse within the ion tail. This immersion may enable scientists to detect the signatures of the ion tail and observe changes in the solar wind associated with the comet.

Key facts

  • Dates: 30 October – 6 November 2025 (Europa Clipper); 25 October – 1 November 2025 (Hera)
  • Spacecraft: Europa Clipper, Hera
  • Event: Potential immersion within 3I/ATLAS’s ion tail
  • Publication: RNAAS
  • Predicted observations: Ion tail signatures, changes to solar wind

Timeline

From 25 October to 1 November 2025, Hera is expected to possibly be immersed in the ion tail of 3I/ATLAS. Following this, from 30 October to 6 November 2025, Europa Clipper may also enter the ion tail, providing further opportunities for observation.

Official statements

“This alignment allows for unprecedented observation of an interstellar comet’s ion tail,” said Samuel Grant, the lead researcher on the report.

Context

The observation of comet 3I/ATLAS is significant due to its interstellar origin. Previous studies of comets have provided valuable insights into the early solar system, and the ion tail of 3I/ATLAS may yield information on the solar wind and magnetic fields in a manner not previously possible.

What’s next

As the dates approach, scientists will prepare for data collection and analysis during the immersion periods, aiming to enhance understanding of interstellar comets and their interactions with solar wind.

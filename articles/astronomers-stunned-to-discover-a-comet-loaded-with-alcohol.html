Astronomers Stunned To Discover A Comet Loaded With Alcohol

Astronomers have discovered that Comet 3I/ATLAS is emitting large amounts of organic methanol, according to a team using the Atacama Large Millimeter Array (ALMA). The findings were made during observations conducted from August to October 2025 as the comet passed through our solar system.

This discovery is significant as it sheds light on the chemical composition of interstellar objects. The team aims to understand better the origins and materials of comets from beyond our solar system. Lead author Nathan Roth, a professor at American University, emphasized the importance of this research, stating that it provides a unique insight into the comet's makeup.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is emitting high levels of methanol.
  • Observations were made using ALMA from August to October 2025.
  • The comet's methanol-to-hydrogen cyanide ratios were measured at 70:1 and 120:1.
  • These levels are second only to comet C/2016 R2 in methanol richness.
  • The James Webb Space Telescope previously observed the comet's coma, revealing a composition primarily of carbon dioxide.
  • This is the first documentation of methanol emissions from ice particles in an interstellar object.

Timeline

In late 2025, astronomers utilized ALMA to study Comet 3I/ATLAS as it traveled through the solar system. They focused on the comet's emissions, particularly methanol and hydrogen cyanide. Earlier, the James Webb Space Telescope provided initial data about the comet's composition when it was farther from the Sun.

The team measured the methanol-to-hydrogen cyanide ratios on two occasions, finding significant levels of methanol, which prompted further analysis of the comet's chemical properties.

Official statements

"Observing 3I/ATLAS is like taking a fingerprint from another solar system," said Nathan Roth, lead author of the research.
"The details reveal what it’s made of, and it’s bursting with methanol in a way we just don’t usually see in comets in our own solar system," Roth added.

Context

This discovery follows ongoing debates within the scientific community about the origins of Comet 3I/ATLAS. Previously, astronomers were uncertain whether it was a natural comet from another star system or had different origins. The new findings further enrich the understanding of the chemical diversity found in comets.

What’s next

As Comet 3I/ATLAS continues its journey out of the solar system, scientists will analyze the data collected to gain deeper insights into the characteristics of interstellar objects and their implications for understanding the formation of solar systems.

Interstellar comet set to shoot past Mars

Interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS is scheduled to make a close approach to Mars on Friday, October 6, 2025, passing within 29 million kilometres of the Red Planet. The comet is travelling at a speed of 310,000 kilometres per hour. There have been no reported injuries or fatalities related to the comet's approach.

This event is significant as it marks only the third known interstellar object to traverse the solar system. Both the European Space Agency and NASA have mobilized their spacecraft and rovers to observe the comet, which provides a unique opportunity for scientific study.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS will pass within 29 million kilometres of Mars.
  • The comet is travelling at 310,000 kilometres per hour.
  • The nucleus of the comet measures approximately 5.6 kilometres across.
  • The closest point to the Sun will occur by the end of October 2025.
  • The closest approach to Earth is expected in December 2025.

Timeline

On October 6, 2025, comet 3I/ATLAS will reach its closest point to Mars. The European Space Agency and NASA have begun preparing their instruments to observe the comet. Observations from the Hubble Space Telescope have indicated the comet's nucleus size and characteristics.

Official statements

“The approach of 3I/ATLAS is a rare opportunity for scientists to study an interstellar object,” said a spokesperson for NASA.

Context

Previous interstellar objects that have passed through the solar system include 'Oumuamua in 2017 and 2I/Borisov in 2019. These events have sparked interest in the study of such celestial bodies and their implications for understanding the universe.

What’s next

Following its approach to Mars, scientists will continue to analyze data collected by various spacecraft. The next significant event is the comet's closest approach to Earth in December 2025.

3I/ATLAS Comet Live Tracking: Earth Approach Real-Time Update and Position Of Interstellar Object

A mysterious space object, identified as 3I/ATLAS, is approaching Earth and will be closest on October 29 or October 30, 2025. Discovered in July 2025, the comet has drawn attention from NASA and the International Asteroid Warning Network (IAWN), but poses no danger to Earth.

The interest in 3I/ATLAS lies in its status as the third known interstellar object. Authorities are closely monitoring its trajectory and have provided live tracking updates to the public. The comet will pass at a distance of 1.8 astronomical units, or approximately 167 million miles, from Earth, making it observable through telescopes.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS discovered in July 2025.
  • Closest approach to Earth expected on October 29 or October 30, 2025.
  • Distance at closest approach: 1.8 astronomical units (167 million miles).
  • Live tracking available online through The Sky and NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope.
  • Comet expected to leave the solar system between November 27, 2025, and January 27, 2026.

Timeline

The NASA-funded ATLAS survey telescope in Chile first reported observations of comet 3I/ATLAS to the Minor Planet Center on July 1, 2025. Following this, astronomers gathered additional observations from three different ATLAS telescopes worldwide. The comet's unusual features have sparked discussions about its origins and physical properties.

As 3I/ATLAS travels deeper into the solar system, scientists will conduct detailed studies to enhance understanding of interstellar objects.

Official statements

“Knowing the pathway of a comet or a space object allows astronomers to predict how close it will come to Earth and what more it entails,” a spokesperson for the IAWN said.

Context

3I/ATLAS is notable for being the third known interstellar object, following Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov. Its unique characteristics provide valuable insights into the diversity of celestial bodies from other star systems.

Observations of 3I/ATLAS will contribute to existing knowledge about interstellar objects and may reveal new scientific phenomena.

What’s next

As the comet approaches, astronomers will continue to monitor its trajectory and gather data. Detailed studies will be conducted to further understand its composition and behavior before it exits the solar system.

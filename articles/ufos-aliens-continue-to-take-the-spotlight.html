UFOs, aliens continue to take the spotlight

Public interest in UFOs and extraterrestrial life has surged following remarks from former US President Barack Obama and current President Donald Trump. The renewed focus comes after Obama stated that the odds of extraterrestrial life are statistically significant, and Trump announced plans to release government files related to UFOs and aliens. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this resurgence of interest.

This renewed attention matters as it reflects ongoing public curiosity and speculation about potential extraterrestrial life. Authorities, however, maintain that there is no verified evidence to support claims of alien existence. The Pentagon has stated that investigations into UFO sightings are ongoing, even as some individuals accuse government agencies of withholding information.

Key facts

  • Former US President Barack Obama remarked on the reality of aliens on February 16, 2025.
  • President Donald Trump announced plans to release UFO-related files on February 17, 2025.
  • The Pentagon reported 485 UAP incidents from May 1, 2023, to June 1, 2024.
  • 56% of Americans believe in the existence of aliens, according to a YouGov survey conducted in November 2025.
  • California has the highest number of reported UFO sightings in the US, with over 17,000 cases.

Timeline

On June 24, 1947, pilot Kenneth Arnold reported seeing strange objects in the sky, marking a significant moment in UFO history. This was followed by the Roswell incident on July 8, 1947, where debris was initially described as a “flying disk” before being retracted as a weather balloon.

The 1960s brought a new dimension to UFO debates with high-profile alien abduction cases, such as that of Betty and Barney Hill in September 1961. In November 2004, US Navy pilots reported a strange craft off San Diego, and similar reports emerged from the US East Coast between June 2014 and March 2015.

On July 26, 2023, three former US intelligence officials testified before Congress regarding UAP sightings. David Grusch claimed that “intact and partially intact” alien vehicles had been recovered, while Ryan Graves and David Fravor shared their experiences with UAPs.

Official statements

“I saw no evidence during my presidency that extraterrestrial beings had contacted us. None whatsoever,” Obama stated.
Trump claimed that Obama had revealed “classified” information, stating that he would instruct relevant agencies to begin releasing files on UFOs and extraterrestrial life.

Context

The mid-20th century marked the beginning of mass interest in UFOs, driven by notable sightings and incidents. The term “Unidentified Aerial Phenomena” (UAP) has since become more common, reflecting a shift in terminology as investigations continue.

The National UFO Reporting Center has processed over 180,000 UFO cases since its inception, with the US leading in reported sightings. Public belief in aliens has grown, with recent surveys indicating that a majority of Americans believe in their existence.

What’s next

Investigations into UAP sightings are ongoing, with the Pentagon reviewing over 1,600 cases. The public can expect further disclosures as Trump’s administration moves forward with the release of UFO-related files.

Trailer for Spielberg’s UFO Movie Releases This Month

A trailer for Steven Spielberg’s untitled UFO movie will debut on December 19 during screenings of “Avatar: Fire and Ash.” The film is set to release on June 12, 2026, through Universal Pictures.

This film matters as it marks Spielberg’s return to the sci-fi genre, with a focus on themes reminiscent of his earlier works. The production has generated significant interest, especially with the involvement of renowned composer John Williams and screenwriter David Koepp, who previously collaborated with Spielberg on “Jurassic Park.”

Key facts

  • Trailer attached to “Avatar: Fire and Ash” screenings starting December 19.
  • Film release date: June 12, 2026.
  • Production wrapped in June 2023.
  • Cast includes Emily Blunt, Josh O’Connor, Colin Firth, Eve Hewson, Colman Domingo, and Wyatt Russell.
  • Director of photography: Janusz Kamiński.

Timeline

Production for the untitled UFO film wrapped in June 2023. A special event at Universal Pictures recently unveiled footage of the film. The trailer will be shown with “Avatar: Fire and Ash” beginning December 19, 2025.

Official statements

“The preview showed action sequences — including chase scenes, mysterious ‘men in black’-style cars, and a dramatic car-train crash escape involving Emily Blunt’s character,” according to reports from DanielRPK.
Josh O’Connor described the film as reminiscent of “old-school” Spielberg, referencing classics like “E.T.” and “Close Encounters of the Third Kind.”

Context

This upcoming UFO film marks Spielberg's 30th collaboration with John Williams. The director's last two films, “The Fabelmans” and “West Side Story,” received critical acclaim, earning multiple awards and nominations.

What’s next

Filmgoers can expect the trailer to enhance interest in “Avatar: Fire and Ash.” Further updates regarding the official title and additional marketing materials for Spielberg's UFO film are anticipated in the coming months.

Oddsconsin: UFOs and Alien Abductions

Wisconsin has reported 2,544 unidentified flying object (UFO) sightings since 1947, according to the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC). This figure places Wisconsin at the forefront of UFO sightings per capita in the Upper Midwest, with a rate of 4.32 sightings for every 10,000 residents based on the 2020 population of 5.9 million. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to these sightings.

This data highlights Wisconsin's unique position as a hotspot for UFO activity, prompting interest from both the public and researchers. Authorities have not disclosed any formal investigations regarding the validity of these claims, but sightings continue to be reported across the state.

Key facts

  • 2,544 total UFO sightings reported since 1947.
  • 4.32 sightings per 10,000 residents in Wisconsin.
  • Largest concentrations of sightings in Milwaukee, Madison, and Green Bay.
  • Common shapes include spheres, ovals, disks, and triangles.
  • Some reports involve alleged alien encounters.

Timeline

Since the first recorded sighting in 1947, Wisconsin has continuously reported UFO sightings. The NUFORC database has compiled these reports, although it does not validate the claims. Notable incidents include:

  • 1990: A report from Madison describes an alien encounter involving a bright orange light.
  • 2009: A Milwaukee resident reports seeing "three translucent inter-dimensional beings."
  • 2019: A Kenosha resident claims to have seen a reptoid alien.
  • 2023: A father and son encounter a copper-colored rectangle while hunting, which the father initially mistakes for a turkey vulture.

Official statements

“The guy I was with was about to shoot at him [the alien] but his gun didn’t work,” a witness from the 1990 Madison report recalled.
“I seen an alien that looked like a reptoid, and it looked back at me,” stated a Kenosha witness from 2019.

Context

Wisconsin's UFO sightings are part of a broader trend across the United States, with the highest numbers per capita typically occurring in western and southwestern states. Reports vary widely in description and detail, contributing to ongoing public fascination.

What’s next

While no formal investigations have been disclosed, the continued reporting of UFO sightings suggests that the interest in these phenomena may lead to further examination by local authorities and researchers in the future.

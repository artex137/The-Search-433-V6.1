UFO, Aliens' Vehicle? NASA's Missing Object Spotted at Texas House

Ann Walter discovered a large object drifting across the sky near her home in Edmonson, Texas, on a recent day. The object, which landed in a nearby wheat field, turned out to be a boxy piece of scientific equipment, approximately the size of a sport-utility vehicle, attached to a large parachute and marked with NASA stickers. No injuries or fatalities have been reported.

The sighting has drawn attention as NASA confirmed it was searching for a piece of equipment that had been lost during a recent launch. Local authorities are cooperating with NASA to recover the equipment, which is used for high-altitude scientific research.

Key facts

  • Location: Edmonson, Texas, USA
  • Object Size: Approximately the size of a sport-utility vehicle
  • Parachute Size: Estimated at 30 feet (9 meters)
  • Launch Site: Fort Sumner, New Mexico, about 140 miles (225 kilometers) from the landing site
  • Launch Date: Equipment launched a day prior to landing
  • Agency: NASA's Columbia Scientific Balloon Facility

Timeline

On a recent day, Ann Walter observed an unusual object in the sky while at her rural home. The object landed in a wheat field belonging to her neighbor. After contacting the local sheriff's office, she learned that NASA was looking for the lost equipment.

NASA had launched the equipment from Fort Sumner, New Mexico, as part of a research mission. Walter later spoke with representatives from NASA’s Columbia Scientific Balloon Facility, who confirmed the object's purpose: to collect data on stars, galaxies, and black holes.

The equipment was successfully retrieved by a team from NASA who arrived with a truck and trailer to collect it.

Official statements

“It's crazy, because when you're standing on the ground and see something in the air, you don't realize how big it is,” said Ann Walter. “It was probably a 30-foot parachute. It was huge.”
“The researchers came out with a truck and trailer they used to pick it up,” Walter added. “It's kind of surreal that it happened to us and that I was part of it.”

Context

This incident follows a series of high-altitude balloon launches by NASA aimed at conducting scientific experiments. The Columbia Scientific Balloon Facility regularly launches equipment to study various astronomical phenomena. Previous incidents involving lost equipment have raised questions about the safety and management of such research missions.

What’s next

NASA continues to investigate the circumstances surrounding the equipment's recovery and will assess the success of the research mission. Further updates from the agency are expected in the coming days.

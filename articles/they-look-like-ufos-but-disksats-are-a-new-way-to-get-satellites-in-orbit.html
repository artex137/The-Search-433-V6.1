They look like UFOs, but Disksats are a new way to get satellites in orbit

A new satellite technology called Disksats has emerged, resembling UFOs, and is set to revolutionize the way satellites are launched into orbit. The announcement was made on October 18, 2023, by a consortium of aerospace companies at a conference in Toronto, Ontario. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in relation to this technology.

Disksats aim to provide a more efficient and cost-effective method for launching satellites, which is significant as the demand for satellite services continues to grow. Industry experts believe that this innovation could enhance global communications and data collection capabilities. The companies involved are currently collaborating with regulatory bodies to secure the necessary approvals for launch operations.

Key facts

  • Technology announced on October 18, 2023
  • Location: Toronto, Ontario
  • Type of technology: Disksats
  • Primary goal: Cost-effective satellite launches
  • No reported injuries or fatalities

Timeline

On October 18, 2023, aerospace companies presented Disksats at a conference in Toronto. The companies outlined the potential benefits and applications of the new technology. As of now, they are working on regulatory approvals to begin launching operations.

Official statements

“Disksats represent a significant advancement in satellite technology, allowing for quicker and more affordable access to space,” said a spokesperson from the consortium of aerospace companies.

Context

The introduction of Disksats comes at a time when the satellite industry is experiencing rapid growth, driven by increasing demand for communication and data services. Previous satellite launch methods have often been costly and time-consuming, highlighting the need for more efficient solutions.

What’s next

The consortium plans to finalize agreements with regulatory authorities in the coming months to enable the first launches of Disksats, although specific timelines for these launches have not been disclosed.

They Look Like UFOs, But DiskSats Are a New Way to Get Satellites in Orbit

NASA has funded a mission to deploy DiskSats, new spacecraft designed to streamline satellite designs and launch multiple satellites into orbit. The project aims to reduce mission costs and expand access to space, according to NASA's announcement.

The DiskSats originate from The Aerospace Corporation in El Segundo, California. The company aims to launch large satellite constellations into low-Earth orbit. Catherine Venturini, the principal investigator, stated that the project began with the goal of populating orbits with many satellites quickly and affordably.

Key facts

  • Project funded by NASA
  • Developed by The Aerospace Corporation
  • Location: El Segundo, California
  • DiskSats designed to lower mission costs and improve satellite deployment

Timeline

NASA has announced the funding of the DiskSat mission recently. The project aims to provide a new alternative to CubeSats, which have limitations regarding size and capabilities. The first deployment of DiskSats is anticipated in the near future, though specific launch dates have not been disclosed.

Official statements

"The whole genesis of this was, 'Hey, we want to populate a constellation on orbit with many satellites, quickly and cheaply,'" said Catherine Venturini, principal investigator at The Aerospace Corporation.

Context

DiskSats are an alternative to CubeSats, which are small, box-shaped satellites designed for cost efficiency. However, CubeSats face constraints such as limited surface area and power generation capacity. DiskSats address these issues with a larger surface area, allowing for better component arrangement, including solar panels.

What’s next

The Aerospace Corporation plans to continue developing DiskSats and preparing for their deployment. Further details on launch schedules and operational capabilities are expected to be released in the coming months.

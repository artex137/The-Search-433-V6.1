SPHEREx Imaging Reveals Increased Sublimation Activity on 3I/ATLAS

The SPHEREx observatory has captured new imaging of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS, revealing increased sublimation activity since its closest approach to the sun in late October 2025. The observations were taken between December 8 and 15, 2025, and have shown significant changes in the comet's gas emissions compared to earlier data.

This discovery is crucial for understanding the comet's composition and behavior. The findings indicate a dramatic increase in activity, suggesting a new ice reservoir may have become active. Scientists are analyzing the data to gain insights into the processes occurring on 3I/ATLAS and its potential implications for future interstellar studies.

Key facts

  • Discovery date of 3I/ATLAS: July 2025
  • Closest approach to the sun (perihelion): Late October 2025
  • SPHEREx imaging dates: December 8-15, 2025
  • Increase in CO emissions: 20-fold compared to pre-perihelion data
  • Increase in H2O emissions: 20-fold compared to pre-perihelion data
  • CO2 emitted flux: 33% larger than in August 2025

Timeline

3I/ATLAS was first discovered in July 2025. Initial spectrographic data was collected in August 2025, revealing minimal H2O gas and a CO2 coma as the comet approached the sun. During this time, emissions of carbon-based organic compounds were not detected.

Following its perihelion in late October 2025, SPHEREx observed the comet again in December 2025. The new imaging indicated a significant increase in gas emissions, with both CO and H2O emissions increasing by 20 times compared to earlier observations.

Official statements

“The CO emitted flux has increased ~20x, meaning that the CO/CO2 abundance ratio has also increased ~15-fold. This implies that a new ice reservoir is now active along with the one supporting the CO2-coma,” the study authors stated.

Context

Prior observations of 3I/ATLAS indicated it was relatively inactive before its perihelion, with low levels of detectable gases. The significant change in activity post-perihelion highlights the dynamic nature of such interstellar objects and their potential for providing insights into the early solar system.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to analyze the collected data from SPHEREx to further understand the sublimation processes on 3I/ATLAS and assess the implications for future interstellar research.

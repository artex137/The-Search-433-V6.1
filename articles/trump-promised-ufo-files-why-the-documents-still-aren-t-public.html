Trump promised UFO files. Why the documents still aren’t public

Former US President Donald Trump has called for the release of classified files related to unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) following a surge of public interest sparked by a recent podcast appearance by former President Barack Obama. Obama’s comments about the statistical probability of extraterrestrial life revived fascination with UFOs, but weeks later, the promised documents have yet to be made public, according to CNN.

The lack of released files has raised questions about the complexities involved in the disclosure process. Government records on UAPs often contain sensitive information about military technology and intelligence operations, which requires careful review before any public release. Officials have stated that the review process can take months or even years due to the volume of material involved.

Key facts

  • Former President Barack Obama made comments about aliens during a podcast interview that went viral.
  • President Donald Trump has called for the release of UFO-related files.
  • As of now, no documents have been publicly released.
  • The review process for UAP files involves multiple government agencies.
  • The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office at the Pentagon is coordinating the investigation of UAPs.

Timeline

In mid-2023, Barack Obama joked about the existence of aliens during a podcast, prompting renewed public interest in UFOs. Shortly after, Donald Trump urged government agencies to begin identifying and releasing classified files related to UAPs. Despite the call for transparency, no files have been released as of the latest reports.

The All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office, established to investigate unusual aerial sightings, is currently involved in the review process of UAP records. This office collects data from military personnel and various detection systems.

Official statements

“The process is rarely quick,” an official familiar with the review process said. “Trained security officials must examine each document line by line.”

Context

The current interest in UAPs is not unprecedented. Previous releases of UFO-related documents in the 1970s and later decades primarily contained routine paperwork, such as sighting reports and internal correspondence. Many of these reports detailed unremarkable observations that did not lead to significant findings.

Experts warn that the upcoming release may yield similar results, with much of the information potentially lacking dramatic revelations. Sensitive materials, particularly high-resolution images from military satellites, are likely to remain classified due to national security concerns.

What’s next

The review process for the UAP files is ongoing, with no specific timeline for when any documents might be released. Investigators continue to work through the extensive material accumulated over decades.

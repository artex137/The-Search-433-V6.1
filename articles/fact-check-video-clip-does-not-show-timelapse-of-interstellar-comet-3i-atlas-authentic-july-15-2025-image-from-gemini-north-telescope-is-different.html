Fact Check: Video Clip Does NOT Show Timelapse Of Interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS

A video clip circulating on social media does not show a timelapse of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS, according to a fact check by Lead Stories. The clip was posted on Instagram by @allbrightbretthunter on October 26, 2025, and misidentifies the image as being from the comet, which was captured by the Gemini North telescope on July 15, 2025.

This matter is significant as misinformation about astronomical phenomena can mislead the public and hinder scientific understanding. The National Optical-Infrared Astronomy Research Laboratory (NOIRLab) released the authentic image of 3I/ATLAS to aid scientific inquiry into the characteristics of this rare comet.

Key facts

  • The mislabeled video was posted on Instagram on October 26, 2025.
  • The authentic image of comet 3I/ATLAS was captured on July 15, 2025.
  • NOIRLab released the actual image of the comet.
  • Reverse image searches for the clip did not yield exact matches.
  • Google's AI misidentified the image as 3I/ATLAS in social media posts.

Timeline

On July 15, 2025, astronomers using the Gemini North telescope captured an image of interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS. This discovery marked the third known interstellar object to pass through our solar system.

On October 26, 2025, a mislabeled video clip surfaced on Instagram, claiming to show a timelapse of 3I/ATLAS. Lead Stories initiated a fact check to clarify the misinformation.

Official statements

“Interstellar objects are visitors from solar systems beyond our own, and the third ever such object, known as 3I/ATLAS, has just been discovered,” said NOIRLab in their official release.

Context

Misinformation regarding astronomical events is not uncommon, with social media often amplifying incorrect claims. The accurate portrayal of such phenomena is crucial for public understanding and scientific discourse.

What’s next

Lead Stories plans to update their fact check with analyses from microbiology experts regarding the origins of the mislabeled video clip.
